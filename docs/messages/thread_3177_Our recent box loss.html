Our recent box loss

From: the_cignacats (mlcigna@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-10-16 17:13:18 UTC
We too have recently lost multiple boxes, one even due to what
appears to be malicious human intervention, and it was really tough
for us to swallow. It actually turned us off to letterboxing, and
since we are still adapting to having a baby boxer, we decided that
it might be a sign that we should take a break for a while.
However, we recently had a really nice experience that has turned us
back around and I thought would be nice to share...

One of the first boxes we ever hid a few years back was placed in a
local nature center. It has gotten a lot of activity in the last
year, especially from what appear to be new families of boxers. We
were a bit concerned that it may go missing what with all of the
traffic it had been getting. Luckily, its hiding spot is pretty
well covered and every time we have gone to check on it, we have
found it exactly where it belongs, well cared for, and covered
properly. Way to go new boxers!

Then a few weeks back, I received an email from one of the staff
members at the nature center. She said that the maintenance crew
has been doing some grounds cleanup and the spot where our box had
been hiding was removed and no longer exists. The maintenance crew
brought the box to the office staff, who then got my email address
from the logbook. She said that she was holding the box for me
until I could pick it up and gave me her phone number. When I
called her, she was quite excited. She said that the entire staff
loved the letterbox and had never heard of boxing before. She had
made copies of the info sheet she found in the box for the whole
staff (it's a small staff mind you) and they had all gone out the
letterboxing.org for more information. She said that she was going
to look into carving her own stamp so that she could get out there
and find boxes on her own. She even went so far as to thank us for
hiding the box there, as it has brought many people and families to
the nature center over the last few years. Overall it was a really
nice experience and wonderful to have new people so excited about
boxing and to have been lucky enough to have had our box discovered
by such kind people that went out of their way to keep our box
intact.

We are hoping to get out boxing in the next few weeks before it gets
bitter cold here. Perhaps a few new finds will get us even more
excited and we can spend the winter months carving in anticipation
of the spring. Happy boxing!

The CignaCats
