I remember when my grandmother gave me my dad's set of Hardy Boys books --
that was in the late 60's/early 70's so many of them were probably the
original printings... but they are long gone, probably sold by my mother at
a garage sale for a quarter each -- oh well at least some other kid got to
read them.

How about the Happy Hollisters? Anyone else read those (am I dating myself
here)? They were my favorites -- about a family with a bunch of kids (5 I
think) that went to cool places on vacation and came across mysteries to
solve. I have slowly been collecting these books over the years as I see
them reasonably priced at flea markets and on E-Bay, etc. Have been
thinking it would make a cool letterbox series (so many ideas, so little
time... I should have been a teacher ;-)

Never much read the Hardy Boys books (but as a four year old thought that Sean Cassidy was REALLY cute on the Hardy Boys Mystery show that my family would watch on Sunday mornings ;) but I loved Nancy Drew. I loved those kids

I learned to read on the Happy Hollisters! They were my sister's books, but
I borrowed all of them and actually missed the characters and stories when I
ran out of new books to read. Natalie I started over and reread the series.

then. Good idea to hunt them down. I can't find anyone who has ever even
heard of them. Nancy Drew and the Hardy Boys were great for older kids, but
the Happy Hollisters met the voracious appetite of the young reader! Make a LB
series out of those kids. They were much like us, finding mysteries while
on vacations. They deserve a comeback
