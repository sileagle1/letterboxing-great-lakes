Hi all,

Yesterday I found my 100th box! It only took me 25 months! heheheh . . .
actually by the end of the day I was at 102 finds.

I have also screwed up my courage and (encouraged by Shutterfly) am starting
to carve my own stamps. I hope to replace the commercial stamps in all my
planted boxes with hand-carved ones very soon.

My question is: what is a good size for a stamp? I am finding that I am
not adept enough to carve very small ones . . . if the largest dimension
were about 3 inches, would that be pretty much acceptable for all? If not,
then maybe 2-1/2 inches???

Thanks,

Chris (Trail Rider)

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2006-08-15 00:25:18 UTC-05:00
Congratulations on your 100th!! It is the first really BIG milestone. We
are also REALLY happy to hear you will begin carving!

Don't worry about size, carve what you are comfortable with. I think that
generally anything that is within 3.5" x 5" will fit in most letterboxer's
logbooks. If you have something larger, you can just mention it in your
clues if you'd like.
