MI: Old Mission

From: Armadillo Jo (jes7o@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-09-06 20:26:36 UTC-07:00
Ah...a little Labor Day letterboxing was in order
today, before the weather turned foul. We headed up to
the Old Mission Peninsula and found:

Old Mission
Old Mission Lighthouse
Archie Park

All are in great shape! Old Mission Lighthouse is VERY
near a Geocache, so the logbook is nearly full with
entries (there are about 5 pages left.) Old Mission
was a tough, but extremely good find. We second
guessed ourselves and ended up coming back to the spot
we thought it was all along. Archie Park has the
cutest stamp!

We attempted Leland Fishtown, but it was rather dark
and it seems that the location where the box once was
is now part of private property and sort of fenced
off. Didn't get to the Sleeping Bear Dunes, as we were
too busy loafing around Lake Leelenau and Fishtown. I
did get to pick up a fabulous new pair of Tevas and
threw away my old stinky hiking sandals. My feet are
happy!

Also, picked up Jackie's Beanstalk HH at Archie
Park...and it has left MI...for points unknown. Maybe
a rest at that fancy schmancy Hoosier Hostel will do
it good, but who knows where it'll end up....

--Armadillo Jo

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-09-06 23:58:42 UTC-04:00
Hey, thanks for the report on the Old Mission Lighthouse box! Yes, that
geocache... I keep hoping it will go away, disappear or something. It has
been there since a couple of weeks after the box was placed -- about every
second entry in the online geocache log indicates that they also found the
letterbox. They have both survived for about 2 years now, so I guess I
shouldn't be too concerned (?).

It is interesting that several of my letterboxes along the lakeshore areas
have geocaches practically right on top of them -- it just seems so
coincidental that with all the open space we have along the lakeshore, they
end up within feet of each other. I know the Point Betsie Lighthouse box is
regularly found by cachers also. I actually have about 12 geocaches that I
watch that are within feet of my letterboxes and are often found by cachers.
It is a nice way to keep tabs on the letterboxes when I don't hear from
letterboxers who find them. There is one letterbox that has had about twice
as many cachers as letterboxers -- they have a system in the logbook --
cachers sign in from the back and letterboxers sign in from the front. The
geocache isn't even at that place anymore and there is still a steady stream
of cachers that find the letterbox looking for the [now non-existent]
geocache.

Anyway, I will be up to Mission Point in a couple of weeks so I will plan to
bring up a new log book for the box then.

SC

From: aquamarine31577 (karajmcc@aol.com) | Date: 2004-09-07 16:14:28 UTC
I did some Labor Day weekend boxing as well up near TC. I'm glad the
Jackie's Beanstalk HH was picked up so quickly. Archie Park is a
pretty cool stamp (and the logbook is pretty full - we had to start
back at the beginning on the back of a page to stamp in it).

Fun story about Old Mission Peninsula LB...My friend and I found the
spot alright, but it was empty. We could clearly see where the box
had been, and with a bit of disappointment we stubbornly poked around
all the other potential hiding spots. Grumbling dejectedly, we were
about to give up when a geocacher brought us back the box, as well as
a cammo-covered geocache. We were excited to get the stamp (lovely,
SpringChick) and took the liberty to create a new (albiet very close)
hole for the geocache.

We also tried our hardest to find the Old Mission-45th Parallel box,
with no luck. I'm glad to know it's out there, and kinda bummed that
I didn't stumble upon it.

My letterboxing weekend also included Roaming Jolly Roger (neat
stamp, Bindle Babe!), Shrine of the Pines, Interlochen (talking about
searching all available rotting stumps before actually finding it!),
Serendipity (lovely...I'm glad I really examined the clues before I
left home, or I would have been in for a big disappointment), and
Point Betsie (Esperanza's box). The sun had already set when we got
out to Pt Betsie, and after a good 20 minutes of trying to wedge that
box out of its hiding spot, it was dusk. We tried for SpringChick's
Pt Betsie box afterwards, and I'm pretty sure we were in the right
spot, but we had no flashlight and were ultimately unsuccessful
feeling around in the dark for this box. Oh well, some other time.

Hope everyone had a relaxing weekend.
Peace, Keltic Kara
