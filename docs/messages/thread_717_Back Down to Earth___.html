Back Down to Earth...

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-10-03 15:44:52 UTC

Hi everyone! OldShoe and I are back from a week in the Western
Upper Peninsula (MI). Wow! What a trip! Although we were a bit
early for most of the color, we had gorgeous weather most of the
week and had the opportunity to visit many new places -- with and
without letterboxes. We averaged about 8 miles a day hiking -- the
most memorable hikes being several in the Porcupine Mountains, the
Sturgeon River Gorge near Baraga and Hogback Mountain in Marquette
(OMG, what a trek that was!). We drove winding dirt roads to
countless waterfalls, saw several lighthouses, visited many
historical places and saw hundreds of deer, a couple bald eagles and
a wolf (but no moose or bear), not to mention being immersed in
breathtaking scenery for the entire week. I have right around 1000
photos -- isn't digital wonderful?

Thanks to everyone who planted new letterboxes in the western U.P.
over the past couple of years -- the hunt for the box took us to
many places we probably would not have otherwise found. We logged
about 17 letterbox finds (hit F200 at Canyon Falls -- thanks Tim!),
checked up on several of our previously planted boxes and planted 7
new boxes (I know, I swore I wasn't going to plant so far away from
home -- but my heart is in the U.P. and I couldn't resist). Clues
to follow.

Anyway, I will try to catch up on list messages over the course of
the next few days. Thanks Azobox for keeping things running
smoothly here and a great TOTW last week!

Happy Trails!

SpringChick
