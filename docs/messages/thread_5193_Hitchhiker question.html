Hi everyone:
I am heading to Chicago for the weekend, and thought I'd do some
boxing. I would like to hide my first HH in an existing box and
wondered if anyone had any advice on stamps and containers. I recently
picked up a HH in a box and it was a small, beautiful little stamp in a
neat glass tube container with a lid. I still have not received my
carving supplies to make my own stamps that I ordered from Dixie on
line, so would it be weird to do a small store-bought stamp? I fear my
signature stamp is too large for a HH. Should I put it in a case or
just a ziploc bag? Thanks!

Popcorn Lady


Re: [LbGLK] Hitchhiker question

From: (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2008-07-16 00:37:22 UTC
Hi Popcorn Lady (love that name)! You can use a store-bought stamp for a HH if you want to. Although I think the preference is hand-carved, there is nothing that says it must be. The thing you will find with a store bought stamp, especially for a HH, is that the majority of the stamps sold by retail stores have wood backings, which can make the stamp a bit bulky. The idea with a HH is to make it small enough to fit easily into most existing letterboxes along with the box contents already there.

Some people do use separate containers for HH's, but most of the time they are just a bagged log and stamp, which leaves them smaller and more flexible to put into a letterbox. The glass tube container you found sounds interesting, but I'm not sure how well the glass will hold up over time and it would be a mess if it broke inside a letterbox.

Have fun in Chicago -- some great boxes there!

SpringChick

From: Chris Mothkovich (chris@mothkovich.com) | Date: 2008-07-15 19:38:03 UTC-05:00
Hi Popcorn Lady (love your name!):

Every hitchhiker I've ever found (14), and also the 3 I've launched, have
been hand-carved stamps. Sometimes the HH and its logbook have been just
inside a plastic baggie, and sometimes inside a very small container such as
a film canister.

You probably know this already, but the HH stamp image is stamped into the
logbook of the host letterbox, and the host stamp into the HH logbook. As
the HH moves from box to box, the stamp image from each host box shows its
travels.

Good luck boxing in Chicago!

Trail Rider
