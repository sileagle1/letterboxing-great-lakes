From: Anne and Dick (rhesse34@hotmail.com) | Date: 2005-05-03 13:55:41 UTC
Very Good! However, on a more serious note....
DON'T forget check YOURSELF (very carefully) for ticks.
A year and a half ago (Oct.'03) while LB'ing with Martini Man and
Wisconsin Hiker, I picked up a tick. It attached itself, and I found
it the next evening. The area around the bite was red, swollen,
itchy and about the size of a quarter. I checked out the tick with a
disecting microscope (at the local public health office) and it was a
deer tick.
So, I went to the doctor. The test for Lyme disease was negative,
but because of the other symptoms I was put on two weeks of
antibiotics. I continued to feel fine, but the bite continued to
itch for several weeks.
Last August (10 months later) I started having chills, then sweats,
then some aches and pains, then fatigue (a walk to the maibox
required a two hour nap to recover), then drenching night sweats (4 t-
shirts and 8 towels), then a rash. During this time I was seeing my
doctor and tests were being made, but there was nothing conclusive.
Then he decided to test for Lyme disease and it came back positive.
About 12 hours after starting the antibiotics I was pretty much back
to normal.
BUT... There had been no ticks, no red, itchy spots, in fact, I
could not find any insect bites anywhere on my body. Lyme Disease is
bad stuff and the ticks a very small. Please be careful.
Merlin
