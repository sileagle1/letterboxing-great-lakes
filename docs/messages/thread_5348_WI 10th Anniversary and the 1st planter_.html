WI 10th Anniversary and the 1st planter!

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2008-10-10 02:41:30 UTC
After we got home from the anniversary event, I was curious to see if
I could get any info on the person who planted the first box. I
turned to my friend "Google" and found a few references, including
one that noted he was the advisor & instructor for a fencing club at
a high school in Green Bay. The article also noted that an assistant
instructor was Ms. Dierdra Baumgart. Hmmm.... "Dierda" was the name
of a person who also planted some of the early Wisconsin boxes.
Since the article included his email address, I sent him a note to
see if he was indeed the planter and if Dierda was also an early
letterboxer. Here is his reply:
===================
Yes, I am the planter for the Cave Point letterbox. Both Dierdra and
I planted a lot of boxes, but haven't been out boxing in a long time.
Every once in a while if I find myself near one of the boxes, I'll
check if it's still there, but I think others have taken over
maintaining them. (Which is just fine with me, by the way, as I
don't get up to Door Co. as much as I used to.) It's good to know
that there are more boxers in Wisconsin now, and that some of ours
are still around.

Steven
==================

I then sent him another note asking how he got started and this is
his interesting reply:

================
Debbie,
Yes, Dierdra and I still know each other; in fact she is my fiance.
I first heard about letterboxing about the same time everyone in the
US read about it in the Smithsonian article. A friend of mine read
it and thought we should try to get something started in Door Co.
where we both worked as summer help at a resort. I started searching
the internet, and made contact with the others just getting started
around the country... Map Surfer, Der Mad Stamper, and many others.
I planted the Cave Point Letterbox, the first box in Wisconsin, on
Sept 26, 1999. (Or was it 1998? I know the day, but I'm not sure
about the year... been a while.) My friend, Todd, was the first to
find it, though he had no stamp. After that, I planted a few more
around Door Co. and Green Bay (where I live), and even placed one in
Madison where I had gone to college. Another boxer from Madison had
the first one in that area. All the Madison boxes went missing
quickly as I recall. Dierdra placed a lot of boxes around Green Bay,
but didn't take weather and people into account. Some washed away,
others were stolen. She had a series along the East River that when
put together formed a map of the area. It was really well done, but
all long gone now. We both got busy with other activities and jobs,
and being the only boxers around it wasn't that much of a challenge,
so we lost touch with letterboxing. I think some of my better
letterboxes are still out there though... Cave Point, Mink River, and
Cana Island were all intact the last time I was able to check any of
those locations. Most of the rest of the Lighthouse series were
casualties of weather, wear, and critters (furry as well as human.)
Back in those days, the message board (or one of the original ones, I
think it's moved twice since then) was often contentious with people
arguing the fundamentals of what letterboxing should be and how it
should be run. Some things never change, do they? Some of the early
founders left the hobby because of the issues, but I guess many of
them are still on the boards. Maybe now that there are more boxes
around, it might be the time to get back into things.
Steven
================

Since we're in a historical frame of mind this past week, if anyone
is interested in seeing the 2001 map & list of the 34 existing WI
letterboxes, here is a link:

http://web.archive.org/web/20010629003203/www.letterboxing.org/wi/wi.h
tm

Wisconsin Hiker
