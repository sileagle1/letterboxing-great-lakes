It shouldn't matter what direction you are facing, since the compass bearing
is based on North which will be the same regardless of what direction you
are facing. Here is a brief summary of how to use a compass:

To find a certain bearing, turn the dial of a compass until your desired
degree is aligned with the direction-of-travel arrow pointing ahead, and
turn the entire compass until the needle matches the orienting arrow
underneath (put red in the shed). Find a landmark and follow the
direction-of-travel arrow to it.

If you want more details, this is a good site:
http://www.earthsci.org/fieldsk/comp2/comp2.html and Ryan Carpenter has a
lot of good info on AtlasQuest. This is the page that might be most helpful
to you:
http://www.atlasquest.com/tutorials/compass/bearings.html

Hope this helps!

Wisconsin Hiker
