I often letterbox alone, and I carry pepper spray in my pack. It
doesn't offer perfect protection, but nothing could. I also have a
super-loud whistle, which could be used to scare off coyotes or bears
(as well as creepy people), but also, if I should need help in a
remote area, it can be heard for quite a distance - even when my cell
phone doesn't get reception. My advice to be prepared, stay aware of
your surroundings, but don't let fear keep you from what you enjoy.

A man lit himself on fire near one of my plants last year - but
fortunately they found the body before anybody could go looking for
that
box!

TrailTroll

From: xxxxxxxx (BrighidFarm@comcast.net) | Date: 2008-09-12 07:04:36 UTC-05:00
I hate to say this............

but I think as the population increases and more and more vacant property
gets bought up by developers and developed......

and more and more video cameras are placed all over the place that can
identify people who do bad things and the cars they drove to do those bad
things.........

about the only places left to "safely" dump murdered bodies or to "safely"
mug people are going to be the forest preserves.

Just have to be more and more careful. I've got a good strong gut instinct.
I've had a couple of serious close calls when letterboxing alone (once in
DeKalb County and once in Kane County) but my gut kicked in and told me to
move on outta there while there was still time to do so and I ALWAYS do what
my gut tells me. ALWAYS. My sister had one close call in Kane County but
her gut instinct kicked in too and she got outta there.

I carry boxcutters so that might help me in some way (they're also illegal
to carry in some jurisdictions tho), I carry spray, I never go out boxing
alone with small children, I carry a good strong whistle, and frankly, I
think my voice is even stronger than any whistle when I want it to be. Just
ask Caveman. :-) And I've taken some self-defense lessons. I carry the
boxcutters and spray right in my bluejeans pocket so that I can reach for
them immediately. The whistle is in my other pocket. The guy who gave me
self-defense lessons told me never to put the whistle on a lanyard around my
neck because the person could overpower me and then easily use the lanyard
to strangle me. OKaaaaaaaaaaaay, so I keep it in my pocket, attached to the
lanyard, and the lanyard attached to my belt.

I don't know if those taser things are legal in IL or not. I know folks can
buy them over the internet. Knowing me, I'd probably end up tasering
myself. :-)

Yeah, my cell phone is always with me, but Murphy's Law says there will be
no reception in that area at any given time that I need it. Been there done
that. And it's gonna take too long to get help to a remote area anyway.
But I always have it turned on so the GPS thingee is working and can maybe
trace me if I'm abducted.

I don't know if Caveman would pay any ransom on me or not if the idea was to
abduct me for money instead of murder. Probably not. :-) It's not like we
have any money anyway tho, so it's not like that would be a possible
consideration.

~~ Mosey ~~

From: Katrina Lustofin (drkaty@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-09-12 14:10:25 UTC-04:00
A little off-topic, but when I was taking field biology courses in
California, way back when I was a Master's student, we took a lot of field
trips into remote canyons and forests to look at plant diversity. At the
beginning of the semester, the professor gave us the following guidelines
for field trips:
1. always be in groups of at least three people
2. stay within shouting distance of another group
3. make sure some one in the group has good navigational sense and can find
their way back to the van
and
4. "walk quietly away from any dead bodies you might find and don't ever
tell any one about it." Apparently had been a problem in previous years and
he was tired of explaining to the tiny little local police force why he was
in such a remote location, so his solution was to pretend the dead bodies
weren't there. Personally, I would found a different canyon to take my
class to.

I'm happy to say we did not find any bodies during my semester in the class
(or at least, if there was a body found, the person did as instructed). And
I don't think I've ever planted a box anywhere near any dead bodies...

Katy
who doesn't *carry* protection, just the end of the leash to which her
protection is attached ;)
