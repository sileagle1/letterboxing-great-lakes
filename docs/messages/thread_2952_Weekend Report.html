Weekend Report

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2006-08-15 06:04:34 UTC
We planned a 3-day weekend AUG 12-14 to finally embark on our Around
the World in 80 Days tour. Unfortunately our travels had an
inauspicious start with the cancellation of the Lake Express ferry.
This meant a long drive to MI, but we arrived safe and sound.

We had received our very professional passport on July 31 and
deciphered the clever clues that week. We were excited to begin our
actual tour on Saturday morning. We were able to visit 6 cities on
Saturday, 7 on Sunday and the last two on Monday. Woo hoo!! We were
quite pleased to be able to visit every city in our whirlwind tour.
Very nice stamps and some challenging searches!!

After our first day of travels, we enjoyed the generous hospitality
of the Doglvrs family. We enjoyed dining with them, exchanging LB
stories and getting to meet the basis for their trailname - dogs
Emily, Alex and Lana. The family are true animal lovers since they
also have two cats!!

We had hoped to also meet with Azobox, but the challenges of our
searches on Sunday consumed more time than expected.

Luckily we had enough time on Monday to visit many of the newer boxes
in Yankee Springs and during the course of the weekend found three
other boxes useful to another Quest.

We thought we might be able to rendevous with Grandvilla &
MobiusKlein on Monday evening before we caught the late ferry home,
but ALAS, the ferry was cancelled AGAIN! Another long drive, but we
arrived home safe and sound with approximately 1,400 miles under our
belt.

In addition to the boxes, items of interest along the way included
many deer, two fat racoons scurrying up a tree, a turkey hen crossing
the road in front of us, tasty turkey jerky and some MI peaches
(we're amazed that they grow peaches in MI!).

Thanks to all of you who place boxes and make these adventures
possible!!

Wisconsin Hiker & Martini Man

From: (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2006-08-15 09:09:03 UTC-05:00
Ferry seems to be having lots of problems lately. Some
supposedly "weather" but many mechanical. Their website has revised
schedules to accommodate some maintenance this coming weekend.

Two years ago we took the ferry and both crossings were on time.
However BindleBabe and Mrs. Doglvrs ran into a cancellation last fall
when they were coming to WI. Moral of the story is to call in advance
before heading to the ferry dock (or check their website). Luckily we
did this early enough yesterday that we were able to start driving well
before the ferry would have departed and therefore got home 1.5 hours
earlier than if we had taken the ferry. However it would have been
much more relaxing sitting on the ferry sipping a glass of wine!
