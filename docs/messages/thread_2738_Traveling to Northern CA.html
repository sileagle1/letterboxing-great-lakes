My husband and I were in San Francisco last summer and did some
boxing. I have to say that my favorite box we found was the San
Francisco Columbarium letterbox. The columbarium is a burial vault
since cemataries are not allowed in SF. It is filled with alcoves
that house the remains of many SF familes. Some of the alcoves are
very old and display beautiful old urns while some of the newer
alcoves are lovingly decorated with momentos, cards, photos,
stories, etc.

We never would have thought to visit the columbarium had it not been
for letterboxing, but it was one of the most beautiful places we
went while there. The stain-glass windows and mosaics are beautiful
and make for wonderful photos. The box only took a few minutes to
find but we spent about 2 hours there just looking around - crying
at some resting spots and laughing at others. It is worth the trip!

Here are a few photos - http://www.noehill.com/sf/landmarks/sf209.asp

Have a great trip!
The CignaCats

From: cerealcityboxers (cerealcityboxers@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-07-04 13:41:43 UTC
My wife and I visited "very" northern California in September of 2003
and did some letterboxing. The furthest south we ventured was
Eureka. If you get a chance to go "really" north in California, we
suggest the "Oasis" box in Eureka, the "Super Sewer" (we know, it
sounds kinda wierd, but it was a nice walk), and the absolute best
hike was in the Redwoods doing the "Skunk Cabbage" box a little
further north in Orick. There's something primevil about walking in
the Redwoods. Have a great trip.
