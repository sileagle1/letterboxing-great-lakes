>Speaking of the peanut can, has anyone seen the new
>"Charlie and the Chocolate Factory?" When Willy
>Wonka said, "Don't touch that squirrel's nuts" all I
>could think of was Ryan's box at the GL
>Gathering last month and all the fun we had with it.

My weekend consisted of Charlie and the Chocolate
Factory (brilliant, but as I have never seen Willy
Wonka, I only have the book to compare it to....and I
read it in the 3rd grade...those squirrels were
definately demonic, nutty and I couldn't wait to see
Veruca Salt attacked), deciphering my passport (100%
successful, trip around the world now in planning
stages), and having my eye-balls glued to Harry Potter
(which has inspired my co-workers and I to concoct a
crack-pot theory that Harry Potter and the Star Wars
Saga are essentially the same story. Yes, this is what
social workers discuss over lunch.) At 1:00pm Sunday
afternoon, I decided that being holed up in my house
like a hermit reading and sleuthing was vaguely
pathetic....and went letterboxing. As I was getting on
US 31, my mother calls to report that it's raining
torrentially in the general direction I'm heading. It
was beautifully sunny when I left my house!
Undeterred, I went to Berrien County, MI.....where it
had just stopped raining....started at the rest area
and was too freaked out by the speeding cars, muggles,
traffic to think straight about the clue. Went to one
spot and didn't find a clump of stumps. I had an
unusual amount of paranoia about the rest stop,
thought the cops would come after me or something.
Decided to wait until I had company to try again.
Optimistically, I headed to Warren Woods. First off, I
was freaked out again because this loop is very
deserted and I was alone. I gave a half-assed attempt
to find the box, and decided that I would just try
again when I try for the first box (safety in
numbers!). On the way back to the car, in the middle
of the trail was a huge, black SNAKE. Now, i've
encountered snakes before...but this one was BIG and
taking up the entire right side of the trail. It's
head was facing the left side. It's at this point that
I begin swearing and praying simultaneously. It's like
Indiana Jones, I HATE SNAKES! So I stand there,
approximately 100 feet from my car (which is on the
other side of the serpent), and weigh my options. I
can A: attempt to go around the snake, but risk it
strking out at my legs as I go by B: get a running
start and jump over the snake, risking it striking as
well. C: Step back 20 feet and throw sticks with the
hope that it will mosey on across the path. I chose
option C....I threw some small sticks at it. Nothing.
So I just stood there still for a few minutes.....and
then the freaking thing decides that I'M not a threat
and crosses the trail, allowing me to pass. I got back
to the car and decided, NO MORE LETTERBOXES TODAY! So
all of that, and not one letterbox to speak of! But
yet another adventure to write about :)

Ug, snakes!

--Armadillo Jo
