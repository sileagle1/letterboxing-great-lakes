Now, I do believe I can claim to be a member of the Planters Who Goofed Club - including that subgroup of planters who lost their own clues. From: Atom118 (Aatom118@gmail.com) | Date: 2010-04-14 10:51:40 UTC-05:00
Recently, we had a 'Planters Who Goofed' moment.

We picked up our '2009-Bad Year' series on the 3rd....so, we had a BB in
there (my excuse for poor counting), and I'm sure I counted all the
boxes SEVERAL times while collecting them. A week later, I'm making
logbooks to turn them into HH's....'hhhmmm....were is the one from
June'...'don't know, I thought you counted them all'...after quickly
going through them in my head...I counted all the places we had hidden
the boxes...but was one short. So, after checking our clue...yep, sure
enough, we had forgotten one. Needless to say, we were out there again
this past weekend to grab the last one!

(sadly, this is the second time in two months we've forgotten where we
hid stuff.....last month while doing maintenance we had to call my
sister-in-law while out on the trail to look up and read us the clue.
She laughed and asked, 'Uh...are you guys trying to find one of your own
boxes?!')

Atom118 of Atomic Beans

I think we might be the president and CEO of this club! Considering we planted a LOST box in Minooka and then forgot about it and never wrote up the clues, only realizing it after the gathering. It really is amazing how much an area can change. Some changes are really drastic...Like our Kingsley Bend box! Talk about cutting down a whole forest! There was no way we'd be able to track down where that box was.
