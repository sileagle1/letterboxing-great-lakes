Re: PZkut ... aka: moving right along ... (grin)

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-12-06 02:22:08 UTC
Like BindleBabe, I use the iron transfer method exclusively. You
will have to experiment a bit with your particular iron as far as
the heat setting and the timing, as all irons are different, but
once you used to it, you should get a nice crisp result every time.

You need to start with an image printed with a laser printer or
copied on a toner-based copy machine. I have heard people get
decent results with an ink-jet printer, but I have not ever been
able to get it to work. Maybe it depends on the brand of ink-jet
printer you are using? I have both an HP and a Canon and neither
have been successful for me.

So, print or copy your image at the size you want it, leaving plenty
of white space around the printed image. Lay the image side down
onto your carving material, making sure that all of the carving
material is covered with paper (the reason you want to leave extra
paper around the image). I use medium heat on my iron -- it has
temp settings from 1 to 10 and I us 5 for transfers, but like I said
that will vary -- not hot enough and the image transfer will be
light, too hot and you will damage the carving material.

Place the iron over the entire design and firmly hold it down. I
don't use any steam and I don't move it around, just leave it in one
place, holding it down rather firmly. After about a minute (again,
it will take some experimenting to get the time right), remove the
iron and lift up the paper from one corner slowly -- it will have a
bit of "stick" to it. Don't remove the entire piece of paper all at
once because if it didn't transfer well, you can't get it back down
in the same place, so just peel it back a little at a time. If the
image is not dark enough or you reach a place where it did not
transfer good, lay it back down and apply some more heat to the
areas in need of attention.

Once it looks good, finish peeling back the paper and you're ready
to carve!

Good luck -- sounds like a neat stamp!

SpringChick
