From: SpringChick (springchick1219@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-02-08 11:16:07 UTC

I know a lot of people are anxious to make travel plans for the
gathering and I was wondering if we might be able to nail down a
location soon? "Detroit area" leaves the door pretty wide open as
in Michigan anything east of Lansing that fits into a loose triangle
between Ann Arbor, Detroit and Flint is dubbed the "Detroit" area.

I think I saw in a previous message that you guys were going to try
for something a bit SE of Detroit proper and wanted throw out a
suggestion for Waterloo State Recreation area where we held the
first GL Gathering. It is a huge park that actually is spread out
over several physical locations, many with miles of very nice
trails -- opportunity for planting boxes is great! There are
several clean picnic shelters -- nice size with tables, grills,
electricity and restrooms at the shelters.

The location is convenient for those coming from other states as it
is just a few minutes off I-94, about 1 hour from Detroit proper and
1/2 hour from Ann Arbor and Jackson, so lodging should be plentiful.

Just a suggestion...

SpringChick

From: pippintbk (pippintbk@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-02-08 17:21:50 UTC

Forget not that the weekend of June 18th is a major NASCAR weekend
in the Detroit area. While I grant you that the race itself is down
near Adrian in the Irish Hills section, I did a quick hotel search
in Ann Arbor and came up basically with the big zippo as far as
hotels go. Jackson was pretty much the same. I did not call about
camping availability at Waterloo State Recreation Area, however its
proximity and the fact that it's a direct shot down to Adrian might
mean that they too are full up for that weekend. I know I mentioned
this once before to this group and was pretty much told that the
gathering wouldn't be affected - I'm not sure that will truly be the
case. NASCAR weekends in Detroit have the same effect on hotel
rooms as Notre Dame football weekends or graduation weekend in South
Bend - hotels can and will charge "rack rate".

NASCAR expanded that weekend's race schedule and added a race, so it
may indeed impact the event.

From: Deb McClurken (springchick1219@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-02-08 12:47:03 UTC-05:00
Yes, and there is a Tiger Ballgame and the Strawberry Festival in Belleville
is that weekend -- all these things will impact lodging availability
anywhere in the Detroit area, which is why I think we need to get a location
set soon.

I did a quick Expedia search for lodging in Jackson and Ann Arbor. As you
said there is nothing in the Jackson area, but several rooms, in a wide
range of prices, still available around Ann Arbor and many sites open at the
Waterloo campgrounds. There are also a handful of hotels and B&B in smaller
surrounding cities.

I just threw out Waterloo as a suggestion to get some ideas going and
because we had remarked at the first gathering at what a nice location it
was and the folks on the committee for this year were not at that event.
When I was scouting locations for the 2003 Gathering, there were a few very
nice parks in the Ann Arbor area also. Perhaps something in the more
immediate Ann Arbor area would be an idea.

SpringChick

I am going to the NASCAR weekend in both June and
August at MIS. While we now camp at the track, we did
look for hotels a couple of years ago and the closest
ones we found where 45-90 minutes away and were
charging over $100/night. Traffic coming into and out
of the track on race days (Friday, Sat, Sunday) where
backed up for about an hour, also they put special
"traffic routes" in effect for the track that weekend,
so if you will be heading that way. I suggest you
visit the MIS website at www.mispeedway.com to make
sure you travel plans won't be re-routed.
