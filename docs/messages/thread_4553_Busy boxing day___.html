on the trail and then also met a family who had recently started boxing
in a different area of the park.

Glad to hear everyone is having fun and we appreciate the status
updates!

I too appreciate the contact the placer Emails I got today.

Wisconsin Hiker, your Email prompted me to carve and plant a new letterbox.

Kinda along the lines of my limited time only stamp of February of this year
(for those of you not on list yet, I hid a box in my driveway the night of a
snow storm.... to find it you had to find our house and shovel the driveway
BEFORE I got up for work the next morning).

This box is a little more forgiving as far as time limit... it will remain
until someone finds it!!! Could be tomorrow, could be next spring -- but why
risk it, hurry up before someone beats you to the punch!!

CLUES;

Find our house... take the interstate, to the avenue to the street. We are
the white house down that street!

Once at our house start cleaning out our gutters. This is a micro box hidden
beneath the leaves and sludge of one of our gutters.

If you clean all the gutters and come up empty handed, move on to the next
house as you probably cleaned out our neighbors gutters by mistake . . . they
would be very grateful of your mistake.

Good luck and happy hunting!!

Hart x6

P.S. I can't take all the credit for this idea, so thanks Wisconsin Hiker of
being my inspiration!

Wow, luckily so many of YOU were out & about finding boxes - we got to
live vicariously through all the status update reports! We had
originally planned to do a nice 10-mile hike in the Southern Kettle
Moraine area with our hiking club on Sunday. However our conscience
got the better of us and we stayed home to do MORE mundane house
stuff! So intead of actually being out on the trails ourselves, we
enjoyed hearing that even more of our boxes were found on Sunday - the
WI State Bird, way in the northwestern part of the state, a couple of
boxes at Devil's Lake (a really nice place to hike), Governor Dodge
(more good hiking), and another finder for our most recent box in
Illinois.

We're looking forward to seeing a bunch of you at the "Wedding" this
weekend. We haven't been to IL since the SILO event (so missed the
locusts, floods, and other IL natural wonders). It sure looks like you
guys have been BUSY down there - so many new boxes for us to find!!
We'll barely make a dent on Saturday morning....

I second your idea of changing Wisconsin Hiker and Martini Mans Trail
names to "Legs of Steel". I to remember the pain that was felt in my
legs hunting for some of their boxes at Devil's Lake State Park. First
they send you up one side of the blufs walking up a trail that takes
you over 600 vertical feet looking for their box and then down the
other side. Not to bad but then they entice you to look for yet another
of their boxes. This time on the other side of the lake. Here you have
walk straight up the bluffs climbing your way up and over 684 vertical
stome steps to get to the top. Thank goodness for the reward at the
top. Once you get there alive you can and enjoy the wonderful views
along with their stamps. Keep on planting those ever elusive bokes!

RE: [LbGLK] Re: Busy boxing day...

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2007-11-05 20:51:21 UTC-06:00
LOL!!! You guys probably won't believe it, but on the day we planted
those Devil's Lake boxes we started out hunting boxes at Mirror Lake with
our 9-year old nephew and 12-year old niece. It was a VERY hot day, so
after boxing in the morning we surprised them with a visit to the Noah's Ark
water park. After we spent the rest of the day there, we left at about 5
pm. It was still quite hot but all 4 of us, tired from all the sun & water
park activities, made the hike all the way up & down & around the lake to
hide the boxes. The kids were groggy starting out, since they had fallen
asleep in the car, but picked up steam by the time we got halfway around.
After hiding the boxes, we all changed into our suits and went for a late
swim in the lake before heading home. Of course we stopped to find the "I
Believe in Ferries!" box on the way. A long but fun day!

Wisconsin Hiker

P.S. I just got ANOTHER report of people out hunting our boxes this
weekend! Aimlesst was getting in the Christmas mood with our Botanicals
series. I should tally up all the reports & logs we got - I guess everyone
was out & about before the snow flies. I DON'T like the dark & cold period
we are moving into...
