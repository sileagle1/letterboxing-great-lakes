A Day at Bristol

From: (duncan53211@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-07-11 00:09:23 UTC
Well, I got back from the opening day at Bristol. Got to meet
Dumpster Diva and Shutterfly and do an exchange. I also got to watch
them hunt for a few boxes. It was fun watching from the sidelines -
very different persective on letterboxing.

I did tweek the web page a little bit when I got home. Word got to me
early that the Pippin box was gone, and I couldn't find it for a
while. I hid the box on Thursday, and apparently on Friday they did
some landscaping. I finally moved a couple of rocks and finally
exposed the rock it is under. It should be fine now. Hopefully they
won't move the rocks back. Also, the bench for the "Ale" is gone, so
I changed the story to sit on the rocks there. I also fixed the clues
Rua wrote for St. Bridgid's Cross. You don't add the mancala number to
36, you put it in front of 36.

I also discovered that one of the sisters is back at the "dirty"
establishment, and that she is very interested in participating again.
So I moved the clues to "Please no rats in the soup" up to the
letterperson area on the webpage. Currently there are no rats in the
soup, as the are having trouble finding any around. She gaurntees
that the soup will be meatier once they catch some.

Also, if you have a chance to talk to Jane the Phool, she is a
letterboxer as well. She was not ready for exchanges today, but may
be in the future.

I should also note that I found out today that Letters, Letters,
Everywhere will not start until July 18, and will still be Sundays only.

Dragon


Re: A Day at Bristol

From: Angela (missangi1210@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-07-11 23:35:14 UTC
The day was a blast! It was pretty neat finding Dragon. Meeting
anyone I've talked to online is always fun. I got a kick out of Jane
too. We're sitting there, being all quiet about exchanging stamps,
and here is Jane yelling from the rooftops, "ARE YOU LETTERBOXERS!?"
That was priceless. Later, we had a longer conversation about boxing,
and rubber stamps in general.

The boxes were great. Dumpster Diva is great at finding and picking
them up without drawing attention to herself. She walked right behind
a couple once (inches away) to place the box back, and they didn't
notice her at all! Every time we were walking in circles trying to
find a box, we thought Dragon was hiding in the shadows, laughing at
our stupidity. Well, we know he was at least once. Bell, what bell?
Our "duh" moment at St. Brigid's Cross was also great. I don't think
Dragon saw that one, though. :P

If you get the chance, go to the faire. It is a ton of fun. Before
you go, though, check the website for coupons. You can get a few
dollars taken off the admission price with them.

Shutterfly
