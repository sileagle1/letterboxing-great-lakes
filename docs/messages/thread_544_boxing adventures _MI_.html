boxing adventures (MI)

From: bindlestiff_boxer (bindlestiff_boxer@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-08-15 13:51:23 UTC
Yesteray, Bindle Bum was away doing prep for an upcoming sailboat
race, so I took the opportunity to go place another box and do some
solo boxing as I want to get some more of the boxes around Muskegon.

First up was Spring Chick's Sung Harbor box, which is in great shape
and getting plenty of visitors.

As I was getting my backpack on in the parking lot, I thought I saw a
family in the picinic area getting ready to go boxing. You know how
we think we see them all the time; and it turns out not to be true.

Just in case, when I stamped in, I added the time plus a note to look
for the LbNA logo on my backpack and flag me down if there were other
boxers out today.

I hiked on and crossed over to the Devil's Kitchen trail. Beautiful
hike, but longer than I thought it would be. On the way back, I
peeked but didn't see anyone near the Snug Harbor box, so thought,
just like usual, I must have been wrong about the people at the
picinic area.

A ways further on the trail, I noticed that there were a couple of
boys messing around in the woods up on the West side of the trail.
After I went past, one of them yell, "It's her! Mom, Mom...I found
her!"

I got to meet the Found Objects Family, vacationing here from Denver.
They're geocachers doing a little letterboxing after seeing the Time
article and investigating online. They don't have a rubber stamp,
they only use found objects. "Cache in, trash out" the boys said,
almost in unison. Today they were using a leaf to stamp in with.

This was the first time they had done an exchange. I told them this
was the second time I'd been prepared when I met a letterboxer in the
wild. This promptly earned me the name Wild Boxer(LOL!)from the
youngest boy, who had been having trouble with Bindlestiff. (Enders
Angel has never let me forget the time we ran into Grandvilla and
Mobiusklien and *I* didn't have my logbook, stamp or anything, but
she did, and got to count it as an exchange, but won't let me.)

I then drove up to the Block House to work on boxes in that area.
Found Julies Locket safe and sound. OMG .. someone had already
stamped in with the same date! I may have just missed another trail
exchange. If you know who signs in using CRZ and their names, that's
who signed in yesterday.

By then, my gimpy foot was complaining and it was nearly time to head
over to Dockers to meet up with Bindle Bum and the others for dinner.

I got an email from Spring Chick saying she checked and the Indian
Point box is not missing .. it's alive and well! Arrrgh ... I'll have
to go back for that one. I get restless and edgy knowing I've missed
a "live" box!

Also, someone has cracked the code and solved the clues to the
Mystery Box we just placed. Congrats, on being first finder!

Clues to the box I placed yesterday will be coming soon.

Enjoy!
