We've been holding hitchhikers hostage!

From: Doodle & Deedle Bug (doodle_n_deedle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2011-08-10 17:32:46 UTC-07:00
It's been a very busy year for us, unfortunately not leaving us time to do much letterboxing recently other than an occasional drive-by. I was cleaning out our letterboxing bag and *gasp* found SEVEN hitchhikers that we'd collected over the last year or so when we were more actively boxing that we never got back out into the wild (I think one of these may actually be a cootie).

I can't believe we ended up with so many, and I know we won't be doing any letterboxing any time soon to get these out of our house. So... if you'd like to help a poor, stalled hitchhiker get back on its journey, send me a private email with your address, and I'll mail one out to you. The least I can do is help to spread these hitchhikers out across the Great Lakes region.

I hang my head in shame,

Doodle Bug.

Re: We've been holding hitchhikers hostage!

From: Katrina Lustofin (drkaty@gmail.com) | Date: 2011-08-11 08:53:06 UTC-04:00
When I was looking through one of my bags earlier this summer, I found a
flea that I hadn't remembered getting at the GLG last year. Since I was
missing the GLG this year to go to a friend's wedding, I decided to take the
flea with me and release it in a box near the wedding location. I didn't
really pay attention to who had the flea last or where it came from, but
after started stamping it into the box, I realized I was putting it in a box
that had been planted by the last finder of the flea - and then when I went
to the next box on my list, I found that the flea had been picked up from
that box. So this flea traveled halfway across the US, spent a year in
Yuki's backpack, and then traveled right back to a stone's throw away from
it's previous location.

So I'll pass on the offer for HHs, since I'm clearly not doing a good job
with helping them travel :)

Katy
