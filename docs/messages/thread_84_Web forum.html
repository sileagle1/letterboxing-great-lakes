Hi John,

Glad to hear you were successful with your stamp and looking forward
to seeing your box posted soon!

You present some good points in your post. It is true that there
are many similarities between letterboxing and geocaching, so much
that many people were actually taken in by last week's April Fool's
prank -- after all, marrying of the two hobbies is a conceivable
notion. I know that in this area of the country especially, where
letterboxing is not as prolific as it is on the East coast or the
Pacific NW, many people participate in both LB and GC.

Likewise the pros and cons of web forum vs e-list can be debated and
there will always be those who prefer one over the other. I think
they both have advantages and disadvantages.

That being said, while I certainly think it is advantageous for both
the LB and GC to take a united stance on land-usage issues and other
areas of concern that ultimately effect both hobbies, it is my
opinion that the two are distinct, and neither hobby is at the point
where it is ready to embrace the other and form one homogeneous
activity, particularly if you are a purist to either hobby.

SpringChick

The geoboxing prank raises a good point. On a corporate level, if
Groundspeak were to buy out LBNA and try to control letterboxing the
way they try to control geocaching, most people would find it
abominable. The Groundspeak site can get very political. You may
have noticed how unfriendly their forums can be at times. Yet many
geocachers have taken a step back from that and recognized that
geocaching.com is basically just a newspaper for advertizing geocaches
(and even absense-of-geocache "virtuals"). They have their rules to
define what constitutes a geocache, and even rules that define
"letterbox hybrids", a kind of cache that strips all the letterboxness
except the stamp out of letterboxing. Yet those rules exist mostly to
cover them from legal trouble.

So my hope is that letterboxers don't associate the picture of
geocaching painted by Groundspeak with the sport itself. There are
lots of geocaches that don't fit the mold set in place by the
corporates. My dad and I placed Stegabox One, which can be found with
or without a GPS, and with or without a stamp. Not to spoil anything,
but another stegabox is in the planning.

To emphasize the sameness of the two sports again, I understand that
back in Dartmoor, it was several years before stamping was part of
Letterboxing. I see the same thing happening with geocaching. First
people traded just curious items. Then you started to see more
signature cards, and now, a lot more stamping. And while there are
differences between the two sports.. chiefly that geocaching requires
the GPS receiver, the differences are trivial, and anyway, I think
strength is in diversity.

In reading this list, there have been several posts about the Parks &
Rec policy situation that I thought contained really good ideas. Yet
I wish that those ideas were in the Migo forums too, because then they
would more easily reach Sandrich, who already has a dialog going with
Parks & Rec about the treasurehunt policy.
