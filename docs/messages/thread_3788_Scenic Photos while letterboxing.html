From: Doodle & Deedle Bug (doodle_n_deedle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-02-27 06:35:42 UTC-08:00
A while back, SpringChick was looking for people to
email her scenic shots taken while letterboxing. I
meant to send her a shot or two that Deedle & I took
while on the trail, but with a wedding and moving and
general life never got around to it. Anyway, Deedle
decided to take some of these shots to put together a
photobook. We are amazed at how beautiful these shots
are, especially since they were taken with a regular
old Kodak digital camera.

If you'd like to view the photobook, go here:
http://share.shutterfly.com/action/welcome?sid=8IYs27dm0aucg
(tiny url http://tinyurl.com/ynwet5 if the above
link doesn't work). Click the View Photo Book button
at the bottom of the page, then the Zoom button on the
right of the page for best viewing. All but the last
4 pages were taken while letterboxing (or while
visiting the area to letterbox, as in the
Geneva-on-the-Lake photos). The photos on the last 4
pages are from our honeymoon.

Doodle Bug.

From: Scoutdogs (DogScouts@hotmail.com) | Date: 2007-02-27 13:52:13 UTC-05:00
Awesome! Well done! Letterboxing really does take you places that are really beautiful that you might not otherwise see.

I've been to some of the places in the Cleveland metro parks you photographed as well as the two Columbus Art locations. Nice memories. I used to hike the Brandywine Falls trail all the time. Used to be able to go down to the river level at the bottom of the falls. There's a tunnel for the river near there that is REALLY cool! I don't recall it's exact location though or which trail it's on. If the water's not running high, you can walk though the long tunnel, made long ago by the corps of engineers using bricks, and get to the other side to climb on large boulders and enjoy the peacefulness. The rough rock bottom of the tunnel was rarely dry and often moss covered, so careful stepping is highly recommended if you find it! But if I recall correctly, that was past a "no trespassing" sign I ignored. What can I say, I was young and stupid. Used to go there alone! Looking back, if I had fallen and cracked my head... no one would have found me.
