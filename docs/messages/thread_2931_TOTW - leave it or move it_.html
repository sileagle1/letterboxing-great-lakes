TOTW - leave it or move it?

From: azobox (jparkerg@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-08-10 14:09:39 UTC
Here's one to ponder:
Say you're searching for a box and find it, but not in the precise
spot that the clues say it should be, should you (1) leave it where
you found it (assuming it's concealed and appears to have been
intentionally placed vs. a critter dropped it there) OR (2) move it
to the location that (you think) the clues said it should be?

BTW, I don't think there is a hand-and-fast right answer to this one,
myself.

Just curious..

azobox

Re: [LbGLK] TOTW - leave it or move it?

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-08-10 11:02:29 UTC-04:00
If it appears that the box was carelessly left where it is -- i.e. not
covered well, not really even in a hiding place, etc. then I would suspect
that either an animal or non-boxer had been responsible for the positioning
and when finished I would put it back where the clues suggested (as best as
possible since it is not always easy to deduce the exact location).

If the box was hidden well, just not where the clues seemed to indicate it
should be, I would put it back where I got it from and cover it well.

In either case I would send a little note to the placer to let them know if
I had moved the box and/or it appeared to be in the wrong place. I think
that is the most important thing -- to communicate back to the placer so
they can head out to inspect for themselves or make a change to the clue,
etc.

SpringChick

From: Brad Maynes (Brad.Maynes@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-08-10 11:08:50 UTC-04:00
I think that I can safely speak for J and I this time. As a general matter,
we leave it where it was, unless, as SpringChick points out, it has
blatantly been moved by an animal/non-boxer, e.g. is out in the open. In
practice, we've found that a large number of clues are sufficiently vague as
to the final spot that we can't really justify moving the box to where we
think it should be.

From: ainawgsd (gmc81@randomkitty.net) | Date: 2006-08-10 19:34:56 UTC
It depends on the situation. Generally, assuming it's concealed and
appears to be intentionally placed, I would leave it where I found it.
As someone else has stated, clues are often very vague and the
location that *I* think the clues say it should be is not
automatically where the placer wants it to be. That said, if the
clues are very clear and obvious (IE the box is under a pink stone and
there's only one pink stone in the area), then I will move the box to
where I think it should be.

RE: [LbGLK] TOTW - leave it or move it?

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2006-08-10 21:38:30 UTC-05:00
We generally leave a box where we found it. We are of course sometimes
tempted to "relocate" it to where we THINK it should be, but since we don't
KNOW, we put it back where we found it and notify the placer. An example
was "The Old Well" which we found early last month. We almost gave up, but
widened our search considerably and did in fact find it. We had seen some
great hiding spots where we thought it should be, but when we found it, it
was in a more open area, "somewhat" covered by some loose grass & twigs. It
didn't seem like the greatest spot, but since it seems to have survived for
quite awhile, we left it where we found it. After contacting the placers to
let them know we found it, they were surprised, since they thought it was
missing. Apparently it WASN'T in the right spot anymore! However we never
presume to move someone else's box (rightly or wrongly). The only boxes we
move are our own when we are doing a maintenance check, if we find they have
"migrated" from their original location.

Wisconsin Hiker
