From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2007-03-06 04:51:59 UTC
Great to see you posting these clues on the LBNA site! It's the site
I most like to use for clues.

You don't need to be in a "competition" with another state to get a
nice feeling of satisfaction on getting boxes placed in another
county. We (the greater "we", not just Martini Man & I) were
diligently plugging away on Wisconsin and enjoyed seeing the map get
filled in. It wasn't until late in the game that Michigan challenged
us to the "friendly competition". Both states ended up winners by
getting boxes in all counties.

I must say the visual of a gradually filled in map is motivating, so
if Bindle Babe reads this, maybe she'd be willing to create and
maintain one for Indiana (hers were much more professional than my
hand-colored one, and since I'm out of town on business, my resources
& time are a bit more limited now). We can't expect the LBNA
webmasters to do frequent updates, so it is best to create one
ourselves if we want it to be "real time". Texas ended up doing the
same thing we did - people planting boxes in counties, notifying the
group and someone updating and posting the state county map.

I'll send you a potential train robber graphic separately. Might not
be suitable for a tiny stamp though...

Wisconsin Hiker
