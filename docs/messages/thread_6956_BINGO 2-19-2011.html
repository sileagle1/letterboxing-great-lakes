While Interlochen doesn't help us out for Special Springchick Saturday, it is special for a different reason! Interlochen was the very first letterbox that Team Rabbit ever found....all those years ago...in the pouring rain....where we figured that we would just naturally, instinctively know how to use a compass when we got out in the woods, even though neither of us had ever used one before....and where we quickly discovered that we were completely wrong about that! lol And somehow, (AKA-after searching many random stumps in the general area) we still managed to find the box (Yes, those crazy clues that we found online actually led to real "treasure"! There was really something there!) ahhhh....the memories! And that was the first of many, many, fun, awesome letterboxing adventures for Team Rabbit!!

Anyone else want to share the story of finding their first letterbox??

Gladly. Mine was The Roadside Table and we knew exactly where it was and like you we also read the compass wrong. So, we kept going back. I remember going there in the snow and the entire steps up were ice. I had a bad knee at the time and fell down the edge of the hill and almost landed in the creek below. After pulling myself up, we found the box. NOPE! it was a geocache within 10 ft, so we had to go back. I asked Springchick for a clue and in typical fashion she did not respond. I came to learn that she took this madness seriously and am grateful she didn't give me a hint as it was so fun finding it. (Thanks Deb...I cursed you for months. Just kidding...don't come after me a scary monster cake, OK?) However, that was not actually the first box we actually found. We found Thumper days before getting the Roadeside Table. Funny story...we were standing right on top of Sprinchick's box the entire time. testament to a good hide! BTW...the geocache nearby was a good one (you didn't hear me say that). Both boxes are now missing. :(

Oh...and by the way, Mrs. Familyof6 is playing the 50 BOS game and had a great day with Interlochen today. Anyone else have luck?
