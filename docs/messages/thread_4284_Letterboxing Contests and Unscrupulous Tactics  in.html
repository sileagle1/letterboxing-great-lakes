I have to second WH on this. We love Letterboxing and take our
children along with us. That said, they understand the rules and we
remind them frequently of stealth, etc.(I have one that loves to
forget - sigh. Getting the 6 yr old to be quiet in the woods is a
true challenge - and he wonders why we do not see many
critters....). If a hiding place is easy to get to and no one is
around, we will let the children "re-hide" the box and we ALWAYS
double check them on it. I know the oldest one gets it (she is a
better rehider than I am at times - helps to be closer to the ground
at times, I guess, LOL!). The other one, well, he is 6 and is
learning. Therefore, we are extra diligent with him. We do all this
simply for the fact that we do NOT want to ruin it for others.

However, when dh and I get the chance, we do go without them. It
only happens a couple of times a year (like this last Sat.), but we
take advantage of that time. We go for the more difficult ones then.

Anyway, this is sort of a rehash of prior thoughts on Letterboxing
with children.
