Reminder about trail discretion

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2012-06-04 15:11:15 UTC
I received an email this morning from a Nature Center telling me to remove my listing for a letterbox I have planted there. The message included this:

"On Sunday June 3, I found a family on Phantom Prairie trail attempting to walk off trail into the brush. They handed me a piece of paper titled 100 Ways for Barbie to Die: Inattentive Flying."

The family mentioned probably isn't anyone on this message board, but perhaps we all need to put reminders in our clues that potential searchers need to be more discreet and not divulge what they are doing, so as to protect the secrecy of our boxes.

Wisconsin Hiker

By the way, I did reply to the Nature Center that I was sorry to hear that
they did not support letterboxing and that the foot traffic was much less
than the trails made by the deer population. I also sent them this:



========================



You may be interested in looking into how some other states are supporting
and encouraging letterboxing. We have enjoyed this hobby for almost 9 years
and have boxed in Connecticut and New Hampshire (as well as 40 other states
so far). We found 10 of the boxes in the New Hampshire preserves last year
and will be returning to search for the other two this fall.



Connecticut:

http://www.ct.gov/dep/cwp/view.asp?a=2697

&q=322826&depNav_GID=1631



New Hampshire:

http://www.nature.org/ourinitiatives/regions/northamerica/unitedstates/newha
mpshire/events/50th-anniversary-preserve-letterboxing-challenge.xml





It is unfortunate that two of our neighboring states (Illinois and
Minnesota) work so hard at eliminating this healthy, creative and fun
activity.



Debbie

===================



Not sure I'll convince them, but I at least want to educate them that not
ALL natural areas have the same opinion that they do!

Re: Reminder about trail discretion

From: Mary (drewes.mary@gmail.com) | Date: 2012-06-04 19:32:10 UTC

My "crazy excuse" today was assumed by the park guy to be wondering what had happened in the area of the 4 Queens LB in Jefferson County - um, yeah, sure. Turns out they are clearing and restoring the area around some Native American mounds in the preserve. The LB was in a stump ADJACENT to one of these mounds (that I'd previously been unaware of). The LB was actually in a geocache that took over the hiding spot - the cache contents were (wet) toast, but the logbook and stamp were fine, much to my shock. My many thanks to Wisconsin Hiker and Martini Man for telling me about the changes in the preserve! I do intend to replant in another area of the preserve away from the mounds - and I do intend to check on another LB there when I go back. I pretty much grabbed the stamp and logbook, repacked the cache and left since the park guy probably saw the box contents I'd been looking at.

I appreciate when we watch out for each other's boxes, do maintenance and send status updates!

- Sunny
