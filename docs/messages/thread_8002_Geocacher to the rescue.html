Geocacher to the rescue

From: Atom118 (Aatom118@gmail.com) | Date: 2014-09-15 09:28:44 UTC-05:00
I've heard some bad things about geocachers on this board, but I've not
really ever had a run in with one. In case they are getting a short
straw, I'd like to report about a geocacher to the rescue.

I had recently gotten a sad report about a box of mine being missing.
This was a box Aimlesst planted for us for Boxacon in 2009. It was one
of my more favorite stamps, 1984, so I was sad to hear that it was
likely gone. However, last week I got an email (via LbNA via Wi Hiker)
saying that it had found open along the trail. We happened to be in the
Milwaukee area this weekend, so I was able to meet up and get my box.
It turned out that my 'hero' was a geocacher who had been out checking
on his own boxes when he found mine. He said he didn't want (in his
words) "A Muggle to find it and throw it out" so he grabbed it so he
could help get it back to where it needed to be. The stamp is in good
shape (the logbook is not), and I'm happy to have it back in my
possession and thankful to Ranger Boy.

Atom118 of the Atomic Beans
