"Project Pajama Pants" to be available at GLG

From: Kathi Krankoski (krank@ameritech.net) | Date: 2009-05-25 16:40:30 UTC-07:00
I know this is kind of late notice, but I found this letterbox (created for the WI-10 gathering last year) amongst my collection of stuff here at home and decided to bring it along to the GLG. However instead of pajama pants, to receive this stamp, please bring a box of cold drink mix packets for bottled water (such as Propel, Gatorade and Crystal Lite To Go), as requested on the Operation Support Our Troops - Illinois website. Please do not bring any out-of-date or soon-to-be-expiring products.

I AM NOT COLLECTING PAJAMA PANTS OR SUNDAY COMICS, SO DISREGARD THAT PART OF THE MESSAGEIN THEAQ CLUES.

Letters to the troops are still welcome.

See http://www.atlasquest.com/showinfo.html?gBoxId=91695for more information.

From: Barb Funk (barbfunk3@gmail.com) | Date: 2009-05-25 21:23:37 UTC-04:00
If I might make a suggestion---during my son's most recent deployment, I
collected and sent probably close to 2500 of those little drink mix
packets during their 16 month deployment. I got tons of feedback even
after he and his soldiers got back to American soil.

Bottom line---if you have a choice of what brand to buy---the WalMart
brand is by far their drink of choice----plus it costs less than the
other brands so your money goes farther. No matter who I sent these
packets to---most of them preferred the ones closest to orange juice
tasting flavor---but they will take anything, obviously.

Buying these for our combat soldiers is WELL worth the time and money
you spend. Even the drinking water gets SO HOT that they have to figure
out ways to try to cool it down so they can even drink it. So these
drink mixes go a really long way toward making drinking water non-stop
far more bearable. (Those in my son's platoon would soak one of their
Army t-shirts and hang it on the end of their Howitzer keeping their
drinking water supply down wind so that the evaporation process from the
t-shirt would cool the air 10-15 degrees and perhaps drop the temp of
the drinking water to about 90 degrees.

I can also tell you that this is probably the single most wanted item of
all!!!

Mega hugs and thanks for doing this klk/fw and all who contribute!!! It
DOES make a difference to our soldiers!!

.....going back to lurking again! =D
