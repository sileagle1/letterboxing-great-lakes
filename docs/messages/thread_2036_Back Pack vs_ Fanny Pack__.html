Back Pack vs. Fanny Pack??

From: cdem_ltrbox (wp4@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2005-11-21 14:16:20 UTC
For Christmas, I wanted to get the kids their own packs for carrying
their stamps and logbooks and snacks and whatever else is currently
being lugged about by me in my backpack. I'm going to sew those
great "Great Lakes" letterboxing patches on them. I can't decide if
it would be better to have a backpack or a fannypack for them. Which
do you all prefer and why? I would appreciate any feedback. Thanks.

BirdWoman of Waltzing Pigs




Re: [LbGLK] Back Pack vs. Fanny Pack??

From: AJlb (ajlikesbikes@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-11-21 06:36:39 UTC-08:00
this winter i am designing my own letterboxing pack. it will be more of a messenger bag, though. i am placing all my gear on the fabric as to where the pockets should be for everything that i want included.
currently i am using a backpack. i like the size because you can fit trash, souveniers, etc. in them, as well as my lb gear. however, depending on the age of the kids, maybe a fanny pack would be less bulky/heavy for them.

From: beautifulpeople99 (jennifer.mccready@gmail.com) | Date: 2005-11-21 14:48:25 UTC
We actually use a teeny-tiny backpack that really can only fit the
essentials (i.e., our logbook, markers, compass, camera, and one bottle
of water). We switched to this after J decided that B's hiking backpack
was waaaayyy too heavy for normal letterboxing! I am very happy with
the new one; it is canvas, has one pocket, and is so light that I
hardly ever notice it while hiking.

J of J&BBooks

Re: [LbGLK] Back Pack vs. Fanny Pack??

From: Doglvrs RASW (doglvrs_4@msn.com) | Date: 2005-11-21 15:00:47 UTC
I have LBing kids - 8 & 11.
They have fannypacks with 2 bottle holders (we got them at Walmart, and it
came with 2 bottles)
These work perfectly. My 11 yo daughter keeps here's all neat and tidy and
is also the official "first aid box carrier" for the family.
my 8 yo son has been forbidden from putting all his "treasures" in the main
part of the pack, where his stamp and logbook are...so he brings only 1
water bottle w/him on the trail and uses the other bottle pocket for his
treasures.

These have worked best for us, because the pack is easily accessable - and
doesn't promote the stopping and standing a backpack does.

Just my opinion, for what it's worth.

From: Charles Zamzow (cerealcityboxers@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-11-21 09:35:29 UTC-08:00
We usually use a small backpack, for letterboxing supplies, birdwatching supplies, trail snacks and water. However, when we are urban letterboxing, we simply place the letterboxing supplies in a fanny pack and go. The nature of urban letterboxing provides plenty of snack stops and watering holes. We have no experience letterboxing with children, so we can not address that issue.
