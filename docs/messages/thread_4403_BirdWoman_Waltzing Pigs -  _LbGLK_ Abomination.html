Gosh Carol, lighten up! Sounds like a gasket is about blow. Hey, how about that Heather Mills? CHA CHING!!! LOL That was practically an assassination too!
Personally, I am over cemetery letterboxes. We have an abuse problem in my area
with a significant number of boxes being planted in cemeteries (planters, learn how to spell the word! If you can't spell cemetery, you shouldn't be planting boxes there). Often in my area, the stamp does not correspond to anyone or anything in the cemetery, what was the point of the exercise then? Hunting in the cemetery also has a sense of morbidness and infringment of personal space running all over a place of peace. I have encountered grieving visitors at graves close to the letterbox locations, how nice of me to be rummaging in the bushes as you're wailing away over there! There are plenty of places to plant boxes, planters just need to get creative. To this on going store bought or hand carved debate- There really is no excuse to ever use a store bought stamp. To carve a stamp is neither difficult nor expensive to produce and like anything, requires some practice to master-it is not rocket science people! It requires patience but minimal artistic ability, if
you can trace a line, you can carve a stamp. Print an image, transfer it to the carving medium, then trace the lines and remove the unwanted parts-come on, get with it, make the effort and quit whinning! Like anything, you can do anything you put your mind to so use it and excercise it! You might surprise yourself at your attempts. TRY!!!
