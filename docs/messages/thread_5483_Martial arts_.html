Ah, yes. Has nothing to do with our trail names, but we did both do Tae Kwon Do for a while. Flying dragon did it for about two or three years, made it all the way to blue belt before recurring injuries caused him to quit. I made it to 1st gup (just shy of black belt...after first gup is cho dan bo "provisional black belt" and then first degree black belt) but now my schedule makes it hard to attend classes and I've kind of lost my motivation. I also did taek-gyeon, which is an older Korean martial art that is more flowing and is said to be one of the foundation martial arts for tae kwon do. I'll probably go back to it sooner or later though as it was a lot of fun.
