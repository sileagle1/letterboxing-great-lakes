Great day squishing pennies

From: (duncan53211@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-10-28 23:37:56 UTC

OH! It feels so good to be out and about boxin' again. I took some
time around the teacher conferences today to go check up on my
Creative Writing, Watery Grave, and Squished Pennies boxes. All are
in good shape and the last two now have camoflauge tape on them.
There seems to be alot of new letterboxers in WI. I hope they can
make it to the gathering!

For you train lovers out there, when I went to check on the Squished
pennies box, I found there weren't many left, so I put 10 pennies on
the track closest to the box and waited. To my excitement I heard a
train approaching soon and looked forward to many squished pennies.
But NO! It was on the wrong side - on the other track! Argh! So
then I had to leave and go back to the conf. I returned a couple of
hours later and found all ten pennies still sitting on the track
unsquished! I couldn't believe it! So, I took out 10 more pennies
and put them on the other track, thinking I got to win one way or
another. Then I sat back and rested on the rocks over the box and
waited for one of the many trains that travel through to squish my
pennies. I waited for 30 minutes, then decided to stroll through the
woods some, since no train had come. After waiting an hour, I was
just about to give up, when finally a train came on the far track! It
was Amtrak passing through going to MN. The train quickly passed and
I jumped out and started looking for my pennies. Only one was
squished! The others had just fallen off before being squished! Then
as I'm collecting my unsquished pennies, I hear another train coming
on the same track - just behind the Amtrak! So I quickly replaced my
unsquished pennies and went back to my observation spot. I was having
so much fun watching the second train pass that I suddenly jumped out
of my shoes when another train came roaring through from the other
direction on the closer track! Never heard it coming. "Woohoo!!" I
thought, "I've got me 20 squished pennies in one sitting!" After they
passed, I spent a few minutes searching for the pennies and had only
had a chance to find a few when two more trains came through - just
minutes after the other two! Unfortunately, one of the trains stopped
to layover and prevented me from going back to the letterbox directly.
So I had to walk west down the track to the end of the train to get
around it. On the way, yet another train came by! Finally I was able
to get back to the box and place the 8 new squished pennies into the
box. The other 12 must've went to "Squished Penny Heaven". (There
MUST be a place - right Czeltic Girl?) Anyway, as I was heading home,
yet another train came through minutes after I left. So after waiting
an hour with no trains, I get 7 within 40 minutes! WEEEEEEEE!
