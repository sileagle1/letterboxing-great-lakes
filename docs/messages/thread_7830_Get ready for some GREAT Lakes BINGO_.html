Get ready for some GREAT Lakes BINGO!

From: (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2013-11-24 20:08:32 UTC-08:00

Yep, it's that time of year - time to start plotting your winning Bingo strategy!

 

I hope you all are looking forward to a nice Thanksgiving holiday with family and friends. Martini Man & I have much to be thankful for and one of those things is knowing so many fun letterboxers in the Great Lakes region!

As most of you know, part of our winter fun is playing the Great Lakes Letterboxing BINGO game started by SpringChick back in 2006. The illustrious Mr. Familyof6 then took the reins for two years and now I'm honored to be hosting the BINGO game for a second time. We're hoping to have a lot of participants for the 2014 game, so spread the word. You sure wouldn't want to miss experiencing the slow motion thrill of victory and agony of defeat that this game is famous for!

The necessary files are ready for your use in the "Files" section of the message board (just scroll down to the "Great Lakes Letterbox BINGO! folder, click on it, then click on the 2014 BINGO Game folder).

General instructions are in the Bingo 2014 FAQ.pdf file, the terms to fill your card are in the BINGO 2014 Word List.pdf file and the blank card is found as two formats: BINGO GRID 2014.doc and BINGO GRID 2014.pdf.  

 

You may wish to use the MS Word version to type (or cut & paste) your terms directly into the grid. You can then save a copy and send it as an attachment in an email to me.

 

Then, make sure you mail a booty prize and $2 to my address (see FAQ file) by Decmber 31 OR.... if you are attending the Dead of Winter event on January 25, you can give me your booty and $2 then if you want to save the mailing costs. However you still need to get your BINGO card to me by the deadline of DECEMBER 31 because the game starts on January 1, 2014.

I wanted to get the files posted early enough so you could still get out to find some of the boxes on the Word List, if you haven't already found them. It will give you a few more choices for filling your grid and, more importantly, you'll get to find more great letterboxes! I'd much rather be out letterboxing this weekend than venturing to a store filled with those crazed "Black Friday" shoppers!

If you have any questions, just shoot me an email, or post the question to this board.

Wisconsin Hiker

P.S. Martini Man didn't want to be deprived of playing, so he has not been involved in any planning for the game and won't be involved in any aspects of pulling words, etc.  He is hoping he does better than last year!
