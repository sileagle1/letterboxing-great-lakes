Re: [LbGLK] Letterboxing Contest

From: baliddle (baliddle@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2007-06-20 19:46:08 UTC-04:00
At 06:27 PM 6/20/2007, you wrote:
>I have a dilemma. I recently found out that a group from a nearby town
>is having a letterboxing contest. I have a feeling that my recently
>planted boxes are being found by whole groups (quite large) and at
>least one box location has been compromised. Any suggestions?

I would pull the clues and/or actual boxes for a while.

If you can figure out who the organizer of this event is, I'd contact
them and explain some of the stealth/secrecy issues involved and
point out that this isn't a great way to treat other people's
boxes. If they want to have this type of contest with boxes they
planted, go for it. But they shouldn't be doing it with others boxes
without consent.

Also, I would post a note to this board (or whatever local board
applies) to notify other planters in your area that this is going on
and their boxes may be in danger.

Bali

Re: Letterboxing Contest

From: vanman021657 (fholbein@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-06-21 13:43:57 UTC
While pulling your boxes and or clues resolves the immediate issue,
are you really sure that there is an issue at all?

I.E.

-- Is the organizer a LB'er and doing this to bring non-LB'ers into
the fold?

-- Are all of the participants already LB'ers? If this is the case,
then they are already familiar with the stealthy and secretive nature
and you should lend them credit for complying with the rules of our
hobby.

I don't see where this is any different than a gathering. In a
gathering, all the permanant traditional boxes are at risk of large
groups searching for them at the same time. But I've attended 3 mini
meets and 1 very large gathering and found that most folks head out
and very seldom do they all go for the same thing at the same time.
Everyone has their own method for this hide and seek game we play.

Perhaps you should contact the coordinator of this event and see how
they are handling this. I'm sorry, but I see where there is panic
here instead of welcoming additional trail stamps into your log books.

Just my take on the matter,

VanMan

From: Silent Doug (silentdoug@letterboxing.info) | Date: 2007-06-29 22:18:49 UTC-04:00
I agree with azobox.

I once pulled the clue to one of my boxes in the past when it's
lifespan was threatened by a curious newspaper reporter who wanted to
do a story on letterboxing. Since my box was only one of a few in the
area, and since the area was far from where I live, making
maintenance difficult, I wasn't at all happy at the prospect of
dozens of people trying out the hobby for the first time on my box.

A couple of months later, when I was sure that the proposed article
wouldn't be materializing, I restored the clue.

SD
