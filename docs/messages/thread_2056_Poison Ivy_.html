The great thing about poison ivy is that it is so wonderfully
versitile! It can be a bush or ivy, stand alone or have a host and
take several different forms. What also makes it great is that the
leaves have many different shapes and colors, which makes it SOOOO
easy to identify! What is probably the absolute best thing about
poison ivy is that the chemical that causes the rash is found on all
parts of the plant, not just the leaves so even touching the stems
or "trunk" in the winter will give you a rash (don't ask how I
know). I LOVE this wonderful plant!! (Hmmm... could this be
sarcasm?) It gets even better if someone were to burn it with other
brush and leaves... how do you scratch an itch in your throat and
lungs??

Here is a site with many pictures of poison ivy, oak and sumac...

http://poisonivy.aesir.com/view/pictures.html

as a bonus they have rash pictures as well.
