Close Encounters of the Letterboxing Kind...

From: Armadillo JMO (jes7o@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-08-06 18:19:33 UTC-07:00
Yesterday, I was fortunate to have the afternoon off
of work. I utilized my time by going on yet another
letterboxing trip (this hobby is ADDICTING!) Headed up
to the Indiana/Michigan state line at Hesston to go
after the Hard Scrabble box. As I was pulling out of
the deserted Hesston Steam Museum post-stamping, a
minivan with IL plates pulled in the drive way, headed
for the general direction of the letterbox. I didn't
stick around to find out if they were letterboxers or
just vacationing IL people. Then I headed to Warren
Woods State Preserve near Three Oaks, MI. This really
shouldn't have been a challenging box, but it appears
that a bench threw me completely off course. The clue
states that the bench is before a particular landmark.
So I pass the bench and come to a spot where 2 VERY
large trees blocked the path. I'm looking EVERYWHERE
for this landmark. I go around the trees...nothing. I
start to look at every cotton pickin' beech tree I can
see from the trail. Nuttin. So after about an hour of
nuttin....I head back to the car. I get to the spot
where the bench is, and began to look at EVERY tree on
the left for the landmark. A tree BEFORE the bench had
it, and the Walking Tree was (very)obvious from this
point forward. However, as I was discovering the
location of the walking tree, a family came up the
trail and were rooting around. They didn't have any
letterboxing equipment with them, but they weren't
moving too fast. I just nonchalantly walked off in the
general direction of the box and had a few grand
moments with the Walking Tree. My, is it ever
breathtaking!
In a few weeks I'm planning on heading to the
Cleveland area to visit relatives and of course, do
some letterboxing. Can't wait!

Armadillo JMO
