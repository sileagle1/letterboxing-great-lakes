Tiptoe, being the art teacher that she is, was lost in cyberspace in
October of 2002 looking for new carving materials...she stumbled
upon the LBNA website. Tonto at the time was in his usual trance
position in front of the TV watching some sporting event. He heard
her scream in delight, "You aren't going to believe this!"
He answered with a disinterested, "What is it now?"
"There are actually people writing clues and hiding hand carved
stamps out in public areas ALL over the country!"
A polite, "Oh, yeah?" was all he could muster at the time. He went
back to his TV and she memorized the LBNA website!

We didn't get around to the actual letterboxing until the following
February (2003) when we were heading out to visit family in
Virginia. We stopped for the South Mountain Trinity boxes in
Burkittsville, MD on the AT,(Yes, the same area that the Blair Witch
Project Movie of yore was filmed) The boxes had been planted by the
Apple Dumpling Gang. Our first box was the "War Correspondence Box"
found in the snow! The carving blew us away, and Tonto being a
history buff was equally hooked by the location that we would've
never found otherwise!

Eleven states, several years later and with over 400 boxes under our
belts, we are still loving the adventures that the boxes bring our
way.
Tiptoe & Tonto
