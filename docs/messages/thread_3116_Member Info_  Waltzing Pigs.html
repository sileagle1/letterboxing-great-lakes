Hi,

Our family trail name is the Waltzing Pigs; individually we are
BirdWoman (I enjoy bird watchingwhich is a great cover when out in
the woods), Money Man (my husband's job), Do-Re-Mi Gal (my daughter
the violinist), and Ball Magnet (my son's nickname one baseball
season when he kept getting beaned by the ball). We chose Waltzing
Pigs as family name because it is a play on our last name andat the
time we chose the namewe had a guinea pig who was part of the
family and that was our way of including him/her (we never did
figure the gender out).

We started letterboxing in the summer of 2004. We read an article
about letterboxing in Ranger Ricka kid's nature magazineand
thought it sounded really fun. Being a borderline obsessive-
compulsive person who has to do things the "right way" (I think that
comes with the territory of being a critical-care nurse), I went
directly to the Web site to see how things were done. We used an
exacto-knife to carve some pretty crude stamps out of white erasers
and stapled some paper together to make our first journals. And
then the kids and I were off to search for our first letterbox one
fine summer day. We had a great time looking for Cars on a Stick,
even though we never actually found the box. Time went by and we
decided to try another boxSUCCESS! Our first find
was "Architectural Mystery". Then we spent a nice fall afternoon
hunting down a bunch of boxes by FungusWoman (and finding a
hitchhiker) and we were hooked. OK, I am more hooked than the rest
of them but we do all enjoy getting out together and seeing places
we never would have thought to go toand finding stamps to boot.

The kids and I have found a little over 200 boxesMoney Man lags a
little behind because he has to work and make the money while we go
on our little outings. We started placing boxes about a year ago
and have placed around 15 boxes. We've enjoyed meeting fellow
boxers on the trails and at the 2 gatherings we have attended. It's
been fun seeing new and unfamiliar stamps in logbooks as more people
take up this hobby/obsessionhope to meet more of you out there.

BirdWoman of Waltzing Pigs
