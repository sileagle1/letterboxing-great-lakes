So, any suggestions of what to do when reaching an LB milestone, or any
stories of your milestones?

I am coming up on 100 finds, most of them coming in the last 6 weeks -
and NO, I am not obsessed, thank you very much. I am a little
disappointed that I don't remember exactly how I heard about LBing,
when I started (?August 2007) or what box I found first - especially
odd as I scrapbook and like to be organized. I'm thinking of making
#100 be one from the State Park where I think I first went LBing, or
have it be one of the WONDERFUL Great Circus Train Stamps, or some
other box - or just let it happen. I know I cannot wait until the Oct.
4 LbGLK Gathering and celebration of the 10th anniversary of LBing in
WI - really hoping to go to that!. I know I will order an AQ mug
(traveller clue!), and maybe some LBNA/AQ/100F patches.

From: Kathleen Brade (brade@comcast.net) | Date: 2008-08-17 12:25:19 UTC-04:00
Wow! Congratulations WH and MM!

I love the idea of leaving behind a hitchhiker in the box. We may have
to do that but we'd have to do it every 150 or 200 boxes since we're
already beyond 125. Or maybe we'll celebrate by planting regular
boxes. I had considered a patch that shows the find count but it
changes often enough that I decided I didn't want to spend time sewing
on patches that few people besides me ever see.

We did pick a particular box to visit when we were going to hit 100 but
it wasn't a huge decision. If we had been in a different area, we
wouldn't have stopped letterboxing to wait for that particular
letterbox. We also picked a particular box (one of Spring Chick's
mystery boxes) for #101. Other than those two, we haven't concerned
ourselves with which box we found and what F number a box is.

I have considered creating a special stamp for our find count and then
using the special stamp in conjunction with our personal stamps in the
logbook being visited as well as our own books but I haven't done that
yet... (I think it was someone on this list who gave me the idea, when
he or she suggested a special "first finder" stamp.)

-- MichKathy
