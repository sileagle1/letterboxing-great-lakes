Wow! is right! We figured we'd see 3-4 other people for lunch on Wednesday
and were therefore VERY surprised at the big turnout. We had a great time
chatting with folks we had met before and meeting some others we only "knew"
from posts or seeing their stamps in logbooks. As usual, there were a
flurry of exchanges which we managed to fit in around the food. Thanks to
FungusWoman for initiating this gathering and for posting some photos so
quickly!

We spent the last 3 days of 2005 boxing in Northern IL and still have a LOT
to come back for on future trips. We didn't realize that when we "headed
south" for part of our holiday week that we would be leaving the snow
behind. Yes, although WI still had (and HAS) snow on the ground, only a 90
minute trip to the south left it behind. It made it much easier to find
those "faint trails" mentioned in the clues. Unfortunately it wasn't
actually WARM, but I guess upper 30s is OK for December. It sure is a lot
less crowded on the trails in the winter, so it is easier to actually
retrieve the boxes without being seen. It is also interesting to see the
lakes/rivers/ponds with various ice formations. One looked like it had "ice
bubbles" erupting from the center and it was odd to cross a long bridge over
ice.

We spent Thursday in the Brookfield area at the impromptu gathering and also
finding several FungusWoman boxes (really liked the Chicago Portage
sculpture) and the interesting "Christmas Around the World Series" by the
Waltzing Pigs. Friday we were a bit further west and north. We especially
enjoyed the hike at "Poo Shoe" and the funny stamp. However our boxing on
Friday was cut short in mid-afternoon by sleet which quickly turned to rain.
We checked into our hotel and read instead. Luckily this morning it was dry
and we were out on the hunt early in the day. We can't believe how many
nice forest preserve areas there are in Illinois - lots of great spots
tucked in the residential communities. Our longest trek was when we went
"fishing" for those colorful "Fish of Belize". My toes were starting to get
a bit cold on that one, even WITH my liner socks on. We had a lucky last
day of the year - we found every box we looked for! (For a short while we
thought "Baby Boy" had been kidnapped, but luckily we found him safe & sound
after a bit more looking.)

Happy New Year to all of you and we're looking forward to more letterboxing
fun in 2006!

Wisconsin Hiker & Martini Man
