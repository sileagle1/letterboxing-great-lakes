Hello All,
Deb, I'm glad you began the TOTW here.
First off, I'm a packrat and I'm afraid it's a trait that has
followed me into letterboxing. SO the pack continues to grow and
grow...I've disovered that it's now HEAVY. Yipes.
Okay, quick inventory..
Stamp and several ink pads are bundled with compass, paper, pens,
baggies of several sizes, a spare logbook, wad of paper towels,
alcohol wipes (for our hands as much as anything else), a few snacks
(of course!)into a small over-the-shoulder or around-the-waist bag.
That goes wherever we go and might conceiveably find a letterbox OR
ANOTHER LETTERBOXER. Nothing worse than having that on-the-trail
encounter and not having your stamp!(It happened to me while I was
checking boxes last summer.)
If we're going to be out for awhile, that smaller bag is supplemented
with a backpack. Clipboard, groundcloth, matches, maps, flashlight,
simple first-aid stuff, spaceblanket, knife,(most of the "Ten
Essentials" for you serious hikers), and food and water. Oh, and a
cellphone.
Hey, I said I was compulsive.

A number of the items I've listed I've learned from other
letterboxers.

Looking forward to hearing others' responses.

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2004-03-30 23:13:03 UTC-06:00
Our pack is a "mini" backpack, purchased from a clearance rack at an office
supply store. It isn't as big as our regular daypacks we use for hiking,
but is roomy enough to hold the necessary items. We carry most of the items
mentioned by everyone else:

journals, stamps, clues, ink pads (and now lots of LePlume markers!), wipes,
a pen, compass, gloves, extra baggies in various sizes, a Swiss army knife
and a mini "leatherman" tool, a nice masonite board to act as a lapdesk
(gift from Merlin!).

Some other things we carry are an extra logbook so we can replace one if we
find a box with a damaged, missing, or full logbook. (We found one once,
but this was before we thought about bringing a spare along.) We also have
some duct tape rolled around a pencil. Duct tape can be used to patch a
hole and could be handy for other things.

We have several "first finder" key charms on the zipper (from Agent Ink &
Z-fan boxes) and the LBNA patch on the outside of the pack.

In the winter we use additional packs we received as gifts from Martini
Man's parents. These packs are actually a combination of pack & campseat.
Our regular LB pack can fit inside this pack. Having the campseats is
really handy in the winter when there might not be a good place to sit or
temporarily place items. Being able to sit down to take care of stamping in
(aided by the lapboard) is great. The pack pouch is then under the seat, so
it is convenient to place the box lid and other items as you go about the
business of stamping logbook, journals, etc.

I've put a photo of our pack in the Photo section of the LbGLK group. It
shows many of the items we always have in our pack.

Wisconsin Hiker

Since we are all making an effort to get out and do maintenance on
our boxes, and no doubt, scouting out new locations in the process,
I thought a topic pertinent to that was in order...

What do you look for when finding new places for boxes? What are
the qualities about a spot that make you say, "this is the spot?"
Do you have potential clue elements in mind as you look? Do you
tend to favor trails in the woods over urban locations or vice
versa? Do you seek out a specific type of hiding spot? I am
interested in both the big picture -- surrounding area, down to the
specific qualities that you favor in a hiding spot.

From: Leapin' Lizards (emptyenergy1@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-04-05 21:31:55 UTC-07:00
>
> What do you look for when finding new places for
> boxes?
We look for places that we would like to visit with
our friends and families. The places doesn't always
have to be a place of pure beauty. Just interesting.
Lately, we have been looking to place boxes in large
series... We have several that have been placed or are
just about ready to be placed. The series are either
city wide, state wide, or nation wide such as our
Route 66 series.

What are
> the qualities about a spot that make you say, "this
> is the spot?"
As far as specific box placement, we look for areas
that will not hold water...and are easy to camoflage.
Having a view of what you brought the finders there to
see is cool too...(when it happens):)

Do you have potential clue elements in mind as you
> look?

Sometimes things just click...one future series is
going to require quite a bit of knowledge of the topic
of the series. Sometimes Sara gets an idea for a
story. She loves Spring Chick's stories.:)

Do you
> tend to favor trails in the woods over urban
> locations or vice
> versa?
Woods...we never really feel comfortable finding boxes
in the city...too hard to look like you belong there.

Do you seek out a specific type of hiding
> spot?
Any spot that is dry, fairly difficult for an animal
to remove the box, and that allows the clues to work.

Leapin' Lizards

Based on what I am hearing, perhaps a routine TOTW is not the best
route to go given the mix of old and new members and that so many
topics have already been discussed.

What I would like to propose is an impromptu TOTW. Anyone who has a
topic that they think would make a good discussion topic for the
list, please post prefacing your subject with TOTW.

A few ground rules:

1) Anyone can post a TOTW.
2) When posting a new topic, please begin your subject line
with "TOTW." When replying to a TOTW, please reply to the original
post and keep the subject line as it was.
3) Remember to delete parts of the message you are replying to that
are not pertinent to your post so that they do not get so long for
those receiving the digest.
4) If there is an active TOTW, please wait to post yours -- by
active TOTW ,I mean something that was posted within the past 3-4
days and/or is still receiving input and discussion
5) If you were a part of a previous discussion on this topic, please
do not post "we already discussed this" as the poster may be new or
may be interested in feedback from folks who were not around during
the previous discussion. If you have participated in a disussion on
the topic at hand and don't care to share your response again, don't
say anything. If you want to post a message number as to where the
previous thread can be found as additional input, that is fine.
6) If you have something to add to the discussion, please feel free
to post, but try not to post just saying, "yeh, me too." We want to
hear your perspective.
7) Keep in mind that by nature a TOTW solicits opinions and they may
or may not agree with yours, however each person's opinion should be
respected.

Good? I will post a starter TOTW in the next message... and after
that, it is an open forum.

SpringChick
