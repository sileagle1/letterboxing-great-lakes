A Cemetery Box

From: Familyof6 (familyof6portland@yahoo.com) | Date: 2009-04-07 11:14:41 UTC
I have a pretty awesome letterbox story that involves checking on some of my boxes. While I was driving out past a certain unnamed pioneer cemetery in Ionia County (has to be unnamed since my box is a mystery)to check the status of some boxes of mine. I noticed that the large tree where my box was stashed was GONE and that the headstones dating from the 1840's to 1880's were being restored. I w3as thinking that my intricate stamp was missing but decided to check out the stones anyhow (because I love this location).

Anyhow, I noticed that behind two headstones that were being restored and propped up against another were concealing my box...intact and with a note and phone number to call. Well, I called and come to find out that the woman was an ancestor of the man that I carved the stamp about (The Lord's Anchor box...in case you're wondering).

To make a long story short, she was delighted about the box and we chatted a bit about letterboxing and the history of this cemetery (again, I can't tell you the name or location or it may give the box away). By the end of the conversation, she asked if I would replace the box. I said yes. And....4 things came of it:

1. My box, book, and stamp were safe.
2. I had the opportunity to talk to great, great grand-daughter of the ancestor...
3. ...and the opportunity to help restore the cemetery....
4... and I get to replace the box in the cemetery!

Now, how awesome is that?
