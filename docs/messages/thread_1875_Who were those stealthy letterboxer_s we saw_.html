Who were those stealthy letterboxer's we saw?

From: Melisa Hills (mshills1@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2005-10-08 16:00:04 UTC-05:00
We were out working on the Circus today here in Menomonee Falls, WI and we
heard, ahead of us, another family (we heard the cry of someone saying "I
Found It" when we were stamping into one - we never did catch up to them,
but their stamp was a book & below was MKE BC and it was signed Aimlesst.

Then, when we realized the time (about 3pm), we were headed back towards
the parking area and we came across a couple. The woman was wearing a Red
Fleece pullover and I think either she or the man with her had the MSOE
logo on what they were wearing. We saw the gentleman slyly moving his
letterboxing equipment around him as we passed by (gave me a giggle)
especially when I looked back and spied the LbNA Logo patch on his
backpack! I would have gone back to ask who you were and swapped stamps,
but Hubby needed to go into work for a while and the children were cold!

We found stamps from Boch's Circus Elephant all the way to the
Contortionist before we had to turn back. The only one we were unable to
locate out of that was the "Trained Lion, WInter" - he evaded us entirely,
hiding quite well under his rock blanket!

I will say, though, after seeing all the cool stamps out there, my husband
is pretty well hooked now and wants to carve our own personal stamp (vs the
stamp that I had purchased eons ago, LOL!). He was not nearly as enthused
about this as I am until this series and the ones we located in Peoria, IL.

We plan to be back out there to finish up the series on Friday afternoon
(sans children! They will be in a History re-enactment in McHenry, IL with
friends of ours - another place to LB on Saturday afternoon when we go to
get them!).

The Cat Herders
