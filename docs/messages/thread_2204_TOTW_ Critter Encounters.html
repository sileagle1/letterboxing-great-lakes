>
> Azobox, we're on the same wavelength, because I was
> thinking of this when I read the TOTW post and had
> started searching the archives for the "we hate
> snakes" story we'd shared with the list that prompted
> you telling us your story.
>
> From message 4006 in the archives, this happened last
> June on our way home from the Great Lakes Gathering.
> Luckily we didn't encounter any snakes, but if only I
> had a video camera with me!
>
> "Then we travelled to find the Lake Erie Creature.
> Reading the clues on the way there, I saw the line
> "Pass the turtle crossing sign. Pass the snake
> crossing sign." I looked at Deedle. "Snake crossing?
> Do they mean a Slippery When Wet sign?" Nope, they
> meant a snake crossing sign, and I'm like, completely
> paranoid of snakes, esp. reading warnings about the
> Michigan Massasauga Rattlesnake all weekend long. And
> that morning, in Hudson Mills, I saw not a
> rattlesnake, but a garter snake or something like it
> (thin body, black with a green or yellow stripe) when
> I went to look where I heard rustling along the path
> as we walked. I think I handled myself very well, I
> didn't scream, I didn't jump into Deedle's arms and
> make him carry me the rest of the way. But he did get
> to retrive all the rest of the letterboxes that day.
>
> But back to the Lake Erie Monster...looking over the
> clues, it was a short walk, on a paved path, no reason
> we should actually come across any snakes, right?
> Until we got to the part where the box was actually
> hidden, and we'd have to walk off the path. Of course
> _I_ was not going. So we studied the best path to take
> to the hiding place, and once the coast was clear, my
> brave Deedle, going all Army-Comando style, grabbed
> his walking stick in both hands, and ran to the hiding
> spot, causing a great russle in the
> foliage. He figured that if he made enough movement
> and noise any snake would high tail it out of there.
> He checked the spot, grabbed the box, and then ran
> back to the path the same way. Oh, the things he'll do
> for me!"
>
> Doodle Bug.
