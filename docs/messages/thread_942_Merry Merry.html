Carve, carve, carve! That is what I am hoping to do. I'm way behind on
getting some stamps and boxes out. I have 5 designs on "pink stuff" which
have traveled to several states with me this year (along with carving
tools), but for some reason the surface of the pink stuff has still not felt
the tip of the tools. I thought I'd spend some evenings in a hotel carving,
but that only happened once. Usually I'm crashing from a long day of work
or boxing, or planning boxing activities for the next day. These "almost
stamps" should have the status of hitchhikers - I think they've been in at
least 5 states! Now I've also got lots of logbooks and those good
Rubbermaid containers just ready & waiting for the stamps.

I'm planning on catching up this winter since temperatures near zero do NOT
entice me to do much boxing. But if it would get up into the teens......

Happy Holidays everyone & happy carving!

Actually, this will be our first winter as "official
'boxers" too. (We actually found out about
Letterboxing in fall of 2003, but didn't try to find
our first box until spring of '04).

I'm with Wisconsin Hiker - the cold, cold temps keep
me from wanting to venture outside. But since we're
over 50 finds, and our original signature stamp is
starting to wear, one of our winter ventures will be
to carve a new signature stamp. It's designed, we
both approve of it, now just to decide which of us
will carve it and when. We'll probably be symbolic
and start using it when we hit 100 finds. I've also
got a few ideas for some new letterbox plants, so
working on designing those stamps and writing witty,
clever clues...and of course, since we are at over 50
finds, finally organizing all those photos we've taken
along the way should take me well into spring....

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-12-22 14:22:39 UTC-05:00
My first winter I carved and even did a fair bit of letterboxing -- I
actually love hiking in the snow, as long as it is not sub-zero and the wind
is not straight from the north. I remember doing First Letterbox on the
Lake and the Birthday Box at Saugatuck Dunes in the winter. One is
protected in the woods -- the other is exposed to the lake winds. My
fingers were so cold I could barely do the stamping! I also put together
the Moo-Mahh virtual letterbox series that winter.

Last year I kind of took the winter off from letterboxing due to a lot of
other things going on at the time and I was really itching to get back at it
when spring rolled around. As we are in the midst of remodeling parts of
the house and I have several art projects I am dying to spend some long
snowy winter weekends on, I will probably ease back again this winter, with
the exception of a really cool project that I and a fellow 'boxer are
working on for spring.

Here's wishing a very Merry Christmas to all you Great Lakes Letterboxers --
may Santa bring you lots of carving goodies and rubbermaid.

SpringChick

From: (katy@dsa132.org) | Date: 2004-12-22 19:54:52 UTC
I'm also going through my first winter as a letterboxer and I have to say
that until last week, I hadn't considered winter an obstacle to boxing.
Then, of course, I traveled north to visit the folks and the temperature
dropped into the single digits... I'm wishing I had brough my carving
stuff with me.

I'm planning to do lots of carving this winter (I just got the idea to do
a series for next winter based on the Rankin-Bass Christmas specials -
that will take me a while), plus make up some hand-made journals for some
odd sized containers I have. If I get truly ambitious I will try a few
more different methods of binding logbooks. I'm also hitting some urban
boxes and drive-by boxes that I probably wouldn't do in the nicer weather
because I'd rather be out hiking then.
