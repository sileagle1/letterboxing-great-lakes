A couple of people asked for the recipe for the strange domed cheese
appetizer I brought to the Gathering. Well, here it is, and I have to
credit cinneberi, fellow boxer, for this:

Cheese Blossom

1 (8 oz) package sliced provolone cheese
2 (8 oz) packages cream cheese, at room temperatire
20(or more)pistachios, shelled and chopped
1 cloves garlic
1 jar green pesto
2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
3 oz oil-packed sun-dried tomatoes

Line a medium bowl with plastic wrap, leaving enough overhang to cover
the top. Reserving 3 slices of provolone line the bottom and sides of
the bowl with remianing provolone, overlapping the slices. For the
cream cheese layer, process the cream cheese, pistachios and 1 of the
garlic cloves in a food processor until blended; scrape the mixture
into a bowl and set aside.

For the tomato layer, drain the tomatoes, reserving the oil. puree the
tomaoes with a small amount of reserved oil in the food precessor.

Spread a layer of cream cheese mixture over the cheese slices lining
the bowl. Layer the pesto, half of remianing cream cheese, all the sun-
dried tomatoes, then top with the rest of the cream cheese. Cover with
remaining provolone cheese slices. Bring edges of plastic wrap together
over the top and secure with twist tie. You can freeze this up to 3
months.

When ready to serve, open plastic wrap, invert bowl over platter, then
remove plastice wrap. Serve with crackers (my favorite being Carr's
wheat!)
