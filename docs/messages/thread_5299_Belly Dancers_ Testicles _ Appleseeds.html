Belly Dancers, Testicles & Appleseeds

From: beckers410 (Mathw613@aol.com) | Date: 2008-09-19 03:40:12 UTC
Get your minds out of the gutters, you sick sick people! :-)

On September 7th, we made a trip to Rockford. Unlike the one we made
last April the day was sunny and warm. The city looks much more
inviting without a deluge of rain falling on us.

We decided we wanted to be home to get the kids in bed on time . . .
meaning leaving Rockford at about 5pm.

We made our first stop in Grand Detour, IL to plant a box at the John
Deere Historic Site. Thanks to Judy, a manager there for contacting
me and giving me permission to hide the box there.

Check out PW's "John Deere Plow" while there... its almost a 2 for 1
deal. Sorry PW, for the close proximity, I felt compelled to follow
Judy's lead on the hiding spot.

We then went on to Byron where we found one of the IRT boxes by
p.granola honoring the Turkey Testicle Festival.

Let me just say how proud I am of our family. We ALL WALKED, the
twins (age 2 1/2) walked too... no stroller!! This is a big milestone
for us. William didn't want to walk at first, so Becki put him on her
shoulders. I SO wish I had a camera with me, as he clung to her face
as if he were a cat and she trying to put him in a tub of water.
Hilarious.

He did get down and walked most the rest. We had a wonderful time,
looking at all the flowers, bugs, grasshoppers, etc.

The girls grabbed some "walking sticks" and Maddie followed suite.
Then Will picked up a "walking stick" that was about 3 inches long
and proceeded to walk bent at the waste so that his stick could touch
the ground. LOL that was too funny.

Once home I Google Earthed our walk for this box and it was just shy
of 1 mile!! Way to go Katie, Alex, Will & Maddie!!!!

We stopped in town for some lunch, right in the lunch hour, so we
decided to try Shorty's "Sams Drive in" on our way home.

We then got lost and ended up on the north east corner of Rockford
and found "Peace Plaza".

Then decided to go further into town and find a new one by River Rat
and an older box by RaqsEnigma "Shimmy for a Cure". The reason we
didn't find Raqs box back in April in all the rain was the one line
in the clues that said, "look out at the carnage. Your prize lies
somewhere in this chaos."

Despite that, we found the box!! Yipee!! Katie, Alex and I all did
that hike, and the girls did a great job balancing on a fallen tree.

We went on and found "Softball Series: Go Thunder" left by azure from
Seattle.

Then we drove back to the south side and found Atwood and found two
new boxes hidden since our last visit. Again, this was much nicer in
the sun!

Wow, what time was it? What time were we to leave? Goodness, time
flew. We needed dinner in a bad kinda way.

We proceeded to get VERY lost on the south and west end of town, and
no one seemed too safe here. Not a fast food place to be had.

After we got out of dodge, we headed west (with empty stomachs) and
found "Larger than Life: Johnny Appleseed" What a fabulous carving!!

We changed the twins diapers in the dark in the cemetery and headed
back south to Byron. Dinner was served at 8:30pm!!!!!!!

We were to leave at 5 and be home by 7:30, here it was an hour past
the kids bedtimes and we were just now getting dinner.

Needless to say, Shorty, your box will have to wait until next time!

All in all we had a great time finding 8 boxes and 2 HH's, and
planting a box to boot.
