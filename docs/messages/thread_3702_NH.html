Hi Ish!

I was planning to hide an indoor letterbox where I work, but I'm rethinking that idea now. We know that a lock-n-lock covered in camo is a letterbox, but since most people don't know what a letterbox is, they would see something that looks suspicious, wrapped in camo tape and think it's a bomb. Why take a chance. Letterboxes were originally hidden in nature, so indoor boxes or boxes hidden on federal property (like a postal box) might not be the way letterboxing was intended. I'm planning to stick to trees, bushed, rocks and other nature crevices. I don't need the FBI busting down my door in the middle of the night asking me questions. I like to sleep at night.
