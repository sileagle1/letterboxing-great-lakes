MI: Sandy Seasons Boxes Replanted

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-08-22 22:49:19 UTC
Another gorgeous day in West Michigan -- a bit too windy for
boating, but perfect for hiking. I headed over to Lake Harbor Park
in Muskegon and replanted the Sandy Seasons boxes (finally). I
slightly relocated the Winter box and modified the clues accordingly
as I happened upon the geocache in the area. Hopefully relocating
the box will help keep it from being continually uncovered and left
in the open by disappointed cachers.

While I was there I checked up on Arbo's Muskegon Beach Box and it
is alive and well, but is very lonely -- no visitors since September
2003! Perhaps now that there are 5 boxes in that park, this lonely
box will see some visitors as this would make a nice weekend
afternoon destination for anyone in the area.

Although I have lived in West Michigan all of my life and have been
fortunate to have Lake Michigan close by and just a natural part of
my world, it never ceases to awe me. As I came up a little crest on
the trail and it opened up to the lake, I got goosebumps from the
feeling that I had just happened upon a secret. The wind was
kicking up some decent sized waves and the sound of the surf was
really quite awesome.

We who are have this as a part of our world truly are blessed -- may
we never take it for granted.

SpringChick
