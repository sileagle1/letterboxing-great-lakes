Very good questions, Bali. When I started 3 years ago, I bought a large
Strathmore book. Not practical and so I cut out my stamps and laminated
them. Then that got to be a hassle (and an expense), so I bought 2
smaller journals, one for exchanges and my plants, another for Finds.
Then I went to a gathering in Louisville (the first one, 2006). Then,
again I carried 2 logbooks to the GLG 2006 last June. Trying to keep
track of two books, not stamping in your sig stamp in your own book, and
making each look "just right" was too much of a burden for me, but I
marvel at the work others have done to make scrapbooks.

So my newest solution (and a nod from Spring Chick) is to create a new
logbook for each gathering I attend. I did this in March and it worked
out great. Of course, come June there will be many more letterboxers and
lots of boxes to find in one day. Come look for me and fill up my
homemade, 96-page logbook. I hope I have enough room, but not too much
room. I will retire the book and start another right afterwards. Stamp
upside down, right side up, whatever suits you. It's quite a relief not
to stress over this now.

My finds and exchanges will occur anywhere and everywhere in the book;
random acts of stamping. I think I'll have much more fun going back and
reminiscing about the day. Chaos reigns.

By the way, Spring Chick ... those logbooks I've been making fit
seredipitously in my built-in desk's cubby holes.
