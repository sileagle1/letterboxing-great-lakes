From: cdem_ltrbox (wp4@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2007-08-28 18:10:04 UTC
Things have been so quiet on the board--maybe a TOTW will stir things
up. For those of you who have placed boxes: which box is your most
visited one and which is your least visited one? And why do you
think that is the case?

For myself, our Cemetery Stroll #1: Showmen's Rest is our most
visited box. We're on the 3rd or 4th logbook now. It has an
interesting--and true--story (it is the burial site of a large number
of victims from a circus train wreck in the early 1900's), so I think
the story appeals to people. Plus, it is in a heavily populated area
and it is not a long walk.

Our least visited box(es) are the series of 5 boxes that we planted
in Alaska. Of course, it is fairly new so people haven't had much
time to search for it yet (no reported finds yet). However, I think
they will remain lightly visited because: Alaska doesn't seem to have
a lot of boxers and our boxes are not easily visited by the cruising
tourists. A lot of people seem to use the cruise ships' land tours
with their specified stops and these boxes really require you to have
a car so that you can visit the sites. In fact, our travel agent was
shocked that we were going to rent a car for a week and drive around
Alaska--none of her customers had ever done that before. You'd think
we were going to some dangerous and foreign destination, instead of
one of the 50 states.

So, what's the scoop on your boxes??? Maybe it will spur some visits
to your least visited boxes...

BirdWoman/Waltzing Pigs

I would have to agree that the word mystery seems to scare away a lot of people. I have several mystery boxes that seen less than 6 visitors in 3-4 years, but my least visited box is Swan Cemetery -- as far as I know only 1 person has been there in nearly 3 years.

Until I pulled it this winter, my most visited box was Old Mission Point Lighthouse. Since then it would have to be the series of boxes at Duncan Woods in Grand Haven. Although just in the past month there has been a rash of finders to several of my U.P. boxes -- glad to see so many people are vacationing in the U.P. It is surely an awesome place to vacation and letterbox! If you are headed up that way yet this summer or fall and will be spending any time in the Porcupine Mountains, I may have something you are interested in (yes, there are a few boxes in the Porkies). E-mail me offlist...

For all of you lighthouse enthusiasts, the Old Mission Lighthouse box will be put back in place in October, as well as replacement boxes for Point Betsie and Grand Traverse Lighthouses.

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2007-08-29 01:04:32 UTC

Wow, I'm really surprised how many cemetery boxes have been mentioned
already! "Most visited" boxes by Waltzing Pigs, Hales Angels, and
Bali and "least visited" by SpringChick. We haven't been to most of
those mentioned, but HAVE been to some of the WP's cemetery boxes and
have really enjoyed them - very interesting cemeteries and stories.
I think there is at least one more WP cementery box that we haven't
tried yet.

I think my most popular box is the very first one I planted in a
nearby suburban park. I simply titled it "Wirth Park" and it is a
nice little walk in the woods, with a little history and a tiny bit
of math. I think it's been visited a lot because it is one of the
older boxes in this area (nearing 4 years old) and it is fairly easy
to find. Other popular boxes of mine are "Playing Possum" in IL
and "Motown Wonder" in MI. Not too hard to figure out why - they are
in parks that were the sites of previous GLk gatherings. Gathering
sites are a popular destination even long after the event is over
since there are usually a lot of boxes to find without having to
drive around much. One other trend I noticed is that boxes
in "vacation" spots are also popular. I have one box in Door County
WI that gets a lot of visitors and I also get a lot of reports for
the box we adopted in Hawaii.

I have 4 boxes that are tied for "least popular". To my knowledge,
none have been found yet. One in Mexico planted in MAR 2005, one in
Oregon (SEP 2005), one in Washington state (SEP 2006) and one in
northwestern WI (JUL 2007). Not many people visit the southwestern
coast of Mexico and not everyone wants to do some long hikes in
mountains. However I'm sure someone will be finding the WI one soon,
since I just got FF reports on 2 others we planted last month in WI &
MN.

Interesting TOTW, BirdWoman!

Wisconsin Hiker
