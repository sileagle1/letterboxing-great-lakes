Topic of the week ideas

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2005-11-30 04:36:00 UTC
Well, if we're ever in need for some more ideas for a "Topic of the
Week", here's a list dvn2r ckr post on the Pacific Northwest regional
group:

Potential Topics Of The Week:

o Favorite letterbox you've found (state, country or world)
o Favorite letterbox you've planted (state, country or world)
o Favorite clue

o Wackiest letterbox you've found/planted
o Tackiest theme for letterboxing
o Favorite type of letterbox container
o Favorite type of carving media, tools, etc
o Favorite type of logbook, material
o Favorite style of clues
o Ideas for future 'statewide/regional' letterbox series
o discussion about regional LB maintenance week/month/year

o Source(s) for LB inspiration
o Hobbies beyond letterboxing
o How far are you willing to drive (per day/week/month) to find an
interesting letterbox?

o Most bizarre occurrence while letterboxing?
o Most odd discovery while letterboxing?
o What has letterboxing taught you personally?
o What has letterboxing taught you about others?
o Where hadsletterboxing led you that you otherwise would never
have known about?
o Has letterboxing inspired you in other areas of your life?
o Do you incorporate letterboxing in other aspects of your life?

o Least favorite materials for letterboxing
(media/tools/containers/etc)

...

dvn2r ckr

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-12-01 11:47:07 UTC
There are some really great ideas here! I've tried to steer clear
of the "favorite box/clue" topics, as far as commenting on other
people's boxes (i.e. finds) because it is so subjective (we all like
different things about letterboxes for different reasons) and it
could potentially be discouraging for people, cause hurt feelings or
unintentionally give spoilers.

But certainly commenting on your favorite box planted and the other
topics listed offer a wide variety of discussion opportunities.

Thanks for posting this!

SpringChick
