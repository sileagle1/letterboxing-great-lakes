Lots of ideas

From: speedsquare_lbxr (ruhlette@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-02-14 15:37:55 UTC
I've been thinking about creating a county quest series for my new area. Plenty of history,
plenty of old houses (Log cabin, Federal to Mid-Century Modern styles), colorful celebrities,
artists and some Firsts. I want it to be available at the local historial society. I was thinking
of hosting a (very small) gathering of letterboxers that might want to help me make quest
books. Once all the clues have been solved and letterboxes found, the completed series
would earn a patch or decal or something.

Our local library has a wonderful online catalog and newspaper index going waaaay back. I
am spoiled.

This series might act as a WOM in that I would probably announce it here, and at our local
museums. My hope is that it gets students and parents interested in the rich history of our
area.

So, if I manage to pull this off in 2007, this will be my resolution.

speedsquare

Trailtracker - I'm not so far away!!! I've seen your stamp in the past
six months, most likely in the Dayton area ... or was it Houston Woods?
I'm just across the border.

Today I made a new discovery!!! Level 3 snow emergency here in Wayne
County. DH has been very sick since Monday evening. We have a lot of
snow and ice, so I figured out how to use our monster John Deere today.
Not a bad job as I had to go downhill on the drive. The great discovery
was what I wore!!! I think they're called Carharts ... quilted orange
canvas jumpsuit. Luckily his fit me okay and kept me toasty. This is the
perfect outfit for letterboxing in colder temps. My regular clothes
underneath, and some waterproof boots, and I could letterbox ALL DAY.
I've already claimed them to be "my clothes" when I need them.
