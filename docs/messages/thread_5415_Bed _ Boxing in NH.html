Bed & Boxing in NH

From: tiptoetonto (tiptoetonto@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-11-19 04:17:38 UTC
We are back from our four day foray to New Hampshire and the Bed & Boxing event
sponsored by Mark & Sue Pepe in Jackson, New Hampshire. WOW! We sure had a great
time! We are here to disclaim any notion that New Englanders aren't as hospitable as us
good ol' Midwesterners! Our only hope is that if any one from outside our region comes
to one of our events, that we should be as gracious as we were treated!

What was so nice about this kind of event is that it was with a fairly small group (around
30) that over a forty hour period of time were able to really get to know each other. There
wasn't that pressure to choose between hunting for boxes or meet and exchange. There
was time for both. We were able to share several meals, hikes, and leisure time together.
We thoroughly enjoyed the company of everyone! Although most of the group knew each
other, many having traveled to Dartmoor or cruised with Mark & Sue, they welcomed us in
as if we had been boxing with them all along. The New England letterboxers were fun and
fabulous!

We arrived in NH early the first day in order to box our way across the Kancamagus Hwy.
before the event started on Friday night. We got off the plane in Manchester and were
greeted with a heavy drizzle of rain which would be our weather for the next three days. I
thought we were in Oregon! We drove straight up to the western end of the Hwy. and hit
our first box in NH. The clues warned this was not a box for children!...VERY
SLIPPERY....repeated in the clues and read to Tonto NUMEROUS times. The location was a
swift moving, wide river with lots of scattered boulders causing mini waterfalls all across.
Quite beautiful! A very large flat rock, about 35 feet square, jetted out across the river
that was mentioned in the clues for a bearing reading. It was at an angle facing
downstream. And did I mention VERY SLIPPERY???? Oh, that's right, you heard me didn't
you? Well guess who didn't?

Our first box and Tonto's feet slip from under him. He is flat on his backside sliding in
slow motion right before my very eyes down the slope of the rock toward the river! In
typical fashion, my first response isn't, "Are you hurt?" but blood curdling, ""What the
blankety-blank do you think you are doing!!!" Fear for me manifests itself as anger in
split seconds! Miraculously, his momentum stopped inches from the edge. I was out in
the middle of the rock and couldn't get to him. He was so stunned he just laid there
sprawled out and motionless. Tonto was down for the count!

All I could think about is "Dang! There goes our letterboxing trip!" How sick is that?
Don't answer...I know I'm bad. Thankfully, by the time I made it over to him, he was able
to slowly get to a standing position. He said his elbow was hurting a bit, but other than
that he was O.K. Of course he blamed it on his worn out boot treads. Yeah right! His
entire back side head to toe was wet, but he managed to gingerly make his way back to
the shore. His stubborn Dutchness came out as he immediately started looking under
roots for that elusive box. Way to go Tonto! Shake off that near death experience and go
for the carved rubber! You're the man!
Unfortunately, we never did find the box, but that was the only one that eluded us the
entire trip! He had been baptized by water and earned his reward by finding all the other
attempted boxes. Warning: New Hampshire can be very slippery!

The weather was rainy, but those New Englanders are a hearty group and we boxed in the
rain, the sleet and drizzle, and still managed to have such fun. We scaled more rocky,
uphill trails than I've seen in a long time. I guess that's why they call it the granite state!
There is some of the stuff scattered on every trail! We saw gorgeous waterfalls, but no
moose. Some of the boxes we found had been in the wild for over five/six years. That
was exciting to see "old" boxes still in place. The food, comraderie, and fun made for a
memorable trip. We'd go back in a heart beat!
Oh, I almost forgot about Betty the ghost! But, that will have to be another story!
Tiptoe ....and Tonto
