TOTW - letterboxing destination vacations

From: tiptoetonto (tiptoetonto@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-08-01 17:14:00 UTC
Wow! We've been away and out of touch for about two weeks ...Reading
the past posts is like a novel....Such characters!...Such plots!
Sweaty women boxers...SPY's... apologies..."Monty Python's" needing
adoption...Faire boxes missing....and 500 memebers - some with
aliases...imagine that!
While letterboxing (as we know it) is in a constant state of re-
inventing itself...We went on a "Letteboxing Destination Vacation" to
actually get out there and enjoy all the perfect aspects that
attracted us to the hobby in the first place. Beautiful hikes,
learning about the historical, geological and cultural points of a
given area...and did we mention BEAUTIFUL, STUNNING hikes????!!!!!
Unfortunately, we were only able to go away for three days when we
were called for a family emergency that has knocked us out of
commission...(Tiptoe's father is very ill...)
Anyway...as for the "Letterboxing Destination Vacation Getaway"...We
want all of you in the Great Lakes area (and beyond) to consider
Hocking Hills in Southeast Ohio! Oh My! We were so impressed with
the area! Now, we love all of our Midwest area..but, this was a
surprise treat!
We were able to hike about 25 miles...and not wanting anyone to jump
on us about letterbox counts...we added 43 to our logbook! Again,
the trip is more about the hike for us, but the letterboxes "told" us
where to go. Without the boxes there is no way we could've
experienced the area as we did!
The area is comprised of "seven wonders"..actually seven parks within
an area...As we would go to one, Tonto would claim, "This has to be
the main attraction," and then we would go to the next area and it
was equally if not more incredible.
One evening, we traveled 40 min. away to the outdoor theater
production,"Tecumseh". Beautiful evening - enjoyable and
informative...but very loud canons - our ears are still ringing.
Tonto really got into it! We stayed in a marvelous cabin with a hot
tub to soak our tired bones after hiking all day..had a great meal in
a one room log cabin...but, missed our planned dinner
guest...trailtracker...only bad part of the whole trip!
Many letterboxers know about this little jewel of an area...as noted
by the full logbooks...but mostly folks from the Buckeye State...I
think they have kept this a secret to maintain the non-commercial,
rural, and natural feel...it was fabulous! Except, cell-phone
addicted Tonto was constantly on the lookout for a pay phone...(no
cell phone service in the area except in Logan)..I accused him of
being Amish...inside joke about pay phones!
Trailtracker has to take a lot of credit for making our trip
OUTSTANDING, she supplied us with a key which made it possible to
find our way around...BTW..We planted our own box while down
there...now, if only we can remember the clues to write them down and
post them...this is why some of us shouldn't be allowed to plant
boxes!
As for the TOTW...what other "letterboxing destination vacations"
would any of you 500 (minus the aliases) fellow boxers recommend?
Tiptoe & Tonto

The Chicago Dogs definitely second TiptoeTonto's suggestion! We lived in Ohio for four

and wonderful stories and history.

visit from time to time...

Funny you should describe the trails this way..Tonto and I both on
several trails thought exactly the same thing! Almost magical with
the sunlight streaming down in distinct rays illuminating moss
covered bolders to the point they looked phosphorescent! We half
expected Frodo to pop out from behind a rock....It was sunny the
entire three days we were visiting Hocking Hills, but I don't think
we actually saw the sun except in a few of the parking areas due to
the amazing canopy of trees, cliffs and rocks....
And even more "Lord of the Ring-like" were the huge "slump blocks"
with the giant foot-like roots attached with a towering tree reaching
up to meet the hundreds of other tall trees...Looked just like
the "Walking trees"...or the feet of giant prehistoric birds! Very
impressive!

I'm thinking of borrowing one of those fairy costumes from the "fairy
people" at the Ren Faire for my next foray into the Hocking
Hills...I'd look marvelous with my hiking boots, fanny pack...and
gauzy wings...We won't even imagine the tutu!
Tiptoe

From: Barb Funk (mmfunk99@greenapple.com) | Date: 2007-08-02 18:33:30 UTC-04:00
To Tiptoe and Tonto and The Chicago Dogs,

Y'all are being very generous with your comments! Thank you so much.
The best part about planting boxes down there and putting the key
together for those who want to get the most out of the trip to the
Hocking Hills area is in the sharing of the stunning sights down there.
I know that many agree---that is the essence of letterboxing! Hocking
Hills is truly a gem of the state of Ohio.

I have been going down to the Hocking Hills area at least once per
month---if not once per week---for at least a couple of decades. I
never tire of the scenes. To get to Ash Cave first--at the crack of
dawn is truly a gift from God and helps put everything in perspective.
Several other letterboxers have now joined in to place some down
there---and I have to say I get a big honkin' smile on my face when I
see that! ANY excuse to go back down is BONUS!

It gives me great joy to know others feel the same way about this area
as I do....and as Grandma Gatewood did when she was seen there. I just
love that she was the first female thru-hiker of the AT at the age of 63
and her training was at Hocking Hills.

Tiptoe---I hope your father is okay!!!
The Chicago Dogs---happy belated birthday this past Monday!! =)
