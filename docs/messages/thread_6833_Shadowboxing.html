Shadowboxing

From: tiptoetonto (tiptoetonto@yahoo.com) | Date: 2010-12-22 19:26:32 UTC
We attend a church with over 700 members. Due to split services there are about 300 to 350 in attendance at each service. This time of year the numbers are inflated with visitors and last Sunday was no exception. We were thrilled to have our daughter and her boyfriend with us. Now, this is the same daughter that looks down her nose at her parent's favorite hobby of letterboxing, but her boyfriend seems to show some interest - (We suspect this is just a ploy for our favor, but we'll take his support of the hobby in any form we can take it).

The sermon was well under way, when all of a sudden the pastor begins to talk about people enjoying God in nature..he needing an illustration, points to us and says, "Now, what's that thing you two do? Shadowboxing?"

At which Tonto shouts back up to the pulpit correcting him, "That would be LETTERBOXING"!

Throughout the congregation were pockets of hysterical laughter from those that have heard of our strange hobby. There were many confused looks as to what was taking place - first with the pastor asking such a strange question, and then this tall old guy shouting back up at the pastor in the middle of the service! But most noticeable were the audible groans coming from our daughter who now had slipped down in her seat trying desperately to become invisible.

Just wondering...if anyone else has ever been called out at church for being "skippers" on a Sunday to go ....um...shadowboxing? ....or... where we can rent a new daughter for Christmas...ours seems to have disowned us!
Tiptoe & Tonto
