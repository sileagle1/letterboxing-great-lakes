Sunfield SPY's from a member and a "contestant"

From: unclebubba17 (darinjweller@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-07-27 01:00:40 UTC
If you have read our website, www.sunfieldspys.org you would realize
that we are a non-profit charity organization committed to bringing
activities to the youth of our community at little or no cost to the
parents. We run many many fund raisers throughout the year and have
many great events to bring enjoyment to children. However, this is NOT
one of those events. This multi-month "treasure hunt" to try to find
as many boxes as possible. The "contestants" consist of mostly couples
from the area, 40-60 years old, with the exception of me a 30 year
old, that are all respectful of every box and every rule of
letterboxing. I have seen no signs of any wrong doing from our
organization and take great offense to any accusations of them. The
help line is a guys phone number we all know and call him if we are
lost out in the woods and need a hand if he has already done the box.
He is not "walking" people right to boxes. We started this thing
because he thought we would all enjoy it and most of us are having a
blast with it. I was planning on continuing this after the competition
is over, now I am not so sure if this is the way the "letterboxing
community" holds itself. Please email me if you have any questions
about what we are doing. I am more than willing discuss just about
anything.

Darin Weller
Guys and Dolls

RE: [LbGLK] Sunfield SPY's from a member and a "contestant"

From: xxxxxxxx (BrighidFarm@comcast.net) | Date: 2007-07-26 21:24:42 UTC-05:00
I'm just playing devil's advocate here..........but can we really criticise
this type of "competition" when we have a lot of "regular" letterboxers who
place such a high high value on their own F-counts, even to the point of
reporting about how many boxes they've found in a day and such? And we now
have ways to keep all those tallies online even.

What really is the difference between someone attempting 20, 30, or 50 boxes
in one day and the sort of "competition" that these folks have put together?
It seems to me that it's just the idea of "personal best" against "team
play" but what's really the difference in the long run?

I mean, if it's "all about the hike", I don't see that there's much that a
person can have appreciated about the hikes if they're finding 20, 30, or 50
boxes in a day. It's hard to believe that there was much, if any,
stop-&-smell-the-roses time.

This is being done by "newbies" to letterboxing, but is it really any
different than the "power boxing" done by many of "our own troops"?

Now, I could ask...........how many boxes are these hunters placing? But
then again, we have plenty of "our own troops" who have never placed a
single box. So can't really criticise there either. Planting isn't a
requirement for a person to be thought of as a "letterboxer."

Another poster made a comment about not wanting the U.S. to become like the
U.K. with requirements such as the "100 found boxes" thing. But the thing
is, that's true, but it isn't true. To officially buy a new catalog of
placed boxes, one needs to be a member. On the other hand, old catalogs are
for sale all over the place. Very easy to find. Plus, and this is the
important thing to me, there are scads and scads of folks totally willing to
help out and "mentor" a newbie when they run across one.

Maybe what's needed to combat all the media information out there that
doesn't stress good techniques and letterboxing etiquette at all, thus
leaving newbies often totally in the dark, we need people willing to take
out new people and be willing to show them the ropes. Showing people in
person how it's done, I think, would be much more effective than people
skimming thru articles they may find on LBNA or atlasquest. As good as
those articles with information and tips can be, let's face it, when most
people are reading things on their computer monitor, they're busy skimming,
not really digesting the information. I don't really know how we could
accomplish this tho. I'd certainly be willing to go out with new folks on,
say, Sunday afternoons. But how to get the information out there that
someone is willing to do it, and how to assure safety, I have no idea.

Just my two cents.......

~~ Mosey ~~

I'm a "foreigner" here, but I would like to add that if this were going on
in my area, I would appreciate it if the organizer would notify me what is
going on as a courtesy to me, the box owner. Any time there is unusual or
excessive activity around a box, there is greater risk and a greater need
for maintenance. As a box owner, I would like the opportunity to opt out of
having my boxes involved in such an activity (and I probably wouldn't have,
but it would be nice to have the option).

That said, it sounds like a fine idea and what a shame it was so
misunderstood initially. That probably could've been avoided with a little
communication ahead of time, especially since letterboxers are typically
generous souls and they may well have found a way to make it even more fun
for the participants had they known what was up!

unclebubba,
Thanks for coming out and explaining the group to us some more. It
puts my mind at ease somewhat that the members are concerned with the
stealth and etiquette that boxers typically try to uphold. I don't
really like the idea of using letterboxing as a contest to get as
many as fast as possible because I think that may diminish the
satisfaction and enjoyment of the finds. But, as long as the boxes
are cared for, handled well and replaced and rehidden well, I guess I
don't care if that's how they are found.

I did read the SPY's main site and it looks like a terrific
organization. I'm happy to hear that your members have worked out
the clues themselves. When I saw the hotline thing, it made me think
that the box locations were being "given up", the code answers were
being given out and the finders weren't doing any of the work for
themselves. If that's a wrong assumption on my part, I'm glad and
I'm sorry for jumping to conclusions. There's a mystery box out
there that I found the location of, got there and sat around for a
long time trying to figure out. I gave up after an hour or so and
went home empty handed. I'm still trying to work it out and hope to
get it eventually. I was thinking that this box was just being outed
to anyone in your group who asked and it upset me that people that
didn't really figure it out would claim it as a find.

To me, a great part of the fun and satisfaction of finding the boxes
is being able to figure those things out on my own and having an AHA
moment. Even if it takes several attempts. I thought your members
were being cheated of that experience by having an easy out through
the hotline.

I still don't like the idea of a hotline or the blog giving out any
types of hints or clues to people's boxes. Especially if its to
boxes that were planted by people other than the contestants in this
group. I can see having tips like "the areas flooded", "the box is
missing", "the road is under construction" or what not. I just hope
that the codes or locations for mystery boxes aren't being just
handed out to everyone.

I'm still concerned about one of the post's on the SPY blog site
about one member's "power hunting" tactics and destroyed pants. It
makes me think the area surrounding the box may be destroyed as
well. Again, that could very well be a misinterpretation on my part.

Welcome the this group and to this great hobby. I'm sorry it was
under these circumstances. I hope this hasn't soured you to the
hobby. It takes a lot of time, energy, effort and love to put a box
out in the wild. We may just be like overprotective parents freaking
out about our kids safety and trying to protect them as best as we can.

Bali
http://bnlbaligrl.blogspot.com/
