Hi alls, I'm from Milwaukee and new(ish) to letterboxing. My take on it is this:

I read about letterboxing in a local magazine. How else would I have found out about it?
Letterboxing needs exposure. It doesn't have to be a tight little ball of hardcore boxers
hellbent on keeping it "pure" somehow. There's nothing to "get" about it. It's really fun.
It's a past time. More people reading about it means more new letterboxers and
eventually more letterboxers that may even pass whatever standards makes them good
enough to be in the in group. Some boxes will get ruined. Just like some boxes get eaten
by squirrels or thrown out by random nonletterboxers. It's a hazardous hobby.

Besides, everything gets "ruined" eventually if you have that perspective on it...its like that
music band that you really loved when they were underground and then, ut oh, they "sold
out," became popular and now the new fans aren't good enough somehow or weren't there
for the beginning so they must not get it.

At some level this all just becomes elitism.

KM

From: Melisa (mshills1@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2007-10-10 18:00:22 UTC-05:00
I have to say it is not about keeping LG'ing secret, elitist or
anything like that. It is simply about people who do not take time
to find out exactly what they are doing. Taking "instructions" on
Letterboxing from the media as to how the hobby is generally done is,
well, rather foolish. They cannot seem to present a balanced view of
anything and the fact that they would not let the letterboxer remain
anonymous is a bit much. How come in other articles, they can
mention a "source", but not for this one? Go figure.

When we found out about Letterboxing a 2-3 years ago (not from the
media, but via WOM), I made a point of reading everything there was
available from the "Getting Started" section on the Letterboxing.org
site (was not aware of Atlasquest at the time). Since then, they
have even posted the Letterboxing 101 in that section and it
clarifies most of the issues that newbies would have. And yes, when I
introduced a couple of other families to letterboxing in the last
year or so, I gave them copies of the Letterboxing 101 document,
explaining it will make their lbing more fun for them and for those
that come behind them. They came with us a couple of times and then
they were off!

Would one take up other skills without finding out what it is all
about first? Most likely not. I am not asking that they write a
paper, but use a little common sense when getting into a hobby that
involves other people and having a little respect. Sadly, so much of
our society is selfish and self-centered, many of them could care
less about the box once they get what they came there for. While
critter or weather damage happens, along with the random idiot child
who is bent on destruction of whatever they happen across, the rest,
I believe is mostly avoidable if people would simply take the time to
find out exactly what is expected of them.

I often wonder if people who are just starting out and do not bother
to read up on the hobby figure that the Letterboxing fairy placed
these boxes out there and this same fairy will come back and re-hide
it properly? Hmmm - could be similar to the geocachers who cannot
read the box labels that indicate that it is a letterbox and not a
geocache, but take the stamp anyway and leave a trinket behind...

Another thought that I have in regards to the reporters is that they
seem to think this is a lo-tech version of geocaching or
something. I think because geocaching involves an electronic device
that gets you most of the way there - no clue solving, no individual
hand carved stamp to see, etc. that it may be too hard for them to
wrap their brains around and write a quick article about. I do not
mean to be insulting, but this does take a little more effort than
turning on a machine and plugging in some coordinates, KWIM? And,
with the smallish area that most are allotted space for their
articles, there is more involved in the hobby than they are able to
put into a small piece - does that make sense?

Sorry, I really did not mean to rant here, but I get tired of hearing
that because we want the media to treat a hobby with respect and
write a thoughtful article that points people in the correct
direction for the hobby, that somehow, the letterboxing community is
snooty or something. Hopefully, ya'll will be kind to me in your
responses and not nasty. I am recovering from some bug and I know my
thoughts are probably not totally coherent here.

The CatHerders
