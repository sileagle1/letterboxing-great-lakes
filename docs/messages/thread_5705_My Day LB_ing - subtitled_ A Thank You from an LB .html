From: walesflyermary (drewes@WI.RR.com) | Date: 2009-03-06 19:26:48 UTC
Being newish to planting LB's, I had quite the adventures in my efforts to plant during today's late spring thaw:

-Checked out and dismissed around five neighborhood parks for planting
-Muck at least 2 inches deep in a riverside park (yes, I wore boots)
-Muck at least 2 inches deep in a dog park (yeah for boots!)
-Water at least 2 inches deep in many places
-Ankle deep snow in places
-Wet socks - and plant legs
-Practice looking nonchalant in a not-yet-fully-open area of a dog park for a cop doing rounds (my 3rd LBing encounter with a cop)
-Being barked at by dogs in said dog park
-Lose notepaper with newly written clues on it
-Tried to pry loose a rock from the frozen ground so it could help secure an LB
-The usual spiderwebs and branch scratches
-Planted 4 new boxes and relocated another

-but, the "piece de resistance" - picture this - Wisconsin Hiker's sig stamp "whispering" in my ear sage planting advice - "Provide cover for the finder near the hiding spot"

So, a big THANK YOU to Wisconsin Hiker and Martini Man for their fine example, gentle advice that will prolong the lives of my boxes, patience and enthusiasm for our fun hobby!

- WFMary
