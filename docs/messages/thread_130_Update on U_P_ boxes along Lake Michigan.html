Update on U.P. boxes along Lake Michigan

From: Tom & Jill (tj_mich@ameritech.net) | Date: 2004-04-13 22:28:52 UTC
Here's what we found out along the U.P. portion of the Lake Michigan
Circle Tour this weekend, going from east to west:

Mighty Mac (St. Ignace - by Esperanza): Straits State Park is
currently closed for some construction, so we weren't able to get in
to check on the box. It was fine as of last August.

Cut River Bridge (Epoufette - our box): Box is fine, though it's
currently covered by a few inches of snow. The last visitor was on
March 8th.

Top O' The Pickle (Naubinway - by Esperanza): Box is in great shape,
completely dry, and has had over 16 different visitors/groups. We
found the Blackjack Acorn Hitchhiker here, and sent it along at
another box.

Seul Choix Point Lighthouse (Gulliver - by SpringChick): Seems to be
missing. At least, it was not in the same spot as when we found it
one year ago. I'll e-mail SpringChick off-list to discuss, so that
she can make the final determination. This is still a great spot to
visit -- a neat lighthouse on Lake Michigan, just a few miles south
of the highway.

Kitch-Iti-Kipi (Manistique - our box): The area around the box has
lots of standing water (melting snow), but luckily the box itself was
just above the water line. Doing fine, but has only had a couple of
visitors.

Fox Park (Cedar River - our box): We re-planted Beach Bum, formerly
of Wells Park, here.

Wells Park boxes (Cedar River): Sadly, we removed our 3 boxes
here. The web site has been updated.

Henes Park boxes (Menominee - ours): Poplar Point and Lily Pad Pond
were both in fine shape. Babbling Brook was gone! We searched
around for several minutes and finally found it under a bush about 10
yards away. Probably was moved by an animal. It's now in a
different, nearby location with better protection, and the clue page
has been updated.

Menominee North Pier Light (Menominee - our box): Doing fine and has
had several visitors.


We hope that more of you will have a chance to find these boxes this
summer. The new Lake Michigan Circle Tour publicity should bring a
little extra attention, too. Also, we noticed some good parks and
locations along the route that are worthy of new letterboxes.... time
to get the carving tools out again!

--Tom & Jill (TJ_Mich)
