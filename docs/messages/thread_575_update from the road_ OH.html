update from the road, OH

From: Armadillo Jo (jes7o@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-08-21 16:13:22 UTC-07:00
Hello everyone! Glad to see I completely missed the
last few days of discussion. We, (my mom (trailname:
Ugler) and I, left on Thursday afternoon and rolled
into Bowling Green to find Ay Ziggy Zooba alive and
well. We stayed in BG that night and headed up to
Pemberville the next AM to find Harry's Camp. Of
course, on the one day that we set aside to letterbox
all day...we get RAIN! I did some initial research on
this box, went to Pemberville, and was unsucessful. We
have an idea of where it might be, but it was raining
too hard to give it a good try. So...we headed up to
Port Clinton and kept hoping for less rain. NOPE. We
got to where Rest in Peace lives, made some over
assumptions, got soaking wet digging around were I
*thought* it was...and then gave up. When I got back
into the car, I knew exactly where it was (in a place
too easy for words) and found it. We stamped in the
car and double bagged everything. Then we headed up to
Marblehead to East Harbor State Park to find Hazard, a
relatively new box. By this time, it was letting up a
little...so we decided to give it a whirl. What a neat
hike, with beautiful views of Lake Erie!
Unfortunately, right when we had deciphered the clues
and was closing in on the box.....it started to
downpour. We found the box...and were the FIRST
FINDERS! Yay! We did the human shield to keep
everything relatively dry and got soaked heading
back...even through the rain gear. I highly recommend
this box, if nothing for the spectacular views! We
then headed to the East Harbor box, as it seemed to
be, and was, a drive-by kind of box. We stamped in the
car again and double bagged everything again. We had
One Hundred Acre Woods on the agenda, but we were too
soaked to try them. Tomorrow we hope to do some more
boxing in the NE Ohio area and will head back on
Monday. We're planning a plant tonight and one on
Monday.

Armadillo Jo

From: Doodle & Deedle Bug (doodle_n_deedle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-08-22 17:00:48 UTC-07:00
Maybe it's just that area around Port Clinton that
causes rain to fall when one's trying to box in the
area. Deedle & I were also faced with varying degress
of rain back in July when we were out that way. We
now carry a compact umbrella (the kind you can find in
Wal-Mart that brags that it's full sized but folds up
small enough so you can stick it in your purse) in our
letterboxing backpack for instances when we're boxing
and it starts to rain so that we can use it to shield
the stamping-in process.

Congrats, Armadillo Jo, on the First Finder. Now I
don't feel so bad about us finding that hitchhiker in
Ay Ziggy Zoomba :)

Doodle aka Diane.
