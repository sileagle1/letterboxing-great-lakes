From: baliddle (baliddle@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2007-10-27 21:10:52 UTC-04:00
Well, that was fun! I think the Creepy Crawl seemed like it went off
without a hitch! I didn't hear any stories of anyone getting
arrested, or hassled or lost! No one complained that I got any
directions wrong in my clues! No one got stung by the Wasp Woman or
bitten by the Wolfman! I guess the only major fubar on our part was
that Wisconsin Hiker and Martini Man beat us back to the final
resting spot! Oops. So much for trying to grab some lunch! Lots of
people finished the crawl a lot sooner than I thought. I figured it
would take 3 1/2 to 4 hours... Spartan Fans finished in just under 3
hours! Whew!

I hope everyone had a good time! It was fun for me! I'm sorry I
didn't get a chance to talk to people too much at the after party. I
was running around doing a million things and by the time I was ready
to chill out, everyone left!

I want to thank everyone for participating, and especially for
contributing additional food, ice, plates, cups, napkins, etc! I
appreciate the help with everything! (I especially appreciate that I
ended up with the left over revel bars! Woohoo! Who'da thought
there'd be leftovers of those!!! Yum!)

Thanks for coming, if you did... and sorry we missed you, if you
didn't make it!

Bali

From: xxxxxxxx (BrighidFarm@comcast.net) | Date: 2007-10-29 08:03:09 UTC-05:00
I missed the original post, but from what I gather, the group Rick is
looking for is raogk.org

It stands for Random Acts of Geneological Kindness. You can check on their
site for someone who volunteers in the county that you need information
from.

I'm a volunteer for DuPage County, IL. Things have suddenly got busy 'round
DuPage..........I'm heading over to the county courthouse today to search
for things for 6 different requesters that have emailed me in the past three
weeks. Nobody at all contacted me in September. All of a sudden, family
trees are important. :-) Must be because we're gettin' closer to the
holidays.

Libraries are a good source, especially if the library has a great reference
librarian. Googling for historical societies in the area can also be a
great source. Even if a person wants to do the legwork themselves,
contacting the RAOGK volunteer for that area, the volunteer might be able to
give them places to contact that would be good for that specific county.

~~ Mosey ~~
