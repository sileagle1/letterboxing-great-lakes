REPORT: Cook County, IL

From: Mosey (PonyExpressMail@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-06-19 14:56:13 UTC-05:00
Had a little spare time this morning for a few hikes so gassed up the truck
and set out.

1. Shake! Shake! Shake! in Orland Park, placed by Howling Coyote. Super
simple, yet elegant, creative, and *very* cute stamp. The view of the lake
was nice, but the stamp was worth going for even if Howling Coyote had
hidden it at a garbage dump. At least in my opinion. Box was in good
shape.

2. The Turtle by the Lake in Palos Heights, placed by Big Cat. Box was
placed recently and was in good shape but I didn't figure it would be for
long since neither logbook nor stamp were bagged whatsoever. And there was
a large brush type marking pen in the box. I figured the first good summer
rain that comes along..............

Assuming we get any good rains this summer. Assuming we get any rain at
*all* this summer.

So I left everything bagged. I'm figuring maybe Big Cat is a brand new
placer?

3. Lake Katherine and the Waterfall, also in Palos Heights, also placed by
Big Cat. This one I just could not find. Clues seemed very
straightforward, everything seemed to fit, but at the very end, I just
didn't seem to have come up with the right place. No yellow marked boulder
could I see.

But I couldn't spend long on it since it was the last box of the morning,
and I had already spent so much time investigating the Lake Katherine Nature
Area and laughing at everything. In one sense it's a beautiful place. An
awfully lot of work has gone into it. In another sense, I've never felt so
manipulated or that anything in nature was so contrived in my life. And The
Rules! My word, The Rules! I haven't seen so many rules since I was a
teenager and started dating. I took a photo of "The Rules Board" because it
blew me away. And continually, on the hike, there were signs saying "Don't
you dare do this!" and "Don't you dare do that!" I felt like nuns were
waiting with rulers around each bend in the trail.

I was rotflmao tho when I went down a particular trail the clues say to take
and lo and behold there was a large downed tree blocking the trail. The
Doer of the Evil Deed? A beaver! It looked like it had been chewed down
very recently. The nerve of some beavers! Because I'm sure there's a rule
against chewing down a tree in that particular woods. If there isn't,
there's soon going to be. :-)

A nice, cool, fun morning.

~~ Mosey ~~
