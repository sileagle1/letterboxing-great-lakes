"SpringChick" wrote:
>
> Although I won't be able to join in the boxing part of the day, as
> the owner of many of those 41 boxes, I would like to give some
input.
>
> First of all, I appreciate the comments about being careful not to
> give spoilers. There are some mystery boxes in the bunch, and
there are others where bits and pieces of information are missing or
need to be fit together to arrive at the location. For somebody
familiar with the area or somebody who has previously been to the
park or box, this may seem obvious, however to another person, these
would be things that need to be figured out. Although I certainly
cannot stop people from collaborating to solve clues, mystery boxes
are not really meant to be group projects.
>
> Secondly, I am concerned about a group boxing activity. Several
of the boxes in this area are in Muskegon State Park. As many of
you know, the Michigan DNR recently implemented a "pay to place"
policy for geocaches and letterboxes. Fortunately the staff at
Muskegon State Park have taken a lenient stance on this policy and
rather than pull the boxes or charge for their placement, they are
monitoring the boxes, watching for social trails and other
environmental impact. I have worked very hard to establish a
relationship with them and to show them the positve side of
letterboxing. A handful of boxers coming through every couple of
weeks or so isn't a problem; 25 people descending on a box at one
time is a whole other game. In addition to the environmental aspect
of many people boxing together, I have concerns about the
discretionary aspect. How inconspicuous can a group of letterboxers
really be? It is hard enough to keep a low profile when there are
2or 3 of you and a dog, let alone an entire group. I would hate to
see any of the area boxes -- mine or otherwise -- be compromised
because a flurry of activity raises the curiosity of other hikers or
nearby observers.
>
> So in short, I am wondering if it is not better to provide a
specified period of time for people to do some boxing in the area --
individually or as small teams, rather than as a large group, and
then meet up with each other after that? That way those who are
interested in jacking up the "F" count can head off to the drive-
bys, those looking for a hike can head another direction and those
looking for some awesome scenery can head yet another way. This
will also allow those who want to take the time to work through some
of the mystery clues the opportunity to head off to those boxes
without fear of giving anything away to others in the group.
Everyone has the same access to the clues at letterboxing.org and
should be able to decide what works best for them without having to
create a group itinerary.
>
> If anyone has specific questions about any of my Muskegon/White
> Lake/Montague area boxes, or may be wondering which would provide
the easiest/toughest/most scenic hike, please feel free to write me
off list.
>
> Also, I am interested in what may transpire for later in the day --
would love to meet up with the group for an evening meal or bonfire
if there are folks who will be staying around that long.
