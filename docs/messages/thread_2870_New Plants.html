From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-08-03 23:25:42 UTC-04:00
The "green" counties at one time were a representation of the counties with
letterboxes. As this is a manual process and there are only a couple of
people who do the map graphics, it is not accurate. Since letterboxing has
virtually exploded in the past couple of years and there are several dozen
new boxes planted every day (as compared to a couple dozen a month when the
county coloring first began), it is nearly impossible to keep this
up-to-date and frankly I think we stopped trying to keep up with it. At
some point, the color variance should probably just be eliminated, but it
isn't at the top of the to-do list.

Even when there were fewer boxes and is was easier to stay on top of it, you
never could be sure as many mystery boxes are listed without a county and
may be in a county that otherwise appears boxless. On top of that, now that
some people list their boxes on AtlasQuest and not on LbNA, there wouldn't
be an easy way to keep up with boxes listed both places.

The best way to find out if a county has boxes is to check the regional
pages on LbNA and do a search on AtlasQuest.

SpringChick
