Goose Chaser

From: beckers410 (Mathw613@aol.com) | Date: 2007-08-13 19:39:08 UTC
As some of you know a pioneer in letterboxing in the Quad City area
was Goose Chaser. She planted nearly 20 boxes in less than a year and
her carvings are among the best that I've found.

Today is the one year anniversary of her death. You can check out the
details on the tribute page on Atlas Quest. She was a wonderful woman
and volunteered much of her time and resources to increase the
enjoyment of others.

Now a year after her death many still find her boxes, she left a
legacy for us to continue to enjoy. Our very first stamp ever was a
Goose Chaser stamp. And just last week we found the last two
remaining Goose Chaser stamps that we had yet to find.

A few of her boxes have gone missing, one in particular "Lincoln Park
Art" -- we attempted to find this one on our first day of boxing and
couldn't find it. Another boxer went to check on it and confirmed it
was missing.

This box has gone missing before, however, it was recovered. Now it
has been gone for 9 months!!

Today being the anniversary of Goose Chaser's death I wanted to stop
and visit one of her boxes. I had planned on stopping at "Tom's Tale
Tales" as there is a great view there and I could spend some time to
reflect on Goose Chaser's life and contributions.

I wont get a chance to stop by Tom's Tale Tales so I figured I would
drive by Lincoln Park -- Though we never found the stamp I knew she
was there and at one point her box was there.

As I was leaving the park I saw a couple of grounds crew out and
about and figured, "what the heck" so I stopped to ask the dreaded
question, to which I already knew the answer "what are you talking
about?"

As soon as I asked one of them said, "oh with camo. on it?" and he
went into the shed and produced Goose Chaser's "Lincoln Park Art."

I am still doing my little happy dance. I am thrilled I was able to
recover this box of Goose Chaser . . . on the anniversary of her
death no less.

On the inside cover the log book is written,

"Fellow Letterboxers, this box was placed by Goose Chaser. She passed
away on Aug. 13, 2006. Please guard this box with care and pass on
this sad news.

Thank you -- Goose Chaser's Family"

We plan on doing just that! Thank you Goose Chaser for making my day
on this sad sad anniversary.

Hart x6
