From: Lizz (lizz.glossop@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2008-04-19 11:22:57 UTC
You could call them pyramids..

when i used to work the Bingo fundraiser for my daughters school they
had all kinds of games/shapes.

postage stamp - a block of 4 in any corner of the card.
boxcar or brick - a 2 x 3 block this was sometimes limited to must
include a corner, othertimes anywhere on card as long as it met the 2
x 3.
several letters of the alphabet are able to be made, I, T, A, L, C,
etc. usually limited to being right-side up, but sometimes can be
played to any rotation.
picnic table - an x plus one completed side (all the Bs, all the os,
the top row or the bottom row)
Christmas tree - all the Ns plus the 2nd & 3rd I's and Gs. turn that
upside down using the 3rd and 4th Is and Gs and its a top or dreidel
(sp?). i guess if you look at the Christmas tree it could be a
shroom. Fungus Woman would probably like that better.

I know there are others, but can't remember them.

PPE - who will have to go play BINGO on POGO to refresh her memory. I
don't do that much anymore since i discovered Letterboxing.
