What a crazy small world

From: Gooble Gobble (gooble_gobble_3@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-01-11 12:36:12 UTC-08:00
I work part time for my father in law doing database
management at his company. I currently have a 3-4
month project where I have to call people and get
their email addresses. There are 5500 people I have to
contact, and today was a little strange. I came across
a company in IL. that we had not done business with
since 1999. Usually the person who worked for any
given company that long ago is no longer with the
company. I looked at the name of the person I was
supposed to ask for and it struck a note of
familiarity with me. The name was Chris Mothkovich,
hmm I thought, what are the odds it's who I think it
is? After my little blurb about why I was calling I
said "I have a strange question to ask you", "Are you
a letterboxer"? After a short pause there came a
resounding, "Yes I am". I then introduced myself and
we had a wonderful conversation about boxing and how
we came to be letterboxers.

What a strange coincidence. I've personally never met
or spoke with Trail Rider before but it was so cool to
have our paths cross that way. I never would have
imagined I would come across another boxer while doing
something totally unrelated to letterboxing.

Just a neat little story I thought I'd share with
everyone.


Gooble Gobble

From: Doodle & Deedle Bug (doodle_n_deedle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-01-14 06:27:17 UTC-08:00
What a fun thread! A few years ago, we had met a
local boxer who used the trailname Blaze at an area
letterbox gathering. A few months later, Deedle & I
were participating in an organized bike ride in the
Cuyahoga Valley. The ride was called "Rails n'
Trails" and you rode the Cuyahoga Valley Scenic
Railroad to Akron, and then biked back to Cleveland
along the Towpath Trail. As Deedle & I were waiting
to board the train, I tugged on his shirt and
whispered, "Hey, over there, isn't that Blaze??" Sure
enough, Blaze was also a cyclist and doing the same
tour we were!

So, does anyone else have any stories of meeting
letterboxers in a non-letterboxing setting?

Doodle Bug.

From: Stacey Bayliff (jose_quiti@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-01-14 13:26:10 UTC-08:00
I work in the local school district where I live. I
have introduced several co-workers to letterboxing.
One day I was looking at the list of new AQ members
and recognized the name as one of the secreatries in
our school. Next time I saw here I asked if it was
indeed her. She said "yes...Mrs. XYZ got me hooked on
letterboxing". I had gotten Mrs. XYZ hooked! And she
passed it along.
I also recognize lots of girls names that signed into
my logbooks because their Girl Scout Troop found the
box.
And my husband somehow figured out that the security
guard where he works is a letterboxer, but I don't
know how that conversation went.
