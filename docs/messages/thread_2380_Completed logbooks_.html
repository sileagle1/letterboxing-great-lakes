RE: [LbGLK] Completed logbooks?

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2006-04-10 20:11:54 UTC-05:00
We've only had two of our boxes that had fairly full logbooks. Since there
was enough room in the box, we just added a second logbook and left the
first one in the box for those that were interested in seeing who had been
there before them.

We also added a second logbook to the "first Wisconsin box" since when we
stamped into the first logbook, we stamped on the last page. We certainly
left the initial logbook in that box for historical posterity. (We
generally carry 1 or 2 spare logbooks with us so we can add them to a box if
needed - such as if current book is full or it is severely damaged).

It depends. I've been re-planting boxes now after the winter and so far, I
haven't kept the old logbooks in them because there's just no room. Most of
my boxes are in areas where there tend to be a lot of people around, so my
boxes for this year have gotten smaller. Not microboxes, but also not
really enough room left over for the previous two logbooks after the stamp
and new logbook go in. If possible, tho, I think keeping the old logbook in
is a nice touch for finders.

Re: [LbGLK] re: Completed logbooks?

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-04-12 13:42:58 UTC-04:00
I've done both -- if the logbook is quite small and filled quickly and there is room, I leave it in the box along with the new one as the stamps in there are quite recent. If it is larger and has tons of stamps in it and/or doesn't fit along with the new one, I take the old one home.

I have found that once they are home, I look through them and then just put them in a drawer. While I agree with the sentiment that I would hate to lose the stamps and the history, I just as strongly feel I would like to share that history with future finders of the box.

Whatever works for the particular box I guess.

SpringChick

BTW -- I see that Mark Pepe has picked up on this topic over at the Newboxers list, so if you are looking for more input and additional ideas, you will probably find input there from other areas of the country.
