I hope Spring Chick and Azobox realize just how much their little
games meant to all of us...Now that "WiHi" mentions it, we all at
some point every day for the last 100 plus days escaped from the
stress and complications that life threw at us and, even if for just
a minute, thought about the game...Each day gave us a renewed sense
of hope that we either could cross off another word or look forward
to doing better the next day. I can't think of too many other things
that got my consistent attention on a daily basis during that same
time...Well, maybe the annoying daily thought that comes around 5 PM
of, "What should I make for dinner"...But that is another topic.

I don't think any of us would have fallen for letterboxing in general
if it was all about instant gratification. Let's face it, many of
our friends and family don't get why we would spend hours pouring
over clues and puzzles that eventually may lead us on a wild goose
chase that is miles or even states away! All for the possibility of
finding a treasured piece of rubber in a plastic container that comes
with no guarantee that the box will even be there (or in our case,
that we were able to figure out the clues correctly!)

As letterboxers, we can feel good that we have discovered that the
gratification comes in the journey! But, there is the additional
hope of an EXTRA reward at the end of the trail of a box to stamp
into. Sounds a lot like the BINGO game with WOM's and more elusive
clues being a sort of side wager game....don't you think?

Congratulations to the official Bingo winners!

grasp. Our hope for you is that the snow will melt quickly!)
