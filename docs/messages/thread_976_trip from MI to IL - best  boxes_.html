From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-01-17 13:11:18 UTC-05:00
There are quite a few boxes in the Grand Haven area, but I think BindleBabe
has nabbed most of those already and that may be a bit early in your trip
for a leg-stretcher anyway. There is a 3-box series in Holland (It's Dog's
Life), although one of the boxes is currently listed as missing. I have not
done these myself as I have been waiting for the missing box to be replaced,
but they do not appear to be difficult hikes at all.

The Bird and Butterfly, also in Holland, are a longer hike, with some steep
climbs. South of that, there are a handful of boxes at Saugatuck Dunes
State Park if you have not been to those already. I did Birthday Box and
First Letterbox on the Lake in the winter with several inches of snow on the
ground. My Old Man Winter box was planted in the winter, and my Spring
Fling box should also be accessible in the snow. Azobox also has a box
there -- it may be a bit tougher in the snow, but not impossible, depending
on how much snow and how the wind has settled it. These are all medium
hikes, and if you study a park map, you will probably be able to figure out
how to nab them in one loop around the park. The Mt. Baldy box in Saugatuck
is not a good winter box -- 282 steps that are very icy in the snow.

You may also want to check on a few planted by Azobox down in the South
Haven area. It was a beautiful spring day when we were there, so I can't
say how they might be in the winter -- perhaps he has made the trip over to
check on them when there was snow on the ground? If I recall correctly,
these were medium hikes also.

You definitely do not want to do the Beach View by the Dunes box at Warren
Dunes SP on this trip. It is a very long and difficult hike. It is however
a rewarding hike (if you go into it knowing what you are getting into), so
do try to make it back down in nicer weather once you are up for a longer
hike.

I think that about covers the Michigan part of your trip.

Have fun!

SpringChick

Hi Enders Angel,

There are many accessible boxes in the Chicago area, many of them near
museums and attractions. Below, I've listed some urban letterboxes that
might fit the bill.

Fungus Woman's Brookfield boxes are near the zoo, accessible by Metra,
near refreshments and require very little walking.
MRLC: Sit...Sip...Stay...Box 1 Brookfield, IL
MRLC: Happy Apple 1 Brookfield, IL
MRLC: Hurray for Hollywood 1 Brookfield, IL - these three are near the zoo,
all drive by or take Metra to the zoo stop

Fish Out of Water 1 1 Brookfield/Riverside
MRLC: Come Grumpy, Go Happy 1 (these two are one stop earlier than the
Zoo stop).

Pico's
Historic Chicago Box #1: The Clarke House 1 Chicago, IL Great location in
historic district
Historic Chicago Box #2: The Columbian Exposition 1 Chicago, IL Not far
from the Museum of Science and Industry

Matt the Rat's
Chicago Underground 1 Chicago, IL Great box, but only if you're feeling
very, very adventurous - also, steps
Ilin 1 Chicago, IL - Drive by on your way to the big city

If you're so inclined, I may be able to join a gathering in Rockford, also. Lots
in Rockford too. In addition to what Shutterfly wrote, I recommend

151 Questions (one of my favorites)
Symbol of Rockford
I Want YOU, To Want ME! -both of these are in a city park
Peace Plaza, for its location- very easy driveby

If you want further details or recommendations, email me privately.
