Speedball cutter blades

From: beautifulpeople99 (jennifer.mccready@gmail.com) | Date: 2005-07-13 19:38:11 UTC
So, as a fairly new letterboxer (less than a month!), I went today to
buy a Speedball cutter at my local art shop. I decided to give it a
shot, since my practice rabbit stamp, carved with an exact-o blade
looked... well... let's just say the rabbit wound up without a tail and
a gaping, gory hole where his eye should have been! Anyway, the store
carried Nos. 3, 4, and 6 for the cutter so I bought them all, but I was
wondering: what do most people prefer? The store said they would be
willing to place a special order for me, but I would hate to ask them
for a No. 1 and then really never use it. Any feedback would be helpful!

Thanks,
J&BBooks





Re: [LbGLK] Speedball cutter blades

From: Gabrielle Coil (gmc81@randomkitty.net) | Date: 2005-07-13 13:39:00 UTC-07:00
The two blades I use most often are the no. 1 v-shaped and the no. 4 (?) u-shaped gouge. I use the no. 1 for most of my carving and the gouge to remove large sections of carving material (usually on the outside edges of the image although occasionally within the image if the space is particularly large). I think the no. 1 blade would be a valuable addition to your set. Depending on the amount of detail and your individual carving technique, the no. 3 might be adequate but the no. 1 allows for much greater detail. The no. 4 might also be useful, but I personally find the no. 6 to be just too big for most of my projects.

Kimono Girl

From: Kyle Klein (armadillo1111@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2005-07-13 13:57:08 UTC-07:00
I just started carving recently and bought a speedball kit. This came with a #1, #2,#3 and #5 as well as a cutter.

I use the #1 almost exclusively, only using the larger ones when I have relatively big chucks of material to get rid of.

In the comments I have read from others, people would often like something smaller than a #1 for even finer detail, so I am pretty sure you will find a #1 a good investment.

Kyle
