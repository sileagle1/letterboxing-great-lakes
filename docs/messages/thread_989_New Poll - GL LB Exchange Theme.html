From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-01-25 06:03:14 UTC-05:00
I would hope that regardless of which theme is chosen, everyone will feel
free to participate. I understand your concern about some subjects being
more difficult to carve than others, but this is not a carving contest and
even primitive can be good. One of my favorite stamps was a very simple,
outline-style lizard I found out east a couple of years ago -- carved by an
8-year old child. I think that most of us are capable of carving beyond
what we feel is within our limits, but are comfortable where we are at and
are a bit intimidated by going further than that. You might be surprised
what you can do if you just take your time and take it one cut at a time.

On the other hand, for any subject there are interpretations that would make
a simpler carving -- for instance a wildlife theme does not necessarily
dictate that you carve an image of an animal detailing every hair on his
body -- there are many suitable images to be found with little or no detail,
simply the animal outline. Or how about interpretting a wildlife theme by
carving a stamp of animal footprints instead of the actual animal? Likewise
with a person -- I had an image ready to carve of a well-known individual
who for many years spent summers at the Actor's Colony in Muskegon. I
didn't end up using it because somebody else beat me to the spot, but the
image (just a face) was quite simple -- half positive, half negative, no
detail, yet it was very clear to see who the person was.

You get the idea -- stretch the limits of what you think you are capable of,
think outside the box and interpret the theme to make it work for you --
regardless of which theme is chosen, and I am sure your carving will be
great!

SpringChick

From: Armadillo Jo (jes7o@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-01-25 03:50:06 UTC-08:00
I wholehearted agree with Spring Chick regarding
carving. I believe that most of us are capable of
carving a lot more than we think we can. I never
thought that I would be able to carve a person, but
this weekend I tried and carved a pretty good stamp of
Jean Lafitte for my upcoming New Orleans trip. I think
we all get caught up in the details instead of
remembering simplicity. A good transfer method also
helps, for Lafitte I used a 4B soft lead drawing
pencil, outlined a picture from the internet and
rubbed it on the carving medium. If I could find my
scanner, which for some reason is missing, I would
scan in a picture of it. Dang it, where did I put that
scanner?
