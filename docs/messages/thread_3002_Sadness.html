From: doubledragonletterboxer (M8KC5008@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-08-25 13:26:52 UTC
I don't really care how many boxes I find (some of you maybe aghast
with horror). I still keep track, but when I get to 100, I get to 100.
I just like the journy along the way. I like puzzle boxes the most,
because it showes the most creativity. Someone took time to make it
fun, not just turn here 50 paces. That is also what I like about
placing a box, the challenge to come up with a new and interesting
concept to the game. Placing a box, for me, is not enough. I like to
make it interactive. Also finding a neat and interesting place to put
it, somewhere you (as a finder) might not have ever thought to go. We
have gone to some beautiful places that I will remember the rest of my
life, all thanks to some special boxers. If the game ever turns into
how many boxes can you get or how many boxes can you place, I don't
think I would like to play any more. It would become meaningless and
unchallenging.
Double Dragon

From: Doodle & Deedle Bug (doodle_n_deedle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-08-25 06:52:42 UTC-07:00
Here here. As for our count, I have no idea any more
- we lost track somewhere after 100. One day we'll
count up all our images again, but again, the count
doesn't really matter to us. We like visiting the
places, being outdoors, the thrill of finding the box
especially after we've been scratching our heads going
"It must be here someplace!!" for ten minutes, reading
through the logs to read comments and say, "Oh,
so-and-so was at this box once!" And I've said before
that one of the reasons we love letterboxing so much
is because of all the places we've been that we never
would have thought to visit had we not been seeking
out that box. Of course, though, we (I) also have
that "got to catch them all!" fever and so if it's
planted, I want to find it, no matter if it's a quick
drive-by or a 5 mile hike.

And like DD, we want our boxes to be a little
interactive, too. Not that drive-bys and
straight-forward clues are bad - sometimes those boxes
are just what you need after a run of no-finds to
renew your belief that you CAN still hunt these things
out. But one of the reasons we have so few plants
right now is because I've got some great ideas for new
boxes, just haven't had a chance to implement them
just yet. My goal is to one day have a box that's so
"wow" it makes the spotlight on Mark & Sue Pepe's
website ;)

Doodle Bug.
