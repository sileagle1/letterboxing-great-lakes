From: (KarenBeid@aol.com) | Date: 2007-03-18 18:31:53 UTC-04:00
FlyGirl409 here,
I thought I would report in on our first real letterboxing trip. CMB (my
hubby of 20+ years) and I had to take our daughter to Merom for a retreat
weekend anyway and our son was out of town on a school activity so we planned a
weekend away too. We decided to see how many of the Evansville, IN boxes we
could find in a short amount of time. This was so much fun that I think CMB
is now hooked and says he may have to rethink the name I assigned him and come
up with a signature stamp design of his own now.

We found 13 boxes on Sat. 3/17 and just one today, Sun. 3/18 since we were
in a hurry to get back to pick our kid up on time. All finds have been logged
already too. There were so many cool carved stamps and great hiding places
too. We barely got about half of those in the general area so already know
we need to plan another trip to the area to get the rest...maybe daughter will
go there for summer camp too...hmmm...

After this weekend we feel we almost know dream a dream since we found 10 of
her boxes, 3 by Toadfrog and one by TouchTrek. We learned a lot more about
where to hide them, what to hide them in and how to write the clues. The
trip has inspired up to put on our thinking caps and see what box ideas we can
come up with on our own.

We also went to Hobby Lobby in Evansville and got some MasterCarve to try
carving on and some other types of carving tools and stamp pads too. I had
call a Hobby Lobby here in Indy asking if they carried supplies to carve your
own stamp and was told they didn't carry anything like that so either they don't
or the person I got on the phone is clueless. But that store lost a big sale
because they didn't know what they had or didn't have it.

I think I also have my niece who is a young single parent of 2 twin boys (8)
and a girl (almost 12) excited about trying out letterboxing too as it could
be a very rewarding and inexpensive way for them to have some family
bonding/fun time together.

We noticed a variety of box types and log book sizes and styles too. Our
personal stamps were barely small enough at about 2" square to fit into some of
the smaller boxes. So we are wondering if some boxers have different sized
personal sig stamps to take this log book size issue into account since we
saw some logged in that were much bigger than ours and others that were very
small. Many of the stamps in the boxes we found we not mounted at all and we
wondered how long those last or if they tend to crack or curl.
Many stamps were wrapped in paper towels, then inside zip top bags and we
thought that was a great idea.

Also we are currently using a very small log book to stamp our finds into
that is barely 4 inches square. Is there a recommended size on log books or
will any plain paper notebook work?

I know I have lots of questions, but until this weekend we didn't know
enough to ask any so I am making up for it now.

Thanks for all the help this group has given us so far. We have felt very
welcomed and enjoyed our short time here so far.
Karen aka FlyGirl409

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2007-03-18 23:36:39 UTC
Thanks for the interesting report - it sounds like you had a great
day and that you are becoming as addicted as the rest of us! It's
nice to hear that both you & your spouse enjoy it - that means you'll
probably be able to get out fairly often.

I'm sure there will be lots of people answering your questions, so
you'll get lots of input. Here's mine:

We read a lot before we started letterboxing and therefore realized
we should have fairly small signature stamps if we wanted to stamp in
some of the smaller logbooks. Ours are about 1.5" x 1.5" or so.
However I have seen other people with various sizes of the same
image. We don't want to carry a bunch of stamps (need the space in
our pack for all those colored markers and ink pads!), so we just use
our original small size stamp for everything.

We prefer finding stamps that are unmounted since we can usually get
a better image from them. Many times we have encountered stamps with
wood backings that have warped and we get a poor image. We have
learned that on wood backed stamps we may need to press from
underneath the paper, and press the paper ONTO the stamp in order to
get an impression. However it still isn't as sharp as if you pressed
down on the stamp from above on an unwarped surface (we tend to get
some paper movement with this technique, causing some
ghosting/shadowing, but it is still better than missing entire
sections of a stamp because the wood has warped and pulled a section
up). This is especially a problem on larger stamps. We've also seen
a variety of other mounting material, but truthfully we are happiest
(have the best stamping results) if the stamp is not mounted at all.
We've found over 2,000 boxes and I think we've only come across 2 or
3 stamps that had cracked/broken for some reason.

I'd recommend at least a 5"x7" logbook, or perhaps a 6"x8", based on
the size of some of the stamps we have found over the past 3.5
years. It's best to have something with heavier smooth paper so the
ink does not bleed through and you get a nice image. We made the
mistake initially of getting logbooks with textured paper.
