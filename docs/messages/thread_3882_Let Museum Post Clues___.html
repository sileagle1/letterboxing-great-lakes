I would like some words of wisdom about this. One of our Underground
RR boxes is in Lombard, IL near a tiny little museum (which is
actually the homestead of the person that the letterbox is about).
The clues start you out at the homestead, where you need to get some
info from outside signs. Then you use these numbers in your clues to
find the box, which isn't a terrible distance away.

I got an email today from someone (a volunteer?) at the homestead,
wondering if they could put some information about this letterbox in
the museum. I haven't replied yet, because I wanted to get some
other opinions. This museum is really small and it is only open Tues
and Sat. afternoons (so I don't know how much traffic they get). Am
I asking for trouble (i.e. a soon-to-be missing box) if I let this
museum post the clues? I really don't know if they could give some
of the "rules" of letterboxing (stealth, rehide it well...)along with
the clues, which makes me a little leary. Then I thought, maybe I
could just have the box in the museum, but the hours are so limited
that this might not work so well.

Is there some way to work with them, without posting the clues??

Anyway, any thoughts....

I had forgotten that that box was even there. The area right now is pretty
bad with litter and trash because of how heavily the area is used by kids.
Some folks in the area have kind of been calling to see if the Sheriff's
dept might be agreeable to taking a jail work crew out there and doing some
spring clean up. There's also a crew of Scouts that might come out and do
some spring clean up. And our landscaping company also said they might be
agreeable to going outside the fence and doing some. None of that will
probably come to pass but just a note of caution to keep an eye on the box
as we get into springtime because if anyone is picking up trash in that
area, they might not look that carefully at what they toss into their
garbage bags. Anyone who finds the box needs to rehide it really well and
not leave it laying out or within sight.

Personally, I don't know as if it'd be any too good for the box for the
museum to post the clues. The box is too close to the museum, it's not a
long walk at all, and I think folks are likely to go follow the clues and
check it out without doing any studying up at all about the game. Maybe the
museum, rather than directly giving out the clues, could let folks know that
there's a "game" attached to the museum, and have the museum direct folks to
letterboxing.org instead? That way folks might wander into a little
research on letterboxing before they wander over to find the box. I'd be
afraid of teachers taking classes of kids, or parents taking groups of
children, to follow the clues after they finish at the museum because it'd
be another good "learning tool" for math and such without realizing the need
for discretion and rehiding well. Especially in the springtime. And where
the box is hidden is well-travelled by kids on foot and on bike -- they're
likely to want to show the box to their pals without realizing the danger
they'd be putting the box in.

On the other hand, you can always recarve the stamp and redo the box if any
of the above should happen. And maybe the museum doesn't get that much
traffic anyway as far as field trips. I would kind of think it would be a
popular place with the local schools, but maybe not.
