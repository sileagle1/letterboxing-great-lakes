One simple thing you can try if you have MS Word is to:

1. Insert the picture picture file into Word,
2. Right click on the picture
3. Choose Format Picture
4. Under Image Control, Color: choose Black & White (You can then play
with brightness and contrast settings to get closer to what you want)
5. You can also "crop" an image within MS Word if you want to only use
a portion of a picture.

Not as good as actual graphics software, but sometimes works pretty
well.

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-11-08 20:10:46 UTC-05:00
I use PaintShop Pro but I imagine you could get similar results with other graphic editing softwares. It takes some tweaking and playing around to get the best contrast, but the basic steps I use:

1) Increase contrast VERY high so there is a clear delineation between dark and light -- adjust brightness as necessary if everything is too light or too dark.
2) Split channels to RGB -- this will give you three different copies of your image -- a red, a green and a blue -- choose the one that is closest to a black and white (pay attention to which areas are black and white). Usually the green is closest to a black and white, but sometimes it will be the red or the blue, depending on the colors in the original picture.
3) Now decrease the colors to 2 (black and white) to remove the gray tones.

SpringChick
