Deedle & I have a question about hitchhikers that we don't remember reading an answer to previously...when you find a hitchhiker and then take it to a new home, is it customary to stamp the new letterbox's journal with the hitchhiker? Re: Question About Hitchhikers

From: (duncan53211@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-05-09 14:30:43 UTC
Generally yes.

The hitchhiker's log book should typically be stamped with your
personal stamp, the stamp of the box it is found in (if the hider
did not do it), and the stamp of the box you hide it in.

You should also stamp the HH stamp into your logbook.

The box's logbook, both where you find it and where you place it,
should also be stamped with your personal stamp and the HH stamp.

There are some exceptions to this general setup however. If the
logbook of the HH is small, sometimes the HH owner will tell finders
to only stamp the HH log book with only your personal stamp OR the
stamp of the box, not both, to save room on the HH logbook. This is
the case with the HH I currently have in my possession.

Dragon

From: Rayvenhaus (rayvenhaus@myndworx.com) | Date: 2004-05-09 07:44:18 UTC-07:00
Yes it is actually. Here's the normal sequence of events for hiding,
handling and moving a hitchhiker:

1) While out casually letterboxing one day, you stumble across a Hitchhiker
in the letterbox you've just uncovered.
2) The Happy Letterboxing "I've discovered a Hitchhiker" dance ensues!
3) Check to make sure that the Hitchhiker was stamped into the letterbox
that you discovered it in.
4) You stamp into the Hitchhiker's log book and take it with you, if you so
choose to do so.
4) When you've found the perfect letterbox to place it in, you stamp it into
that letterbox, wrap it up oh so gently and place it in the letterbox for
the next lucky person to find and repeat!


IHTH,

Steve - Tam Rayvenhaus and new member to this list.

Re: Question About Hitchhikers

From: Deb (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-05-09 15:23:24 UTC
And adding my two cents...

When you get home, if so requested by the person who placed the HH,
please take a moment to drop them an e-mail to let them know the
status of the little traveller, or perhaps post your find to the HH
Yahoo list and/or send an update to Pete and Wanda for the HH
tracking page they maintain.

The biggest complaint people have about HH is that they plant them
and then never hear of them again. I am sure many of these HH are
just fine and are happily travelling the country, and are not
actually lost as the placer fears.

It only takes a minute to send off a quick note to the placer and it
is very much appreciated!

But do be aware, there are a few folks who do not want to hear the
status of their HH or have it posted on the HH list -- in which case
please respect their request.

SpringChick
