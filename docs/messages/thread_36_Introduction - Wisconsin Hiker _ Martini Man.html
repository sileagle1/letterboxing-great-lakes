Introduction - Wisconsin Hiker & Martini Man

From: wi_letterboxer (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2004-03-27 21:13:01 UTC
Letterboxing names: Wisconsin Hiker & Martini Man
Real names: Debbie & Jon

We both have been active in a local hiking club for over 6 years and
like to take hiking vacations. We also enjoy puzzles and for years
have had a "page-a-day" calendar with a puzzle that we look
at over dinner. Therefore when a friend from our hiking club sent an
email about letterboxing in May of 2003, I was immediately
intrigued. It combined several things we liked and my research of
the main website revealed that there were several boxes nearby
(primarily thanks to "The Dragon"). I did more reading and
research
using the Internet and was anxious to get started. I designed our
signature stamps and had them custom made (didn't want to rely on
my untried carving skills).

The first box we looked for (and found) was in the Madison, WI area
in July. We went out in shorts and sandals for our first few
searches, but eventually realized this was not a good plan - lots of
scratches and shoes falling off! We've been out on the hunt
every
month since then, having lots of fun along the way, and also
introducing some nieces & nephews to the activity. Our niece
actually chose to write a paper about letterboxing for one of her 5th
grade assignments (she got an "A"). In addition to Wisconsin,
we've
found boxes in Illinois, Wyoming, and Hawaii.

Of course I started talking about LBing to our friends, relatives,
hiking club members, and casual acquaintances. Most thought we were
nuts, but a few were also captivated by the concept and have joined
in the fun. In an attempt to create more interest in the Milwaukee
area, I gave a brief presentation at a local outdoor store in March
2004. Working together with "The Dragon", we used this
occasion as
the basis for the first Wisconsin gathering and were pleased to meet
the other local letterboxers that matched the stamps we had been
seeing.

The first boxes we placed were in Brookfield, WI (a suburb of
Milwaukee) and included custom-made stamps. However I finally
started carving this month and was fairly pleased with how the stamps
turned out, so we now have 2 boxes with hand-carved stamps.

For only having a small group in Wisconsin, I'm very happy to
report that there are many types of clues and locations. I
think "The Dragon" has been very creative in trying a huge
variety of things with his boxes and that has been a great example
for those of us who started more recently.

We're looking forward to meeting more of you in the Great Lakes
area, hopefully at the spring gathering if the date works out!

Wisconsin Hiker
