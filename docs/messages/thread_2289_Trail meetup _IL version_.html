From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2006-03-09 21:37:31 UTC-06:00
Speaking of trail meet-ups, heres a belated report on our letterboxing
weekend last Saturday & Sunday:



We started off early Saturday morning to find Snowy Evening, a new box
placed in Milwaukee by Fermont (first box planted!). It was a sunny, Snowy
MORNING as we wound our way along the trail, boots crunching in the snow.
It was good to be out on a sunny day.



Next we hopped in the car for a longer drive down to Wauconda, IL for All
the Worlds a Stage #1: Kindergarten and Singing Hills Forest Preserve
(saw a hawk soaring overhead in a brilliant blue sky}. Both boxes were in
fine shape. Next it was over to Volo for LCAS. They were doing some
controlled burning at the bog which was interesting. When we walked on the
boardwalk to the deck at the center of the bog we could hear the ice
cracking beneath us a sure sign that things were warming up and spring was
on the way. We had our coats off or unzipped and didnt need any mittens.



Next was a visit with Ichabod. A nice spot to stamp in a table and a
view, it doesnt get much better than that! Lots of geese honking away at
this spot. (I found out later that the placer of this box, Domedreamer from
Connecticut, is a relative of the Cottontails and we may get a chance to
meet them when we are in CT in September.)



After that it was on to the new mecca of letterboxing Elgin, IL! We found
Stick a Fork in Me and the Keith Haring boxes, all excellent carvings. At
this park we met KissMeKate and Sylvie Rose La Fee and of course exchanged
stamp images and chitchat. On our way back to the car we ran into the
Cottontails with friends Cake Baker and K8E. More exchanges and chitchat!
We barely had time to wrap up the day with The Cottontails Easter Egg
Hunt. We had to be patient here since someone was practice driving in
the parking lot, driving in circles over and over. Arrgh! Just when we
thought they were gone, they were back AGAIN! We had heard there had been a
lot of LBing activity here in the morning, but we didnt meet anyone (but we
think we passed Cmoon while driving from one park to the other.). The
stamps were very nice, as were the stamp containers. It was on this series
that we reached our F1200 mark (but not at the same rate as that couple who
found 800 in one year!). We also realized we had had one of those rare
days we found every single box we looked for!



We then decided to call it a day and headed over to Glenview for dinner and
a hotel stay. We were up early the next morning to go out on the hunt for
the nine boxes KiddyWriter had recently placed (also nice carvings). Unlike
Saturday, the morning was overcast and by the time we found the ninth box a
few snowflakes were floating down. So much for spring!



Next was Grosse Point Light. When we arrived there was a pretty bluish
hue on the horizon, but by the time we were done stamping in everything was
white/gray as the snow started up more seriously. We decided we had time to
detour over to Buffalo Grove/Palatine to try Buffalo Creek (found) and
Spring Window (didnt find). The snow was now wet and heavy, so out came
the umbrellas, especially needed for the stamping in process.



Then it was on to our final destination, Lake Forest/Lake Bluff. Beetle had
asked us to retrieve her Beatles boxes, so we didnt have to worry about
stamping in out in the snow. Instead we simply collected the boxes and
carried them along with us. This was a very pretty walk with all the new
snow clinging to the branches as we walked along the river/creek. We
decided we had time for one more box, so we headed for Sunrise Beach. It
was funny tromping along a snow covered sand beach in a snowstorm! I dont
think weve ever been to a beach in those conditions before The clues had
mentioned a shelter, so thats where we brought the box to stamp in, and
then returned it to its hidey hole.



Our last stop was the Full Moon restaurant where we had made plans to meet
Beetle for dinner. In the parking lot we opened all the boxes we had
collected and had a Beatles Reunion in the car as we stamped in. Great
stamps! We then turned them all over to Beetle when she arrived. We spent
an enjoyable hour or two chatting with her over dinner before we headed
north to home.



All in all, a GREAT weekend! Thanks to all of you who plant boxes and make
these adventures possible!



Wisconsin Hiker & Martini Man
