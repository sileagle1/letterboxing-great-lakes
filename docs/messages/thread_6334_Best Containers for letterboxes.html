From: Atom118 (Aatom118@gmail.com) | Date: 2010-02-10 12:00:40 UTC-06:00
Think about how you are going to plant the boxes. Will it be someplace
very wet? Could rocks or branches fall on them? Will they be in direct
sun and get very hot/cold? Are there lots of animals around that might
chew on the box?

While cheap boxes are inexpensive, they also tend to be cheap in
quality. You might want to consider how heartbroken, if at all, you
will be if you box gets destroyed.

Just a thought.

Oh, an from experience, don't use old food containers unless they are
VERY well cleaned.

Atom118 (a fan of the press and lock boxes)

And now for something different....
We have been using LnL's for some time and like them, but they are a bit pricey. I've always liked the idea of a screwtop container as the seal would be pretty secure, easy to use, and weathertight. But... where to find one that's large enough and is inexpensive?
Well, I just got back from the hardware store, and as I was walking down the housewares aisle to get to the hardware section I spotted plastic storeage tops for Mason jars. Eight of the wide mouth tops were $6.99. They wouldn't rust or corrode and a pint wide mouth might be a pretty good size for a LB. Yes, I know they're glass, but how many times have have you seen a really old, unbroken beer bottle out in the middle of nowhere? Sure, there are broken ones too, but if some can last out in the open and unprotected for years, how long might one survive in a safe place? Of course I wouldn't recommend using one where the clues use that hateful phrase, "Look under the rock."
Just a thought...
Merlin

From: Kathi Krankoski (krank@ameritech.net) | Date: 2010-02-11 15:20:05 UTC-08:00
Something that I don't think has ever been mentioned, but when closing Lock'n'lock (or similar) type boxes, you should always close opposite sides together (i.e., both long flaps, then both short flaps, rather than one at a time going around the circumference of the box). It prevents torquing, bending, and misaligning the lids. (I actually learned this little tidbit through my day job...we use super heavy duty boxes with similar latches as anaerobic chambers, and that's what the instructions say to do.)
