From: baliddle (baliddle@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2008-05-19 12:28:02 UTC-04:00
Hi!
I'm looking for some opinions on an issue. Last year, I had a couple
temporary boxes placed in great hiding spots.

Now, I'd like to plant permanent boxes in the same spots. They would
be completely different clues, stamps, etc. But, anyone who found the
temp box may experience deja vu.

I love both of these spots and there were limited hiding spaces, so
I'm thinking I may have to reuse the spot if I wish to plant there again.

What do you think? If you were the finder would you be disappointed?
Any thoughts would be appreciated!

Thanks,

Re: [LbGLK] New box, old spot... opinion?

From: (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2008-05-19 11:55:27 UTC-05:00
My opinion: If it's a different box(stamp) and the clues are different, than it would be a new box to me, even if I had found something else there before. It would be the same situation of a box disappeared, either intentionally or due to animals/muggles, and someone else planted a box in the same spot.

Searchers may recognize the general area from the new clues, and they can then decide if they'd like to revisit the spot. We're going to be in Pennsylvania for a few days of hiking and noticed that there are several new boxes on some trails we've hiked before. Since we enjoyed the hike to Pulpit Rock and the Pinnacle, we'll be heading up again to hunt for the boxes. (But we probably won't remember if any of them are in the SAME spot as a box we found a few years ago.)

Wisconsin Hiker

Actually, I've had a finder get in touch with me to thank me on an issue
like this. :-) I had one Limited Edition Christmas Box (2004) one year
that had some rather bizarre clues. Only two boxers ever figured out the
clues and found the box. Since that box was retired and it was a great spot
with what I thought were great clues, I used the same hiding spot and same
clues. One of the two finders has since moved out of the area, but the
other one got in touch to say thankyouthankyouthankyou. :-)

So that was one finder who wasn't disappointed. It hasn't happened to me
as a finder, but if it did, no, it wouldn't really bother me. It's not THAT
big of a deal. There are better things to flame placers over. :-)
