The Great Lakes Letterbox Exchange

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-01-22 13:39:10 UTC

We've had several inches of snow overnight here in Michigan and it
is still coming down heavy. A good day to carve. Of course my mind
is spinning through possibilities for a "Mo' Music in Motown" box
for this year's gathering, but also I have a few other projects
brewing.

One is a project I would like to put out to the members of this
list. It is actually an idea I got from another letterboxing list I
am a member of and thought it would be fun to do here since many of
us are whiling away winter hours carving.


The Great Lakes Letterbox Exchange:

What it is: A themed letterbox swap among members of the Great
Lakes Letterbox group.

How it works: Each person interested in participating is given the
name of another participant. They then create a letterbox based on
the theme to send to that person. The recipient is responsible for
choosing a hiding spot, planting the box, writing and posting clues
and providing ongoing box maintenance. In return, each participant
will receive a box from another player whose identity will remain a
mystery until the box arrives in the mail. The person you are
creating a box for will not be the same person you receive a box
from.

A few ground rules:

1) You must agree to meet the dates for the swap. Dates Are As
Follows:

Submit theme ideas: January 22-23
Voting on the theme: January 25-29
Sign-ups: January 29-February 5
Info to participants re: who they are creating for: February 15
Boxes mailed to recipients: April 15
Boxes planted and clues posted: June 1

2) The box you create should fit the theme of the swap.

3) You will be sending your assigned recipient a complete letterbox -
- stamp and logbook in a weatherproof container, labeled as a
letterbox.

4) Stamps must be hand-carved -- you do not need to be a stamp-
carver extraordinaire -- crude and/or primitive stamps are sometimes
the best finds... but the stamp must be hand-carved.

5) To make it easier to hide the boxes, please try to keep
containers no larger than about 6" x 6" in size.

6) Boxes must be planted in traditional letterbox style -- they
cannot be turned into Postal Letterboxes and should involve a modest
hike (at least a 1/4 mile).

7) Clues can be any style, but to ensure that nobody's efforts just
lay in hiding, never again to be found, they should not be total
mystery boxes -- clues should give at minimum a city and site name.
Beyond that, creativity and conundrum are encouraged.

8) Boxes must be posted to the letterboxing.org clue site once they
have been planted and clues must be accessible either at
letterboxing.org or on a personal web site which does not require
membership or a password for access. If clues are on a personal web
site, they still must be listed at letterboxing.org, with a link to
your web site.

9) Acknowledgement must be made in the clues as to whom the creator
of the box is.

10) Anyone who is a member of this group is eligible to participate,
regardless of geographic location.


The Theme? I am open to suggestions... I will take ideas over the
weekend and then post a poll of all suggestions received. The poll
will be open to voting next week and the theme receiving the most
votes will be it. ONCE A THEME HAS BEEN SET, I will take sign-ups
(watch for further details on the information to send me when
signing up).

So... First step -- get me your theme ideas before Sunday at
midnight. You can either send them to me private e-mail or post the
list (suggesting a theme does not obligate you to participate).

SpringChick
