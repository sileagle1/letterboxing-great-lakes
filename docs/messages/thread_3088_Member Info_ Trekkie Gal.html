Hi, I'm Trekkie Gal. I heard about letterboxing from my co-worker,
Well Heeled, about a year and a half or so ago. At the time, it
seemed like an interesting concept, but not something I was planning
on starting. Since I am single and have no children, I didn't
really want to go tromping around the woods by myself. Then in
June, Well Heeled told me about virtual letterboxing. I immediately
like the concept because it involved trivia and sitting at my
computer, two things which I enjoy. I signed up on Atlas Quest and
did about 25 virtuals in 2 days.

After a few weeks, Well Heeled took me to a park near our office to
find my first traditional box. After that I got a little more
interested in the actual letterboxes, but I still didn't think I
would be any good at carving. I have ZERO creative ability.
Finally I did buy some carving materials and tools and used an
acetone transfer for the image. It came out ok, and was
surprisingly easy to do. Since then, I have planted 1 traditional
box and a hitchhiker, made a personal traveller, and am involved in
7 postal rings (my favorite aspect of letterboxing thus far). I
have also found several more traditional boxes - a couple in urban
locations and a couple by tromping around in the woods...by
myself. :)
