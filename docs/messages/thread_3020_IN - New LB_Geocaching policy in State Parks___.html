IN - New LB/Geocaching policy in State Parks!!!

From: ruhlette (ruhlette@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-08-29 07:56:18 UTC-07:00
Wisconsin should not feel alone. I received an email from the IN-DNR's
interpreter for Pokagon SP less than a week ago. Seems *someone* placed a
mystery box there. There is a new Geocaching policy for Indiana, and
letterboxing is covered under the same Bulletin #46.

We've exchanged several informative and friendly emails. There is a limit
to the number allowed in the park and they must be permitted. I told him
I would go and check on the mystery box and decide whether or not to keep
it there. He said he enjoys geocaching and that letterboxing was
something he would be interested in, when he found some time. Wouldn't
you know, he went to look for mystery box! He didn't find it, but the
terrain evidently is not as it was when it was placed. He told me where
he looked, but he was not in the right spot, so there is hope that it may
still be there. I will be in the area sometime in September or early
October and I will be picking it up and deciding whether to apply for a
permit. Seems silly to do so, now that the mystery is solved.

But on the positive side ... I asked about how receptive Pokagon SP would
be for an event/gathering in 2-3 years. Knowing there was a geocaching
event there, I asked about a possible letterboxing event. I suggested
asking for permit for a number of Temporary boxes (3-7 days). It sounds
like we might be able to get approval for this. I plan to continue
talking with him and the other park personnel about this. I hope we can
show them how environmentally conscious we are as a group, and how it
would promote the great park Pokagon is.

I will keep you updated as I learn more.
