Idiot Girls Go Hiking!

From: Armadillo JMO (jes7o@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-08-02 19:43:41 UTC-07:00
Well, it was a full weekend of letterboxing for
Armadillo JMO. On Saturday I headed towards Fort Wayne
to check out the Johnny Appleseed Series. 1 for 3. Was
not able to find boxes 1 and 2 because I was looking
in the wrong place (and knew it!) It was hot and I had
a 9 months pregnant friend along, who was VERY excited
about letterboxing and kept trying to get into the
woods, where I was searching for #2. Since I didn't
want to exhaust her too much...and she was scaring the
crap out of me hiking around in unbuckled Chacos...we
decided to try again when it was A: less hot and B:
she was less pregnant.
On Sunday, I was bored and decided to go to Warren
Dunes State Park, MI to go after the "Beach View at
the Dunes" box. Lord almighty. Beautiful, but hellish
hike. I'm not a big fan of sand dunes to begin with,
and now we have an even more strained relationship.
Heat exhaustion. UG! I did live, however, and found
the box rather easily (even though we weren't on the
original trail!). Collected some awesome pebbles and
sand glass on the beach and saw the sunset. It was
worth the pain!

Let me just take this opportunity to remind the
newbies and everyone else to CARRY WATER, lots of it!
In my haste, I left the water in the car and ended up
drinking water out of Lake Michigan (which,
incidentally, was fairly clean and yummy). In case I
die of e-coli poisoning tomorrow, I stress to
you....BRING WATER!!!

...and if anyone would like to know the SHORTCUT to
finding this box, e-mail me off list...because there
is an alternate (easier) way to get to it than over
the sand dune and through the woods...

Armadillo JMO...out

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-08-02 23:52:44 UTC-04:00
I feel your pain regarding the box at Warren Dunes State Park. The very
same thing happened to Old Shoe and me when we hunted that one. There
really is no indication as to how long the hike is from the clues; actually
it appears to be a rather short hike. WRONG! Long and grueling. We too
left the water in the car and were dying of thirst halfway to the box. So I
echo your advice about bringing water. But like you said, it was a nice
stamping in spot and worth it in the end. (Was the pregnant friend with you
on this hike too?)

Regarding your offer of a shortcut... If you wish to discuss alternate
routes to the box with other letterboxers, that is your option. I do
however ask that neither the discussion nor the offer are mentioned publicly
on this list. Somebody went through some effort to make these clues and for
whatever reason, they laid out a certain route to their box, making for a
challenging hike. If you as a letterboxer discover a shortcut while on the
trail or know the area well enough to have the inside info on the back way
in, that is great for you -- figuring out a shortcut using a map and sense
of direction can be a challenging and fun part of a letterbox hike.
However, it is disrespectful of the original placer to publicly offer this
to other letterboxers. Think about from the perspective of it being your
box... more than likely you would like people to follow your clues to the
box, after all that is the reason you wrote them, right? If one or two
people know or figure out a shortcut, no problem... but if somebody
discovers a shortcut and then begins passing along the shorter, alternate
route to your box to other letterboxers, that is discouraging.

Thanks!

SpringChick

I went there on July 31st and found the spot where
Johnny's Love was *supposed* to be....but had bad

easily seen. There WAS a lot of trash around the spot,
but nothing indicating that the box was mauled by
drunks slinging MD 20/20 bottles (the majority of the
trash consisted of liquor bottles). I'll let you know
if I do find it.

In other news, I went for a casual hike yesterday and
had an unfortunate incident involving my little dog
and one insane woodchuck/rodent/otter/whatever. No one
was hurt, but it was enough to rattle me. The little
dog didn't seem to care. The only way to process my
grief was, of course, to make a stamp commemorating
the event. This will be a mystery box with a rather
hilarious story and stamp. It will be planted this
weekend sometime.

Letterboxing...therapy for when animals attack!
