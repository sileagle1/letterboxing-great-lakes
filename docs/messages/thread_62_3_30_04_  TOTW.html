Many of the other posts mentioned most of the key items in our
letterboxing backpack. Instead of a ground cloth, we carry a couple
of stadium cushions that have been retired from regular season use.
These are the rectangular variety, which help the pack keep its shape
without adding too much weight or bulk. The extra padding is
appreciated by the knees or backside when the ground is wet, the rock
is pointy, or the stump is crumbly. We pack out whatever we pack in,
but were also adding a bag to pack out the litter we see along the
way.

We learned about letterboxing when Wisconsin Hiker e-mailed the local
hiking group about it. We checked out some of the LbNA materials and
decided to give it a try. One of the local boxes is close to home
and we had a commercial stamp on hand that was just right for us, so
we matched the stamp with a name and off we went. Our first find
included an Azobox hitchhiker, which encouraged us to continue
letterboxing. In the future, our letterboxing adventures may inspire
us to carve our own signature stamp or design a custom stamp. Were
not ready to replace our signature stamp, but we have carved a couple
of stamps, adopted a couple of boxes, planted one box, and have plans
for a few more stamps and boxes.

Thanks to SpringChick for starting the regional group and the great
logo stamp!
