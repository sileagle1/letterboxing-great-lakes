The current concept of Artist Tradings Cards was begun by an artist in
Zurich back in 1997. But it actually goes waaaaay back hundreds of years
ago when artists made very tiny individual works of art to give out when
they visited royalty, nobility, or any sort of wealthy "patron" to drum up
business. In other words, they were really the first business cards.

ATCs measure exactly 2 1/2 inches by 3 1/2 inches, no exceptions. They can
be any medium, some artists even make 3 dimensional ones, but for the sake
of storing them conveniently, most artists make them flat, or textured and
embellished but close to flat -- so that they can fit into a plastic sleeve
for safe keeping, kind of like a baseball card or collectible postcard does.

The original idea of ATCs was to NOT sell them in any way, shape, or form --
that was considered unethical (and, by purists, still is). They were for
trading between artists and for giving as gifts. ACEOs were later developed
(Artists Cards, Editions and Originals) in order to by-pass the ethics of
not selling ATCs. ACEOs can be sold. ACEOs can also be prints and limited
editions. As far as ACEOs go, artists often sell what they make themselves
and folks who buy them from artists or who might inherit them, etc. will
sell them. Now, however, the line has been blurred by many folks and ATCs
are often sold with the seller having no qualms about doing it.

If you want to see a ton of examples, go to ebay and do a search for ACEO or
ATC. Under the category, click on "art" because otherwise, if you leave it
on "all categories", it'll bring up a whole bunch of Honda ATC stuff. :-)
Some of the artwork is absolutely incredible.
