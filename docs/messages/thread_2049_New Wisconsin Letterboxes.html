From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-11-28 16:32:14 UTC
I think in the situation you are referring to, the placer planted the
box and made notes as to the location but then misplaced them before
writing up and posting the clues. I have done this myself a few times
only to find them months later. A couple time I had to actually go
back and retrace the trail to the box, making new notes.

There is also the case of "lost" clues for boxes that were placed some
time ago and clues were posted to the old letterboxing.org website, but
never made it over to the new letterboxing.org site when the transition
took place a couple of years ago. This most likely would have been
because the boxes were unclaimed at the time of the transition. So
even though the boxes are in place and some people may have old copies
of the clues, the clues are not on the main clue sites -- some people
refer to the these as "lost" clues. You can access the old web site
and also the letterbox basement by following links in the LINKS section
of this list.

SpringChick
