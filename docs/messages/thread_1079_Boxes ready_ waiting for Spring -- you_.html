Boxes ready, waiting for Spring -- you?

From: bindlestiff_boxer (bindlestiff_boxer@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-03-17 12:33:12 UTC

Counted up all the boxes we have waiting to be placed (17) the other
night, then counted up all the stamps that I already have carved (22)
and realized I've got a bad case of 'I-miss-letterboxing'

Who else has a stash of boxes just waiting to go?

I've been carving all winter so I'll have boxes to place along our
cruising route this summer (it gets hard to do much detail work when
under sail sometimes) However, I'm missing boxing so much, I'm afraid
I'll want to run right out and place them all at once. LOL

The launch day for our boat is in the first week of April, but some of
the ice-fishermen have said White Lake still has almost a foot of ice
in some places! Geez oh pizza ... I thought it was bad last year when
we had to break through one of those thin-skin of ice layers that
comes from it getting really cold overnight. (Like the mudpuddles that
freeze over, then you jump on them in the morning while your waiting
for the school bus) I wonder if the Coast Guard ice-breaker MACKINAW
is available? LOL

Enjoy!
---PJ
aka the frozen Bindle Babe

Re: Boxes ready, waiting for Spring -- you?

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-03-17 17:11:02 UTC

I have about a dozen boxes ready to go out -- a couple of these have
been ready to go for over a year, I just haven't got back to the
place, and another 25+ stamps carved up waiting to go into boxes. I
need to retire some of my current boxes first though. One person
can only maintain so many boxes. Last year I spent way more time
doing box maintenance and checking out "not found" reports than
scouting new locations and hunting newly placed boxes.

Hoping to get out for a few hours on Saturday -- I have really been
itching to get back on the trail. I went out hiking this winter
twice, but neither included new letterbox hunts, just checkups on
existing boxes. Saturday probably will be more of the same, but
still a welcome walk in the woods.

Sunday afternoon I have a family birthday party thing over in
Grandville and if the weather cooperates I will probably stop
afterward to pick up the Turkey Day series to retire them. So, this
is your last chance if you have not done these boxes... 5 boxes
along a 1 mile paved pathway. The boxes were planted in the late
fall, so they are definitely winter accessible.

SpringChick

Re: Boxes ready, waiting for Spring -- you?

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2005-03-19 03:10:33 UTC

We don't have a stash of carved stamps but have instead been placing
them as we go along throughout the winter - 11 boxes placed since New
Year's Eve and one more before the end of this month. We actually
have enjoyed getting out in the winter months and appreciate that
others have placed boxes for us to find in the past few months so we
didn't go into withdrawal waiting for spring! I need to carve a few
more for placement in April & May, including 1 or 2 that we'll place
out on the east coast at the end of May.

We are really looking forward to more Leapin' Lizards boxes!!
Hopefully we'll also have a chance to hit not only some more Illinois
boxes this summer, but also some Michigan boxes if we make it to the
Gathering...

Wisconsin Hiker
