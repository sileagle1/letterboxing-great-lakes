From: Silent Doug (silentdoug@letterboxing.info) | Date: 2004-09-03 17:55:20 UTC-04:00
At 05:03 PM 9/3/2004, you wrote:
>Just wondering what the largest number of boxes have you found, and how
>many miles did you have to travel to find them in a single day? Weather
>permitting, I think I'll be driving about 200 miles round-trip and we'll
>be up a the crack of dawn.

Up until a couple of weeks ago, my personal best day was 17 (not including
hitchhikers). I did it twice, once in and around Kernville, CA, and once on
the way from Erie to Detroit via Cleveland (one box in Erie, then 16 in
Cleveland).

Then I went to Connecticut for the first time....

:-)

Jennifer and I found 32 on a single day in SW CT a couple of weekends ago
on a Saturday, starting at 8:30 am and wrapping up at 7:30 pm.

Last weekend, I went boxing in SE Massachusetts and found 19 on Saturday,
then we found another 17 in NW CT on Sunday (including 5 in the DEP state
forest letterboxing program to qualify for their patch).

Of course, the boxes in CT and MA are so much more highly concentrated than
elsewhere in the country, so it's easy to rack up big numbers without
driving a lot of miles. And we've found so many of the boxes here in my
neck of PA that it's hard to find too many more than a dozen in a single
day, and that might include 150 miles of driving. When we go out boxing
around here, we're much more interested in a good day's hiking, and don't
plan for lots of finds but rather to enjoy longer hikes and interesting
places. But when I travel, I tend to try to find as many boxes as I can,
since I don't know when I'll be back to that part of the country. For me,
it's all part of the fun, both the days with lots of finds and the days
with challenging hikes and all the variations in between.

Doug

From: speedsquare_lbxr (ruhlette@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-09-05 02:33:20 UTC
Well, folks, I"m Back! We started out in the fog at 6:30 am! We returned after 250 miles
and found 14 boxes of 19 boxes. I was first finder on several new boxes, yeah. I missed
Schoolbus ... shooting range nearby gives me the heebie-jeebies and there was a huge
monster tractor/truck pull setting up. We were kinda conspicuous and didn't linger. At
Franklin County Park, we found a number of scoutdog boxes, but Napalm is probably
missing; found the definitive urinal tree and paced correctly to the stone, but alas, nothing
below. We passed on 2 ... today the holiday campers were out in full force and it would
have been a little rude to go through the picnic campsites. We saw a baby deer in full
daylight grazing by the road and a huge turtle with great color near campsite 84. We will
return later. We skipped one of scoutdogs' boxes at Brookville Dam due to time. So we
really only seriously looked for 16 and came up with 14. It was a very humid day. We saw
a HUGE old oak tree and the hiking was really quiet and relaxing in southeastern Indiana. I
highly recommend the Mary Gray Bird Sanctuary boxes ... even without a map.

Thanks to all who responded to my post.

speedsquare
