From: TrailTroll (kanderson@american-community.com) | Date: 2007-11-02 17:22:37 UTC
Although I enjoy boxing with others when I can, I probably do 90% of
my boxing alone. I have never had any problems. You need to be
especially aware of your surroundings, be prepared to ditch the hunt
if you ever end up somewhere that you don't feel safe, and trust your
instincts. I also carry pepper spray attached to my letterboxing
kit. I take my cell phone, but that doesn't always get reception, so
I carry a referee whistle to help summon (hopefully helpful)
strangers in case of minor injury like a twisted ankle. You can
never be totally safe, but that's true of life in general. Life's
too short to spend it afraid to letterbox!

TrailTroll

This topic has been discussed here and elsewhere in the past and one
of the suggestions made by SpringChick (who also boxes alone) deserves
repeating.
She reports that she calls her home answering machine as she heads
into a given park, thereby alerting the homefront of her last known
position.
When she emerges from that park, she calls again.

What a great way to leave the electronic trail of breadcrumbs.

blend in,

From: doubledragonletterboxer (M8KC5008@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-11-03 14:09:17 UTC
OMG! It is so hard for me to box alone lol. I have a very active
imagination. I will never forget the mysetry box by Cichlid Guy. I
wanted to go find it so bad because I knew I would be the first
finder. So I got my cell phone and a knife and all the stuff I might
need to defend myself and went out. It was a lil long walk, and the
further you walk the more it felt like there was no one around to
hear you. This trail meandered though peoples back yards, but they
were like backyard farms they were so big. Then I came across a yard
that looked like it was someones private dump with TVs, friges, and
tables (you name it it was there). There was not a sound to be heard,
except for insects buzzing by. That was it! My heart was racing, I
just pictured someone coming over the hill in overalls spitting
tabaco saying, "Looky what we got here". All I could think
was "squeal like a pig" and I ran all the way back to the car!! LOL
we later went back out there, and my husband was like ,"You were
scared of that?". When I was with him it just looked like a pile of
garbage, but out there alone it looked like a pile of garbage
belonging to a crazed lunatic that might stuff bodies in those old
friges. Well thats what you get when you watch too much court tv lol.
DD

From: baliddle (baliddle@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2007-11-03 10:34:49 UTC-04:00
That's hilarious, DD!

I only went out boxing by myself once and I didn't really like it at
all! I did leave a message on my answering machine at the beginning
of the day saying where I went, but I didn't think about leaving a
message before and after every stop. That'd be smarter. My message
was like "I'll be here, here, here, here, here, here..." all spaces
spread out over an entire county! So, if anything really had
happened to me, that's still a pretty big search area.

I did okay on all of them except the last one which was at an old
Indian burial ground and it was the last box I did that day, so it
was getting dark. You have to drive down this very long skinny dirt
road with lots of trees (I hate those kinds or roads in the
evening/night anyway) until you get to a dead end. There's this
little small trail, not well used that heads off into the burial
ground. I had already been talking to myself about how stupid this
was to be driving around in the middle of nowhere down this scary,
lonely dead end road. Then this trail was really spooky, and again,
it's nearly dark, so I was totally freaked out and thinking how dumb
I was and wondering how long it would take someone to find me if
something happened. When all of a sudden, this deer came running
through the woods and across the path right in front of
me!!! YIKES!!!! Needless to say, since I was already to tense, I
flew about 10 feet in the air, let out a loud gasp and a few swear
words! LOL But, the burial ground was really cool and it was a nice
spot. I did manage to appreciate it after I chilled out for a bit.

I'm glad I went, and obviously nothing happened. But, the advice
you've had here is good. Bring a phone, and some protection. Trust
your instincts and be prepared to abandon ship if things don't seem
right or safe. Have fun and be safe!
Bali
