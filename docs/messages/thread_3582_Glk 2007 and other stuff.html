Glk 2007 and other stuff

From: tiptoetonto (tiptoetonto@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-01-14 04:51:52 UTC
Tiptoe and Tonto will also be camping at a hotel for GLK 2007...

For the GLK 2007 we would like to carve a stamp of one of our
favorite 50's icons.."Roy Rogers and Dale Evans"

...also a special stamp that will only be available to those
letterboxers who actually were alive in the 50's!

We returned earlier this week from Tucson, AZ where we were limited
to finding 13 boxes due to Tonto being ill or having to attend
meetings...But, we were able to meet for an exchange and dinner with
Kristal and Ron, who are prolific LB planters in Arizona...a
highlight of the trip!

Before we left for AZ we had decided to plant the first of a series
of boxes we want to carve to show our gratitude to letterboxers that
have given us some of our favorite hikes....thus, while in Tucson, we
planted the first of "Tiptoe & Tonto's Favorite Letterboxer"
series...hopefully more to follow...as we do indeed have several
favorites...Oh, we forgot ..it's like children...You ALL are our
favorites! But, our old eyes couldn't possibly carve that
many....So, we will have to limit it to a few...

Speaking of the newly planted box..it was a first of sorts for
Tiptoe...Tonto was too weak to go on the hike that we had chosen to
hide the box. The canyon trail included a 1200 foot elevation
incline...(impressive to this flatlander!)...and about 6 miles round
trip (if you take into account all the wrong turns...)over boulders
and switchbacks with breath-taking scenery...Tiptoe REALLY wanted to
go on this hike...But, no one else was willing (or able)to go with
her...So she did something she's never done before...while Tonto
struggled through meetings one morning she went alone...She did loose
the trail twice which added to the pedometer and caused a bit of
anxiety...But, eventually had an exciting, stamina building workout
and got the box planted.

That evening, her jello legs were propped up in high heels for a
formal dinner. The conversations before the dinner included people
swapping stories about what they had done during the day...most had
played golf, shopped, spa stuff etc. When Tiptoe told them she had
hiked up to the Maiden Pools they were surprised...One man informed
her that he had just heard the hotel staff talking about a woman who
had gone up that very trail later in the afternoon and gotten lost
and had to be fetched. And while up in the canyon she had run into a
mountain lion...Egads! Tiptoe knew cougars were up there, but
wouldn't allow herself to think about it...too much...she did pray a
lot on the way up and down...and made it back safe and sound!...But,
still wouldn't advise anyone to hike that trail alone...As hard as it
is to believe, it isn't the first time she's done something foolish!

We also, boxed in snow ...which was an adventure because we started
out in short sleeves!....We highly recommend the Tucson area for
letterboxing adventure..they have a ton of boxes and something for
everyone!
