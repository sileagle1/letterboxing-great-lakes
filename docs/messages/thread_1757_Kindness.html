From: scheherezadebox (scheherezade1@gmail.com) | Date: 2005-08-24 20:33:15 UTC
Awhile ago I arranged an exchange with a Letterboxing friend. She
lives in New Hampshire and I have never met her, we have only chatted
on line and e-mailed. She said she just wanted to send a box to me as
a gift, to plant in West Michigan.

Well, today in my mailbox there it was, I tore it open and found a box
all wrapped in camo and inside was the most beautiful gift a person
could ever give me, a stamp carving of a rabbit. Not just any rabbit,
the one from Kit Williams book Masquerade. (A Real Treasure Hunt, with
clues hidden in the beautiful paintings and text in the book.)

I can not express how this has touched me and I can't tell you how bad
I want to hold onto it forever, like a Real Treasure, I guess kindess
is the greatest treasure on Earth! Oh, and Letterboxing!

Scheherezade

From: scheherezadebox (scheherezade1@gmail.com) | Date: 2005-08-25 02:19:01 UTC
Sorry to inform you Speedsquare but. . .

Ummm, Nope! They dislike the new and improved Scheherezade quite a bit.

The old one use to dote on them all day and night, trailing after
them, picking up every little crumb they dropped, singing and dancing
for them. Never ending attentive devotion. Cleaning house like a mad
woman, writing love letters all over, even on them.

New Scheherezade, loves Letterboxes toooo much and the house is a
wreck, and I sing songs to my stamps, to tupperware and love songs to
my computer when it delivers me a new e-mail.

I am a doomed woman, teetering on the brink of Letterboxing insanity.
Has anyone ever lost custody of their husband and daughter because of
Letterboxing???

Well, I am off to try to learn the art of balance!

Scheherezade

market since November. 13 jewels were hiddend around the USA and the
last one was found last weekend at Newaygo State Park. The man who
made the book was a follower of Kit Williams and decided if he ever had
the time, he would do one. Well, he made a boatload of money on
Software, so when he retired at 50, he wrote this fairy tale, self
published it and commissioned $1 million of jewels. I have enjoyed it
alot and what is very strange is few letterboxers have participated
(lots of those geo people) and few on the Trove talk groups have even
heard of letterboxing. Oh, well - details about it are at
