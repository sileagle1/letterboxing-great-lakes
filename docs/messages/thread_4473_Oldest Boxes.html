First, the oldest box in IL is/was the one in Evanston. It has been
missing for quite some time. It was amazing that it survived as long
as it did as the place was very tenuous. I personally found it and
checked on it a couple of times and verified it was missing. Without
checking my records, I'm sure it was placed before 2000.

As for WI, I've been trying to get up to Door Cty. to check on
placement dates. The main reason is that we are trying to have a 10
year anniversary gathering for WI and I needed to know the date of
the placement of Cave Point and to verify that it was the first
plant, as there is some dispute as Beetle brought up. My memorory
needs clarifying, but I beleive that Cave Point IS the first in Sept.
1998 and Mink River was placed in the spring of 1999 (sloppy 9 looks
like an 8 in the log?).

Just a note on Mink River...it may not be there much longer. A
ranger contacted me to remove it, but we have tried to contact him
MANY times without any response from him. Personally, this has led
me to believe that he either doesn't have the authority he said he
had or the email I got was a fake. As for now, I'm not removing the
box until he contacts me again.

Tonto and I found the Mink River box back in May of 2003. While looking for it, a prof
from University of Illinois came out of the "cabin" to see what we were doing...He took one
look at us and asked if we were looking for the Mink River letterbox...Surprised, we told
him that indeed we were. He nonchalantly walked us over to the exact location of the box
(he must've known how inept we are at finding boxes) all the while explaining how much
he didn't like the intrusion from letterboxers as the area was a rare habitat of the
endagered Hine's Emerald Dragonfly that he was studying. We have marveled that the box
has remained in place for the past 4 years knowing that the authorities have known (and
not been happy) about the letterbox's existence on the property. We did engage him in a
long diatribe about the benefits letterboxing brings of getting more people connected with
nature...but, back then letterboxing was still in it's infancy and the number of letterboxers
actually finding the box was very few compared to the visits it probably now generates.
Tiptoe...and Tonto

The Hine's Emerald Dragonfly is also the reason DuPage County forest
preserve folks would prefer not to have letterboxers and geocachers in
Waterfall Glen preserve in Darien, IL without an approved permit. From what
I understand, there's a slim possibility they might be there, tho as of
March 2007, when the study was finished, Waterfall Glen wasn't included as a
site. It's one of the most endangered of the dragonflies.

For anyone who might be interested in locations where colonies have been
found in IL to possibly avoid placing boxes there, the March, 2007 report is
at:

http://www.museum.state.il.us/research/entomology/hines/2007_IL_HED_final_re
port.pdf

There are a few sites in Kane County along the Fox River for the dragonfly.
I did see one Hine's in the vicinity of one of the Grail boxes out there.
As soon as I saw that, we left. Didn't want to look any further for the
box. Saw a couple of birds in that area also that are on the threatened
list.
