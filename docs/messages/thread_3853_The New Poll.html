You're right about that! We also are willing to endure harsh
conditions in our treks, but DO like to be warned about anything out
of the ordinary ahead of time.

Included below is most of a clue for the scariest LB adventure we've
had so far. We walked down the hill and wore water shoes & swimsuits
because the clues had provided enough info as to what we would be
encountering. We had our LB gear in plastic bags in a small pack
attached shoulder height. There were huge waves out in the ocean,
crashing onto the beach and then surging into the channel. A moment
later that same water was heading back out to sea, trying to suck us
out with it! There were 3 guys ahead of us trying to cross - 1 made
it but the other 2 decided to give up. Martini Man then made it
across with our books, got a large branch to use as a balance (or
sticking point to hold onto) and came back to help me. I had the ink
& stamps in a small pack hooked around my swimsuit strap. We slowly
made our way across, leaning to the lagoon, then leaning to the ocean
as we grasped the stick and fought our way across the strong current.

Yes, we did find the box and were the First Finders!

Wisconsin Hiker

CLUE:
This letterbox is located in Waipio Valley on the Big Island of
Hawaii. After you reach Waipio Valley Lookout, park and enjoy the
views. Then, venture down the 25% grade, 4WD only, single lane road
with EXTREME CAUTION. This is a very narrow, single lane road, and
drivers on their way up have the right of way. If you don't look out
and pull over for drivers coming up (they have periodic pull-over
spots), you have to back up the STEEP hill to let them by. Don't let
your brakes overheat - use LOW gear. If the road is wet, I would not
recommend driving. You may be able to walk down, but check the posted
signs first.

Once you have reached the bottom, take the road that leads to the
beach (take a hard right at the bottom). There are several [muddy]
roads, but just stay in the middle. There are usually cars parked at
the beach. Park anywhere and proceed to the beach where a channel
separates the sand into two beaches. Cross this channel WITH EXTREME
CAUTION. It can be very deep. When I crossed it, it was about 4.5
feet deep. Swim across, if you wish, just make sure you have a
waterproof pack for your letterboxing log book!

After crossing the channel, walk along waterfront until you see the
first "lone" pine tree. Take a left up the small incline at
the "lone" pine tree, through a stone path to the dirt car path,
heading west. At the first grassy knoll with three pine trees, I saw
a bunch of butterflies frolicking in the shady paradise.

Follow the road until you see some old trees "sleeping". AT 220
degrees there are some palm trees and twin "multi-trunk" trees. Take
35 steps through the grass, toward the "multi-trunk trees". At the
base of the "multi-trunk" tree on the west side is the letterbox.

Enjoy the peaceful paradise before heading back up the steep hill.
You can travel around the valley if you desire.
