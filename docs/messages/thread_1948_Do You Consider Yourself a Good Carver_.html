Do You Consider Yourself a Good Carver?

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-11-03 13:31:45 UTC
I've been asked to provide a list of Great Lakes group members who
are good carvers and would be interested in contributing a stamp to a
Great Lakes Regional Showcase Traveling Event Box (that's a mouthful).

Back up... you may be aware that there is a new Yahoo! group for
Traveling Event Stamps -- people who are organizing events can go
here and requests traveling boxes for their event and letterboxers
who have made up traveling boxes can list their box here. The group
organizer (She Who Plants) acts as the middle man between the carvers
and the event organizers to get traveling boxes out and around the
country. As part of her effort to showcase the carving skills of
various letterboxers across the country, she is putting together
regional stamp packages done by carvers from within each of the
regional areas. These packages will then be sent to various events
across the country.

We have so many wonderful carvers in this group that I am not
comfortable just picking certain people, not to mention I am not
personally familiar with the carving of every person here. There is
also the possibility that somebody who is a wonderful carver may not
be interested in participating in this project.

So, rather than me just choosing people, I thought I would ask for
volunteers who are interested in submitting a stamp for this
project. How good does your carving have to be? Well, in my
opinion, every carving is a worthy carving, so as long as you are
comfortable with the fact that your stamp will be making the rounds
to various events across the country, your carving is good enough.
She is looking for 8-12 people to participate, hopefully representing
all of the states in our region. If there are more people than that
interested, I will ask if we can have additional, otherwise it will
need to be limited to the first 12 who respond. I do not have
details as to theme or anything like that yet.

Please e-mail me privately if you are interested in being part of
this project.

Thanks!

SpringChick
