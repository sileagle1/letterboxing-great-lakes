Someone noted that Matt the Rat's boxes included commercially created
stamps. As I recall, Matt designs the image himself and then has the
stamp prepared by a commercial firm.
Matt, correct me if I got that wrong.
Anyway, here is a source to have your own drawings or copyright-free
art made into stamps. There are two links. The first describes the
main site and the second tells you how to prepare your images.
What you'll get: a 7" x 9" sheet of ready to cut apart and mount
rubber stamps. Obviously it's most cost effective to squeeze as many
images onto that 7 x 9 sheet because the price per image naturally
drops. It's in the neighborhood of $35 per sheet. But it's certainly
cheaper than paying $5 - $10 per stamp.

www.readystamps.com
www.polyclay.com/ready.htm

Of course, I strongly encourage you to carve your own stamps. You CAN
do it.
But, if you'd rather transfer your own images onto good quality
rubber, try these links. BTW, the people who do the work at this firm
are especially in need of rewarding employment and they do a great job!
I am not in any way connected with these businesses.

Good luck,
