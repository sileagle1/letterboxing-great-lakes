REPORT: Will County, IL

From: Mosey (PonyExpressMail@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-07-10 11:16:38 UTC-05:00
Spent the cool part of yesterday morning (if it could be said that there was
a cool part) and found 1 box at Messenger Marsh and 3 boxes at Messenger
Woods near Homer Glen, IL, all placed by Howling Coyote.

Found out just how old my maps are. Homer Glen isn't even on them. Will
have to stop by Chicago Motor Club and get a few updated ones. So I was
heading out for rural Homer Township instead of Homer Glen. :-) Only it was
not-so-rural-anymore Homer Township.

All 4 boxes in very good shape. Stamps very cute. I could be carving for a
hundred years and I won't get letters this nice.

The last of the three boxes at Messenger Woods took me a bit longer than
scheduled. Well, actually, more than a bit. But by the time I found the
box I was rotflmao at myself. I don't want to give anything away for others
who might go searching, so I won't go into detail, but I know those woods
and I thought I knew exactly where box #3 was. Wrong! I ended up searching
three times instead of one. So I guess I could say that I went looking for
six boxes and found three. Dumb dumb dumb.

Another thing I found, or that found me, was horseflies. Was bothered by no
ticks, very few mosquitoes, but a couple of large horseflies were real pains
at Messenger Marsh. The variety of birds at the Marsh was wonderful, and
the stamp was well worth the drive down there.
