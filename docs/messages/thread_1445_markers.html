From: Silent Doug (silentdoug@letterboxing.info) | Date: 2005-06-16 11:28:20 UTC-04:00
At 10:14 AM 6/16/2005, you wrote:

>i would like to add more color to my stamp images. can anyone recommend a
>brand/ type of markers that would work well in logbooks?

Try the LePlume II markers, from Michael's or AC Moore. They have a long
brush tip that make it easy to ink a stamp.

You can get 12-packs of them in assorted colors, which is a better deal
than buying them individually -- but look out for the 40% off coupons that
both stores offer in weekly newspapers to really get a good deal (and don't
forget that both stores honor competitor coupons!)

Remember to "huff" on the stamp after you ink it to restore some of the
moisture.

Crayola markers DO work, just about any marker will, but the art markers
such as LePlumes or Tombows are much "juicier" with ink, so you can really
get a good coat of ink down on the stamp. Sometimes a marker will dry out
on the image if there isn't enough ink - which is why everyone says to
"huff" on the inked image to remoisten it. A new set of Crayolas will work,
so don't sweat it if you can't get a set of more expensive ones.
