Labor Day Weekend and Powerboxing

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2004-09-09 01:54:06 UTC
We had a fabulous weekend of hiking and boxing in Michigan, primarily
in the Muskegon area. Our travels included Muskegon, Grand Haven and
Grand Rapids. We used the new high-speed ferry between Milwaukee and
Muskegon to shorten our driving time and it worked out very well. It
was a speedy and relaxing way to get from Point A to Point B.

The parks on that side of Lake Michigan were fantastic! Impressive
dunes, beaches and forests gave us plenty to enjoy. We got very
familiar with the terrain as we hunted for Spring Chick's boxes.
If you've never tried walking up a mountain of sand you just
don't know what you're missing ... A variety of clues kept
our brains going at the same time our legs were churning up the
sand. The hikes were rewarding not only in terms of physical &
mental exercise, but also in terms of vistas and creative well-carved
stamps.

Other nice boxes and hikes near the lakeshore included a series by
Keltic Kara and one by Hummingbird.

We also enjoyed looking for boxes further inland including several
neat boxes planted by Grandvilla and Mobiusklein, one by Bindle Babe,
and the topical Election '04 series by Lucky Charms.

Making some of the hunts extra fun in Grand Rapids was the fact that
Delirium and her husband joined us, traveling over from the Detroit
area. Afterwards the 4 of us joined SpringChick & Old Shoe and
Grandvilla & Mobiusklein for a delicious dinner and lots of LB talk.
We really appreciated the hospitality of these MI boxers!

We had a great weekend and would definitely take the ferry again.
There are probably enough boxes in western MI for at least 3 more
weekends of "powerboxing", north and south of Muskegon.

For those of you who are wondering what our counts were:

16 boxes on Saturday
20 boxes on Sunday (tied our previous record)
17 boxes on Monday

Total = 53 boxes!
