Ultimate Loser . . . . . No More

From: (Mathw613@aol.com) | Date: 2019-05-22 02:19:27 UTC

I've been playing Letterbox BINGO for 11 years now.


The first, or second year, I played, someone won by playing nothing but General Terms.


I tucked the idea in the back of my mind, to possibly do the same thing, at some point.


But it was always to hard not to pick a box that I had found, or a person whom I had exchanged with, or an event I had attended.


As each year progressed, there was a common theme . . . . I never won. Not a side game, not the big game, not the biggest loser. Nothing. 


I became both superstitious, and disheartened.


Superstitious in that I've said NOTHING about this until now. I never wanted to say anything, and then end up winning, and wondering "what are the odds?" So, I've said nothing.


Well, the last 3 or 4 years, I've gotten within a handful of words. A year or two I even got within the last word or two . . . unfortunately, the final word I was waiting on, it appeared EVERYONE else was also waiting on.


So, this year, I decided would be my last year of playing . . . and I played nothing but General Terms -- in order -- the first 5 words in each column. I did mention that I was disheartened.


Things started off going pretty well. Several words went my way. The trend pretty much continued . . . the last week, things were looking pretty dang good.


Side games were being completed and won -- once they were all won all I had to focus on was the full card.


Getting down to the final week or two, things were still looking really good. However, I could say the same thing for the last 3 - 4 years. 


Congrats NamVet on your big win. 


I only had 2 more chances for a win of some sort.


Then it happened. The final word I needed. I filled in my final box. I Emailed Atomic Beans . . . and I tied with Trail Rider for the final game. 


After 11 years of drought, I can now claim the title of "Ultimate Loser No More."


Also, playing on my superstitions, I haven't mentioned never winning anything until now -- AFTER I received my Bingo Booty. Didn't want to pull on any emotions, or guilt trip my way to a better booty.


Thank you, again, AtomicBeans for a great game. Thank you all for your wonderful "Booty Gifts." 


As to my retirement from Letterbox Bingo . . . We'll have to see how I feel in December, however, I'm already warming to the idea of pushing on.


Sorry for ranting . . . but 11 years is a doggone long time. 


hx6 -- who sucks at picking BINGO words.
