From: junk4joann (junk4joann@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-08-10 17:47:02 UTC
...checking out Lake Park in Milwaukee. Recently I was reminded of
this park when I saw some gorgeous blank note cards at Schwartz
bookstore. I thought to myself "Wow, what a beautiful park. That looks
almost like Lake Park. Oh! It IS Lake Park..." I bought the bookmark. :)

Anyway, Lake Park is one of those urban treasures that you may not
even know about. I've been told the designer also designed Central
Park in New York. His name is Olmsted. Here's a website discussing the
park's history: http://www.lakeparkfriends.org/history.shtml It has a
lighthouse, statues of lions and other folk. I think you can play
golf, there are concerts, it's near the lake (duh) and near UWM.

I don't want to break any rules... but there may or may not be a very
nice letterbox series there. I'm just sayin'. (IS that rule breaking?)
