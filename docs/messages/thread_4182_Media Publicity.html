I read your comments about the PD reporter writing a story. Today while
flipping through Family Fun magazine I came across a cardstock add for
GMC that is titled "Learn it. Love it. Letterboxing" It has a full page
about what Lb'ing is and even provides an image of a GMC truck and
gives instructions on how to carve and mount the finished stamp! It
provides a website where you can experience the Chevy Suburban
interactive letterbox camp (did not try it). It does list LBNA and AQ.

Family fun is how I got started letterboxing and the original article
was pro enviroment and learning experience.

I was surprised by the big corp. tie-in.

Did anyone else see this?

From: olsentrio (hlo281@aol.com) | Date: 2007-06-18 18:17:58 UTC
Although I have not been LB'ing for very long (9 months), I see a big
difference between this and geocaching. Geocachers seem more
interested in "the find", whereas LB'ers are more interested in
the "craft aspect" of the hobby; i.e. hand-carved stamps, homemade
logs. Of course, we still love to log that find though : )
