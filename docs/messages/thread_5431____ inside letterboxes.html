I'm amazed that people would actually leave any large sum of money inside a letterbox. Hard core letterboxers have always felt that finding the hand carved stamp inside the box was 'the prize.' Don't get me wrong, it's not that I wouldn't love to stumble across a box with $100.00 bill inside, but if word got out that there was CASH inside letterboxes, that could open up a whole new can of worms and create problems. If people went searching for letterboxes as if it were some sort of $$$ treasure hunt hoping to find money and there wasn't any $$$ inside, they might simply toss the box aside and not value the real intended prize contents. Just my thoughts.

Several years ago, I created The Great Rondout Train Robbery Letterbox and I included several $1 million dollar bills (replicas) because the story (clue) told of how the stolen millions were never recovered from this famous crime.

Come to think of it, occasionally I use to find first finder paper certificates inside boxes, but not recently. Those were some artistic tags!
