Washington Co., IA

From: beckers410 (Mathw613@aol.com) | Date: 2008-04-29 01:44:32 UTC
Yesterday at about 10:15 in the morning Becki suggested we drive to
Washington Co., IA (isn't she wonderful?)

Washington Co., is littered with boxes by Maude.

Our first find was a nice easy box -- esp. with little kids in the mud.
Maude used a technique of not using a box, rather a custome made
weather proof bag . . . Skeeter said, "its not a letterbox, its a
letterbag!" Bonus there was a HH inside!

We went on to find a couple more before lunch, after lunch we figured
out the clues to "A Great Combo." Hidden inside a 1930's nightclub that
is closed on Sundays and holds off hours during the week.

I knew the restaurant was closed on Sundays, but we were 2 hours from
home and just had to try. I attempted to open the door and to my
surprise it opened!! I went downstairs and found out that the
establishment was closed, but they were having a kids birthday party
downstairs... the woman I spoke to knew of the box and handed it to me!

The best boxes are the ones you aren't expecting to find . . . but do
anyways.

Bonus, it also had a HH inside, the Hines Emerald Dragonfly HH" it is
one of my favorite HH's we've ever found (minus a small store bought
stamp) because A) It was released in 2004 -- 4 years ago! And 2) It had
some BIG names inside (many of which I recognize), released by Merlin &
Andrea! Found by Sophie & Me, Kiss Me Kate, The Christmas Elves,
RaqsEnigma, Shutterfly, Laughing Coyote, The Dragon, Jacks, Acorn,
Leapin' Lizards, Wisconsin Hiker & Martini Man, and a bunch of names I
didn't recognize.

We ended up finding 7 boxes and the last box of the day contained
another HH -- 3 total.

Thanks Maude for the fun day . . . looking forward to meeting you and
others at the NE Iowa Gathering on Sat.

hx6
