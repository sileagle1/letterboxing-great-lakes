Hitchhiker "Book" ???

From: Mosey (PonyExpressMail@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-06-15 12:30:51 UTC-05:00
In one of the boxes we found today, we found a "Hitchhiker Book." No stamp,
just a book. At first we thought the stamp and book got separated somehow,
but the way it reads inside the logbook, it sounds like there may never have
been an accompanying stamp.

It says:

"Hi! This is a hitchhiker book started by Bob on August 18, 2002 at Holy
Hill Wisconsin. Stamp it, sign it and move it on to a new location. Let me
know where you found this. RobbyRobot2002@yahoo.com."

For having been started in August of 2002, there isn't much in the book.
One family found it on Jan 4, 2003 and a note in there from them says they
didn't have the time to pass it on until Oct 19, 2004, when they placed it
at Starved Rock State Park. It was picked up from there on Oct 23, 2004 and
placed at Oakhurst Run in Aurora, IL on April 4, 2005. Then we found it at
Oakhurst Run today, June 15.

Tried to contact "Bob" but the email addy was dead. So Bob, if you're
reading this, the above is what's happened with the "book" so far. And if
it did have a stamp at one time, it no longer does.

Is there actually such a thing as just a hitchhiker *book*, no stamp at
all??? I've never seen one before.

~~ Mosey ~~

From: Mosey (PonyExpressMail@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-06-17 17:06:18 UTC-05:00
"Bob" launched the book (with or without a stamp) in August, 2002, and we
were only the third finder in the book. One family kept it from Jan, 2003
until Oct, 2004 before they set it free at Starved Rock in IL. Then it was
found in Oct, 2004 and set free in April, 2005. Then we found it this
month. The folks who found it in Jan, 2003 didn't put down any contact
info, nor did they sign with any trail names. It was a parent (or two)
accompanied by a 7 1/2 year old and a 4 year old. The 7 1/2 year old wrote
that this was their first letterbox. The folks who found it at Starved Rock
were also the folks who placed the box I found it in. I didn't contact
them, I just assumed they would've known what they were doing, but maybe
not.

But the first family could have lost the stamp in all the time they kept it.
However "Bob"'s writing sort of indicates that there might not have been a
stamp since he/she only mentions the book.
