Big Hole adventures

From: M Clan (joshmonty@hotmail.com) | Date: 2005-08-28 03:39:21 UTC
Well, on my way from WI -> MI, I stopped in Homewood, IL to pick up
a few newly planted I-94 Traffic Crawl boxes. I was excited by all
of Tiptoe & Tonto's boxes. "Big Hole" sounded interesting because
I've driven by that quarry often and the chance to explore it a
little more was enticing.

I found the ball park, made it to center field and enjoyed the view
of the quarry and I-294 (quite a sight!). I then found the box in a
very neat hiding spot and stamped in. While closing up the box, a
pickup pulled up to the gate and a guy hopped out and unlocked it.
I was 20-30 yards away from the hiding spot, so I quickly stuffed my
markers, the box, and my gear into my backpack while the truck drove
up near me. The truck had the Quarry logo on it and a man stepped
out and asked how I was doing. I told him things were great and
that this was quite a view. He just kind of stared at me for a
while. I then asked him a couple "quarry questions" (when was it
started?, etc.) and we chatted for a bit. After a couple minutes he
told me this area was private property and that the Director of
Security for the quarry was coming around to give a tour and
wouldn't be happy if I was around. I told him that I'd take off.
As I was leaving, I could see him open up a gate to an overlook of
the quarry that they were apparently using for the tour. Earlier,
the guy had told me that the overlook was closed off because of
vandalism.

I needed to keep going east so I kept the box in my pack while I
quickly found "The Compleat Angler", dropped off the "Treetops"
hitchhiker, and kept heading east to my destination. (I picked up
SpringChick's "Welcome to Michigan" box along the way.)

So now I'm in Pittsburgh (after a night in MI) and will mail the box
back to Tiptoe & Tonto. I'll let them decide what to do with it.
It was a neat spot and hopefully something can be done.

It was my first run-in with security personnel while
letterboxing ... not terribly exciting, but interesting nonetheless.

Father M Clan



Re: Big Hole adventures

From: tiptoetonto (tiptoetonto@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-08-31 22:07:57 UTC
Oh my! We sure didn't think this "Big Hole" box would cause such a
ruckus! M Clan (and Acorn) had let us know of the "Big Hole"
letterbox going on sabatical to Pittsburg over the weekend. We
posted the missing box on LBNA ASAP...Unfortunately, two new
letterboxers that we had introduced to this great pastime of ours
had printed the clues earlier in the week and had not gotten the
revision...Oops! Did they have a tale to share with me when we all
returned for the first day of school!....Apparently, M Clan ran into
the Material Service Corp. security, but these two gals ran into the
Thornton police! Questioning what they were doing....they just flat
out told the officer all about letterboxing (potential
newbie?)...They didn't think they explained it very well..He told
them to "Well, just do what you gotta do quickly and be on your
way." They were feeling really stupid as the clues are very
forthright and the box just wasn't there...They weren't amused to
find out that "Big Hole" was enjoying a great vacation out east with
M Clan! They returned to their car with their perverbial tail
between their legs!

For the record, I've written for permission to replant at the site
and I'm contemplating making a visit to the police station! What we
will go through just to plant a tiny camo tupperware! But, all of
this does make us think about the resemblance of our benign hobby to
more covert activities. After all the same parks, reclusive nature
spots, etc. that attract us also are appealing for others to carry
on with dangerous, illegal or even criminal activities. It is rare
to find a woods, lake or park that has not been altered by the
debris left strewn about by partying types. It is a given that we
share the trails and public spaces with their ilk...And now with
heightened security measures being taken around public venues we are
even more likely to be targeted as suspitious characters...Now, some
of us are indeed characters...but I'm thinking it might be helpful
to share ideas that other letterboxers use to not "look" so
suspitious....Personally, we like the "Birding-geeky" look, or
the "Official data-collecting-clip board-carrying naturalist"
look....any others?
Tiptoe & Tonto
