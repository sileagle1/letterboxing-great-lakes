I, too, have been interested in this thread. I am inclined to follow WI Hiker and
SpringChick's lead on this one. I googled and found an information bulletin effective date
Jan. 1, 2005 for geocaching activities in Indiana. I put a copy in the files for future
reference. I didn't see any direct reference to letterboxing; back in the middle of 2004
there were less than 100 boxes in the entire state. LB'g was probably far under the radar
at that time. Knowing where boxes have been placed and finding a number of active
boxes, I think we would be better served by showing the powers-that-be we are
responsible and sensitive to the environment. But when change comes, I believe we will be
resourceful enough to find a better mousetrap. I feel for SpringChick and all of her MI
boxes, so I better get up there before the game is over. I think it is only a matter of time
before one of the "big-town" stations here discovers this great secret. A local reporter
does local interest stories and outdoor activities now and then. I have even seen him in
the building where I work doing features on local artists and other interesting pieces. I am
not tempted to tell the secret, but since my stamp is all over the area, a savvy investigator
may seek me out. I am cautious.
