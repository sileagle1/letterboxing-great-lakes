From: Bone Yard Recorder

Hello, let me introduce myself. I am BONE YARD RECORDER.

As of January 1st, 2008, our county in OHIO (SANDUSKY COUNTY, not to be confused with the town of Sandusky) had 2 boxes... one is a micro box the other a small store bought stamp. And so I have had to travel miles and miles to learn about letterboxing and to retrieve boxes... so as I met other boxers from around the state and became friends, I told them of my empty county and they started giving me and sending me stamps that they had lying around and some even carved new stamps for here.

Residents named the county after an Indian term for "at the cold water." Our county has a lot of Civil War history and heroes. President of the United States Rutherford Birchard Hayes ranks as Sandusky County's most prominent resident. His home, Spiegel Grove, is now an historical landmark operated by the Ohio Historical Society. Union General James B. McPherson also lived in Sandusky County. He was killed during the American Civil War and was buried in Clyde, Ohio. We have a Heinz 57 plant here and an old Pioneer Sugar plant, Christy Knives and shears were founded and manufactured here. The GREAT BLACK SWAMP occupied most of the land here in the beginning. Most of Sandusky County's residents find employment in agricultural positions, with seventy-percent of the county's acreage under cultivation. Sandusky County farmers are the fifth largest producers of tomatoes in Ohio.

I am on a quest to fill my county as full as I can. So if you have a stamp you would like to carve or dig out from an old postal, an old signature stamp, cootie, hitchhiker, event stamp or personal traveler I would love it if you would send it to me for planting here. You will be the carver and be contacted every time someone logs it in.

I have planned 4 events for this year starting in April and would very much love to include a letterbox stamp to be planted here permanently, no matter how small, from you!

Thanks a million for your time.

Your boney friend,
Bone Yard Recorder
