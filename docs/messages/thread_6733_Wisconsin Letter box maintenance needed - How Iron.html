So I eventually get to the Lakeshore Lights LB - the sun finally came out just before I got the box! - and I replace the box itself. The location is lovely, by the way. Then I go on to the next Manitowoc location - I leave the area as I think there was a major cross country meet going on. I go to Schuette Park, and find that the Trail Troll box needs replacing, too. Good thing I had a box to plant with me - I'll just use the container from that one. But I forget about the pencil in the baggie of original stuff, and crack the box. Arghhh. So, while I have notified the placer, I'm also asking that if you're going for this box, please try to replace the cracked box. Happy - and dry - trails, everyone!

- Sunny

--- In LbGLK@yahoogroups.com, "rlovely1@..." wrote:
>
> While on vacation in Wisconsin a few weeks ago we hunted down a letter box that is listed on the letterboxing.org site and titled lakeshore lights. It is located in Manitowoc along the walkway to the lighthouse. When we found the box it was totally cracked, beyond repair, and someone had tied a white grocery bag aroung it. I think to help keep it dry? The ziploc bags had holes and the contents were totally soaked, the log book beyond repair. I tossed the book, dried off the stamp, made a quick logbook with paper from my log book and bagged the stamp and logbook seperately and then put those in an additional ziplock. As I didn't have a lock box container on me, I had no choice but to place the ziploc back in the cracked container and then in the grocery bag. I was hoping that a letterboxer in the area might be able to provide a little TLC and give the letter box a new container. I sent an email to the placers via letterboxing.org but have no idea if they actually received it or if they did, if they are able to do anything. I thought I'd cover all the bases by placing a message here. Hope that's ok.
> hppy2Bmom

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2010-09-11 22:22:10 UTC-05:00
We think it is a bad idea to put a pen or pencil inside a baggie - they
often puncture the bag and in your case it sounds like they broke the
box??!?! Thanks for doing the maintenance for future finders.



We just had to restock our maintenance supplies in our pack after our recent
trip to NY. We added or replaced numerous baggies & logbooks in boxes that
needed them and used our spare box to replace a very lightweight container
that was cracked/shattered. Over the years we've found that it is also good
to carry a good supply of napkins for drying out boxes!
