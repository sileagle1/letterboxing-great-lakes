I had an idea recently. While I do hike and bike the Northern
Illinois trails, I spend much more time on the rivers in a kayak.

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-06-04 00:26:24 UTC-04:00
I recall from a trip out east seeing some letterbox clues to boxes
accessible only by kayak or canoe. I think it is a cool idea even if you
could only get to them via water. I've considered doing a "boat to it"
series along the Lake Michigan shoreline, but just haven't had the time to
make work of it. I also have one to plant on an island at a lake in
Michigan where a person would have to have some sort of watercraft (or be a
really good swimmer) to get there.

There is nothing that says letterboxes have to be accessible by foot or that
every letterbox needs to be accessible to every person. Some of these more
remote and obscure boxes turn out to be the most prized finds, just because
of the effort to get there and the fact that you have found a box many
others will pass by because it is not so quick and easy.

Of course if you place boxes that are a bit more difficult to get to, you
also have to be willing to accept that there may not be so many finders.
Personally that doesn't matter to me -- I'd rather have 3 people who had to
really work to get a box (i.e. difficult clues, hike, location, mystery,
etc.) -- and therefore got an immense sense of satisfaction when the box was
finally found, than 30 people who drove up to the box on the way to the
grocery store. Quality vs quantity.

Go for it, great idea!

SpringChick
