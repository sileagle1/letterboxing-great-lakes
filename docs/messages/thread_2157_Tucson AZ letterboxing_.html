Tucson AZ letterboxing!

From: tiptoetonto (tiptoetonto@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-01-11 00:38:48 UTC
Wow! We feel like we had an out-of-body experience last week! The
week after Christmas Tiptoe attended the mini-gathering at the
PAW...sat next to Martini Man and Wisconsin Hiker....I think some of
their hiking energy rubbed off! Tonto had a Reading Conference in
Tucsonlast week ...Tiptoe went along for some fun in the sun....and
to sneak in a little letterboxing between conference meetings
(Thankfully, most meetings were held in the evenings!) We logged in
more than 30 miles of hiking up and down gorgeous mountains, canyons
and spectacular desert views all thanks to the terrific Arizona
boxers placing a plethora of boxes!

We found 19 of the attempted 20!
Kristal & Ron boxes:
"To the Bat Cave," "Tombstone: The Town Too Tough To Die"
and "Goin'Batty II

angel1551us box:
"Grateful Dead"

Pat & Cat boxes:
Honeybee Canyon Park

Desert Rat box:
"Tohono Chul Park"

Funhog boxes:
"Flycatcher" and "Madera Canyon" - our 1st Funhog boxes YaHoooo!

We can't say enough about these boxes by Azroadie:
"The Thing", "Rincon Valley", "Sabino Canyon", "Bear Canyon",
"Agua Caliente Hill", "Phainopepla","Sentinel Peak", "Golden Gate"
(1st Finders!), "Kennedy Park", Brown Mountain"

and HH..."Happy New Year 2005" found in Golden Gate (now heading
back east with us to continue into 2006 somewhere out of the desert
sun!)

We searched for mizscarlets "Red Eagle" with no luck...

We've listed all the boxers and their boxes so that we could in this
forum "publicly" thank each one of them for making our trip so
fabulous!
The weather is perfect, scenery couldn't be more beautiful and there
are boxes for just about every kind of boxer.
NOTE: This report has not been funded by the Greater Tucson Tourism
Board! It is all true testimony! So, if the winter weather has you
down...head to Tucson NOW! We can't even imagine doing these boxes
in the summer heat!

The best thing we learned about the trip, you ask?
We proved to ourselves that by hiking one day over 11 miles in the
mountains that we can surely tackle one of Wisconsin Hikers longer 9
mile hikes that we've been putting off!
It brought back memories and felt like the "Olden days" in the 70's
of backpack trips to Colorado....Oh, it was a thrill.....
Tiptoe & Tonto back in Chicago
