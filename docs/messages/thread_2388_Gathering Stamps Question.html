Gathering Stamps Question

From: Steve & Christa (sbellock322@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-04-14 02:38:50 UTC
Mrs. Cottontails and I were getting the package ready to send to
Wisconsin Hiker with our stamp for the 2006 Gathering and it got me
wondering. This is the third stamp that we have done, including the
stamps for TESS, that will be going out into the world without our
help in placing. This makes me wonder, at the gathering in June, and
at future gatherings where the TESS series might show up, can we look
for a box that we carved? If we do, can we count that as a found
box? Should we just skip the box with the stamps we carved? I was
just curious what other people thought. I will be posting a similar
question on AQ because I really want a broad spectrum of opinions on
this, but the opinions here are of greater personal value.

Thanks

The Cottontails

PS. We were FF on a few boxes today!




RE: [LbGLK] Gathering Stamps Question

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2006-04-13 21:54:33 UTC-05:00
Hi!

Yeah, we have thought about the same thing. I have already sent 2 stamps to
the east coast for gatherings in the fall. I carved the stamp, but someone
else will plant them (this will be a first for us, since we have usually
planted our own boxes for gatherings, even when they were in other states).
We have decided that we will count these new boxes as finds (if we find
them) when we are out in Connecticut (SEP) and Delaware(OCT). When we HIDE
boxes for a gathering (the Haunted Harvest and the upcoming Wandering in
Wonderland), we don't count the boxes we plant for others as plants or
finds, but we do stamp the images in our journal. We just add them to our
Miscellaneous stamp count since we didn't actually create the stamp/box and
we don't find the box. We generally encourage the creators of boxes for an
event to later post them as their own plants on LBNA so they can get the
feedback from finders, but we offer to check on the boxes if any problems
are reported. So far, so good, on the Haunted Harvest boxes.

We don't consider something a "plant" unless we actually created the stamp
AND the clues. However we have decided to count a box as a "find" even if
we carved the stamp, but didn't create the clues. This fall will be the
first time we will have the experience of searching for a box with a stamp I
carved, but have no idea where it is planted....

Each person/team will probably have their own opinion, but this is the way
we've decided to handle it.

Wisconsin Hiker

Re: Gathering Stamps Question

From: beautifulpeople99 (jennifer.mccready@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-04-14 13:36:45 UTC
We have decided to take a similar approach for the upcoming Spring
Fling in MI. We are carving two stamps, but not hiding them. We have
decided to hunt for them like they were somebody else's stamps and
count them as "finds" but not "plants". I think it will be pretty
fun! :-) Also, I think we are going to re-plant out boxes after the
event, at which point we will probably count them as normal plants...
since we will be writing the clues and maintaining them.

J of J&BBooks
