Report from Indianapolis

From: Silent Doug (silentdoug@letterboxing.info) | Date: 2005-04-18 09:19:54 UTC-04:00
I returned yesterday from a great trip to Indianapolis! Here's a report:

On Thursday, I met up with Speedsquare & Artdog (& Leopard JJ) and had a
great Mexican dinner, and got to exchange personal stamps and travellers. I
had a lot of fun, so thanks to you both for coming out to meet me!

On Friday, I took a cab from my hotel to Ft. Harrison, and then hiked a
couple of trails to successfully find:
Independence Day 2004 (Artdog)
More Than Corn & Morning Glory (Racing Family)
Crosscountry Crossroads (Formidable Donuts)
Cicada Land (Leopard JJ)
Hoosier Hostel - Shady Rest (Speedsquare)

I didn't find Picnic in the Park, but the groundskeepers were mowing the
area and there were two schoolbuses of kids having a picnic lunch, so
combined with the regular dog walkers I just didn't have the opportunity to
poke around.

Then, I hiked over to another nearby park and logged these two finds:
2005 Great Lakes Exchange - Hoffman Tower (Artdog/Fungus Woman)
Power of the Force (Armadillo Jo)

I overshot Power of the Force by a mile and hiked clear to the other end of
the park, but it was such a nice day and I had time on my hands so it
wasn't a disaster.

Then I hiked back to 56th St and Post Rd where I got some refreshment and
caught a cab back to my hotel. I think I hiked 9 or 10 miles for the day,
so I got a pretty good workout (and a bit of sunburn on my neck and arms,
too!).

Doug

trying to meet-up? Leave it to me to coordinate things. I do a
Mapquest search for Mexican restaurants near Silent Doug's hotel and
come up with an address which I forward on to Doug and speedsquare.
We come to find out there is no such restaurant at this address. I
do a drive-by, notice someone standing near the parking lot and
almost stop to ask directions. Instead, I head down the street to
another little Mexican place, assuming I've made a mistake. After
sitting down and feeling a little bummed, it dawns on me that the
person standing near the parking lot was probably Silent Doug!!! I
head back and sure enough, find Doug and speedsquare trying to
figure out what to do. Later in the evening Doug admits that he
started to leave, after he couldn't find the restaurant, only to
think, "Wait a minute, I'll go back...maybe there's a clue!"

It was a fun night, thanks for meeting up!
