Boxing at Fort Custer, Battle Creek

From: ehughes47 (ehughes47@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-09-27 00:48:21 UTC
Well, we had a great day at Fort Custer today - finding all six
boxes we were looking for - not without a couple challenging moments
though.....not the least of which was being surrounded by GUNFIRE!
Yes sir, apparently there were squirrel hunters in the woods (that's
according to one of the bikers who passed us). Believe me that was
the fastest stamp job I ever did - though I replaced the box as
carefully as ever - us letterboxers can't be put off by a little
gunfire, right??? Really can't believe they'd allow shooting to go
on in a park while it's being used by non-hunters - if the hunters
want to shoot each other, that's another whole story.......

The other minor challenge was our fault - we REALLY have to get
better at this compass heading thing. Even have a book that
explains it - suppose it would help if at least one of us actually
read it!

If you haven't been to Fort Custer - it's a really great place to
spend a day.....letterbox, hike, bike, ride horses if you're lucky
enough to have one, fish, swim, picnic, and I guess in the winter
you can go watch some sled dog events....how neat would that be??

Hope you all had a nice weekend.....

Eileen

> Yes sir, apparently there were squirrel hunters in the woods....


There are parts of the Fort Custer Recreation area that are posted
for No Hunting, but as you noted, there are parts of the state land
in which hunting IS permitted.
Back when the TOTW was a listing of gear that letterboxers might
consider adding to their packs, I noted that we carry florescent
orange vests. It was during a letterboxing adventure in New Hampshire
that I first saw hikers wearing the bright blaze hunter gear. (They
even had 'em for their dogs!)So now during any hunting season, we
wear the orange. They're not terribly attractive, but it's pretty
cheap insurance.
For the record, we AVOID woods and fields that are open to hunting.
Parks that are adjacent to open-hunting land present problems as well.

It's best to know what the open seasons are and to check with the
local park rangers about off-limits areas.

azobox
