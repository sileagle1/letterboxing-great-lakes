Postal explanation (was Re: Michigan to Mississippi via Indiana)

From: Kathi LK Krankoski (krank@ameritech.net) | Date: 2004-11-23 09:52:58 UTC-06:00
A postal box is similar to regular, in that it has a stamp and logbook, and
you stamp as you normally would. Thing is that it isn't hidden with clues.
It gets mailed from person to person. Some have sign up lists and only
accept a certain number of participants. Some do have clues or other
conditions that must be met to receive it (See Fish Out of Water 3, for
instance). This one is different in that I want to share it as a Lb version
of Christmas cards with all the members of LbGLK, so I'm not limiting it to
numbers, and continuing to solicit people to join the list. I want it to
come home for Christmas, but will send it back out afterwards as needed to
get as many of us stamped in as possible.

So as I said on a previous message (assuming yahoo sends these out in the
correct order I'm responding!), send me your snail message offlist (ASAP)
and the Kranks will come for a visit! (Don't worry, we're good houseguests,
and don't eat much!)

--klk/FungusWoman
