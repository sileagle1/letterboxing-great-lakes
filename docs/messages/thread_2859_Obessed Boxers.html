Obessed Boxers

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2006-07-31 06:06:27 UTC
OK, like some others in this regional group, we know we're a bit
obsessed. Why else would people voluntarily go out in this heat &
humidity?!?!

First, here are the low points of the weekend: HEAT, LOTS OF
HEAT, "SWEAT DRIPPING FROM US MAKING IT TRICKY TO STAMP OUR LOGBOOKS
WITHOUT DESTROYING THE IMAGES" HEAT!

Annoying flies & mosquitoes. However they were no match for the PAIN
we experienced when stung by some type of ground wasps as we were
following Ebenezer's instructions in Blue Mounds, WI. Martini Man was
walking ahead of me when all of a sudden he started hopping around
yelling and swatting at his legs. At first I thought it was stinging
nettles (which we always seem to encounter), but he was even more
excited than usual. He told me he had been stung and I should be
careful. I then saw a swarm of bees a foot above the trail, so I
carefully took a detour around the area. We stopped a short while
later so MM could examine his wounds (He had been stung on each calf,
almost symetrically) and I could check the clues. I actually SCREAMED
when I was stung on the tip of my middle finger by some of the same
wasps! We did manage to find the box after all of this agony, but I
couldn't open it with my injured hand - MM had to take care of that.
This all happened before 2 pm. After a party at our friends in
Janesville and returning to our hotel room, we both were STILL in
pain at 11 pm that night. Luckily by the next morning we were
feeling OK.

OK, now for the HIGH points! We found 8 boxes in south-central WI on
Saturday (3 FF and 2 Hitchhikers). While at the Rest Stop box we saw
a teeny hummingbird. At first I thought it was a bee going from
flower to flower, but it looked odd. Upon closer observation I
realized it was a hummingbird - maybe a baby hummingbird? We also had
a nice lunch at the Grompy Troll in Mount Horeb. If anyone is boxing
out that way, we'd highly recommend this brew pub for lunch or dinner!

On Sunday we boxed in North Central IL, finding several River Rat,
Hiker Babe and Nature Nerd boxes. While biking on the Jane Addams
trail, a bird once again caught my attention. This time I yelled as
we were passing between some scenic rock walls and a LARGE turkey,
who was startled by us, flew directly over me from one side of the
wall to the other! I don't know who was more startled, the turkey or
me!

We also stopped in to see Raqs at her rummage sale, and managed to be
in time to say Hi! to Acorn & Golden Boy as they were leaving. Raqs
had done a great job hiding the Rummage Sale logbook and stamp, so we
had a challenge to get stamped into this one! We went on to find a
few others in the Rockford area before heading south to search for
some P. Granola boxes. We did well at Shabbona State Park, finding
all 7 new boxes (even though we were using flashlights for the last
two....). Our tally for Sunday was 20 boxes, 1 miscellaneous stamp
(the Rummage Sale stamp), and 1 hitchhiker. We reached a personal
milestone on the very creative "Creatures Along the Trail" series, in
that we logged our 1,500th box! This was about 2 weeks after our 3rd
anniversary of finding our very first box (on July 13, 2003).

Thanks SO MUCH to all of you that hide boxes for us to find,
especially Dragon, who did a lot to get the Milwaukee area seeded
with boxes. Our counts as of today are: 1504 boxes found, 70
plants, 268 exchanges, attended 7 events, found 72 hitchhikers, and
also stamped 94 "miscellaneous" images (personal travelers, traveling
event stamps, boxes we hid for others, etc).

After 3 years we are still enjoying this hobby immensely and are
looking forward to meeting more of you at events and enjoying the
adventures of looking for the boxes you place.

Thanks!

Wisconsin Hiker & Martini Man

From: (QuiltsNBees@aol.com) | Date: 2006-07-31 15:07:03 UTC-04:00
Sorry to hear about the wasp stings -- you are dedicated! As far as the
baby hummingbird, you might have been seeing a "Hummingbird Moth" (Hemaris
thyshe). They look like little hummingbirds, but just a little different. We see
them all the time during the summer and early fall at work. Book says they
inhabit forest edges, meadows and cultivated flower gardens and produce a buzz with
their wings that is similar to but softer than a hummingbird. They're cute.

'Boxing can be an obsession...

QuiltsNBees

Well, your story had me laughing... not at your pain, but at the fact
that you kept going. There's just something about letterboxing that
makes everyone temporarily insane so that we just can't give up even

For the wasp stings -- my parents live in Florida and are big golfers.
They have fire ants and bees (and probably wasps and other stinging
insects). They carry a benedryl spray bottle in their golf cart so
they can immediately put it on all stings. I was stung a couple of
years ago down there, and about 10 seconds after my dad sprayed the
benedryl on the sting, it stopped swelling and stopped hurting, so I
can vouch for the fact that it works, surely for bee stings. My
parents have used it with the same results on fire ant stings. So,
that might be something to add to the letterboxing pack.

Good luck on your next 1,500 finds!! Wow!
