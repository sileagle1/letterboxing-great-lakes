From: Chris Mothkovich (Eirene@xta.com) | Date: 2005-10-21 09:17:18 UTC-05:00
A couple weeks ago, I received a warning from someone through the
letterboxing website that letterboxes hidden on Winnebago County forest
preserve property (as two of mine are) were in jeopardy of being confiscated
by the forest preserve people. I have checked both of mine and they are
still in place so far. The warning mentioned that letterboxes were going to
be grouped with geocaches and under the same restrictions. I checked the
WNFP website and read that permits are required to place geocaches and
letterboxes. So far I haven't done anything.

Two of the six boxes I placed last year came up missing by early this spring
(neither on forest preserve property, however). I was pretty discouraged by
this. I am wondering whether it would be a good idea to pull all my boxes
in late fall/early winter and replace them in early spring, rather than
leaving them out to endure another northern Illinois winter. What do you
guys do?

Chris (Trail Rider)

Yeah, we'll teach them a lesson all right. How does a bear trap sound?

Seriously though, if it is a group of high schoolers, they really need
to find something else to do with their time. Stealing letterboxes is a
pretty stupid thing to do to get their kicks...especially when most
people have never heard of the hobby before. I can just see it now...
(fade into a HS hallway)

Teen 1: So, what did you do this weekend?
Teen 2: Oh man, you wouldn't believe it! I annoyed so many people by
going out into the woods and...
Teen 1: Eeeewwww! I've heard of those creepy people before!
Teen 2: No, listen. I went out to the woods, looked for these little
Rubbermaid containers, and get this, I threw them all away! (triumphant
grin)
Teen 1: Uh...how do these containers get into the woods?
Teen 2: These people called letterboxers place them there. It is a
scavenger hunt sort of thing that they like to do. Long hikes with a
reward at the end, you know.
Teen 1: So...you spent all your free time traipsing through the woods
looking for plastic containers just so you could throw them away and
annoy a few people? Dude, you're lame! (walks away)
Teen 2: But wait! It's funny though! (chases after Teen 1)
Teen 1: (mumbles) You wouldn't catch me in those creepy woods unless
there is a Hollister hidden in one of those little plastic boxes.

And that is how the story would go. Teen 2 would then be known all over
the high school as one of those creepy people who hang out in the
forest preserves all day...lol. Or something like that. ;)

Okay...I think it is time to go be productive. I think I'll skip out on
subbing today to go steal some geocaches!! Who's with me?

Well Angi, even though I was warned that the forest preserve peeps might
confiscate letterboxes, none of mine on WCFP property have disappeared. The
two boxes of mine that disappeared over the last winter were on state
property (Rock Cut) and Boone County property (Kinnickinnick). If anything,
I would blame a curious raccoon (perhaps one who had been trapped and
released by an annoyed suburbanite, and who thus knew that rubbermaid
containers mean FOOD) on the Rock Cut disappearance, and perhaps kids for
Kinnickinnick.

ALSO - if it was indeed the FP guys, why wouldn't they have taken ALL the
boxes? I checked on your "Tuber" just a short while ago and found it in
place - although it was uncovered. I piled the bark pieces back on top.

Anyway, I would think the FP would be more concerned about the prevalence of
solitary people sitting in cars waiting for a hookup in their parking lots -
especially Blackhawk Springs.

How long ago did you find Tuber? I was going to try to get out there
this weekend to see if I could find it. Today I checked Creatures...
#1 (with bonus box) and they were both gone.

Just to be on the safe side, I contacted the WCFPD to see if they
have been or plan on finding and getting rid of letterboxes in the
area. The only thing that makes me wonder if someone actually took
them or not is that there is not one trace that they were there
before. There are no plastic pieces from animals chewing them or

well.

I don't know. I'm going to drop the issue until I hear back from the
WCFPD. I just want to be able to pass on the information to the
others in the area if they are planning on picking them all up soon
so we can get them out of the preserves. :)
