From: lesann847 (Acorn@fools-errand.org) | Date: 2006-07-06 13:02:06 UTC
My faithful, intrepid squire and I continued the quest on Independence Day. Perhaps the
forces of evil were on holiday, but we encountered few obstacles at the waypoints of our
quest. Our greatest trial was the cost of fodder for our steed.

The shades surrounding Bring Out Your Dead lay at peace; although there were some giant
beech trees in the vicinity that looked like knight-eaters, we passed unscathed.

A foul miasma warned us of the vicinity of The Three Headed Knight before we reached
him, but did us no lasting harm.

Sojourner ghosts were resting, despite the damp cries of "Bring Out Your Dead." Water
had insinuated itself into the wrappings, but the book and image remained dry as dust.

While battles with fire from the sky and giant caterpillars enhance a knight's fame, I must
say that sometimes a glorious day with few struggles is a great blessing to this humble
and grateful knight,

Acorn
