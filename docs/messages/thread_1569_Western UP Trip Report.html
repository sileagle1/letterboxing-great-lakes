Western UP Trip Report

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2005-07-07 02:51:17 UTC
We just returned from a nice trip to the western part of Michigan's
Upper Peninsula (UP) and thought we'd post a report for those that
might be interested in reading about it.

Since we started from southern Wisconsin, we first had to pass
through northern WI to get to the UP. We stopped along the way to
find the Rib Mountain Rendezvous box and while we were there, also
checked in on the Rib Mountain box. Both were in good shape. Next we
hid a new box (clues will be posted in the near future). We also
took a detour west to find the Flying Fish and Smoky Bear boxes (both
in good shape).

[Side note: The box we placed in WI was in a formerly "boxless"
county. That leaves "only" 40 more counties in Wisconsin that are
boxless. It would be great if any Wisconsinites (or others traveling
in our state) considered placing a box in one of these counties. How
long will it be until Wisconsin is entirely "green" on the LBNA
site??]

Our ambitious goal in the UP was to "see the sights" by visiting all
the letterbox locations in the "western region" that we knew of at
the end of June. There was certainly a lot of driving involved in
touring this area filled with forests, lakes and rivers, but also a
lot of nice outdoor experiences.

We'll start by listing the boxes that we didn't actually find, even
though we were in the general location. The only clues that really
stumped us were the Copper Country Autumn box. The clues are 4
photos. We found the first easily enough and found the second after
some effort. Unfortunately our eyes weren't discerning enough to
recognize the third going forwards or backtracking and we weren't
optimistic about recognizing the 4th even if we did find the right
trees pictured in the third. Reluctantly we agreed to admit defeat.
For two other boxes (Big Bay Point Lighthouse and Hogback Mountain)
we were pretty sure we were in the right spot for both, but after
searching rather diligently, we couldn't find a box at either
location. Two other boxes we did find, Shamrock 1 & 2, but neither
contained a stamp or a logbook with any evidence that a stamp ever
existed. Instead the placer left "message cards" in the box, about
the size of a business card. Not surprisingly, the messages previous
visitors left were primarily "where's the stamp?" This format seemed
odd to us, especially since the boxes contained the standard "About
Letterboxing" handouts that describe a box as containing a stamp and
a logbook. We decided not to stamp in at these boxes.

Amazingly, we did manage to find all the other boxes and all were in
good shape. We added a few baggies to some new boxes that didn't
have any and dried out one or two that were slightly damp. This was
really good considering how much snow this area gets in the winter
and how "old" a few of the boxes are. We will send private messages
to all the placers to give them an update on the status.

Listed below are the boxes we found. Many of them had stamps from
some of Michigan's great carvers and there was also an excellent
stamp placed by a Floridian only a week or so before we visited.

Beach Bum
Beaver Trail
Canyon Falls
Fumee Lake
Henes Park Series #2 & #3 (After we got home we found out TJ_Mich was
doing maintenance recently and that he replaced #1 this weekend and
posted new clues)
HWY 41
Lake Gogebic
Leelinaw
Marquette Harbor Light
Presque Isle Park #1
Rails To Trails?
Rainbow Canoe
Readers Box
Sparrow Rapids (2)
Strawberry Lake Letterbox
Sugarloaf Mountain
Superior Falls
Superior View
The Engineer
The Paulding Light
U.P. Pasty
UP Wildflower Series - Blueberries
UP Wildflower Series - Jack in the Pulpit
UP Wildflower series - Silver Mountain Wild Rose
(We also found one hitchhiker during our trip)

Highlights of our adventures included:

3 scenic waterfalls (Superior, Canyon and Bonds) - Definitely
walk a bit further down the trail at Canyon to see the canyon walls
after the falls and also walk down to see Bonds Falls at Paulding
Light. After a short walk and some stairs, you will get to a point
where you can stand right next to the falls as they make their
initial drop. Pretty cool!

Hiking in the Porcupine Mountains.

Sitting on the beach right outside our hotel, drinking wine and
watching the sun set over Lake Superior at 10:00 pm. We love these
long days!

Picking wild raspberries and blueberries along the trails.

Seeing and hearing loons on the lakes.

Eating a pasty for dinner and find the Pasty box as a fitting
memento.

The views from Brockway Mountain Drive in the Keweenaw Peninsula.

Visiting the northern end of Hwy 41. (Maybe we'll visit the
southern end later this year)

A sense of accomplishment in getting to the summit of Hogback,
even though it was misty/raining. In those weather conditions we
certainly didn't have any view of Lake Superior - we were lucky to
barely make out some treetops below us.

HUGE expanses of forest.


Perhaps we'll make a trip to the eastern UP next year!

Wisconsin Hiker & Martini Man

P.S. We also hid two new boxes in the UP; clues will be posted in
the near future. One of the boxes is in the only western UP county
that didn't have a box yet Iron County. That region could now be
all "green" after I post the clues!
