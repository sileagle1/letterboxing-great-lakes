Help with LbNA statuses

From: Tom & Jill (tj_mich@ameritech.net) | Date: 2005-06-08 16:45:15 UTC-04:00
I'm sure most of you are aware of the recent enhancements on the LbNA
site, that
allow a Placer of a box to log the status as "OK," "Missing," "Retired,"
"Repair," or "Unknown." Also, everyone other than the Placer is able to log a
personal status for each box: "Found," "Attempted," or "Ignore." Only the
placer can see the entire list of everyone else's status.

The site's new Help page even suggests that Placers don't delete boxes that go
missing or are retired, since they would then disappear from everyone else's
"Found" lists. Instead, just mark the status appropriately. Makes sense to
me... until I found out yesterday that the statuses I assign to my
Placed boxes
are only visible to me, the Placer. They don't seem to be shown to
anyone else.
So now I'm confused. Am I missing something here?

I'd like to keep our missing/retired boxes listed on the site, if possible --
especially the missing ones that I plan to replace. But I don't want to do
this if it's going to cause confusion to all future searchers. Has anyone
figured this out? I can e-mail the webmasters with this question, but thought
I'd check within our local group first. Thanks for any help that anyone can
provide.

--Tom of TJ_Mich


Re: [LbGLK] Help with LbNA statuses

From: (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2005-06-08 16:44:56 UTC-05:00
I personally plan to delete any boxes of mine that disappear - I don't
like to clutter the site with boxes that no longer exist. Since not
all boxes I find (nor all boxes I place) are listed on LBNA,
the "count" on the site isn't accurate anyway. I am continuing to keep
my own list for box statistics.

However I do LOVE the tracking statistics on the site for several other
reasons. It gives you some info as to who has found your box and when,
and it allows you to "hide" all the boxes you have placed or already
found and thereby show only those that you still may want to hunt for.
This is a GREAT enhancement to LBNA!

Wisconsin Hiker
