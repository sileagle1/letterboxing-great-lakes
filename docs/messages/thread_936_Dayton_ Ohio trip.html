From: Doodle & Deedle Bug (doodle_n_deedle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-12-16 10:21:04 UTC-08:00
Well, of course we feel that our Kumbaya Letterbox in
Strongsville, Ohio is a "must see," but that's because
it's our first plant and my first carved stamp (Jeff
carved our current signature stamp). Well....it's
also our only plant so far. But with winter I've got
all sorts of ideas for new plants in the Cleveland
area...maybe some will be in place by the time you
pass through!

I'm very partial to the Giddyup Series in the
Cleveland area. Also, if you want an awesome view of
Lake Erie and a letterbox hunt to boot, look for On a
Clear Day in Lakewood. The Back Woods Goblin and
Kelmark (I don't think either belong to this list) are
the self-proclaimed letterboxing "king and queen" of
Medina county, which you'll pass through en route to
Columbus. They've both got some really fun and unique
plants in the area.

Keep us posted as your trip gets closer!

Doodle aka Diane.

From: Brenda Tassava (bktassava@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2004-12-20 23:39:30 UTC

There are several nice stamps in the Columbus, OH area. In Powell,
there is an intricately hand-carved stamp from CT that is a must-
see. While you're there, you can also go for "Buckeye State of
Mind", as it is nearby. Scoutdogs has a nice microbox from her Movie
line series in NW Columbus, and there is another plant
called "Doll's Head Swamp" a little further up. There's also a very
nice series in Gahanna at Friendship Park, which is in NE Columbus.
