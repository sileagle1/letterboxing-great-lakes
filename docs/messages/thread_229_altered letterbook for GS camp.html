altered letterbook for GS camp

From: Kathi LK Krankoski (krank@ameritech.net) | Date: 2004-05-22 22:26:02 UTC-05:00
Hey, everyone. I've been working all day (okay, not ALL day!) on a new Lb
for camp. I think I mentioned my idea a while back...an altered book that
would actually be the journal for the box as well as the hiding place of the
stamp. (There's a niche in the back of the book to hold the stamp.) Has
anyone ever done anything like that before? The book I'm using is an old
(1953) pronounciation dictionary, and my altering is pretty
rudimentary...basically just playing with some of my stamps to illustrate
words on some of the pages. On the page that has "BLIND", I stamped the
three blind mice. For "ALIEN" I stamped Worf from ST:TNG. A few pages are
a bit more elaborate: "ANTS": I multiply stamped an image of three ants,
and stamped out the words "the ants go marching 3 by 3". I just wanted to
do enough to give the girls some idea that writing in the book was okay, and
spark a little creativity without stiffling their own ideas by being too
overwhelming.

Anyway, I'd like to bring this along to the Gathering so that all of your
can stamp into it, if you don't mind. I'd like them also to see what some
"real" letterboxers' stamps look like (not the pathetic stuff I've produced
so far!!...I *really* need a workshop!).

The name of the box...er, book?...is "May I have a word with you?" The
clues imply the lodge it will be hidden in...oh, heck...here they are:
"May I have a word with you?

Ah, camp! The perfect place to relax and forget about your problems, at
least for a few days.

You say the Pioneer lifestyle is not for you? Well, perhaps youd prefer
some of our other accommodations. Perhaps some flush toilets? Indoor
fireplace and kitchen? Nearby swimming pool? A separate room for your
leaders to escape the noise of your troop? Sounds rather idyllic, dont you
think?

What? Whats that you say? Youre trying to read these clues to your
troopmates but dont know how to pronounce that "I" word in the last
paragraph? Well, you know what they always say, "Look it up in the
dictionary!" As a matter of fact, one of the amenities (okay, look that one
up too) at camp is a collection of books for your reading enjoyment. Find
the dictionary and youll find a most unusual letterbox!!"

["Pioneer" is the name of the first box I placed there...and refers to the
fact that it is hidden at a platform tent site called Trailblazer. The name
of the lodge is "Idle-A-While", hence the reference to "idyllic". The book
itself will be on a bookshelf by a set of old encyclopedias that never get
used, so no one will pay any attention to it unless they are looking for
it.]

Hey, this is kind of nice that you guys can't go looking for this box! This
way I can share this info with you instead!

--klk/fw

From: azobox (jparkerg@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-05-23 09:38:13 UTC
Kathi,
I've done quite a bit with books and niches.
Although I considered using one for a letterbox container in a
library, my plans were slowed when librarian acquaintences cautioned
me that the books are regularly moved within a libray's shelving
systems. That, of course, would preclude a successful and secret
placement without the "intruder" book being detected by staff.
So that plan went to the back burner.
Nevertheless, I have made many "hollowed-out" books and developed a
rather interesting method of removing a major part of the page block
without using a craft knife.
I'll bring some samples to the gathering, although the method is not
one that can be done without special power tools. And it does not
lend itself to conventional book altering.


azobox
