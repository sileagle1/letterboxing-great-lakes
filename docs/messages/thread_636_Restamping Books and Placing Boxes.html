Restamping Books and Placing Boxes

From: catscholar (catscholar@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-09-03 03:41:32 UTC
Hi! I've been lurking for awhile and trying to get up to speed as to
what discussions are going on here. I've been letterboxing for about
a month now and have found 17 letterboxes. This weekend I will be on
the lookout for a good place to place a letterbox.

I have some questions that I hope you will be able to help with.
When I first started letterboxing I was so excited that I just used a
store bought stamp when I stamped in a logbook. Now I have made my
own stamp with a picture and my name on it. How do you feel about
people restamping logbooks? My second question is about etiquette.
Is it okay to place a letterbox in the same park as someone else? I
know not in the same area but maybe on a different trail or on the
other side of the park?

I look forward to hearing from you!

Sealpoint

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2004-09-02 22:50:32 UTC-05:00
My opinion:

1. If you want to, feel free to restamp the books you already found and add
a note that you now have your "real" stamp. I've seen people stamped in
more than once - perhaps because they have a new stamp, are checking their
box, visiting again with friends or whatever.

2. I generally think it's nice to have a few boxes in a location,
especially if it is a larger park. Makes the trip all the more worthwhile!

Re: [LbGLK] Restamping Books and Placing Boxes

From: Delirium (donaldson_home@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-09-03 05:35:41 UTC-07:00
I agree about with the concensus on the restamping question.

As for placing boxes in the same park, I would suggest (and it's just that, a suggestion) that you contact the placer of the original box before placing your own. I've been on a few hunts where one person's box accidentally ruined a riddle from another person's box.

Aside from that, with a little courtesy, you may make an ally who will check in on your box while doing maintainence on yours. One of my boxes has another box, by a different person, in the same park, and we'll check on each other's boxes.

But I'm all for multiple boxes in parks. Like Wisconcin Hiker said, more boxes in one location makes for a good outing for me!
Cheers
Delirium
