Wave to the Fox??

From: SKJJJK (col.one923@spwl.net) | Date: 2006-01-15 00:52:22 UTC-06:00
After putting our letterboxing on the back burner for a few months busy
with school and what not, we just really had this song in our heads this
weekend....."On the first day of Christmas.....", thanks to the
CignaCats. We jumped in the car and headed to Brookfield, but also did
a quick check to see what else was in the area. I made a mental note of
a few nearby boxes, but our focus was going to be the 12 Days of
Christmas. We enjoyed the easy jaunt around the lake. Met lots of
dogs, runners, a few dogs, walkers, some dogs, in-line skaters, and more
dogs! Two out of the three kids even found a dog pile to step in! Part
way through the clue sheet, we were instructed to "wave to the fox".
Fox? What fox? We didn't see a fox or anything that looked like a fox.
We decided we were missing something that wasn't necessary to our hunt
and moved on. After finishing up, I began to think about the mental
notes I made about the other boxes in the
area......think.......think.......aha! Some of us are slow, but two
trips around the lake was worth it. We caught the best sunset and we
would have missed it if it weren't for the second trip around the lake.
It was a cotton candy sky and Blue Thunder thought it looked like a
rainbow of sorts. On the way back to the car, he stopped and said
"wait, I just like to watch the sky to see the clouds move" and then
commented about the tire tracks in the clouds. The colors were
gorgeous. Don't know what it is about letterboxing, but it has been a
few months since we've been out on the hunt, and I think it has been
about that long since I've noticed the sunset too. It was good to be
out on the hike again. That indoor track at the athletic club just
doesn't seem to measure up to a letterbox hike! We picked up a
hitchhiker along our way (thanks Cat Herders), so I guess that will be
an incentive to not wait another 2 months before we get out there again.

We did make a trip up to REI before heading back home again. Not sure
we would have found that box except perchance, we brought Little Green
Monkey with us (as we always do) and being the monkey he is, he was
crawling and somersaulting all over the floor as only the 5 under crowd
can do without really drawing too much attention to themselves. The
rest of us were trying to look discreetly, while "browsing" the store.
The clues were easy to decipher, but the hiding spot was not easy to
find. Thanks to Little Green Monkey we found it in short order and none
too soon as the store was closing just as we finished stamping in.
Green Monkey thought it was quite a novelty to find a letterbox in a
store, so when we stopped at Half Price Books before heading back out of
town, he thought we were looking for another letterbox. Sadly, he found
none there.

All in all, we had a great day. We listened to Les Miserables Radio
Theatre on tape for the drive over, had two great walks around the lake,
saw a gorgeous sunset, picked up a hitchhiker, were successful at REI
and finished the day out with the conclusion of Les Miserables. This
one goes in the books as a great day! Lots of memories to keep. Thanks
again, fellow letteboxers!

The Puddlejumpers
Hyacinth (10)
Blue Thunder (8)
Little Green Monkey (5)
