Member Intro: Yooperann

From: yooperann (wfisher47@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-09-18 12:04:32 UTC
I'm practically just a lurker on this site, but I read regularly and
still think of myself as a letterboxer, even though at this point it's
probably been a year since I placed a box or looked for one other than
one of my own.

I started letterboxing in the summer of 2002 after my daughter found
the LBNA link in a blog. I live just outside Chicago (Oak Park, about
to move to Forest Park) and have placed boxes around here, but am
frustrated when they keep disappearing. I've had better luck with my
boxes in the Upper Peninsula of Michigan, where we spend most of our
vacation time, and where I have a lot more time for carving and clues,
not to mention eager nieces and nephews who area always looking for
projects. I also like to look for boxes on trips. I'm going to
Stockholm in March and one of the first things I did after I bought my
tickets was to check whether there are any boxes there (unfortunately
the answer is no, or, more precisely, not yet!).

I'm a terrible carver (and somewhat intimidated by the incredible
artistry of many members of this group), but I love writing clues,
expecially clues that tell a story. I incorporated a lot of my
father's family history into a bunch of boxes we placed on a family
trip to Wyoming in the summer of 2003, and even though a couple of the
six boxes have disappeared, I still hear often from people who've
found them, including one recently who said they spent an extra day in
the area after they found my clues!

I've been to one LbGLK meet (the first) and one micro-meet here.
After we get settled in our new place next month I'm hoping to be able
to do more boxing and join you all in person more.

Thanks for the topic, SpringChick!
