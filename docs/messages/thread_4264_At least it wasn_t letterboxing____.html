At least it wasn't letterboxing....

From: Doodle & Deedle Bug (doodle_n_deedle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-07-23 06:08:28 UTC-07:00
Last night, Deedle & I were watching Law & Order:
Criminal Intent on Bravo, our typical Sunday night
gearing-up-for-another-workweek activity. In the one
episode, the detectives had a search warrant for the
murder suspect's car and were searching a backpack in
the trunk, which had a bunch of Nemo-looking fish
keyrings, along with some other miscellaneous
trinkets. He then pulled out some electronic device,
and as he pulled out a notebook I realized the
electronic device was a GPS unit. I looked at Deedle,
wide-eyed, and he had the same expression on his face.
We both cried out, "She's a geocacher!!!!"

Sure enough, in the next scene, back at the police
station, the detective had pulled up a webpage talking
about geocaching (I don't think it was the official
geocaching webpage, rather one made for the TV show's
use). Since the squad was looking for the murder
weapon, they got the idea that the suspect had hid it
in the park where she'd found her last cache. So the
next scene is a bunch of police searching this park.
Of course, when they found the cache, it was buried in
the ground, which I'm pretty sure is a geocaching
no-no as it is in letterboxing.

As Deedle & I said to each other, least she wasn't a
letterboxer!!!

Doodle Bug.
