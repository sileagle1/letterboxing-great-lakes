Holy Grail updates, IL

From: lcoil79@ameritech.net smokey (ainawgsd@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-09-01 19:50:47 UTC-07:00
Flying Dragon had to go fix his mom's pond today, so while we were in the area I decided to take the dogs and check on the Holy Grail boxes at Wolf Creek State Park (Sir Lancelot and King Arthur). FD got a report from some letterboxers recently that they had some trouble and couldn't find Lancelot. I am happy to report that all of the boxes in this park are in place and in great condition. The clues for Sir Lancelot do need a little tweaking as I had a hard time finding the box following them as written, but not much. And I am also happy to report that the ticks were not as bad today as they were last time I was there. I still came home with more than I wanted to, but mostly just the tiny ones and mostly on my feet where I obviously failed to apply an adequate amount of deet.

From: lcoil79@ameritech.net smokey (ainawgsd@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-09-02 18:42:53 UTC-07:00
Sure. Using the original clues, go right instead of left at the small maple (don't worry, you won't wander lost forever...the Red Fox trail is a giant loop, follow it long enough and it'll take you right back where you started) and follow the trail overlooking the lake around the bench and up the hill. Then continue down the trail (there will be a 90* right hand turn and a lot of winding) to the next intersection. From there, look for the arrow (it's sort of pointing off into the woods rather than down the trail) and follow the clues as written.

I suspect you got to the maple and then the next intersection was only a few feet past that (at least that's what I did when I misread the clues). When the clues were written, we hadn't noticed that trail at all! The revisions make the hike just a little longer and slightly more physically challenging since it throws in a little bit of a hill, but I suspect they will make the clues more clear.
