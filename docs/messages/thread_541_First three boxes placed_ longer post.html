First three boxes placed: longer post

From: Eloise Mason (nee Beltz-Decker) (eloise@ripco.com) | Date: 2004-08-13 20:06:48 UTC-05:00
They are:

Ducks and Drakes
http://letterboxing.org/BoxView.asp?boxnum=10056

Named for wildlife you're likely to see while pursuing the
cluetrail. :-> Quickish and fairly easy; probably 10-20 min start to
finish even with boggling at maps and other losing-of-the-trail.

Famous Faces #1: Abraham Lincoln
http://letterboxing.org/BoxView.asp?boxnum=10057

First of a series I plan to do which will each start from a statue
of some famous Dead White Male from US history. :-> We have a lot of such
statues, so it's not a horribly limiting theme for my carving skills. The
stamp is of the same person the statue was. Fairly simple; very scenic;
stroller-friendly.

Fitness Challenge #1: Downtown Marathon
http://letterboxing.org/BoxView.asp?boxnum=10059

First of a series intended to both be physically demanding and
urban-centered. Takes 40 min to an hour, probably, and is somewhat
trickier clue-wise than the other two. No stairs, just curbs and lots (and
lots!) of flat walking.



I'd actually be interested, as a new placer, in going 'hunting'
for these with another 'boxer, as a silent partner, just to see how
someone who isn't me interacts with what seem to me to be perfectly
reasonable clues. :-> I won't hint unduly, honest, and Dagonell can vouch
for how good of company I can be on a hunt. I'm free this Sunday-Tuesday,
and after that my schedule gets a little more complex, if you're local and
interested.
