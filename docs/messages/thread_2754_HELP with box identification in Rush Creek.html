From: Angi (ShutterflyIL@aol.com) | Date: 2006-07-10 02:19:57 UTC
Okay...Acorn is totally going to kick me for being so stupid even
though she tried to help me out with this box for SO LONG! Here is my
story, bear with me on this one...

Way back in 2004 when I first started letterboxing, my fourth find
was the Milk Capital box...or so I thought. You see, when Wiener
Mouse and I went searching for it, we went the OPPOSITE direction
from where we were supposed to go. We either didn't have a compass or
we didn't know how to read the one we had (my how much we've
grown...lol). Okay, so we get to the tree we thought we were supposed
to be at and searched like crazy to find it. We were so excited when
we actually did find the box. We stamped into it, wondering why a
handcarved SNAIL stamp was in the Milk Capital box. We shrugged and
put the box back where we found it and went on our merry way.

Fast forward about a year. I see a stamp in Laughing Coyote's book
that I don't recognize and I ask her where she got it. She tells me
it is the Milk Capital stamp. Confused, because it certainly wasn't
the one I found, I decide to go check it out. I went back to the
location to find the box once again and I have no luck. Since I found
the box once before, I didn't feel I needed the directions, so I left
them at home. I did the same thing EVERY time I went back looking for
the box. Every time I search and do not find anything, I ask Acorn
about it and every time she informs me that the box is still there.
Little did I know WE WERE TALKING ABOUT TWO DIFFERENT BOXES.

Fast forward to today. I went letterboxing with Trail Rider and I
decided that since I had been having so much trouble with it, I
wouldn't "help" her find it at all. I gave her the clues and she
started walking in the OTHER DIRECTION from where I had gone previous
times. Sure enough, I peeked over her shoulder (like I ever doubted
her) to look at her compass and noticed she was correct in going the
other direction. She found the box, safe and sound and without my
signature stamp in the log. This proved that I didn't find the box at
all, but a completely different one. Of course I finally stamped into
it...lol.

Now my question to all of you wise letterboxers is this: WHAT BOX DID
I FIND THE FIRST TIME? I could never find the clues to this box and I
have had it recorded as the Milk Capital box for over two years now
and that was completely wrong. There were a ton of other signature
stamps in the other box, so I know someone from this list had to have
found it. If you need to see a photo of the stamp in order to help me
out, let me know and I will email it to you.

Help me out here, people...PLEASE! :)

A very confused...Shutterfly
