potential new letterbox container???

From: Kathi Krankoski (krank@ameritech.net) | Date: 2008-05-29 20:36:53 UTC-07:00
Saw something interesting at Target today, and wondered if anyone has tried it yet.........
Ball (the canning jar company) has a new line of plastic freezer jars. The smallest size is 8 oz, and comes in a package of 4 for $2.99. They are round, with a screw cap lid which screws into the jar itself (not onto the jar, like a conventional lid). Unfortunately, the lid is a lovely shade of orchid (nice for hiding in a dense patch of violets!!), so it would need some camo tape. They seem sturdier than the disposable food storage containers, but lighter thanthe conventional kind. Being freezer jars, they should hold up to the winters. And the label says "Keep your creations fresh"! Maybe they even intend them for letterboxes! (-:
I've posted a picture in my FungusWoman album.
--klk/fw

Re: potential new letterbox container???

From: Kathi (krank@ameritech.net) | Date: 2008-05-30 14:33:11 UTC
I think my main concern is the way the lids fit into the jar, that
someone may not set them in straight and may screw it on at an angle,
making it less waterproof or even stripping the threads. I also
wonder if dirt or grit might more readily get in there, also
affecting the seal. I've used screwcapped plastic jars and
centrifuge tubes before, but with conventional lids, and haven't had
problems.

--klk/fw
