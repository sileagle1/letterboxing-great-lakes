DuPage County - RANT

From: Leslie (coyotespaw@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-05-03 05:40:50 UTC-07:00
So after careful design, carving, making the log book,
finding the perfect spot at the location that the
stamp goes with, I finally had my first letter box
ready to plant... and remembered "Darn, someone said
I need a permit to place this in DuPage County Forest
Preserves."
So, trying to do the right thing, I call up the
offices, and find out that along with the permit, that
has to be approved before you can place the box, there
is a $35 fee.
Don't get me wrong, I love to support the forest
preserves, the money goes to a good cause.... but I
went to their website, and they have fees for
everything! Fees for the off-leash dog areas, for
model boats, model airplanes, even use of some of the
grills in the preserves! OK, I was disturbed.
Unfortunately, I will now not be placing my box where
I wanted, and will try to find either a city park
close by, or over the border in Cook County.

Howling Coyote

From: Mosey (PonyExpressMail@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-05-04 10:18:25 UTC-05:00
I read the DuPage County rules that were uploaded to the files section. I
think one line is pretty indicative of the county's thinking.

I don't think they really want geocachers (and from what I understand,
letterboxers are lumped in there too). I think they say it all in one
sentence. The sentence where they state that virtual caches are welcome. I
think that says it all. : )

I've lived in DuPage County for over 50 years and the preserves are
beautiful. They're not as pretty as areas that many other states have but
they're beautiful given the features they have to work with here in DuPage
County. Even though there are a ton of volunteers, it also takes a ton of
employees to maintain the preserves. A lot of money goes into both buying
the land and then doing the maintenance and repairing any damage.

The rules listed for the geocaches (and letterboxes) involve having to fill
out paperwork that an employee or two is going to have to review. Then the
placer has to meet an employee (not a volunteer) on the site of where the
cache/box will be placed to determine that it's a suitable site. Then the
placer has to check in with the employee once a month stating that the
cache/box has been checked that month and that there's no problem. And if a
report doesn't come in, I'm assuming that the employee then has to go yank
the box, or tell another employee to.

They also might be worried that if no-gooders show up to vandalize the
boxes, will they also damage something else in the preserve while they're in
a vandalism mood? Mmeaning more expense? In other words, the permit fee
also includes a sort of "liability tax" maybe?

Is that something the taxpayers should have to pay for or should it be a
pay-as-you-go type of system? I can see both sides. People that play golf
have to pay, people that want to be involved with horses (Danada) have to
pay, people fishing have to pay for a fishing license, people using the dog
parks now have to pay -- everybody pays their part if they're using the
preserves for anything more than hiking, sunbathing, or eating their lunch.
That keeps the general all-purpose tax that I (and all DuPage homeowners)
pay on my property taxes a bit lower.

On the other hand, it keeps many homeowners who are already living from
paycheck to paycheck from being able to participate in many of the programs
offered. (Of course, they CAN still participate in geocaching or
letterboxing as long as someone with more money has placed caches or boxes.)

I think the outlay of $35 per each little cache or box seems high because
it's all concentrated to the placer. There's no way to charge the finders.
Whereas if a group is having a picnic, the fee can be divied up among all
the participants. Or if a person is taking a class, the fee can be
proportionally lower because many people are going to be signing up for that
same class.

Awhile back, I was up in Lake County to go look for a couple of boxes in
Independence Grove. I was unpleasantly surprised to find that there was a
$5 fee for folks to enter the area if they're not Lake County residents.
And that's just to get into the park itself. There's no guarantee that the
boxes will still be there or will be in good condition or anything. So as a
finder, I don't know as that I want to be charged much to go looking for a
box that might not be there or might not be useable. : ) So a box could
have 100 finders. But unless the finders are charged, the poor placer has
to pay a lot more. I didn't consider $5 a bad price tho. I didn't end up
finding either box and I don't think I'll pay another $5 for the privilege
of going back up there and searching again, but if I'd found the boxes the
first time, it would have been worth it to me.

I would have preferred if they left it on the honor system to begin with in
DuPage County. Just state environmentally safe rules they want followed and
see if people stick to them. If there were any problems, THEN go with the
permits, screens, checks, etc or eliminate caching and boxing entirely. But
I don't think they want the caches out there period, because caches are a
dime a dozen around here. I think the county minds might be working
overtime in worrying about the possible damages.

I had hoped to replant Pippi's and Keith's boxes. At $35 a whack, I can't
afford it. I might try a couple and see how it goes. Has anyone on here
actually gone through the system and had a box approved yet?

Mosey

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-05-04 12:47:45 UTC-04:00
I've been watching this DuPage County thing with interest because Michigan actually has a very similar policy in their State Parks. It was instituted last spring, but nobody seems to be enforcing it. I haven't yet decided what my course of action will be once they decide to put their foot down.

Every year I pay the State of Michigan for a pass for my vehicle -- I have been doing this for years, since I had my own vehicle -- the cost is currently at $24/yr. This enables me to use any state park in Michigan, for the entire year. Now if I want to enter the park with my yearly pass and fish, I need also to have a state fishing license -- this allows me to fish for an entire year at any park in the state -- for $28/yr I can get both the standard and the salmon upgrade. If I want to use my state park pass and take my vehicle off-roading I need also to purchase an off-road vehicle license -- for about $20 I can off-road at any orv area for the entire year.

My point is -- most all of these things are charged and licensed annually. I would not mind paying an annual fee to be able to plant letterboxes in the park -- as many as I want, at any park, for the entire year. But $35 each is a bit ridiculous.

SpringChick

I'm not sure if you're talking about DuPage County, IL or if you're talking
about SpringChick's MI, but if you're talking about DuPage County, I think
you'd have to be a registered voter of DuPage County. Or possibly be a
non-resident but have a friend(s) either on the county board or on the
forest preserve district board (they're two separate groups now). The
county board would have to be approached.

It might work to the negative for non-residents to complain. And I don't
know just how many registered voter letterboxers there really are in DuPage.
The pendulum of emotion has sort of been swinging against non-residents
lately. For example, with the dog parks. The fact that all dogs now need
their own license to be present in our really nice off-lead dog park areas
didn't sit well at first with many residents. But when people realized that
it will help keep many of the Cook County dog owners out of the DuPage dog
areas -- Cook County owners can obtain licenses also but at higher prices --
it greatly grew in favor and the idea passed. People had often been getting
more than a little ticked off at all the Cook County dog owners bringing
their dogs into DuPage dog parks. Cook County just doesn't have much at ALL
in the way of nice dog parks in the suburbs where dogs have a lot of room to
legally run and play off-lead. Instead of pushing for dog parks in their
own areas, large numbers of Cook County residents were bring their dogs to
DuPage dog parks.

So I can imagine the possible reaction if non-residents complain about the
placement fee for letterboxes. "Awwwwwww....poor babies.........let them go
place letterboxes in their OWN preserves...." I don't know if they've done
so already, but they might even think up a HIGHER fee for non-residents to
place a box if non-residents happen to complain. : )

Another thing to consider is the fact that DuPage County is one of the
wealthiest counties in the country when it comes to household income. It
ranks in the top 20 in the entire country. Altho I think Lake County beats
it out. I think Lake County is something like #5 in the country and DuPage
is something like #10. $35 might not seem like much to most residents here.
So the general income of the area might be something to consider.

A lot of people who work in this county can no longer afford to live in this
county. I know with me that all I need is one more property tax increase
(literally) and I'll have to move. I've got one of the cheapest houses
currently available in the county. So if I do end up having to move, I'll
be forced out of DuPage if I want to stay with a single family unattached
home. I just can't afford the mortgage plus the property tax. But them's
the breaks. Then I'll be one of those people paying for a non-resident dog
license for the dog parks. : )

So DuPage is both beautiful and ridiculous all the way around.

But if you were talking about MI, then ignore all of the above.

Mosey

Why is everyone ranting? There is no fee for placing a geocache or
letterbox in DuPage county. The individual handling the new permit
system is a geocacher himself and promotes the activity. He was the
sponsor for the geocache activity at Fullersburg preserve on Earth
Day teaching visitors how to use the GPS and find a hidden geocache.

For those interested in applying for a permit in DuPage county, look
at the rules and regs in the file section and call the visitor
services number. They will send you an application.They expect to
post their rules and application form on their web site in a few
months.

I have three permit applications in now. Everyone at the forest
preserve has been VERY friendly and helpful working through their new
process. I have worked with their representative at Lyman Woods and
she approved my place and even thanked me for bringing more visitors
to the preserve.

You won't have to do anything extraordinary to get the permit and I
have found it very easy to work with them. Friendly and supportive
they are!
CMoon

Thank you for your recent inquiry regarding geocaching within the

additional questions.
Thank you,
Lori Kennedy
Visitor Services
Forest Preserve District of DuPage County
(630) 871-6419
