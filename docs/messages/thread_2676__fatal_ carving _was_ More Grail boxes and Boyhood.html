Oh, Dragon! I know how you feel because I've done that exact thing
myself ... more than once, even!

After the first time and doing the actual re-carving, I decided I
would try to fix it before doing all that again.

You know that acrylic gel medium I use for mounting my stamps? Well,
I love that stuff because it's strong and I use it for a million
things. I used it to repair two of those "fatal" carving errors.

The first one was large enough that I could glue the little piece of
carving material back in place with the gel medium. And after it
dried, it stamped up just fine.

There was one "fatal" error that was such a tiny bit of fine detail
that putting carving material back in place was going to work. I just
used my magnification light, a tiny straight pin and some gel medium
to "build" the missing area. After I built it up, I stuck my finger
in some soapy water and smoothed out the top of the gel medium, wet
the straight pin in soapy water and scraped off the excess gel medium.
Once this dried it also stamped up pretty darn good. And I saved
myself a ton of re-carving time. (grin)

Just a thought from the field (so to speak) You might want to give it,
or something like it a try.
