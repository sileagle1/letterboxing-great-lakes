Western Suburbs (Chicago) Documentary

From: cdem_ltrbox (wp4@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2006-03-09 05:14:35 UTC
Although I like searching for just about any letterbox, ones with
some interesting historical or natural information about the site
really appeal to me. Last night, MoneyMan and I were watching a
documentary on our local PBS station about various communities in
the western suburbs of Chicago that are on the Burlington or Chicago
& Northwestern train lines. They would give some interesting
information on the community or its history. After watching for a
while, I noticed that we had visited a number of these spots
searching for letterboxes (which I thought was kind of cool). The
first letterbox we ever found--Architectural Mystery--was about one
of the architects mentioned on the show. They showed the "Cars on a
Pole" in Berwyn (although we could never find that box). Other
areas mentioned that we have found or searched for letterboxes were
Brookfield Zoo, Riverside, Graue Mill, Mayslake, Wolf Road Prairie,
Hoffman Tower in Lyons, the Naperville River Walk.

One of the last suburbs mentioned was Forest Park, where we have
planted several boxes. The show mentioned the Jewish cemeteries
(where Cemetery Strolls #2 and #3 are) and Showmen's Rest (which
would be Cemetery Stroll #1).

When they were talking about Berwyn, they mentioned the annual Houby
Days Parade--to honor mushrooms. That sounds like a box made for
FungusWoman!

From: ruhlette (ruhlette@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-03-09 17:03:19 UTC-08:00
I wish I could see that special. I lived in Forest Park for a couple of
years and Berkeley for 7+ years. There is a letterbox about 1/2 block
from my very first house. I can't think of anything significant about
Berkeley, except that World Dryer was located there. Used to be the only
name in hand dryers at fast food restaurants. Check them out, World
Dryer, Berkeley, IL 60163.

My brother was shocked that I would move from my Forest Park apt. The
location, above Golden Drugs, Harlem and Madison. Literally, I could walk
down my steps and walk to more pubs/restaurants than I had fingers and
toes, and not have to find parking. Oh, to be young again. My favorite
places were ParkyDogs (for the fries), and Johnny's Beef (Italian Beef and
Italian Ice) on North Avenue. Lots of other great spots too. Not exactly
on the Metra Pub Crawl line, but hikers won't noticed the difference. I'm
going to try to get back to Chicago this summer, after the GL gathering.
Bird Woman, my mom is going to visit her sister, Aunt Tillie, next
weekend.

The Program is called "Chicago's Western Suburbs: From Prairie Soil
to Prairie Style". It will be on 2 more times in March on Channel
11: 3/12 at 7pm; 3/20 at 7:30pm.

During the pledge breaks, the director mentioned that he had already
done several other shows on the Chicago suburbs and is working on
one now about the Fox River Valley and the SW suburbs. I think it
would be interesting to find these videos at the library or
wherever; esp. since we had a big boxing trip in the north shore
suburbs in January (and a number of Beetle's boxes had an historical
angle) and there have been a number of boxes listed in Chicago
recently with a historical bent.

Actually, I just went and checked our library system's Web catalog
and there are 3 DVD's listed: NW suburbs, North Shore suburbs, and
South suburbs/NW Indiana. The producer--Geoffrey Baer--also has one
on Chicago Neighborhood via the "L" and one on Chicago by boat (and
you don't have to worry about a tour bus dumping sewage on you while
you're on this video tour:)
