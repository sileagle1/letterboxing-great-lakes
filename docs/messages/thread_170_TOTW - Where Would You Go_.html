Re: TOTW - Where Would You Go?

From: Deb (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-04-26 18:18:54 UTC
Okay, if the world were my playground, I would choose Dartmoor. How
could one not want to be immersed in the history, walk the places
where others walked in search of letterboxes so many years ago?

But, if I were limited to the US, Connecticut would not be my first
choice. Yes, lots of boxes and very nice people and such. But,
maybe too many boxes for me to find the challenge. I would have to
say that my first choice would be the Pacific NW. Perhaps because
that is an area I have not ever visited before, but more likely
because I am less prone to be stumbling over box #400 while
searching for #399. And the hikes... there is something to be said
for that also. I rather like letterboxing that requires some
rigorous hiking to get the prize.

Of course, this is all generalization as there are no
doubt "quick 'n easy" boxes in the PNW, and there are certainly
certifiable LB hikes in CT.

Close second is Maine. A large part of that is just because I would
love to travel to Maine, but there is also quite an intrigue to try
my luck at a few of those legendary legerdemaine boxes.

SpringChick

1) where would you go if you had the opportunity to take a
letterboxing trip anywhere in the US?

I'd go back to the Pacific Northwest, in a heartbeat! In Feb, I was
in Portland, OR on business for a week. In the 2 days of free time I
had available, I was able to meet some really terrific local boxers
who helped me do some power-boxing; there are more boxes in the
Portland area than in the entire state of Mich, I think! (grin)
These wonderful friends also took me on a couple of fantastic hikes
through old-growth forest... amazing, breath-taking, awe-inspiring
and I didn't get nearly enough time to indulge in exploration!

2) In the world? Why would you choose to go to that particular area?

Ooooh... tough one. I think it's a toss up between Dartmoor and
Costa Rica. Dartmoor because well, HISTORY and Costa Rica
because ... one word: sailing! (grin)

Without telling my answer to him, I just asked Bindle Bum and he
picked "the Bahamas" and gave same reason. Simpatico, hey? (grin)

Enjoy!

Spring Chick:

Speaking of "rigorous hiking"...if you ever happen to travel down
toward the Shenandoah valley, you MUST check out a letterbox that is
in the Shenandoah National Park! It's called the "Bear Fence
Letterbox", and is located at Mile 55 in the park, at the Bear Fence
Rock Scramble. This hike is AWESOME...It takes you up and down
twisty paths and huge boulders, and exposes you to mountain views
that take your breath away! The terrain made it a challenge, but not
to the point of wanting to give up...and finding the letterbox was
such a thrill. It was planted by a young couple in the spot where he
proposed marriage to her (romantic, huh?). This was--hands down--
the very best hike I have ever taken in my entire life.
