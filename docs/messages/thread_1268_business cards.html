From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-05-03 15:27:33 UTC
Okay, so I wasn't at the mini-meet, but I am curious about the business
cards. Are these like geocaching signature cards? Do you leave them
in the boxes you find as a signature or the boxes you plant to provide
info, or both?

I know about a year ago Azobox showed me some business-card type cards
he was using in letterboxes. They had a nice background picture with a
tree that kind of looked almost like a watercolor or chalk picture and
had all the pertinent LB info. I thought these were kind of a neat
idea. I think this is even the company he mentioned he had gotten them
from.

Is this what you guys are referring to? Are there other people doing
this now too? It's kind of a nice clean way to leave your info in the
box -- very convenient for people to quickly take one rather than have
to write down your e-mail from inside the log book, etc.

SpringChick

Re: [LbGLK] Re: business cards

From: Doodle & Deedle Bug (doodle_n_deedle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-05-03 10:24:17 UTC-07:00
Deedle & I print up "business cards" to put in our
plants. Rather than leaving in full sheets or half
sheets of paper with the history of letterboxing and
what it is, we parred it down to "Congratulations, you
have found a letterbox! If you don't know what
letterboxing is, take this card and visit
www.letterboxing.org to learn more." We also have our
email address on the cards, so people can take the
card and contact us if our box needs attention. We
just printed them off on our home computer on the the
Avery business card sheets.

Doodle Bug.
