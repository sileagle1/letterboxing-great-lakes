From: Coffee Beans (coffeebeans06@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-05-23 16:03:18 UTC-05:00
Hehe, well, that's sorta why we had to get the grail quest done sooner
rather than later (well, not really :P). Our wedding isn't going to be
until next spring though, and we managed to have some time earlier this
spring for a few weekend road trips. :) And it's a good thing we did,
at the rate gas prices are going up. I've seen a couple stations here
in Chicago where the premium gas is up to $3.99... eeek!

But anyway, really, once we got going with the quest, we built the whole
thing up and just really wanted to finish the quest first. I know, I
know -- it wasn't all about the competition (although there were a few
other boxers out there hot on our trail which kept us going... you know
who you are ;) ), but we are both dorks and wanted to be more "cool" in
the eyes of our fellow letterboxers in the region by finishing the
quest... and finishing first just adds that much more to the "cool"
factor. ;) And yes, we realize that "cool" can also be read as "crazy,"
as our families keep telling us. :P

But we had so many memorable (again read that to be "crazy") trips doing
these boxes that made it all worth it. For example, our last box ended
up being "The Old Man from Scene 24," which we did at dusk on Saturday
April 7th, right during that huge snowstorm that hit Cleveland and
prevented the first weekend of the Indian's home baseball games. No, we
hadn't looked at the weekend forecast (granted that was silly of us, but
really would you have thought that there would have been *that* much
snow... at the beginning of April?!). Of course, after driving from
Chicago to Cleveland (oh, I didn't mention that we didn't take the most
direct route in any way/shape/form --seriously, I dare you to even try
to guess--, but if I tell everyone that, we will just be forever marked
as "those crazy Chicago boxers" no "those cool Chicago boxers" and Atom
will never let me live it down, so I won't), we weren't going to *not*
get the box, so we trudged through the park at dusk through at least a
foot of snow and still heavy snow falling and found our last Grail box. :)

So anyway, if you have the time/resources for the quest and are up for A
LOT of driving around, it's definitely worth it -- lots of memorable
experiences and new places to see. :)

Coffee Beans
