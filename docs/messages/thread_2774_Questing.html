Questing

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2006-07-16 05:07:49 UTC
When we heard that the Catapulted Cow had landed in a new spot, we
decided it was time to continue our quest for the Holy Grail in the
southern realms of our region. Some may think us foolish knights to
undertake the quest on such a scorching day, but we consider
ourselves brave & hardy knights and would not let heat deter us.

We set a lofty goal of visiting nine auspicious locations to help us
in our ultimate quest. However time did not allow us to meet this
goal and we were satisfied instead with finding eight new boxes and
grail clues. In addition, we had some interesting diversions to find
six other boxes including one called a "mystery" box. This box was
placed in 2004 and the tenacious "Team Taco" was the first to visit
it in 2005. Surprisingly, we were only the second visitors, almost
two years after the box was placed!

Our grail quest took us to several locations. However what should
have been shady forests seemed more like steamy jungles and the
pleasant prairies were more like blazing deserts! The torrid heat
and the vicious deer flies were impediments, especially in our search
for the Grail Beacon. However we persevered and secured the
important stamps and codes. Not surprisingly, we were not fit to be
seen (or smelled) in public after these challenging excursions. We
therefore headed home for a refreshing shower and some tasty
margaritas. We think margaritas should be the official Grail
beverage!

Wisconsin Hiker & Martini Man
