Holiday Boxing Report (WI & MN)

From: wi_letterboxer (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2004-07-05 22:54:52 UTC
Since biking is still not feasible due to my arm injury (I'm the one
in a sling in the Gathering photo), we decided to spend the holiday
weekend LBing. We planned a large circuit that incorporated a hike
with our hiking club.

We started out after work on Friday and headed to Norwalk and the
Tunnel #3 box. We discovered that while it is possible to find this
box by starting at 8:15 pm, we HIGHLY recommend searching for this
box earlier in the day! The route is somewaht challenging and the
fading light made it more treacherous. We found the box at 9:00 pm
and made it back to our car by 9:45 pm. As our friends said later in
the weekend - we are two SUPPOSEDLY intelligent people, but going for
the box at that time of day was probably not the wisest decision...

We spent the night in La Crosse, then began our Saturday searching in
a nearby county park. We planned this section of our circuit to take
us north along the Mississippi River, then hook back east and south.
We were very happy to find all the boxes we searched for:

Goose Island
Daisy Deer's Birthday Party (3) - First Finders!
Trout Creek - First Finders!
Red Wing
Elk Mound
Bell Mound

We stayed in Black River Falls on Saturday night. On Sunday morning
we joined members of our hiking club for a hike in the state forest.
During the hike we planted a new box (clues will be posted soon).
After the hike we looked for the 2nd box of Dragon's WI Mystery box.
This was our 2nd attempt, but no luck! We then headed to Wausau and
found the following in good shape:

Rib Mountain - First Finders!
Edgar Allen Hippoe - First Finders!
45-90

After spending the night in Wausau, we headed further north on Monday
morning and were successful in finding the Timm's Hill box. We then
headed south to return home, stopping in Baraboo along the way to
find the Man Mound box.

It was fun visiting places we'd never been to before and learning
some interesting tidbits along the way. All in all, a great weekend!

Wisconsin Hiker & Martini Man
