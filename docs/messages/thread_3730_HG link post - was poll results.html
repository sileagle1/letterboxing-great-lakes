From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2007-02-14 06:56:48 UTC-05:00
For the most part I would agree with what WI Hiker has said, particularly when it comes to duplicating a box idea as a postal. I also consider postal letterboxing to be a completely different hobby -- not that it is bad, just that it is not the same thing and really has grown to the point where it has taken on a life of its own, far beyond the definition of traditional letterboxing.

With letterboxing as prolific and widespread as it is, duplication is inevitable. There was a time 3-4 years ago, particularly when all clues were still being posted to the big list, that it was easier to have a handle on what was happening across the country in letterboxing. Not only did you see all of the clues as they were posted, but it was not uncommon for people to share ideas they were working on, etc. But with the way letterboxing has grown by leaps and bounds and monster jumps, it would be nearly impossible to keep tabs on every new box or series that is posted, even sometimes within your own area of the country. Along with this there is more of a disconnect with other letterboxers across the country, and thus a lesser degree of respect for other people's ideas. Very rarely do we see a person post about an idea they are working on, because invariably if/when they do, somebody comes along and swoops up the idea for themselves.

But having said that, I would agree that nobody owns an idea and it is to be expected that if you had a brainstorm that manifested itself into an awesome box or series, somebody else will eventually have a similar idea.

As a box planter, I do generally try to find out if my newest brainstorm has already been done by browsing the clue sites and/or reading the lists, etc. But this is mostly because I want my series to be original, not because I feel that it is sacred ground if somebody has already done it. If I find that there is already something similar out there, I may decide to scrap it or I may decide that my execution is unique and do it anyway. I have written to other planters and asked them if they would be okay with me doing something similar, but if it is in a completely different area of the country and the other box/series is not brand new, I may not even do that. Depends on the situation.

When I first started letterboxing, one of the first series I did was a Michigan lighthouses series. I planted a couple right away and while I was busy carving up stamps for the next few, somebody else planted similarly-themed boxes in the exact locations I was targeting. As I already had the stamps carved and had visions of doing this multi-box lighthouse series, I was disturbed. But in reality, how could they have known I was doing this? I ended up writing to this person and asking how they felt about me planting my boxes anyway, as long as I kept the location somewhat removed from their boxes. She was okay with that and I did plant my boxes. Little did I know that the whole idea was doomed as these stamps would routinely disappear, no matter how often I replaced them. I used to joke that there were people out there collecting SpringChick lighthouse stamps, just waiting for me to post a new one. Anyway, the point is that nobody owns an idea or a location and if you letterbox long enough you will at one time or another find yourself on both sides of the issue.

All this being said, there are a few things that I think a person should try to avoid out of respect or courtesy...

- Planting a similarly-themed box in the same/very close location that another boxer has a letterbox is not cool
- Scooping another person's idea before they have executed it is stooping pretty low (assuming you were aware they were doing it)
- Duplicating another person's complete idea is less than creative -- planting a duplicate Circus Train series is one thing, but executing it in the exact way the previous planter did, i.e. clue style, series make-up, etc.is a bit much
- Planting a box in very near proximity to another person's box that gives away something about their box -- for instance if their clues are mystery clues and your nearby box solves the mystery elements of the first box (this is tough though because sometimes, particularly when a box is a mystery, it is conceivable that you may not know you are coming behind and doing this)

But hey, when it comes down to it -- nobody owns a letterbox idea or location and we all have different opinions on what is or is not respectful and courteous.

SpringChick

I pretty much agree with what Trekkie Gal, WI Hiker and SpringChick
are saying. I just want to point out a few things about the ring in
question since I happen to be a participant in it:

It's a Monty Python themed ring. That means ANYTHING Monty Python.
Granted, lots of people chose to do images based on the Holy Grail.
But there's also the Arguement Clinic, the Ministry of Silly Walks,
and my own Spanish Inquisition stamp in addition to a few others.

Out of the 20 or so people in the ring, only three of us are in the
Great Lakes area. I wouldn't be too worried about competing Holy
Grail boxes in the Great Lakes area.

When my stamp returns, I'll probably plant it out in the wild.
Why? It's a stamp of Terry Gilliam (not to give too much away for
those other two of you in the ring) and since he was born in
Minneapolis, it's appropriate. Now if I had chosen a Holy Grail
themed stamp, I would definitely check and make sure I wasn't going
to be stepping on any toes before I planted it since I know about
the Grail project.

Overlapping ideas is inevitable, especially with popular things like
Monty Python, the circus and lighthouses. I don't worry about it
because the chance of it being carried out in the exact same way is
pretty slim. And if it is? Then I can see being upset and
complaining.
