From: azobox (jparkerg@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-07-25 12:47:34 UTC
I had an interesting experience yesterday which made me think of our
recent discussions about new players in the letterbox game. I was out
doing a bit of maintenance and checking on some of my boxes.
The first thing I noticed was that several (okay, only four out of
six) were not re-hidden well at all. That happens, right? But the
other common thread was that two of them were signed-in within the
pasts three days by self-described new letterboxers, one group
without stamps yet. Logbooks were not returned to plastic bags in
several cases.
I was reminded of a comment on another list that we should endeavor
to treat all boxes as though they were our own, both to protect their
future and to show the best example to new letterboxers.
I'm encouraged to read the enthusiasm of some new members here.
