Gathering etiquette

From: duncan53211 (duncan53211@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-06-10 03:37:53 UTC
Just a couple of reminders as we near the gathering:

1) If you are bringing hitchhikers to the gathering, please do not
place them out "for everyone to stamp into" unless this has been
specifically requested by the owner of the HH. Hitchhikers may be
placed in the various boxes at the gathering for others to find and
move on as normal.

2) If there is anything on the "Show Your Stuff" table, it is there
for viewing only. Do not stamp any letterboxes on that table - you
must find them when they are placed if you want to record them as a
find and get their stamp image.

3) There is a specific time set aside for exchanges. Although there
will be letterboxers arriving late, leaving early, etc., as much as
possible, leave all exchanging to that time frame and be there for
others to exchange with you at that time. Exchanges can be made at
any time, but if it is left "willy nilly" as last year, everyone finds
themselves exchanging all day with no time to box!

4) It is not really proper to ask someone if they have a WOM box. You
might ask if they have a clue to share, but even that is borderline.
Just keep your eyes and ears peeled when you are at the gathering, and
on this talk list. BTW, I think some people may be leaving for MI
soon, so some people might want to make certain post no later than
this weekend ;-)

5) If you are in to collecting personal travellers, don't forget to
check for their clues.

Thanks!
See you at the gathering!
Dragon

you to ask someone you know that has a WOM box for their WOM clue. It

From: (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2005-06-13 13:37:28 UTC-05:00
We'll be interested in seeing and stamping the Circus stamps! We'll
count them the same as we do all other "traveling event" stamps -
as "Miscellaneous". It's a category we use for anything we don't count
as a Find, Plant/Place, Event or Hitchhiker. We've chosen to only count
one official Event stamp in our Event count, so our "E" count simply
represents the actual number of events we have attended. Any other
extra event stamps get included in our miscellaneous count. In addition
to Traveling Event stamps, our Miscellaneous count includes personal
travelers, HH someone else has in their possession and offers to have
us stamp, postal boxes, etc.

I know everyone does it differently, but that's the approach we've been
using.

Wisconsin Hiker

From: TJ_Mich (tj_mich@ameritech.net) | Date: 2005-06-13 14:40:20 UTC-04:00
One idea might be a separate "EB" count for all the interesting boxes that are
available at an event, but require no problem-solving to find. I'd use it for
boxes like these, and others that are too fancy to hide, and are just
available
on a table to stamp into. We've only been to one event so far (the 2003 GLG)
and there was only one box that fit that criteria -- one of the "K-Martha"s --
so just counted it as "F" since we had no other category.

I think it does make sense to keep this type of box separate from:
- the "E" count, which shows how many events you've attended.
- the "F" count, which seems best for boxes that you truly have to find...
whether by word-of-mouth clues, or published clues distributed at the
event, or
so on.

Will be interested to hear others' ideas on this topic. I certainly
don't worry
too much about how we count our boxes, but it's nice to have a
consistent method
to use.

--Tom of TJ_Mich
