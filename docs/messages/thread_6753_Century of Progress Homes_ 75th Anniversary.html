Century of Progress Homes, 75th Anniversary

From: Janna Odenthal (jannaode@yahoo.com) | Date: 2010-10-03 13:11:32 UTC-07:00
Oct 22nd-24th, Fri 6-9pm, Sat-Sun 9am-3pm; Century of Progress Homes 75th
Anniversary; Houses, built for the 1933 Chicago World's Fair, were brought to
the resort town of Beverly Shores, Indiana, 75 years ago. Today they are the
property of the National Park Service Indiana Dunes National Lakeshore, leased
to Indiana Landmarks, and subleased to private individuals who are undertaking
the restoration of the homes with their own dollars based on approved plans.An
exclusive preview party on Friday will feature two visionary homes to celebrate
Indiana Landmarks' 50th anniversary and 75 years of these storied homes.
(Sat-Sun tours with shuttle $15, Fri preview$75 for non-members; Non-shuttle
tours free, but lots of walking, including many staircases)

Re: Century of Progress Homes, 75th Anniversary

From: tiptoetonto (tiptoetonto@yahoo.com) | Date: 2010-10-04 22:49:20 UTC
We actually took Mark & Sue and Cardinal over to Beverly Shores for a look at the city of Chicago from Indiana's perspective (and to see the Indiana National Lakeshore) last week. One of our favorite spots is right where the Century of Progress Homes are being renovated. It has been interesting over the last 30 plus years to watch as they fell into decay and now the slow transformation back to their original luster.
Part of their private purchase agreement included that they not only be restored as close as possible to their original plan, but they would be open to the public at least once a year.
On our trip we started at the fair's site near the Museum of Science and Industry by nabbing Atomic Beans White City box and driving where the houses originally were installed before being barged across the lake to their current site. It was a nice tie-in to the plaques outside the houses explaining the trek.
Tiptoe & Tonto
