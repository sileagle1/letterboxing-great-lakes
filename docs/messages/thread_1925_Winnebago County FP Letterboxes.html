Winnebago County FP Letterboxes

From: Angi (ShutterflyIL@aol.com) | Date: 2005-10-25 23:39:43 UTC
So I emailed the Winnebago County FP people about letterboxes and
their policy. I sent this nice, long email asking how those of us who
have boxes removed by the county could get them back, among other
things. All I got for a response was, "Attached is our Geocache and
Letterbox policy." which I could very well have read online. That
still didn't answer my question about what happens to the boxes (and
even caches) once they are removed, so I responded, kindly, asking
them the same question.

The information in the policy can be found by going to the website
and downloading the PDF file, but I will summarize it here as well to
save the hassle.

$10 gets you a permit to place 2 letterboxes for 1 year. After the
year is up, you either pay another $10 or you remove the boxes.

You must check on your boxes every 30 days and communicate to the
preserve workers that you have done so by email, snail mail or other
means.

Only 3 boxes can be placed in a preserve at one time.

Size can be no greater than 4"x8"x12".

Anything not registered with them will be removed. (But we still
don't know what happens to them after being removed.)

Where can I get the job of letterbox removal? Whose job is it to sit
in front of a computer and figure out clues? What fun that would be!

Anywho, it looks like I won't be placing letterboxes on forest
preserve property anytime soon. If anyone out there happens to be out
and finds any of my boxes, please remove them yourself and let me
know. I will pay shipping or arrange to have them returned to me some
other way. If anyone else in the area wants me to do the same for
them, I would be more than happy to. :)

Shutterfly
