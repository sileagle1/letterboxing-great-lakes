WI Letterboxing 10th Anniversary Event

From: Chris Mothkovich (chris@mothkovich.com) | Date: 2008-10-05 13:04:05 UTC-05:00
And Nam Vet and I will add our THANKS and KUDOS to The Dragon, the excellent stamp-carvers, and also the most excellent potluck cooks. The boxes were educational and varied, the clues were great, the hikes took us to varied terrain all over the park, the FOOD WAS DELICIOUS, the weather was most excellent, and Whitefish Dunes State Park is absolutely beautiful. Thank you again, Dragon! I'm sorry we didn't get a chance to visit more with you and with the other boxers, but we were out on the trail almost all day. We boxed with GGG Hiker and Gruntmur in the morning, tailing another group, and with Cherokee Rose 2 and Hawkeye in the afternoon - great company all around :)

Also a very special thank you to Beetle for arranging the pre-gathering fish boil and hiding the Famous Fish series. This was my first fish boil and I have to say the fish was delicious (albeit quite bony). I got a big kick out of Beetle approaching the gaggle of people watching the fish boil pot and inquiring, "Is there anyone here who . . . isn't one of us?" heheheh

Nam Vet and I are pretty worn out today, we don't really know how many miles we trudged . . . I mean HIKED BRISKLY . . . yesterday, but it had to have been over 6 hours total, including a trudge - I MEAN HIKE - up to the top of Old Baldy. And today on the way home, we made a detour to find "The Killer Rabbit" (thanks Hawkeye for the cluesheet).

I haven't downloaded the rest of my pictures yet but when I do I'll see if any are good enough to share.

THANKS AGAIN to all (especially The Dragon, I know you went to a LOT of work to stage this event), we had a great time at our first outside gathering other than the "Birds of a Feather" event which we organized ourselves. Hopefully you'll see us at another event in the near - or distant - future :)

Trail Rider ~ Chris

Re: WI Letterboxing 10th Anniversary Event

From: Kathi (krank@ameritech.net) | Date: 2008-10-06 05:04:44 UTC
Time for me to add my two cents, including thank yous to The Dragon
for spearheading the weekend (hope you're feeling better!), WiHi for
the great event stamp (very glad it was carved by a Wisconsin boxer),
everyone else who contributed boxes and food, Beetle for the Friday
night pre-event, and Mr. FungusWoman for being so patient with me and
my "Hobby" (I can always tell when he says it how he capitalizes the
H...). While he didn't find much other than a few coins (nothing
old) with his metal detector, he did find a piece of pottery nearby
which he turned in to the nature center (they were quite pleased to
receive it).

But I'd like to also thank everyone who contributed to Project Pajama
Pants. Holy cow! We collected EIGHTEEN pairs of pants, a pile of
letters, and a stack of comics and comic books. There was also some
cash donated, so I'll be purchasing a few more pairs before I take
them in. Thank you all so much! I've noticed that there aren't that
many stamps in the logbook, so if you made a donation but missed
stamping in, drop me a note offline and we can make arrangements for
me to mail you a copy of the image.

It was kind of cool driving through Manitowoc because that was where
Mr. FW gave me my ring those MANY years ago. We used to go up there
quite a lot, but haven't been there in a long time, so we explored
his old fishing haunts and looked around at how much the area had
changed. I didn't think to bring the clues to the Killer Rabbit,
which was my Dad's carving. Next time!

--klk/fw
