From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2007-03-04 08:06:08 UTC-05:00
Here is a link to Silent Doug's cafepress store:

http://www.cafepress.com/letterboxing

I have previously purchased items from here. I was satisfied with the quality of the t-shirt material that I received -- it was a mid-weight cotton knit and did not shrink too badly -- but they probably vary by style. The one I purchased was just a basic grey T-shirt that had the "Get a Clue" logo on it. It looks like he is not carrying that item any longer. I also purchased that great "Get A Clue" steel coffee mug that people are always asking me about and I have left behind at numerous gatherings (thanks to all those who have sent it back to me)... mine has seen a lot of use over the past few years and countless trips through the dishwasher and finally the coating with the print on it beginning to peel off. I was hoping to replace it, but it doesn't appear he is carrying this any more either ;-(

I don't know for sure, but I wouldn't think the GLG5 merchandise is a Silent Doug endeavor for a couple of reasons -- first, I seriously doubt that he would do this without asking the group or the event planners, and secondly, it is a different cafepress store.

SpringChick

From: RDM R (buckeyekids3@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-03-04 05:33:14 UTC-08:00
Hello all,

As those of who have been reading the firestorm on AQ know, I am the one who put up the sayitstore.com site. First, I want to say here, that I hope I have not stepped on any toes and if I did, I appologize. Second, I want to answer some of the questions that were posed. M&8X was not behind the design idea, but I have spoken to her and she has said she liked it very much and has endorsed it. I am going to split any profits with her, since she is putting in so much effort to pull this gathering off, she deserves it. I have ordered one of the Ohio Letterboxer shirts (the womens V neck) and the shirt quality is good. (I wanted to make sure of that before I made anymore designs.) The items are printed as they are ordered, so that is why there are so many choices. I put them all up, to give everyone somethig they liked. They use a direct print (instead of iron on) method on the shirts, which like any other shirt, should hold up if turned inside out in the wash.

I hope I have cleared things up. Please contact me if you have any other questions or concerns.

BuckeyeMom

I do understand where 3BuckeyeKids is coming from and actually admire her spirit of entrepreneurship, although I will admit I don't quite understand the reason for her initial dishonesty. Where I feel the line has been crossed is with the event shirts. Although many events have an associated T-shirt, this is really something that should fall to the event planners to design and/or commission. Planning a gathering is a lot of work, some of it fun, some or it just plain tedious. Some people would consider designing the "official" gathering shirt to be fun and would look forward to the chance to create this, others might consider it to be tedious and would just as soon somebody else did it. But... that should be the decision of the event planner.

So it really is up to M&8x and Eagle Eyes. If they want to go with this design, that's fine. If they would rather come up with something else, just tell us when/where to get the "official" GLG5 shirt and we will support that.
