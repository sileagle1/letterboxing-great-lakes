From: xxxxxxxx (BrighidFarm@comcast.net) | Date: 2008-07-13 15:06:16 UTC-05:00
Oooooooooooh, great trailname..........I love popcorn! It's a shame it
leaves an odor on the fingers so can't eat it on the boxing trail.

As to first finder stuff...............

Do it if you want to, don't if you don't want to.

There's nothing that says you have to or that you're expected to. And I
don't really know as if it's actually even thought very much of anyway
because in only a very few cases did the first finder ever contact us to
even let us know they had found the box. We did have one guy, tho, who was
the second finder contact us, upset at the fact that we had nothing in the
box for the second finder. (The first finder had mentioned the certificate
and gift in the logbook.) That made our day. We were rollin' on the floor
over that one. That was really good. I would guess that that person was
pretty much a newbie.

I used to make up a "First Finder Certificate" that gave the name of the
box, date it was planted, the location of the box, and it had a stamp image
on it. I did it on the computer so that I could scale it down and make it
small.

In the future, however, I'm making ATCs as first finder certificates. On
the front will be the stamp image as part of the ATC design and on the back
will have the rest of the information. It'll allow me to get a little more
creative with using the stamp image.

I used to also put in some sort of "gift" that pertained to the box or to
the area for the first finder..........but as boxes have to get smaller and
smaller and smaller to find unmuggleable hiding places, I'm probably not
going to do that in the future. People who boxed with children did contact
us on occasion, to tell us they liked that because the kids liked it. Or
sometimes I'd put in a gift certificate to a local store. For example, for
the box by Yorktown Shopping Center, Pippi put in a gift certificate to the
shopping center. For the box in Long Grove, I put in a gift certificate
good for any of the stores in Long Grove. That kind of thing.

Could a "gift" be thought of as too much like geocaching? Possibly. I've
rec'd comments like that from other letterboxers. But we only did it for
the first finder. And I tried to make the gifts a little nicer than the
trinkets found in geocaches and a little more appropriate to the box and/or
the area. So I think that set the idea apart from geocaching.

~~ Mosey ~~

RE: [LbGLK] First finder

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2008-07-13 21:09:14 UTC-05:00
Yeah, no worries about "first finders" certificates, since you are actually
providing an extra prize for many finders of your boxes! We enjoyed the
popcorn (and also purchased some very tasty FRESH squeezed lemonade!). If
we have any hitchhikers in our possession when we plant a box, we often
include that as a "first finder" prize. We've sometimes included something
else relevant to the box, such as an ornament in a Christmas-themed box,
etc., but we don't put something in every box we plant.

We've only run into a couple of boxes in WI that had ink pads. Due to
weather conditions and space constraints, the vast majority of letterboxers
do not include them. As Mosey mentioned, many of us carry a wide spectrum
of colored markers in addition to some ink pads so we can create
multi-colored images. If you go that route, I suggest you use a 40% or 50%
off coupon from the Sunday paper for Michaels craft stores and then get some
LePlume Marvy brush markers.

Wisconsin Hiker
