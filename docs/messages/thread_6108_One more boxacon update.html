It was lovely that the weather cooperated last weekend. I'm sending this to the event list as well, so sorry if you see it twice. I am going out to the park this morning. Re: [LbGLK] One more boxacon update

From: TJ_Mich (tj_mich@ameritech.net) | Date: 2009-10-10 16:41:26 UTC-07:00
We must've been just ahead of you on the trail! I think we left the parking area around 9am. Luckily we bumped into OLJ and LK over near the dog exercise area, and they confirmed that Jedi Weapon was indeed going to be pulled, and that we shouldn't try looking for Flash Gordon either. We also couldn't find Shards of Honor, just as WiHi had reported, but all the others on that loop were doing fine.

Just want to add our thanks for all the work in organizing Boxacon. It was a ton of fun, a great location choice, and I'm sure it'll take us a few more trips to eventually find all the boxes.

Today's highlight was, as we hunted the last few boxes, our 3-year-old (after gaining confidence in "finding" and "replacing" the earlier boxes under my supervision) insisted on all the rest of us staying on the trail while he headed in himself to "find" each box! No matter that he had no idea which particular tree or log to look near. He also did the same for "replacing"... so then Dad had to think quickly and make up a random excuse as to why I needed to re-enter the woods as soon as he came back out, just to be sure that each box was properly placed and covered!

Thanks Aimlesst and your whole crew of helpers!!
--TJ_Mich

From: wi_letterboxer (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2009-10-11 04:08:21 UTC
Just got back from wedding reception, so only a short note. It was FREEZING on the trails today!! We were out from about 7:30 to noon on the trails, then another hour and half at the shelter trying to warm up with hot chocolate, holding our hands over a propane warmer and hot dog coals while we ate lunch and did a few exchanges! My toes were almost totally frozen, but luckily finally thawed at the church later in the afternoon. It wasn't deep snow, but there was sme on all the trails and the paved trail was icy in spots.

It was a nice event and we're hoping to return to the park tomorrow to find more of the boxes. Today we only had time for the series Gooble Gobble planted and 2 others (plus a few right near the shelter). Unfortunately we missed seeing a lot of people since they were out on the trails during lunch.

There was a cool "severed finger" dish at the potluck and Gooble Gobble had assembled an impressive haunted house that contained new hitchhikers for the first 14 people/groups that finished his series. I'll post photos later.

Many thanks to Red Cat for organizing the event!

Wisconsin Hiker

From: TJ_Mich (tj_mich@ameritech.net) | Date: 2009-10-12 06:57:09 UTC-07:00
That raises an interesting question. This box (GREAT movie reference, by the way! I remember going to the theatre for thisone!)now has permanent clues, but is listed on AtlasQuest as an Event Box. That means it won't ever appear in any typical letterbox searches or listingsof the Waukesha area; it can only be found by perusing the Boxacon event listing and clicking on 'Event Boxes'. AQ expects event boxes to be "a stamp or box brought to an event or gathering for just the duration of the event. Many event stamps are tabletop boxes, but an event stamp generally won't have clues, may not have a logbook, and will only be available for the duration of the event or perhaps the day or two after the event has ended."

I'm not picking on you, ArchaeoFrog, just pointing out a subtlety of box listings that affects any event plants which are going to turn into permanent letterboxes. Perhaps we all (myself included) should havelisted these as "Traditional" boxesrather than "Event Boxes"if they aregoing tobe in place long-term. After all, they have clues, are going to bethere a while,are in the woods, have logbooks, etc. If not, future finders areVERY unlikely to stumble across the clues... who would ever think to look at the listing for a past event and read the clues for what are supposed to be temporary, mostly tabletop, boxes?

Again, just bringing this up for discussion to see if there are thoughts from the group. I'm not at all opposed to having all these plants at a single park... it'll be kinda like our own slice of Connecticut here in Wisconsin!... but wouldlike to see ifwe can come up with a standard approach for clues to make them mostenjoyable and useful for future finders.

That
raises an interesting question. This box (GREAT movie reference, by
the way! I remember going to the theatre for this one!) now has
permanent clues, but is listed on AtlasQuest as an Event Box. That
means it won't ever appear in any typical letterbox searches or
listings of the Waukesha area; it can only be found by perusing the
Boxacon event listing and clicking on 'Event Boxes'. AQ expects event
boxes to be "a stamp or box brought to an event or gathering for just
the duration of the event. Many event stamps are tabletop boxes, but an
event stamp generally won't have clues, may not have a logbook, and
will only be available for the duration of the event or perhaps the day
or two after the event has ended."

I'm not picking on you,
ArchaeoFrog, just pointing out a subtlety of box listings that affects
any event plants which are going to turn into permanent letterboxes.
Perhaps we all (myself included) should have listed these as
"Traditional" boxes rather than "Event Boxes" if they are going to be
in place long-term. After all, they have clues, are going to be there
a while, are in the woods, have logbooks, etc. If not, future finders
are VERY unlikely to stumble across the clues... who would ever think
to look at the listing for a past event and read the clues for what are
supposed to be temporary, mostly tabletop, boxes?

Again, just
bringing this up for discussion to see if there are thoughts from the
group. I'm not at all opposed to having all these plants at a single
park... it'll be kinda like our own slice of Connecticut here in
Wisconsin!... but would like to see if we can come up with a standard
approach for clues to make them most enjoyable and useful for future
finders.
