RE: [LbGLK] Portage - Devils Lake - Oshkosh

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2012-10-01 22:57:40 UTC-05:00
Hmmm.... I don't really have any restaurant suggestions. However I can tell
you that if you do the loop hike around Devil's Lake using the Bluff trails
(~ 4 miles), you should be able to get 3 of my old boxes, 2 Lock, Shock &
Barrel boxes, a Verona Bird Patrol box and the new "Balanced Rock" box by
AfroCircus. I recommend that you start on the north end and do the West
Bluff trail, then loop around the south end of the lake and back up to do
the East Bluff trail. You should probably still be able to get a chocolate
malt at the concession stand on the south shore to give you energy to get up
the Balanced Rock trail to the East Bluff! The colors and views should be
fantastic this weekend at Devil's Lake!



Wisconsin Hiker



P.S. We've taken kids as young as 6 or 7 around the lake, so it isn't all
that hard of a loop, but does involve some elevation gain. Wear good shoes
and go UP the Balanced Rock trail, not DOWN!

From: hnjruetten (hnjruetten@yahoo.com) | Date: 2012-10-02 14:18:41 UTC
In Oshkosh, there is a cafe/coffee shop/dessert place downtown called Caramel Crisp that I would recommend. It's a nice atmosphere to relax and look over your logbook or plan your day.

Our boxes in Oshkosh have fairly easy clues and a couple are driveby. Of all our boxes in Oshkosh, South Park and Asylum Point would be the two longest ones in terms of time/walk. But both are still less than a mile.

Hope that helps. Have fun on your trip and enjoy Oshkosh. Let me know if you have any other questions about Oshkosh.

The Gamer Ruettens

Re: [LbGLK] Re: Portage - Devils Lake - Oshkosh

From: (Mathw613@aol.com) | Date: 2012-10-08 23:33:17 UTC-04:00
Merlin & Ardea -- I attempted Balanced Rock box as well. I too was Leary of the placement - I would've labeled that as an extreme box. It even mentions in the clues that the rock climbers anchor their ropes there...... why then would you place a box there? I moved all the loose stones away to find nothing. I didn't stick my hand into that hole though.

NorthStarr -- sorry we didn't meet up. It would've been nice to see you again.


We attempted Verona Bird Patrol's box, the Balanced Rock box & Wisconsin Hikers 3 older boxes (2005) -- we found all but the Balanced Rock.

We parked at the north parking lot, hiked up the west bluff to the south shore, then up balanced rock trail and the east bluff trail back to our van.

We had a cool day, that suited us fine for all the hiking we did. The fall colors were gorgeous! Becki & I had a wonderful time together (kid free)!

Next time we will have to hit the lower trails for the rest of the boxes, though, it would be hard passing up the bluff trails -- the scenery is so much nicer up high.

We spent the night in Portage.

The next day we drove to Oshkosh and found nearly all of The Gamer Ruetten's boxes in town (minus one we didn't attempt). After picking up a stamp carved by Kirbert, we found the South Park box in Oshkosh -- that was our F1,500!! Then drove up to Asylum Point Lighthouse (we didn't attempt the second box in this series due to hunger). We cleaned Oshkosh out save for one box (now we regret not getting that one - oh well).

Driving home it got dark early (boo), we did pick up pugnasties "Patriotic Pug" and estella_naparella's "James T. Lewis" boxes on our way home. Tried a couple of boxes in Madison, however, the darkness and the need to get home at a reasonable hour trumped and we gave up the hunt and headed home.

Sunday, we went to first service at church then headed out to Oregon, Illinois to a youth center/retreat I used to go to as a high schooler. What wonderful memories came back to me. We attended the Olde English Faire at Stronghold and met up with BristolStitch and found a couple of her boxes at the Faire.

A busy, yet extremely satisfying 3 days.

hx6
