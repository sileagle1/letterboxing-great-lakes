What a great day to go letterboxing!

From: Kathi (krank@ameritech.net) | Date: 2008-11-02 19:44:29 UTC
You're not going to believe this, but I went with the kid and her BF
(the ones who unravelled the mystery of FOOW2 this summer) to
Matthiessen SP yesterday. She of course, performed the Million Death
Routine because I talked to him about Lbing and showed him my
logbook. Couldn't exactly tell if he was interested or just being
polite, but either way, I had a captive audience. His main reaction
was "I'd be so p***ed if I put all that work into that and someone
took it." which unfortunately may have been the situation with three
of the four boxes we were looking for (talking to the placers about
them offlist). But he did seem to be genuinely pleased with the
Batman stamp I carved for him, and used it to log into those boxes we
were able to find.

So anyway, we were coming up from lower dells and all were tired, but
I needed to find this one last box. I'm sure we were in the right
place, but it didn't make sense. Not to post any spoilers here, but
something just didn't seem right about the clues. So I'm
watching...one likely spot, but no...the placer wouldn't do that. So
I started looking at the other side of the trail and investigating
suspicious looking hiding places off that way. Eventually though we
reached the landmark that says we've gone too far without finding
it. Oh, well.

Then I realized I lost my sweatshirt. Think the young lovebirds
would offer to go look for it for me? "We'll wait there on the
bench." Sure, let the old lady with raging plantar fasciitis go
search for it. So I left my bag with them (shoulder's getting a
little sore by now too) and started back. Asked some hikers I passed
if they saw it..."Yeah, out by the stairs". Dang! So back I
trekked. Yep, there it was...all the way back at the far end of the
stairs. Well, at least nobody took it. So back I went. Guess I'll
look for the letterbox as long as I'm here. Back to that suspicious
spot. What the heck. I'll check anyway...there's no one around to
observe me. YES!!! There it is! Of course I have nothing with me,
so I must admit what I did...I tore a page out of the logbook and
huffed on the stamp. A very faint image, but I got it! And I did
have my necklace with my mini stamp around my neck. Huffed again and
I was able to log in (no pen to write the date though - next time!).
Victory is mine!

The park itself was really crowded, whether that was due to the
campaign to pack the parks or just plain gorgeous weather, I don't
know. I was able to locate all five of my Matthiessen and Buffalo
Rock boxes, so that's a good thing, though I was not able to open any
up (other than Bluffalo, which had no one around at the time) to
check inside because there were so many people around. The Lower
Dells at Matthiessen looked like Oak Street Beach in Chicago on a
summer weekend it was so swarmed with people. (So much for a
peaceful commune with nature....)

But the best part of the day? When we dropped "Batman" off at his
place, he thanked me for taking him along, and when Papillon left to
head back to her dorm she hugged me and said "I really had a good
time today, Mom." Like the commercial says, "Priceless!"
