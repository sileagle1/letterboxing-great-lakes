Re: Anyone else experiencing a ton more missing boxes this year?

From: mthkvch (chris@mothkovich.com) | Date: 2009-07-28 22:04:38 UTC
Braving the mosquitoes, I went out this afternoon to check two of my boxes at a local forest preserve, and found that they were both gone. Both of them were hidden quite well with practically NO chance they would be accidentally stumbled across by muggles. In fact, the special container we constructed for the stamp & logbook had even been removed. I don't want to sound paranoid, but this leaves me with no other explanation than that someone deliberately targeted and removed these boxes. These last two boxes (the last of the "River Fauna" series) make a total of SIX of my boxes that have disappeared recently:

Swoop - my one and only Atlasquest Blue Diamond box
Grab
Irish Penny
Larry's Landing

This is very discouraging. These great stamp images (carved by my friend Krait) are gone forever. These boxes will not be replaced, as Krait has lost interest in carving. He always told me he was going to "retire" after his 100th carve.

I have one more stamp of his that is yet to be planted. I'm going to have to make it a mystery box, I think, to try to keep it safe from whomever is confiscating my boxes.

Trail Rider ~ Chris

RE: [LbGLK] Re: Anyone else experiencing a ton more missing boxes this year?

From: xxxxxxxx (BrighidFarm@comcast.net) | Date: 2009-07-28 18:33:13 UTC-05:00
Were all six in a forest preserve? Any chance that forest perserve
personnel might have been following the clues and confiscating them?

We lost quite a few a couple of years ago, some of them like yours -- in
areas where muggles never would have been looking. This was why Caveman
decided to go ahead and insist that he wants to replace them but list them
on atlasquest and use that system where a person has to have found a certain
number of boxes in order to see the clues. I don't like that idea at all,
but he figures that forest perserve personnel won't bother going to that
work to see if there are clues to any boxes in their preserves there, and
other non-boxer people who might want to intentionally harm the boxes might
be too lazy to record their finds just to see if there are any clues. I
dunno. It might work.

In this neck of the woods we have an absolute ton of geocaches. Any chance
they were near geocaches?

I've been kind of wondering about DuPage County boxes in the DuPage County
forest preserves. They used to require a permit for caches and letterboxes.
Then they began hiding their own combo caches/boxes -- many of which have
gone missing quickly. (To the point where, as soon as I see a box listed
now by one of their rangers, I go find it because I just know it's going to
go missing soon.)

And this year, when I went to their site where they list the types of
permits folks need to have for various activities in the preserves, I can't
find anything listed for caches or boxes. So I'm wondering if they still
have the permit program and if not, does that mean caches/boxes aren't
permitted at all now. Not that I'd get a permit anyway.

But it's kind of amazing what boxes manage to stay in place in the DuPage
preserves year after year without a permit and what boxes manage to
disappear even when quite well hidden. I don't get it.

We've also had a ton of rain this year. Wettest spring since 1928 according
to what I read. Any chance they might have gotten washed away even if the
boxes didn't *seem* to be placed where water might be a danger under
"normal" spring weather?

I hope you find an answer that works for you!

~~Mosey~~

RE: [LbGLK] Re: Anyone else experiencing a ton more missing boxes this year?

From: xxxxxxxx (BrighidFarm@comcast.net) | Date: 2009-07-28 20:18:46 UTC-05:00
The problem with putting the links on LbGLK is that not all that many
letterboxers actually subscribe to the yahoogroups lists. I know more
boxers who don't read any of the groups than those who do read any of them.
I maybe read only 1/10th of the posts even tho I'm subscribed to the lists.

I admit that I don't like logging my finds online either. Never have liked
it. Caveman actually went and began logging our finds on atlasquest and
just did a good number of our local finds, not finds where we found
out-of-town ones when we were away on business or anything, and I think he
gave up around 200. Then he got bored. :-)

The boxes being hidden from even veteran boxers if they don't log their
finds was a big reason I was so against it at first. Another reason was
that I didn't like the fact that it was a commercial site.

But the times, they are a'changin'. After what we went thru a couple of
years ago with losing boxes, I really don't feel like even putting them out
there. I wouldn't be "protecting them from newbies" per se. I'd be
protecting them from whoever is disappearing them. I still think that, for
my neck of the woods, it's the people who "control" the use of the land
we've planted on. Yes, when we make a carve a stamp, make a box, hide it,
write clues..........we're giving up that box to the wild.........releasing
it so to speak. On the other hand, I have severe carpal tunnel syndrome.
It takes a lot for me to even be able to carve a mediocre stamp. We had
boxes out there from 2004 to 2007, no problems. Shouldn't say that. Sure
there were problems. But I could point each time to the likely cause as
something that was non-human. Then something happened in 2007 and they
began disappearing like crazy in cases where it had to be humans.

So we figured, we're moving anyway, let's not bother putting them out there
for 2008. But the real estate market went south and we probably won't be
moving until 2010 or 2011.

And we, personally, hate to see boxes turned into mystery boxes because we
*are* so short of cash and don't really want to go chasing after boxes where
we think we've got the location right and end up not having it right. Or
working out elaborate clues only to find out that we can't afford the gas to
go there anyway. So I hesitate to make boxes that might cause other folks
like us to not go searching for boxes because *they're* short of money.

Plus, there *is* a learning curve to finding boxes, so really difficult
clues or making mystery boxes *would* sort of be protecting them from
newbies. I *do* like difficult clues tho. I really don't mind if we know
we have the right area but we can't find the box because the clues stump us.
I'm cool with that. I was cool with that as a newbie.

With a found box count, if I understand it right, the found box count
required doesn't need to be a very high one. Yes, malicious folks could go
in and easily log one or two boxes to qualify. But Caveman thought maybe
folks like rangers and other landowners wouldn't bother going thru even that
little amount of work to see if there are any boxes they want to confiscate.
Then again, maybe they would take the time to bother.

I dunno. There's just no easy or perfect answer.

~~Mosey~~

for 2008. But the real estate market went south and we probably won't be
moving until 2010 or 2011.

And we, personally, hate to see boxes turned into mystery boxes because we
*are* so short of cash and don't really want to go chasing after boxes where
we think we've got the location right and end up not having it right. Or
working out elaborate clues only to find out that we can't afford the gas to
go there anyway. So I hesitate to make boxes that might cause other folks
like us to not go searching for boxes because *they're* short of money.

Plus, there *is* a learning curve to finding boxes, so really difficult
clues or making mystery boxes *would* sort of be protecting them from
newbies. I *do* like difficult clues tho. I really don't mind if we know
we have the right area but we can't find the box because the clues stump us.
I'm cool with that. I was cool with that as a newbie.

With a found box count, if I understand it right, the found box count
required doesn't need to be a very high one. Yes, malicious folks could go
in and easily log one or two boxes to qualify. But Caveman thought maybe
folks like rangers and other landowners wouldn't bother going thru even that
little amount of work to see if there are any boxes they want to confiscate.
Then again, maybe they would take the time to bother.

I dunno. There's just no easy or perfect answer.
