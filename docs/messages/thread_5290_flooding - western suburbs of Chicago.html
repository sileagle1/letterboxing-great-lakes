flooding - western suburbs of Chicago

From: Kathi Krankoski (krank@ameritech.net) | Date: 2008-09-14 18:25:33 UTC-07:00
No big news here, but we've had some serious flooding in my area (just a couple blocks from my house is the convergence of Salt Creek into the DesPlaines). Many roads are closed (don't try going to Brookfield Zoo - you won't get through). I wouldn't recommend trying for any boxes in the vicinity of Salt Creek or the DesPlaines. With all the standing water...it isn't safe (both from the point of pollution and just plain slippery mud). Let's hope none of the boxes are heading down the river again like last year.

--klk/fw

Re: [LbGLK] Re: flooding - western suburbs of Chicago

From: Carol Osada (cjosada@hotmail.com) | Date: 2008-09-14 22:21:18 UTC-06:00
Today, I was driving through those western suburbs of Chicago and the rivers are almost to the top of the underside of the bridges over the river. Tree trunks aren't visible and the leaves and branches are dragging in the water. In addition to the devastating clean up problems facing the homeowners, my thoughts were also of letterboxes that might be floating down the river. When we hide a box, none of think that the water level would rise 8-12 feet. But it has!

And in addition to the 6-10 inches of rain we got Saturday through Sunday, the weathermen are still forecasting more light showers tonight and through Monday.
Good day to stay inside and....carve new stamps for new boxes.

Beetle
