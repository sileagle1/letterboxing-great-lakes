Connecting through letterboxing

From: yooperann (wfisher47@comcast.net) | Date: 2004-10-21 02:00:11 UTC

I wrote recently about how an ordinary box or location can be made
special by the clues which reveal a deeper meaning. It turns out I
was more right than I ever imagined.

This summer I placed a bunch of boxes in Wyoming in honor of my
father, who grew up there. My clues have a lot of family history in
them. Today, thanks to a random internet search, I got a "contact
the placer" email from a first cousin of my father's, age 73! His
mother, my great aunt, moved from Wyoming to Washington state in the
1940's and I never met her, let alone her children. Yet here we are,
connected. I was able to send him a copy of my father's memoirs,
which include many stories about his father. In turn, I just got a
scan of a charcoal sketch of my great-grandfather who died sometime
shortly after my father was born in 1914!

This hobby can take us interesting places in more ways than one!
