A short trip to WI

From: Armadillo Jo (jes7o@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-11-01 17:40:54 UTC-08:00
Went to Milwaukee this weekend to visit a friend and
did some light letterboxing along the way. On Saturday
evening, we found the "Miller Park" box at a scenic
overlook over the Menominee. My friend, who has been
to Miller Park a zillion times, didn't even know it
was there! It got dark pretty quickly, so we attempted
"Creative Writing: The Neverending Story" and the "WI
Hitchhiker Hostel". Got all of the parts to the
clues...but the library was closed! So we made note of
the hours and called it a night. Sunday, we went to
Hart Park for some "Squished Pennies" action. It was a
beautiful morning, and the woods here are very
interesting. Lots of twisty and busted up trees. Only
one train went by, so we didn't get to squish any
pennies. Retrived the box and then headed to the other
side of the park for "Watery Grave". We really enjoyed
this area as well, got the box first
(an-ti-ci-pation!) and then spent some time looking at
the actual watery graves. On our way back to the car,
I said to my friend, "Hey Brad, this is an example of
a good place to put a letterbox" referring to a nearby
log. So I lift out the bark, and voila a geocache was
living there! The more you box, the more obvious
hiding spots become I guess. Then we headed to a neat
little preserve and park in Wauwatosa and found
"Figure Eights". We didn't run, but we did enjoy the
scenery. That afternoon, back to the library for the
Neverending Story. What fun! Also visited the Hostel.
Headed home, and got the "Grosse Point Lighthouse"
right before dark. Can't wait to get back to
Milwaukee!!!

--Armadillo Jo
