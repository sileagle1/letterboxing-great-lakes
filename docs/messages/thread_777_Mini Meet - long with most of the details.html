Mini Meet - long with most of the details

From: bindlestiff_boxer (bindlestiff_boxer@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-10-18 15:07:28 UTC

We had a little bit of everything for weather on Saturday. There was
fog in the early morning before our meeting but the sun peeked out a
tiny bit as we were leaving the restaurant ... then we got the rest
of it. I checked the stats at NOAA and found that the temp during
our event was in the low 40's with winds blowing from the west at a
steady 30 and gusting to 39! We had occasional sprinkles mixed with
tiny hail/sleet balls and ended with NIGHT BOXING!

After enjoying lunch and "Exchanges" all around, we set out for Lake
Harbor Park. We found all of the known boxes in this park, and
picked up two HH's!

First was the Muskegon Beach box, originally placed by Arbo and
being maintained by SpringChick. Inside we found a surprise HH "No
Skin Off My Back" (here's the link to SpringChick's Haunted HH
series ww.letterbox-mi.com/series/hhh_main.asp )

Next was the newly placed Fish Out of Water #7, which is a joint
effort between Fungus Woman and Spring Chick. WOW! Amazing detail
on this tiny stamp.

Then it was on to conquer the "Sandy Seasons Series" I say
conquer, because there is a monstrous 80 foot sand dune to climb
before you can begin this series. Oh, but the great carvings of
these stamps is sooooo worth it!

Before we could find the Winter box, we stumbled upon a Geocache.
Had a little teaching time as I shared what I knew about Geocaching.
(here's a couple of links if any of you are interested: their
equivalent of our `big list' is at www.geocaching.com The Michigan
site is at http://www.mi-geocaching.org/ )

I had a large, long-life lightstick in my backpack available for
trade, but the DogLvrs kids passed on taking anything from the
cache. We all stamped in and signed the logbook.

After finding and stamping into the Winter logbook while the sleet
was coming down in little pellets, we replaced both and hid them
well. Jokes all around about the appropriate name of the box (grin)
given the current weather.

We found the Spring box in good condition and went looking for the
Summer box.

Wooohie! As if the wind blowing 30+ across the cold waters of Lake
Michigan and up the open dune wasn't terrific enough it started to
rain then sleet again! Never fear you can't call us `fair weather
boxers' no sir, we are dedicated boxers!

What an amazing location the Summer box lives in! If the quality
carvings on SpringChicks stamps aren't enough to tempt you, then the
view just before getting to this box and then the "magical" quality
of this place is worth doing the series.

We thought the Autumn box would be our final find in this park, but
we were wrong! Yippee "Witch Hazel" another of the Haunted HH's.

We made a quick stop at Burger King for "kid fuel" and took a quick
detour to the Lumbertown #1 box, which was in good shape. Then it
was a nice drive via the scenic route along the Lakeshore to the
Duck Lake area.

Clues were passed out for the Celestial Celtic series I placed for
the event. DogLvrs had brought along the clues for my Life is
Good/Cooler at the Lake box, which is also nearby.

What fun to watch someone run through your clues! Everyone did a
great job with the two boxes at Marcus Park but this is where our
single injury occurred. "W" of DogLvrs took a tumble from
the `bouncing log' However, true to our motto of the day, he
continued on because he is no fair weather boxer, no sir he is "a
dedicated letterboxer"

As we went past the Lake Michigan Beach parking area, I was in awe
of the waves. Huge breaking rollers, cresting and tumbling before
they smashed against the beach with a deafening roar of thunder.

We were racing against dark as we counted the squares on the paved
path toward the Celtic Sun box. We were able to find the box
without breaking out the flashlights, but I think we needed them to
stamp into the logbook.

Geez, oh pizza it sure can get complicated when you have 2 HH's
and 3 Teams that all have to get stamped. Our gang came up with an
efficient `assembly line' that assured all the stamping that needed
to be done was done and that everything went back where it belonged.

One box left to go, and this time we really were night boxing!
Wow .. a first for me, but be careful not to trip or get a stray
branch in the face. Our `Purple Heart boxer' was boosted up and
given the honors of bringing out the Celtic Star box.

We were lucky and only had brief periods of light showers during the
day, but the rain did come down pretty steady for part of the trip
to Team Bindlestiffs winter digs.

Once we arrived, the kids were thrilled to learn there was a WOM box
waiting for them in the newspaper tube! That brought the "F" count
for the day to and even dozen.

While we warmed up with some hot homemade soup, the Pale Puppy Posse
told us how they had worked out cipher clues for the Midshipman's
Hitch Mystery Box in the car on the trip over to the event. They
also noted that they observed some of the landmarks for this Mystery
Box on the way to dinner; so they were planning on getting one more
box before they headed home.

A good time was had by all you can check out some of the pictures
here: http://tinyurl.com/5gz38

Enjoy!
