TJMich

OK, here's my opinion:

The park is nearly 300 acres, with lots of trails. Even though there were
80-100 letterboxers there on Saturday and many returned again on Sunday, we
really didn't run into many people at the box locations. The boxes are
fairly spread out, although a few ended up pretty close. I therefore think
it would be fine to leave all, or most of them there. Perhaps those that
are really close could be relocated a bit.

"I'd Rather Be Fishing" (aka "Mr. Funguswoman) asked about the origin of
the name of the park on Saturday. Here's what I found out: The park was
named "Minooka" by an 8-year old girl who won a "name the park" contest. The
name means "maple forest" or "good earth" depending on which Native American
dialect is used for translation.

Here is some other interesting info: The park land was previously portions
of various farms and the largest parcel was owned by the HART family! Were
these some relatives of yours hartx6??

By the way, did anyone find the "Shards of Honor" box? It has eluded us so
far..

Wisconsin Hiker
