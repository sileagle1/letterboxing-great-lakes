RE: [LbGLK] Airlines and compasses

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2006-07-16 23:44:06 UTC-05:00
We've taken compasses through in our carry-on luggage lots of times, not
even thinking about it. Is there a concern it would ruin the compass? It
doesn't seemed to have damaged ours....

Note: We ALWAYS take our LB stuff as carry-on, not checked baggage. We'd
hate to lose our logbooks, or anything we need for LBing if we are on our
way to a vacation destination. This means logbook, packs, ink, personal
stamps and hiking boots if we are headed out for serious hiking. We might
check our boots on the way back home, but not our irreplaceable LB stuff!

Wisconsin Hiker

Re: Airlines and compasses

From: Steve & Christa (sbellock322@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-07-17 22:09:22 UTC
It is interesting that you say this about checking your letterboxing
stuff. My sister and her two girls were out from CT at the end of
June/beginning of July and they checked their boxing stuff. The
airline "lost" it, and then gave them the run around for 4 days while
they waited for it to dry out. When it was returned my sisters
logbook was still damp and now a little mildewey, not to mention that
most of their images were ruined! I guesss you can say, "Lesson
learned"
