From: Coffee Beans (coffeebeans06@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-06-10 11:56:00 UTC-05:00
What are the odds of running into a fellow letterboxer... in a
completely random, non-boxing setting... in a city of about 3 million?
Well, believe it or not, it happened to us yesterday afternoon! :)

Atom and I stopped by to get some coffee refreshment at the Starbucks on
the corner of State and Wabash, and who should we spot just hanging out
with someone else at an outside table? None other than Azobox! (so much
for your motto "blend in" ;) ) Atom pointed him out first, and both of
us had to look twice, but sure enough -- it was him! We walked over to
a very surprised Azobox, who then proceeded to introduce us to his
brother as "Coffee Beans" and "Atom 118." His brother just sorta nodded
and went, "Huh, more letterboxers" or something like that. Anyway, it
was great fun. :) And maybe we'll make it 3 weeks in a row seeing each
other, as Atom and I will be in Portage/Kzoo next weekend to visit my
parents for father's day. ;) Where should we meet this time -- the
Meijer on Westnedge at 2pm on Sunday? ;) j/k

Has anyone else had an experience at all similar to that? I'm even
curious how many times people have run into fellow boxers on the trail
in a non-gathering setting? It's happened to us 5 times in the past
year and a half.

Coffee Beans

From: Doodle & Deedle Bug (doodle_n_deedle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-06-13 08:49:36 UTC-07:00
OK, back from Canada for our 1st anniversary vacation,
eh. :) A few years ago in Ohio, there was an
unofficial mini-meet to say good-bye to The Gamecock,
who was moving to another state. That's where we
first met The Cats Meow. We also got to meet Blaze,
who planted the Squire's Castle letterbox (and Katy,
I'm sure if you sent her an email she'd be able to let
you know as far as she knows what the box's status
is). Meeting Blaze was significant for us because her
Squire's Castle box was our very first attempted find.

So, a few weeks later, we were doing this Rails n'
Trails bike tour in the Cuyahoga Valley National Park
where our bikes were loaded on the train and we rode
the train to Pennisula, then biked back to where we
originally boarded the train. As Deedle & I were
waiting to board the train, I tugged on his shirt
sleeve, pointed, and whispered, "Hey, isn't that
Blaze?" Sure enough, she was not just an avid
letterboxer but also an avid cyclist doing the same
bike tour we were!

Doodle Bug.
