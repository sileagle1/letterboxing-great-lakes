Letterboxing concerns- pulling my boxes

From: Scout Dogs (DogScouts@hotmail.com) | Date: 2006-10-17 00:08:14 UTC-04:00
Because my schedule has not allowed me to do the maintenance on my boxes
that they require and because they have been out since 2003 (the ones that
haven't gone missing) and because of all the new, careless letterboxers out
and about... I have been pulling my boxes as I get the chance.

As much as I enjoyed finding boxes in the wild, my knees have gotten to the
point that I just can't do it without hurting the day of the hike and for a
day or two afterwards. :-(

All of mine have been drive ups or short hikes with easy clues, because
that's what I like to find and what my knees could handle, but that leaves
them vulnerable to the "one find wonders" as someone called them.

I have already pulled all the Franklin Co. Park boxes (which aren't
available during the winter anyway) and most of the Miami Whitewater boxes.
I'll be getting the rest really soon before it gets too cold, since that
makes it even harder for me to get out and about.

I'll be pulling Metamora and Harrison boxes next. Good news for those that
still want to find my boxes is that I can't get to TX and MI any time soon,
so those will be out for some time (if they don't go missing) unless someone
is willing to pick them up and send them to me.

I'm still involved in letterboxing through postal boxing and I enjoy using
my stamps to create art that I send out to other stamp artists in swaps and
at random through several different stamp related groups like the Carving
Consortium and Artsist of the Round Table. So my stamps get used and
enjoyed, just in a different way. I'll also still be willing to send stamps
to be placed for gatherings if I have time to carve.

I have enjoyed meeting so many great people, seeing such beautiful and
interesting places and enjoying the solitude of walking in the woods by
myself. I will miss that very much, but will still attend gatherings when I
can and I'll have a few event boxes floating around. I thank everyone that
has hidden a box for me to find, hosted a gathering or sent me an email
(about any topic.) Letterboxing has brought a lot of joy to my life and I
look forward to continuing that through postals.

Just wanted to give everyone a heads up if you notice many of my boxes going
into retirement. If there are still some out that you are just dying to
find, let me know if you can get to them quick and I'll put off retrieving
them till you find them.
