It is a matter of preference. Some folks just like to put the HH
out there and let it be found without any announcement. Some people
like to hear when their HH is found, others don't. Generally
speaking if you find a HH and the person has left a note in the log
requesting to be notified of the find, it is courteous to do so.
But ultimately whether you post to the list about any of these
things is up to you.

If you do choose to post, there are a few options. There is [yet
another] Yahoo group where a lot of people post HH info -- plants,
finds, retires, etc.

http://groups.yahoo.com/group/letterbox_travellers/

This group was formed because some of the people at the big list who
had placed HH did not want to hear about their travels. So it was
decided to create a separate group for talk about HH.

That being said, there are people who report HH finds, plants, etc.
on the big list and on the regional lists (such as this) and as far
as I know, none of them has been tarred and feathered for doing so.

Last of all, Pete and Wanda keep a comprehensive list of HH and
their travels. They monitor the various Yahoo lists for posts about
HH and you can also e-mail them directly to add a new HH or report a
find. Their site is at:

http://alum.wpi.edu/~p_miner/travelers.html

Hope that helps!

SpringChick
