Ack!!! It finally hit Cleveland! Look at this
message that was in our inbox this morning.

What to do, what to do? Actually, the first thing I
did was call my friend, who USED to be a reporter for
the Sun Newspapers before he got burned out & quit, to
get the inside scoop on this reporter. (I love having
connections.) My friend told me that this reporter is
a pretty fair guy, that he should treat the story
well. Just from my knowledge of the paper, this piece
will be more of a Community Life cover-story. This
reporter doesn't have a home office per se, he writes
what are known as general interest "enterprise"
stories.

(For those not familiar with the Cleveland area Sun
Newspapers, it's a once-a-week newspaper with several
different offices, supposed to focus on specific
communities. So section A stories that appear in the
Old Brooklyn/Brooklyn edition won't all necessarilly
appear in the Westlake/North Olmsted edition, but then
other stories, like community life features, will.)

So I guess we will talk with him eventually - it's the
quandry that some of the rest of you have faced - you
don't necessarily want Letterboxing to be out in the
open, but if you don't talk to the reporter he'll find
other means. My biggest worry, esp. with him wanting
photos, is that boxes will be put in jeopardy. Maybe
we'll just plant a box specific to the occasion that
we can use as a "demo."

If anyone who's dealt with this before has some
insight they'd like to share, please pass it on to us.


Doodle Bug.
