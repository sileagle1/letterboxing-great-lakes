Cheap Erasers for Carving

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-03-03 18:11:43 UTC

Just wanted to pass along the deal I found on erasers last night.
Typically I like to carve on PZKut (like the consistency but wish it
was thicker). The pink stuff and MasterCarve will also do if need
be. I also like Staedler erasers but hate sanding off the bumps and
they are not always large enough since the Grand size is no longer
available. Every now and then I run across a cheap vinyl/plastic
eraser that it quite carvable but it seems like they are hit or miss
and I often can't find the ones I like the next time I look for them.

Anyway... last night at one of the local $$ stores I ran across
white vinyl erasers -- 5 in a pack for a buck and each eraser is 2
3/4" (wide) x 1 1/8" (high) x 1/2" (thick). I figure I can get 2
letterbox stamps out of each of these -- that is 10 stamps for a
buck and because they are 1/2" thick, no need for mounting.

I carved one up last night to test the consistency and they carve
nicely -- not as nice as some of the material mentioned above, but
not bad -- certainly doable for the price. I was able to get a fair
amount of detail on the little flower I carved. The one thing I did
find though is that they don't do well with direct, extreme heat. I
usually transfer my image using my iron and that made kind of a
sticky mess. I can work around that though. I think they will hold
up okay -- MI gets hot in the summer, but not as hot as my iron.

The store I found them at is called Dollar Discount -- this
particular one was a Dollars & Deals the last time I was there so
I'm not sure if the organization changed names, or they changed
hands. All of their merchandise is the same as before so I have to
think they are one and the same.

Sorry to anyone in the Muskegon, MI area... I cleaned out their
supply.
