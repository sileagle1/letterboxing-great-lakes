What a great day!!!

From: duncan53211 (duncan53211@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-04-22 01:54:32 UTC
Boy, have I been longing for a day like today for, like, forever, it
seems! I had a perfect LB day. Met the Lizards to take care of some
business and we did the new Princess Bride series together. After we
survived the ROUSs, we found a certain Kanji letter at sunrise. We
then parted company, but crossed paths where we could rock 'n roll all
night and party every day. Lucky for me I was able to sneak in some
snowshoeing (yes, even on a day like today), but decided against some
outdoor ice skating. Instead, I picked up a couple of hobbies at a
local indoor ice rink and even caught sight of Santa and his crew
nearby. I finished the day with a final hobby of biking and on the
way back to my car I spied a suspicious group of people (three
generations) and noted a certain logo atop a piece of paper they were
holding as they passed. Accusing them of their actions, they admitted
to being guilty so I had them do an exchange with me! Turns out they
are relatives of a co-worker of mine and even had his little daughter
with them! They were out for their third find and will hopefully join
this group and have official trail name soon! All in all, 15 for 15
on letterbox finds and 3 exchanges! Like I said, a great day!

I'm sure I'm not the only one - let's hear your great day stories!

Dragon

massive pee-wee league soccer extravaganza!

From: (Mathw613@aol.com) | Date: 2007-04-21 23:53:45 UTC-04:00
Ok, we had a good but looonnnggg day as well.

We (me, wife and 4 under 5) made our annual trek to Freeport, IL a couple
months earlier than usual (due to our scheduled trip to Oregon this June --
which we may cancel due to our small 12 hour trial run of today).

There are 21 boxes in Stephenson County, I knew I shouldn't set my goals to
high so I was figuring maybe 15 or so LOL.

We found our first box right out of the shoot (well, after the 2 1/2 hours
it took us to get there), and bonus, there was a H.H. inside :-)

We then realized our gas tank was in the red, and I missed the oh so obvious
straight into town route and swung ourselves 7 miles out of our way on the
bypass. No sweat mates, we made it :-)

Next stop was Union Dairy for some oh so good ice cream and a little history
with Debate Square where Lincoln/Douglas had their second debate.

Next was Krape Park, a favorite part of our trip and now that we have
letterboxing in our blood there are 6 boxes in the one park!!!! But alas, the park
was under some MAJOR overhauling and we were only able to find 1 of the 6.
This in part because our crew was getting hungry and apparently ice cream isn't
enough for brunch to hold us until dinner. Aside from only finding 1 box the
waterfall (man made) was shut down, the ducks weren't out to feed & the
carousel wasn't running yet (did I mention we went a couple months earlier than
usual -- and now we have 6 hungry crabby people?)

Three hours after we arrived and we had a grand total of 3 stamps found
(including a HH). We met some local boxers at Krape Park, River Rat and Nature
Nerd and on their suggestion we were going to eat in Lena a short ways off, but
first I wanted to plant a box in Freeport -- not a wise move with a
hungry/crabby crew on hand . . . I was sweatin' bullets imagining that I was looking
like a rack of lamb in the eyes of my sweet adorable children.

I did plant the box, not happy with the spot but it will have to do until
next year.

Off to dinner we went, mmmmmmmmmm sweat salvation.

After diner we found 6 more boxes for a total of 9 finds (including HH) and
1 plant. Now, children are asleep and mom and I are turning in as well.

Hart x6

P.S. every time I typed the word "crabby" in the above Email I first typed
it up with "pp's" instead of "bb's" changes the whole meaning. I'm just glad
the whole day turned around and it didn't totally go down the crapper LOL

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2007-04-22 04:22:17 UTC
Yes, it WAS a great day! As some of you know, I've been working on a
project out in California for the last 9 weeks (with at least 2 more
weeks to go). This has limited our boxing activities. However I am
home this weekend and with the good weather forecast, we planned to
take care of some odds and ends on Saturday and then spend all day
Sunday doing some boxing. However when the Princess Bride series
was posted last night, we decided to skip the gym this morning and do
some boxing instead. It was a perfect morning - great temperatures,
sand hill cranes in the fields, lots of birds twittering and NO
mosquitos! In addition to the Pricess Bride series, we also found
the Witch's Lair and checked on my Skating box (that was the
only "not so great" part of the day).

Looking forward to another fun day tomorrow as we head north for
several boxes.

Wisconsin Hiker

Kimono Girl and FD,

We all applaud your wonderful accomplishments yesterday!!! Sadly, I
still have strings of lights outside that need removing. I do believe
you have served as motivation for me to not be tempted to hunt boxes
today and instead keep my letterboxing addiction under control--for at
least today?! So thank you for that!! :)

My yesterday was great also, but only indirectly, did it have to do with
letterboxing. We had an awesome "Showcase of Scouting" event for Cub
Scouts and Boy Scouts which included a district pinewood derby car race,
a reenactment team that fired muskets, muzzleloaders and cannons, a
70-foot suspended all-rope bridge, blow darts, tomahawk throwing, some
AWESOME dutch oven cooking of hawaiian chicken, pies, pork and more,
archery, leatherworking and tons more. We were able to recruit 15 brand
news scouts and over 75 boys and their families had the best of times
experiencing all of these various stations.

(Since I was free to take all the promotional photos and promote
scouting all day long, I was also free to promote letterboxing to those
I felt would be good and stealthy candidates! I also used some of my
carved stamps to stamp the scouts hands, depending on which events they
attended yesterday!) I love it that many of us can find ways to combine
our passions!

~Trailtracker
