I don't mind the 'white stuff' usually. However, the 'white stuff'
makes parking in the city very challenging. The Atomic Beans when out
for breakfast and upon our return we found our neighbor stuck trying to
get out of the spot in front of where we had been parked earlier. When
I asked, "do you have a shovel?" he seemed to think I was crazy. He
thought he'd be able to power his little car out of the spot and over
the snow-plow ridge without problem. WRONG! So, after I helped him
push his car out of the spot, we managed to settle back into the spot I
had shoveled clean earlier in the day. If everyone would suck it up and
shovel their own spots, the street would be clean after a day, and then
we could all get back to our normal parking scheme. Instead, I'm sure
that all my hard work will be taken by one of my lazy neighbors upon our
return home this evening. So, it seems that I'm off to go do some more
shoveling. We are very tempted to stick out our own folding chairs to
reserve our own spots....however, we doubt that will help....stupid
neighbors.

And when not at home, that first "SPLAT" into the 4 inches of muddy
water when you get out of the car while out shopping isn't much of a joy
either.

Sorry for the rant. Back to you.
