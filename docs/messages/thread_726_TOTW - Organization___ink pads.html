It seems that in the midwest at least, most boxes do not contain ink
pads. None of mine do. It's probably a combination of local custom
and climate; since most of us didn't start out finding ink pads in
the boxes we searched, we didn't include them in boxes we placed. And
I've heard stories about pads freezing and then making a mess when
they thawed.

That has lead me to carry a rainbow of pads with me "just in case"
the placer has created an extra special stamp. Colored markers are
also a useful alternative when the stamp warrants. Recently
the "Parrots" and the "Turkeys" made me glad I brought colored ink.

Now, about books, I take the easy way out. We have a spiral-binding
punch and so I just make my own books and use the plastic coils to
bind them. The coils are available in several diameters and colors. I
don't use fancy paper. Size is variable, depending on the box.
I'll admit they are not elegant, but they get the job done. I do hand-
sew very small books for Hitchers. I'll admit that I do admire some
of the wonderful hand-made books we've encountered (for example:
SpringChick's "Big House", as I recall).

It's good to revisit these issues as new boxers join the fold.
