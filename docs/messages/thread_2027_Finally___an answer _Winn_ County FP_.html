Okay, so I finally (!!!!) got my answer:

- - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - -

Dear Angela,

I have three letterboxes that I think are yours. You can come in to
our office (open Monday through Friday from 8:00 AM until 5:00 PM) to
pick them up. In the future if you wish to letterbox or geocache, you
need to register and pay a $10 fee for each calendar year.

Sincerely,

Evelyn

- - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - -

So, if anyone else has "lost" a box in the Winnebago County forest
preserves and wants to go with to see if they have them at the
office, let me know very soon. I hope to go sometime next week to
pick them up. :)

I was thinking (I don't know if I posted this before or not) about
adding a "clue" to the LBNA website under Winnebago County with a
warning about placing boxes that aren't registered. I would feel bad
if someone new (or who doesn't read this message board) comes along
and decides to place a box in the county only to have it picked up.
I would put a link to the website and the file where the rules and
forms are located. Does that sound like a good idea...or should I
ask...one I won't get flamed for? I don't count my places using the
numbers on the site, so I suppose that won't be a problem.

Angi / Shutterfly

Sounds like a good idea. We have the same thing in DuPage county.
Although the permit system is a year old, there have only been three
permit applications for letterboxes.
I met with the Ranger who approves the DuPage Forest Preserve
locations this past Monday to receive and affix my long
awaited "Official DuPage Forest Preserve Geocache" permit stickers to
my boxes. She (the Ranger) said they were starting to contact geocache
placers to have them fill out permit applications or remove their
caches. She said they will work on letterboxes later as there are so
many fewer of them.
I don't know about Winnebago but the guy in charge in DuPage is a
geocacher so "knowing" the boxes is natural for him. Really, I haven't
met a dud in the whole process so the permit system should be nice and
easy, and its free!
CMoon

p.s. - I did relate my dissapointment to the Ranger that the permit
sticker said "geocache"!
