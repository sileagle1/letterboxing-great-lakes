From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2006-07-20 22:02:28 UTC-05:00
Yeah, we go with kids sometimes and they know it is supposed to be a
"secret" kind of thing. However we don't want them to lie, so we have said
that it is OK to say we are doing a scavenger hunt, which it is - clues to
find something. We often do not want to explain letterboxing for various
reasons if we do not know the person.

Like many of the rest of you, we have rarely actually been asked outright
what we are doing. However many people must be wondering just WHAT were
those two middle-aged adults were doing with rubber stamps & markers in the
middle of the park/woods/trail?!?!? Although most people pretend to ignore
us, we have gotten comments like "Looks like you're having fun! or Looks
like quite a project!". We simply answer "YES!" We did once say we were
"looking for a pen we dropped from the deck" when an official police type
person appeared from nowhere on a trail in Florida. However we didn't need
to worry about setting a bad example because we were boxing with my mom
instead of our nieces & nephews!

Wisconsin Hiker
