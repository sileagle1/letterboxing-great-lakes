RE: [LbGLK] Re: About the Great Lakes Gathering

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2005-02-11 16:44:17 UTC-06:00
No need to wait for the snow to melt - I've planted several boxes in the
past month. It's really not that difficult. Besides, there are no pesky
bugs nor as many scratchy plants to claw at you. It is actually a lot
easier to get to some hiding spots. If you're worried about tracks in the
snow, you can just wait to publish your clues until we get more snow or
there is some melting. A lot of places have enough deer tracks, dog tracks
or people tracks to not make it too obvious what route you took to plant the
box.

We have no idea if we'll make it to the Gathering this year. Timing/date
seems OK, but distance, traffic and lodging considerations will definitely
impact our decision.

What I've seen on a "show your stuff" table are some stamps or boxes that
people created and that are somewhat unique or special for some reason,
interesting methods of recording your finds and plants (some have created
very impressive scrapbooks), photos of interesting letterboxing locations,
etc, etc. Basically anything related to letterboxing that you think someone
else might be interested in looking at.

Wisconsin Hiker
