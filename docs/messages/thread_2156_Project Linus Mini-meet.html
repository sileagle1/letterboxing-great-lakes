Project Linus Mini-meet

If you're a letterboxer who also crochets, this mini-meet is for you!

Back at the end of September I placed the Project Linus Postal
Letterbox. Project Linus is a national organization that collects
hand made blankets for children in hospitals and otherwise in need.
I invited letterboxers who crocheted to make me a granny square to
receive the box. I thought piecing together granny squares from
around the country would be a fun way to create a crocheted blanket
for this wonderful cause. I was hoping to receive enough squares
(around 36) to make one blanket. As of last count, 222 squares have
been received!

Mrs. Doglvrs, being the fabulous person she is, offered to meet me
one afternoon, half way between our homes for some company and
blanket making. After some discussion on the postal board this has
officially turned into a mini gathering. A room has been booked at
the Michigan City Public Library in Michigan City Indiana for
the `Project Linus Mini-meet'.

We have four of us already attending and only have room for 15 so we
do need you to let us know if you plan on attending. There will be
stamp exchanges, an event stamp, and at least one letterbox to
find. The Project Linus PLB will also be there to be stamped into,
but the main emphasis will be on joining the donated granny squares
into blankets.

The details are listed below as well as in the events list on Atlas
Quest. If you have any questions, feel free to contact Mrs. Doglvrs
or myself.

Hope to see some of you there!

jacks
