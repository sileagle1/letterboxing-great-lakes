In Defense of Simple Clues...

From: M Clan (joshmonty@hotmail.com) | Date: 2005-09-15 12:41:28 UTC
While I - like many letterboxers - appreciate a good riddle, puzzle,
cryptic clue, poetry, or fascinating story in my clues, there is one
reason, I appreciate simple, straightforward, "easy" clues as well.

I have a 3 year old and a 1 1/2 year old. The 1 1/2 year old is
simply along for the ride, but the 3 year old loves looking out for
the next fork in the road, bench, or other marker in many of the
relatively simple clues.

So the M Clan has planted some of both and has thoroughly enjoyed
finding boxes with both clue types and encourages you all to keep
planting - whether simple or complex, prose or poetry, story or
instruction.

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-09-15 12:34:05 UTC-04:00
Also makes it much easier to fit a variety of boxes into a trip. I am in the process of planning a couple of trips right now and while our destination locations (Shenandoah National Park, Door County, WI and Porcupine Mountains) will hold longer hikes and trickier clues, en route there and back, it is nice to have quicker leg-stretcher boxes that don't require a half day stop. As I try to fit in everything, I am having to eliminate some of the boxes along the way that require longer hikes, even though I really had my heart set on doing them.
