Tip for Hiking Alone...

From: Deb (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-03-30 17:47:54 UTC
When was writing up the post about what was in my pack and mentioned
my cell phone, I thought of a good tip to pass along...

I like to hike alone. I love the solitude of walking through a shady
summer forest, a little breeze in the trees, the sounds of birds and
critters... Only on a couple of occasions have I felt uneasy,
neither time in Michigan. I consider myself to be able to fend for
myself, but you never know what you may be up against and you can
never be too careful. Not only is there the threat of another human,
but animals or even just a simple trip on an exposed tree root can
leave you stranded out in the woods.

When I go out boxing for an afternoon by myself, I don't usually
leave a note or even specifically mention where I am going, sometimes
I don't even know for sure until I am there. If I were to not make
it back home for whatever reason, OldShoe wouldn't really have a clue
where to send the search party. I don't remember where I heard this
idea, but I thought it was a good one and I have been doing it ever
since.

One of the most important things if you are hiking alone is to make
sure that your spouse or another family member or friend (the person
who would most likely be contacting police if you were to disappear)
knows how to access the LbNA clue site and search for a letterbox.

Then while you are out, use your cell phone to call your home (or the
answering machine) and leave "check-in messages" as you go. I call
each time I get to the start of a hike. I leave the time, my
location (city, park, street), the name of the box I am setting out
to find and where I am planning to head next. Then when I get to the
next location, I do the same thing... This way it would be easier
for somebody to figure out approximately where you might be if you
don't make it back.

Be smart and be safe...
