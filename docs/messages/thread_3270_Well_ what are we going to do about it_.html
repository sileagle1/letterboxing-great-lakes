Sending letters to the reporters is a great idea but it will only
get us so far. Do any of you know someone in the news buisness that
could publish our side of the story in a little blip, after all it's
not what you know but who. I realize this isn't breaking news but we
might be able to get a sympathetic ear somewhere. Could we ask Choi
(is that how it is spelled? is'nt he the LBNA site manager?) to
maybe have one of those pages that you agree to certain things
before it allows you to view the clues. I can't think of where I saw
those type of web pages but I think it was when I was looking at my
college records, where it has I agree or disagree buttons. I'm I
making any sence? Like you have to sign up on LBNA to be able to get
clues and in the sign up conditions is reading over the etiquette of
LBing and agreeing to those terms. There is an etiquette page on the
LBNA site, but you as a responsible person have to seek it out. By
doing something like this you would throw it in their faces (they
actually have to press a botton to get rid of it), and some maybe
preturbed because you have to sign in to get clues. Well I don't
know anything about the nuances of web design and how hard this
would be to do but I thought I would throw it out there. This is the
major site where people get their clues, if we can stop it at the
source we may have a chance to educate alot of people before they
even try the sport.

From: Carol Osada (cjosada@hotmail.com) | Date: 2006-11-06 11:51:41 UTC-06:00
Now we're all starting to really think! These are good ideas. We need a plan! We need to work together with the webmasters to assure that this sport/hobby doesn't die at the hands of some weekend family thrill seekers. One reporter, one family, another reporter, another magazine...we need to get to the root of the problem.


We need to create an "SOB Campaign".....as in who is the SOB who caused another letterbox to go missing? Maybe an animal, maybe a newbie, maybe an irresponsibly written article published in a magazine or a newspaper. SOB also stands for SAVE OUR BOXES!!!

Starting today, we launch the campaign. You won't see billboards or TV ads or magazine articles about this campaign. But it's a movement that we all must promote! We put so much time and effort into creating boxes. Clues MUST require more work and thought! Plain and simple. We may never be able to put out these media fires as they pop up one after another, but we can do something to protect this sport/hobby by causing the average thrill seeker to work harder to find a box. More mystery involved. We are our own best guardians for our letterboxes. It's come down to survival of the fittest.

Beetle

Re: [LbGLK] Well, what are we going to do about it?

From: (mshills1@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2006-11-06 17:18:35 UTC-06:00
Please, please, please remember that we weekend family thrillseekers are
not all bad! We are very aware and have trained ourselves and our
children well. I am a research-a-holic by nature and when I found out
about letterboxing almost 2 years ago, I read everything I could find on
it (Yes, I went to both major sites and to Silent Doug's as well).
There are plenty of families that are here that have the utmost respect
for the placers, the boxes, etc. We love letterboxing. I can honestly
say I have gone places I would have never gone before (or would have
cared about). Thank you to those who make drive-by's AND those long
hikes (You know who you are, LOL!). I have to say a big thank you for
those longer hikes - we had our fall church hike in the Southern Kettle
Morraine - those 3 miles up and down through there were WAY Easy this
year because of the letterboxing we have done!

How many of you have gone by your first find site and mourned its loss
(to park clean up - it was retrieved by the placers), LOL! My children
and I went to our favorite easy place to hike a couple of weeks ago and
knowing the box was gone, stood and stared at the place where it had lived!

The Cat Herders

From: paul_m_48329 (paul_m_48329@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-11-07 04:05:49 UTC
Making the Rules of LB Etiquette (or the Getting Started pages, etc)
required reading before one can access clues is a good idea, but how
to enforce it? Simply clicking on I Agree/I Disagree doesn't really
verify that one has actually read it. I mean, after the first couple
of times, does anyone read the terms & conditions when you install
new software? And how many minors hit the "I'm Over 18" button?

So how about this? Guarantee that someone will read whatever page(s)
necessary before entering more 'sensitive' areas such as the LB clue
pages by making it fun & in keeping with the whole clue-solving feel
of LBing; a puzzle of sorts (read: password-protected) whereby in
order to unlock the 'secret area' of the site one must provide info,
i.e. a password, which can only be gathered by carefully reading the
required pages. I'll leave the details to someone who's more adept
at clue writing than I, but I'm picturing something akin to some
clues I've seen in which one was required to gather info from
historical or informational signs at a given location. We can't
guarantee that what someone reads will 'sink in', but perhaps this
may be a more pro-active step in the right direction...
