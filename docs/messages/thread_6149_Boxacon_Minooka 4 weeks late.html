Oh my goodness do things look different with the leaves down! I got a little disoriented trying to climb the hill up to the Simak box. The leaf layer was SO thick and dense that I couldn't find the trail at one point, when some walkers came by, so I followed them up to Simak's tree. I bet they thought it odd when I didn't finish the trek up the trail, but grabbed a plastic box from under a tree then turned around and headed the other way. The Clifford D. Simak LB is well, will get a little TLC before "returning" to Simak's hometown area in the coming weeks. It was great to see so many people had logged in!

Boy, do they ever look different!A clue that says "look forthe large dead tree" sure is less helpful on November 1st than it was on October 3rd. ALL the trees look dead; at least they have no leaves! However, with all the ground cover gone, the typical hiding spots like logs and stumps are a lot more apparent, so I guessthe difficulty levelsort of evens out... as long as one doesn't get lost along the barely-apparent trails.

I must've beenat Minookaaround the same time as you today. I was stamping into another box just down the trail from Clifford. I took it out into an open field so that I wouldn't be right at the hiding spot... anda few minutes afterI had everything spread out, a group of 30 hikers emerged from the same path! A couple of them asked if the Tupperware contained any lunch for them... sorry, folks...

Ah, it sounds like the Vagabond club was out again! We know some people in
that club. When we were at Minooka a few weeks ago we had to wait for a
bunch of them to go by. We watched the group to see if anyone we knew was
out & about and sure enough, 2 people that are also in our hiking club came
along the trail. They immediately asked if we were letterboxing, since they
know our obsession.



Martini Man was able to come home from his business trip last night rather
than tonight, so we actually were able to get out for 2 boxes ourselves
today. We also checked on my Year of the Ox box and it was in perfect
shape, waiting for a few more visitors.
