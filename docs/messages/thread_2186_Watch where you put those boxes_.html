From: ruhlette (ruhlette@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-01-18 06:53:17 UTC-08:00
My aunt sent me a link about a year ago about a geocache that was
detonated in Muncie, IN. Turns out the geocacher placed it behind a
business in the center of town and someone thought the item looked
suspicious. This is why I was apprehensive about opening the first
ammo-box I found along the way to a letterbox. I've not seen a geocache
since, but I know where many can be found in a stone wall.

Though it's not likely to happen, if I ever tire of letterboxing, I might
pick up eco-scavenging, geodashing or benchmark hunting; these don't
require placing anything.

Be safe and smart,
