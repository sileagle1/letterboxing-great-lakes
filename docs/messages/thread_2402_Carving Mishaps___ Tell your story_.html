From: pennypenny_sl (chancey@cablespeed.com) | Date: 2006-04-15 23:00:29 UTC
Hi Friends,

I figured I'd start a new thread here for a little fun -- Carving mishaps! I'm sure there are
some real good stories out there. Here's my latest letterboxing tale of woe....

I'm fairly new at letterboxing... and have only placed 3 boxes, and carved 6 stamps (this
includes my own personal stamp, an event stamp, and the next box I'm working on.) I just
realized this morning that I have to start my new stamp over again:

My mistake... and I knew eventually there'd be times that I'd have to start over on a
stamp... was that I finished a beautiful one (IMHO, lol!) -- got it all ready to place -- and
realized I had misspelled the word "chickadee" on it!!!! ARRRGGGHHHHH! I spelled it
'chicadee' instead. I searched in vain on the web to see if perhaps it can be spelled both
ways but no luck there. So I'm off to start this one over.

The one other stamp I carved once which almost bit the dust was a smaller baseball stamp
that my 3-year-old found on my table. I'm sure he wanted to imitate mom and decided to
try his hand at the hobby. He took a pretty good chunk out of it, (and yes, I'm lucky the
chunk wasn't out of his finger,) but with a bit of glue and a day or two under some weight
it was saved. For that stamp I learned NEVER to leave my carving box any lower than 5 feet
off the ground!

My lesson I learned today (duh!!!) is to always check and double-check the spelling if I'm
using words in my stamps!!!

Have a nice weekend, everyone!

PennyPenny
South Lyon, MI

I carved a stamp with the name written under the image, but I wrote
the name directly on the stamp instead of reversing it, so the
letters were backwards when you stamped it. I think that's a pretty
common mistake, but frustrating all the same. Fortunately, the
letters were underneath the image, so I just cut off the bottom and
used the picture without the words!

By the way, PennyPenny, I had great fun with your South Lyon boxes on
Friday. I found all three, despite having to try twice on two of
them because people were too close for me to retrieve the box. All
three are in great shape - and really nice stamps. I look forward to
more plants from you!

From: Kathi LK Krankoski (krank@ameritech.net) | Date: 2006-04-16 23:22:33 UTC-05:00
Okay, a while back I mentioned a box I placed in VA, 'Two Brothers', last
summer when we had mini reunion at my niece's house. The stamp I originally
carved was this whole family tree of letters: T(om) + K(athi) -->
M(ichelle), plus my brother-in-law's side as well, which goes one generation
further than ours (grandchildren). Letters are not my forte, but I wanted
the practice. I thought it came out halfway decently, until I realized I
put the wrong letter for my niece! Duh! An "M" instead of an "S", right in
the middle of the stamp - not fixable either. Back to the drawing board.
The final stamp just says "2 Brothers" - I wasn't going to go through all
that again!



--klk/FungusWoman
