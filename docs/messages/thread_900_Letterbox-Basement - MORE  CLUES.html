Re: Letterbox-Basement - MORE CLUES

From: Tom & Jill (tj_mich@ameritech.net) | Date: 2004-11-23 21:27:44 UTC

You can read about the origins of the letterbox-basement on the main
list, in messages 35994, 36026, and 36045.

If I remember correctly, it houses all of the clues to letterboxes
that were on the "old" letterboxing.org, that as of ~October 2003,
hadn't yet made it onto the "new" letterboxing.org. A bit of recent
history...

Prior to ~spring/summer 2003, only the webmasters could post clues
on letterboxing.org. Whenever someone planted a new box, they'd
post the clues as a message on the main list, and the webmasters
would add it to the site. As the popularity of letterboxing
increased, this became too burdensome for a handful of webmasters,
and the site was upgraded to what we have today: boxers create an
account with a username and password, and enter their own boxes on
the site. During that transition, most people who were still
actively letterboxing entered their own clues onto the new site.
However, some of the older boxes, or boxes from non-active folks,
were left "unclaimed" and never made it over. Some of those boxes
might still be in place; some are probably missing. By all means,
if you find one of them and want to enter the clues on the new site,
that's a great way to let other boxers know it's still active. (If
you decide to adopt a box in this manner, you might want to try
contacting the original placer one last time, if you have an e-mail
address for them.)

Another good reason for the new format is that it clearly indicates
who planted each box. The webmasters (understandably) have no
desire to take on the legal responsibility for boxes that don't
belong to them -- for example, when a state/national park official e-
mails to say "get this box out of here."

Hope that helps explains what you're seeing.
