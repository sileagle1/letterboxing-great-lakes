2006 Great Lakes Gathering

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2006-01-04 04:51:45 UTC
Hello & Happpy New Year!

Today was the first day we could make reservation requests at the
local parks and I'm happy to report that we have reserved space for
the 2006 Great Lakes Gathering on Saturday June 3 at Lapham Peak
State Park in Delafield, WI.

There will be a discounted daily rate for attendees who don't have a
WI State Park sticker. Here's a link for more info about the park:

http://www.dnr.state.wi.us/org/land/parks/specific/lapham/

Martini Man & I have been tossing around a few ideas for a gathering
theme, but if anyone else wants to make some suggestions, please send
us an email or post something to the board.

I'm really busy at the office this week, but will create a website
for the gathering in the near future.

We hope to see many of you at this event, so be sure to mark the date
on your calendars! (It will give us SOMETHING to record, since WE
sure haven't been marking any BINGO items yet...)

Wisconsin Hiker

Well, here is some good news for Cigna Cats! I just started looking at
the on-line text of "Through the Looking Glass" and how does it
start ....

CHAPTER 1

Looking-Glass house


One thing was certain, that the WHITE kitten had had nothing to
do with it: -- it was the black kitten's fault entirely. For the
white kitten had been having its face washed by the old cat for
the last quarter of an hour (and bearing it pretty well,
considering); so you see that it COULDN'T have had any hand in
the mischief.

The way Dinah washed her children's faces was this: first she
held the poor thing down by its ear with one paw, and then with
the other paw she rubbed its face all over, the wrong way,
beginning at the nose: and just now, as I said, she was hard at
work on the white kitten, which was lying quite still and trying
to purr -- no doubt feeling that it was all meant for its good.

But the black kitten had been finished with earlier in the
afternoon, and so, while Alice was sitting curled up in a corner
of the great arm-chair, half talking to herself and half asleep,
the kitten had been having a grand game of romps with the ball of
worsted Alice had been trying to wind up, and had been rolling it
up and down till it had all come undone again; and there it was,
spread over the hearth-rug, all knots and tangles, with the
kitten running after its own tail in the middle.
........


For those of you who want to read basic on-line versions of the two
books, you can find them here:
http://www.cs.indiana.edu/metastuff/wonder/wonder.txt.gz and here:
http://www.cs.indiana.edu/metastuff/looking/looking.txt.gz

There are also .html versions available and if you go a-googling you'll
also find illustrated versions. It is amazing how many different
artists have done illustrations for these books!
