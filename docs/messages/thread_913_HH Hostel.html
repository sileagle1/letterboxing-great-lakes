extreme temperatures and be watertight. Gander Mtn sells a similar style, and it was
cheaper than otterbox online. Last time I looked it was holding up well. I'll check after
our first Good Snow. I made a hostel registry and letterboxers are doing a good job of
checking in and checking out. If you you want to see an example, email me privately and
I'll be happy to share it.

Re: HH Hostel

From: the_cichlid_guy (the_cichlid_guy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-12-01 04:11:31 UTC

I don't see why two would be a problem. The more the merrier. Our
boxes are going to be very far apart. Grand Rapids is a couple hours
away from where I am going to be planting mine. Also my hitchhiker
hostel is going to be a mystery box. All of the HH hostels that I
have found have always contained a logbook and a stamp for the box. I
even saw one that had two logs. One for the visitors and one for the
hitchhikers. It is a good idea to mark on the logbook and stamp
something saying, "Do not take from this box. Not a HH stamp." Just
so there is no confusion. I have seen as many as three hitchhikers in
a box at a time. It would be easy to mix up unless marked. If all
goes well I am hoping to have it planted before Christmas. Good luck
with your box. I look forward to finding it someday if you decide to
make it.

-Ryan
The Cichlid Guy
