From: Rose (rosy_357@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-09-04 14:01:54 UTC
I don't have a problem with store bought stamps as long as they are good and have
something to do with the box itself (now bad clues are an entirely different subject,
however). I must admit, when we first placed our box it had a store bought stamp. We
had only been letterboxing for about 6 months and had not gotten into the carving-
stamps bit yet. Plus, I'm the kind of person who doesn't like to do only a semi-okay job
(it's still pretty amateur but at least decent). In fact, buying the supplies and trying out
carving had been on my "to-do" list for quite some time when finally Trailtracker gave me
a bunch of carving goodies for Christmas one year. If it hadn't been for her extra nudge
(perhaps push?? lol) who knows how long it would have taken me.

As long as people pick interesting sites to plant their boxes and have clues that make
sense I'm fine with the store bought stamp. I do love the idea of notating that the stamp
is store bought in the clues on the letterboxing.org site - but I certainly wouldn't let that
stop me from trying to find their box. If I had done that I would have missed out on some
absolutely fabulous boxes in gorgeous locations otherwise. Don't get me wrong, I really
love seeing all the beautiful stamps that people far more talented than myself carved.
Many are truly works of art! But give the rest break...they may just be getting into
letterboxing like we were and are testing the waters. If letterboxing becomes a hobby that
is too difficult to participate in or people aren't welcoming then what's the point? There's
room for all of us.

Re: Store Bought Stamps, Secrets, and Grumpy People

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2007-09-05 17:12:05 UTC
You don't need to apologize for using store-bought stamps. While many
letterboxers prefer hand-carved stamps, particularly those who are
focused on the art aspect of the hobby, there are many other
letterboxers who don't care, or at least not enough to avoid boxes
with purchased stamps if the box is at a nice location, etc.

It is your letterbox, you are free to include the image you want,
hand-carved or store-bought, you are free to hide where you want and
clue how you want. The important thing is that you need to be willing
to accept that because of the choices you make in this regard, there
may be people who pass by that box. This is true in regards to the
type of location or hike, just as it is with the type of stamp you
include in the box.

While it is considerate to include this information in your clues, it
is not a requirement. There is a common misconception that the
planter owes it to finders to provide all kinds of information about
their letterbox so that a finder can decide if they want to go look
for it or not. Everything about a letterbox is so subjective that for
a planter to try and please every potential finder is ridiculous. For
everyone who wants the clues to specify how long the hike is, how
steep the terrain, what the stamp is like, etc. there is another
person who does not want this information provided, rather would like
to be surprised when they get there. The thing about letterboxing is
that one should set out without an expectation, other than to *make*
their experience enjoyable -- whether that be because of the location
or the company or the box itself, that is up to the finder.

Personally I would rather have an interesting location and a good hike
with a store-bought stamp than a boring location or drive-by with a
hand-carved stamp. Okay, that's not quite the truth -- actually, I
would prefer to have it all, but if I had to choose, a killer
hand-carved stamp would not be my top priority.

So if you truly feel you cannot carve a stamp and using store-bought
stamps is the only way you are comfortable planting a letterbox right
now -- don't hesitate because of this. There are plenty of
letterboxers who will look for the box regardless.

SpringChick

I have to say that while I definately have a strong preference for a hand carved stamp (I have to admit to being just a little disappointed when I find a store bought stamp, especially if I wasn't expecting it to be purchased) I don't really mind the store bought stamps too much. If I were going out of town on a trip, a store bought stamp might deter me from looking for a box (unless it were a quick, short hike at a point in the drive where a good stretch was needed) and knowing that it's store bought rather than hand carved or commissioned will knock it down towards the bottom of my priority list. But it wouldn't completely deter me from going after a box. Looking through my first logbook, quite a few of my first finds were purchased stamps. The images may not be unique and I may not like them as much as most of the hand carved images in my book, but they do hold some pretty fond (and funny) memories. In fact, the only time I've ever really been upset about finding a
store bought stamp was when we were in Ohio and we were looking for one of Scoutdogs' boxes. Scoutdogs was the one who introduced me and Katy to letterboxing and we were really looking forward to this box because her stamps are AWESOME. When we found the box, someone had replaced her stamp with a cheap store-bought foam stamp. I was SO bummed!
