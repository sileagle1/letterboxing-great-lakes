From: Doodle & Deedle Bug (doodle_n_deedle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-09-25 05:12:33 UTC-07:00
Ack! Ack! Ack! Deedle & I don't get the Plain
Dealer, but had my mother seen this she would have
called me right away, being fully aware of her
daughter's letterboxing activity, but I had to take my
parents to the airport this morning and so she didn't
read the paper over her coffee today. I'm
hyperventilating here. Granted, I knew it was only a
matter of time before something like this hit the
paper in our neck of the woods, but still...

OK, I've calmed down enough to actually READ the
article, and I guess it's not that bad - I mean, the
reporter did include the line about "Secrecy is part
of the letterbox mystique," but there have been much
better articles, too. Wee Walkers (who I've met
before) line is smacking me with its irony, "Some
people are afraid that it would be spoiled if too many
people find out about it." So we're gonna do a front
page Metro section story on it!

It does only mention letterboxing.org, though, not
AtlasQuest. The map they included as a graphic is
cute, but...but...did they HAVE to list Huntington #1?
That's one of the oldest boxes in the area. Wait,
what's that I hear.... I think it's the Cleveland
MetroParks powers-that-be changing their geocaching
policy to include letterboxing now, too!

And that was very rude for the reporter to oust
Travel'n Turtle's library box. Especially since we
haven't had a chance to find it yet ;) How much you
want to bet the librarians are trying to figure out
the clues to that box right now? *smirk*

Doodle Bug.
