I've been on unintentional hiatus... work just got really crazy at
the end of May and has been non-stop the past few weeks. I thought
last year was overwhelming because I was new to the job, but this year
was far busier -- everyone says because this year I know what I need
to be doing. Anyway camp is full of kids now and things are settling
into a pleasant routine so it should slow down for me a bit.

I apologize to anyone who e-mailed me and didn't get a reply... I am
afraid that my e-mail box overfloweth. I will be slowing wading
through messages over the next few days, and then resuming work on
getting my box clues back online... my web hosting issues could not
have come at a worse time.

It has been cool around here, actually quite pleasant weather for
letterboxing and I have wistfully thought of the trail and unfound
boxes many times.

SpringChick

I thought I'd take this opportunity to introduce myself. I'm Bill, my
trail name on LbNA is Chilly, and I live in the most southwesterly
part of west Michigan. Retired Navy photographer, engaged to Mary,
work part time, sail, ride motorcycles, xc and downhill ski, ski
patrol... I come here via geocaching where I am planting a couple of
letterbox hybrids. To bring this around to the topic, they'll be in
the most northwesterly part of southwestern Michigan. Been doing my
homework, trying to absorb the history and traditions, just finished
carving my first stamp, got a couple of lock n lock boxes together...
I'm writing the clues both with, and without, the requirement to use a
GPSr, and will post the latter on LbNA. Looking forward to visiting
lots of boxes, putting out a few. and running into many of you along
they way.

Chilly
