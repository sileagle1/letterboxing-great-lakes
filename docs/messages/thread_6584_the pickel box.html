From: bob (tat2bob@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2010-05-05 08:34:14 UTC-05:00
as some of you know or may have found we have a box called "the pickel box" and if you read the clue youll find out he was a co worker of mine and the other day I got a email that shows just what a great hobby this is and youll never know where or whats gonna happen but I just had to share this everyone this is the email from lbna:
and thank you who ever threw away the clues
Hello,

Pickels was married to my cousin Bonnie, who passed away many years ago. One of my friends works at the Citgo station in McHenry, and while he was cleaning out the trash bins, he came across a printed copy of clues for The Pickel Box. Terri also knew George from driving gravel trucks. He saved the paper to show me. After I read it he said do you want to go and check this out.

So this evening about 6:30, we went over to St. Mary's Cemetary for an adventure. We got to say hello to George and Bonnie. We checked the evergreen bushes, but did not find a box.

I thank you so much for doing a tribute to George. He was a great guy and left us much too soon.

Sincerely,

Debbie xx xxxxxxx

From: one love journeying (jeners@gmail.com) | Date: 2010-05-05 09:09:23 UTC-05:00
What a beautiful story. It brings tears to my eyes (but I'm a sap like
that). It's amazing how connected we all are... and how the love we put out
there continues to touch lives even when we don't know about it. I'm glad,
in this case, you got to know about it.

There is something to say about "Changing the world, one box at a time" as
our GLG is titled. When we plant our boxes, we do more than just put a box
out into the world... we add another point of connection between time and
space for an unpredictable web of fellow human beings.

Thanks to all of you who are a part of this hobby. I feel so blessed that it
has come into my life, not just for the adventure it has brought, but for
the community of amazing people that I get to meet, connect with, and share
in this love for letterboxing.

With reverence,

One Love Journeying

From: one love journeying (jeners@gmail.com) | Date: 2010-05-05 15:34:57 UTC-05:00
Good question, Dragon. Perhaps we should try it anyway. Or maybe write him a
letter. Post it on all of the sites. Spread it around so that he is bound to
run into it. There are so many more battles he could fighting for the
environment... such better places to put his energy to make a difference. I
would like to be able to commnicat to him or others like him that
letterboxers and geocachers are not the ones he should be worrying
about. Our hobby brings people to the beautiful natural areas that he cares
so much to protect, not only creating financial contributions to help
support them, but also increasing awareness and appreciation for those
places. What's more, if we did not have all of the beautiful gifts that our
environment and natural spaces provided us, we would not be able to
have this hobby we so enjoy! If anything, I would argue that
letterboxing creates a sense of appreciation and respect for the
environment. If I could, I would appeal to him to team up with letterboxers
and geocachers to support education of how to plant boxes respectfully,
which areas we should avoid walking through, and even trying to organize
movements to support areas that need financial help to be saved. I mean
hell! Throw a gathering at a run-down park and require donations to fix it
up and we could make a serious difference! Anyway, that's my soapbox on this
matter. I was disheartened when LK told me about the individual who is
taking boxes based on the presumption that they are harming the
environment. *sigh*

Whoever you are out there, I send you my love, and I hope that you find a
more positive way to channel your love for our Earth.

Respectfully,

One Love Journeying
