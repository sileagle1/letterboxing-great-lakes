Do check the rules about firewood, but as a Master Gardener who is from the
epicenter of the Emerald Ash Borer area, please do not transport any
firewood from your home to a campground. All of the original borer hotspots in
the Upper Peninsula can be traced back to campground areas where people
unknowingly moved firewood. See
_http://www.emeraldashborer.info/surveyinfo.cfm_ (http://www.emeraldashborer.info/surveyinfo.cfm) for more info. 10
states and Canada are now involved with upwards of 10 million trees dead or
dying.

I know that taking your own firewood saves quite a few bucks, but the
economic costs are huge to the region as a whole. Ask one of my mother's
neighbors who got hit with a $10,000 bill to remove their dead/dying trees from
their yard. And, no, insurance doesn't cover it.
