From: Katrina Lustofin (drkaty@gmail.com) | Date: 2011-08-29 12:58:19 UTC-04:00
I am more likely to consider where the HH has been than to consider where
the planter lives (frankly, half the time I somehow fail to find the
planter's name on the HH entirely so there is no way I would know where that
person lives). I try to move it somewhere it has not been before.
Sometimes, though, I am just trying to find a box big enough to hold an HH
and any appropriate sized box will do.

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2011-08-29 19:33:53 UTC-05:00
We usually take several trips each year, so we try to "mix 'em up", moving
them from one area of the country to another. For example, we found one you
(Atomic Beans) released and will take it out west with us rather than put it
in a box around here (especially it was originally part of your "A Very Good
Year" series, which many people in our area already found. We bring HHs
that we find in other regions back here for a visit.

From: cpjenks2001 (cpjenks2001@yahoo.com) | Date: 2011-08-30 18:01:04 UTC
We travel around Michigan quite a bit, though not into other states very often. When we find one, I try to bring it to another part of the state. I'm from the GR area, so if I'm heading toward the east side of the state or up north, I'll bring it there, and vice-versa.

This reminds me of another question regarding HH's.....
Suppose I'm checking on one of my own boxes to see if it need repairs and I find a HH. Is it in bad form for me to take it? Or should I leave it for the next finder?

Re: HH etiquette

From: be_a_badger (schuppner@uwalumni.com) | Date: 2011-08-30 23:15:10 UTC
We move them out of state, if we have plans to travel to a state that's new to the hitchhiker. Otherwise, we try to move them along more quickly and closer to our home. If we find one in a series, we're not likely to leave it in another box that's in the same series.

Size matters. Those big ones are hard to move.

We also look for a box that sees a fair amount of traffic to avoid stranding the hitchhiker for too long.

The Christmas Elves

Re: HH etiquette

From: hnjruetten (hnjruetten@yahoo.com) | Date: 2011-08-31 13:19:52 UTC
We have learned to take ALL our supplies along when checking on our boxes as we have found several HH. We stamp them in our logbook and then leave them for the next person to move along. We want others to have the joy of finding it on our box to make that box all the more memorable. Also we are not necessarily planning on doing our own letterboxing in the near future and we don't want to leave it on our bag for too long.

The Gamer Ruettens
