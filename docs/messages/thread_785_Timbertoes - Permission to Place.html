I also subscribe to the "forgiveness rather than
permission" stance,
however, I did have a really good experience asking
for permission
not long ago. I've already mentioned it here, but to
recap: We
wanted to plant a series in a WI county park and, at
the prodding of
my husband, called the county headquarters for
permission. The
director was very interested in the hobby after I
described it and
gave me blanket permission to plant in any of our
county's parks. I
sent him the clues as well so he could see how
harmless the hobby
was. He, in turn, sent a memo to all his park
employees with a
glowing tribute to a hobby that would encourage hiking
and the number
of park visitors... he also attached the clues to the
memo. It must
have been effective because those logbooks are full of
thumbprint
stamps, signatures and commercial stamps from families
that have
tried the hobby.

Given that response, I feel pretty comfortable placing
without asking
first in most cases.
