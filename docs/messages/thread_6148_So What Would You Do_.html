So What Would You Do?

From: sunnysideupm (drewes@WI.RR.com) | Date: 2009-10-30 23:10:53 UTC
Might be a silly question knowing us LB folks, but here goes: You just pulled into a park about an hour away from home (you're in the area for another errand) when you realize you left your LB stuff at home, BUT you do have the translated clues that you worked hard on. Quick check of the clues - don't need a compass for this one. So what would you do?

I made sure I had a pen, said a little LB prayer that there'd be enough ink on the stamp to get a "huffed" image from it, and went searching. Turns out in my case that box was not there - gee, maybe, that and the Latin language clue are why the box hasn't been found in four years. I'll report the details of my attempt to the placer, mark the box "ignore", and consider another try if I ever find all the Dane County boxes (HAH!).

So, what are your tales of minimalist letterboxing?

- Sunny

From: (Mathw613@aol.com) | Date: 2009-10-30 22:42:18 UTC-04:00
One time I was heading out to a town about an hour and a half away for
work.

I didn't have any boxing gear at all, all I could do was print the clues
and grab a sharpie on the way out the door. No sig stamp, no logbook, no ink.

I was going to stamp the image on the clue sheet and then later put it in
my logbook.

I found the box, colored the stamp, huffed and then NOTHING... the sharpie
was dead!!

So, well, as the title of the Email says, "What would you do?"

The logbook in the box was index cards cut in half, hole punched in the
corner and bound with a metal ring.

I opened the metal ring and took the page out that had the image of the
stamp on it. I figure the only thing missing for future finders would be the
page in the log with the image of the stamp on it. I was, after all, the
first finder.

I had the opportunity to apologize to Pugnasties in person at Boxacon :-)
