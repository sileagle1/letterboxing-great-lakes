BoaF Gathering

From: wi_martini_man (jrussell6@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2008-06-22 12:31:03 UTC
Wow! What a fantastic Birds of a Feather event yesterday!
TrailRider and Pastor's wife were so well organized (they even
obtained some great weather for us). Their husbands and other family
members assisted with cooking, photos, and generally keeping
everything flowing. There were a ton of great carvngs and the bird
theme was carried out in variety of creative ways. The trails
surrounding the lodge and river were nice and we had a chance to
explore all of them on our hunts. Not only did we find a lot of
boxes, the hostesses gave everyone a CD with bird-themed songs by
many different artists. We've only listened to a bit of it so far,
but we'll have a chance to hear the rest when we drive home later
today.

As usual at an event, the food was tasty (especially those Revel
bars!) and it was great to see so many "old friends" again. It was
also fun to meet people like Northstar and Espresso Beans who were
attending their first event. Northstar has a great logbook, with
annnotations for every box find.

Thanks TR and PW for organizing and executing a wonderful day for all
of us!

Wisconsin Hiker & Martini Man

From: Atom118 (Aatom118@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-06-22 12:51:45 UTC-05:00
DITTO, DITTO, DITTO, DITTO, DITTO....

We had a WONDERFUL time at the gathering!

The park was perfect for a gathering...everything was spread out, but it
was all along the same trail. Result....most of the boxes were far
enough away from one another that there were not 'packs' of folks at
each box, but we were able to bump into most of the groups along the
way. (we managed two new exchanges as a result, one with Frog A Nobies
and one with a new local boxer who didn't know about the
gathering...just wanted the box that was newly posted on LBNA!) Great
to meet boxers who we only know via email and box comments, and, as
always, nice to catch-up with our other boxing friends. It was nice to
see so many GLKers. I guess the location was also perfect since it
seemed to be nearly equidistant from Madison, Milwaukee, Chicago, and
Iowa (give or take).

Trial Rider, Pastor's Wife, and company were tremendous hosts.....They
had everything under control, and they didn't seem to have forgotten any
minor detail! We felt a bit lazy...when we got there in the morning,
and were leaving in the afternoon, our offers of help came back,
"Thanks, but I think we have everything under control." And they sure
did! (BTW, what was the results from the voting)?

Oh, and the boxes! WOW, there were some wonderful carves of birds, and
there were a few ingenious hiding locations (including the hiding spot
for the retainer...). We hadn't seen things like that before.

GREAT, GREAT day!

Atom of Atomic Beans

From: Carol Osada (cjosada@hotmail.com) | Date: 2008-06-22 13:20:24 UTC-06:00
I knew I would miss a great letterbox gather, so I am looking forward to the photos.
The Mukwonago Relay For Life I attended on Friday and Saturday raised $105,300 + and that didn't reflect the matching corporate donations. So we probably hit our goal of $150,000 for the American Caner Society. This was my first relay and it was both fun and emotional. Each of the 24 teams had to have at least one or more persons walking the track from Friday at 4 pm till Saturday at 9 am. I did my share of walking laps, especially the Sat. 1-2am for the Dress As Your Favorite Rock Star lap. My tribute to Sir Paul McCartney was fab! Yes, I have photos of Beetle as a Beatle! But all that lap walking was more than any day of letterboxing. The abuse we subject ourselves to is amazing. Today, I just want to rest. I was thinking of my letterbox friends. Sorry I wasn't able to attend the BofaF gathering, so I still sent Blackbird Flies instead. With Love From Me To You! Beetle
