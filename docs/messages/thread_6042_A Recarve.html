How about trying to fix the old one and leaving it in the original box and then making an add-on box for the new stamp, with a one-step clue in the back of the logbook (you could explain what happened)?

Doesn't it kind of suck when your carving progresses to the point where you feel that the level of skill and precision detract from the charm of your stamps? While I greatly admire the talent displayed in finely detailed carvings, there is a definite charm to the rudimentary, primitive carvings (especially those where the carver has the skill to maintain that primitive look even though I know they have what it takes to do a finely detailed masterpiece).
