Re: First box you found stories

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2008-01-04 02:49:06 UTC
When did you find your first box? July 27, 2002
Which box was it? Chicago Underground
Who was the placer? Matt the Rat
Where was the box? Downtown Chicago
Was anyone with you? OldShoe

Anything else you want to share about that first ever letterbox? I
had read about letterboxing several months earlier in the blog of a
New England web friend and immediately got excited about this thing
that combined everything I loved (hiking, art, puzzles, clues,
treasure hunts). Imagine my disappointment when I discovered there
were no letterboxes anywhere near me (at the time, Michigan had only a
handful of boxes on the east side of the state). I kind of forgot
about it until we were planning a weekend trip to Chicago later that
summer. I checked for clues and sure enough there were a couple of
boxes in Chicago (back then, there were literally only a *couple* of
boxes in the metro Chicago area). I found a suitable rubber stamp (I
actually found a stamp that said "Spring Chicken" and cut off the "en"
part), and we stopped en route at an office supply store to pick up a
notebook that sufficed as our first logbook. At the time, OldShoe was
not really buying into the whole thing and insisted he did not need a
stamp of his own. Fortunately for me, it rained most of the weekend,
so hunting for a letterbox *underneath* the streets of Chicago made
perfect sense. Our journey to the box was part comedy and part
creepy. Imagine two middle-aged, middle-classed, touristy types (i.e.
camera, khaki shorts, Birkenstocks) walking around in the underbelly
of Chicago, poking around in support posts looking for a rubbermaid
container. For a while, we had a scruffy guy with a cart following us
and had to ditch him before we could continue looking for the box.
But we found it, actually OldShoe found it, which made him a convert
and left him asking, "where is the next box?" So we left there and
headed to the Magic Hedge, another Matt the Rat box which is an even
creepier story for another time (needless to say we have a deep
respect for Matt the Rat boxes). Despite the unsavory aspects of
those first two finds, we spent the whole 3 hours back home plotting
where we would plant letterboxes in West Michigan... and the rest, as
they say, is history.

SpringChick
