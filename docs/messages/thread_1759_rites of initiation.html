From: Kathi LK Krankoski (krank@ameritech.net) | Date: 2005-08-25 12:41:14 UTC-05:00
Took my Dad out boxing today! First we headed to the Showmen's Rest.
Interestingly, we discovered that my dad's aunt and uncle are buried right
next to the bloom that's not a bloom!



Next we went to visit Bob Mann for Meadow Walk and Field of Dreams. Found
the former with a year old hitchhiker released by SheWhoPlants.



Then to plant the hook even further, I took him to a couple that I had
placed, even though I didn't have clues with for him to follow. Barn
Theater and Hurray for Hollywood were on our way home, and then I treated
him to a coffee and Red Bulldog at the Cyberdog.



Oh, he forgot his stamp at home but signed in - he's going by "Boxer in(to)
Training" (he's into Lionel model railroading).



--klk/FungusWoman

From: tiptoetonto (tiptoetonto@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-08-25 20:57:33 UTC
We are sighing at your seemingly perfect day in initiating your Dad
to LBing! We couldn't help but re-live the nightmare day that my
sisters took my Dad out to find one of our boxes on one of the
hottest days we had this summer - He couldn't understand why we had
put all that history "fluff" to explain the box...why we had him
go "all over cock-robin's barn" to see the whole place when he could
have gone backwards on the path to find the box in seconds flat! He
got eaten alive by mosquitos, my nephew who was along for the event
got stung by bees and his face swelled up. If that wasn't enough,
they read the wrong coordinates and couldn't find the box...So, what
does dear old Dad do? He grabs my sister's cell phone and calls
Tonto to yell at him about what a stupid hobby this is and where in
the heck (not exactly his word) was the darn (again, not exactly his
word) letterbox. This normal behavior would have been easily
handled by Tonto, but at that very minute he was right in the middle
of a sales presentation to a big school district and took the call
thinking it was an emergency - after all it was coming from my
sister in Ohio...we are in Illinois! and the box was in Indiana!

I don't think we will get him out on the trail anytime soon! But,
your account of LBing with your Dad gave us some hope that it might
be possible to try it again...maybe after we've imbibed him with a
little bit stronger drink than coffee....Maybe Martini Man could
help in that catagory!
Tiptoe & Tonto

However one of our friends from the hiking club has expressed an
interest in boxing, so she and my mom are going to try it together. I
carved them signature stamps this week and delivered them (w/logbooks)
on Tuesday night. I told them they were on their own for getting
inkpads and compasses. My mom has successfully explored LBNA and
identified some "easy" clues and boxes for them to tackle. But she
also asked "where are those other places you look at for info?" I sent
her some links yesterday and she is in the process of figuring out how
to get on this message board, as well as looking at some other sites I
recommended. We're leaving town tomorrow, and I expect that they may
go out on their first hunt either this weekend or next. I wonder what
kind of stories they'll have to tell us when we get back home?!?!
Hopefully it won't be like Tiptoe & Tonto's story..... (plus I hope
they remember to take their stamps along!)

He he he, the above reminds me of the first time
Deedle & I went out to search for a letterbox. We
kept griping about how the clues were too vague, and
if WE had placed the box we'd be more specific, blah
blah blah. However, even though we never found the
box (turns out it might have already been missing
before our ruckus of resorting to looking in every
hole in every tree along the path), it just made us
want to find a box even more, and as much as we
complained about the clues we still thought, "But we
must have done something wrong..." We have learned to
appreciate and love the "awful-vague clues" now and
laugh when in our planted box logbooks people have
complained about how the clues needed to be more
specific. Ahh, maybe one day in the future they'll
get it, too. :)
