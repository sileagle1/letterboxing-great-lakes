From: Matthew & Renee (jedisquirrel@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-05-26 14:47:16 UTC
I figured that since everyone was getting a kick out of the Kimball
Library box that I'd recommend a couple of other fun virtual hunts
(for all of these rainy days, eh?).

The Sunshine series by CodyBCleo is also a virtual representation of
real boxes (ie: real stamp images!). It can be found at

www.geocities.com/codybcleo/Sunshine

and (certainly not to be forgotten) our very own SpringChick has a
series (we're still working on #4 ourselves...)

www.confessionsofdecember.com/lbna/virtual/moomahh.asp

Not only are these stamps wonderful to see, they are (I presume)
exclusive to this virtual hunt! I'm sure our host is much too modest
to plug her own boxes in such a manner, so let me do it- any of you
who have had the chance to hunt for (and find) a SpringChick
letterbox know the quality of both the hunt and the stamp at the
end!! These are certainly no exception! Moo-mahh!

Re: Virtual boxes

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-05-27 10:16:08 UTC
Thanks for your kind words Matt ;-) You are correct in your
assumption that the Moo-Mahh stamps are available only for
completing the 4 phases of the virtual hunt. There was a 5th stamp
image that went only to the person who finished first -- BeMa from
VA.

I had a lot of fun creating the virtual challenge -- it is a good
winter activity. I had ideas for a couple of others but I put them
on hold because it is really a lot of work to keep up with it as it
is so dependent on other people's web sites. I've had to change
clues and reroute visitors several times because the referenced
sites disappeared, moved or changed their site design/options. If I
do another one, it will be more dependent on information that can be
found in various places on the web, not on specific web sites.

Glad you had fun with it!

SpringChick
