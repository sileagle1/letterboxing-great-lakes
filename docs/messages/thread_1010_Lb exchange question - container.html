Lb exchange question - container

From: Kathi LK Krankoski (krank@ameritech.net) | Date: 2005-02-06 18:30:12 UTC-06:00
Will we be providing the container and everything, or just the stamp and
log? Not only would postage be less to not include a container, but when we
place it, it might be easier to fit our own container to the ultimate hiding
place and its environmental conditions (ie, super heavy-duty waterproof as
opposed to a more sheltered spot which could get away with a less-extreme
container).

--klk/FungusWoman

Re: [LbGLK] Lb exchange question - container

From: Deb McClurken (springchick1219@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-02-06 19:52:10 UTC-05:00
You have some valid points, but in an exchange I participated in about a
year ago we did it that way -- for pretty much the same reasons -- to save
on postage. People sent just stamps and log books and the recipients were
responsible for putting it in a container and planting it. It seemed like
that held some people up with getting the box planted -- having to get a
container that the parts would fit in. Seems like when the box is sent all
complete and ready to go, the process flows smoother. So in answer to your
question... you are sending a complete letterbox, stamp, log and
container -- any size (including micro if you wish) up to about 6" x 6"
maximum.

SpringChick
