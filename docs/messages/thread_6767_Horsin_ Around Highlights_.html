Well, it seems a new tradition has started, and no GLK event is complete without some sort of medical drama from me.........this time it was taking a header out on the trails...scraped and cut face, broken glasses, sprained pinkie, and this morning's bonus, a shiner! And once again, I have HowD Girl and Lampwick to thank for tending to my first aid needs...or maybe the combination of the three of us is a jinx? Nah...I'll take my chances...I have a great time boxing with them!

When I called Mr. FW to tell him about it, his response was a nonplussed "I'm not surprised." However, a few minutes later we heard ambulance sirens in the distance. He immediately called me to see if it was for me!

He had a good time acting as my social proxy at the shelter while I was out on the trails (he gave the potluck a definite thumbs up), and Hershey thoroughly enjoyed herself, reliving her puppyhood (She will be 13 on Tuesday!).

RE: [LbGLK] Horsin' Around Highlights!

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2010-10-17 20:51:39 UTC-05:00
Highlights:



Dinner w/a group of LB friends on Friday night and then a return visit to
the Thai restaurant with the Leapin 'Lizards on Saturday night. It's always
fun to do some evening socializing separate from the hubbub of the event.



Great weather and nice trails, especially since we were able to enjoy them
while chatting with BirdWoman, Gibson Girl, Pitties & Shorty!



Many interesting stamps, especially the "When is a Horse not a Horse?"
stamps.



A chance to meet some newer boxers, including SamWasHere who we have seen
logged into many boxes in our area. He seems like a very enthusiastic young
boxer with an interest in codes, which bodes well for some future
interesting plants as he gets older...



Delicious food and we really appreciated the talents of NamVet who got those
Usinger brats grilled perfectly.



Many thanks to Trail Rider, Nam Vet, Pastor's Wife and Steering Lives for
hosting a fun event!



Wisconsin Hiker & Martini Man

We were thrilled to attend our first event, and especially happy to meet Beetle, who has been a great resource for all of our questions. I do wish we had met more people, because we really enjoyed our conversations with those we did meet.
