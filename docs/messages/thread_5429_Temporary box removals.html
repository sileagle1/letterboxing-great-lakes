Yep, we had to keep a box overnight in South Carolina last month. It was a
drive-by and we stamped in while in the car. Before we could put it back,
another car pulled up and unloaded some serious camera gear to take photos
of Table Rock Mountain across the way. It was obvious that they would be
there for quite a while, and since we had plans to meet people at a certain
time, we had to leave & therefore took the box with us rather than try to
rehide it in plain sight of the photographers. However since it was early
evening, we didn't think there would be many other searchers that day and we
returned it early the next morning.

Wisconsin Hiker

P.S. I certainly wasn't suggesting that "Mystery Letterboxers" were bad or
irresponsible. All of them that visited my Demon's Dwelling box certainly
seemed to do a good job, even though they didn't contact me, so I'm fine
with that. As I said before, the box was in almost perfect condition even
though it is almost 3.5 years old. It's nice to know that it has brought
some pleasure to even more people than I realized.

However we DO really appreciate getting the notes from others to let us know
the status of the box and how they did on the hunt! Since we aren't able to
check on all of our boxes very often, those emails allow us to get more
frequent feedback. Thanks to all of you that do send notes after finding
our boxes!

I agree, as far as people who might have forgotten their stamp.

But sometimes, there's not a whole lot of choice. I've been in a position 4
times now where I had no choice but to take the box with me temporarily.
Granted that's 4 times over almost twice as many years but it does happen
where I'll find a box, take it away to someplace secluded to stamp in, and
then go to return the box only to find a veritable picnic taking place right
where I have to return the box. Once recently, in Oakbrook, IL on the
McDonald's campus, a love-in was taking place when we returned to the spot,
so we took the box home, waited a few hours, and then returned. We thought
we were posolutely, absitively, safe since it was Halloween. I mean, who
goes out into the woods on Halloween. Who goes out into the woods to *make
out* on Halloween?? Haven't they watched 'Blair Witch'????

Sometimes there's just no way to create a reliable distraction where I can
feel really sure the box was safe, and to me, the ultimate rule is to keep
the box safe. My sister once almost ended up having to take a box home.
She was out letterboxing on the N.I.U. campus and ended up spending
something like 5 hours there, waiting for the scene to be free enough so
that she could replace the box. She was just on the verge of having to take
the box home when the place cleared out sufficiently.

For that reason, the safety of the box, we don't like to stamp in anywhere
in the actual vicinity of the box. We tend to go far away. And sometimes,
when we've returned, conditions have unfortunately changed for the worse.
And it's always possible that someone else came along looking for the box,
didn't find it, and naturally didn't see us because we weren't right there
stamping in.........we were far away stamping in...........and might have
left, assuming the box is missing. I've been thinking of making up a little
business card that says something like "If you're looking for a particular
treasure, it hasn't gone missing, it's just being stamped into at the
moment. It shall return soon." The only trouble is, if someone waits
around for us to return, and we don't know they're boxers, we're not just
going to walk up with the box in hand, we're going to hide in the woods
until we see the coast is clear.

Actually, it's happened 5 times, but once was to my own box. I went to
check on the box, which was at the very tip of Boy Scout Island in Geneva,
IL, took it back to my car early in the morning to quietly see if any
maintenance was necessary, and lo and behold, when I went to replace the
box, it seemed like all the fishermen in the world had come out of the
woodwork to congregate at the tip of that island. I hadn't thought of the
fact that people who fish often start their fishing very early in the
morning. So that box got replaced but unfortunately rather rudely. Because
the fishermen just never showed any signs of leaving.

So finally, my Caveman was tired of waiting, decided to become a klutz for
the day, "tripped" over a tree stump near the shoreline, and fell into the
Fox River. Created quite a splash. Continued to flounder around out there
and made a ton of noise and splash while I snuck over and replaced the box
since he now had the attention of everyone on shore except me. The only
downside was that he then had a large bunch of fishermen more than a little
angry with him and he was soaking wet to boot and it was a 45 minute drive
home to dry clothes. But, yipee, I got the box replaced. And I let him
stop at Starbucks in Geneva so that was one very small thing that made his
day a wee bit better. :-)

I think frankly the reason I got out of the habit of contacting placers
after a find had to do with plain ol' discouragement. I'd say that in the
past, quite literally 95% of the boxers who found my boxes didn't contact me
after finding them. And among those 95% were all too often the same boxers
who I *had* contacted after finding *their* boxes. So it became
discouraging and I gave it up. It seemed like they didn't think it too much
trouble for others to contact them but that they were just too busy boxing
to bother doing it themselves. Some boxers even emailed me after I'd
contact them, telling me how happy and pleased they were to receive my email
and our story of finding their box and to keep those stories comin'. Yet
they couldn't be bothered to return the favor?

And I'm not at all saying you were one of them Wisconsin Hiker, because I
really don't remember if you were or not. But I do remember that several
"prominent" boxers in this area never returned the favor. And I felt hurt
and discouraged by that. So I simply stopped for the most part and now I
just go online and record it as a "find" so that the placer at least knows
that it's still there. I do always carry supplies with me so if a box needs
maintenance, I go ahead and do it. But I rarely take the time to contact
the placer and write anything of our experience.

And, like I said, I *don't* mean to cast aspersion on Wisconsin Hiker
because I don't remember at this point who the boxers were. I remember
seeing several prominent names who had posted in the past about wanting to
receive feedback and finders' stories, yet never *once* contacted me after
finding a box of mine. So I truly felt, why am I bothering?

We even had a nasty note once left in one of my sister's boxes by a boxer
who was prominent in boxing in the past and who had posted here about the
fact that finders shouldn't say anything at all in a logbook if they
couldn't say something nice. And then they leave a nasty note themselves in
a logbook???

With the housing market the way it is, it doesn't look like we'll be putting
our house on the market again & moving to Tennessee anytime soon so I might
place boxes out there again and see. I've missed having boxes out there but
we've been soooo unsure of when we might be too busy with moving. I can't
wait to retire and get outta Illinois.

Again, I don't mean to point this Wisconsin Hiker's way at all, but merely
to use WH's post as a jump point....................I think all boxers on
the list need to take stock of themselves and figure out if they're
following that good ol' Golden Rule of do unto others.
