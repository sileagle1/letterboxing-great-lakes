great day of letterboxing

From: tiptoetonto (tiptoetonto@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-10-29 21:42:54 UTC
To take advantage of the last, late light of daylight savings time,
we spent the whole day up in the Wadsworth IL/Kenosha WI area
letterboxing. Almost a perfect LBig day...except for the wind and
the normal Tiptoe & Tonto issues!

We would've had more time if..Tiptoe hadn't lost our logbook at a
box and Tonto had to go back for it...And, to prove that she isn't
the only forgetful one, we had to waste 30 more minutes to go back
to the restaurant where we ate lunch to fetch Tonto's jacket!

We were at the Jelly Belly factory where Tonto had stubornly gone to
the "tree" to look for the hidden box without the clue sheet while
Tiptoe stayed in the car. He had been looking for a very long time,
when she got annoyed and told him to, "just forget it!"
"Fine! he huffed. YOU get out and fight the wind and look in the
millions of roots for it.
Which she stubbornly did while he drove around the block. She had
just gotten out of the car and was muttering to herself (things not
appropriate for this audience!), when two people in a car pulled up
and rolled down their window with obvious smirks on their faces.
"Hey Tiptoe, are you looking for a box?" they said.
When she looked up she felt like AAA had sent out their best and
brightest emergency letterboxing recovery team...the Leapin'
Lizards! They got out of the car and literally walked over to the
correct spot and produced the box in a matter of seconds!
Oh happy day!

We found a possum either playing dead, or REALLY dead, inches away
from a box! That was a first!

We were mistaken for Carthage College parents (football fans) as we
stopped to sing the National anthem at their football game while
looking for Hiker Babe's stamp. The coolest part of this box was
stamping in on the beach while sitting on a "stone sofa" overlooking
Lake Michigan...the coolest stamping spot ever!

We would like to especially thank CinemaBoxers for some very fine
stamps and spots! Throw in boxes by Leapin' Lizards, Atom 118,
Hiker Babe, and adventureteam which all added up to us finding 21
out of 22 attempts and over ten miles exercise for our old legs!
Isn't letterboxing the best????
Tiptoe & Tonto
