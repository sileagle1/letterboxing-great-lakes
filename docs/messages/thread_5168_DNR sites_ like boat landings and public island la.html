DON'T CALL!!!!!

PLEASE don't bring up letterboxing with the DNR anymore than it is
already. The geocachers did us enough of a dis-service. They
contacted the DNR to set up a policy (even though the letterboxing
community told them not to, and definitely not to lump letterboxing in
with the geocaching) and the next thing you know they put restrictions
on geocaching AND letterboxing in WI. If you mention letterboxing to
the DNR, they are reminded to actively enforce their "policies" or
create new ones, much to the dismay of the rest of us. Please let
sleeping dogs lie! :-)

Technically, to place a box in a state park one needs to complete a
form and submit it to the park ranger. The form is set up for
geocaching, but letterboxers are supposed to use it. Since the policy
has been in place, only two parks that I know of have asked the
letterboxers with boxes on their property to fill out the form - one
was Rib Mountain and the other is in Door Cty I believe. I had no
problem with the ranger at Rib Mtn, although I had to move my box off
of protected land. I have not had to complete the form for any of my
other boxes in state parks, even though I know that the park rangers
are aware of the boxes being there (including High Cliffs where I am
currently camping at). I believe the same applies for the rest of DNR
land. Most letterboxes planted on DNR land are planted following
letterboxing etiquette, and if an issue with the DNR arises we
apologize afterwards and fix it (cf Mink River and Rib Mtn boxes). (As
a side note, this is pretty much what is happening with the MN state
parks too. I have never been asked to document my letterboxes there,
even though I know the rangers know my boxes are there.)

The best thing to do is to plant the box following the regulations
provided on LBNA. One should never plant a box so that it harms
sensitive areas, regardless of whether it is DNR land or not. If the
DNR land you are looking to hide a box on is a sensitive area, don't
plant a box there. The DNR has maps of their sensitive areas online I
believe. Letterboxes are not litter, especially if they cannot be
seen, as a recent post on AQ attests to. If people regularly walk
where you intend to plant a box, it must not be a sensitive area - at
least not near the trail (just don't go off trail much).

If one is new to planting boxes, it is probably a better idea to plant
some boxes in a local park or other "safe" areas first and wait (at
least a year) to see how well they hold up, before attempting to plant
a box that may be more problematic. In other words, gain experience
in planting boxes before attempting harder plants.

Just my 15 cents (adjusted for inflation)...
Dragon
