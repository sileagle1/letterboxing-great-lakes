From: munchkinstamper (vracker@michigan.gov) | Date: 2004-04-02 21:09:19 UTC
Hello fellow Letterboxers! I'm Munchkin Stamper from the Lansing
area. My Sister and I were introduced to Letterboxing while
attending a Rubber Stamp Camp Retreat weekend 3 years ago. Two of
the stampers that planted those boxes for that weekend have turned
out to be real "kindred spirits" to both of us (and our mom and
other sister). Together we planted a LB series at a Stamp Retreat
that was great fun! The theme was Wizard of Oz. Since then, none
of us travel anywhere without our journals and clues. Last fall we
planted a 7 box series called Aiden Lair just south of Jackson. I
have boxes in East Lansing, Clare and North Carolina. I'm going
back to NC in May to plant more at Chimney Rock (where they
filmed "Last of the Mohican's"), Lake Lure (where they filmed Dirty
Dancing), and maybe some at some beautiful waterfall locations.

We attended the Gathering that Spring Chick held. So far it's our
one and only, it was very enjoyable! and cold! Can someone share
where the next one might be location wise? I guess I thought it
would be Michigan again but it doesn't sound like it.

Sorry to ramble, it must be the Friday let-me-outside crazy person
coming out!

Thanks Deb for getting this site up and running.

From: LeapinLizards (emptyenergy1@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-04-04 06:12:54 UTC
Hi! We're John and Sara, otherwise known as Leapin' Lizards.

It was great to meet those who made it to the Wisconsin gathering and we
hope to meet more fellow letterboxers at the Great Lakes gathering this
summer. We thought we would take a minute to introduce ourselves and
share how we got into this crazy thing called letterboxing. . .

Sara: I have created a monster! About a year and a half ago, I came across a
blurb about letterboxing in an outdoor travel magazine. So I asked John what
he thought and he said...

John: Cool! Let's try it!

Sara: We started letterboxing in January 2003 and since then we've seen lots
and lots of great places. Places which I'd personally love to stay and visit the
non-letterboxing points of interest, as well, to which John always assures me,
"We'll come back." But as time goes by, and we have yet to return, I know it's
just a line . . . so typical of a guy.

John: Well, if Sara's legs weren't so short, we'd get to the boxes faster and
have time to visit the other places.

Sara: Like in Michigan! So many cute towns and shops!

John: So many letterboxes! The makers of Advil really need to thank Spring
Chick for the amount of Advil we purchased during our trip.

Sara: But it was worth every sand dune we had to climb! We must have
walked 70 miles during those five days.

John: But really, letterboxing has been a great way for us to spend time
together, take walks together....

Sara: I'm still convinced he only holds my hand to drag me along...

John: ...and teach us patience....like waiting for Sara.

Sara: . . . and gain endurance, like when John makes me walk in sub-zero
temperatures into stright-line winds off of Lake Michigan.

John: And criminal law, like when were stopped by Missouri police for a
random drug check of our vehicle while driving home from a letterboxing trip.
"Really, officer we're just looking for boxes with stamps in them. " Thank
goodness we had the pictures to prove it!

Sara: We recently marked our 200th find (and retired our first signature
stamp). Currently, we've planted 16 letterboxes in 6 states and released 5
hitchhikers.

John: We have several series ready to be planted, too...

Sara: Yes, he has close to 100 boxes ready to be planted. He tells me that
he has carved all of the stamps, but I'm convinced he has his art students
carving them...Sometimes when he's sleeping, he yell's out, "You call that a
stamp! I've seen better stamps carved out of potatoes by kindergartners!"

John: I would never let that happen in my sweatshop, er, classroom.

Sara: Like I said, I've created a monster. I've now got him on a 12-step
program.

John: Yes, I'm on step 10 at 340 degrees.


Happy boxing--

Leapin' Lizards :-)

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-05-28 05:54:49 UTC-04:00
Welcome to the group Kaycee! I don't know if there is a regional list that
covers the eastern Great Lakes more specifically, but you are welcome to
stay here -- we don't really have any boundaries. I think you will find
that a lot of the folks here are concentrated in the MI, WI, IL area but
letterboxing tends to take off in pockets with wide open spaces of
letterboxing-void in between. The regional groups have popped up around the
concentrations of boxers, and those folks in between are free to join up
with any of them.

Although some of the posts here would pertain specifically to boxes in
states quite a distance from you, I think you will find that in general the
topics are not so specific to locale and are of interest to you.

Sounds like you are off to a great start!

SpringChick
