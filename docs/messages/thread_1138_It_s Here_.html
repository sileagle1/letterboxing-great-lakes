From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-04-06 14:09:33 UTC

Hmmm, I didn't know there was a link at Pete and Wanda's site -- I
will have to check that out.

The clues are not a secret, but I have always intentionally kept it
rather low profile since by nature the box contains HH, and the last
thing I want is for it to be vandalized and other people's stamps to
disappear -- now if I could just control the critter curiosity ;-)
Probably because of this it has seen low volume turnover -- I think
there were 4 HH to come and go between last spring and September when
I again pulled it for a new log book. Although considering the other
boxes in that park didn't see many more than that, maybe that isn't
so bad.

The actual clues, like my other boxes, are on my web site and have
been on the letterboxing.org site off and on as the box has flip-
flopped between active and inactive.

I have the new log book done, but haven't had a chance to put it back
out yet. I was hoping to get over to that park this weekend to do
box maintenance and bring it with me then -- although that won't help
you for Spring Break ideas.

One thing I have concluded about my letterboxing during the past
year -- I spent way too much time doing box maintenance. My time is
so much more limited now than when I first began boxing (and wasn't
working full-time and had no grandkids and pending weddings). During
the past year I have spent most available letterboxing time tending
to boxes and most carving time replacing box stamps and log books.
It was more fun when I was spending all my time planting new boxes
and hunting other people's boxes. I've considered retiring a bunch
of boxes, but have a hard time choosing because I look at the list
and think, "yes, but people really liked that box..." I'm not sure
what the solution is, but something has to give...

SpringChick

I've found this happen to me as well. The bonus I have is that I
have a week of spring break to take care of most of my boxes, then I
can spend the rest of the time hidding and finding. I think the
solution is to rely on other letterboxers to:
1) inform you that a box was found or possibly missing
2) perform any maintenance that may be needed and to let you know.

If we all helped each other out with this (and the new tools on the
LbNA site will help) all of our maintenance work would be
efficiently handled. Kudos to WI Hiker and Martini Man (and others)
who do this regularly and have helped me out a lot! Thanks!
