From: timbertoes33 (toomey@genevaonline.com) | Date: 2004-07-08 14:52:14 UTC
Thanks for your responses regarding my post about asking permission
to place boxes. Since I want to be a good example to my kids, I've
decided to err on the side of asking permission when in doubt, yet
keeping in mind your suggestions to not stir the pot. You'll be
pleased to hear this: this morning I contacted the Public Works
department that handles my county's parks to ask permission to place
boxes in a particular park. The gentleman I spoke with asked lots of
questions about letterboxing in general, thought it sounded
interesting and even suggested a park he thought would be a good
candidate for a box! After getting the website address from me, he
gave me blanket permission to place boxes in any of the parks under
his jurisdiction. Fortunately, I made the call in the presence of my
kids and praised the man's openmindedness to them after hanging up.
What a great experience.
