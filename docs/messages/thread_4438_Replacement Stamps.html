I don't think there's any real "protocol" to consider -- it's basically what
you're comfortable with doing and want to do.

Personally, since the original is a store-bought stamp and the new one is
hand-carved, I would just replace it in the box. Plus, I would make a note
in the clues so that anyone who had been there already and wanted to go back
for the hand-carved image could do so. Not that they would be likely to
read the clues again, since they've already found the box, but who knows?
You could make a little post about it on the list in case folks who have
already found the box read the list.

If your original stamp was a hand-carved stamp and then you had carved what
you thought was a *better* stamp, that would be different -- in my opinion.
In that case, if it was me, I would leave the original stamp in the box
because many times finders really like to see the beginning carvings of
placers.

But, in the end, it's all up to you how you want to do it.
