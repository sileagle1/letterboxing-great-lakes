I Actually Met Another Letterboxer on the Trail!

From: Jenny (enigma3742@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-07-05 15:41:06 UTC
This has never happened to me before so I just had to write about it.

I was in Freeport, IL this weekend hunting for River Rat and Nature
Nerd's Krape Park Treasure Hunt Series (what a beautiful park!) and
as I'm walking down some stone steps I ran into another letterboxer
that already had the Waterfall box out! I never thought I'd get to do
an exchange that didn't happen at a gathering. He introduced himself
as Wwwaverider and we went off to find the next box, Merry-Go-Round,
together. Sadly, we ran out of luck on that one. It was still a
beautiful day for boxing, though. I did all of those boxes completely
out of order because I got turned around when coming into the park, so
I followed Wwaverider to the final two boxes in the series and
entertained myself watching him work his way through the clues as I
had done earlier in the day. Since we found the boxes in different
order, we got to share First Finder honors as well :).

So how often has this happened to others?

It happens (or rather *almost* happens) to me and Flying Dragon more often that I would have thought considering the lack of local boxers. I've only met other letterboxers on the trail once, and it was actually
in the parking lot. We were looking for the correct trail head (we only
saw one sign and it was the wrong trail) and they pointed us in the right
direction. A weird silence ensued and then they asked if we were
letterboxers. When we exchanged stamps, we realised we had been right
behind them all afternoon! We commiserated about the more impossible
clues for the park and passed along clues for boxes in a nearby park that
they hadn't known about and then went upon our merry way.

Ever since that, though, when we've been unable to find a box and certain
that we are in the right place, I wonder if we aren't just two steps
behind some one and they have the box in their hot little hands at that
moment...

Katy

Actually, that's how Deedle & I encountered our first
(and so far only) exchange on the trail. We'd gone to
find the Lake Medina box here in Ohio one Saturday
morning, and as we were sitting around the lake
stamping in and making sure that none of the dog
walkers were watching us, a woman with a small bag
walked into view. She also had a sheet of paper in
her hand, and Deedle started whispering, "She's a
letterboxer!" Well, sure enough, after a few minutes
of watching her, she was walking and pausing at all
the spots the clues told you to, so just before she
was ready to go into the woods to find the box that
was still in my lap, we walked over and said, "Excuse
us, are you looking for this?" and held out the box.

If you ever spot Deedle & me on the trail, there
should be no mistaking that we're letterboxers...I've
got an LbNA patch on our bagpack, and Deedle has an
LbNA logo medallion on his walking stick.

Doodle Bug.
