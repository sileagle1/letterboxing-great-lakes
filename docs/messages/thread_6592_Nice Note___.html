I thought I'd share this email I received yesterday. It's very nice when people take the time to do a little research if they find something unexpected, and especially nice when they send a "Contact the Placer" email! (It turns out the box they found was "Mom's Homemade Pie". The stamp was carved by TJ Mich and we won the stamp in the Cakewalk activity at the "MI Fall Gathering: A County Fair!")

Wisconsin Hiker

========================

Hi, My family went for a walk on Mother's day today. As we took a snack break under a nice shady tree, my daughter poked around like 6 year olds do and said "I found a treasure!" She pulled a camo-taped box out of a tree crotch. We were very surprised as we have never letterboxed before. Turns out she just happened to pack a little notebook and pencil in her backpack and could sign in. I'm not completely sure this is the exact box you are refering to in this clue because the clues imply a rock pile and this was in a tree crotch, but I recognize the "Wisconsin Hiker" from a green card we saw in there. But of course we did not know anything about clues to find the box - it was an accident! Anyway, everything looked to be pretty safe and dry and this letterboxing stuff looks pretty cool and fun for families. I think we will give it a try!
