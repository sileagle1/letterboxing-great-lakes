Great Lakes Gathering... Going Forward

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2009-06-05 10:51:33 UTC
Another successful event behind us, it is time to begin looking forward to our next annual Great Lakes event, at least to get the basics nailed down so a venue can be reserved and people can plan ahead.

First I would like to take the opportunity to thank Speedsquare and her helpers, as well as everyone who has hosted the GLG Gathering over the past 6 years. Hosting a gathering is a lot of planning and hard work and each one of you has done an awesome job! Each year the GLG has been thoughtfully planned and has provided an exciting opportunity for Great Lakes letterboxers to come together for a day/weekend in celebration of this hobby we enjoy. The event has come a long way since our first little get-together in Michigan 7 years ago!

Having come full circle, with all of the states in the region hosting the event, we are at a crossroads and before moving ahead, I would like some feedback and discussion with the intent of making the annual event for our region continue to be one of the premier letterbox gatherings in the country. I'm hoping to see some good discussion over the course of the next week or so and then if there is anything requiring a decision, I will put up a poll for voting.

A few things I'd like to throw out and see ideas on...

- As letterboxing grows in popularity within the region, we've seen the annual GLG gathering become larger and larger, to the point where it is becoming more difficult to acquire a venue large enough to accommodate the group. Most states already have their somewhat annual state get-together, as well as many smaller events throughout the year. Is the continuation of the annual regional event desirable and realistic or have we gotten to the point where we have outgrown it?

- I realize the timing of the event makes it difficult for some people to attend. With a group this size, there is never going to be a time of year that is good for everyone, and I do think it is important to establish a date and stick with it so people can plan ahead. That being said, as we move into a new cycle, I am interested in what the group thinks about the possibility of moving the gathering to another time of year to hopefully make it accessible for some of the folks who have not been able to participate in previous years. The caveat would be that if the event is moved, the new date would remain consistent throughout the next 6-year rotation.

- A plan for choosing location moving forward... two options are 1) open it back up for bids from any state or 2) use the rotation that has been established and start again at the beginning (i.e. Illinois would host in 2010, then Michigan, Wisconsin, Ohio, Minnesota, Indiana...)

- Any general feedback about what works really well and what could be improved to make the event even better!

SpringChick

Re: Great Lakes Gathering... Going Forward

From: speedsquare_lbxr (ruhlette@yahoo.com) | Date: 2009-06-06 01:44:37 UTC
Before much more is proposed and discussed about the next and future Great Lakes gatherings I have been composing something in my head this past week. I'm not sure I will cover everything in this post, but I need to get my thoughts down on paper before time passes.

First of all, thank you all for your kind sentiments about the event at Pokagon. As some of you know I had the notion of hosting this group event for a few years. It wasn't feasible when we had two mortgages in cities 60 miles apart, and to find a venue that would accommodate an ever-increasing number of attendees wasn't easy. Indiana State Parks have online reservations and reservations can be made only 1 year in advance. I missed the opportunity in 2008, so on May 30, 2008 I reserved the Apple Orchard shelter only after the CCC shelter (#1 choice) had been reserved by 7:30AM! I didn't know on that date if any letterboxers were working on hosting, but I took the risk.

Pokagon was the obvious choice for location, trails and amenities. I also enjoy Turkey Run, Brown County and McCormick's Creek for the scenery. They would have been more difficult for travel. They are also "southern" if you consider temperatures at this time of year. I find Central Indiana is on average 7-10 degrees warmer than Chicago and Wisconsin. As it happened the temperatures were very comfortable at the shelter and on the trails. A blessing.

The size of this gathering definitely was manageable. In the beginning I was very concerned about overflow and party crashers. Sometime in December the sign-ups had met the shelter capacity of 150. It would be my opinion that some did not attend for economic reasons. The day before I left (Thursday) there were about 128 sign-ups at Atlas Quest. When I returned home Sunday night, the sign-ups fell to a number around 110. We had a few "new folks" that were not signed up anywhere, and a few no-shows. If this number remains constant, we will find it easier to procure venues. I do like having this gathering as opposed to splitting up and having individual states hosting 6 annual events. Some states (at least, my neighbor) already have events on a more frequent basis.

I had hoped to get more help from Indiana letterboxers I know. Some have moved out-of-state, some are no longer active, and are leading busier lives. I worked on details and did my best to keep costs low and affordable for all. I do not know what the previous events' budgets were, but my target was not to exceed $300 or $2 per attendee (150 max). I have to mail off the event history book after I add pictures and event information (still undone), but to-date I expended about $250, and free will donations were just shy of $216. These numbers are rough I do have an itemized listing should anyone want to know more detail. I am not complaining about my out-of-pocket expense because I should have been more aware what to expect. I've been very generous when donating to past events. So from a group perspective I think a little discussion about costs and expectations should be addressed. Asking for money is not comfortable for me, but future organizers may benefit from a frank discussion.


I truly appreciate Pale Puppy Posse and her organizational skills in taking care of the food. I do not enjoy that aspect of large parties. Had it been up to me, I would have headed to the Inn or brown-bagged it. I enjoyed Doglvrs' roll ups, Tiptoe's tortellini salad, FungusWoman's Nuts4U, and 3 Oreo cookies which took about 3 hours to finish.

I hoped I had planned the kind of event you wanted to attend. After attending 2 previous Great Lakes events, I experienced first hand the fun and chaos. Too many boxes, too little time? Or too few boxes, bored letterboxers? How much do I need to find for the attendees to do before and after the event? Too much scheduled, no time for socializing? All of these things were considered and once I realized I wanted to have the least complicated event, everything else seem to fall into place from picking a theme to allowing folks to come and go without any time demands. My goal was to minimize the stress for you, as well as me. A nice escape from reality.

As to the set date for the annual Great Lakes event I like this weekend and can make it work. Right now, May is a super busy month for my household. Mother's Day weekend or Memorial weekend is when our family opens up the lake house dock, boats, clean the gutters, grounds, etc. Graduation always seems to fall the night before the letterboxing event. If the group consensus is "change" I definitely support the caveat. SpringChick's comment that this event becomes one of the premier events for the larger LbNA community would suggest we are to encourage and invite boxers outside our region, and changing the date may not be as attractive. Perhaps we have already set a tradition one that others have come to plan years in advance? Who knows?

Respectfully,
~speedsquare

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2009-06-08 19:20:20 UTC
1. We'd DEFINITELY like to see the annual regional event continue! As others have noted, it is one event that people from many different states will make an effort to attend. We've really enjoyed attending them for the last 6 years (only missed the first).

2. We don't care what weekend or month it is, and also don't care if it is held on a consistent date. We'd personally advocate one of the 3-day holiday weekends (Memorial Day, 4th of July, or Labor Day) since that allows more time for travel. However we realize that some people may have extended family commitments on these holiday weekends.

3. We'd like to see the event kept in the same state rotation as originally established (ignoring the "extra" year that MI hosted).

4. As Speedsquare noted, there are costs involved with hosting the event. We've hosted 2 larger events in the past and were very pleased that each time the attendee contributions to the DONATIONS box were more than adequate to cover the outright expenses for the event. I shy away from setting a "per person" suggestion, since it may penalize families and I also realize that people have varying abilities to contribute. Perhaps suggesting a range for a group or family to cover costs and/or identifying the major costs that need to be covered would reduce the likelihood of falling short. One way to save costs is to keep the boxes in an area as permanent plants, rather than sending them back in the mail. For the events we hosted, we luckily had very few boxes to return. Another suggestion for keeping host costs down is to ask attendees to bring other things instead of a food item (such as nametags, etc.)

We're looking forward to attending more events later this year!

Wisconsin Hiker & Martini Man
