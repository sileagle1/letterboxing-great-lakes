TOTW - Historical boxes

From: azobox (jparkerg@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-04-07 11:37:43 UTC
All the talk about historical boxes and preservation of some classics
has caused me to rethink some of my plans about retiring/replacing
some of mine.

I have been swayed and convinced by friends here on the Great Lakes
list, including TeamRabbit, TJMich, JBBooks, and Tiptoe & Tonto, among
others.

In a couple of cases I've decided to leave the boxes, but to move them
slightly within their current parks. This is in line with ongoing
plans to shift some clues out of the mainstream LbNA site and onto
less public ones. So what that might mean is that a box still exists,
but its clues are no longer quite as public.
I do intend to retain/maintain some boxes which had fallen on hard
times, especially if they are `historically important', at least in my
neck of the woods or maybe just to me.

For example, "First in the `Zoo" was the first letterbox in Kalamazoo
(county or city). It was first placed in 2002. I have recently moved
and re-clued it to a safer location. Those clues will likely be
available at LbNA and I will strive to keep it current.
On the other hand, I have just moved the Breakfast Elves to new spots
(cereal bowls?!) within their current park. The old clues will not
work anymore. The new clues will be placed elsewhere. (Perhaps hiding
where Snap, Crackle & Pop can be with their other cereal pals?).

I am considering replacing a very popular box on the (City of) Portage
Bicentennial Trail that was retied a couple of years ago. I'll likely
leave those clues at LbNA.

I note that for the most part, boxes placed for the Michigan Fall
Gatherings have retained their less-public clue status and, as a
result, probably their safety, too.

It was clear yesterday as I revisited a number of more mature boxes,
that the ones with accessible clues (LbNA) have many more and
geographically diverse visitors. Out-of-staters hit those more public
boxes for sure.

As has been noted here by others, maintenance and upkeep are a
continuing challenge.
