From: cdem_ltrbox (wp4@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2007-05-10 04:57:32 UTC
You know you're a letterboxing geek when...............



..........you've put together a binder with all the clues and
logbooks to the boxes you've placed.

We've placed some letterboxes (not as many as some, but a decent
number) and have some filled-up logbooks that we removed from the
boxes. THose logbooks were scattered around the house in various
locations, and were just asking to get misplaced or (horrors)
recycled during a cleaning frenzy. So I decided to put a binder
together. For each letterbox (or series) that we've placed, I
printed out a copy of the clues and put a copy of the stamped image
on the clues. Then I put each set of clues in one of those plastic
sheet protectors that fit into a binder. Then I slipped the filled-
up logbooks into that plastic gizmo along with the clues. Now I can
easily look at things whenever I want--it is nice to see everyone's
stamps and comments. I have the binder on the shelf with my
scrapbooks (which is another addiction--I mean hobby!)

BirdWoman/Waltzing Pigs

Re: [LbGLK] Re: TOTW: You know you're a letterboxing geek when...

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2007-05-10 18:37:23 UTC-04:00
I have several maps -- a Michigan map, a detail map of just West Michigan (my home area), a detail map of the U.P., a map of the greater Chicago area, the greater Detroit area and then several small city maps -- Kalamazoo, Lansing, Ann Arbor, Flint. It is really cool to see the maps with all the pins on, but it is very difficult to keep up with the "boxes yet to find," especially in areas where there are many boxes. As my maps become more saturated with plants and find (different color pins for mystery boxes), I have been considering redoing them and not marking the boxes yet to find with pins. I have also considered taking down some of the smaller city maps to make room for a U.S.map.

SpringChick
