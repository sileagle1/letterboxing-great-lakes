This past summer I did a workshop at camp for 12 girls, ranging in age from 9-16. The workshop was only 5 days, 1 hour each day. Most were totally unaware of what letterboxing was before they signed up, although two of them had been letterboxing before, one just a single time with a friend's family and the other letterboxes quite regularly with her grandmother.

I had made up and planted two letterboxes before-hand. One letterbox was for the compass reading exercise so the clues were basically a series of compass directions, including triangulation. The second letterbox was a traditional "go on a hike on the trail" letterbox.

I broke up the time as follows:

Day 1 - overview of letterboxing and stamp carving -- hand out erasers and each draw a basic image to carve (on paper with pencil and then tip over and rub onto eraser with fingernail)
Day 2 - finish drawing if needed and carve stamps
Day 3 - finish up stamps and decorate a logbook (we did not make them from scratch as there was not time, just decorated pre-purchased books)
Day 4 - learn how to use a compass and find a letterbox using a compass reading exercise
Day 5 - put it all together and go out on the trail to find a letterbox

The girls had a great time and are asking for an "advanced class" in 2009 where they just get to letterbox the whole time using different kinds of clues. I haven't given much thought to the 2009 class yet, but will for sure be doing something as it was a very popular interest group. I need to figure out a way to keep the hunt fun for everyone, not just the couple of kids out in front of the pack. Perhaps a couple versions of the clue and break the group up into teams, each with a different set of clues leading to the same spot. I would also like to incorporate making logbooks, although have been tossing around the idea of a separate interest group for beginning bookbinding, just not sure if I can dedicate 2 hours each day to teaching interest groups (not that I wouldn't love to spend the entire day teaching interest groups... but duty calls elsewhere).
