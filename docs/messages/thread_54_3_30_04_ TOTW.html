From: Richard Hesse (rhesse34@hotmail.com) | Date: 2004-03-30 09:54:48 UTC-06:00
Deb,
Glad to be part of the group. Here's some "bag" thoughts.
Our "bag" is a smallish, canvas shoulder bag (Musette?) of probable war
surplus origin: about 9"x7"x3". In it we keep the usual logbook, a good
compass, stamps, inkpads, pens, pencils, and clues. We also have a set of
"Brush Markers" for the stamps that require multiple colors, a 9"x7"x1/4"
piece of Masonite that works well as a small lap table, a wad of paper
towels for drying out the LBs that have been "moisture compromised", some
extra zip bags should they be needed, and some alcohol wipes (unscented!) to
clean off messy stamps. And, lest I forget, an LbNA patch on the cover
flap.
That said, we've been thinking of a somewhat different approach. I'm a
forester and have a "Filson Cruiser Vest". It's heavy cloth, has upper,
front pockets for a compass and pencils, two lower, front snap pockets (for
stamps? inkpads?), two large, inside pockets (for logbook and clues?), and a
pocket that covers that entire back for all the other things plus lunch,
camera, small dog, etc. It would probably work really well. The down side
is that while I've had mine for many years, news ones cost around $60, but a
fishing or photography vest would probably work well too.
Hope this helps.
Merlin and Ardea

From: Anne S. (aine_42@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-03-30 16:45:02 UTC
I've got a fairly simple pack, but it serves me well. It's a canvas
messenger bag. Contents include: stamp, stamp pad, brush
markers, my journal (which has very handy aluminum covers --
they make for a nice hard surface to stamp on), clues, a pen or
two, compass, Leatherman mini, mini Maglite, gardening gloves
(for the hunt -- like I said before, nature and I are not the best
of friends) and a couple little packets of hand wipes, since I
always seem to get ink on myself.

The Leatherman and Maglite were sort of afterthoughts. They
usually reside in one of the pockets of my jacket, but since we're
moving into warmer weather (and hence, fewer pockets or no
jacket), I moved them to the bag. The flashlight has proven
invaluable in locating letterboxes that have fallen a little farther
into tree hollows than they're supposed to be.

I like the idea of carrying extra baggies. I may have to keep a
couple in the bag. Thanks for the tip!

~anne

From: Deb (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-03-30 17:32:16 UTC
For the first year I used a comfy old khaki backpack that was
structureless -- one big pocket with a small front pocket on the
outside. I liked it because I had had it for a long time (i.e.
didn't have to buy it), it washed up beautifully and it was as big or
little as the stuff you put in it. Unfortunately, I am also a pack
rat and as the assortment of stuff inside the pack grew, it became
harder and harder to find what I was looking for since it was all
jumbled in the same place together.

Last summer I bought a real backpack with several pockets. The bad
thing is that it is bulkier and has more structure to it so it feels
clumsy even when there is not a lot in it.

There are two large partitions -- the back one is the "dirty
pocket." In it I carry and boxes/HH that I will be placing, boxes/HH
that I pick up, a couple of spare plastic grocery bags for trash or
whatever and extra supplies (various size baggies, letterboxing info
sheets, a sheet of "this is a letterbox" labels, a sheet of "this is
a HH" labels and a sheet of labels with my contact info on).
Sometimes I throw in an extra container if there is not already too
much stuff in there -- otherwise there are usually a couple in the
vehicle just in case.

The other large partition is the "clean pocket." In it I carry
clues, log book, a small spiral notebook for jotting clues and notes,
our stamps, a variety of ink pads, unscented wipes and a roll of TP.
This pocket also has a handy plastic sleeve that is just the exact
size for my compass.

There is a medium sized pocket on the front in which I keep a small
first aid kit, gloves, a small flashlight, matches, a long-handled
wooden spoon (works great for digging in sand*) and sometimes extra
socks if it is wet out.

On the outside there are also two elastic-top net pockets on the
sides -- one carries a bottle of water and the other my cell phone,
and a small slot pocket on the front that nicely holds a GPS -- no
not for geocaching, but I do have several of my box locations marked
as waypoints in the GPS to make it easier to locate them from the
lake when we are out on the boat.

I also always have my camera bag with me, although usually just the
small one when letterboxing.

* about the digging... I know you are not supposed to dig when
letterboxing, but placing boxes in the sand dunes of West Michigan is
unique. What was a totally open root cavity when you placed the box
may be under 2 foot of sand when you come back -- keep that in mind
as you search for boxes in sandy areas also. And digging in the sand
is not really harmful to the environment -- at least not the amount
of sand me and my wooden spoon can displace in an afternoon.

Oh, Deb,
Your comment about the camera...
I never used to carry one (I'm not much into taking pictures) BUT,
I've discovered that it's the perfect accessory to create the
impression that you're doing something (other than letterboxing).
I've been openly carrying a camera during boxing excursions for the
past few weeks and find it the perfect justification for just
standing there and looking around curiously -- as letterboxers do --
when anyone else is in sight.
I like the inclusion of tools in the pack -- "just in case".
The other thing I didn't mention earlier is that we carry bright,
hunter-orange vests in our pack during the fall. I first saw
letterboxers in NH wearing them during hunting season and it made
good sense.
Yeah, be safe.

From: Duncan53211 (duncan53211@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-03-31 01:28:54 UTC
My bag is a medium sized camera bag. It is just large enough to
hold my log book, inking pens, and stamp, and leaves room for other
things I may add from time to time, like HHs, wiping towels, my
camera, extra baggies, etc. My compass fits nicely in a little
front velcro pocket, which is easy to get at when needed. Usually I
leave the "bigger" items or "not often used" items in the car. If I
am going on a "plant", the bag is big enough to hold the box too!

As a side note, I often put the clues in one of those plastic "sheet
protectors". I learned that one real quick after letterboxing in
the snow and rain.

I also second the "take a camera" idea for the same reasons Azobox
mentioned. In fact, for one of my boxes it is recommended. If you
get to the location of the box, which is on the edge of some very
public "private" property, they don't bother you if you look like
you're taking pictures. They don't like "tresspassers" looking at
their "property" but are okay with people that are "enthusiastic"
about their "property" and leave them alone. I always found this
double standard interesting.

Now for a question. I've been wanting to get my pack down to just
logbook, stamp, and ink pad into a tightly fit pack, so I can wear
the pack while running on the trails to a letterbox. The camera bag
I use now flops around too much when I run. Any runners out there
that have a solution? (Fanny packs flop around too.) Thanks!
