paces and steps?

From: Carol Osada (cjosada@hotmail.com) | Date: 2009-11-18 22:11:53 UTC-06:00

Yes, the dictionary uses the term 'pace' and 'step' interchangeably. But orienteering clubs define a pace as every left/right combination. I read another definition in a Girl Scout outdoor training manual and it states that a pace is approximately 28-30 inches in length; that would be a left/right step combination...or one really HUGE step. LOL!



If a Lb clue includes steps and paces, it's probably best to state your personal definition in the clue.



Pace counting
Counting the number of paces you take to estimate the distance you travel. Paces usually are counted one for every left/right combination. You need to determine your standard pace for various conditions (at least one for walking and one for running) on a fixed course of known length.
