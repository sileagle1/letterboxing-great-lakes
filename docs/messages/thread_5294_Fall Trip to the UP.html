From: (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2008-09-18 14:07:06 UTC
We just returned from a week in the Porkies. We found all 10 of the boxes
planted by the park -- they are in a nice variety of places within the park and
all easy hikes. I have a couple of boxes in the Porcupine Mts. that are a bit
longer hikes, but exceptionally scenic so worth the time and distance.

We camp so I can't recommend a place to stay based on personal experience,
although we did eat at Konteka in White Pine one day (one of the few places in
the area with wireless Internet access) -- the food, the view and the service
were wonderful -- it seems like a really nice place. The other place somebody
had mentioned the Americ-Inn is always busy (judging by the number of cars in
the lot), but we were not real impressed with the restaurant there.

One of the park letterboxes is along the Presque Isle River at the west end of
the park. Even though this one is a little farther away, don't skip it. The
Presque Isle River area is one of the prettiest areas of the park -- there are
several waterfalls and rapids along the river here within the stretch of a mile.

Bond Falls, which is one of the most scenic falls in the U.P., is located near
Paulding which is within easy driving distance from the Porkies, and has a
couple of letterboxes. Just down the road from there is a new box planted by
Turtle's Mom at Imp Lake which was a really nice trail. Nearby is Agate Falls,
also a beautiful waterfall, but no letterbox there. Heading west, somebody had
mentioned Superior Falls, which has an amazing canyon wall along the mouth of
the river -- I have a letterbox there and it sounds like there may be a couple
others available shortly. I also have a box on Lake Gogebic, which could easily
be a stop on the way to/from Bond Falls.

If you head into Ontonagan (the closest city to the Porkies with a real grocery
store), make time to eat at Roxey's Lounge... it is kind of a dive bar, but
they have the best burgers in the world!

You should see some great fall color. We usually go at the end of
September/beginning of October and hit the color right on, although we have run
into snow a couple of times also. This year we went earlier than usual because
of the event they were having at the park, but I much prefer the fall color
season!

From: Jean (papeseed30@wildmail.com) | Date: 2008-09-18 20:19:58 UTC
We spent nearly a week in the U.P. this summer. A great hotel was
the Super 8 in Houghton. I was astounded! It's right on Portage
Lake and for $75/night we got the Lake side of the hotel with a
GORGEOUS view!

We also stayed at the Laurium Manor Inn B & B in Laurium. Our first
time at a B & B -- never thought we'd be able to afford one. But our
room was just under $100.

We had a lot of "couldn't finds" for letterboxing on our trip...that
was probably our error. But a few of our favorites were "Shanty Boy"
near Crystal Falls, "Bear Lake" near McClain State Park, and "Flying
Fish" at the Houghton Co. Historical Museum. We also had a great
time touring the Ghost Town of Central. We will definitely be hiding
a lb there if and when we go that way again.

Have a great trip! We love the U.P.! --Papeseed

We just returned from our trip and had a wonderful time. Thanks to
everyone who gave us suggestions! We spent our time in the Porkies
and the Keweenaw Peninsula area. The fall colors were incredible! One
of the great things about letterboxing is that people usually hide
boxes in interesting places. So if there's a box there, the area is
probably worth visiting. Plus, we learned a lot of background
information about the U.P. just from reading clues! If you want to
experience the highlights of the Porcupine Mountains, all you have to
do is find the series of letterboxes planted there by the park
rangers. (I still can't believe RANGERS actually PLANTED letterboxes!)
