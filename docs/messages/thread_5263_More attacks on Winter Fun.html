More attacks on Winter Fun

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2008-08-25 04:59:53 UTC
Well, my Winter Fun series continues to have bad luck.

Two of the 4 boxes had previously disappeared, one definitely due to
animals. Today we checked on the remaining 2 and animals had also
been at work. We have some very aggresive critters in Waukesha
County! Last week we were in New England and we found a lot of boxes
that were 4 years old and looked like new. This is definitely NOT
the case around here!

The Winter Fun series boxes were planted in JAN 2005 and have seen
hard times. The blue rubber edges on the Rubbermaid containers seem
to be especially enticing to WI critters (but not Connecticut
critters).

Today we found the remains of the Winter Fun - Tobogganing box. Lid
was quite chewed up. Log book was slightly chopped; stamp was no
where to be found.

The opposite was true for the Winter Fun - Ice Fishing box. We went
to check on it today and the tree it was in had totally collapsed and
therefore it took us a while to even find the right spot. We had
almost given up when I spotted something on the ground. It was the
lid! I then spotted the stamp laying loose on the ground with a
minor bite out of it. An animal had obviously attacked the box. We
managed to find bits of shredded baggies and a part of the plastic
binding from the logbook. Never found the box itself, nor any sign
of the actual logbook. We picked up the pieces, threw most of it
away, and brought the stamp home. I'll probably put a replacement
box (with original, slightly damaged stamp) back out within the next
few weeks. I'll put the box in the same general area, but obviously
a different resting spot!

Bottomline: Of the 4 Winter Fun boxes, 3 are history (Skating,
Tobogganing, Snowshoeing) and I will not be replacing them. The Ice
Fishing box will be replaced within the next few weeks.

One thing we have noticed - if the Rubbermaid boxes are covered with
camo tape, animals are not as attracted to the box. By covering up
that tasty blue rubber, it diminishes the appeal.

Wisconsin Hiker

It's the tasty BLUE Rubbermaid rubber the critters really like. We seen some boxes where the whole perimeter of blue on the lid has been nibbled on. If we cover that up with camo tape, it seems to deter them. We're starting to use more Lock n Lock containers now, instead of the Rubbermaid. That may also help (although I think it will be easier for animals to open the LnL. Generally they don't like PZ-Cut. They might try a taste, but then decide it's yucky and leave the rest of it alone.
