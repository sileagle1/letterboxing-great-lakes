RE: [LbGLK] Re: Any other Cheeseheads noticed...

From: Sara Byron (sarabyron@att.net) | Date: 2012-05-14 21:14:57 UTC-05:00
I agree that there are many animal culprits out there, but as a new boxer
you will find that there are also humans who feel that letterboxes (and
geocaches) are litter and trash the environment. Some of the boxers I've
met actually create new, secure sites to hold their clues, as some people
search for boxes with the goal of destroying them. It seems ridiculous to
me, with all the real problems in the world that they could apply their
efforts to ... but they feel they are cleaning the world. My guess is that
you
are not running into them, if the boxes are strewn about. I've only
planted a few boxes, but I know that the DNR has checked on them - they view
them all as geocaches, but they have requested that I correct the gps
locations, or they will remove them. My point is that we have to be careful
and there are many opportunities to be thwarted.

Sara of JeffersClan


Re: Any other Cheeseheads noticed...

From: bristolstitch (bristolstitch@yahoo.com) | Date: 2012-05-15 04:57:25 UTC
The DNR clearing them away has become a real problem for certain. There is one land manager I know of in this area who has been known to troll Atlasquest.com and Letterboxing.org searching for boxes to get out of her park.

Some people have begun to recommend no longer putting the "muggle cards" in the boxes, just explaining "this is a letterbox. Please do not destroy or remove contents." They leave off the websites. It's sad that land managers don't realize that this will bring people to their park thereby increasing their revenues, without their having to do extra advertising.
