From: Bill and Ann Fisher (wfisher47@comcast.net) | Date: 2004-05-05 07:04:00 UTC-05:00
Nice topic Deb. I've lately become a semi-active chowhound. I've been
thinking that it's a lot like letterboxing, in that the idea is to find
interesting out-of-the-way places and encourage other people to visit them
too. And, like letterboxing, it's something you can do at whatever level
of involvement suits your present circumstances and something you can slip
away from for months at a time without worrying about whether you'll be
welcome when you return (hmm, starting to sound like Weight Watchers--but
that's another story ;-) I've also been encouraging my chowhound buddies
to check out letterboxing.
Most chowhound events are just a meal at some new restaurant, but
several times a chowhound contingent has cooked and served dinner at a
homeless shelter. My most recent expedition involved an all-day hike up
Milwaukee Avenue in Chicago, checking out Polish diners, Mexican elote
carts, and Guatamalean grocery stores, and ending at the famous Superdawg,
11 miles from our start.

Here are some links

chowhound photos, including, if you scroll down, from the Milwaukee Avenue
expedition (I'm in a couple of them for those of you who would recognize
me) http://www.scottphillipsauthor.com/mike/mikespage.html

Superdawg

http://www.superdawg.com/default.cfm

I had hoped to make a letterbox to commemorate the Mil-walk-a-thon but it
didn't happen. I may yet, however.

From: Doodle & Deedle Bug (doodle_n_deedle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-05-06 09:44:19 UTC-07:00
Jeff (Deedle) is a rock-star-in-the-making. He's been playing drums since he was 8 years old and recently joined a new group in the area - Ghouls & Disciples (yes, he's into the hard rock). They won't be ready to gig for a while yet, but he's excited because he's missed performing since he left his last band (creative differences ;)

I'm also a musician, but in the classical sense. I'm a second violinist for the Parma Symphony Orchestra here in the Cleveland area. We're a community orchestra, it's lots of fun & we perform 4 times a year - October, December, March & May, usually the first Sunday of the month. If you're planning on a Cleveland area letterboxing trip why not plan it around one of our concerts? :)

Jeff also loves taking photos (esp since his parents bought him a digital camera for his last birthday) and I've recently been engulfed by scrapbooking, which is a nice combination, huh? Jeff's been taking scenic photos of the places we've gone letterboxing, and since we couldn't find just the right journal to use for our stamps we decided to use blank index cards as our journal (that way we never run out of pages) so my next scrapbooking project will be intermixing his scenic shots with our letterbox stamps.

Before I post this week's topic, I will put in my two cents on last
week's topic...

I am a person who jumps into way too many things as I learn about
them... For the most part, I am compulsive for a while and then
interest fades away and unfinished projects make their way to the
dark corners of closets and basement shelves.

But there are a few things that I have stuck with through the years -
- some constant interests, some that I return to over and over
again. These interests are writing poetry, photography and various
forms of artistic endeavors. Oh, and I guess I could throw in web
design, although that really is so much a part of my everyday life
that I really don't even think of it as a hobby any longer.

All of these interests can be further explored through my eyes at my
various [ever-evolving and not too frequently updated] websites...
