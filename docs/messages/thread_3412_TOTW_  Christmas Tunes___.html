Beetle's favorite Christmas tune is: Wonderful Christmastime By Paul McCartney
I find myself humming and singing this song throughout the Christmas season. "We're simply having a wonderful Christmastime...."

Happy Xmas (War Is Over), By John Lennon is another good contemporary song, but I just can't stand listening to Yoko's screeching. I prefer that song sung by other artists.

And, to show you how vast my appreciation for music is, my favorite Christmas CD is: Holiday Sing Along With Mitch By Mitch Miller
What a classic sound! I grew up watching Mitch Miller and The Gang on TV. I have two copies of the album (complete with song sheets inside), but last year I actually found the album on CD! I couldn't believe that it was available. No song sheets inside however.

Oh, this will be hard to narrow down. Every year, my son & husband
give me a new Christmas CD so I have lots to choose from!! But my all-
time favorites would have to be:


~~O Come, O Come, Emmanuel--like the tune and the words

~~The 8 Polish Foods of Christmas--by VeggieTales (although the entire
CD is pretty funny--esp. all the commentary between songs)

~~What Child is This--I've always loved that tune

~~Carol of the Bells--I play in our church's handbell choir and would
just love it if our director would pick this some year (I played it in
college and it sounded really great)

~~Mary, Did You Know? (by Mark Lowry)--I really like the idea behind
the lyrics: Did Mary really know that her Son would grow up to calm
storms and heal the sick and raise the dead--and die for her (and our)
sins. Last year, our Sunday School acted out the scenes from this song
while I took pictures. We put the pictures to the music to create a
music video. Very moving--lots of crying moms when we showed it.

~~One King (by Point of Grace)--"One king held the frankincense, One
king held the myrrh, One king held the purest gold, One King held the
hope of the world"--Beautiful song.

~~Jolly Old St. Nicholas--the only song my sister can still play on the
piano. As soon as she gets to my folk's house for Christmas, she sits
down and plays it--so I like it for the family tradition.

BirdWoman/Waltzing Pigs

The Little Drummer Boy is my favorite Christmas song. A couple of
years ago I put together a CD of 19 different versions/artists singing
the Little Drummer Boy -- OldShoe, who is not one to play a song he
especially likes over and over again, just cringed when I played it --
now I just listen to it in the car once in a while when he is not
around. The Bob Segar version is one of my favorites, along with the
versions with Bing Crosby & David Bowie, Johnny Cash & Neil Young and
the Joan Jett version.

A few other songs I would have to include -- "O Holy Night" (not
partial to any particular version), Emerson, Lake & Palmer's " I
Believe in Father Christmas," John Lennon's "So This is Christmas"
and "Happy Christmas (War is Over)" -- until this year the Neil
Diamond version was my favorite, but the Sarah McLachlan version on
her new Christmas CD, Wintersong, is giving him a run for his money.

And just for fun... "Snoopy's Christmas" by the Royal Guardsmen
and "Nuttin' for Christmas" -- the version of this song I had as a kid
was done by the Blue Jean Singers, but I have not been able to find
that version, at least not a worthy copy. The Barry Gordon remake is
pretty close.

As for instrumental, "A Carol for Alex" by Wind Machine, "Jenna's
Dance" by Paul Cardall and "Angels in the Snow" by David Arkenstone
would have to be there.

To round it off, anything by Trans-Siberian Orchestra or Mannheim
Steamroller.

Can you tell I love Christmas music? I make a holiday mix every year -
- I will have to check out some of the music suggested by other
posters here for this year's CD. I really like the song, "In the
Bleak Midwinter," but haven't found a version that blows me away (yet).

SpringChick
