Starved for letterboxes?

From: Coffee Beans (coffeebeans06@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-06-26 10:22:43 UTC-05:00
Boy, this board has been pretty quiet lately, so I figured I'd share a
report on our most recent letterboxing trip. :)

Atom and I went down to the Starved Rock State Park (IL) area over the
weekend. Apparently a few other letterboxers had the same plan, as we
stamped in a day after RacerCar (I think) on several of our finds, and I
got an email that LibraryGrrl and Gram Judy were there this weekend as
well! Anyway, HEAVY rainfalls all Friday night made things a bit wet
and muddy, but nothing we (and our tent) couldn't handle. ;) We started
out on the cool, completely overcast Saturday morning with a
semi-challenging box: "Effigy Tumuli" by Matt the Rat. This box had a
few "attempted" finds marked from last summer, but we were up for a
challenge. We reached the place where we thought (knew!) the box should
be, and after a few readjustments of the compass, we managed to find
it! The box hadn't had any visitors in just about a year and was
well-hidden, so we felt good about ourselves. This good find set the
tone for the rest of our day. We next went to check out the buffalo and
find "Shuffle off to Bluffalo" by FungusWoman. A little buggy back
there, but another great find. :) Afterwards, we did some hiking at
Starved Rock State Park itself, but mostly at the western end, which
sees a lot less visitors than the east side where the lodge and
visitor's center are located. We found "The Point" by edna, followed by
a very pleasant hike through Illinois Canyon for the box there by
Travel'n Trout, although the "mostly dry creek bed" was certainly not
dry on Saturday. :) We then headed over to the lodge area to find
"Starved Rock Buffet" by Matt the Rat and "Stargazer" by edna. Both had
fun and semi-tricky clues to follow so we had to wander around a bit,
but we ended up successful for both boxes. We also went to where we
thought "Over the Creek" should be, but had no luck there. Perhaps it
was just the overgrowth preventing us from seeing something obvious.

Sunday morning dawned cloudy again, but at least no more rain. We
packed up our very muddy and damp tent and headed over to Mattheson
State Park. We REALLY enjoyed this park. We found "A Lil Bit of
Flutter" and "Hershey's Hike" by FungusWoman, Papillon, and Hershey of
course. :) The trail along the canyon bottoms there were great and not
very busy. We definitely recommend doing the "long" version for
Hershey's Hike -- we did take the "shortcut" back to the car later on,
and it was definitely less impressive. From there, we drove out towards
Princeton, IL to do some boxes by the Pitties. The city parks there are
very large and clean -- there were several people out enjoying the day
at the parks. The boxes there were very nicely done as well. Next, we
turned around and headed back east towards home. On the way, we took a
brief detour up IL 71 to search for a few more FungusWoman boxes: FOOW2,
FOOW6, and Lutefish out of Water. The location for FOOW2 was a popular
stopping place on Sunday it seemed, for 2 other cars stopped while we
were attempting to find the hidden box. Unfortunately, we had to give
up once the others arrived. We did find FOOW6 and Lutefish successfully
however. These boxes are very neat little stopping points to break up
the drive.

Overall, we had a great weekend. So if you're "starved" for some good
letterboxes, hopefully now you know where to look! :)

Coffee Beans (and Atom)

Hey, I remember when we boxed in those areas, but it was almost 3
years ago! There might be some newer boxes there now...

We're working on a trip report from our most recent trip, but if
anyone wants to read about our Memorial Day adventures, I'll post it
below (we originally posted it on the Southeast regional board).
We'd HIGHLY recommend boxing in the Stone Mountain (Atlanta, GA)
area - lots of nice hikes and great carvings!

Trip report:

Hello everyone!

We arrived home yesterday after our 4-day trip to GA. The SE Annual
Gathering at Stone Mountain triggered our trip, since we had read a
lot about all the boxes there. We assumed it was a large park where
we could do lots of hiking. However after I booked our flights I
Googled the park and got worried - it looked like a theme park with
lots of "attractions". Oh, oh... well we were committed, so we also
printed some clues for other boxes in the surrounding area just in
case.

We flew out Friday night and headed for the park Saturday morning,
not sure what to expect. Of course we had a HUGE bundle of clues to
juggle, so when we got to the park we just took a random turn and
ended up near the covered bridge & quarry. OK, we had some clues for
this area, so we hopped out of the car. Hmmm... this seemed like a
very nice area. Trees, water, trails - just what we like! Our fears
were laid to rest immediately. While looking for one box in the
quarry area, a couple started walking near us - drat! Time to look
like we were consulting our map and just admiring the area. The
woman proceeded ahead, but the man wandered along past us. All of a
sudden he asked "Did you find it?" Oh GOOD - Just another
letterboxer! He said he thought it was further ahead, where the woman
was and sure enough we headed up to retrieve it and do our first
exchange of the day with Llama Mama. After finding several boxes, we
headed to the picnic area to meet some of the local boxers. We were
greeted by several and started the process of doing exchanges and
chatting with people. Some were names we recognized from message
boards or as planters and others were new to us. After a pleasant
few hours, we headed off with a small group led by StarSAELS. He had
offered to act as a "tour guide" for a climb up the rock and a search
for many boxes along the way up & down. Wow - he is ORGANIZED and we
really appreciate his willingness to spend his time helping us find
our way around the mountain. We also were amazed at the detail on
his 99 Bottles of Beer stamps.

We had hoped to meet up with the group again for dinner, but got lost
trying to find the place. It didn't help that we never asked the
name of the restaurant, nor exchanged cell phone numbers, so we
couldn't even tell anyone where we were trying to get to! After
checking 3 places, we decided to just eat some pizza ourselves and
head back to the park. We found a few more before the laser light
show and then headed back to our hotel for a much-needed rest.
[Exchanged with 24 letterboxers, Found 28 boxes and 1 Hitchhiker]

We figured that Stone Mountain would be very busy over the holiday
weekend, so we decided to visit different parks on Sunday and Monday,
then return to Stone Mountain on Tuesday. We headed to Tribble Mill
park on Sunday and after a rough start (couldn't figure out what
trail we were supposed to start on, but eventually got something to
work and went from there). Eventually we backtracked from one of the
first clues to find the "No Parking" sign and that got us going on
more clues. The plus side of being disoriented was that we found a
box that we didn't even have clues for! This was another nice area
to spend the day walking. However there were way too many boxes here
than we could find in one day, so we eventually called it quits and
headed back for another good night's sleep.
[Found 22 boxes and 1 Hitchhiker]

Monday we headed down to the Davidson-Arabia Mountain Nature
Preserve. First we found several boxes on the "flatter" part of the
park, then headed over to climb up the granite. We again really
enjoyed the walks - Martini Man especially enjoyed being on the
Arabia peak since it was so undeveloped and we had the place to
ourselves. We finished up in the park by mid-afternoon, so decided
to head back to Tribble Mill again. Didn't have as much luck finding
some of the boxes - especially frustrating when we know others found
them not long ago. What were we missing?! Oh well, you can't find
them all!
[Found 19 boxes]

Tuesday it was a return to Stone Mountain. We started off at the
covered bridge/quarry area again and after finding a few newer boxes
we took off on the Cherokee Trail. I had a whole packet of clues
clutched in my hand since we would try to find some going forward,
reverse engineer some going backward and we never knew where we might
find the things shown in all the picture clues... A great walk with
views of water and mountain along the way. Also mostly shaded and a
nice breeze, who could ask for a better day? We did fairly well and
many clues went from the packet to Martini Man's pocket as we found
them one by one. After completing the circuit we headed to the
Nature Trail and then the Songbird Trail to find a few last boxes
before we ended our 4-day extravaganza.
[Found 23 boxes and 1 Hitchhiker]

We both agreed that this was one of the nicest areas in the country
we have boxed challenging clues, good box placements and excellent
carvings!!! Thanks so much to all of you who have placed boxes (we
will be sending individual "Contact the Placer" messages in the near
future), to Red's Bunch for organizing the gathering and to StarSAELS
for all his recommendations and tips for our visit to the area.

Wisconsin Hiker & Martini Man
