Moving

From: Delirium (donaldson_home@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-09-22 05:45:06 UTC-07:00

Well, it's official. My husband, who works for Ford, went and got us transfered. And to *Memphis* of all places. Memphis, as in "hey y'all, how all y'alls doin' I reckon you're fixin' to have some sweet tea" Memphis. (Unless there's a Memphis, Michigan, in which case I have NO idea what's going on)

This presents a multitude of difficulties, some of which I'll list below for your email amusement (hey, it's not you being shipped off down south, you might as well get a good chuckle)

difficulty one: My husband is leaving in ten days. I'm not leaving until Christmas break. This means that it's me who gets to fix the house, clean the house, get the house up on the market and get it sold, all before December 21st. No biggie.

difficulty two: I've never moved in my life. Well, there were those exile years I spent up in Lansing during college, but other than that, I'm SE Michigan, born and bred. And I'm *moving*?? Did I mention that I'm moving to **MEMPHIS**?

difficulty three: I have to find a new hair stylist! This fact alone might make me stay if I dwell on it too long. I went to school with my stylist! She stole my sixth grade boyfriend from me! She's the only one in the world who knows just how long to leave the highlights in! I can't leave her! Maybe she'll move down to Memphis with me....

but the BIGGEST problem is this:

Go look at how many boxes there are in the Memphis area. Go ahead- look. And I'll even broaden the field for you, since we're looking for houses across the border in Mississippi...I'll wait...you go look.



Grim right? THREE BOXES! And my mother-in-law placed one-third of them. So there we are. I'm leaving the only home I've ever known, to go across three states, one time zone, and a whole culture, only to settle in a landscape barren of letterboxes.

But, there's a silver lining in this ugly, ugly cloud- I'm taking all but two of my boxes with me (my apologies to Team Taco, who went searching for one of my boxes I'd pulled for this reason and hadn't warned about) and planting them down south. I'll be the foremost letterboxing presence in the greater Memphis area. All will fear me! I will rule by madness and tyrany! All will bow down before my- well, you get the point.

So, the rest of my boxes will be pulled in the next month or so, with the exception of "Kensington Haiku" and my new box, carved just for the occaision- "So Long Michigan, '75-'05". Team Rabbit has generously agreed to not only adopt those boxes, but also take over planning for next year's Gathering- which I will be at, come hell or high water. And I WON'T be sporting a new southern accent. But I might be sporting some extra pounds if there's a Waffle House anywhere near me. *grin*

Cheers,

Delirium
