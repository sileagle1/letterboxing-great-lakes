Awesome day of planting

From: Gooble Gobble (gooble_gobble_3@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-09-06 22:31:31 UTC-07:00
I was out this afternoon planting The Nights who say
NI! and came across several cool things. First and
foremost I saw my first ever Coyote! A strange
coincidence considering the Nights box was carved by
Howling Coyote!!! Second, I was in a small (or so I
thought) park 2 blocks from my house that I had never
been to before only to discover it's huge! I saw a big
white-tailed deer running through the forest perhaps
15 yards away and several species of mushrooms in
profusion. I have been really jonesing for Morel
mushroom season and doing alot of reading online about
it to increase my odds of finding more next year. This
last May was my first time out and now I'M COMPLETELY
HOOKED! Talk about treasure hunts, oh my goodness.
With shroom hunting you never know if you'll even see
a Morel and when you do it's better than finding a
mystery box it's taken you 6 months to solve because
you can eat it. Also the Morel season lasts for only
about 1 month a year (unless you move further north to
follow the weather) If you have never hunted the
elusive and delectable Morel, DO IT! you will have a
new obsession to look forward to every spring. Just
what we all need right? another obsession to make sure
we never get anything done. But seriously, DO IT! If I
get lucky and find enough this next spring I will be
bringing some to the gathering to share with you all.
I've read some incredible stories of people finding 40
+ lbs. in one outing! If you have never tasted the
Morel you don't know what you're missing. I better
stop typing, this message has strayed off topic,
sorry, but you really need to hunt for Morels next
spring. Even if you don't like mushrooms you can sell
the Morels for $20 + per pound! MMMMMM Morels ( read
with a Homer Simpsonesque longing in your eyes and
make sure to drool). Umm where was I, oh yeah, great
day of planting and dreaming about the future.


Gooble Gobble


P.S. You lucky folks in Wisconsin have some of the
best Morel hunting grounds around I hear.
