Immediately following Thanksgiving, Christmas, New Years, and all the family holidays at this time of the year, what we need to do it get out of the house, far far away from the cookies and treats, and hike it off...and maybe find some letterboxes. But the snow keeps falling and the ice is dangerous, so we stay inside.

But for those who 'need' an outlet for some artistic, letterbox related, creative inspiration, SILO #3 is sure to get you back on track. Lock and I hope that the SILO #3 party plans will get your creative juices flowing and inspire you to carve some new stamps for the event. The idea of floats fits the theme of the gathering, but we hope it will inspire you to "think outside the box" with new ways to create letterboxes. And logbooks...let's see if we can create some fancy logbooks too. Not mandatory, but always a creative challenge.

Don't forget to post your new boxes on AQ where the event is listed. I just posted 4 that I have completed.

Glad to hear that some people are planning to wear masks and dress up, however it is optional. Maybe you just want to wear something green, gold, and purple - Mardi Gras colors. But it's sure to be a festive time, and at this time of the year....we need some festivity! Will it ever stop snowing?

Cooties? There are always cooties lurking at a gathering. But maybe not like the epidemic that broke out at SILO #1. I bet if I continue to look again in my inner jacket lining I might still find another cootie or two from that event. Itch! Itch! Itch!

I'm looking forward to Lock's carving workshop. Maybe some of his artistic talent and skills will rub off on us.

WFMary - Seems my level of enthusiasm is a topic of discussion. I could get MORE enthusiastic, but trust me, I'm trying to keep it to some level of normalcy; failing miserably, but trying all the same.

Brat Pack IL is certainly full of the magic of celebrating Mardi Gras. (Any reason to celebrate is a good reason.) They have been in contact with me, but I can only imagine all the ideas that they are coming up with. All that enthusiasm and creativity feeding off each other can only mean one thing....lots of surprises in store!

Fungus Woman has mailed her event boxes to me and hopes to be able to arrange an early flight home to attend SILO #3. More great stamps! But here's another enthusiastic soul....she also sent a CD of the HOTTEST Mardi Gras jazz music for us to play during the event...guaranteed to melt those winter letterboxing blues.

The list of letterbox party goers is growing, but we're also looking for more event stamps to be hidden on top of tables and other creative indoor places. Post your boxes and clues on the event listing. There can never be too many boxes at a gathering and with this theme, the sky's the limit for flamboyant images.

Of course, I'm looking forward to Lock's carving tutorial workshop; a perfect indoor winter activity for letterboxers who can't wait for spring searching. He will spend about an hour or so talking and walking you through some carving tips and tricks that he has mastered.

The Parade of Floats will certainly be a Kodak moment! We hope that some of these creative floats will become permanent indoor boxes. I thought I was done, but no, I decided to add some 'sparkle' today.

Mardi Gras goers toss and trade beads, but letterboxers attending a Mardi Gras theme event can trade beads and LTCs too. You can never bring enough LTC "favors" to this event.

I know the folks at the SILO are just waiting to see what "that group" is planning this year. They are still talking about SILO #1 and #2. I guess we left an impression! The Krewe of Dartmore will be there soon....let the good times roll!

I'm home after a weekend at Girl Scout camp and some outdoor hiking, sledding, and letterboxing. I think I have corrupted the minds of 5 HS age girls and possible some parents. When we arrived at the property, two of the young ladies immediately commented..."I wonder if the letterbox is still here...." and they wanted to go hiking in sub zero temps. to find it. It was still there too! To see the smiles on their faces was precious. Can you say addicted?

Bourbon Street....you've got competition! See ya'll soon down at SILO #3.

Beetle
