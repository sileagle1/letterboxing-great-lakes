From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-05-20 06:31:54 UTC-04:00
Kathi,

Sounds like you have it pretty well covered! It was very cold (and rainy)
last year, even though it was May 31st. But that didn't dampen spirits one
bit -- we had a great time despite the uncooperative weather! I really
can't think of much besides the things you have mentioned. A couple people
brought items to show -- for instance Matt the Rat has taken some
bookbinding classes and has done some awesome handbound books. He brought a
few of these with him and we all ohhhed and ahhhhed and vowed to run out and
sign up for a bookbinding class. A few people brought stamp carvings they
were especially proud of.

Although this didn't apply to our gathering last year, I know that at some
of the other gatherings, people have taken the opportunity to distribute
clues to unpublished letterboxes. Also HH stamps are a common thing to
bring to gatherings. I am not a big fan of the HH table concept, where a
bunch of HH are just laid out on the table and all of the people at the
gathering just stamp the HH (except in the case of legendary HH like
K-Martha), but since there will be opportunity to do a fair amount of
'boxing and I believe Tim has a HH hostel coming, you may want to have a HH
with you. Don't have one in your possession right now? Make a new one and
launch it at the gathering!

No doubt I have left off something -- anyone else?

SpringChick

Kathi,
Yeah, and did SpringChick mention that it was really, really COLD?
There was a wonderful urban/microbox workshop by Yooperann and
samples of boxes and camouflage techniques were available for
examination. So,if you have special containers, 'cammo', ways of
hiding or attaching microboxes, or other techniques you've been using
successfully, bring them along and share your ideas.
(I'll bring one of my urban 'library' boxes since there have been
some questions about that genre.)

I'll try to remember if there was other 'stuff' that caught my
attention last year and post more later.

elsewhere -- anything to add?
