Bristol and 10th anniversary update

From: duncan53211 (duncan53211@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-06-26 17:37:44 UTC
I've been wanting to get info out on a more timely basis, but
everything is slow for me now that I severely cut up my hand on some
glass last Sat.

Thanks to Leapin' Lizards, the Bristol stamps are being worked on. I
still do not know whether they'll be ready for opening weekend though,
but they may be. Stay posted.

I'm also looking into the possibility of having ALL the Bristol boxes
from the past five years available one of the weekends. Originally, I
was looking at 7/19, but now I'm thinking of the weekend of 7/26.

Also, I am working on the 10th anniversary of LBing in WI gathering.
We will have the gathering at Whitefish Dunes SP in Door Cty - the
site of the first LB in WI. The two dates the shelter is available
are 10/4 and 10/11. Please vote in the poll as to your preference if
you would like to come. Note that the poll closes on Friday night
(needto reserve the shelter quickly).

I am seeking input for a theme for the gathering as well as any help,
including making and hiding boxes, and the making of the event stamp.
I am also working on some special things for the gathering which I
won't announce until they are firmed up. Email me (please don't post
it to the GL site) to let me know of any help/suggestions/themes you
can offer. I'd like to make this OUR celebration, not just mine as
much as possible.

A couple of things to keep in mind:
-Whitefish Dunes has no camping - there is camping at Potawonami and
Penninsula SP nearby.
-Whitefish Dunes is a state park so you need a sticker. However,
there is limited parking at the county park connected to the SP which
is free.
-Both parks have limited parking. Carpooling (esp. with gas prices
these days) is highly encouraged.
-No boxes made for the event will be permanent (temporary boxes only).
The park is on fragile land, and they've been VERY nice to allow the
one box to remain. Boxes will be/should be placed so they have VERY
LOW impact on the land, and should NOT be placed anywhere near the
dangerous cliffs or in sensitive areas. Boxes may also be hidden in
the county park nearby with the same restrictions.

Watch for more info to come...
