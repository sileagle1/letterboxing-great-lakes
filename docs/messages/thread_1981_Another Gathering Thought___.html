Another Gathering Thought...

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-11-10 12:10:39 UTC
I have also considered that maybe we have outgrown the regional Great
Lakes Gathering and it would be more feasible to go with the state-
concentric gatherings such as the MN gathering, the MI Fall Gathering,
etc.

I had hoped that as letterboxing grew in the region and the state-
focused gatherings began to pop up as we saw in the past year, that
the regional GL Gathering would continue on as the yearly overall
event for everyone in the region. But large gatherings can be
overwhelming and driving 6-8 hours to a gathering is not feasible for
many people. So perhaps it makes more sense to put effort into local
gatherings.

Thoughts?

SpringChick

Re: [LbGLK] Another Gathering Thought...

From: (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2005-11-10 08:22:48 UTC-06:00
I figure that all gatherings are pretty much open to anyone, so I don't
really differentiate between what is "local", "state", "regional"
or "national". We've attended an event in Connecticut and I know at
least a few others in this region have attended gatherings in other
areas of the country. Most gatherings are publicized on the discussion
groups and AtlasQuest, so I believe that is considered an "open
invitation".

I also figure that it's fine to have multiple gatherings in the same
time period. Not everyone will be available on the same weekend, nor
will everyone be willing to drive to a given location. If there are
people willing to organize an event, I say "go for it!". More
gatherings increase the likelihood that there will be at least one that
each person could make it to. For example, there was an event in
Illinois on SEP 17 and one in Wisconsin on OCT 1, only 2 weeks later.
Some people could make it to one, some to another, and some to both.

I think it would be good to continue the tradition of having a
gathering that is called the "Great Lakes Gathering" and that it
rotates between the 5 states that are in the region (assuming there is
someone willing to host in that state).

We're hoping to make it to as many as possible!

Wisconsin Hiker

From: azobox (jparkerg@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-11-10 14:32:12 UTC
I have quite mixed feelings about all of this.
Having attended all three of the 'official' Great Lakes Gatherings, I
enjoyed the chance to meet some of the boxers more distant from my
home turf. But, as the numbers of letterboxers has increased
dramatically over the past several years, so have the attendees at
events. Tales of HUNDREDS of people attending some out of state
gatherings frighten me. One of the things I enjoy about letterboxing
is that it happens best in small numbers.
I wonder if the small get togethers and the quasi-regular statewide
events are replacing the feasibility of the regional gatherings? Or
maybe the biggies will continue but not as the sole event for some
letterboxers?
The Great Lakes region is large. An event in MN will not draw people
from OH, and vice-versa. As we collapse the areas to more driveable
distances, some MI people will drive to IL or IN and some WI people
will drive to MI or IL. But maybe we've simply outgrown our need to
have a single event in which to meet others. Maybe an event in a
central location (whatever THAT might be) on a set weekend is the
answer. That way, the state events can take place on non-conflicting
dates elsewhere during the letterboxing season (whatever THAT is).
I seem to remember at least three events in MI this year in addition
to the Great Lakes Gathering in June. I'm also recalling at least the
same number in WI.
So, like I said, I have mixed feelings. But I'd hate to see quickly
scheduled events supplant some others which carry some history.

This is an important discussion for our region.

I second SpringChick's call for commments.

azobox

From: Doglvrs RASW (doglvrs_4@msn.com) | Date: 2005-11-10 15:55:58 UTC
My 2-cents:
I really like the idea of a "set" location on a "set" date as the regional
gathering.
I like the idea of knowing where it will be and when so I can plan
accordingly.
I really, really enjoyed attending 2 state gatherings this year, in addition
to my first Great Lakes gathering. I wouldn't miss the regional gathering
for anything! Knowing when and where it will always be, would leave me open
to picking and choosing the gatherings I might like to attend in other
states.
I also enjoy chance to meet with people that live all over the Great Lakes
region. While large gatherings don't necessarily scare me, I find it fairly
difficult to hunt up boxes at larger gatherings, because I spend so much
time talking to people I know and don't know. This is an "OK" thing for me,
because I can box anytime, so I make the most of meeting new people which is
really fun for me.

So, in short, my thoughts are that with a set Regional gathering (possibly
larger) and other State specific gatherings (probably smaller) we may
achieve the "best of both worlds" scenario with the "goer" choosing which
gathering suits them best.

[LbGLK] Re: Another Gathering Thought...

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-11-10 16:47:03 UTC
I like the idea of having a set location and a set date. I think this
would allow people to make travel plans well in advance and also would
help other event planners to avoid date conflicts, etc. Back when the
decision was made to move the GL Gathering around it was to make it
accessible to everyone in the region, both in terms of attending and
hosting, as it was the only gathering held in the upper midwest. There
are many other gatherings now and those options make this less of a
consideration with the GL Gathering.

WI Hiker raises a very good point... If the gathering were to be at
the exact same park every year, it might get a little boring. Of
course there are generally enough boxes sent in by attendees that there
would always be enough new boxes to find on the day of the event
itself, and there are continually new boxes being planted in all areas
of the GL region, so travelers should be able to find new boxes along
the way. But, variety is nice and it may be more interesting to set
the "location" to a specific geographic area with several parks rather
than one specific park. This would also be wise as having a set date
may cause problems with shelter reservation if it were limited to a
specific park.

One other consideration... When choosing a permanent location, I think
that the immediate obvious consideration is the proximity to the
majority of letterboxers in the region. However, in my mind an even
more important consideration is the availability of event hosts. There
needs to be a willing volunteer host nearby or willing to travel a bit
to plant boxes. I can't just say, okay this is the place where it is
going to be every year and hope that somebody comes forward to host.

Thinking this through a bit...

SpringChick
