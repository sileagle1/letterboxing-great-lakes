From: Baliddle (baliddle@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2007-05-08 10:52:43 UTC-07:00
I don't bother to put a pen or ink pad with my boxes. I put a not that no ink pad is included. I live in SE MI. almost every box we've found with an ink pad included, the ink pad is way over wet. So, I don't bother. Most people will have their own, anyway, I believe. If my stamp looks better in a certain color, I add a note with color suggestions.

You can add your clues to either or both of those sites. (Or neither if you want to distribute them by WOM). I put them on both, just in case one or the other goes down.

Welcome and have fun!
Bali

From: Barb Funk (mmfunk99@greenapple.com) | Date: 2007-05-08 13:58:32 UTC-04:00
Welcome Blue Sky in Kazoo and congrats on your first finds!

I will jump in and respond with just a few of my thoughts and others
will add comments as well. You will get as many different replies as
there are letterboxers and that is just one of the beauties of this hobby.

The first suggestion many of us would make is to take some time to do
some homework by checking out the many enlightening resources available
to you on the home pages of both AQ and LbNA. Under "What is new?" on
the AQ home page, there is a wonderful new section explaining so many
aspects of letterboxing in detail.

At the bottom of the LbNA site, there is a glossary of terms that really
helps as well, but these are just the very, very beginning of quite an
educational adventure. My own interests have taken many different turns
since I found my first box in March of 2004 and that is what keeps this
going for so long!

A few things I think we all agree upon is that letterboxers must be very
careful not to give away a location of a box while searching, not to
damage the area, to reclose the box or container completely and to
rehide the box better than found. After that, it gets crazy and goes
all over the board.

For me, I do not put stamp padss into my boxes. I did when I first
started planting boxes and most of the stamp pads only added to issues
with the logs being ruined when they might not have been, otherwise. I
have seen some others that held up fine, but I have also had one where
the inked water spilled and ruined my jeans, shoes and jacket when I
first opened the box. ughhh Red dye everywhere. I learned the hard way
and the owner of the box lost a ton of signatures that might have been
salvaged had the log only been wet. Most folks carry either stamp pads
(many of them for some of us) a complete set of Marvy Markers or both,
and I would think that we all carry pens or pencils too. It is nice to
come to a box where the color made a difference because of the stamp. A
box I found recently included a stamp of Piglet and the pink was the
perfect color. I was happy to use the stamp they provided!
Regardless, it is all up to you. There are really very few rules, but
we all have opinions of what we like best or what we have found works
best for us based upon why we love this hobby/sport/addiction!

I'll end my comments here and let the next person jump in! Congrats and
welcome again!
Trailtracker, Ohio
