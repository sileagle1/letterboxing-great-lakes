Magic Hedge report - IL

From: Matt (ratpatrolhq@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-08-20 17:52:37 UTC
Well, at last I have an answer to all those who have asked about the
status of the Magic Hedge:

Last night on the way home I rode my bike down to the lake to check on
the box. I thought it'd take just a few minutes and I'd be on my way.
Walking closer it was amazing how the landscape has changed in just a
few months since I placed the box. Tall weeds and seedling trees where
there had only been sand, a huge permanent puddle where there had been
dry land. When I got to the hole where the box was hidden, there was
someone else there! A big ol' mama raccoon, staring at me. It didn't
run away, no, instead it retreated to the hole where the letterbox was
hidden.

I was wondering how I would ever check on the box now, when suddenly I
saw 3 more little raccoon faces peeking out. The perfect hiding spot
for a letterbox was now a cozy raccoon home. Its not at all the place
where you would expect to see raccoons living, but its also not the
first time I've encountered raccoon families living in letterbox
hiding places! These raccoons were adolescent, nearly grown, so they'd
probably been living there a while.

I waited a long time, the raccoons would come out of their house, poke
around in the puddle for a while, play/fight each other, and then race
back to the hole at the slightest sound. Finally they all went off
into the bushes and I dared approach. It was pretty frightening to put
my hand into the dark space to feel for the box in case there was one
little critter left behind, but better my hand than stick my head in
there!

Well, the end of the story is that the Magic Hedge letterbox is gone,
again. If you want to find a cute raccoon family, however, follow the
clues in the evening and stand very still until they come out of their
house.

Matt the Rat
