Re: [LbGLK] Outdoor Opportunities near Chicago?

From: Gabe (ainawgsd@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-08-29 13:08:55 UTC-07:00
There's lots of outdoor stuff to do within a few hours of Chicago. Unfortunately, being from east/central IL (about 2-3 hours south of Chicago area) I'm not particularly familiar with most of them though. I have been to Starved Rock and Matthiessen State Park are both in northern IL near Utica and they shouldn't be more than 2 hours from Chicago. They are both very scenic. If he really wants back-country camping, Sand Ridge State Forest (in west central IL (about 3.5 hours south of Chicago) has 55 miles of backwoods hiking trails with 12 primitive camp sites. He would need to get a permit from the forest headquarters first though.

As for weather, it is certainly cooler here, with highs in the mid 80's lately and lows down to the upper 50's and lower 60's at night. It is however much more humid here, which can make it feel like nearly 100 during the day. Next week it's supposed to be around 80 during the day with lows right around 60 and a chance of rain.

Re: [LbGLK] Outdoor Opportunities near Chicago?

From: Gabe (ainawgsd@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-08-30 09:55:10 UTC-07:00
I suppose it depends on where you plant. I'm not sure how busy Sand Ridge is, but I have found several of my boxes in some of the less busy parks out in the open on the trail (usually because someone has done some sort of maintenance which resulted in the removal of a structure they were hidden under) but in perfect condition. I've also had a few that I have chosen not to replace because they consistently turn up missing. Probably the biggest thing to be careful of is to not plant too close to a river unless you are able to make it a seasonal box and pull it about mid-winter to avoid the late winter/spring flooding. While the river trails are very nice and some of them have great hiding places they tend to be highly prone to flooding (I think I've lost more boxes that way than to muggles or wildlife). Closer to the Chicago area there are probably more good hiding places, down here in Central IL we have a lot of flat land and not very many large
rocks except in the flood plains so we sort of have to make do with tree stumps and fallen logs.
