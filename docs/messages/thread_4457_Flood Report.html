Flood Report

From: Atom 118 (hersh118@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2007-09-24 09:38:24 UTC-05:00
Coffee Beans and I finally got out on the trails to check on the status
of our boxes post the 'Great Des Plaines Flood of 2007.' We managed to
check on a dozen of our boxes, including the ones we thought were at
most risk. We are happy to report that we found them ALL intact.
(Squash, Future Noble, Beautiful Flower, All four boxes of the HHH
series, Of The Planes, Two Trees in the Forest Hostel, Wicked, and Des
Plaines River).

However, we did decide to retire 'A Future Noble.' The box was in an
area prone to flooding, and based on the mud on top of the log hiding
it, this box must have been under 2-3 feet of water. This was the
second maintenance visit where I've had to dig the box out from under
the log. EEEK! Plus, all the flooding has changed the trail, and you'd
have to be a VERY GOOD letterboxer to be able to navigate the current
trail (although I know a few of you could still probably find it). As a
result, we decided to take the box with us. We also checked on Turtle
McQ's 'Chicag-O' that was hidden nearby, and I'm sad to report that this
box seems to be gone. Here too you could see the effects of the recent
flood. This box had been hiding behind several very large logs. Now,
none of the logs were anywhere nearby.

I had also gotten a report that my 'Of the Planes' had been damaged, and
it was. This box has been temporarily removed. (Evidently the last
finders found much of the contents missing/strewn on the ground. All
that remained was the stamp in the box. I'll make an new log, and fix
the box.)

We also had a chance to check the final box of our HHH series. The box
was intact, but the trail might be difficult to navigate, and as much of
it seemed a bit washed over. The riverbank near the box is 3-4 feet
above the river's surface. The box is another 2-3 feet off the ground.
Given both those facts, the river sediment was another 1-2 feet ABOVE
the box! We were very impressed that With a Cherry on Top STILL managed
to find the box, post flood.

We might go check the rest of boxes near the river sometime soon. Since
these boxes were the ones most likely to have been washed away, we are
rather confident that the others should be fine (as they are all much
further away from the river).
