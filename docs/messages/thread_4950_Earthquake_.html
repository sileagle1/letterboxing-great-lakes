I was out on the tractor planting at the time and felt absolutely nothing.

But when I got back in from the field, Caveman said the horses went nuts
from about 4:15am to about 4:45am. However, he also felt absolutely
nothing. Felt nothing, heard nothing, saw nothing. But he knew something
was up because I have one Appaloosa that the sky pretty much has to be
falling before he'll get upset, and Caveman said that even he was a really
nervous nelly. So we knew that if THAT particular horse got upset,
something was going on.

Checked for any barn fires. Nope, nothing. Checked for any predators like
the cougars they seem to be seeing around the Chicago area. Thought maybe
one of 'em was lurking around the barns. Nope, nothing. Came in the house
for breakfast, flipped on the TV news, and there it was. Earthquake.

We desperately need a new garage. But did the earthquake swallow the
garage? Noooooooooooooo. But I guess no damage at all is for the best.

We had one here in central IL a few years ago (I think it was March, but don't remember for sure).  Both FD and I thought it was the dog scratching an itch at the foot of the bed.  But when it continued and I woke up enough to realize that the glass in the windows was shaking it dawned on me that it was an earthquake.  Having lived in CA and the pacific northwest I've slept through plenty of earthquakes before, but for some reason that one unnerved me and it took a while for me to get back to sleep.  My boss also blamed it on the dog, as did a few other people we knew with dogs!  I don't remember it, but my mom remembers when we had one back in the mid-80's when I would have been in kindergarten.  IL is actually on the New Madrid fault line, it just isn't a very active fault line compared to the ones along the Pacific ring.
