Bingo Q & A

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-12-06 00:27:44 UTC
I've received a few BINGO questions in e-mail and here on the list...

Q. Is it okay to use a trailname if you got the person's personal
traveler but didn't exchange signature stamps?
A. Yeh, I'll let this go as a personal traveler is sometimes the stamp
that a person chooses to exchange instead of their sig stamp.

Q. Is there a minimum or maximum $$ amount for the Bingo Booty item?
Can we send more than one thing?
A. There is no min or max and you may send as many items as your heart
desires.

Q. We don't meet the requirements to use any of the letterboxes,
trailnames or gatherings because we have only been letterboxing a few
months...

... can we just choose anything we want then?
... can we not play then?

A. You can only use the letterboxes, trailnames and gatherings if you
meet the criteria. If not, you will need to choose from the general
letterboxing terms for your list. There are enough general terms that
you can fill your card -- you will even get to make some choices as
there are 7 general words in each column and you only need 5.

... isn't this unfair to newer people?
... won't we be at an unfair advantage?

A. Qualifying to use some of the letterboxes, trailnames and
gatherings does not give a person an advantage in winning the game, it
simply gives them a larger subset of words to choose from for their
card (which, depending on your perspective, is not necessarily even a
good thing). The purpose of this is to reduce the possibility of
duplicate cards, not to give anyone an advantage. The words are drawn
randomly and each word has the same odds of being called as the next,
no matter which category it is from.

Thanks everyone!
