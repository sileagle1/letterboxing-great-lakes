From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-06-13 12:55:54 UTC-05:00
Our thoughts and prayers in Texas are with y'all, our friends to the north.
We had a heck of a lot of flooding spring a year ago - although our rains
came in smaller quantities over a period of several weeks. It sure has
changed our landscape though!

Let me offer a word of encouragement box-wise - we had quite a few boxes
planted near and around lakes and bodies of water. Many of them washed
away, many of them were underwater for months. We are finally sorting them
out and many of the submerged boxes have survived! Unbelievably - they've
survived! A few were soggy, but many were unharmed. We can hope y'all are
as lucky. The whole experience has been a textbook for good planting...but
that comes later.

Stay dry y'all, and take care!

--
Barefoot Lucy

there it may be a near 14 hour day drive time alone... unless there is an

We live halfway between Cedar Rapids and Iowa City about a 1/2 mile from
Lake MacBride. Pretty much all bridges are closed and LOTS of roads.
Almost impossible for us to go anywhere. We don't have water up to our
yard but the water table is so high and the ground so saturated that the
water has come UP through our sump pump twice now flooding our basement.

placing sand bags to help friends try to save their houses. I am just so
tired! Physically and mentally. Can't wait to be able to drive where
ever I want to go again! :)

I had a box ready to plant at one of my favorite bridges, Sutlif Bridge.
It was over 100 years old. Cool place. But sadly most of it is gone,
taken out by the river. :(

On a happier note... I don't care how I have to go to get there but I AM
going to BOAF! I need a break and what a better why to relax then spend
a day than with all you great people! :)
