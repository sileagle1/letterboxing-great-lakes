I first heard about letterboxing on NPR, I'm pretty sure it was on the
Sunday morning show. They were talking to someone who had found a
letterbox on the Mall in Washington, and I remember being shocked that
something like that could be someplace that would be that security
minded! It took me quite awhile to try letterboxing because I had
heard that you were supposed to have a homemade stamp, and my artistic
abilities are fairly minimal. I really wanted to try it and kept
thinking about it, but was stopped for want of a stamp! I finally
found someone who made my stamp for me, and that started me off and
running. (I just searched on NPR's website and the story I heard was
April 10, 2004 and I started letterboxing August of 2005. An entire
year and a half that I missed for want of a stamp!)
The clues update: I've made a couple minor changes to my clues for
Channel Cat and Bald Eagle Days. The directions to the boxes are
unchanged, I corrected a typo on one of the highways to the park on
Channel Cat and clarified info on Cassville on Bald Eagle Days. There
is a Town Pump restaurant that I mentioned and people had stopped at
the Town Pump gas station. They must have thought that I was out of my
mind recommending the sandwiches there! The gas station does have soft
serve ice cream, so that probably added to the confusion.
