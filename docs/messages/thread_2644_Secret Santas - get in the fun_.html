Christmas in July Secret Santas 2006

This event was inspired by the great bingo booty prizes. We are a very impressive and
generous bunch. Christmas in July is open to all members of the LbGLK group; in order to
get presents, you have to give presents for this to work.

My experience with Secret Santas is varied. In one case, we began giving small gifts on a
daily basis for one week, commencing on St. Nicholas Day. In another, it was just one BIG
gift, on St. Nicolas Day.

Deadline for sign-up is June 23rd.

I don't want this to get out-of-control and prevent some from participating so I am
making a decision to put dollar-limit on the total cost. After you sign-up, please email me
your mailing address (will be forwarded only to your Secret Santa(s)) and indicate, to me
alone, the total amount you would be willing to spend (don't forget postage costs.)

I am toying with a few scenarios:

1. One gift exchange each boxer receives one package from one other boxer (to be
received in July).
2. Weekly exchange For weeks ending July 9th, 16th, 23rd, and 30th. (4 weeks)
surprise the same boxer with 4 small gifts.
3. Same as option #2, but each week the boxer will send a small gift to a new boxer (4
separate boxers could be same gift.)

I will want to select an option quickly, so that participants have time to plan and create
something special. (Aside will someone send me the information to make those velcro-
pockets? I want to know the extra allowance you have for stamps.)

The database has been created and I started with myself as an example. If you are a team
and want to participate individually, please use a separate line for each team member. (I
think Rainy Day Rabbit would be thrilled to have a bottle of rubber scraps to play with.)

More details will be posted June 24th.

Disclaimer Elf #3 may have overlooked something important when preparing this activity.
Elf #3 reserves the right to make modifications to the original plan.

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-06-10 08:17:07 UTC-04:00
I agree with what BindleBabe is saying here about making the dollar amount
even across the board, and ideally that will be low enough that it will not
prevent people from participating. As pointed out, it is the thought, not
the gift itself. I know to some that is akin to saying it is about the hike
not the letterbox and many have strong feelings to the contrary, but life is
all about the journey folks.

Anyway, I have a concern about the comparing of gifts... The last thing I
want to see is hard feelings in our otherwise harmonious little group. I
know that people get excited and want to share the gift they got, but even
with a set dollar amount, there can be vast differences. For instance, when
I have in the past participated in Secret Santa, I have made the majority of
the gifts that I give. While I stay within the set monetary amount for the
supplies that I need to purchase for the project, I generally do not count
the value of supplies I have on hand (unless they are significant) or my
time. The finished item generally would not fall within the specified
dollar amount if a person were to go out and purchase it. As an example --
a few years ago the crochet eyelash yarn scarves were very popular. You
could easily whip one of these things up for a few bucks worth of yarn and a
couple evenings of your time. While the yarn would fall into the set
monetary value, the finished scarf would be significantly more if a person
were to purchase it at a craft fair, etc. In other situations, items are
found on sale or clearance at significant savings over the original price
and even though it appears to be a more costly item, the actual amount the
giver spent was within the set limit. To me this is part of the fun of the
Secret Santa thing in the first place (actually any gift-giving venture) --
matching the gift to the person and getting the most for my money while
doing it. So you see, you really cannot make comparisons between items
people receive and I think people need to understand that from the outset.

SpringChick

From: speedsquare_lbxr (ruhlette@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-06-10 14:34:01 UTC
Bindle Babe - removed duplicate entry.

While I have some time, let me add my thoughts from the comments thus far:

I am very concerned about the equality issues. That is why I asked for opinions on a
spending cap. I was thinking, ten to twenty dollars (total activity max), whichever option is
decided. I entered my name into the database for an example, but I really should not be
participating. Life is really getting in the way right now, and the last thing I should be
worried about is letterboxing. I went ahead and removed my name. I would have
probably made my gift item(s). I agree with SpringChick about the value of time and
materials on hand.

The one thing I would like to convey about "equal value."

Equal value is in the eye of the beholder. To me, a gift is something you give, or receive,
with no expectation of reciprocity. I see this all the time, and I wish more people would
challenge it. I spend a lot of time planning my gift-giving. Last Christmas Aunt
speedsquare did not go out and spend 30-50 dollars on each nephew and niece. She
wrapped up the great-looking LbGLK patch and a certificate to each of them. The
certificate entitled the bearer of one full day of outdoor activity with their aunt. The older
kids looked it over, said "cool, a patch," then tossed it aside for the next gift to be opened.
This reaction is no different than the years before when we spent much more money on
them. The youngest, 5-years old, really loved the gift and the certificate. Of course, we
still have to spend the day with them (they haven't picked a day yet), and it will probably
cost a lot in gas to visit them this year, but it's the thought that counts.

If someone is concerned about not getting an item of equal value, then maybe this activity
isn't for them. Or we rename it the "booty exchange."

Keeping the dollar value low, say under $5, means that everyone will need to be more
creative. I was thinking, how cool it would be if someone were to send me a postcard each
week from an area he/she went boxing and the postcard included an image from a
favorite letterbox that day. Kind of a "wish you were here" type of gift. Anticipation of
something in the mailbox is the best part for me. And I would treasure that far more than
a store-bought item costing far more.

Here's a thought. Do we really need to tell everyone about the gifts we send/receive? The
Bingo game was different in that it was a game of friendly competition. I don't liken this
activity as a game. I think we need to be considerate with each others' feelings. So I
would not be in favor of publicly discussing "what we got for Christmas."

Please continue your comments. I will be the first to admit I haven't thought of everything
yet. See that disclaimer.

~speedsquare

From: Carol Osada (cjosada@hotmail.com) | Date: 2006-06-10 23:53:38 UTC-05:00
There sure is a lot of talk about $$$ and the Secret Santa idea. The best gifts don't cost much at all. Why is it that we want to regulate the cost of the gifts to assure that it's a fair and even gift exchange? Life isn't always fair or even. Some things can't and shouldn't be regulated.

Some of the best gifts I've received don't have much of a $ value at all. I don't knit, so I don't have any of those fancy yarns that some letterboxers use to tie their logbook pages together. So when someone recently sent me an assortment of yarns (a couple yards of each color tied onto pieces of cardboard), it was the best gift in the world in my eyes!!! If you tried to figure out the cost, I'm sure it wouldn't come to much at all. It's the thought that counts. It's about surprises, and sharing, and thinking about the other person, and the real spirit of Christmas and Ho Ho Ho! A post card says I'm thinking of you, but it only costs less than 50 cents to buy a card and then mail it. Gifts that are hand made are more personal too.

Or, we don't have to do Secret Santas....we can just send out random acts of kindness with no strings attached.

Just me thoughts tonight. I know that everyone has their own opinion of this subject.
