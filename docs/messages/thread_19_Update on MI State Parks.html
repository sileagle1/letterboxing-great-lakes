Update on MI State Parks

From: Deb (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-03-25 10:17:08 UTC
The MI geocaching steering committe met with the DNR yesterday tp
try and change the new policy. Here is the post that was put up at
MiGO after the meeting:

"We have liftoff I met with my contact for Parks & Rec this
afternoon, and had a very pleasent and productive meeting. He
enlightened me as to the rationale for the Guidelines with respect
to both the fee structure and the time period involved.

The concern with respect to a cache's limited lifetime, is the
creation of social, or "spur" trails. The Parks & Rec agency has
been mandated to both provide recreation areas, and also be good
stewards of the land. My contact is genuinely trying to meet both
(at times opposite) obligations. I assured him that we geocachers
also wish to be good stewards of the land, and that when a spur
trail has been created, the cache owner usually gets a pretty quick
update from the logs. So we have agreed that a cache may stay in
place for up to 1 year, providing that is moved (without having to
pay another fee) every 4 months, (or sooner if the Unit Mngr decides
that a spur trail has been created). I have pledged the support of
not only the Board of Directors, but of every member of our great
organization, in being environmentally, and historically sensitive.

The fee for placing a cache will remain at $35, but will be good for
1 year. Also, it is possible for one use permit to cover more than
one cache (details to be worked out) It should be noted here that
the Park & Rec bureau has further been mandated by the State of
Michigan to be entirely self-supporting! This mandate does not
affect the other agencies of the DNR, only the Parks & Rec! As a
result of this, our new friends at Park & Rec have lost $9 million
in funding from the state. In other words, the Park & Rec agency
recieves NO tax payer, or other dollars from the state of Michigan.
Therefore, the only way the Parks & Rec can recieve any money is
through stickers, parking, and Use Permits. By the way, Use Permits
are not limited to only events in the traditional definition of the
word. This is how the Parks & Rec is structured to recieve any
money.

Having agreed to this set of revisions, my contact is going to run
it through his Mngmt staff, and then publish the revised guidelines.
As soon as we have them, we will be able to publish them as well."

- - - - - -

So the short of it -- still the $35 fee per placement, but that is
now good for 1 a 1 year placement, although you still need to
relocate the box every 3 months.

Do they really think anyone is going to pay this?

Seems like they are actually standing to lost money this way. For
one, they need somebody to deal with the permits and fees and
paperwork, and to routinely check out the boxes in the park, whereas
now, they just go about peacefully co-existing. Seocndly, if I
planted a box (for free) and myself plus only 5 other letterboxers
paid the daily usage of $6 for that, they would net $36 without
having to hire anyone to do anything. By instating the charge, I
pull all of my boxes and they get nothing.

Anyway, I know... I am preaching to the choir here. Just wanted to
pass along the latest.

On a more positive note... I got another e-mail from my letterboxer
friend who works at the State Park here and apparently although
there is a policy, there is room for individual decision-making on a
per park basis and if the ranger decices to, he can look the other
way. Perhaps that is why I have not received the official "come get
your boxes or be prepared to pay" notice.

Deb,
Thanks for the update.
I agree with your conclusions.
And I really do understand the plight of all agencies of state
government -- they are having to make do with much less and they
naturaly turn to ANY revenue source that looks viable.
BUT, this does seem short-sighted. The administrative costs to
enforce this kind of measure will be significant. And the amount of
money raised will be minimal. If some calculation was done based on
the number of boxes and caches in place and an assumed reveue stream
was derived, it certainly will not translate into automatic
compliance and dollars.
I have eight boxes on state land. Five of them have had several
visitors from out of state visit them. Now, can I say that none of
them would have purchased daily permits had it not been for my
letterboxes? No. But I suspect that the visitors' fees would outweigh
the permit receipts.

Now, on the issue of 'social' trails. Yup, no question, it happens.
It happens with or without geocaches or letterboxes and, no doubt,
the caches and boxes do contribute to the problem. But that's also
part of useage and wear and tear that occurs when humans enter
nature. There is a series of boxes in west Michigan (NOT on state
land) where the 'boxer respected the park rules and kept all boxes
within a couple feet of the trail. It worked. No harm, no foul.

I'm sad and sorry that we've come to this juncture.

As of yesterday I've marked all of my clues for boxes on state land.
The notes indicate that the boxes will be withdrawn before June, 2004
and I advise potential seekers to email me prior to their search to
determine if the boxes are still in place.

I'm sure I'll purchase a State Park pass again this year and continue
to use the parks; it just won't be as often.

Thanks to MIGO for the progress they/you made.

azobox

I also think the people of this state would agree that we should
