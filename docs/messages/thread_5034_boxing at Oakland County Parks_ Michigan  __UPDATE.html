From: heather (heatherstreets@comcast.net) | Date: 2008-05-12 16:25:29 UTC
Even though, I didn't get a response from the original post, I am
going to update what happened at the meeting.

I met with Derenda and she explained they have been talking about
letterboxing for 1-1/2 years. They want to change the
designated "geocaching trailways" to include letterboxing.
So in addition to being a member of the michigan geocaching
organization, they will accept members of atlasquest.org or LbNA.

She asked about a board of directors, I think Geocaching Michigan
has one. I told her I wasn't aware that letterboxing had any.

When I asked about temprorary boxes (those planted for events) she
told me that those boxes could be planted for the day outside the
designated trailways.

They will add something to their website, just as they have for
geocaching, about letterboxing. She will do her research and
include the LB code of conduct. I explained to her that I think
letterboxing is less invasive to nature than geocaching. It was my
understanding that GPS can only get within 30ft. Whereas
letterboxes give directions that are right to the box. She did
mention that geocacher tend to make their own trails through the
woods.

She stated they haven't gotten the response that they expected to
their geocaching program. She also gave me a letterbox that was
turned in about 6 months ago.

Thanks,
Spartanfans
