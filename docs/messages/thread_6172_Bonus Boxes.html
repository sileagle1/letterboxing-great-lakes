Bonus Boxes

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2009-11-21 04:23:09 UTC

It always surprises me when I see bonus boxes listed on web sites. They
were originally intended to be surprises and only for those that were
alert enough to pay attention to the letterbox & logbook where the bonus
box clues might be located. Here are the definitions posted on
letterboxing.org and AQ:

LBNA

bonus box - A letterbox with a clue that is not published, but rather
hidden inside another letterbox. Usually a bonus box will be hidden near
the host letterbox.

AtlasQuest

bonus box .....It's considered bad form to tell others where the clue
for a bonus box is.

From: FungusWoman (krank@ameritech.net) | Date: 2009-11-21 05:11:26 UTC
I'm guessing you are referring to the three New Orleans bonus boxes. Perhaps that isn't the correct terminology in this case. When I heard that Shorty and Pitties planted their series of stamps and I had three carvings here doing nothing in particular, I offered them as additional stamps once the clues were already posted and in place. I obviously can't speak for them, but my guess is they listed them just so finds can be recorded. I don't mind one way or the other since I don't keep track of my stats, but yeah, it is nice to hear when people are out to find them. I suppose in that sense they could be listed just as mystery boxes without linking them to the series and without listing where the clues are. So as they stand now, in the truest sense of the word, you're right, they shouldn't be listed as "bonus boxes". Maybe an "appendix" to the series!? (-:

--klk/fw
