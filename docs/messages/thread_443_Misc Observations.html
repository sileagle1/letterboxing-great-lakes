Observation #1
It was time for some quarterly "box maintenance" this month. Some were easy
to do since we accompanied potentially new converts as they found their
first boxes (some that we had hid). Of these two, both were in fine shape,
but one was totally exposed sitting NEXT to the tree it was supposed to be
inside. We rehid it. Within the week my nephew & his fianc reported that
another box of ours was open and contents scattered. They managed to find
everything, cleaned and dried the stamp and box and returned to its spot.
The logbook was quite wet and damaged, so they took it with them to return
to us. We went back within the week to put a new logbook in the box. When
we checked on another of our boxes, we found it was cracked, open and
scattered. Once again all the pieces were nearby and luckily the logbook
was only slightly damp in a corner. We brought this one home to repair and
then returned everything with double-bagging.

Moral of the story?
1. I highly encourage everyone to do some periodic box maintenance. If you
catch a problem early enough you can probably resolve it before the entire
box is lost.
2. Double-bagging logbooks is our new standard.
3. We really appreciate an email from a finder to let us know the status of
a box. If it is in good shape then we know we don't need to spend time
checking on it. If it's not, then we can address the problem ASAP.


Observation #2
The TIME article has spurred the interest of at least some people in
Wisconsin. I received an email from someone to let us know they had read
the article in TIME and then found the box we planted in Madison recently.
They were the first finders. We engaged in an email correspondence and I
tried to give him some additional tips and places to look for more info on
LB (including what to do with hitchhikers). They (family with 2 kids)
sounded liked they were already hooked and they were making plans to look
for more in their area.


Observation #3
We're planning an LB trip to Illinois next weekend and it was interesting to
see that one of their state parks actively promotes letterboxing:
http://www.starvedrocklodge.com/lbox.html This is quite different than the
attitude of some other state and national parks. We're hoping that the
Starved Rock boxes are actually in place and haven't disappeared due to the
publicity (or due to the tornado in April). We'll let you know next week...
