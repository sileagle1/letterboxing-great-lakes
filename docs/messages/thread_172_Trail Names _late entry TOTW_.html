I confess: our trail names were chosen unilaterally by myself ...
Bindle Bum had no input into what his trail name would be. (grin)

I stumbled on letterboxing while surfing the net one night, link
hopping. I think it began with an image search on google, but I'm
not even sure of that.

I was instantly captivated by the idea and within hours had carved
my first stamp. LOL ... hacked up an erase with a kitchen knife at
2am! Went to Michael's the next morning to purchase "the real
stuff" and created our signature stamp.

I didn't know ANYTHING about signature stamps at this point. I
hadn't even been out to look for letterboxes yet. (grin) I thought
our signature stamp had to be something we could both share. LOL!
So, I tried to come up with initials for the stamp which could apply
to either or both of us.

Background: we're passionate about sailing and inordinately fond of
our 'good old boat' Bindlestiff. You can see where this is going,
can't you? (grin)

Deeper background: Bindlestiff is a synonym for hobo or gypsy. My
spouse is former military ... we once moved 9 times in a five-year
period.

OK, so the initial "B" and a sailboat image were going to be a part
of the signature stamp. From there I just tried to come up with
something that would go with "Bindle" and fit both/either of us.
So, it became a double "BB" (Bindle Babe and Bindle Bum) a sailboat,
and a 'bindle' aka: hobo bundle-on-a-stick.

Note: as with many things on the internet; Bindle "Babe" and
Bindle "Bum" have NOTHING what so ever to do with how we look in
real life. ROFL!!

The first one I carved, I was still working in "reverse image" mode.
(ie: I carved out the lines and left the background as the part that
stamped) Once I felt more comfortable with carving, I carved it the
other-way around. I also learned that our stamp is a little on the
large side, so I carved a tiny sailboat, which we use as
our 'alternate' signature stamp.

Enjoy!
