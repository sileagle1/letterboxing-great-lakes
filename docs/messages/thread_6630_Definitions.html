Definitions

From: Irish (irishbain@rocketmail.com) | Date: 2010-06-07 05:41:28 UTC
We all know about slackboxing. At LBCon, we decided to coin "Factory Boxing", where one person fetches back to the enormous group and we assembly line the components of the box(s) down the chain.

After GLKG 2010, I propose we add "social boxing". This does not refer to the massive exchange session at the shelter, but rather to the practice of finding boxes by following the much trampled and muddy path left by the previous 50 boxers as they slog through the underbrush!

At some point, we actually started cheering boxes that had no logbooks :)

Ah yes, the social trails helped us today. We didn't think we had gotten to
the right spot for a box yet, but I spotted a trail and told MM I was going
to check it out. He said "you don't even know what you're looking for!". I
thought we might find an unpublished box or bonus box, but it turned out to
be the very same box we WERE looking for, but thought was further down the
trail. In other areas, if we missed some landmarks due to the more
jungle-like conditions and ended up backtracking, we ended up trying to
notice social trails as much, or more, as we tried to spot the landmarks
mentioned in the clues.
