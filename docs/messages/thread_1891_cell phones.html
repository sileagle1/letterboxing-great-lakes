From: Kathi LK Krankoski (krank@ameritech.net) | Date: 2005-10-13 10:18:25 UTC-05:00
Terrain plays a big role. Out in the hills of Virginia this past summer,
our Verizon phones worked sporadically. We'd have to drive around to find a
spot with service, find one, and then move a few feet away and lose the
call. Back home though, I find that I get poor reception in my own kitchen
(perhaps the microwave or other electronic interference?) but fine elsewhere
in the house. Honestly, though, I think Verizon was much better a year or
so ago - or maybe the new phones we got this spring aren't as good as the
old ones? So many factors to take into consideration - hard to say for
sure.



--klk/FungusWoman
