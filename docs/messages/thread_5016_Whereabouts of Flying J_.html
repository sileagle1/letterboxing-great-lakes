Yes, I know of Flying J, but I've never met her. I use to email to her. She lives in the Green Oaks area near Libertyville, IL. She placed many boxes in the area and I sought them out when I first started letterboxing. But I don't think she is active any longer. Her daughter is a Girl Scout in one of the troops in my area. I can contact Flying J through her daughter's troop leader. She has several great boxes, but if they need some TLC, maybe she will tell us what her plans are. I especially love her one letterbox clue about starting where it looks like a space ship landed - it REALLY looks like that too!

We used to be in contact with her too via occasional email but it's probably
been something like a couple of years now since "talking" to her. Will be
in Long Grove next Friday so if her Buffalo Grove Winnie-the-Poo box needs
any attention, I can check on it if she wants and replace baggies or
container, assuming the stamp at least is still there. IIRC, she was
putting the container itself inside a gallon size ziplock which really isn't
the best thing given our weather and where she was planting the box. But
given the snow & rains we've had, that box might have washed away
completely.

I'd kind of be surprised if her Virgo box was still there. At least from
what I understand, the Ryerson Woods folks took a sudden dislike to boxes
and caches and were out to get rid of 'em either last year or maybe it was
even 2006. For awhile, Ryerson Woods had quite a few letterboxes planted
there. The Virgo stamp was a really nice stamp. I can stop up there and
check on that one too if she wants and do maintenance if it's still there.

I think those were the only two of hers that we ever had a chance to go
searching for.
