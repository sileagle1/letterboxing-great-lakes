Moving old box to a new location......

From: azobox (jparkerg@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-09-02 20:10:09 UTC
Here's a question for you:
I have a box or two which I removed from their previous locations due
to state park restrictions. I'd like to re-plant the boxes elsewhere.
In one case I altered the stamp slightly -- now it's a "new" stamp(?)
So, in a new location with new clues, is it a "new" box? (This is not
important from a "PFX" perspective).
I ask mostly from the point of view of the seeker. Since some finders
would be disappointed to find the box for a second time, I want to
make sure that it's known to be "so-and-so" box, altered/replanted.
Given all that, would you include the original log book, with the
stamps of those who found it in its previous location? Or would you
make a new logbook, including its history when placing in a new
location?

Just curious what some of you old hands -- okay, "experienced
letterboxers" -- might suggest.


azobox


Re: [LbGLK] Moving old box to a new location......

From: Delirium (donaldson_home@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-09-02 18:02:06 UTC-07:00
Personally, one of my favorite things about finding a box is seeing who's been there before me. In which case, I advocate keeping the original logbook, but writing a note in the first clean page explaining the nature of the replant- date and location of original plant, etc.
However, in the case of "Freebird", which was a stamp I created for the Gathering, I created a new logbook when he was re-planted back home, simply because I wanted a record of that snapshot in time.

Cheers,
Del
