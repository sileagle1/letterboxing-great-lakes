Double D,

I have been thinking of your problem ever since you Emailed it. I was
thinking of glue scrap pieces of eraser, any kind of filler at all, etc.

Nothing really came up. Bali, I am intrigued at your suggestion and think it
maybe possible. I was just impressed someone responded with something that
may work.

I was standing in the shower thinking of Bali's great suggestion when the
great idea hit me (when else other than in the shower?)

Don't do anything to the stamp at all. That is where we all think the
problem is, but its not, the problem will be in the image you stamp ~ It is THERE
that needs to be fixed and not the stamp itself.

Just have a fine tipped marker that doesn't bleed in the color you are going
to use (Creative Memories have a GREAT set that may or may not be available
individually). After you stamp in just have your fine tipped marker and draw
a line in the image where the line should be and viola.

No extra clutting/glueing/headaches and the problem is solved . . . hope its
not to late (I would have recarved the whole image by now).
