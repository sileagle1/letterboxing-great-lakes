Re: [LbGLK] Re: All Quiet on the LbGLK Front - Ipod thread

From: Coffee Beans (coffeebeans06@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-12-15 10:47:20 UTC-06:00
I also have the 4th gen 20 gig color iPod. I mainly use mine when I'm
analyzing data at work, so I can tune out my annoying co-workers! ;)
But occassionally I hook it up to speakers at home too. I mainly just
like having all of my music in one place, and I use the shuffle feature
a lot.

Since the newer, video model got released though, I don't think you can
buy the older iPod versions unless you can find them used on eBay or
something (not recommended). But Atom just got a 30 gig video iPod a
month or so ago, and he likes it. Since you're mainly just looking to
hook it up at home, the 30 or 60 gig one is probably your best bet. The
nano and shuffle models are mainly for the convenience of being teeny
tiny and easier to carry around.

Some people complain about their iPods breaking or crashing, but the
only firsthand knowledge I have of that happening is from my old
roommate who was constantly dropping hers on the ground and leaving it
in her bag/purse which she would casually throw on the floor. :P Within
the first year though, she returned hers at least 2 (maybe 3) times and
got replacements each time from Apple with their 1 year warranty. I've
had mine for a year and a half, and it did freeze up once earlier on for
no apparent reason, but I plugged it into the wall charger and it reset
itself. *shrug*

Hope that helps!
Coffee Beans

Re: [LbGLK] Re: All Quiet on the LbGLK Front - Ipod thread

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-12-15 21:33:50 UTC-05:00
Oh, don't get me going on earphones... I have the hardest time keeping the darn things in my ears, even though I do that twisted contortionistic move where you reach behind your head and tug on the top of the opposite ear as you insert the earpiece with your free hand.

Shortly after getting my first iPod, I took the advice of Azobox and OldShoe, both of whom love their Shure earphones, and paid the bucks for my own pair of these babies. I will admit that the sound is excellent and they even stay in my ears once I get them positioned in there good, but they are the most uncomfortable things... I feel like I am walking around with a square-end steel beam in my ear, and they hurt. I thought I might get used to them if I could just grin and bear it for a while... well I'm still bearing it (but no longer grinning).

So on the advice of a co-worker, I got myself a pair of Sennheiser "twist-to-fit" headphones. Again the sound quality is excellent and these are much more comfortable, but I can't for the life of me get the "twist-to-fit" thing right -- either I twist too far or not far enough and they fall out if I do anything even remotely active.

I now have tried about 6 different pair of headphones and still am searching for the perfect ones. The closest I have come is a relatively cheap pair of Sony earphones that have decent sound (although not as rich as the Shures or Sennheisers), are comfortable enough (as comfortable as anything can be while shoved in your ear canal) and they even stay in my ears -- so what's the catch? They have the funkiest lopsided wire in which one side is longer than the other and the short side is so short that the weight of the iPod is constantly pulling the earpiece out.

Speaking of the contortionistic move that is considered the *proper* way to insert earphones... I never noticed people doing this until I got an iPod. On my recent trip to Rhode Island I saw several people in the airport and on the plane doing the reach behind your head move...

SpringChick
