I learned about letterboxing at a bookcrossing meeting (see
www.bookcrossing.com for more info). One of the ladies --aimlesst--
mentioned that she'd long wanted to start this hobby. Three of us
met and so began our informal team/individual boxing in the
Milwaukee area. Actually, the others were boxing....I didn't search
again for almost a year! However, with a little help from my
friends, I've found about a dozen boxes and am a true convert.

While camping on Madeline Island (in Lake Superior) in August I
scouted out some sites and plan to place a series of boxes when we
return next summer. As an adult fan of Harry Potter, I am planning
a HP series of boxes for the bicycle trail that passes behind my
house....realistically they probably won't be placed before late
spring, 2007, but I'm already enjoying the planning for the clues
and the stamps.

I am a special education teacher, a single parent, a knitter and
spinner, and love to read. The art and exercise aspects of this
hobby appeal to me. I am also a great fan of British folk music and
literature, so hope to combine letterboxing with a trip to England
to celebrate my 50th birthday in a few years.
