Yet another Geocacher issue...

From: duncan53211 (duncan53211@yahoo.com) | Date: 2009-04-28 01:52:48 UTC
I just got an email from a geocacher who was scouting out hiding locations in High Cliff SP (WI) for a Geocache gather. He wanted to turn the three letterboxes of antiquity there into geocache/letterbox hybrids and post them on geocaching.com. He was nice enough to give me a status report on the boxes, but he wasn't really asking for my permission to do this. He felt he had as much right as I did to the ownership of the boxes for some reason. Needless to say, I told him quite clearly to leave these boxes out of anything having to do with geocaching and that I am the rightful owner of the boxes (having taken them over from Ezpernaza with their permission just before they left the letterboxing scene).

I am mostly posting this here to let those of you with boxes in the Winnebago Lake area to watch out, since at least one geocacher up there thinks it is okay to take over another person's boxes. I get the impression that he thinks a box is "abandoned" if it has not been logged into for about 6 months (which is pretty typical for the winter months if you think about it...).

Dragon
