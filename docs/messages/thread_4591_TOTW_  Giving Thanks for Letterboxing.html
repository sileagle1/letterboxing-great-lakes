nice and caring folks.

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2007-11-30 06:17:52 UTC-05:00
Good TOTW! Like you, I am thankful for letterboxing because of the great places it has taken me and even more so, the wonderful people I have met and become friends with from all across the country. In addition, I am thankful that letterboxing has opened up to me a whole new art form (carving) that I had not previously given much thought to and certainly never tried. These days I carve more for non-letterbox projects than for letterboxes and although I still treasure and use my pre-carving rubber stamp collection, I seldom buy new rubber stamps, rather just adapt ideas I see to something I can carve myself.

I am also thankful for letterboxing because in a small way it has helped me to share something I have long held dear -- a love of the trail, the woods, the forest. Since high school I have been a hiker and have long cherished a quiet afternoon walking through the woods on a narrow trail, just listening and observing the world around me -- almost like a secret place at times, providing an escape from the demands and immediacy of life and a place where one can truly just *be.* But it is difficult to convey this sense of magic in words; it really is something that people must experience themselves and become emotionally connected with to understand. Letterboxing has been a catalyst in sharing this with other people as it gives them a reason to get out there (the letterbox) and along the way, they come to understand the power of a simple walk through the woods.

SpringChick

"In every walk with nature one receives far more than he seeks." -- John Muir
