What a Great Week

From: beckers410 (Mathw613@aol.com) | Date: 2009-02-14 01:49:15 UTC
This week has been one of the best work weeks of my life. Don't get
me wrong, I love my job and have many good weeks. However, when a get
to take an extra long route and snag a box on the way -- well, that
makes a great day.

This week I had 4 great days!!

Monday was nice and warm. I decided to check on a couple of my
boxes "A Scramble in the Dark" series in Milan -- last logged find
was WiHi & MM in October, 2007.

It was sureal to walk without a coat on and feel warm. We had a huge
wind storm this past summer and trees had fallen over the path... it
was some bushwacking time. I made it to where I thought the box
was... turned out I was off a few feet. It turns out that the wind
knocked over a tree, that dominoed into another tree.... then another
tree. 3 trees down and my box was sitting pretty in a sweet spot
right in the middle of all the carnage.

On to box #2. The dead log it was hidden in was 100% decayed and
gone. My box was just sitting there right in the open.... nothing
left of the log at all. Everything was safe and dry and ready to go
with this series.

TUESDAY I got to drive to Earlville . . . east of Mendota. I picked
up Maude's contribution to a Teddy Bears Picnic and attempted
Questar's " Kakusha" no luck on that one. This day was VERY warm and
very muddy.... I left a mess!!

Wednesday I drove up to Dubuque and hiked 3.5 miles and planted a new
box, "A Portrait of Julien Dubuque". This was a struggle with all the
snow, ice & mud but I got it in place.... not quite the 4 miles round
trip I was hoping for, but I was happy with 3.5 miles round trip.

Thursday -- nothing :-(

Friday -- I hiked 4.5 miles in Dixon, IL and scouted out tons of
hiding spots for future boxes. The trail was paved and had markers
telling you how far you walked.... I made it 2.25 miles before time
told me to double back. The trail was 3.0 miles total so I fell .75
miles short of my endevour.
