I found an Eastport messenger bag at Walmart for about $12. It has
one section with all sorts of pouches that I can put our markers and
personal stamps in (the flap of the bag folds over this). Plus it
has a pouch for a water bottle and 5-6 different zippered pockets to
stash various things--I just have to remember which pocket to look
in! It's quite a bit bigger(wider) than my old backpack--but now I
have plenty of room for a jacket, snacks for everyone, camera,
binoculars and bird books. Not exactly traveling light :~) Now I
just have to get used to the solo strap of the messenger bag.

Just a note on boxing with kids. A year or so ago, I got smaller
backpacks for each of the kids--enough room for their logbook,
snacks, jacket, etc.

From: PennyPenny (PennyPenny@cablespeed.com) | Date: 2007-04-30 09:45:36 UTC-04:00
Hi there...

How old are your kids? I would suggest that you get each of them a
backpack of their own!

Many 2-year-olds, even, are eager to act like "a big kid" and carry
their own pack. There are many kids packs out there: My son had a
"Thomas The Tank Engine" pack that was small enough to fit him at age
2.5 and he carried his bottle of water and some Kleenex packs in it
when we went on hikes! Now he's 5, and he still uses the same pack
but likes to use his older brother's backpack too (it looks like a
"big kid's" backback, he says!) and carries any number of things -- I
do however keep it limited to the light stuff. My older boys can
carry much more, of course. Spread out among all of us, no one ever
has too heavy a load and it is lots of fun to all help out, too!

I myself use a backpack that has lots of outside pockets and two big
interior pockets. I find I use ALL the outside pockets, since they
are so easy to access while on the trail: Keys, cell phone, mini-
binoculars, bird seed, and two bottles of water make my list of
"Outside Pocket Stuff." Also I'll roll up any current clues I'm using
and stick them in an outside pocket.

The rest of my stuff goes inside my pack: Letterboxing journal, log-
book, and stamps and ink. I also have a big wet-bag in my backpack
(it came with the pack, but a 2.5 gallon zip lock would serve the
same purpose) that we use mostly for we garbage of all sorts; wipes,
juice boxes, wet sox, etc. until we can properly dispose of the stuff.

Hope some of this helps when you're making your pack selection!

PennyPenny
South Lyon, MI
