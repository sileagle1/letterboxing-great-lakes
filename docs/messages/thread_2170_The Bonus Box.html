The Bonus Box

From: Dick and Anne (rhesse34@hotmail.com) | Date: 2006-01-13 15:47:05 UTC
Last fall, while checking our LBs, we discovered that the tree
hiding the second box for the Pilgrimage of Eight Bridges had
finished falling over and was no longer usable. So, we collected the
box and decided to come back later and search out a new hiding
place.
Yesterday we had the chance to go looking. It took awhile but we
did find a good spot. Just to be sure, we continued searching as we
worked our way back to the car. While checking out a pile of rocks
we found a possible cleft... with (soggy but intact) a
LETTERBOX! "Boyhood Adventures #3, Adventures With Dad", placed by
Birkie Bob, a legacy if not the first LBer in the area. Boyhood
Adventures #1 and #2 are still active, thanks to Dragon's TLC, but we
have not heard a whisper about #3. So, Dragon, do you have any
clues? Or clues to clues?
The other funny thing is that it's been found eight times by
Geocachers (leaving all manner of trinkets and the comment, "Found
only a stamp..."). But it has never been found by a Letterboxer, so
I guess we can claim first finders. There's no date as to when it
was placed, but the first entry in the logbook is 3/24/03. The last
is 11/25/04. A couple of the notes say a geocache is 60 to 70 feet
to the west (we'll have to have a look).
The stamp suggests the old toboggan slide which was not far away
but has been gone for years.
Anyway, another tale which proves that letterboxing can be stranger
than fiction.
Merlin

Oh, this is so cool to find a letterbox that was virtually unknown -- like a mystery box without any clues at all!

I remember a couple of years ago when we were headed to the U.P., Ruthann Zaroff aka Mirkwood, a legacy MI boxer, told me of 2 boxes she had placed and never written clues for in the areas we were headed. She gave me the locations as best as she could remember -- one was quite precise and in a somewhat public area and we quickly realized it was no longer there. The other was more obscure and we must have searched at least 3-4 hours in the area she had indicated, bound and determined to find that box. It would have been so cool to find this box, never listed, never found, and a Mirkwood carving to boot! But alas, we ended up walking away without it. It may still be there...

What a find for you!

SpringChick

VERY COOL! This is now at least the 2nd time you guys have found a box with
unpublished clues and this is a historic one! I looked in the "wayback
machine" at old versions of the letterboxing.org site. You're right that he
was one of the first planters in the area. Another BOB placed the the Eagle
box earlier in 2001 (maybe the same Bob Marx who placed the Holy Hill box in
SEP 2001 and the Calatrava/Green One box in Oct 2001?), and later that year
Birkie Bob posted clues to the Mount Bieterman box and had this at the end
of his clues: "Placed by: Birkie Bob on 25 November, 2001 (My first!)".
Birkie Bob placed Boyhood Adventures (2 boxes) in DEC 2001. (I'm guessing
that Bob Marx and Birkie Bob are two different people since B.B. said Mount
Bieterman was his first. However other comments by Moofie & Blaze seem to
indicate it might be the same person???) I checked several different points
in time after DEC 2001, but Boyhood Adventures always showed only 2 boxes
and I didn't see any other box that looked like a continuation. Maybe he
was like Leapin' Lizards - planting boxes, but losing the clues before they
were finalized and published! It's nice to know LL might be carrying on a
tradition!

It would be great if someone could track Birkie Bob down and get him to
write up a story to go along with the box, similar to the first two boxes.

Wisconsin Hiker
