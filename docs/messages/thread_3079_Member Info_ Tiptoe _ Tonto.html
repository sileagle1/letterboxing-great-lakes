Member Info: Tiptoe & Tonto

From: tiptoetonto (tiptoetonto@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-09-18 02:58:17 UTC
While lost in cyberspace in the fall of 2002, Tiptoe came across the
LBNA carving page and couldn't believe that there actually existed a
hobby...er world... that encircled all of her and Tonto's favorite
things. We love hiking, travel, nature, history, art and people.
Letterboxing has it all! It took us until February of 2003 to find
our first letterbox which happened to be on the Appalachian Trail...
in the snow...As posted before, I was so moved by this experience,
that I actually cried. Tonto thought it was great, but he saves his
tears for the big stuff in life...like finishing the Around the
World in 80 Days series! Just kidding!

We've found that our friends, family and ...our adult children (Yes,
that is a truthful oxymoron) think we have lost our minds. One
friend always puts on our answering machine the same following
message..."I guess you two aren't home..probably doing that stupid
match-booking thing you do.." "Match-booking? (Spring Chick, do
you think we could invent a new twist to the game?)

We letterbox whenever we get the chance. We've even fallen into the
powerful hold of letting the boxes dictate trip itineraries. This
has resulted in many shared adventures together that otherwise
would've never happened. (Sarah, of Leapin' Lizard fame, hear us on
this one!)...We are grateful for those times together even though
much of the time we are arguing with each other! (Kiss Me Kate and
Barefoot Ankh can attest to that)But, love conquers all..even
letterbox feuds! It has proven to be the best marriage therapy of
our 26 years togehter.

We aren't the swiftest boxers ..we've reached a little over 400
actual found boxes in 12 states in the four years of boxing. We are
the great procrastinators having only planted a handfull of boxes,
but those have been enjoyable as well when we hear from grateful
visitors...We have thoroughly enjoyed the Gatherings/Events where
we've collected another 100+ "non-found" assorted stamps, and the
coveted 98 exchanges. Getting to know fellow boxers has been such a
treat...It is the creative, warm, fun-loving, clever folks that make
this such a terrific hobby..and put a face behind the boxes.

FREE words of wisdom to new boxers: the tendency to go at
letterboxing full throttle at first may bring more frustration and
disappointment than if you allow yourself to experience all the
different layers of the sport a little at a time. Each box has a
unique spin...and will give you a little more understanding of the
complexities that aren't always evident from the start. Get several
finds under your belt before placing - it will pay off in the long
run. Like savoring that box of fine chocolates. (Ask Forrest Gump
about that...I'm sure he was also a letterboxer!) We still are
often pathetic clue-decipherers, box finders and box placers (think
flood plains)...always something to learn...and figure-out. But it
is such a thrill when you are right!

Only bad thing, is real life and work have this nasty way of getting
in the way! But, that is where the stealth and sneakiness that
letterboxing has instilled in us pays off...Tonto has become an
expert at making a sale or troubleshooting on his cell phone while
simultaneously proding a dead tree with a stick! Thankfully,
clients don't have a clue he is knee deep in poison ivy rather than
in a suit at some office! Oh, the joy of technology!...Now if only
I could get out of the classroom as readily!

Autumn is upon us and brings along our favorite time to box! So, if
you hear squabbling in the woods and see a couple that look like
Jack Sprat and his wife...ask for an exchange...and lend a hand at
finding that "stupid match-book!"
Tiptoe & Tonto
