I have a question for all those letterboxers on this
list involved with girl scouting too. Over the
weekend we received an email from a Brownie Troop
Leader. The troop is mostly 2nd graders, she didn't
say how large, but she said the troop had learned
about letterboxing from a Connecticut troop they're
pen pals with and decided to try it themselves. They
chose one of our boxes for their first hunt and
unfortunately didn't find it, and I'm not sure what
exactly happened but evidently someone who lived by
the park called the police on them!

Anyway, having been a Girl Scout myself, I want to
offer my letterboxing "expertise" to them and offer to
meet with the troop, maybe accompany them on a hunt
and/or help them plant a box of their own. I remember
FungasWoman saying that a letterboxing badge or
interest project had been developed, but I think that
was for the older girls. Does any one know if there's
a badge or something that these Brownies can
incorporate their letterboxing experience into? Or
does anyone have any information they've used to teach
Brownies about the finer details of letterboxing in
the past they'd be willing to share with me?

Thanks!

From: Kathi LK Krankoski (krank@ameritech.net) | Date: 2006-03-28 22:40:27 UTC-06:00
As far as I can see, GSUSA has only created the "Hi Tech Hide and Seek" IP
for the 11-17 group right now. Here is a link to the requirements:
http://www.studio2b.org/lounge/gs_stuff/ip_tech.asp



When we started placing boxes at our GS camp a few years ago, I made up a
checklist of points and concepts I felt were important for the girls to
understand to be able to successfully go from letterboxing at camp out into
the "wild" environment of the "real" letterboxing world. Since our council
at the time was known as Girl Scouts of Whispering Oaks, I carved a stamp
with the logo "LbWO". If the girls could answer all the questions, found at
least five of the private boxes at camp and one wild box, they were eligible
for membership in LbWO.



Doodle, perhaps you can use or adapt this document for the younger girls
(maybe simplify the language a bit). I'll send it to you offlist. (If
anyone else wants a copy, just ask.)



While we are on the topic, I'll give you guys a heads up on a project that's
due to launch very shortly. A few of us have been talking between ourselves
about starting a yahoo group for Girl Scout leaders/advisors/volunteers. I
know there are quite a few of you that already belong to this group. We're
going to make it a private group not listed in the yahoo directory because
of Safety-Wise considerations. I'll post again when things are ready.
