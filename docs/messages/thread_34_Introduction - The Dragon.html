I began letterboxing on 8/6/2002 with Birkie Bob's "Mt. Bieterman"
LB which is near my home in SE WI. I chose that box as my first
since it is dedicated to his cross country coach at Nicolet HS and I
am a cross country coach and actually ran against Nicolet on the CC
course the box is on when I was in HS. For all you new letterboxers
out there, this box took me two tries because I was soooo smart to
read into the clues that he actually meant a "Y" in the trail rather
than a "V" and spent all my time at trail splits to no avail. Of
course, when I returned to find it with my wife to help me (the ONLY
time she's gone letterboxing unfortunately), as I was complaining
about the clues not being accurate I looked up and nearly walked
smack into the "V" in the trail! The box was soon found afterwards.
Morale - just read the clues and follow them, don't try to second
guess them. (Which I still tend to do!) And keep your mind and eyes
open!

Since I am not an artist (as some think for some reason), I took my
stamp image from some of the only artwork I've ever been able to
look nice - my Escher-like tessellations. You can see my "Dragon"
tessellation as the background of my homepage. You can see my other
tessellation art (and some of my student's art) at

http://www.kmlhs.org/faculty/TKUEHL/Precalculus/MESA/Tessellations/TK
%20Tessellation%20Home.htm

My first planted box is "Environmental Classroom" which is at the
school I teach at. It is also dedicated to cross country, as it is
on our cross country course.

I am currently F279.5 P63.5 X20 HH17 E1 and V0 (because SpringChick
never sent me my virtual stamp last spring ;-) )
Why do I have .5's? Because on my last trip down the Mississippi
from Sparta, WI I went to the "Mines of Spain" box and there was no
stamp! (Note to Geocachers - if you're going to do a
letterbox/cache hybrid, don't forget letterboxers exchange stamps!
Please include them in your hybrid box, and don't take them when
you "leave someting/take something!) I quickly made a (poor) stamp
for the box and count it as half a find and plant for now.

Much to the frustration of local WI letterboxers, I am a math
teacher, and I force lots of letterboxers to use math they haven't
used for years to find some of my boxes. But not to worry, I have
plenty of non-math boxes to find around WI and MI. Rumor had it at
our WI gathering that I'm about to get my "just desserts" in
retalliation for my math boxes from letterboxing teachers of other
disciplines!

Last summer, I took a 3 week letterboxing trip through MI. I found
most of the MI letterboxes planted at that time, and even planted
three of my own (I STILL can't believe no one beat me to planting
one in HELL!) I'm sorry I have not posted which boxes I found and
which I didn't and their conditions and the hitchhikers I moved
around. I did mean to do that. If you're interested in knowing
whether I've been to a box of yours (or found your HH), just ask and
I'll let you know.

My current goals are to:

4) Do an "Ohio" letterboxing trip during summer
5) Get to P100 and F500 by my 2-year aniversary on 8/6/2004

Hope to see you on the trail!
