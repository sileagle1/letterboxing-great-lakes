I just wanted to post something I found today on the Minnesota DNR website. I was looking at the Geocaching permit, and it has now been changed to "Geocaching/Letterboxing Application and Permit" and has separate sections for geocaches and letterboxes. Is this new? Hopefully this means that the Minnesota State Parks will now permit letterboxes!

From: artgekko_mn (artgekko@frontiernet.net) | Date: 2010-10-12 15:17:03 UTC
The forms HAVE changed! I haven't looked at them in a while (I still have a stack of the old ones), but I'm liking that you can do it electronically now - fill out the PDF form and send 'em in via e-mail - whoot!

MN State Parks have allowed letterboxes for at least four years. They just had you fill out the same form as the GCers, though it never said "letterbox" anywhere on it.

Last weekend I finally went out and got the GPS coordinates for the locations of the four boxes I had planted (and pulled) for the Spring Fling at William O'Brien. Now I can submit the forms and hopefully get them planted permanently before snowfall!

I have two more planned for Interstate as well. :)
