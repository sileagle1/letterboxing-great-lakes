leave the eyes, nose and mouth raised or the opposite.

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-05-15 11:57:03 UTC-04:00
I think it would depend on the rest of your stamp. The first way (carve out the skin and leave features) is more consistent with positive carving -- where you leaves the lines of your image and carve away everything else. The second way (carve away features and leave the skin) is more consistent with negative carving -- where you carve away the lines and leave everything else.

Doglvrs had a good point -- move the carving material and leave your tool still. Another thing that helps is to carve the inside facial features first, before carving away the outside of the face -- this will help you to have as firm a surface as possible.

SpringChick

Although I can't say that I love speedy cut, I don't think it's really
all that bad either. All of my larger stamps are speedy cut. It
isn't as durable as some other media (erasers) and therefore better
for some applications than others. Both of the signature stamps I've
made are speedy cut and although mine, which is a negative carving, is
doing ok (all those scratches and gouges it aquired before I got a tin
to carry my stamps in add character!) Aina's, which is a positive
carving, needs to be recarved soon as several parts of it have broken
off. Honestly, I've been really into erasers lately. I got a bunch
of cheap pink erasers from the dollar store and I am very much
enamored with them.
