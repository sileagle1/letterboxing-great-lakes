I tend to disagree on this point, although not enough to bother trying to persuade anyone. If you like to play your secret and perhaps subversive method, I'm not here to stop you.

For anyone who would like to play it safer (keeping the park managers on your side- they will mostly leave you alone) the "permit" method for placing a letterbox (or geocache- the same rules apply) is really very easy and can be found here: http://www.dnr.state.mn.us/geocaching/index.html
It is supposed to apply to state parks and trails.

In all fairness, the park staff do have a job in maintaining the park for everyone, and they do have every right to curtail activities that they feel might negatively impact the land. If the only reason they pull a box is 'cause you're not playing by the rules (registering) then I too think that's dumb. But as custodians of the park, they get to make those rules. That's why I think you ought to play on their terms; it's easy, it works, and as far as I know, they don't get involved once the box is planted. That's enough from me, thanks.
