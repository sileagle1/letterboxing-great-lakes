Haunted October afternoon

From: Kathi LK Krankoski (krank@ameritech.net) | Date: 2005-10-14 15:12:25 UTC-05:00
What a fabulous day for boxing! Boxer-in-TRAINing, Muddy Paws, and I set
out to do CMoon, Kookaburra, and Squeek's Haunted Trail series. Excellent
stamps, clues, and hiding places, ladies! Poor MP is pooped and covered
with burrs. We let her run loose a bit (shhh - don't tell [every other dog
in the place was leashless!]) and she made her way into the river, so her
paws did manage to get muddy (before they were just dusty). We hit Waiting
in the Shadows (picked up a HH thanks to Nature Nerd) and History Tower
(stamped in my 2005 sig stamp, since last time I was there I used my old
one).



I noticed the license plate frame on a car parked near the tower: "I love my
boxer". Of course I didn't take it to mean a dog!!



Off to pick the burrs off my sweatshirt and dog..



Thanks for a fun day!



--klk/FungusWoman

Re: Haunted October afternoon

From: tiptoetonto (tiptoetonto@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-10-17 03:06:52 UTC
For those who missed it, THE boxes to find this weekend were the
Haunted Trail series in Western Springs, IL...The weather was so
spectacular that we could've been lbing anywhere in the Chicagoland
area and it would have been fabulous...But, here we found 10, yep,
we said 10 boxes at one of CMoon's favorite haunts - Bemis Woods!
Not only were the stamps great, but we had just begun when the ever
observant Tonto spotted a woman with clues in hand and markers in
her bag! Ah Ha! he thought (rare for Tonto...just kidding) another
boxer - we introduced ourselves and found out she was "Prairie Dog",
and asked her to join us. (Which she willingly agreed....I didn't
have to beg her....amazing!)
We were only half way through the series when the astute Tonto again
spotted two ladies seated on the ground carefully coloring with
markers! We couldn't believe it! We've never run into other LBers
on the trail except at gatherings, let alone twice on the same
trail....So exchanges were made with "PGranola" and "Calliope".
We decided that the way to get everyone out to your boxes is to warn
perspective finders that the box is temporary! Sure worked for
these fun boxes!
We reiterate a big thanks to Kookaburra, Squeek and Cmoon for yet
another delightful time letterboxing!
Tiptoe & Tonto
