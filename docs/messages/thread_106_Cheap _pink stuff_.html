Oops. About laquering....

I laquer before gluing personally. They suggest using the spray
polyurethane, but after giving it a try, I think I prefer to use
brush-on. Be sure you let the stamp image on the wood dry before
you spray or brush the polyurethane on. You don't laquer the bottom
of the wood (the part to be glued to the stamp). After a number of
coats of polyurethene and after it has dried, rough up the bottom of
the wood and the top of the stamp, apply the GOOP, let sit a minute
or two, then put them together. Let the GOOP dry a while in the
open air. This helps get rid of the strong smell of the GOOP too.
