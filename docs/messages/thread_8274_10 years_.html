10 years!

From: Atom118 (Aatom118@gmail.com) | Date: 2016-01-21 07:09:08 UTC-06:00

Ten years ago today, my sister made me and, my then, girlfriend take a random excursion around town on some silly treasure hunt.  Take my word, as any good sister would do, she does not let me forget how I mocked her and how AWESOME the day turned out to be.  Letterboxing has taken us on a variety of adventures where we've see great parts of the country, had fun, and met loads of great people.  It has changed our lives.

Anyway, some of you may have (probably not) noticed, but we've been quietly retiring many of our local boxes over the last few months.  We decided to use our anniversary as a 'reboot' where we'd remove some of our older boxes, that most of you who are interested in finding have found, and replant some of the areas with some new quests.  Some of you (well probably not) may also be waiting for our annual winter series to come out.  We've decided to take the year off from our winter series to change things up. Instead, we have plans to launch 10 new series/individual boxes for our 10th anniversary, and our first series will be planted before winter ends.  We hope you enjoy our 10 for 10, as we've enjoyed finding many of your boxes over the last decade.

Atom118 of Atomic Beans

Ahhh...Congratulations!...that warms our cold hearts this morning...especially since the "compass" was an unfortunate pick for either one of us...but Tiptoe can't complain after her 4-corner victory yesterday!


We looked back to see when did we actually meet the wonderful Beans.  Unfortunately, the exchange images aren't dated, but we remember attending the "Wandering in Wonderland" event June 3, 2006 where we spied a threesome sitting on a blanket stamping into books.  Tiptoe ...well, she tiptoed over to make the exchange with one Atom 118 and two sweet girls, Coffee Beans and Fermont.  The rest is history as they say!

Upon further search, we found the November 10, 2007 "Letterboxing Wedding" stamp entries!  Then came the newly formed team, "Atomic Beans"!

Feeling in the nostalgic mood, we thumbed through all of our books until we found in September of 2010 a Winter Series, "A Good Year," with the cute pregnant Bean!  Mark and Sue Pepe came into town and a book baby shower for the Beans was in order.  Golly, how time flies!  

10 years of boxing for you!  Since we are only 39 now, this means that Tiptoe and Tonto were only 29 in letterboxing years back then!  Letterboxing does have a way of changing our lives.  It has introduced us to natural wonders, hysterical memories, amazing folks and it has kept our minds and bodies a bit younger than they would've been!

Since it's still morning..we are lifting our coffee cups to salute the wonderful BEANS!  ...and many more letterboxing milestones for you and the little beans!

Tiptoe & Tonto

From: (ksaggen@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2016-01-21 08:59:56 UTC-08:00

Ahhh...Congratulations!  Ten years!  Think of the places you have been!


We were feeling nostalgic on this cold morning and looked back in our logs to find the first time we met the Beans...Tiptoe remembers approaching a threesome on a blanket at the June 3, 2006 "Wandering Wonderland" event hosted by Wisconsin Hiker and Martini Man...there she exchanged with one Atom 118 and two sweet girls, Coffee Bean and Fermont.  The rest is history as they say!

Not long after, we see stamp images on November 10, 2007 depicting the fun "Letterboxing Wedding" where we celebrated the results of Atom118 joined to Coffee Beans forming a whole new element...the amazing Atomic Beans!

More time passes and in September of 2010, we see in our logs that we found the delightful Winter series, "A  Good Year" where there is an adorable stamp of a pregnant Bean...and in that same month Mark & Sue Pepe came to Chicago for a visit.  To celebrate we all met for pizza and to give the expecting Atomic Beans a book baby shower!  It's been so enjoyable watching this little nucleus grow into the precious Bean family we all know and love.

Ten years!  Golly, Tiptoe and Tonto are only 39, so that would've made them 29 in letterboxing years!  

You are correct in saying how letterboxing changes our lives...most definitely for the better...marvelous adventure, hysterical memories and incredible folks we've all met along the way.  It's still morning, so we lift our coffee mugs up to you to toast a wonderful family who have contributed greatly to making  this such a great hobby for all the rest of us!...and to Fermont for being persistent with a less than enthusiastic brother!  Sisters DO know best!

Congratulations on 10....and hopes for many more decades to come of fun and whimsy!
Tiptoe & Tonto
