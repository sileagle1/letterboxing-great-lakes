From: (duncan53211@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-04-20 01:05:17 UTC
I scanned a map of Moraine Hills SP and Chain o' Lakes SP today.
They will be placed into the "2004 Gathering" folder in the photos
section.

As you can see from the maps, there are a number of places that one
can drive to, park, and go LB hunting at both parks. (There are also
a number of other nature preserves in the area.) There are also a
number of trails. Let's do this: If you are planning to do a box
or series of boxes on your own (i.e. you make, you hide) for the
gathering or for permanent placement in either of these parks, just
let me know what picnic area or trail you plan to place it/them on.
Then I'll use the other trails/picnic areas for the boxes that I'm
asked to place. That way we won't all be bunched up. Thanks!

As for the "night boxing", I think if we are going to do it, it
would need to be done at Chain o' Lakes, since Moraine Hills closes
relatively early (8pm), although CoL closes at 9 pm. If I can find
(or someone knows of) a place open later that we could use for night
boxing, we'll do it there. I think to allow as many as possible to
try them, I'd prefer to have them available for both Friday night
and Saturday night (planted Friday and picked up Sunday with the
other non-permanent boxes). Granted they'd be technically available
during the day, and it would be okay to find them then, but
the "fun" would be to do them in the dark as intended. I still
don't know all the details about night boxing, but I'm willing to
make this series and do the research, but if anyone else out there
would really like to give this a go, I'd be more than happy to hand
it over to you (how about it Melinda?) I have some ideas I could
share too.

Oh! One way we can get the attention of annonymus letterboxers to
our gathering and to this group is to have some of you plant some
new boxes and include in the box and in the clues a note informing
letterboxers of the gathering and the group. We did this with the
REI letterbox for our WI gathering in March. (
http://home.wi.rr.com/dragonlber/REI%20Letterbox.htm ) If we did
this even for one box in each state, then those letterboxers that
only check the "new" boxes list will see information about the
gathering and the group.

Thanks for all your ideas that keep coming in! They are very
helpful! We still could use more workshops given by people!
Possible ideas - mounting stamps, making hand-made log books,
planning a letterboxing vacation, etc. And, we can ALWAYS use more
boxes for the event! ;-)

Dragon

From: lesann847 (l-fischer@northwestern.edu) | Date: 2004-06-11 13:35:47 UTC
Thanks for the kind words, it's been a lot of fun! Melinda and
Kate put together the best picnics!

Dragon, I'm ok--emailed you last night, but you may not have
gotten it because of the attachment. I sent a new e-mail this
morning with clues right in the email.

I'm going to make a hitchhiker called "Acorn's Wandering Keys."
As I headed back to my car yesterday after an afternoon of hiking
around, I realized I had lost my keys. On the way to the car, I
looked in a few of the places I had been, but no luck. I had
visions of offering a reward to any letterboxer who found them.
When I reached the car, there they were on the front seat.
Apparently, they did not want to join me for a day of fending off the
rain and the mosquitoes. This is not the first time they have
decided to stay in a place when I have decided to go.

I know we're all going to Moraine Hills for the letterboxing, but
don't forget the wildflowers and creatures that initially attracted
us to outdoor activities.

In the last week and a half, I have seen numerous deer, muskrat,
possums and racoons. Kate pointed out more toads than I ever
would have seen on my own. My favorite siting was a fox, the first
I've seen in the wild.

As far as birds, the birding's been great. I'm no expert, but I've
sited cranes, great blue herons, turkey vultures, red tailed
hawks, jays, chickadees, bluebirds, black birds. I spent a good
15 minutes watching a family of sandhill cranes--mama, papa
and baby--foraging from a distance of 30 feet (before the
mosquitoes drove me away).

There are irises, spiderwort, nightshade, buttercups, and
waterlilies among many other blooming plants right now.

I look forward to seeing you all on Saturday.

Acorn
