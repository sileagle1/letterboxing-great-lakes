TOTW: Your Letterbox Must-Have Item

From: Doodle & Deedle Bug (doodle_n_deedle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-02-14 07:16:49 UTC-08:00
Everyone's letterboxing gear and carving supplies
vary, and one of the things I love about the talk list
is hearing about different things that people use,
esp. when I realize, "Hey, we should get one of those,
too," or, "Wouldn't life be so much easier if we
started using that instead..."

So, my proposed topic of the week is, what one piece
of letterboxing equipment can you just not live
without? You know, the item that, when you think back
to your early letterbox days, you just wonder, "How
did I ever get by without this??"

For us, I think it's the plastic school box. We found
it at an area closeout store for like, $1 or $2; it
has openings on both sides; the one side is more
shallow, with dividers creating different
compartments. The other side is deeper and one big
compartment, and it's perfect for us to store our
stamps, pens, inks, etc. all in one handy place, so
that we don't have to rummage around our great big bag
or worry about our stamps getting damaged. I don't
even remember how we carried our stamp & ink pads
around before then - I think we may have stored the
stamp in an Altoid tin and just rummaged for the ink
pads.

So, what about everyone else?

Doodle Bug.

From: TJ_Mich (tj_mich@ameritech.net) | Date: 2006-02-14 07:59:51 UTC-08:00
I'd have to say that one of our most used items (other than a logbook and our stamps, of course!) is the DeLorme Atlas & Gazetteer. If you're not familiar with this, it's a book of maps the same size as a normal road atlas -- but each book covers one state, instead of the whole country. It's detailed enough to show the name of each road in most rural areas... very helpful for planning routes, finding back roads, and locating some of the more obscure parks, waterfalls, lighthouses, etc. I've often pre-plotted trips by using small sticky-notes to mark the location of all the boxes in certain areas.

I haven't yet tried the Microsoft Streets and Trips software that several of you have praised highly, so perhaps this is an outdated method, but it has served us well.

--Tom of TJ_Mich

I agree with DeLorme Gazeteers! I love maps and wouldn't you know, I have

love to hear recommendations. I've also been eye-ing nordic walking poles
... but that's an entirely different activity.

My GPS unit is very important to me too. I am fortunate to have a GPS V so it helps me tremendously when I'm driving - it tells me when to turn left or right, how far to the next turn, how far to the destination.

I myself like the logbook I use. It's a hard-covered zippered daytimer-type
book, with the standard 6-ring binding. I have blank paper I punch to fit

is that they don't sell bigger non-zippered binders for archiving, but I
suppose I can always set up a file box type system.) The inside front cover
of the book has a mesh zippered pouch that fits about a dozen LePlume pens,
and the inside back cover has a pocket I keep my stamp in. Neat and
compact!
