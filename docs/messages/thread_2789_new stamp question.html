From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-07-19 22:59:05 UTC-04:00
Some of the smoother carving materials do not take ink real well on the
first few stamps. I have noticed this with PZ Kut and also the pink
stuff -- some pieces more than others. This will clear up once the stamp
has been used a bit. I have found that if you use a pigment ink or a chalk
ink and stamp a few times, this tends to *stick* better and make a better
base on the stamp than the dye inks or markers.

The boxes that lock on the sides are lock and lock boxes. They can usually
be found at WalMart, Target and Meijer stores. There are also off-brand
imitations that can be found at discount and dollar stores, but be sure to
test the locking edges as I have had problems with some of these staying
closed.

Good luck with your placement!

SpringChick

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2006-07-19 22:25:52 UTC-05:00
Woo hoo - it sounds like you're on your way and we'll have another box to
find in the area!

I've found the smaller size Lock'N'Lock boxes in some Target stores, so you
might want to check there if you haven't already. (I'm not surprised you
couldn't find the camo tape now. I think it will be another month or two
before the Wal-Mart near my office stocks it, since they place it in the
"hunting" area in the fall. I'll just have to stock up enough to last me
for a year. Luckily haven't run out of what I bought last year.)

A simple logbook is spiral bound index cards. You can decorate the BACK
cover if you want, and people will then be able to stamp onto the blank
side, rather than the lined side of the pages. Sturdy paper stock so you
wouldn't have bleed through of ink.

What type of carving media did you use, how did you put the design on the
media, and what type of ink are you using? I think all can have some affect
on how well it stamps.

I'm also excited to hear about a box in another WI county. Atom 118 emailed
me to let me know they had placed a box in Lincoln county earlier this
month, so that will be two more counties with boxes and only 25 counties
still waiting for a box.

From: be_a_badger (schuppner@uwalumni.com) | Date: 2006-07-20 13:58:47 UTC
Master Carve can be a little slippery to start. We use an eraser on
it before we start to carve.

We agree that the type of ink can be a factor, too. We have more
consistent results on all types of carving material with markers than
with stamp pads. A marker bonus is that they usually leave less ink
behind after stamping, so it's easier to wipe off the stamp. We use
Marvy Brush markers.
