MI Fall gathering

From: tiptoetonto (tiptoetonto@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-10-14 03:20:31 UTC
We made it home in record time...Although, our early morning start
caught up with us around South Haven...too full to stop for Sherman's
ice cream to revive us...so we cranked up the music to keep us awake!

We have to agree with Stargirl and El Cajador's comment of enjoying
our time while eluding MANY boxes...But, we left satisfied with the
seven miles of exercise...uh...4 boxes, and lots of fat grams under
our belts. Tonto was more than once off on some rabbit trail mixing
up the order of clues (as usual), while Tiptoe was trying to figure
out true North..which really was South...Nonetheless, we saw some
spectacular fungi, first changing colors, deer up close, and the
weather couldn't have been better!

Best of all was the relaxed feel of being at a family cookout. We
have to agree that it was great to just spend time chatting,
laughing, and being amazed at how big our little letterboxing kids
have gotten in a year! I think Rainy Day and The Nickerboxer are
ready for high school!
We LOVED meeting together for the evening meal rather than breaking
up the day with lunch.
We have mentioned it before, but it still remains true..We have the
best letterboxing event organizers here in the Great Lakes...that
Chick with a Spring in her step and the Box full of Azo (and we all
know what azo is ..right?)are the greatest! Thanks for your amazing
effort in making this hobby so enjoyable for all of us...even if your
clues leave some of us clueless...obviously not so with those
crazy "Found-12-Doglvrs"...must have been the "wooden hand"!
For us it was a perfect way to get back boxing after a very long
summer void of letterboxing!...We look forward to another opportunity
for that cup of coffee next year!
Tiptoe & Tonto

We always go far enough!...it's just in circles and back and forth!
Our favorite line is..."We must have gone too far, let's turn
around.." Only to find out later that we were so close when we
turned around!

> Still re-living the day....Mrs. Doglvrs

Tonto and I are still hearing the little soundbites in our head of
conversations from the gathering...But our favorite that we are still
getting a chuckle over is the following we overheard out on the trail
while trying to find the elusive flying porker....

"Stephanie! Get back here! says dear old Dad to his daughter (who is
running ahead on the trail being thoroughly entertained with a wooden
stick "hand" that she is experimenting with it's many uses).

"WHY?" shouts back the teen as she keeps on going.

"BECAUSE I SAID SO!"...shouts Dad while moving quickly in her
direction mummbling under his breath.

We're glad to see that parenting tactics haven't changed that much
over the years! But, I have to say, I've even tried this tactic on
Tonto when he is off on his forays looking for a box...without
hesitation I can testify it doesn't work on husbands either! I think
I'll find me a wooden stick "hand" with a finger to wag!..or maybe
one with a hook!
Tiptoe
