Woo Hoo! I just love the "Contact the Placer" feature and the people
who use it. This weekend I got reports on 5 boxes AND someone took
the time to send me a message about finding one of my hitchhikers in
Pennsylvania. I really appreciate getting these reports since it lets
me know that my boxes are still in place and whether they need any
attention. It is also fun to hear the reactions to the location,
stamp or overall experience.

Thanks to all of you that take the time to do this!!

I'm not aiming this at you & Martini Man at all -- I haven't gone searching
for any of your boxes this year -- but your post reminded me of something
and so I'm using it as a jump point...........

Personally.......in my opinion.........and it may be MY opinion
only..........but.......

I think it's kinda polite, if a placer has requested in the clues to be
contacted, that the placer send a acknowledgement reply. Even if it's just
a short, one sentence email saying something like "Hey, thanks for getting
in touch about my box!"

We're battin' zero so far this year. And I'm only talking placers who
actually mention in their clues that they'd like to be contacted. (I
realize that the 'Contact the Placer' button is there whether a placer wants
it used or not.) But when a placer has requested contact, it can just kinda
make a person feel as if they're sending a message out into a big black void
when there's never an acknowledgement.

I know some folks only check their email once in a blue moon and that
placers can be off on vacation themselves or what not..........but I'm
talking this is going all the way back to January of this year.

I've been reconsidering whether to bother taking the time to do it anymore.

~~ Mosey ~~

I agree. It's sorta like someone saying "Thank You" and you replying "You're Welcome". I think an acknowledgement of receiving the message encourages the practice of sending them. I almost always send a reply immediately. It doesn't take long to type "Thanks for the status update". However sometimes I may need to go back and review clues before replying to a particular question a searcher has.

We almost always send a message after finding a box (unless we've talked to the planter "in person" ). I'd say that we get an acknowledgement at least 75% of the time.
