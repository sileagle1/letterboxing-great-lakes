From: Delirium (donaldson_home@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-07-07 05:49:24 UTC-07:00

We didn't go anywhere special for the holidy, so I dragged my family on what has become something of a dreadful tradition- an hour drive (if we're lucky) to find boxes.

We went to Sterling Heights to find Team Rabbit's boxes. We got there a little late (6ish), and found the "Cygnus" boxes. I have to say, those were some of the most creative clues I've ever seen. Even after pointing out the solutions to the riddles to my husband, he STILL didn't get them, and accused me of "making stuff up".

Our next stop was Dodge Park for the "Joey's Uncle" boxes. Both husband and child's eyes nearly popped out of their heads when they saw this park. It was like something out of a Norman Rockwell painting, except a Norman Rockwell painting where diversity actually existed. There were families everywhere, children running around, an ice cream truck happily sitting in the parking lot, and, most importantly to my 2 year old, A GIANT PLAY STRUCTURE!

I ended up looking for the boxes on my own, while the other two played. My well-oiled team broken apart by the seductive lure of swings and slides, I didn't find the first box. And I looked! I figured I'd cut my losses on that one and go on to the next one. Well, dark was falling by this point, and I quickly realized that I wasn't going to have enough time to find any other boxes today. I SO stink letterboxing on my own.

However, if the Joey boxes are as clever as the Cygnus boxes, I can't wait to go back and find them! Heck, it took me two attempts AND a tour guide to find the "Woods in City" box. Of course, now that I think about it, I'm hard pressed to name ANY Azobox that didn't give me some sort of trouble.

Cheers,

Delirium, 25 DAYS UNTIL THE TRIATHLON!!! Woo-HOO!
