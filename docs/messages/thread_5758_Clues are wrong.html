From: walesflyermary (drewes@WI.RR.com) | Date: 2009-03-25 20:19:51 UTC
I've already taken up diplomatically letting a placer know that the clues are wrong (in one instance) and omit key info (in another). I had LOTS of time to ponder this while on my detours. And get this, the placer is a friend who I introduced to LBing. I'm only posting this here cause I needed to vent, and the placer doesn't usually read these boards. The intial degrees say head west (280) rather than east (100). Then, being a "goody two-shoes" kind of person, I walked right on by a trail marked with a small round "do not enter" sign, when the clues said take every trail to the left. Turns out that was the trail I needed, and I ended up on a LITTLE detour. ARGGHH. BTW, these were on horse trails, which the placer is familiar with, but I am not. Yes, I did find the box - and got LOTS of exercise.

Plus I wanted to say that I am (theoretically)done with my planting frenzy in Jefferson County, WI. The extent of flooding from last summer and even this spring has been quite surprising to me.

Re: [LbGLK] Clues are wrong

From: Katrina Lustofin (drkaty@gmail.com) | Date: 2009-03-25 16:48:03 UTC-04:00
Kimono Girl and I used to beta-test each others clues for exactly this
reason (well, that and we were the only boxers in the area). After a while
we stopped doing it, and then one of my boxes was reported missing by the
first two people to look for it. I couldn't figure out why - the clues
weren't that hard, it wasn't that far from the trail, and it was clearly
still there when I checked on it. Then Flying Dragon went out looking for
it and called me from the trail to say he had found one of the trees
mentioned in the clue but it was on the *other* side of the trail. Turns
out I had my left and right confused again! No wonder those first two
seekers didn't have any luck. I had to email them back with the corrected
clues and an apology for my own silliness. But since I've never been able
to keep left and right straight, I'm usually happy when problems like that
are pointed out politely to me.

Katy

Re: [LbGLK] Clues are wrong

From: Kathleen Brade (brade@comcast.net) | Date: 2009-03-25 17:31:25 UTC-04:00
I remember one particular letterbox we were looking for a few weeks
after we had started letterboxing. The clues said to go west but going
east is how we found the box. While we were figuring this out, we
observed some other people who seemed to be looking for this letterbox
too; we think they gave up since we never saw them on the right trail.

When we returned home, I emailed the author of the box and let them know
they had the direction backwards but I don't think the clue was ever
updated. Maybe it was their way of keeping the letterbox only for
persistent individuals?

Since then, we've only had a couple letterboxes that weren't where the
clues described and the author readily fixed the clues.

I've also heard about letterboxes where the clues are entirely backwards
but I've never had an opportunity to do such a letterbox.

-- MichKathy (who chuckled at WFMary's proposed nickname for today)
