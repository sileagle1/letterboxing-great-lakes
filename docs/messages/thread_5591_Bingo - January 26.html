From: tiptoetonto (tiptoetonto@yahoo.com) | Date: 2009-01-26 15:19:58 UTC
"According to Jim" he has been trying to explain to "All My (Our) Children" why their
mother is so distraught. He decided to start at the beginning, and he began by answering
their inquiry on why he ever got involved with such a "Bozo".

"How I met your Mother" was one of the most unforgettable "Days of Our Lives." She gave
me "Goosebumps" whenever she walked into the room. She was "Young and Restless" and
looking to be a "Relic Hunter" - a real life "Dora the Explorer". I (Tonto) being the "History
Detective," thought we'd make a great pair, just like "Ozzie and Harriet." I asked her to
marry me. I was "Right on the Money" - she's turned out to be a terrific wife...until now.

Our mutual admiration of scavenger hunts, art and history led us to Letterboxing - even
though we didn't have a "Blue's Clue" about what we were doing. "Step by Step" we found
many boxes together and truly loved the hobby. But then she decided to up the ante and
play Letterboxing Bingo.

She has come up empty-handed for 26 days and her attitude has gone "Due South." She is
so out of sorts that each night I'm not sure if I should sleep "Upstairs, Downstairs." It is all
so upsetting. Her ranting has gotten so out of hand that I'm embarrassed for people to
think that "I'm with Her. I'm considering telling people that "My Name is Earl," instead of
Tonto.

The kids interrupted and said, but Dad, she is making this such a "Bleak House" we can
hardly stand it! You have to do something and send her to "St. Elsewhere".

No, he said, this will have to become the "Life As We Know It" until she gets her first Bingo
word. Since this is a "Family Affair," let's keep this "All in the Family" and not spread
rumors by being a "Gossip Girl" about your mother.

The non-letterboxing kids just shook their heads and told him, you both need to "Get A
Life."
