Dick Blick is my favorite source for good-quality erasers to carve
to make stamps -- and the tools to carve them with! www.dickblick.com will
get you there, if you don't have any in your area (they are a
northeast-illinois company, originally).

I've been compiling some lists of links to places to get, online,
supplies for starting letterboxing On The Cheap, and will be posting it
when I've gotten it beaten into shape and have time.

I'm personally a big fan of Dick Blick. You can go online (www.dickblick.com, I think) and order Mastercarve (which is my medium of choice). It comes in the biggest, most wicked looking chunk you've ever seen. It looks like a blunt force weapon has been delivered to your door and it lasts forever.

Or, if you have Michael's, they carry that Speedball pink stuff. There are some on this list who have a nearly cult-like devotion to it- even going so far as to note differences in barcodes (*grin* You know who you are).

And if you're not sure you're ready for a big hunk of any carving medium, regular erasers really do make excellent stamps. My first couple stamps were done on erasers, and they're holding up just fine.

Of course, since I neglected to read the other replies to this post, I'm probably repeating the very good advice others have given you. In which case, sorry!
Cheers,
Delirium
