The three cooties that we ended up bringing home with us are:

Tuna or Salmon? (by the Brat Pack IL)

Cheers! (also by the prolific Brat Pack IL!)

Bunny-Bunny (by OrionAtEmerson)

We were a bit disappointed that no one had stamped into the cooties
in the "traditional" manner - using thumb print art (oftentimes
related to your trailname). Here's one "offical" definition of a
cootie:

"A small letterbox that is passed surreptitously from letterboxer to
letterboxer. As created by Pandora and Camp Fire Lady, cooties
originally began as an activity for kids at letterboxing gatherings,
in which kids recorded their thumbprints in a cootie and secretly
passed it along to another child or adult. Cooties have evolved into
a very small letterbox that can be hidden in a pocket or backpack of
another letterboxer, and thus travel from individual to individual."

I just posted some examples of thumbprint art that show how they can
be very simple, or as elaborate as you'd like. It's fun to see the
art as a change of pace from the normal sig stamps, so please
consider using thumb art on any cooties that may be in your
possession!

Wisconsin Hiker

Thanks for reminding us about the 'traditional' cooties. I do recall that cootie logbooks were for inky fingers only. But somehow, things evolved and we started carving tiny stamps for those mini-logbooks, and we got away from those cute thumb prints. Some logbooks tell you - Thumb Prints Only. We're generally pretty good when it comes to instructions. So now we know who started and promoted cooties at gatherings.

I went home from SILO #3 with only two cooties:

1. TOKIDOKI #3 by Clio Mouse (all thumb prints too)
2. PI by Orion At Emerson

But that's not to say that I won't find another one in my jacket lining a couple weeks from now. Hmmm....what is that lump in my jacket?

I have to laugh when I see cooties being tossed and flying through the air, or suddenly see them clipped and dangling from clothes.

Warning: Anti Cootie Spray doesn't work! But it is sold in tasty spray flavors.
