Finally getting around to writing and posting the clues to some of
the boxes we've placed in the last month or so.

Our first ever, micro-box is The Serene Swan letterbox, located in
Montague. There is a companion box (the Savage Swan)

Savage Swan was carved in tribute to "Sharon's" ordeal and her
patience. After her watercraft became disabled, she attempted to
steer for shore to do maintenance, when a male swan rushed at her.
He bit her hard enough to bruise her entire hand. Once she was
safely ashore, he kept swimming "en guard" for almost an hour,
keeping Sharon trapped on shore. Her patience outlasted his, and
she was able to bring her repaired watercraft back to the marina.
The Savage Swan letterbox it currently resides on private property,
with permission. It has been requested that I not post the clues.
However, the guidelines I was given allow me to give out the clues
to anyone who finds the Serene Swan and emails a request for the
Savage Swan clues.

The Wood and Water Nymph letterbox had strictly straight forward
already written and ready to publish, when I discovered that my
delay in getting them posted could potentially interfere with
another letterbox. (Well, of course they didn't know I had a box
there before they placed theirs ... I dilly-dallied and didn't get
them posted, so how could they?) So, I took the challenge
SpringChick had offered along the lines of expanding ourselves, to
create a mystery-box or to come up with creative clues and changed
the straightforward clues into quite a little puzzle.

I'm interested in hearing from anyone who finds the Wood and Water
Nymph box (or at least attempts to solve the clues) for some
feedback on what I've come up with. I'm hoping this might be
something, for those of you wanted more than straightforward clues,
to sink your teeth into.

Now, if I can get the rest of the clues sorted out, I can go place
some of these other boxes I've been creating. (grin)

Clues for both can be found either on at letterboxing.org or at our
Yahoogroup groups.yahoo.com/group/Letterboxing_Bindlestiff/ in the
files area.

Enjoy!
