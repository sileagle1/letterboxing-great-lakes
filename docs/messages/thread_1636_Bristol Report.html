Bristol Report

From: Angi (ShutterflyIL@aol.com) | Date: 2005-07-24 17:45:38 UTC
My friend, Jacqui, and I made it up to the faire on Saturday. She had
never been there before, so I was a little worried about how she
would like it. It seems that when it comes to the faire, people
either love it or they hate it. Well, she had a great time and even
did some boxing.

We found a few boxes here and there while we shopped and explored. We
even got an exchange with Jane the Phoole. She seemed to be hiding
the rest of the day because that was the only time we saw her while
we were there, and she isn't hard to miss!

After having lunch out in the car, we noticed that the sky was
getting a little dark. Eh...let's keep going anyway. So we set out to
find the feather fans for Jacqui and also to find Howling Coyote. We
knew she would be there, we even had her picture printed out, so it
couldn't be that hard to find her. (I did go up to someone I thought
looked like her and asked if she was HC...and got a very strange
look) For whatever reason, we kept missing the stage she said she
could be found at, but finally walked by it and heard the sweet
music. Guess who was sitting in the front row? Finally! Well, we sat
there and listened to the music. When it was over we saw her and her
husband walk over. I yelled her name and actually had the right
person this time.

So with the help of Howling Coyote, we found the last three boxes we
were looking for in the Shoppes series just as the start of a storm
was rolling in. Wouldn't you know it...as Jacqui and I were stamping
into that last box...the tornado siren went off!!! Life at Bristol
stopped at that moment. Everyone seemed to stop in their place and
just stare at the others around them. Nobody knew what to do. I
replaced the box as HC went to find out what was happening. It ended
up that it was actually a thunderstorm warning. The tornado warning
had been lifted shortly before the sirens went off. We took shelter
in Laughing Coyote's shop for a while because the storm got bad
fairly quick...heavy downpours, lightning and such. Jacqui and I
stayed in the shop for a while until the rain died down enough to
make a mad dash for the car. We were still soaked, but we made it
without getting hit by the lightning striking over our heads.

So at the end of the day we had two exchanges and found all of the
Shoppes boxes. Well, one we couldn't get because of the storm and all
the people crowded around it. But we saw it, so we know it is in good
shape and know where to find it when/if we go back again. :)

Good luck to those who will search later in the season. The stamps,
and not to mention the faire in general, are great, so you won't
regret going.
