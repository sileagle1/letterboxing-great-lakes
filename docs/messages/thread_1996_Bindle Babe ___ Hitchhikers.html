Journey, I can only speak for myself, but I am not actively boycotting
AQ. I think Ryan has put a tremendous amount of effort into building
that site and I always try to appreciate the volunteered time and work
given to benefit others.

Yes, it's true, I am a registered user at AQ. For personal and
sentimental reasons, I wanted to receive a particular Postal
Letterbox. Registration on AQ was a requirement by the box owner for
obtaining it.

I have no intention of ever registering any of my Hitch Hikers
anywhere but at Pete and Wanda's site.
(http://alum.wpi.edu/~p_miner/travelers) This choice is my way of
honoring them for all of their volunteered time and personal
commitment, freely given to us boxers, in hosting the HH tracking page.

Additionally, my personal perception of Letterboxing.org is that the
site is mainly for listing clues to letterboxes. Since HH's don't
normally have clues, I personally see no reason to list them on
Letterboxing.org

For my letterboxes (not HH's) my published clues will continue to be
listed at Letterboxing.org or on my Yahoogroup
(http://groups.yahoo.com/group/letterboxing_bindlestiff/) and nowhere
else.

In the aprox 2 years that I have been boxing, I have placed over 100
boxes (some are retired and some WOM only boxes) As someone who
recently placed their first box, I'm sure you can calculate the effort
you put into your box multiplied times 100+ boxes and see that it
represents not only the original financial investment but also a
significant investment of time and energy; especially as every single
one of my boxes contains a hand carved stamp, and the majority of them
also have a handmade logbook.

For myself, (and as you, a letterbox owner, will soon see) simply
placing a box does not end the responsibility nor investment of time
required there is time needed for maintenance of the box, sending
replies to emailed box reports - requests for hints - complaints about
vague clues - etc, not to mention keeping clues current by updating
them if the landmarks or terrain used are changed or the box is
verified MIA.

Oh dear, a box is confirmed MIA now you have to post the MIA notice,
recarve, rehide, etc..

Personally, I am not willing to devote any more time in duplicating my
work and listing boxes plus clues at yet another site. (AQ) I hope
that after reading the above information, you can understand that.

I understand that the Letterboxing.org site has continued to evolve
since I began boxing and that there are more features being added all
the time. I'm not particularly in favor of some of those features but
I try not to sweat the small stuff. Let me tell you, the features of
a website that offers me a high-profile, free place to post clues to
my boxes and also provides the ability for seekers/finders to contact
me; definitely falls into the category of stuff not to worry about,
for me.

I think most of my HH's logbooks contain an email address for the
finder to use if they wanted to contact me. So if one of my HH's ended
up in another state, or country (!) I'd be thrilled to either get an
email from the finder/driver or to see that kind of update on Pete and
Wanda's site. Do I think it "would be nice if the new finder could
list them among their finds at Atlas Quest" --- no. And I say no
because, I don't feel anyone's ability to track their finds online
should be my responsibility. It's my personal belief that we are all
responsible for keeping track of our finds. This is what I, and many
others use a logbook for.

If you'd like to track all your finds online, I'm afraid you're bound
to be disappointed. There will always be boxes for which there are no
on-line clues; (for whatever reason .. I can think of several right
off the top of my head) so your online count, using AQ or
Letterboxing.org is forever bound to be inaccurate.

If keeping an absolutely accurate online count is important to you,
may I offer a suggestion? You could get your own web page or start
your own yahoogroup and list all your finds there.

Let me be the first to offer you congratulations on your first placed
letterbox (Paul Bunyan's Baby Blocks) and welcome you to the "other
side" of letterboxing. Yeah! Cheers!! I hope you will enjoy it and
find as much pleasure in being a letterbox placer, as I have.

With regards to your announcement of your first box and that you had
placed my Christmas Swish Tree HH within it, I wish you hadn't posted
that part. HH's are traditionally considered a surprise, and by
posting that your box contains a HH, especially naming the HH, you've
taken away the "surprise gift" aspect of the whole HH concept. Does
that make sense to you?

Silent Doug has put quite a lot of work into providing a comprehensive
website of valuable information for letterboxers. Here is the link,
if your interested: (www.letterboxing.INFO)

Enjoy!
---PJ
