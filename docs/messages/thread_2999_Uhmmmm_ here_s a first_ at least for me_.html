Curious.

Re: [LbGLK] Re: Uhmmmm, here's a first, at least for me.

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-08-24 12:00:39 UTC-04:00
Oh I think personal notes are great -- I love reading them and don't care if they take up 4 pages in the logbook -- I wish more people would take the time to leave a comment! I think where the dissention comes in is the lack of personal stamp with the note (particularly punching holes in the pages of somebody's log book) and when a group or family of several people each *sign* (not stamp) their name on a separate page in the book -- particularly when the book is small. It's really cool that your 3-yr. old can write his name, but I don't really need to know that or care to find it in my letterbox log book -- carve or buy the kid a stamp so they can participate in letterboxing in the way it is traditionally done. The personal rubber stamp (hand-carved or store-bought) is a pretty fundamental part of the letterboxing equation.

I realize there are always going to be exceptions -- casual finders, somebody forgot their stamp, etc. but those are not who we are talking about.

SpringChick

I agree. We always try to leave some little note in

so nosey I like reading what others wrote, too). Now,

notes left in the log has to be "Joy of the River."

amusing reading previous finders' tales of the 6 foot
mosquitos trying to carry their kids off. And then we
had to run before those mosquitos tried to carry us
off, too.
