Enter your vote today! A new poll has been created for the
LbGLK group:

Vote on location for MI Fall Gathering. Event date is
Saturday, October 22, 2005. Location options include:

Yankee Springs Rec Area: Located just a short distance
off U.S. 131 between Grand Rapids and Kalamazoo -- good
central location. There are several miles of prime trails
at this park, however there are no current letterboxes in
this area. Park is open year-round and the shelter is
available. Shelter is roof, cement floor w/ half-walls,
includes grills and electricity. Problem: by October the
plumbing is turned off so there is no water and the modern
restrooms are closed. Nearest outhouses are about 1/4
mile from the shelter.

Hoffmaster SP: Located at the south end of Muskegon
county along Lake Michigan -- not as centrally located.
There are also several miles of nice trails at this park
with a few letterboxes already in place and many others in
the surrounding area. Park is open year-round and the
shelter is available. Shelter is roof w/ cement floor,
includes 1 large grill, a hand pump for water and a large
stone fireplace in the shelter -- there is NO
electricity. No modern restrooms in this area of the
park, but outhouses are located very near the shelter.


o Yankee Springs Rec Area
o Hoffmaster State Park

There is nothing that would prevent me from finding a letterbox! I'd find a way. When I'm on a mission, I am determined to find that box. I'd go back several times if necessary; better prepared each time.

Fungus Woman remembers, because how will we ever forget our St. Nick's experience. One of the clues at the St. Nick's Gathering had a group of us marching through a large swamp area of semi-frozen sewage, but that didn't stop our group. Others gave up the search for that box, but not us. And I'll never forget "THE SMELL" or the fact that my boots weren't tall enough to prevent the frozen sewage from coming in over the top of my boots, and the fact that my feet were freezing as I marched on with icy water sloshing around in my boots, and the cold weather, and the reality of hypothermia etc. After I warned people that I might start experiencing stages of hypothermia, I made a judgment call that I had 20 minutes max. to find that box and get back to the base camp to get the freezing water filled boots off my feet before my body went into shock! Yes, we reached the letterbox. And yes, my feet survived. Thanks again to FW.

So my response to the poll is yes to all the options!

Sometimes there is a challenge to finding a letterbox. Anyone can find a drive by lettterbox. When a box is a challenge to find, that's the memory that remains and that's part of the fun -sharing the story. My feet still get cold thinking about that "fun?" experience!

Beetle
