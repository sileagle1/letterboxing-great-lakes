Box maintenance blues

From: brownrabbit_teamrabbit (teamrabbit@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-04-21 19:01:40 UTC
I haven't seen this subject come up in a while, and after a rather
frustrating morning of box maintenance I figured it needed
reiterating. One of the basic tenets of letterboxing is: Rehide the
letterbox better than you found it. This applies to everyone:
veterans and newbies, adults and children, cat-lovers and dog-
lovers... Obviously the onus of finding a great hiding-space falls
on the placer, but it's then up to the visitors of that box to keep
the secret and reinforce it when necessary. It's sad to find two
boxes in a series missing without a trace, and a third visible when
approaching the hiding-spot.


On a more positive note, these Team Rabbit boxes are open for
business:

Rainy-Day Rabbit
Wedding-Day Rabbit
A Jedi's Training

Keep checking the maintenance database (what a great idea, SC!) for
future openings (how many boxes do you have Dragon - "How I spent my
Easter Vacation")

Re: [LbGLK] Box maintenance blues

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-04-22 11:54:35 UTC-04:00
Oh bummer... I've had a few instances this past week also where the box was missing even though it had been a really good hiding spot. It is disappointing to say the least! At one particular spot I went to check on one of the 80 Days boxes only to find it missing. I couldn't believe it was gone as these are small containers and it had been tucked in its place really well, not in the line of sight from the main trail and was hard to find even if you knew what you were looking for... so I searched and searched (a long time)... still missing. The irony of it is that very close to this place there is a regular letterbox (not mine) and it was sitting there, 3 feet from the trail, totally exposed, bright white plastic container big enough to park a car in... still there (I did cover it though).

I know that finding my boxes missing certainly makes me more conscious when I am replacing a box and willing to take a few extra minutes to make sure it is secure and hidden from all angles. And might I mention... the obvious, tell-tale pile of twigs and bark it not really *hidden well* even if the box is not visible. That is just an invitation to a passerby -- might just as well top it with a sign that says, "look under this pile of stuff."

Hope your future checks go better...

SpringChick

Re: Box maintenance blues

From: tiptoetonto (tiptoetonto@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-04-23 02:05:15 UTC
We also would like to add to this topic..In the last few Saturday's
of letterboxing, more than half of the boxes we've found were
exposed. (Although disappointing, this sometimes has worked to our
benefit!) We have surmised that one of the reasons for this
unfortunate situation can be blamed on the use of leaves as the main
cover. Let us make a gentle reminder that WIND HAPPENS! While
a "smattering" of leaves around the replanted box seems to do the
trick, it only lasts about ten minutes after you've left the box
when the wind demons make those dead leaves dance and fly away!

As a side note: We checked on one of our boxes (Slow Growth Oak)
that I planted against Tonto's better judgement...in a flood
plain...The only sign of it was a suspicious oil slick (ink slick?)
on top of the foot of water covering the fallen log! He's making me
come clean with this formal public confession! "I will listen to
Tonto, I will listen to Tonto...." (Yeah,yeah.... whatever! Like
he's never made a mistake!)
Tiptoe & Tonto

I have another story.

I checked on my Dora series earlier this month. All there, all good to

Cincinnati. If it makes it to August 2006, they will have been out for 2
years. I am amazed, though, that until now, they've been safe.
