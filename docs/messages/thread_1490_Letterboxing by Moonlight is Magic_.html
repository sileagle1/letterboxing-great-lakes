Letterboxing by Moonlight is Magic!

From: (col.one923@spwl.net) | Date: 2005-06-22 09:33:37 UTC-05:00
Just wanted to share a bit of our Moonlit magic from last night!

In our eagerness to find a specific box on the north side of Lake
Mendota in Madison, WI last night we encountered a bit of "magic". We
had been struggling with the clues and finally had an "ah-ha!" moment
yesterday afternoon. We promised to take the kids swimming, so
afterwards we realized it was still dusk and being the first day of
summer and the longest day of the year, we buzzed over to hunt the box
as the sun was setting. Unfortunately, I made some erroneous
assumptions about the box, so we didn't get it this visit. However, as
we headed back to the car it was dark except for the moonlight and
fireflies! All the while the kids were imaging monsters and bears
lurking in the woods, but as we exited the woods, we were met with a
gorgeous view of the moonlit lake.

It looked just like a wall of water in the tree line with a shelf on top
with the city lights balancing on top of that. Because it was dark, we
were seeing two dimensional and our eyes were playing tricks on us. The
kids just gazed and asked "wow, what's that?!" They couldn't
immediately recognize that it was actually the lake. It literally
looked like a very shimmery wall with lights dancing around it.

Mr. Puddlejumper suggested we walk down by the lake and there we found
the lake breeze sweeping across our faces as we gazed at the moon and
the city lights across the lake and listened to the water rolling
against the lake shore. It was mesmerizing and nearly magic. We spent
some time searching the skyline for the Capitol, the UW Hospital
buildings, the airport etc. There was a small sailboat anchored to a
buoy just about 50 yards offshore that sat in the tail of the moonlight
and a few other boats quietly moving about the lake.

It was about that point we wished we didn't have the kids and the dog
along, but it was definitely a very magical memory for the first day of
summer! It was something we would have never experienced if we weren't
out looking for a letterbox!
