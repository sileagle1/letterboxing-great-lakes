Hi! I am new to Letterboxing but "very" old to camping (more than 40
yrs worth) and I train new scout leaders. I had to poke my head into
this message cuz I got to point out 1 very improtant thing!

If the nights will be chilly (and the usually are for fall), the air
in the air mattress will disepate in the night. So unless your
willing to wake up (at least twice) in the night and pump away, I
suggest 2 possibilities for you; 1. get 1 of those lawn chairs you
can stretch out on with no arms (the ones wear you bend foot and
head to middle). I use this for scout camping all the time and
nobody thinks I'm a wuss (I'm also a climbing instructor for BSA.
LOL). or 2. get an air mattress with a built in motorized pump ("D"
batteries) but these can be a bit expensive but worth not having to
drag pump out and start stomping at 2 am!

Another thing, when you pack your personal kit (toiletries), get
some baby wipes and witch hazel (less sting then alcohol). Use these
for cleaning face and other parts of body when showers are not
available. They're also good when driving a long distance and you
need to "freshen up".

I hope no one minds my jumping in! Also keep in mind when buying a
tent that when it says "sleeps 3-4" it means very small people! LOL!
And weather-resistance is NOT non-flammable. But if you don't plan
on plunking tent right next to fire or having a cig inside, you will
be fine! LOL!

Good luck and have fun! Wish I could go! Hoping to do this next fall
actually! Maybe you can give some pointers when you get back!

Drama
