Placing and maintaining a letterbox (or many letterboxes) can be a lot
of work. When a finder contacts the placer, it's one way to let the
placer know that the box is safe and sound. Some finders may not
contact the placer and leave a message because they are not as familiar
with the hobby as many of us are. It's just a common courtesy, sort of
like a thank you note, but how many people remember to send thank you
notes any more. Just not viewed as socially important. Or, could it
be that some finders don't want to contact the placer to report a
missing or damaged box for fear that they will be blamed? I never look
at it that way, because the act of reporting a problem doesn't fit the
profile of a person who takes or damages a box. However, we get back
what we put into this hobby. I have made some wonderful friends who
have shared their creative ideas and talents with me simply because I
took the time to contact them; the placer of a letterbox. Everyone is
different, so don't take it personally.
