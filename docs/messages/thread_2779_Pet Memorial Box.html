Pet Memorial Box

From: doubledragonletterboxer (M8KC5008@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-07-17 13:30:59 UTC
I just recently lost my cat to heart disease last week, he was only
6yrs. I was thinking of making a letterbox in his honor. But I wanted
it to be more than just for me. I know there are alot of animal lovers
out there that are LBers, so I know you all know the kind of heart
ache I am going through. I thought it would be nice to include any
LBer who has lost a pet in this memorial box. I was thinking that not
only could you leave your stamp in the log book, but maybe bring a
stamp of your deceased pet and commemorate the good times you had with
your pet by sharing a story in the log book. I'm not sure if anyone
would be interested in something like this, but I'm going to give it a
try. I think it will help the healing process for me and I hope it
will for others too. I just need a location in SE Mi to place it. Any
suggestions? I was thinking of a spot that would not be too buggy, so
people would not feel rushed. A nice spot for reflection and serenity.
Keep your eyes pealed while you all are out and about. Thanks for your
input.
Double Dragon

From: beautifulpeople99 (jennifer.mccready@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-07-17 13:38:45 UTC
Sorry to hear about your cat. :-( If you are ever in the Ann Arbor
area, there is a box here (by clickermom, I think) called "In Memory
Of..." that is actually very similar to what you are proposing. The
logbook encourages the finder to write down fond memories of pets who
are no longer here. So if you would like some inspiration, or just a
place to write about your cat, you should visit this box! (Plus, the
stamp is great!)

J of J&BBooks

From: brat_pack07 (kevjean@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-07-17 14:00:19 UTC








I think that's a wonderful idea!!! A few years ago, I lost my
Mastiff. His stomach flipped and he already had it flipped back over
twice before. He was outside and I checked on him and he was okay.
I was waiting for my dad to come home to take him back to the vet to
have him checked out. I came back 5 min later to check on him, and
he passed away. I was so mad and upset, I screamed!!! My dad and
our neighbor came and took him away to the vet. The vet Dr. was very
nice and knows are family well and he took care of Maverick for me.
I cried for days!!! About a week later, a neighbor across the street
told me about a greyhound who was abused and needed a good home, and
thought we could help each other out. Well, I wanted him site
unseen, and he's been there for me as I have been there for him. He
was really scared of people, especially men, but a year later, he was
alot better. His name is Waylon. I still cry when I think of
Maverick, but having Waylon helps ease the pain a little.
