From: moonsunmagik (moonsunmagik@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-05-16 22:43:37 UTC
Hi, Bugs!

The "pink stuff" is rubber carving material, put out by companies
like Speedball (the makers of a very popular brand of carving
knives). It's pretty widely used, and it is either loved or
loathed. I fall into column B on this issue because the pink stuff
tends to resist the force of my will a bit too much;) "The white
stuff" suits me and my carving style just fine. "The white stuff"
(put out by Mastercarve, among other co.'s) seems softer and more
pliable to me, but like "the pink stuff", it too is either loved or
hated.

Come to think of it, I'm not sure many 'boxers swing both ways on
this particular issue.... Hmmmmm.

It's up to you and what works for your carving style to determine
what is indeed the better "stuff".


May the best "stuff" win!
Melinda (of Melinda & Kate)

Re: The Pink Stuff?

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-05-16 23:05:04 UTC
Hi Diane,

The "pink stuff" is carving material from Speedball -- it is about
1/4" thick and comes in a variety of piece sizes. Most Michael's
and Hobby Lobby stores carry this stuff if you have those near you --
it is in the block carving supplies which are usually in a little
corner of the aisle with all the fine art and calligraphy pens. You
can also buy it at stores and online in kits with the Speedball
carving tool set. It is one of the preferred mediums of many
letterboxers as it is soft yet firm enough for a good amount of
detail.

It is not to be confused with the "white stuff" which is also made
by Speedball, but a much inferior product that most people find to
be very crumbly and completely unsuitable for any amount of detail.
This material also does not hold up well to time and weather.

There is also a white carving material made by the Staedler Mars
company called Mastercarve. It is available in varying sized blocks
at many hobby stores and also frequently carried at rubber stamp
supply stores. It is quite soft and carves "like butter." It may
be perhaps a bit soft for fine detail, but does have the advantage
of being thick enough (just shy of 1/2") that you don't need to
mount the stamp to make it sturdy. The disadvantage with this is
that it can be rather costly.

Then there is the wonderful white carving material called PZKut.
This stuff is 1/4" thick and is a lot like the pink stuff, maybe
just a bit firmer. Until just recently, it was sold only online but
the supplier has closed up shop and I don't know how long it will
still be available. If you are interested in writing her (she is
still shipping for a while), her e-mail is alp@4pease.com.

There are a few others, but these are the main ones.

SpringChick
