Hi,

My daughters (Gabriella & Sophie) and I (Michelle) are part of
the "letterbox newbies." Like several others, I read about
letterboxing in the August edition of Family Fun Magazine and we set
off that evening to find our first letterbox - "Chickadee Heaven" in
Kensington Park. We had to go back the next day armed with a stamp
pad, stamp and logbook so we could officially log in and then we were
off! Since that night, we have found about 25 boxes and recently
scoped out our first hiding spot.

We live in Brighton, MI and have been very fortunate to have quite a
few great planters (PennyPenny & Flotilla the Hun just to name a
couple of our favorites) in the area to keep us busy. (Thank you to
everyone who has placed a box-- we truly appreciate it!) My hope is
to get over my fear of carving our own stamps and start placing more
boxes soon. Once I do get to carving, "Lanzi Ladies" will most
likely morph into "The Drama Queens"-- it just didn't seem like the
bear stamp we have been using fit the new title-- so keep an eye out
for our lovely new stamp (it's bound to appear anytime now...)!
I work for a theater company by day and anyone who has raised
daughters (mine are almost 11 and 5) knows that "Drama Queens" will
be a perfect name for us-- I just hope we're not breaking any
etiquette by changing our name midway through the game...

What I love most about letterboxing is that it is a great excuse to
get my girls up off the couch and outside in the beautiful parks that
are all around us. They don't even realize that they are exercising
when they are on the hunt-- it's wonderful! We have introduced my
parents (Crazy Grandma & Grandpa) to letterboxing as well which has
created some wonderful days of hanging together in the woods and then
finding a great restaurant to recount our journey. My parents just
returned from a week in Cape Cod where they found about 8 boxes--
most of them just steps from where we have walked in the past! My
Mom recently went through a bout with lung cancer. This has been one
of the best things for her in getting her up and out walking-- I
cannot say enough about how thankful I am to have discovered this new
hobby for our family! My only wish was that my husband was more
interested-- we managed to get him out once. It was nice to hear
that I am not the only one in this boat. I think it is good for the
girls to have things that they share with just me, though-- it's like
our little secret world...

I hope to meet some of you on the trails and at some future
gatherings. Until then, Happy Letterboxing!
