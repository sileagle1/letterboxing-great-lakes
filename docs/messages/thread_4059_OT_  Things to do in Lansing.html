From: rick dannenberg (doglvr895@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-05-17 07:05:04 UTC-07:00
Lots to do in Lansing...however, just so you know -- you will be in East Lansing, a seperate town, sort of. ; )
There is one letterbox placed on the campus of MSU. (Red Cedar box) It's location is fairly unique...I remember the campus is very CLEAN...so, possibly not to many woodsy places...Spartan Fans? Do you know?

Not real far away is the Impression 5 museum - a great hands on museum for kids.
The Capitol building is a fun excursion - and just outside the Hall of Justice is a really cool memorial with a super sweet "surpise" for kids....well, my kids thought it was "da bomb".
In nearby Okemos, there is a very nice park with a neat letterbox (Meridian Historical Village).
Also -- Potters Park is a great way to spend the afternoon.

Hope this helps!
Mrs. Doglvrs

Re: OT: Things to do in Lansing

From: jwalkersmich (jefflaura88@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2007-05-19 17:13:26 UTC
You might want to take them to a Lugnuts game. The Lansing Lugnuts
are a minor league baseball team. Also, the "Say Yes to Michigan"
letterbox is in a small park located near the state capitol and not
far from the Lugnuts Stadium. You wouldn't have to look for the
letterbox, but a visit to the park may be interesting, since there
is a monument to 9/11. The park is really small and a visit wouldn't
take more than 10-15 minutes, tops. It is interesting, though. The
State Capitol puts on a good tour, but I'm not sure if they do it on
the weekends. I'm guessing you will be there during the weekend.

The MSU Dairy Store has the best ice cream. The "Red Cedar"
letterbox is located on campus and there is a scenic spot where it
is located. There is a small river running through campus and
sometimes you see some canoeists going past. There is a butterfly
house on campus, not far from the Dairy Store. There is a Children's
Garden near the butterfly house, but it's geared towards much
younger children.

The "Impression 5 Science Center" is also geared towards younger
children.
That's about all I can remember from our visit there. You may also
be able to contact someone on campus who can offer suggestions.

Have fun and good luck,
J-Walkers
