For those of you interested in colloquialisms for use in letterboxing,
everyday conversation or intellectual stimulation while at work, you'd
enjoy perusing the Dictionary of American Regional English. It is an
exhaustive multi-volume dictionary which details/defines American
colloquialisms and regional word usage. It is being published (4
volumes and counting so far) by Harvard University.

A short paperback is "How We Talk. American Regional English Today: A
talking tour of American English, region by region" by Allen Metcalf.
It's not a dictionary, but features common slang/regionalisms by state.


Also, I looked up "letter box" in these two sources and found no
entries. I looked up "letter box" in the Oxford English Dictionary and,
surprisingly, found no entry. I looked up "letter box" in the American
Heritage Dictionary, the Random House Dictionary of the English Language
and Webster's Unabridged 3rd and found that "mail box", "letter box"
and "post box" can all be used interchangeably, with the caveat that
"post boxes" are usually thought of as public boxes used for depositing
outgoing mail, while "letter box" is chiefly a British term, as you are
probably aware, for "mail box" which can be both a public depository for
out going mail and/or as a privately owned mailbox (at home, for
example) where mail is received.

None of the following were listed in any of these sources:
"letterboxing", "letterboxers", "stamping up", "azobox".
