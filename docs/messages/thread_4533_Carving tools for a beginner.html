Re: [LbGLK] Carving tools for a beginner

From: TJ_Mich (tj_mich@ameritech.net) | Date: 2007-10-26 05:30:15 UTC-07:00
Many folks use just the Exacto knife for the entire carving, and find that this works well.

I find that I use it for the majority of the work, but also use one of the Speedball U-shaped blades (the official name is "lino cutter") to more quickly remove material in the larger areas. If you can only get one, I'd choose the smallest blade you can find, #1 if possible. You can always use it several times to carve what you need, but you can't use a larger blade to get the same amount of detail.

On stampeaz.com the lino cutters are 1.29 each, or 10.75 for an assortment of 5 plus a handle.

Re: [LbGLK] Carving tools for a beginner

From: (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2007-10-26 13:13:30 UTC
Hi Sara,

I was for several years exclusively an Exacto carver and was quite happy with the results. I still use the Exacto for the majority of my carving -- always for letters and details, but find myself reaching for the Speedball gouges to clean away large areas and to make random lines on the surface. I find it easier to get the rough-hewn/woodcut look of carving lines when cleaning with the gouges, whereas when I clear with the Exacto, I tend to end up with a very clean background, which is sometimes desirable, sometimes not -- strictly a matter of preference.

As previously mentioned, you should try to pick up the entire Speedball carving tools set with all 5 blades and experiment with them to find what you like best. If you don't go for the entire set, get the handle and the two gouges -- small "V" and the "U"

SpringChick
