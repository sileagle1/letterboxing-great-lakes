Cell phone coverage out in the wild....

From: azobox (jparkerg@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-10-11 14:25:42 UTC
Since many experienced letterboxers suggest carrying a cell phone
while in the woods, I'm wondering about your coverage.
I am about to change carriers due in part to my discovery that upon
several occasions recently while placing boxes, I was totally out of
touch. (Those who have searched for "80 Days" boxes may know some of
the sites. )
Yesterday I was placing a series for the "Get A Clue" gathering at
Yankee Springs (in S.W. MI) and was surprised to see that I was not
able to use the phone despite proximity to reasonably populated areas.
I am an infrequent cell phone user, but when I want it I don't like
discovering that I'm out of reach/touch.

So, what's your experience with your phone while you're in the wilds?
Am I expecting too much?

As I've shopped the various providers I've discovered that they tend
to (1) badmouth their competition and (2) brag about their own coverage.
Why am I not surprised?

If you don't mind, please share your thoughts on this.

Thanks,

azobox

Re: [LbGLK] Cell phone coverage out in the wild....

From: Melisa Hills (mshills1@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2005-10-11 09:54:35 UTC-05:00
I can rate 3 carriers. I am in Sussex, WI and my husband works in downtown
Milwaukee, but is all over the city at times at their differing sites, as
far out as Oconomowoc and even into Madison on occassion.

My phone: Cingular's coverage is for the birds - that stay put in one spot
- preferably not in my home. My phone hardly works without lots of issues
here in Sussex. I used to work with a lady who had similar issues in Meno.
Falls - I get dropped and drive through dead/very weak zones
frequently. The only reason we have this carrier is because that is who we
get the cheap phone deal through my husband's work.

My husband's actual work phone is through Verizon (his boss needed what
services they offer, LOL, for their jobs). The coverage is much better.
Much fewer droppings, etc.

However, Neither of them had good signal's when we went camping at Mauthe
Lake this fall....thankfully it did not rain - we had planned on relying on
access to the web through his Blackberry and it was a no go.

Now, our favorite, which we do not currently have was U.S. Cellular. We
loved them - we never got dropped anywhere, it seemed! It even worked out
at Mauthe Lake, LOL! We had them for years and years before hubby had to
change jobs. It worked as we drove to Oklahoma numerous times along the
whole stretch of highway. The ONLY place it used to not work and that had
changed the last time we were there and still had the phone (about 2 ys
ago) was down near the cave in Meramec State Park Missouri. It was down in
a deep valley in the forest and it was surrounded by rock,
LOL! Originally, it was a no cell hole, but that is not the case now.

Hope that helps!

The Cat Herders
