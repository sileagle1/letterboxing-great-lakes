Police Log:

911 received several calls at 0649 regarding a woman in a state of
dishabille, jumping up and down with a rubber chicken and screaming
at the top of her lungs. This officer was dispatched and arrived at
the scene at 0654. Paramedics were called as the woman appeared to
have a serious head wound; however, upon closer examination I
discovered that the substance was not blood but large amounts of red
ink. The woman would not calm down and, fearing for my safety, I
attempted to take my bullet from my pocket. Before I could do so,
the woman attempted to stab me with several long hat pins.
Fortunately, a passerby leaped the fence and pushed the woman away
from meall the time yelling "Citizen's Arrest! Citizen's Arrest!"

I was able to wrestle the woman into the back of my police cruiser.
She remained distraught and I heard her rambling on about odd things
like tiptoeing in old shoes? and something about her rubber chicken
or was it a spring chicken?? She appeared to calm down at the police
station when I inked her fingers to take her fingerprints. Then she
started looking furtively about the room and muttering about
cooties. I assured her that the cells were thoroughly sanitized
between "guests".

After processing, I gave her the phone to make her one call-- to her
husband. Even from across the room, I could hear his voice shouting
about "this blasted BINGO game". Based upon his other comments, I
don't believe that he will be posting bail anytime soon.

Report submitted by Deputy Fife
