The weather turned out to be GREAT! By the afternoon, the skies were clear, clear blue, but man was it MUGGY! And Oh! the MOSQUITOES! Horrible. Truly, truly horrible. Those who brought mosquito nets (no joke) actually had a leg up on the rest of us.

The food was great (whoever made that cheesecake/Cinnamon thing, thanks a lot. The sheer deliciousness of that earned me a big old hour run today), and the boxes! It was like the Easter egg hunt I took my daughter on, so many you didn't know where to start! (The Gathering, was, however, missing those psycho parents who insisted on bowling little children over so their kids could get ALL the eggs- but I'm just bitter).

Everyone was super friendly, and as this was my first event, it was so amusing to match faces up with trailnames and stamps in books. Honestly, it was such a friendly group that complete strangers became comrades in a short space of time.

Two K-marthas were there, along with a Marilyn stamp, a LBNW stamp, and so many GREAT "Cooler by the Lake (drake)" boxes. It was nearly overwhelming!

But now, off to go scratch my mosquito bites and start carving more boxes!
Cheers,
Delirium

Gathering

From: azobox (jparkerg@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-06-14 11:45:06 UTC
Congratulations to Tim and all of the others who contributed to a
successful gathering this past weekend.
Like others, I so enjoyed putting faces with names -- that was
probably the highlight for me. Having read your posts or exchanged
email with so many other Great Lakes Area letterboxers, I was excited
about finally meeting you. The great variety of shared stories and
skills nearly overshadowed the hunt for boxes at this event.
And there *were* boxes aplenty!
Among the boxes we did not find was one we were sure had been carried
away by the infamous McHenry Area Giant Mosquitos.
For those attending the event, we were the ones with the full-head
mosquito-netting hoods. For those who did not attend the event, you
might have wanted one. Like most apparel at Saturday's Gathering,
these were not fashion statments.
At one point on Saturday afternoon someone approaching the shelter
from a foray into the woods said that the strong aroma of "Off" was
wafting into the wild and that we seemed to be fumigating an entire
area of the park, but not very effectively.
To all who creatively carved great stamps for this event, Thank you.
To all who labored to hide the boxes, Thank you.
To all who came and shared your wit, your stories, your "show-and-
tell" items, Thank you.
And for those who couldn't make it to this event......I hear that a
date is on the calendar for next year. We missed you this time.

I hope to see you on the trail,

azobox

I wrote it in my calendar.

A suggestion of a location:
Cross Winds Marsh is a place that has a very cool (screened and roofed)
shelter. It is in Wayne County - what is very interesting is that this
wetlands is less than 10 years and was built to remediate the lose of
wetlands when the addition to Detroit Metro was made. I found it
looking for a letterbox by Mirkwood
(http://www.ruthannzaroff.com/letterboxing/). What I love about the
place is there is hiking, water, canoeing and EAGLES are nesting
there. When I was there last August I saw one adult and two very large
babies! Just a suggestion, I'll actually go anywhere in SE MI I'm
easy! http://www.waynecounty.com/parks/cwinds_park.htm
