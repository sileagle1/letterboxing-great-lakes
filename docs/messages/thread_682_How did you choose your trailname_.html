How did you choose your trailname?

From: the_cichlid_guy (the_cichlid_guy@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-09-17 22:33:49 UTC
With all the cool trail names everyone has, I thought this would be a
cool topic for discussion. How did everyone choose their trail names?
My friend started my trail name The Cichlid Guy. I have a couple of
aquariums and keep African Cichlids in them. My friend was joking
with me one day, telling me that I have too many fish and called me
the Cichlid Guy. The nickname has stuck ever since LOL. Have a great
day, Happy Letterboxing

-Ryan
The Cichlid Guy

From: aine_42 (aine_42@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-09-17 23:02:22 UTC
Every time I see your name, I think "Chiclet" (like the gum). :)
Had no idea what a chichlid was. Some of them are kind of pretty
little fish.

FYI -- There are also some posts on this topic in the archives,
starting at about post #359 (I think it became a TOTW subject
one week), if you want to dig back through those. There are
a bunch of new members in the group since then, though. It'll be
fun to hear some of the new stories.

Here's what I had written for mine:
Mine's a fairly simple explanation: I'm part Irish and part Czech,
as is a friend of mine. We decided to come up with our own
ethnic group: "Czeltic." And since my blog's already named
Czeltic Girl, I just adopted it for the trail as well.

My stamp, on the other hand, is a nod to my other hobby of
collecting squished pennies. (The kind you do in machines,
not the kind you leave under trains.)

Czeltic Girl

From: (papeseed@tds.net) | Date: 2004-09-18 10:23:03 UTC-05:00
My trailname came from a mix of my love for both gardening and genealogy. Pape was my maiden name (pronounced the German way, like "poppy"). Hence, Papeseed has a double meaning! My new personal stamp is of a poppy flower.

My husband Mike has begun using the trailname Triclops. No, his stamp is not of a 3-eyed monster. The three "eyes" of his Triclops are actually 3 letter "i's" that stand for:
Intelligence, Individuality, and Imagination -- three things that he values. He is still working on a design for his new personal stamp.

As for our 5-yr old son, Escher...he is too young to decide on trailname yet. But his name is pretty unique anyway... so maybe people think it IS a trailname (and yes, he is named after M.C. Escher).

Being new to this list and letterboxing, I appreciate this thread of discussion, as I'm curious how others have chosen their trailnames. I'm going to try to access the archived posts, but not exactly sure how to do it. --Papeseed

My "spirit animal", or totem if you wish, is the coyote. The coyote is a trickster in native american lore, so I do love solving riddles and clues to find boxes. I have a tattoo of a coyote on my right shoulder with a spirit line (an arrow) on it.

So for my trail name I chose "Laughing Coyote" and my stamp is a variation between my tattoo and the logo for my bakery business.
