From: Scoutdogs (DogScouts@hotmail.com) | Date: 2007-03-06 13:03:16 UTC-05:00
I have a carving tutorial that has some great close up photos that can help with the carving and image transfer questions:
http://sunnisan.com/jacqui/tute-carving.html

As for the coloring, I'd say 99% of my stamps are line images that I stamp in black. The color is then added with colored pencils later.

To use markers to color in a stamp, directly on the stamp:
you can use as many colors as you like and it's OK if they dry before you're ready to stamp. because just prior to pressing the stamp to paper, you re-wet the ink by exhaling slowly on the stamp. You want the moisture from your breath to moisten the ink, but don't exhale to hard or the air flow created dries the ink faster than the moisture can work :-) If you have a super fine mister (atomizer) you can use that too.

When I looked for letterboxes, I only carried a fanny pack with paper to stamp on, my black ink pad (I know, I'm not very creative), a compass, a few paper towels and baggies and some clean-up bags (because I usually had a dog with me.) I also added a small flashlight after too many times of "just-one-more-itis" that had me searching or leaving a trail in the dark. :-)

Vanman, please don't use alcohol wipes on rubber, alcohol can degrade rubber or cause it to get sticky. A wet paper towel in a baggies is all you really need. Save the alcohol pads for first aid :-)

As for letterbox containers, most of mine were microboxes so I used a lot of film canisters and pill bottles. I LOVE the cloth camo tape used for gun stocks and bows (found in sporting goods stores that carry those things.) The cloth hides the box much better than the plastic camo tape and certainly better than no covering. A little bit of plastic sticking out from under a rock or pile of sticks can stick out like a sore thumb and invite the curious to investigate further. When I used the cloth tape, I accidentally dropped a pill bottle letterbox in the leaves and even though it was right in front of me, I had a hard time finding it! I have found some of my boxes completely uncovered and exposed when doing maintenance checks, but they were still in place because that tape doesn't stand out. After awhile it picks up a bit of dirt too, which helps it blend in even more. Can you place boxes without the tape? Sure! But I think the box is safer with some type of camouflage.

From: Irish Bain (irishbain@rocketmail.com) | Date: 2007-03-07 12:27:03 UTC
There's been a LOT of discussion about camo tape since the nonsense in
Boston. The concern is that average Joe Blow stumbling across a
'mysterious' package that has clearly been wrapped for disguise will
over react and yell for the bomb squad. Some locations that allow
boxes have already stated they must be clear, so that contents may be
identified without opening the box.

I personally have switched to doing partial camo. I'm wrapping camo
around the sides in a compromise, leaving the top and bottom of the
container clear. Being an urban boxer, I'd really rather avoid public
panics :)
