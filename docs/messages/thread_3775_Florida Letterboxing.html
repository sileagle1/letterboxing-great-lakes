From: cdem_ltrbox (wp4@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2007-02-22 15:17:04 UTC
Florida had a period of about 5 days when it was abnormally cooland
those were the 5 days we were there visiting my folks. We did a
little letterboxing. We went to Lakes Park in Ft. Myers and
confirmed that 2 of our boxes (Warbling Snowbird and Peek-a-Boo
Seashell) were indeed MIAprobably the victim of park maintenance and
relandscaping. We never got to see their logbooks )~: At least
Sandy Dollar was still there. We also had a chance to find several
other letterboxes in the park, placed by StarSAELS. As we were
looking through the various logbooks, it was fun to see some familiar
stamps: Tiptoe & Tonto, WI Hiker, and Martini Man! We were able to
move along a HH that we had found in Michigan.

We also checked on our Wild Pigs Run Amuck box (those pigs are a real
problem in my folk's part of FL). That box was OK as well. It was
interesting to see that most of the stamps must have been vacationers
there were stamps from a number of states. One entry even said they
had seen 6 piglets as they were searching down the box.

While we didn't get a whole lot of letterboxing done this trip, I did
get to see a lot of birds41 different species. And one new bird to
add to my life lista hooded merganser. I love being able to combine
these 2 hobbies.
