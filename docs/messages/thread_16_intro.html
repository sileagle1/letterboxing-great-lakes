From: azobox (jparkerg@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-03-23 12:20:23 UTC
Hi All,
I'm azobox and my Lb partner is Artistamp. We're in Kalamazoo and
have been letterboxing since July 2002. Artistamp saw an article in
the Washington Post on a Saturday morning and we were off with the
clues in hand that same day. The same two boxes in Saugatuck that
drew SpringChick also beckoned us.
Since then we have logged finds in MI, DE, MD, NM, IL, and probably
some I'm forgetting.

We enjoy being outdoors and often comment that without letterboxing
we never would have visited many of the out-of-the-way places, even
in our own community. Initially stamp carving was a formidable part
of the game for me, but now it's a part that I especially enjoy.
Scoping out good sites for boxes and writing clues that challenge the
seekers are also high points. I've got a couple of unpublished boxes
in urban settings that were a great winter diversion.
One of our thrills is meeting other letterboxers on the trail.
I'm glad to have a regional list that will allow Great Lakes area
letterboxers an opportunity to share concerns and stories.

Like Tom & Jill, we fondly recall the 2003 Great Lakes Gathering and
hope that another might come to pass.

azobox

From: Anne S. (aine_42@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-03-29 22:59:15 UTC
Hi all. I'm finally getting time to post something to the list.

Looks like I'm not alone in being a relative newbie to letterboxing.
Discovered it when someone in an online art group I belong to
mentioned it in a post. Got intrigued and had to go Googling. I've
never been a huge hiker (I'm pretty much an indoor person and
my allergies make non-urban hiking kind of less-than-fun a lot of
times), but I love the treasure hunt aspect of letterboxing and the
stamps. Besides, anything that forces me to exercise can't be all
bad, right? :-)

I found my first box last summer here in the Milwaukee, WI area.
Have found about 20 since then. (Would have been more this
weekend, had we not gotten caught in the torrential downpour
while out hunting for all the Brown Deer Park boxes.) I haven't
placed any yet, but I'm working on some ideas for them. Just
have to find the time to do the carving and the placing.

Took my mom out on a hunt for a box last weekend and she
seems to have gotten the bug. (Yay! Someone to hunt with!) I
even carved a stamp for her. (It's the best stamp I've done so far.
It was hard to let go of it!)

Managed to get out for a little bit on Saturday afternoon and was
heading for the Lucky Leprechaun box. Kept seeing someone a
bit ahead of me on the trail. It never occurred to me it could be
another letterboxer until I saw him disappear into the woods
where I needed to go. I followed him in and got to meet The
Dragon. It was a really nice surprise. He's the first other
letterboxer I've ever met!

I'm looking forward to meeting many more of you and finding and
planting a ton of boxes.

Anne
(Czeltic Girl)

From: Matt Bergstrom (ratpatrolhq@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-04-02 09:57:26 UTC-08:00
Hi everyone,

Just thought I should introduce myself to the new
list. I met some of you all at the gathering last year
and hopefully will meet more at this years event.

I heard about letterboxing a few years ago from my
brother, who is into geocaching (and a techie
engineer). But letterboxing is more up my alley, since
I love book arts, puzzles, maps, local history, and
general hiking & exploring. You may wonder about my
trail name, which came from a bike club I started
(look up Rat Patrol on google if you are interested),
but it fits with the kind of places I often end up
hiking, the places where nature is reclaiming
artificial landscapes. I would love to hide
letterboxes in abandoned factories and under railroad
bridges, but I'm a little worried about any of you
getting hurt (or caught) looking for them, so I'll
stick to safer areas!

In the last year a lot of things have conspired to
keep me away from letterboxing, but I promise I will
soon replace the Chicago Underground & Magic Hedge
boxes that have been lost, and hide a few more.

Matt the Rat
