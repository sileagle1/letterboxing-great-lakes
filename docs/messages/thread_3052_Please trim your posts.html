That's not true. If you double click in the message text, you can
edit it as usual including deleting it. The header with the To: From:
Subject: etc. is separate from the message text. Just double click on
the text of the message and you will then see the text box around it.
You can then highlight all of it (or part of it) and hit delete.

Perhaps an easier route to fix the issue (than to try to figure out
what each persons computer is capable of - each persons Email client
works differently) would be for people to go login to the Yahoo group
site and Click on Edit Membership at the top of the page and then
Change your Message Preference to TRADITIONAL rather than the "New
and Improved" (NOT) Fully Featured Version. The only thing that I
like about the New and Improved stuff is the Digest - it is great,
but otherwise, forget it! On the groups that I get individual emails
from, I have changed it to traditional. Even the groups that I have
digest on, if I think I will be replying regularly to the group, I
change the digest to Traditional. Just something to think about.

Unfortunately, being on Digest has its pitfalls and remembering to
Snip is one of them. At one time or another, we all forget - and
sometimes, people are not aware that there are a ton of emails below
that were not snipped. I am guessing for many of us, this is not the
only Yahoo group we are on and therefore, we are used to working
around it. You soooo do not want to know how many groups I am on,
LOL! Anyway, I hope the first paragraph is helpful.
