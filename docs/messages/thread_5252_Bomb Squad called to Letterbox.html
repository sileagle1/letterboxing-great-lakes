Bomb Squad called to Letterbox

From: Jody Klein (jodyscout@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-08-15 05:42:52 UTC-07:00
My husband heard this morning on his way into work on our local radio station that the bomb squad was called to inspect a suspicious tupperware container covered in camo tape yesterday. Attached is the article from the newspaper with certain parts "blocked out" as this was a mytery box and may reappear at a later date.
They said on the radio that it turns out that the package is part of an on-line scavenger hunt and that no arrests are planned.
Mark said he wished he had heard about it yesterday...he would have gone over and said, hey...I'll open it and show you what's inside!! Not that they would have let him near it.
FYI, our local county park where we will be having The Great Literary Quest is allowing boxes but only inside clear containers so the contents can be seen.
Just thought you all would want to hear about this.
Foodie Mom of The K Crew

From: Sara (fivestmc5@earthlink.net) | Date: 2008-08-16 15:06:24 UTC
The owner of this box called me shortly after it being reported on the
local radio station, she was quite upset, mostly that she was out of
town and didn't know when it was happening so she was unable to clear
it up then.
But the reason I am replying is to say, hey aren't you glad they
assumed it was a Geocache and didn't call it a letterbox in the
reports, that way we don't have people around here logging onto atlas
quest and muggling our boxes with no intentions of boxing but to find
out what these mysterious boxes are that the police thought was a
bomb? If I heard that report on the radio happening right here in my
own town (which it was) and didn't have any clue about either of these
hobbies than I'm be googling it and out to at least see one of these
things. Heck I already went out in search of a Geocache just to see
what was so different with no intention to ever become a Geocacher,
just curiosity got to me. At least I could give respect to the box no
matter which sport, but college kids may not, and this is a college
town.
