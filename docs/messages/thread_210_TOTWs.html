Stamp carving-

My first thought was "Ha! I never make mistakes while carving!"
then I remembered carving some letters backwards. Then I remebered
chopping off part of the image. Ooops!

When I started, I only used an exacto knife. My personal stamp was
made that way and it still work s pretty well. I still use the
exacto knife for doing most lettering and sharp angles. I use a
Speedball cutter for everything else. I see that one can use a
Dremel tools to carve also. I'm thinking of trying that. Anyone
out there with experience using a Dremel tools to carve?

I pretty much only use the "pink stuff". I prefer it to all other
carving material.

I seem to be different in that when I start carving, I just go at
it, easy and hard parts all at once. I've never made a mistake that
forced me to start over. Lucky me I guess.

I also rotate the the carving material as I cut, and sometimes move
the carving material instead of the cutter to make cuts. I do try
to make the area I carve in a bright as possible, but I found using
a magnifying glass to be more difficult than helpful.

When testing the print, I color it with inking pens as I intend them
to be in the end, then just clean the stamp right away.

Excitement-

I enjoy everything about letterboxing! What excites me the most is
seeing a new box placed in my area no matter how easy or tough, the
area, the hike, or the carving! I guess you could say that I still
have the "newbie" bug even after nearly two years!

I've enjoyed most, if not all, of the letterboxes that I've found
for many different reasons. Ones that stick out are-

The three in mid-MO - great hikes, great places to visit. One I had
to wade through waist-deep swiftly flowing water to get to due to
the thunderstorm the night before, but it was fun!

Most of SpringChicks boxes - particularly those in Muskegeon SP and
the thought-provoking mystery boxes. The decoding of the Ghosts
Below box was most fun and interesting!

Monopoly Railroad - I was amazingly the first finder of the whole
series, tho I was beaten to one box by Stampin' Pam (if memory
serves). It is a very neat idea that spurred ideas for boxes of my
own. (I don't want to give anything away.)

I'm sure I'm leaving something out...
