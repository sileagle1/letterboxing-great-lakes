From: speedsquare_lbxr (ruhlette@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-01-18 18:41:11 UTC
Yeah to Minnesota and RedCat!! For those of you wondering, I have been checking out
locations for a large gathering in Indiana that will meet the needs for a large gathering.
One that has good camping facilities, large shelters, good trails and other park attractions.
And convenient for me and other travelers.

The past 2 years were a bit rough trying to manage 2 houses and all that goes with that.
We are down to one house now and recovering from the stress. What I learned from trying
to reserve a state park shelter/location is that I can reserve one up to 1 year in advance. It
is really too early to reserve one for 2009, but if the consensus of the group is that 2009
will be Indiana's turn, I would like to have permission to procure a site about June 1 of this
year. The big shelters seem to get reserved early.

For the sake of discussion, my preference would be one of the following:

1. Pokagon - easy access to the Indiana Toll Road, not far from my family's lake house. It
has 2 large shelters and multiple trails. Camping, Park Inn, water activities and horse
trails.
2. Whitewater SP/Brookville Reservoir - closest to my home; near other local letterboxers.
Camping, moderate to rugged trails, boating, horse trails, fishing. Not far from I-74, and
box-filled SE IN, SW OH. May be a bit warmer than northern parks.
3. Turkey Run - A beautiful park in Indiana. Inconvenient for me to get to, so I'd need to
recruit many more boxers to plant. Has an Inn/Restaurant on site.

If there are others in Indiana who would want to help me, or have another location in mind
for 2009, please contact me. It is unfortunate, but the past 2, and the next 2 gatherings
will fall on the day of, or the day after my dh's graduations. Being the principal, I can't ask
him to skip those events.

I've got to make plans to get to Minnesota now.

~speedsquare

We were at Pokagon last fall--it was wonderful. Walked a little bit
on the trails. They have a nice nature center. Don't know what the
camping facilities are like since we were there in November, but the
inn is marvelous. Lots of common areas to sit and read or play games
or whatever. Long passageway with lots of comfie chairs overlooking
the lake. The lake/beach is right by the inn. They have a very nice
Sunday brunch on Sunday mornings. Plus, they have an organized
birdwalk early every Sunday morning :~)

From: (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2008-03-19 08:06:09 UTC-06:00
The Red Cat has created a blog for the event. It lists information about the event so far. If you're coming from out of town and want to plant a box, you can either mail the box to The Red Cat to plant for you, or you could plant it yourself late Friday or early Saturday and then write out clues for people. You could get copies of the clues made (in the past we've had copies made at our hotel or a nearby office store) or just post a master copy at the event and have people copy them down by hand.

Here's the link to the event info:

http://glg2008.blogspot.com/2010/02/land-of-10000-lakes.html

From: Heather (the_red_cat529@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-03-20 00:08:55 UTC
Yup, there's a lot of information on the blog. It hasn't been updated
as often as I'd like due to midterms and being sick with whatever that
nasty bug was that's going around. Expect more information on the
potluck, etc shortly.
If you'd like to send a box for the gathering, just let me know if
you'd like me or one of my helpers hide it and if you want it to be
temporary or permanent. If you are sending it, I'd like it by May
1st. Of course, you are welcome to hide your own if you plan on
making it to the park before the gathering. In that case, you'll have
to distribute the clues on your own. There will be a spot for clues
like that at the gathering.

I took Gooble Gobble's list of box ideas and posted it on the blog. I
took out the ones that have been claimed and added a few others. If
you want more ideas or have questions about some of the things on the
list, feel free to ask me! Here's a link to the list:

http://glg2008.blogspot.com/2008/03/box-ideas.html

The list of boxes that have been taken is here:
http://glg2008.blogspot.com/2008/02/list-of-boxes.html

-The Red Cat
