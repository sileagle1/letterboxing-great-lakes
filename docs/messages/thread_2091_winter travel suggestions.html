From: klustofin (katy@dsa132.org) | Date: 2005-12-12 17:01:26 UTC
Soon I will be making my annual trek "over the river and through the
woods." Last year I tried visiting a few boxes that were absolutely
hopeless in the snow - it's hard to find a dry creekbed under a foot
of snow, and harder still to differentiate between that and a path
that sees little traffic. So I thought I would ask here if any one
can suggest some boxes close to my travel route that might be findable
even under snow. I'll be traveling along I-70 between Indianapolis
and Columbus and then north along I-71 to I-90. I'm not necessarily
looking for drive-bys, because I like getting out of the car and
letting the dogs get some exercise, but it would have to be super box
for me to drive more than 30 minutes out of my way.

Thanks!

Katy

From: Doodle & Deedle Bug (doodle_n_deedle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-12-12 11:13:15 UTC-08:00
I-71 to I-90, oh wait, that's my neck of the woods!
Our Kumbaya box located in Bonnie Park (just a few
minutes away from I-71) should be a winter friendly
find (unless the box is frozen in its hiding spot) and
although a relatively quick find, the park would be a
good place for the dogs to stretch their legs. The
clue page says where to get off of I-71 & directions
to the park.

There was a recent plant by us called Kiddie Park,
that box should be only about a 5 to 10 minute drive
from the W130th exit of I-71. No idea how winter
friendly a find it would be, though - haven't had a
chance to find it yet (and we live right down the road
- bad letterboxers!). I do know that if it's snowy,
the park is one of THE sledding places in the area, so
it may be too crowded to find the box.

There are also quite a few boxes hidden in Medina
County (the county just south of Cuyahoga, where
Kumbaya is hidden) that may not be too far from your
travel route. The clues for Kelmark #1 - Plum Creek
say it's not far from I-71, but I don't remember how
winter-friendly the search might be; I know we had
some trouble finding the one path and it was
springtime when we went (but that was also on account
of us making assumptions instead of really following
the clues). We haven't had a chance to hunt most of
the rest of the Kelmark boxes, so not sure how close
to the freeway or winter-friendly those would be.

Have fun on your trip, and good luck!

Doodle (and Deedle) Bug.
