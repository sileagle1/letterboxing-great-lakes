From: Kathi (krank@ameritech.net) | Date: 2004-07-26 19:58:28 UTC
This isn't an etiquette issue, but something I discovered this
weekend that I thought might be useful to newbies and unnewbies alike.

We were looking for more hiding places at camp this weekend, where
there are wonderful huge junipers with their multiple twisted trunks
providing a plethora (love that word!) of hiding places. As it was
dusky in the woods, I had a small flashlight with me. When I shined
it into one "hole" around the back of a tree to check out how deep it
was, Papillon noticed she could see the light from the front. It
struck me that this would be a good way to check the visibility of a
hiding spot from different angles, to use the flashlight test. Hope
this helps!
