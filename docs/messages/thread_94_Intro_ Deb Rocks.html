Intro: Deb Rocks

From: debrocks54 (deb.maertens@stpaul.com) | Date: 2004-04-06 20:16:57 UTC
Greetings all! Dragon and Wisconsin Hiker told me about this group a
few weeks ago and I'm finally getting around to joining in.

I feel like I know a lot of you through various email correspondences
I've had with you since I started letterboxing last October. And, of
course, I met Dragon, Acorn, Leapin Lizards, Wisconsin Hiker, Martini
Man, Agent Ink, and others (I know I'm missing names) at a recent
gathering in Milwaukee. While it was a gathering of mostly Wisconsin
letterboxers, everyone was very welcoming to me (a Minnesota
letterboxer). It was great meeting you all and I hope to see some of
you again, and meet many more of you at the Great Lakes Gathering in
June.

Minnesota is sadly lacking in letterboxes right now; although I'm
trying to remedy that. I've now planted 9 (clues to the most recent 5
to be posted very shortly), plus 2 hitchhikers.

I haven't found as many boxes as I'd like to by now. We just don't
have that many here. Yet. I have been able to make a few brief trips
to Illinois and Wisconsin to do some hunting. I'd love to get further
afield sometime this summer. Maybe Michigan? The company I work for
recently merged with a company out in Connecticut. I keep telling my
boss they need to send me out there for business. I can always stay
an extra few days and do some hunting. I hear you virtually trip over
them out there, there are so many!

I orginally got into letterboxing after reading an article in a
stamping magazine. I remember the first time I read the article for
some reason it didn't appeal to me.(Don't know why. I love hiking,
the outdoors, puzzles, art, stamping.)

I read the article again, a few weeks later and it just hit me, I had
to start doing this immediately! I went out and bought materials that
day and was carving stamps later that day and hunting for my first
box the next day. The personal stamp I used in my first few hunts was
very big and not a great representation of me, but I just had to get
out there! After my first few finds I decided I really needed to get
myself a trail name and a stamp to match it.

One of my favorite things, after family, friends and my dogs, are
rocks. I just love rocks! I have them all over my house, garage,
yard, car, work. I carry them in my pockets. Well, you get the
picture... That's how I arrived at my trail name and my stamp. The
stamp was still an early effort and probably too big but I've grown
kind of attached to it and, when the logbook is very small, I just
stamp part of it.

I look forward to participating in group discussions and hopefully
meeting many of you in June. Great to finally have a group geared
towards Midwest/Great Lakes LBrs! Thanks Spring Chick for getting it
going! And thanks Dragon for organizing our upcoming gathering!

Deb Rocks
