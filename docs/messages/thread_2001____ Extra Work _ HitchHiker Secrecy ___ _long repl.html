Journey,

I like your trail name, did I mention that before? The reason I like
it, is because it is a reminder for me that the destination isn't
always what's important. "Journey" reminds me to enjoy the act of
reaching the destination and it fits nicely with why choose to be a
sailor. "Journey" also evokes, for me, thoughts of growth and discovery.

On the evening I discovered letterboxing, I spent almost the whole
night roaming from site to site drinking in as much information as I
could. The next morning, I immediately carved my first stamp (hacked
an old eraser with a kitchen knife) created my first box, made a
signature stamp for my husband and myself and placed the box. I
continued to read as much online info as I could. I felt I had a
pretty good handle on understanding how things were done and what the
generally accepted practices were.

What I'd like to share with you is that despite all the hours, and
yes, it was hours, I spent educating myself I still committed some
blunders. Here's the first one: My trail name is Bindle Babe because I
somehow thought my husband and I needed to have the same signature
stamp. I knew I wanted it to reflect our passion for sailing so I knew
I wanted a sailboat image. Here's the "duh" part for years hubby has
called himself a sailing bum. So I had Bindle (the first part of our
boat name) and Bum excellent! Double letters are always easy memory
hooks. OK .. a sailboat and 2 B's were going on the stamp. But what
to call myself that started with a B (HUSH UP you smarty pants in the
peanut gallery I am NOT itchy!) hmmmm you can see where this is
going can't you? Yes, unimaginative as it is and as unlike reality as
it is, I chose "babe"

If I hadn't been so enthusiastic, I would soon have realized I didn't
have to do it that way at all. Oh well, I had posted the clues to my
first box as Bindle Babe and so Bindle Babe I remain.

Not so terrible, but still it was because I didn't even know what I
didn't know. Now let me share a really big mistake I made with you:

In Spring 2004 I had been boxing for just a few months. I went to
Portland, OR for an artistic event, unrelated to letterboxing.
However, I was able to combine my new-found hobby with this trip and
hook-up with some of the PNW boxers while I was out there.

One of these boxers and I traded letterboxes. She placed my "Portland
Rose" box (Portland is known as the City of Roses) out there, while I
brought back her "MI or Bust" box and placed it here. We "adopted"
each others box that means we do the maintenance and such on each
others boxes.

Later in the summer of 2004 (I had not yet been boxing for a year
but had quite a few boxes found and placed) when I went to do
maintenance on the "MI or Bust" box, I took photos of the logbook
pages thinking I'd like to share them with the woman who created the box.

I posted a note to their regional list saying, "MI or Bust box
photos" or something like that.

Now let's count the OPPSIES, shall we?

First oopsie: the woman who created the box was also a fairly new
boxer at the time and didn't think to check if that name had ever been
used before. Not good. It had already been used by another, long-time
boxer from the Portland, OR area. Well, she saw the post and thought
I was talking about her box. Of course she was kind of upset at the
thought that no one considered seeing if the name had been used before
she had "first rights" so to speak.

Second oopsie: it's considered very bad form to post images of
someone's signature stamp without their permission. I didn't know
that. Somehow in all the time I spent trying to learn about
letterboxing, I had missed that bit of information.

What I'm trying to say is that we were all new to letterboxing at one
time and no matter how careful, how diligent, how sincere we are
mistakes are sometimes made. If we're gracious, we admit our error,
apologize accept the personal growth and appreciate the fuller depth
of understand we now have.

Next: I hope you didn't interpret the "tone" of my previous post as
of being angry with you for posting the location of my Christmas Swish

hadn't posted that. Kind of like wishing someone hadn't spilled their

SpringChick has done a nice job explaining about HH's and that they
are meant as a surprise and that they belong to the letterboxing
community "in toto" and that exclusivity isn't a part of it.

I am sincerely glad that you find LB.org and AQ to dovetail nicely
with how you'd like to manage your letterbox clues. That's great that

About using "OTHER SITES" There are land-use issues regarding
outdoor activities such as geocaching and letterboxing on state-owned
land here in Michigan. These are the root cause for my choice to keep
some of my clues a little bit more difficult to find. For example,
any of my boxes that are located on State Park property are listed by
name at LB.org. However, when you open the clue page, it contains an
external link to my Yahoogroup site.

I know and understand that it's highly unlikely; given our current
state budget situation; that any State Park employee would be paid to
sit around surfing the internet looking for letterboxers and
geocachers who haven't paid the proper fees (geez oh pizza, I want
that job if it ever comes up!!) But hey, I've seen the State make
priority choices that seem even more strange and bizarre, so I'm not
going to rule out the possibility of it occurring. It's my choice to
use external links as a method of staying below their radar, so to
speak. Like locks on doors it's simpler to move on to an easier
target than it is to futz around hopping to external links and poking
around. Does that make sense to you?

I not going into why I feel it's morally reprehensible for the State
to even have this particular land-use policy and mentality.

Up until now, I haven't shared this kind of personal information with
any but my closest friends, as I'm sure about everyone here can attest
to. I've chosen to do so now because I keep getting requests from
people to post my boxes online so they can log them. I'm hoping that
by educating you and others here about some of the things that are
generally not spoken of the knowledge will begin to make its way into
the hearts and minds of those who also enjoy this letterboxing
activity we share.

I hope you will reconsider your decision to take my little Christmas
