Re: TOTW: What Kind of Clues Turn You On?

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2007-09-05 17:18:21 UTC
Depends on the situation. When I am traveling, I prefer more
straight-forward clues that do not necessarily require computer access
to solve as this is not always available when camping and I am rarely
together enough to do all the required research before I leave on the
trip. But when I am home, I enjoy a good figure-it-out clue,
particularly puzzles and math clues. I have done some awesome story
clues -- Doglvrs OK Corral series comes to mind, and like these. I
love logic puzzles, but there are few letterboxes that use them for
clues, and it is no fun solving my own logic puzzles ;-)

And then some days I just want to be told "turn at the green marker,
go three steps, put your hand in the hollow stump and find the box."

That's one of the things that makes it fun for me -- the variety.
Variety is good. Consistency is boring.

SpringChick

My husband likes clues that involve using his compass, and math. I

We really like clues that are puzzles or require some research. That
was the one aspect that intrigued us the most when we started
letterboxing and it gives us a sense of satisfaction to solve the
puzzle/clues. Since we already hiked quite a bit, the clues were the
novelty for us. (We later also developed a real appreciation for the
stamps.)

We also enjoy a story type clue, whether it is based on true history
or just an amusing tale. These can be very informative &
entertaining while we are on the hunt.

Our very favorite boxes are those that cleverly combine clues,
location and stamp image so that they all hang together as
a "package", with each individual component obviously related to the
theme.
