From: ruhlette (ruhlette@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-08-23 04:08:59 UTC-07:00
I try to use Attempted sparingly. Once I knew some would not go after a
letterbox with one or more attempts by other boxers, I realized that
others might interpret the box is likely missing, without considering the
abilities of the boxers who didn't find it. So they may not even put the
box on their to-do list. I do mark boxes attempted if I am *really sure*
I'm in the right spot and have contacted the placer describing where I
thought it was placed. If they say I was in the correct location, I'll
mark an attempt. Then they can verify its status at a later time. Once I
see the confirmation, I'll go back and try again. There is one box in
Indy that I've attempted many times, but just haven't found yet ... Food
for Thought! Can't tell you how many restaurants I've visited and not
found it yet. I have one or two more in mind, but this box is getting
more and more expensive to find (but I'm not complaining a bit).

Re: "Attempted" Status

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-08-23 11:52:12 UTC
This is a tough call... I think finders tend to use this for a
personal record of "boxes that we did not end up with even though we
tried" (and I use the word *tried* loosely here). This may be after
5 hours of searching the spot where it *should* have been, or it may
be after 5 hours of searching a *fitting* spot in the wrong park.
On the other hand it may be because they got halfway down the trail
and it got too dark to continue or they realized they hadn't done
their homework thoroughly and couldn't decipher a critical piece of
the clue. To them it simply means -- "we need to keep this one on
the to-do list."

On the other hand, more and more people are looking at those
*attempted* status logs and using them to gauge whether or not a box
is in place. A box with several *attempted* reports in a row may
leave potential seekers thinking that the box is likely missing and
deter them from even trying, choosing instead to spend their time on
a box that has had recent found reports. This is too bad because
the attempted reports are not an accurate reflection of the box
availability. Can you imagine if this option had been available for
MapSurfer or Legerdemaine boxes? Those boxes can go unfound for
months, even years, although many try on a regular basis.

I know some placers will make a point to add an *OK* status report
after each *attempted* report just to give people some security that
the failed find is not an indication of a missing box. Personally I
don't feel comfortable putting in an *OK* report unless I have
physically gone out and checked the box and this is not always
possible for me to do at the time.

This is one of the reason that I have the logging feature at LbNA
disabled on my boxes. While people can still log status and I
receive these reports in e-mail so I an follow-up on potentially
missing boxes, the status logging history is not available to
potential seekers. I realize this may turn away a few who were
looking for a guarantee of the box existence, but it is their loss,
not mine -- it probably wasn't the right box for them in the first
place.

When looking for boxes, I very rarely even check the status reports
before going out. Just because somebody else didn't find the box,
doesn't mean that I won't find the box and I really don't even want
to know as it may influence my efforts or make me give up quicker.
Besides, missing boxes are just a part of letterboxing... Some of
the best trail stories come from people's tireless efforts to find
an elusive box.

SpringChick

From: Atom 118 (hersh118@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2006-08-23 10:42:39 UTC-05:00
When we first started, we marked all the boxes we couldn't find as
"attempted" if we were SURE we were in the right place (and these first
boxes we were also influence by other "attempted" listings for the same box)

However, after the first month or two, we changed our minds about this
since we realized how such a designation might affect other boxers (as
we tend to be less inclined to search for a box in a far-off location if
it hasn't recently been found but had also been marked "attempted"
months ago). We try to contact the placer and describe where we think
the box should have been. So far our "attempted" rate is maybe only 50%
(ie, half the boxes where there, but we interpreted the clues
differently)....but we are getting better with more experience (and we
try really hard to read the clue fully!)

Usually, after contacting the placer...the placer might mark the box
"missing" or tell us we had the clue wrong (either way, we no longer
need to mark it as "attempted").

Atom 118

Re: "Attempted" Status

From: Heather (bunnyhugger@ameritech.net) | Date: 2006-08-25 16:52:51 UTC
I mark any box that I am unable to find after a decent search as "attempted," even if
I am not 100% sure that I was in the right place. If I know that I didn't actually get to
the end of the clues for whatever reason (e.g. it was getting dark), I don't consider that
an attempt and don't mark it.

I think marking "attempted" in cases where you just plain couldn't figure it out is also
valuable, even if you don't have reason to think the box is missing. It can help the
placer gauge whether his/her clues are too confusing. I appreciate knowing how often
people are trying to get my boxes and a couple of times I have rewritten clues because
of a high proportion of unsuccessful attempts.

If I tried and couldn't find it but am not confident I was in the right place, I just mark
it as attempted, and let the placer decide whether it's worth investigating. If I am quite
sure I was in the right place and did not find it, then I also send a note suggesting that
the box might be missing.

When looking at boxes to hunt, I am not too dissuaded by seeing one "attempted," but
I am cautious if there have been more than one in a row. I do use it to decide how
confident I am that the box is there in cases where I would be making a long, special
trip to get the box. But if the last find date is a really long time ago, I am equally
suspicious of that.

Heather

Re: [LbGLK] Re: "Attempted" Status

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-08-25 18:57:02 UTC-04:00
I don't think that anyone is implying that you or anyone else who liberally uses the "attempted" status is a lousy letterboxer, rather just trying to point out a different perspective. Clues that may be confusing to one person, causing them to miss a box and thus mark an "attempted" status, may be crystal clear to the next person -- we all interpret things differently and approach clues from a different perspective.

As many people have indicated, they do use the status history as an influence in planning future box hunts, so it only stands to reason that marking a box as "attempted" is not something that should be done haphazardly as it can be like red-flagging a box to future seekers. Even though there are situations where this may obviously be warranted (i.e. the case in point that you mentioned about the long-missing box), there are many other situations where it may just be a difficult box or a misinterpretation of clues.

But... on the flip side, for any planters who feel this is a deterent or wish to maintain more of a mysterious nature with their boxes, there is the option to disable status comments from showing up with your boxes. I have done this and it is not because I don't want people to know the status of my boxes, rather I don't want them to be misinformed about the status of my boxes based on the unsuccessful attempts of previous seekers. I provide a box status with all of my clues and will gladly answer e-mails from potential seekers inquiring about status.

SpringChick
