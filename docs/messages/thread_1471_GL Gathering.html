From: Pale Puppy Posse (advantagek9@voyager.net) | Date: 2005-06-20 16:27:24 UTC-04:00
Well everyone has pretty much said it all but I wanted to put my two cents
worth in.

Meeting everyone and putting faces to trail names was the best part for
sure. Putting your stamp on your name tag was a great idea as most of us
can relate to the stamp when we see it then the light bulb goes off!
Second was ALL the boxes! my goodness. Who would have thought that you
could put that many boxes in one park... plus the WoM boxes and all the
other secret boxes that were out there. Thanks for everyone who shared when
we just happened to stumble upon you! :-)
The Circus was huge! and really awesome to go back and look at over
Father's Day. I showed everybody who came to our house on Sunday whether
they wanted to look at them or not!!
Kind of sad that only a couple people picked up on my personal traveler
hint... but maybe you can search back and get it next time we meet.
The Soul Train box was removed and will be placed nearby... I might have to
make it a mystery box after all the inspiration I got from SpringChicks
workshop.
Naughty Dog talked about Dartmoor the whole way home when he wasn't
snoring. We were so tired. I had a school reunion that night and just
couldn't make myself go. Grandbaby was waiting up for me when I got home
though to see who had stamped into his logbook.
Team Rabbit, you guys picked an awesome park to have the gathering in. We
had never been there before and loved it! You desire a big pat on the back
and then some...
As everyone else has stated Dragon's Mo' Mustang box was the bomb! It took
us three time to get it right.. okay we can be a little slow to catch on
sometimes!
Everyone was awesome and was so nice and friendly. I wish those of you who
didn't get to go this time definitely get to attend the next one.. well
worth the time and effort to go.
Still flying high.... and not because of Dragon's Pot Pi :-)

Can you all stand one more pat on the back and huge thank you to
everyone who put so much effort into making the Gathering so
successful? Good, because Bindle Bum and I would like to echo the
praise that everyone else has posted!

Great fun to head out and hunt up letterboxes with some of the DogLvrs
family, Jacks and the kids. Bindle Bum was amazed at how many folks
got his personal traveler and very happy to discover that Cub and he
share a passion for a particular sport!

The only workshop attended was the Mystery one given by SpringChick
and it was GREAT! (I'll be working on some of those clues with renewed
vigor now ... lol)

The Circus was fantastic, but I don't think we got all of them in the
time we were able to spend on site. Thanks Mark and Sue for bringing
it to share. (Speaking of sharing .... you know how it can be when you
travel and drink the water in strange lands. You're both still healthy
... you didn't get sick or catch the cooties or anything while you
were here or anything, I hope)

Apologies for the short stay, wish I could've spent all day and half
the night with you guys. But I was just coming off twelve 10 hr days
in a row and I promised Bindle Bum ... Gathering until noon and then
we'd go sailing.

Thanks again to everyone for everything, it was a most successful
Gathering! Bindle Bum even said he'd likely come to another one with
me ... especially since our boat is likely to be out of the water by
then. Hope all of you who couldn't make it will be at the next one.

Enjoy!

From: speedsquare_lbxr (ruhlette@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-11-11 13:54:04 UTC
I have June 3rd on my new calendar. My ISP was down all day yesterday so I didn't
participate in the discussion.

I like the set date idea for the GL gathering, wherever it lands each year. In the earlier
years I am sure it was important to set a time when the largest number of letterboxers
could participate. It sounds to me that letterboxing is well established in our region and
no matter what date is picked, a good number of letterboxers will show up. And though I
was unable to attend the Haunted Harvest Gathering, from the discussions and photos I
saw, it sounds like a very big event, what I might have expected from the GL regional
gathering. The Joy in the Journey gathering in Plymouth, IN last May was highly successful
and many letterboxers attended as well. Several of us in Indianapolis put together a small
indoor gathering last March and I think about 30 attended. I had not been to a gathering
before and we didn't want to be overwhelmed by announcing it publicly - space limitations
of the site.

What I see developing is that within each GL state, there may be at least 2 gatherings per
year (that's already happening in some, right?). Ohio, Michigan, IN, IL, WI, and don't forget
Minnesota ... a minimum of 13 gatherings to consider annually, or about one every 4
weeks. And I am looking to go outside our region, to KY, in January. While the spirit is
willing, I know it is impossible to attend all of them. If I miss one, there will be another
opportunity soon. So I fill the empty weekends with letterboxing on my own, or other life
demands.

Whether a gathering is designated the "annual GL gathering" or a local gathering, I would
be willing to pitch in for gatherings outside my area and state. I am putting my desire to
help organize a big Indiana event on hold until I know when I may be relocating, it could
be 3 months, or 9 months, or ???

So, I guess, the solution to the question, might be ... first come, first served in terms of
securing a particular date for a particular gathering. We may come to the point where
there may be 2 gatherings in our region on the same date and we will have to make a
choice. I would just like to repeat something I believe ... we will have fun whether 20 or
200 people are there.

Just my perspective,
speedsquare
