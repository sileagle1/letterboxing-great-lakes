Whew!

From: Armadillo Jo (jes7o@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-06-20 17:32:20 UTC-07:00
We just got back from our trip to MI/OH! First off a
huge THANK YOU to Team Rabbit for putting together
such a fabulous gathering! You guys ROCK!

We set out on Friday and started at Aiden Lair to
finish the 3 boxes we missed before. Isn't it funny
that things that stumped you when you first started
letterboxing, are SO OBVIOUS after time goes by! We
had much better luck this time and quickly found the
three we missed before. We stopped by Hidden Lake
Gardens for the HLG stamp and the Big Rooster. After
that we went to Hell and back. We had so much fun
making "hell puns" such as "Geeze, we're crusin' down
the Highway to Hell!" We walked entirely too far, but
found it on the way back.

Saturday's gathering was overwhelming and great!
People everywhere, and I didn't even meet everyone!
The Circus was never-ending.....just when I thought I
had them all...another bag appeared! The Circus was
simply amazing.....Thank you so much, Mark and Sue,
for bringing it with you. Please thank everyone who
took the time to carve such beautiful stamps!

The WOM clues were great! Thanks to everyone who took
the time to be so devious! The CD's and the can o'
nuts were soooo clever!

We went to Kensington on Sunday morning to find
Delirium's two boxes as a stretcher.....then went back
to Hudson Mills to get the Landmarks series and the
Mo' Mustangs. Team Rabbit, the Landmarks were divine
and Dragon, what can I say, ingenious! My door's a
little crooked, and Ugler's Mustang has
hydraulics.....

We then headed to Maumee for Linda Fay's boxes. We
found Aunt Phoebe's Letterbox with no problems....but
the British Kept Coming...well, let's just say that we
had a missing fort, it was removed from the maps, and
we had no younglings to give us the obvious answer! We
did find our missing fort AFTER reading the back of a
sign.....and it was worth it! Great box, with a good
history lesson!

This morning we went to Avon to find some Duck Tape.
We found no Duck Tape. I am heartbroken. I couldn't
speak for nearly 40 minutes.

After I recovered, we went to Defiance to find a bunch
of new A-Team boxes....got the Dam Stamp 2 (we just
love those Dam Stamps!) and Light and Dark (very zen)
at Independence Dam and headed to the cemetery to find
Charmed, Alias, and Thunder Cats. The Alias box was a
doozy, as my Teva's didn't have enough traction to get
up the hill without sliding back down. We got the box
with the use of a stick, and Ugler. Then to Oxbow Lake
for the Star Wars series! The Rebels and the Empire
were found no prob, but a weirdo was hanging out near
Boba Fett....Ugler distrated him TWICE so I could get
the box out and back in.

Tiptoe and Tonto: Glad to see you got the box planted!
I'm sooooo excited! I'm going to find it pretty
soon...

It was a lot of fun, and great to meet everyone! It's
nice to put a face with a name and stamp! I'm probably
going to drag some newbie boxers to at least one of
the gatherings in the fall...so I hope to see everyone
soon!

--Armadillo Jo

From: Mark & Dottie Campbell (Campbell@Barnabas.org) | Date: 2008-07-24 06:08:33 UTC-05:00
We usually use Lock-n-Locks, or a good replica. My box, A River Runs Through It, survived 3 floods with dry contents in the same park along the Rock River. Everyone kept saying to move it and we finally did, and it didn't survive after moving it. So ... the stamp has been recarved, and the box will go to a new location closer to the first location!

Now a funny letterboxing story: 3 girls in the Cleveland area were looking for one of my boxes in an eastern suburb and couldn't find it. As they were walking, they spotted a deer who "waited" until they got closer ... and walked right over to where the box was planted, and licked the spot where the box was!!!

From: Carol Osada (cjosada@hotmail.com) | Date: 2008-07-24 13:15:34 UTC-06:00
Dollar Tree stores carry Lock and Lock boxes, a knock off of the more expensive Lock and Seal boxes. Not all Dollar Tree stores carry these Lock and Lock boxes (with the rubber seal and the 4 closure wings), but for $1.00 each, it pays to drive around to find them, then stock up. Recently, I went to 4 Dollar Tree stores in 2 days...a letterboxer on a mission...to find those boxes. When I did, I bought 30 boxes. I'm set for awhile. LOL!
