Helping a firend.

From: Sara (fivestmc5@earthlink.net) | Date: 2007-10-21 22:55:55 UTC
The husband of the person who introduced me to letterboxing came down
with a condition this summer that has forced him into a wheelchair.
The family of 5 are really struggling with wanting to find a few
letterboxesnow he is healthy enough to be back outside. This family
only letterboxes as a faminy but there is not wheelchair accesable
boxes listed in our area. HOWEVER there are a handfull of boxes that
with a few modification to the clues I know he could reach (such as
insted of going up a very steep hill they could go farther on the
path and end up where the hill would have taken them. or one such box
would just have to be approched from the other side of the park, so
the clues would have to be totally made back up)

**Does anyone think that would be a problem if I redirected my
friends through the areas the wheelchair could pass to some of the
boxes I have alread visited without totally giving away the location,
as I know the family would not want that either. (This information
would only be shared between this family and myself, not listed
online or passed out.) Or as planters does that make you
uncomfortable and in your opinion you feel I should not do that.**

I'm working on planting a few that he can access but I am still
coming up short in the budget each week to purchase any of the items
needed to plant a box. but I do have places scouted out, clues
written and images chosen. just waiting for a week that I have a few
extra dollars to get the supplies.

Thanks everyone, I really respect your opinions and help on matters
like this.

MI Girl - Sara

This doesn't make me uncomfortable as a planter at all.

accessible to your friend in a wheel chair by all means do so. I plant boxes for
people to find, not just the able bodied ones either.
