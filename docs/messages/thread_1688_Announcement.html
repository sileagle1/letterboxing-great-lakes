From: bindlestiff_boxer (bindlestiff_boxer@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-08-10 09:55:23 UTC
You're thinking, "20 letterboxes all placed in a single afternoon ...
how hard could it be to find those," aren't you? (chuckle just you
wait and see)

Within the 100-acre park, in addition to an abundant and diverse
collection of flora and fauna, lie the remains of an old Fish Hatchery
(which dates back to the 1950's) some virgin White Pines that are more
than 250 years old, a canoe launch, 7 trails and now the enticing lure
of 20 little letterboxes from two very different series

Boxes with stamps carved by DogLvrs containing Knights, Castles and
Dragons were placed first, then taking a cue from the courage of the
noble Knights, we bravely went forth to place 9 boxes with my stamp
carvings for the first in the Qwerbert Quartz sleuthing adventures.

One of the stamps I carved remains to be identified, even Mrs DogLvrs
(the inspiration for the Worlds Greatest Music Teacher character)
didn't know exactly what kind of staff symbol it was. We've got a
prize for the first person that can lead us to online confirmation of
its identification. (There's a photo of this mystery music symbol in
the photos section of the Letterboxing_Bindlestiff yahoogroup)

Even though we both beat our own `personal best' in number of boxes
placed on a single day, many "directional notes" were taken in
preparation for and anticipation of clue writing. All that is left to
do now is for Bindle Babe to find time to spin out some fanciful yarns
with embedded clues, have someone do a run-through to verify accuracy
vs errors and then we'll post them!

Have our collaborative efforts piqued your interest yet?

Enjoy!
---Bindle Babe
