From: tiptoetonto (tiptoetonto@yahoo.com) | Date: 2011-10-04 21:10:34 UTC
While checking out art websites for school, (the main reason I'm not out letterboxing on this fabulous day!!!) I came across this website. You'll have to move around the site a bit to get the feel for this artists take on "hunting," but I think you'll see why I found it interesting and connected to letterboxing.

http://www.williamhessian.com/projects/arthunt.html

Although very different from letterboxing, it held some aspects that were familiar. For starters, the whole idea of posting clues and searching in parks and public areas to bring people out into the community had a similar ring.

I was also reminded how often we state that one of our reasons for letterboxing is the collection of quality art images that so many boxers create. Fortunately for us, many of us get a copy, or print, of that image. In this case, it seems a finders keepers policy is behind the fun allowing for only one finder to relish the image. How sad...

The idea of using photo clues reminded me of some of our fun letterboxing clues.

But, after seeing his fee of $1000 buckaroos to put on these events, we are VERY grateful to our event coordinators of our gatherings...a good reminder that when attending events to help pitch in financially to cover the costs!
Tiptoe...and Tonto who is not spending all his time looking at art websites!
