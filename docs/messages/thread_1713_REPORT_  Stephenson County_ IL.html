REPORT: Stephenson County, IL

From: ponyexpresscarrier (PonyExpressMail@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-08-15 01:29:45 UTC
Had to spend a couple of nights in Freeport, so decided to combine
reunions with hobbies and stayed on to do some boxing on Saturday. I
could tell I wasn't in "Chicagoland" anymore when the billboards
started advertising places in Iowa.

I used a 40% deet repellent spray and also sprayed down my jeans,
socks, and boots the night before with a tick killing permethrin
spray. I picked up no tick or mosquito hitchhikers and I found the
following boxes:

1. To God be the Glory, placed by Nature Nerd outside Freeport.
Actually found this box on Friday evening, not Saturday.

Didn't actually figure on having time to get to this box. However,
as is usual for me, I got lost. I took the wrong Rte 20 from
Rockford to Freeport and suddenly, right there, right smack in front
of me, was To God be the Glory. Wow! How nice for me! Didn't have a
bumpersticker on my truck saying "I slam on my brakes for
letterboxes!!" so I couldn't justify slamming on my brakes and having
the 6 cars or so behind me get upset. Not to mention my auto
insurance agent might commit suicide at that one when he found out
the reason for an abnormally large payout. Went to mile marker 26
until I could safely turn around, come back and snag the box. Box
was in good order.

2. Krape Park Treasure Hunt, 6 boxes placed in Krape Park, Freeport,
by River Rat. There's something about River Rat's stamps that I
like. They're rather crude, rather raw, rather Grandma Moses-ish.
But that's exactly what I'd expect a "River Rat" to carve. Very
appropriate.

Had a fantastic time at this park. It was amazing how they've
combined the parts for the wildlife with the parts for the humans.
And, having decided to hike it rather than drive it, and having
forgotten my water in the truck, suddenly coming across vending
machines just placed out in the middle of wherever was kind of nice
too. Probably a good money-maker for the park. Great disc golf
setup. Played a bit since I carry my discs in the truck in the
summer. Lots of things to explore here.

Only sad part was that I couldn't find the last box. Tried and tried
and tried somemore but Box #6 eluded me completely. The boxes were
fun to find -- some of the clues were nicely just slightly ambiguous
and made me stop & think again in my thinking. The statue of Whoofle
the Dragon was great, even tho the dragon stamp was the one I didn't
snag. It was worth the search just to see Whoofie.

The 5 boxes I did find were in good order.

3. Duck's Misery, placed just north of Freeport by River Rat.
Given that it was a nice overcast, cool day, I was surprised at the
lack of traffic on the Jane Addams Trail. Sure wasn't like the IL
Prairie Path or the Great Western Trail. But all the better to hide
what I was about.

Nice walking trail, and a totally straight trail was kind of nice
after all the twists and curves of going around Krape Park. The
butterflies were having a convention on the trail on Saturday.

The box is doing well.

4. Butterflies on the Battlefield, placed outside Lena/Kent by Hiker
Babe.

The box is doing well. But the poor American Flag isn't. That's the
sorriest flag I've seen in a loooong time -- I would think Stephenson
County would be able to dig up at least enough cash to come up with a
new flag for the Blackhawk Monument. That's the most tattered flag
I think I've ever seen. It didn't look like even half of it was left
flying.

5. Artesian Well, placed in Winslow by River Rat.

Had a blast going after this box. I couldn't follow the clue
directions to drive to the box because the Winslow River Days Parade
was just set to begin as I arrived at Hubbard Street. Oops.

So my backpack, my water bottle and I sat down with a very nice
family on their parkway to watch the parade. The people of Winslow
seem extremely nice. Saw some nice antique tractors and one huge
honkin' new tractor that looked like it cost more than my house.
Maybe even more than a couple of houses. And hoofin' it in the
parade was a 25 year old mare named Misty. She was doin' pretty darn
good for an old gal. Band was pretty good too.

Then I walked in to snag the box rather than head all the way back to
my car and drive. A lot of folks sure seem to like the water from
the artesian well -- lot of folks filling containers. Unlike the
water from DuPage County's only artesian well, the water from the
Winslow well is supposed to be perfectly safe. Or so the information
at the well says.

The box was in good order.

6. Where the Buffalo Roam, placed on the Jane Addams Trail near
Cedarville, by River Rat.

Was hoping to get a good view of the bison but unfortunately, they
were dots on the horizon. They were all the way on the other side of
a huge pasture from me. But I did get to get upclose and personal
with two eensy teensy 15 week old mini-Dachshund puppies for about a
half hour or so. Their humans live in a home that borders the
trail. Cutecutecutecutecuuuuute puppies. This stretch of the Jane
Addams Trail, like the stretch earlier in the day, had almost nobody
on it, when it was such a beautiful Saturday for a walk. I couldn't
believe my luck. No secrecy at all needed for that box.

Had a nice walk to the box -- another butterfly convention -- and the
box was in good shape.

~~ Mosey ~~
