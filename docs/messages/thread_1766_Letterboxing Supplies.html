From: cdem_ltrbox (wp4@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2005-08-29 19:30:46 UTC
After this weekend's experience, I'm thinking we need to go beyond our
basic letterboxing supplies (logbook, stamps, ink, printed out clues,
compass) and add a laptop computer with WiFi. LOL. My husband and I
had a free afternoon after dropping off the kids for an outing with
Grandma and Grandpa. We were planning on a bike ride--somewhere--and
then moseying about a little. I threw in our letterboxing backpack
with a few possible clues. Of course, we ended up going nowhere near
THOSE clues.

As we were riding our bikes through a forest preserve, we thought this
would be a great place to hide boxes. Unfortunately, there was no way
to look up clues. Then, as we were passing through an area, something
caught my attention that reminded me of "Samuel Curtiss's sheep
pasture". I remembered seeing clues about a mystery letterbox
relating to this, but my memory wasn't good enough to remember all of
the clues. Another missed opportunity. Then, as we were driving to
pick up the kids, we passed Lyman Woods. We remembered that there
were clues to a box there as well, but again, no way to find the
clues.

Oh, well. Guess we'll be making another trip that way, with the clues
in hand. And then the kids won't be missing out.

BirdWoman

From: Mosey (PonyExpressMail@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-08-29 17:16:27 UTC-05:00
I keep files in a file box in the back of the van or truck with the clues
for each county printed out. Each county gets it's own file. That way if
I'm someplace that seems as if I remember there being a letterbox planted
somewhere in the area, I can just stop, drag out the file for that county
and go thru it.

The only trouble is, the clues might have been updated since I printed them
out -- box missing, hiding place changed, etc. But in general, it works
pretty good for me.

I also keep my stamp, ink pads and markers, etc. in the file box so that I
can just grab them on a whim if I want to.

~~ Mosey ~~

From: Howling Coyote (coyotespaw@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-08-30 08:53:35 UTC-07:00
I have an excel spreadsheet, each tab is a different state.
The boxes/clues are stored on the each State's page - I copy and paste
them from LBNA. Not ALL of them are there, only the states I know I
travel through (Illinois, Iowa, Wiscinsin, Indiana, Michigan right now)
Once I find a box, I remove the clue from the state tab, and list the box
name & date found on a separate "Found" tab.
I keep the spreadsheet on a thumb drive, and we do have a laptop that we
can bring with us on road trips, but I've never actually thought to look
up my clues with it, LOL!
I also keep a stack of printed clues in the back of the car to pull out
when needed :-)

Howing Coyote

From: Kyle Klein (armadillo1111@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2005-08-31 05:58:23 UTC-07:00
I use a PDA to keep all my clues.

I have a free program called Plucker that coverts web
pages to viewable files on the PDA. The way I do it
gives me one PDA file for each region, just like they
have one page of clue listings for each region on the
LBNA web site.

I can carry the PDA with me on the trail and follow
the clues. It is easy to update it any time I need to
before walking out the door in case clues have changed
or been updated. And I can put any regions I need on
there. The whole process takes about 5 minutes for
each region.

Kyle
Team Armadillo
