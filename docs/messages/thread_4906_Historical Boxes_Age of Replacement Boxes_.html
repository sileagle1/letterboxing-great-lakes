Historical Boxes/Age of Replacement Boxes?

From: Carol Osada (cjosada@hotmail.com) | Date: 2008-04-04 13:09:53 UTC-06:00
I still consider letterboxing to be a new hobby. The oldest box I have ever found was from 1999 and it was worth the research and repeated efforts to find it. But I still enjoy finding a letterbox with a hand carved stamp created by a young child. Obviously, their parents are letterboxers too. The effort is there.

Now, I have a question....

If a letterbox was placed in 2004 and after a few years the box disappears (boo hoo!), but the original creator carved the same stamp design and replaces the box, is it still considered an box dating back to 2004? Just thought I'd put that question out there for comments on opinions.

TJ_Mich wrote:

> Personally I think there's an extra bit of intrigue with the boxes that have been in place
for 4 - 5 years, or more... I think maybe it's just because I've seen the clues out there for
so long, and have had them on a list to find, or have plotted them out on a map, or
whatever it might be, that once I finally have the opportunity to find them, it's like making
a long-awaited visit to a famous landmark. Also, how can you not get a kick out of seeing
a logbook that's stamped "The Dragon - P13 F30" or "Wisconsin Hiker & Martini Man -
F10" or the like???

YES! YES! TJ MIch hits the nail on the head!....When this topic first came to our attention, I
could only say that we really enjoy the thrill of finding an older box..it being a
survivor/thriver type thing. But, TJ Mich's point of how we read, solve, re-read, print off,
ruminate over clues (and maybe clues in far off locations...like Michigan!), and for reasons
as varied as boxes, haven't gotten around to actually getting the box. BUT, we still hold
out the hope that someday we might get to actually follow the clues that are embedded
into our little letterboxing psyche's. We can almost picture every turn and rock
mentioned. As soon as I read TJ Mich's post, I immediately thought of a dozen or more
boxes that fit the bill.

We have box clues printed from the dark ages...back in 2002.. that are wrinkled and
running with old purple ditto ink (OK, not that old..ditto ink was even before Dartmoor!)
They even have the old LBNA map around them that I still hold dear and near that I
think..."Maybe this year we can get up there and see if this is still in place"....

Also, for those who enjoy the more challenging puzzle clues...if they are like me, Tiptoe,
(Tonto not so much...) and you have spent hours, weeks, months (or years in my case - I'm
not the brightest, you know...) to solve them. They happen to have been posted a while
back, they are in a distant state, but you hope and dream that you will eventually get to
them....How disappointing it would be to find out that they have been pulled. But, how
awesome to finally get to stamp into a box that only a few have stamped in since 2002!

This recently happened to us out in Arizona. We had clues for a box that we had taken out
with us for the last 3 years that required more than a little bit of scramble and some
sleuthing to get to. It had been out there for centuries in letterbox years. I can't describe
the thrill that it gave us to see that only Pete and Wanda and a few local folks had found it
in all those years. It was so much more satisfying to ink our little boots into that log than
the hundreds previous. I know the placer is not local, and it is debatable that they were
able to maintain it...and it didn't get visitors often enough to keep it's status up to date via
feedback. But, WOW! Was it sweet to find it after obsessing over it for so long.

Our vote ...Keep 'em out there!
So many boxes, so little time...But, we keep plodding along....
Tiptoe...and Tonto

From: (Mathw613@aol.com) | Date: 2008-04-04 18:12:53 UTC-04:00
Beetle,

Leave it to you to throw a riddle out there.

hmmm, I'd say yes and no. How about that?

If the creator, location and clues are the same, it is to me considered a box dating back to 2004.

However, the stamp itself would obviously not be able to be dated back to 2004.

I've found a few boxes. primarily from Pitties & Pastors Wife? where the stamp image is "the same" but new location and new clues... I consider it a new find -- even the "same images" have differences and the second "John Deere Plow" is not the exact same as the first, neither are "Victory" or "We Are Free" which by the way I'm looking forward to finding the 3rd installment someday.

I did find one box that had the same clues and same location but different stamp altogether and I did *not* list that as a new find... I'd already followed the clues and knew where the box was.

Its all schematics and really gets down to splitting hairs.

All this to answer your question, I'd consider it a box dating back to 2004 with a replaced stamp (even if the box is a replacement as well, its still the same location and same clues).

hx6

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2008-04-05 09:55:17 UTC-04:00
That is an interesting question Beetle. I have considered this, even now as I pack my bag to head out later today and replace the original Hoffmaster SP boxes -- same locations, same clues, but new hand-carved stamps (they were my first boxes, placed before I attempted carving) and new logbooks only because the dampness of the lakeside locations has all taken its toll on the paper and ink -- this is where one truly sees the value of stamping with permanent ink as so many of the entries have faded beyond recognition.

Are these then the same boxes that have been in place for the past 5 years? I would have to agree with the previous poster -- yes and no. Depends on the perspective I guess.
