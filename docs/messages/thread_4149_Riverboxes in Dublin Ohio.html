Riverboxes in Dublin Ohio

Dublin Arts Council
Six boxes to be placed along Scioto for game
Wednesday, May 23, 2007 3:42 AM
By Bill Mayr
THE COLUMBUS DISPATCH

Art and a digital version of hide-and-seek will merge in a project
along the Scioto River in Dublin.

"Riverboxes" will feature six artist-made containers in parks along
the river.

Those who want to find the sites will find clues and Global
Positioning System coordinates on Web sites related to Dublin.
"Riverboxes," organized by the Dublin Arts Council, will start Aug. 18.

"Each box will contain an original artist-created ink stamp and a
journal for letterbox enthusiasts to stamp.

"And there will be information about the site; it might be history,
some environmental information, perhaps an artist's response to the
site," said David Guion, the council's executive director.

"Each artist will choose an inconspicuous space. It's a point of
discovery; we're not going to give it (the location) away.

"We'll have clues to point people in the right direction, but it will
be up to them; they'll be treasure hunters."

Participants will be encouraged to take their own journals and ink
stamps when they hunt for the boxes.

They can mark their journals with ink stamps found at the location and
stamp the journal they find with their ink stamp.

The council is working on the project with the city of Dublin and the
Dublin Historical Society. Mike Utt River Clean-Up and Scioto River
Day will be Aug. 18 in Dublin.

Clues to the six locations, Guion said, will be posted on the
council's and the city's Web sites, and possibly on the historical
society's site.

The council also plans to open a blog about the project on its site.
The containers will be kept at their sites indefinitely.

"Riverboxes" is part of a larger arts-council project, "Ripple Effect"
that has produced arts-related exhibits tied to the Scioto. The river
has roughly 7 miles of shoreline in Dublin.

Columbus artist Eric Marlow, selected to make one of the containers,
said he likes the "Riverboxes" idea. "It's an opportunity for people
to get out, explore their environment and experience it in a different
way." He's considering ideas for his box.

"The box will be working with stuff that comes down the river, stuff I
can pick up along the riverbank. I do a lot of work with recyclables,
so I'll probably work that into the river box."

Others selected for the project are Ron Hirschi of Hadlock, Wash.;
Margaret McAdams of Kingston, Ohio; Jean Magdich of Logan; Patty
Mitchell of Athens; and Angelica Pozo of Cleveland.

For more information, visit www.dublinart.org.
