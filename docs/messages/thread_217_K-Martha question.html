Re: K-Martha question

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-05-20 14:10:16 UTC
K-Martha began as a HH crafted by the legendary legerdemaine as a
spoof on Martha Stewart -- legend has it that she logged into one of
his letterboxes and he copied her signature stamp to make the K-
Martha HH. In the beginning it actually did a brief tour of
letterboxes as any other HH. It became a much sought after and
prized stamp both because of the legend of its maker and because of
the colorful flurry of posts that it had generated at the talk list.

Somewhere along the line, in keeping with the legerdemaine spirit of
mischief, he created a second K-Martha stamp, nearly identical to the
first, for the purpose of playing a joke on the infamous Wanda of
Pete and Wanda fame. About that time, both stamps were removed from
circulation as normal HH and began making the rounds of letterbox
gatherings and events, where everyone proudly took a stamp for their
logbook -- for many the only legerdemaine stamp they may ever have.

Early in 2003, legerdemaine schemed up yet another prank, this time
aimed at long-time rival Randy Hall. Rather than carve the third
stamp himself, he commissioned the carving of K-Martha #3, to be an
exact duplication of #2, from one of his letterboxing acquaintances.
All three stamps now make the rounds to gatherings and events. It is
most difficult to tell which is which.

K-Martha #2 made an appearance at our Great Lakes gathering last
year. Personally I have stamped images of #2 and #3, and would
consider it quite a coup to run across the original.

I will do some hunting later today to find some of the original list
messages pertaining to the K-Martha stamps.

SpringChick
