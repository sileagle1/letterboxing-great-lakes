part 2: Why PZkut went orange

From: bindlestiff_boxer (bindlestiff_boxer@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-11-13 02:56:32 UTC
For those of you who don't know .. April is no longer involved with
PZkut, her son Justin is running things now.

After exchanging a couple of emails, I asked Justin the following:

> Do you mind sharing with me the reason you switched
> from the original white to the newer orange product?
> Just wondering if it was a supply or cost issue or if
> there were some other reason for the switch.

I'd just like to say Justin has always been very prompt to reply and
quite willing to be of assistance. It's obvious that quality is a
priority for him. I'm in no way suggesting anything derogatory about him.

Here is his reply to my question:

The quality of the white was degrading slightly for awhile, then
suddenly it took a big turn for the worse. I was no longer able to get
any that would qualify as Grade "A". I contacted the factory, and it
turns out they had switched out some pieces of machinery to reduce
costs. Unfortunately the new equipment made it impossible to get the
white material any longer. At that point I was made aware of the
orange material, and that it was unaffected by the change. As the
orange still generally maintains a smooth surface, I have had to
switch to that to be able to continue to provide a Grade "A" quality
PZ Kut. I know not everyone likes it. If it was up to me we would
still have the white, but unfortunately it is out of my hands.

Enjoy!
---Bindle Babe
