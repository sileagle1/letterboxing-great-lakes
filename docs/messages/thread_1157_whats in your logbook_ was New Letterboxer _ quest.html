Sorry I couldn't resist the word play on those awful "whats in your
wallet" comercials. (grin)

When I stamp into my log, the minimum I like to include:
* Name of the box
* Who placed it/carved it
* Date it was placed
* Location of the box
* Date I found the box
* The "F" count number it is for me

Then if there's time, I like to make little journal entry notes as
well. Those might include who I was with, my feelings about the area I
was brought to or observations made of the flora/fauna, notes about
the clues and/or my experiences solving them, I may jot down something
about the hiding spot or container, or even record my feelings about
the logbook or stamp. I might make notes about the weather (think
MiniMeet day last year!!) the box condition/maintance done, or even
any ideas spawned for boxes of my own (grin)

Most often there isn't time for those kinds of journal notes while I'm
out boxing so I jot down things in my own version of short hand. Of
course I fully intend to get back to those scribbles and turn them
into a lovely journal entry that I can look back on from the future,
with fond memories ... but that doesn't always happen, so I have quite
a few pages in my log that still have my cryptic short-hand scribbles.

Enjoy!
