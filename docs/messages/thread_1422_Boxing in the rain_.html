Boxing in the rain!

From: Pale Puppy Posse (advantagek9@voyager.net) | Date: 2005-06-13 15:24:06 UTC-04:00
Saturday I got to dump Naughty Dog off to work in Monroe MI and headed to
the two boxes in the Hidden Lake Park in Tipton MI.. what a nice park..
since it costs $3 per person I think we need to add a few more boxes :-)
First was the Hidden Lakes box. Nice easy walk and we had a deer stand
right next to the location I wanted to search but it didn't seem to notice
the 3 dogs I had with me! Eventually I headed over to that direction.
Unfortunately for me the box was sitting out in the open for all to see! I
stamped in and replaced the box in a nice hiding spot just as it started to
rain.. So we hussled off to the car and the next location in the park.
Big Rooster... now this box caused me some issues.... first the map of the
park shows only one parking lot... wrong! But I parked in the first and
checked every tree looking like Lumiere.... Now it says that it is just off
the beaten path. Well this is open to debate as to what this exactly means.
I had wandered down the hill a bit on what seemed like a foot trail or
maybe a deer trail. No luck! Oh left the dogs in the car cause its still
raining. Well shoot! no box.... but nature was calling and I am no sissy
girl plus I thought I was well hidden... well with my pants around my
ankles I look up to see a pickup truck staring over the edge at me! They
politely moved on and who knows what they saw! I sure hope it wasn't one
of you guys. Has this ever happened to anyone else?
Off to pick up Naughty Dog so we can do the two boxes in Monroe.... Old
Frenchtown and Lake Eerie Creature #1. I loved the history surrounding Old
Frenchtown but the mosquites after the rain were awful! This is a
letterbox/cache hybrid. SquirrelNut contact me please, found something in
your box you might like to have. Didn't think it appropriate to leave
there... Off to Lake Eerie Creature.
We parked in the lot and are headed in the right direction when I notice a
man, woman and three children. The woman is holding a piece of
paper...hmmmmm. How sneaky can you be with three kids, right. So I wander
up next to her and ask..."So, (me being cool) what is your trail name?" She
blinks in amazement but it was Mama Stork, Sleeping Giant and gang here
from OH. What fun! My first trail exchange. Now did I commit a Faux Pas?
Was I supposed to ask or what? Just checking for next time.
Anyway.... four boxes found while working... doesn't get any better than
that! Well no rain would have been better but can't have everything!

Re: [LbGLK] Boxing in the rain!

From: Doodle & Deedle Bug (doodle_n_deedle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-06-14 07:03:03 UTC-07:00
Oh yes, we've boxed in the rain before, too. We now
carry a small "purse sized" umbrella in our
Letterboxing Bag to aid in those rainy stamp-ins.

Thanks for the boxing report, though, since these
boxes (Big Rooster & Lake Eerie Creature #1) are on
our list to hit enroute to/from the gathering (we
found Hidden Lake & Frenchtown last summer). And it
was fun to read :) We actually passed the actual Big
Rooster last year and I was quite giddy to hear that a
box had been planted in its honor....we loved Hidden
Lake Garden last year when we visited - we spent half
the day there exploring the place.

And an exchange, how exciting! I remember our first
(and so far only) trail exchange - it was with another
area boxer (we're kind of spaced out around the
Cleveland area here). We'd found the box and were
sitting on a bench a decent distance away stamping in,
and waiting for the coast to clear to replant the box,
when a woman with a messanger bag and a sheet of paper
in her hands came into the vicinity. "She's a
letterboxer!" Deedle hissed, but I made him be quiet.
After watching her for a few minutes, it was quite
clear that she was searching for the box, since she
was staning in all the spots we'd been just a few
minutes earlier. So, just as she got to the point
where she'd have to leave the trail to find the box
(which was still with us) we went up to her and said,
"Excuse us, are you looking for this?" and showed her
the box. The boxer's name was Kelmark, and if you
make it to Nothern Ohio, her boxes (planted in Medina
county) are treats to find. She makes you walk! :)

Friday morning our 'boxing weekend begins - can't
wait, can't wait....

Doodle Bug.
