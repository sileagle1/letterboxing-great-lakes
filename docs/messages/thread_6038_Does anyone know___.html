[This is a repost of a message I posted just now on the Atlas Quest Ohio Board, but based on the little information I found concerning this plant, I believe the planters may not have used AQ so I'm reposting here to see if anyone knows anything]

of letterboxers from the Ohio area (I'm guessing around Akron, based on where we were today) with an email address "judeandmolly@hotmail.com"? Deedle & I weren't letterboxing today, instead we were on a bike ride. But as we rode past a tall post along the trail, the familiar gleam of a blue plastic lid caught my eye, so we stopped to investigate, and sure enough, it was a letterbox. And of course we didn't have our signature stamp or even a pen to write in the logbook with.

Here's our big mystery, though - we have no idea what the name of this box was, or who the planters were other than someone with the above email address. I don't want to give away too many details abut the location we were at or the stamp image, in case this box is a word of mouth or mystery plant, but other than the basic "this is a letterbox, please don't throw away, visit www.letterboxing.org for more information, contact us at judeandmolly@hotmail.com" message on the lid of the letterbox, there was no other identifying information. The log had no previous finders, just the image of the stamp - no box name written, no trail name of the planters, nothing. So I couldn't look for information for the box by name. I sent a message to that email address to see what the planters were willing to tell us, but it bounced back to me already. I searched both Atlas Quest and Letterboxing.com for trailnames that matched that email address, or boxes with clues
that fit the location we were at and the image of the stamp but found nothing that matched. And it appears that the box may have been planted for a while, because the container was inside a larger ziplock bag that had a bit of dirt on it and some holes like an animal had been chewing on the bag.

So, if you think you may know who the planters were, or the box name, let me know. If you think you may know but need more information, send me a private message and I'll share more specific details.

Doodle Bug.
