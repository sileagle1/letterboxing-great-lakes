The Naughty or Nice rocks are perishable, currently. Santa kept us
too busy to work on weatherproofing. We were lucky to have them ready
for the gathering.

The rocks are high tech papier-mache. Celluclay + water + spray paint
with a stone texture = rock.

Mix the Celluclay with water to a bread dough consistency. Roll it
out. Wrap the container of your choice with plastic wrap. Shape the
Celluclay around the container in the rock formation of your choice.
Tuck wads of plastic wrap between the container and the Celluclay and
let it dry overnight rock side up.

The next day, remove the wads and the container, and stretch the
cavity a bit since it will shrink as it dries. Bake the rock in a 200
degree oven for a couple of hours, with the rock hole side up, on a
cookie sheet. Alternate hole side up and hole side down as needed.
If you do some of the baking in the evening, you can turn off the
oven, leaving the rock in it, at bedtime so the residual heat
continues to dry the rock. If you bake in several shifts, press on
the inner walls to limit the amount of shrinkage.

Once the rock is dry, trim the inner walls as needed so the container
fits. Then, cover the outside with a spray paint that has a stone
texture. The paint is available in several colors. We used all red
on Nice and a red base with a lighter topcoat on Naughty. Our rocks
survived their snowy day, but you'd want to experiment with
weatherproofing options for a permanent placement.

For those of you who didn't have an opportunity to find Naughty or
Nice, you can glimpse Naughty in some of the St. Nick Gathering photos
taken by FungusWoman and Wisconsin Hiker.
