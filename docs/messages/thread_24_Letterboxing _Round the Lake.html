And now onto more productive things... A new letterbox series!
Unfortunately not everyone lives in an area where it would be
feasible to participate in this venture, but for those of you who do,
I hope you will consider it (and for those who don't... I'm cooking
up another project that should be ready to unveil in a couple of
days).

So what is this Letterboxing 'Round the Lake series all about? Well
no doubt many of you have heard of the Lake Michigan Circle Tour? It
is an established road trip route that takes a person all of the way
around Lake Michigan, through WI, MI, IN and IL. This series would
seek to populate that route with letterboxes.

I won't go into great detail here in the post, but you can check out
all of the specifics in the file I uploaded to the files section.
The short is that I am looking for participants who would like to
participate in the project by contributing a letterbox -- you make
it, you place it, you write the clues and post them where you wish,
you maintain the box and clues. I will then create a central web
page with links to all of the clues and approach the MI and WI
tourist associations in hopes they might feature it as a fun
diversion along the tour. Who knows, we may gain some new
letterboxers!

Want to know more?

http://groups.yahoo.com/group/LbGLK/files/LetterboxingRoundLake.pdf

Letterboxing 'Round the Lake

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-07-19 02:30:11 UTC
Okay, the Lake Michigan Circle Tour series
(dubbed "Letterboxing 'Round the Lake" to avoid name infringement)
is off and running.

I've set up a couple of web pages with information, details, box
criteria and submission guidelines here:
http://www.stampinthebox.com/projects/lmct.asp (similar information
has also been uploaded into the Files section of this group).

The main tour route has been broken up into 13 "legs" and there are
an additional 6 spur routes. When submitting a letterbox for
inclusion in the listing, please indicate which "leg" of the route
the box is located in as this is how they will be organized on the
list.

In reading through the criteria for the letterboxes, you will notice
that these do not have to be new letterboxes or boxes created
especially for this. Likewise the boxes do not have to fit any
particular theme. This is simply the compilation of a listing of
letterboxes that are located along the LMCT route.

The actual list of letterboxes is here:
http://www.stampinthebox.com/projects/lmct_boxes.asp

I have entered in a few of my boxes to give an idea of how it will
work. This is very similar to the I-95 letterbox listing that can
be found at Mark Pepe's web site.
