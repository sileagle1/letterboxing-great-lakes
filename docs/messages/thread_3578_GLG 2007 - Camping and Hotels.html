GLG 2007 - Camping and Hotels

From: be_a_badger (schuppner@uwalumni.com) | Date: 2007-01-13 22:25:10 UTC
For events, we usually choose a hotel over tent camping since
civilized lodging appeals to us after a long, often hot, day of event
letterboxing.

This time, we've chosen one of the more civilized campground options.
You'll find us in one of the 3 cedar cabins in area 2, which have
their own full plumbing. The web site shows that there's still one
available for that weekend, as well as some of the camper cabins where
the plumbing is in the shared building. It also indicated that there
are 2 RVs available for rent, but we didn't see a way to reserve one
on the web and we didn't see them on the map. If an RV appeals to
you, you might want to call the park to see whether that's truly an
option or not.

If we hadn't been able to reserve a cabin, we would have asked a hotel
if they could provide a group rate or at least reserve a block for the
Great Lakes Group. We thought it might be a convenient way to connect
with other letterboxers who were interested in meeting for dinner
after cleaning up at the hotel. Something for the hotel folks to
think about since you can often reserve a block without committing to
a particular number a rooms.
