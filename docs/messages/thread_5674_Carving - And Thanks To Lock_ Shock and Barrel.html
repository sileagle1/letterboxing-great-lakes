From: walesflyermary (drewes@WI.RR.com) | Date: 2009-02-22 04:11:47 UTC
Hey, Lock, Shock and Barrel - used your tips for craving tonight - a
great big THANKS to you all! I never would have attempted this
carving wihtout the confidence I gained from the tips I wrote down as
you spoke, so THANKS!

Though I'm not familiar with sci-fi, I took up one of Aimless' ideas
and looked up books by Wisconsin native Clifford Simak. I'm doing
the cover of Strangers in the Universe (Berkley Medallion edition) -
lots of lines and a water color look. So, the stamp can be inked
with black, then colored in with pencils if desired. If the box is
not needed for Boxacon in October, I could make a trip to Simak's
hometown (Millville) to plant it.

I SO wish I'd known these carving tips when I first started - those
of you who have seen the "Magic School Hops Home" in the Parade Books
know what I'm talking about - SHUDDER. I'm leaving it out there, but
every once in a while the urge to recarve comes up, KWIM?

Carving, getting boxes together, planting and FINDING LB's sure is a
great way to deal with stress.............I sure am glad I discovered
letterboxing!
