Bookbinding Meet Idea -- Feedback Wanted

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-01-19 15:56:56 UTC
Do we have bookmakers in our midst? Letterboxers who would like to
learn some bookmaking techniques that can be used for logbooks?
People willing to teach a bookmaking technique?

I've been thinking about hosting a spring mini-meet that would be a
series of bookbinding/logbook making workshops. I know that a
similar event has been successfully done in CT and possibly in other
areas. I am not committed to any specifics yet, but here are some
rough ideas I've been contemplating:

- This would be a day long event on a Saturday
- Event would be held inside
- Would try to find location in SW MI or perhaps northern IN, etc.
to make accessible for more people
- This would not be an event with many letterboxes to hunt for, but
there would be an event stamp and possibly a few other event-related
stamps/boxes, as well as a period set aside for personal stamp
exchanges with other attendees
- There would be 4 one-hour workshops, each teaching how to make a
different type of book or a different type of bookbinding that would
be suitable for letterbox logbooks. These would be hands-on
workshops in which participants will make a book in each session.
- All project materials will be supplied
- Attendees could sign up for either morning or afternoon or full
day (2 workshops in morning, 2 in afternoon)
- Most likely there would be limited attendee spots to keep class
size manageable and make it easier to secure a meeting room (maybe 12-
15 people maximum?)
- Lunch would be either "bring your own sack lunch" or "order food
in" (attendees to cover cost of their own lunch)
- There would be a small charge for attendance to cover project
materials and building fee

So, I'm looking for feedback... What do you think? Might you be
interested in attending something like this? Is there anyone who
might consider leading one of the workshops? Thoughts on my ideas?
Your ideas?

Please feel free to post feedback/ideas/questions/comments either to
the list or in private e-mail.

Thanks!

SpringChick

Re: Bookbinding Meet Idea -- Feedback Wanted

From: bindlestiff_boxer (bindlestiff_boxer@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-01-20 12:30:32 UTC
I taught bookmaking and binding for several years, SpringChick and did
a couple rounds of logbook workshops for the kids at the WI fall
Gathering. However, I'm not going to be available for a location that
far away.

I suspect you'll get a huge response of people wanting to attend. If
you do, may I suggest seeing if there is enough interest in doing
workshops in more than one area? I know it will depend on having
instructors and facilities available.

Also, since you're asking for feedback, when I held my classes, I
often posted a supply list and had students bring their own materials.
It's much, much easier on the instructor if you don't have to do all
that class prep yourself, and since this seems to be a request for
volunteers, why not make it as easy on them as possible?

If you decide to offer workshops in more than one location, and would
be willing to include West Michigan as an additional site, I'd be
willing to be one of the instructors and ~MIGHT~ even have a location
that we could use with banquet tables and folding chairs where lunch
issues wouldn't be a problem.

Please keep me informed.

Sail on!
---PJ
aka Bindle Babe
