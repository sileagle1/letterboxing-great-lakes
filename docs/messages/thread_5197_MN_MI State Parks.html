As we sit here light-heartedly poking fun of the priority some of the
state parks have put on regulating letterboxing, wishing we could have
the job of seeking them out, envious of the stance the CT parks have
taken embracing letterboxing, I thought I would share a positive
experience.

For those of you in Michigan's U.P. and Wisconsin, schedule a trip to
Porcupine Mountains State Park in the Western U.P. The park staff
there have planted 8 letterboxes and welcome people to come hunt for
these boxes, as well as others that have been planted by letterboxers.
If you wish to plant a box, the only requirements are that it is not
in a place that would endanger humans or the environment, and you keep
your box maintained... no permit needed.

Just goes to show that not all park managers act first and think
later. Some actually wish to encourage the public to use the parks
and trails for their enjoyment and see the benefit that letterboxing
and letterboxers can bring to their parks.

All hope is not lost...

SpringChick

p.s. If you are planning a trip to the Porkies, try to come on Labor
Day weekend when they are having their annual Hunter's Harvest
Festival. Perhaps we'll see you there!
