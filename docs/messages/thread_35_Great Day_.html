Great Day!

From: Deb (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-03-27 20:55:44 UTC
Well I headed out early this morning for Muskegon State Park and
made it to all the boxes there, which took a little longer than
usual because I actually followed the clues to each one to check for
changes in the terrain, etc. All of the boxes were in place and
only very slight damage to any of them.

One was strange though -- at the Big Bear box, I found the box all
snuggled in its spot, well hidden so it appeared that the last
person there had been conscientious -- but the log book was missing
from the box. I am wondering if perhaps it accidentally got put
into somebody's pack with their own log book and stamping stuff.
Unfortunately without the log book, I don't know who was the last
person there. I had taken a spare with me, so the box is back in
business, but I found that strange especially since a very nearby
box had been visited just a week ago and there was no mention of the
log being missing from Big Bear. Other than that -- I replaced a
couple of containers, some baggies and cleaned off the stamps and
all is well.

I also checked on Ghosts Below today and that one also is in great
shape. Unfortunately I had to take it home with me as a young
couple with a flat tire pulled into the parking spot right by the
hiding spot while I was off at a bench checking out the box. They
looked like they were going to be there a while. I will run out
tomorrow or some night this week and put it back in place.

I picked up two HH while making the rounds, both originally placed
by folks on this list -- "Oh, the Places You'll Go" from the Leapin'
Lizards visited a few boxes in Muskegon before landing in the Little
Bear box where I found it. And "How 'Bout a Ride?" from azobox was
left at the Ghosts Below box back in September by the folks who
found him first. Both will be moved on to new adventures soon!
Thanks to both Leapin' Lizards and azobox for the neat little
travellers!

It was a great day to be back out on the trail, yes -- even the sand
dunes didn't feel quite so bad on a gorgeous day as this.

SpringChick
