Lumbering Along in Eau Claire is Active....

From: SKJJJK (col.one923@spwl.net) | Date: 2005-08-22 09:59:13 UTC-05:00
We've had some reports that our Lumbering Along box in Eau Claire's
Carson Park is missing or at least unable to find. So, I asked my
sister to check on the box for us. To our delight, this is her
report......

"I finally checked on the letterbox in Carson Park, the one in the tree
trunk in the main part of the park. It is still there and fully
intact. It was jammed up there pretty good, but it looked like maybe
even more debris had covered it. There was lots of sawdust like from
woodpeckers or something."

Sawdust? Paul Bunyan? .......I did work to hide it well in that old
stump, so was surprised it was missing, having never been found. I was
concerned about an accidental find, and I guess I worry too much. I
will probably add some camouflage tape to it on our next visit to Eau
Claire so it doesn't take as much effort to rehide it with layers of
bark. My biggest fear was that the bark would get dislodged in a heavy
rain. I did think it looked pretty natural though and I guess maybe it
was too natural looking. Anyway, It's a pretty cool stamp, so I'm glad
it isn't missing.

Other news.......We've been back out there again after Mr.
Puddlejumper's encounter with Poison Ivy 6 weeks ago now. It's been a
long 6 weeks for him and the itch still isn't completely gone, but the
cooler weather this weekend pulled us back out on the trail again.
Though I confessed to Jessi, Matt and Nick after finding their Ice Age
Trail box a few weeks ago that it has been a test of our nerves. Not
feeling like we knew how to identify PI and knowing there are lots of 3
leafed plants out there, it just seemed like it was more frustrating
then fun. The kids tend to wonder off the trail here and there or
carelessly brush against trail overgrowth and then there is the dog!

I told Jessi, Matt and Nick that their "Messy Trails" Hybrid Cache just
sounded like more than we could handle right now. They reassured me
that the trails were not overgrown, but that the trail system was
"messy" with lots of "Ts" and "crossroads". So with all the new "quick"
boxes planted in Madison, we got out Sat. to get the second box of the
Duck Pond Series, Peas in a Pod, Lions and finally Messy Trails. I
forgot about the Sunset on Soul box until we were home and Quest for Big
Tooth will wait until another evening or afternoon.

With our appetite for letterboxing whetted again on Sat. we joined up
with KDKadiddlehopper and headed out yesterday to get Famous Faces From
Wisconsin Series: Just a Good Ol, Mounds of the Midwest Series: The
Bend, and I-94 Traffic Crawl: Rocky Arbor. All I can say is that Mr.
Puddlejumper is much more trusting of the kids on the high altitudes and
steep drop-offs than I am! All I could think of was the thread titled
"First Letterboxing Death...." on LBNA! We did survive, but Mr.
Puddlejumper got an earful from me. We loved the stamps, loved the
views and at Rocky Arbor, loved all the Pines. We didn't preread the
clues for Rocky Arbor before leaving home, so didn't have the benefit of
looking at the photo link to the rock we were to look for. There really
were lots of interesting rocks and log piles, but as always, the clues
always make sense when you do find the right spot.

We did have a breakthrough though! Leapin' Lizards reported some poison
ivy in the area of their Mounds of the Midwest Series, The Bend box, so
we spent some extra time to locate and identify it. We are pretty sure
we found it! Hopefully we didn't get into it, but we think we can
identify it better now. Looking at pictures of it just wasn't helping
me any. I needed to see some in real life and now that we have I feel
better informed.

In all, a great letterboxing weekend!

Mrs. Puddlejumper of The Puddlejumpers
