Boxing in Peoria

From: beckers410 (Mathw613@aol.com) | Date: 2008-11-09 06:10:26 UTC
We headed down to Peoria today in 36 degree weather and through a few
snow flakes. We found 7 boxes, including the one I shared about in
our blog.

Here is what I wrote on our blog (sorry for the "cross post" I figure
not everyone checks our blog and may enjoy the story).

Skeeter, Bug-a-Boo and I headed out for "Autumn: Scarecrow" (I mile
long hike). We didn't have any difficulty finding this box, and the
girls kept up with me even up the steep hill.

The most exciting part of this whole hike came when nearing the box
and we saw about a dozen wild turkeys. As we neared the end of the
clues we realized the turkeys were just feet from the tree in which
the box was hidden.

The girls walked right up to the turkeys, that didn't seem to
concerned about this and only meandered away. Once we grabbed the box
and stamped in, the turkeys were only about 20 feet away. The girls
really enjoyed walking after the birds.

Once done, we tried to finish the loop, with no luck. The leaves on
the trail were so thick we couldn't figure out what was a "trail" and
what wasn't.

Rather than getting terribly lost, I decided to head back the way we
came . . . back up the steep hill we went.

Now on to try "Falling Leaf" again. Last time we attempted it was
about 90 degrees outside with about 90% humidity.

This time it was about 40 degrees and we were sprinkled with
snowflakes.

We headed down another trail, this had to be the one. I was excited,
as I thought we were near finding this elusive box.

We went up another steep hill, double around and up again. Alex
spotted 4 deer up on another hill and we all stood and watched them.

The girls were absolutely the best little hikers ever -- WATCH OUT
WISCONSIN HIKER!!! :-)

I estimate we hiked about 3 miles, up rather steep hills in the cold
air. There was no bickering or complaining. They kept up with me, and
smiled and laughed a lot. This hike, though coming up empty
on "Falling Leaf", exemplifies that its not always about the "find."

The girls and I were away from the car for an hour and a half, and I
can honestly say that I enjoyed every minute of it.

As an added bonus, the last short stretch before we got to the car, a
herd (is that the right term?) of turkeys was in the middle of the
trail. I couldn't believe how many there were!! I started counting
and lost count at 39 . . . amazing!

The biggest bummer of this all, is that I left the camera in the
van :-(

Thanks PB for the reply.

You are totally right about the camera... bring it with you and see nothing,
or forget it and see some exciting things.

About the "no whining, bickering or complaining", The girls have always been
good about hiking. The only complaint that I can remember out of Skeeter is,
"Daddy, I get bored when we don't find the box." and that was a year and a
half ago.

We are coming up to our 2 year anniversary of boxing and last week in Nauvoo
we hit F400.

Turning the clocks back 2 years Bug-a-Boo was only 2 years old and Skeeter
was 4 (in fact the twins are now older than BaB was when we first started). All
this to say, they've been doing this a long time and have a lot of Finds
under their respective belts -- its just a way of life for them :-)

If it wasn't for Lbing, we never would go out hiking anywhere, we (Becki and
I) would just be stuck standing behind a swing and pushing (as that's all
any of them want to do at a park).

Yesterday was just awesome though, looking back at them, their little faces
pink from the cool air. The glisten of snot below their little noses, and the
smiles and giggles were just an incredible bonus.
