Beetle-mania

From: cdem_ltrbox (wp4@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2006-01-08 02:01:01 UTC
The kids and I went on a letterboxing adventure on Tuesday--before
they had to go back to school on Wednesday. The ground was soggy from
all the rain and melted snow, but the weather was great that day. We
were enticed up to Lake County as visions of 15 stamps in the "Santa's
Lost Toys" series (placed by Beetle) danced in our heads. Since we
drove all that way, we wanted to hunt down as many boxes as we could.
In addition to the Lost Toys, we also searched out alot more boxes
placed by Beetle: Fatman, the Beatles Series (I loved the lyric clues
even if the kids didn't know what I was trying to sing), Trolley Days
(nice history lesson), Sunrise Beach (where the kids had a great time
climbing around on huge rock piles), and the Great Rondout Train
Robbery (another fun history lesson). The only non-Beetle box we
found was Lake Bluff, placed by Wayfaring Stranger (which has been
there since 2003!) The boxes were all in good shape except Trolley
Days (a very damp logbook--which Beetle is now aware of).

What I found very amazing was that we went looking for 24 different
boxes and found ALL of them--not one missing box! Even more amazing
is that my kids hung in there for a whole lot of boxing without any
complaints or arguments. I guess warm clothes, a thermos full of hot
chocolate, lunch at "the Silo" and the thrill of the hunt helped out.

Thanks to Beetle and Wayfaring Stranger for some great boxes!
