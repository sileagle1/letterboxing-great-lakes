From: duncan53211 (duncan53211@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-04-30 03:00:58 UTC
If I were to plan a "Come See What's Brewin'" Gathering to celebrate
the 25th anniversary of the Brewers trip to the World Series, would
you be interested in attending?

Initial plans are to have it on Sat. 7/14. The gathering would be
during the day at Doyne Park in Milwaukee (within walking distance of
Miller Park and free parking).

"Tail-gate" style lunch and dinner at the park. Gathering themed
boxes, etc.

As a wrap-up to the gathering, we'd all go to the Brewer game that
night as a group. Group tickets would be bought and we'd have our own
section and our group named on the scoreboard!

I need to know if there is enough interest in order to get group
pricing for the tickets. We need 25 minimum and I'm looking at
bleacher seats for $16 each.

If you are interested, please email me by next Sunday to let me know
along with the number of tickets you'd be interested in buying.

Thanks!
Dragon

From: Doodle & Deedle Bug (doodle_n_deedle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-04-30 08:07:02 UTC-07:00
This isn't letterboxing related, but Dragon's post
reminded me and I wanted to share. For those of you
who follow baseball, you may have heard how the
Cleveland Indians first few home games and the weather
were not coinciding nicely, and our "home" games
against the Angles were actually moved to Miller Park.
A few years ago I was in Milwaukee (loved the town,
it reminded me of Cleveland ;) and went to a game at
Miller Park. My friend & I were highly amused by the
Running of the Sausages at that game.

Saturday night another friend & I got to attend an
Indians home game that was actually played at home.
Previously at Jacob's Field, at some point between
innings they'd show an animated Hot Dog Race on the
scoreboard, with animated Ketchup, Mustard & Onion hot
dogs racing on the screen. At the game Saturday, when
it came time for the Hot Dog Race, they actually had
people in hot dog costumes - one with ketchup, one
with mustard, one with onions - a la the sausages, do
the race on the field, like in Milwaukee. Guess those
Sausages left an impression!

Doodle Bug.
