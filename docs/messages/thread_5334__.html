I'll second the thanks. We had an absolutely fabulous time.

I'll share 2 trail encounters. We had a couple and their dog pass us
while we were stamping in, they came from a side trail and went by
us. We continued down on the trail and eventually came to a location
of a box near where the same couple was sitting by the river. We
commented on the lovely location and were comtemplating if we could
quitely grab the box (the couple was further in front of where we
were with their backs to us) continue down the bank and find a place
downstream to stamp in when the couple got up and headed back up the
trail. As they passed us, the woman turned and asked, "Are you
geocaching?" We explained the difference between letterboxing and
geocaching, hopefully this will be one couple that will not disturb a
letterbox if they find one on their travels.

The second encounter was coming back up from the lake on the north
side of the park. We saw a creature in a tree. It was a small tree,
maybe a 10" diameter trunk. The creature had apparently heard us
coming and climbed the tree, clinging to the side opposite the
direction that we were coming from. Unfortunatly for his attempt to
hide, he stuck out on both sides. We went past the tree and stopped
to look and see what it was, I thought it was a porcupine. When we
asked someone at the shelter though we were told that they didn't
think they lived around the area. I thought about it all way home,
because I couldn't think of anything else that fit that description,
so I went on the parks website today. Sure enough, under the list of
mammals for the park, there is a porcupine listing. So that was my
first ever siting of one!
http://www.dnr.state.wi.us/org/land/parks/specific/whitefish/nature/ma
mmals.html

Thanks again to the Dragon for organizing and everyone else who
helped. Thanks for fabulous food and I really want that torte
recipe! We had a wonderful time talking with everyone.
