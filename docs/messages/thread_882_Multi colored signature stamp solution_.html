There was mention of using multicolored signature stamps and wishing you
could easily achieve that look.....I have the perfect solution! As many of you
may know, I own a rubber stamp store here in Rockford, IL called The Goose Barn
and we carry a product (which you can find other places... so this is not
just a plug for business :) ) that allows you to create a custom colored stamp
pad for your signature stamp which then allows you to repeatedly stamp the
perfectly colored image every time. It is called Cut n' Dry. It comes in 2
varieties, Foam or Felt. They are like a huge uninked ink pad (8 x 10) with a
thick, gray foam backing to prevent skidding. The Foam pad is used for making an
ink pad for thicker inks or paints like Pigment Inks, Lumiere paints, Duo
adhesive, acrylic paint, latex paint...whatever might be thicker and you want to
use in a stamp pad. The Felt pad is a thick, gray foam backed felt, like is
found in a regular dye based ink pad- (8 x 10) - It is for making ink pads that
use thinner inks such as dye, permanent or alcohol based inks. This is what
you would use for your hand carved signature stamp. Here's how: Stamp your
signature stamp onto the corner of the 8 x 10 pad of Felt Cut n' Dry in a
PERMANENT ink, preferably black. Cut the CnD after you trace the shape of the mount
on your signature stamp. (Save the remainder CnD for future use.) If your
sig stamp has no mount, cut the felt pad slightly larger than your image so you
will easily be able to eye lining it up. Then use reinkers to color the CnD
on the lined or solid areas you would usually do with a marker, using your
permanent ink stamped image as a guide. It will bleed a little along the lines
or sections so don't over ink the pad. Now, you can repeatedly stamp it in
all those log books without having to use several markers everytime you want
multi colors. Store your inked pad in a zip lock bag or be creative and put it
into a container of some kind, like an empty Altoid tin, etc.and then into a
zip lock bag which will help keep the ink from dehydrating. Just re-ink as
necessary. http://www.rangerink.com/product_cutndryfelt.html Here is a picture
of what you are looking for but the directions are on the back. It is a
product we carry, so if anyone is interested just let me know. If my directions
aren't clear, email me and I will be glad to try again or print the directions
right off the back of the package.
