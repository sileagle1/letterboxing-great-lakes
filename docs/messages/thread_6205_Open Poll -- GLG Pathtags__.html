Pathtags are starting to gain more interest. Many are used to encourage people to hike more -- you get one for hiking 50/100/250 miles, etc.

I found one as a FF Prize, it had a depiction of the planters signature stamp and I found it to be really cool.

They are small round tokens that you create your own design for. I see them kind of like the new patch (ok, all you patch people out there, don't get to offended by that). You can hook them onto your jacket, key chain, backpack or hiking stick.

Here is a web site with some examples http://www.pathtags.com/

You can see there is a pretty hefty start up cost that includes the design & colors, etc.

We, the planning crew of the GLG, would like your input by placing a vote in the poll I just put up.

1) No, don't invest in the pathtags.
2) Yes, offer them at cost (not sure how much that would be yet)
3) Yes, offer them if someone pays X amount of $ for the raffle.

Option three would be if someone buys $10, $15 or $20 worth of tickets for the raffle to be held, they would get a pathtag.

I can add one more layer of information about the Pathtags v. Treasure
Hike pathtags.

Pathtags don't have any official connection to hiking distances---but
what several of us across the country did was USE pathtags as a reward
for hiking x number of miles while letterboxing and have called them
various things including Treasure Hike (pathtags). It is my
understanding that pathtags were originally used (and still are) as a
signature "stamp" or tag and folks can just trade them. I saw quite a
few down at the event in Gatlinburg this past year and they are gorgeous!

You can have them made for anything including the event! But the
Treasure Hike pathtags are earned and are not connected to raffles etc.
Most states that have started their Treasure Hike program and who used a
pathtag as the award, ended up with a cost of around $3-$4 and it varies
with the number of colors you use on the tag. **Give yourself PLENTY of
time to order these if you decide to have them made.**

I was/am on the original group who helped start the Treasure Hike
Program, but here in Ohio we opted to do the patches instead of the
pathtags and so I can't help you directly with more of the actual
mechanics. If you ask your questions on the public AQ Treasure Hike
Program board, I'm positive you will get the web sites and other
information you need to order tags.
