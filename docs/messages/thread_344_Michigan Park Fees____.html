Doodle,
I hope that others weigh in on this one too, but here's my two
cents...
As far as I know, if the box is in a MI State Park, the annual
visitor pass will get you into *any/all* of the parks. I think the
annual fee is $24. (others help me out if I've got it wrong).
Of course, as you may have read, there are policies in place which
reportedly require the boxes to be registered and placement fees to
be paid to the Michigan State Parks folks.

County parks vary from place to place.
I'm sure if you post a list of where you're headed, the MI placers
will help out with specifics.

We're waiting to help you enjoy your Michigan letterboxing trip.

In SE Michigan there are a lot of "metroparks". You can buy a day pass, which is valid at all parks for the duration of the day, or an annual pass. Day passes are about three bones.

Re: [LbGLK] Michigan Park Fees....

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-06-25 23:25:23 UTC-04:00
Michigan State Parks have raised their fees this year, although it has been
several years since an increase, so I can't complain. A daily non-resident
pass is now $8. That is good for all state parks on any one particular day,
unlike some states where it is good only at the park where purchased. The
non-resident annual pass is now $29. Most counties in Michigan charge an
entrance fee to County Parks -- this ranges anywhere from $3 to $6 per day
and is good for all County Parks in that particular county on the specific
day it was purchased. As Delirium mentioned, the Metroparks in the SE part
of the state charge fees similar to County Parks. Other than that, most
local parks do not charge an entrance fee, although there are exceptions
here and there.

If you have a route in mind and post to the list, I am sure we can all
provide you with not only fee information, but also options for boxes that
may be placed in non-fee parks. One thing to keep in mind concerning the
State Parks... as the state parks instituted a charge for placement of
letterboxes this year, if you do happen to run across boxes in those parks,
please be especially discreet.

SpringChick
