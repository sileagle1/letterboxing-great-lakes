From: Robin Barton (RobinBarton@integritivity.com) | Date: 2012-08-25 09:13:01 UTC-05:00
Welcome to letterboxing! Hopefully you'll get a lot of other opinions
besides mine as there are a lot of great people on here.



Regarding placing it where it can be seen, there is nothing "wrong" with it,
you just would be sad to have it go missing. So if there are other items
sitting there and they are undisturbed, I think it would be fine. You might
label it so that if groundskeepers see other people going to it and get
curious, they'll know what it is. There are sample labels on Atlas Quest and
on other boxes.



There is also nothing wrong with a store-bought stamp, although admittedly
some finders may not go after your box because they really enjoy the
hand-carved ones. More importantly, however, is that this sounds like a
tribute to someone special and you might consider putting some more time
into it to make it the very best that you can in honor of that person.
You'll be surprised that you CAN learn to carve a stamp; perhaps not as
intricately as the one you found, but it's easier than you think and you get
better with time. I started with store bought stamps until I found
directions for carving stamps and then made leaps and bounds of progress.
http://www.atlasquest.com/tutorials/carving/ is a very complete tutorial.
You can make a simple one with a cuticle trimmer and a flat eraser, but you
can get great tools from a craft store pretty cheaply too, so you might as
well invest. That being said, folks will still come and find it if you
don't feel up to the task; we were all there at one point. On Atlas Quest
you designate whether it's hand-carved or not. On LBNA, you can put it in
the clues if you wish.



I would also recommend that you wait a bit on planting your box until you
find some more yourself; you will learn a lot about which containers hold up
under the elements, you'll see different kinds of carving materials and
different book styles as well as other ideas for making it special.



It sounds like you have a wonderful idea. I was really eager to make some
when I started too. The more I found, the more interesting and weatherproof
my own boxes became. Good luck to you!

RE: [LbGLK] Re: Newbie

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2012-08-28 09:58:48 UTC-05:00
Great reply, I'll second those comments!



If you are anxious to get this particular box planted and want to include a
store-bought stamp before you start carving, I suggest that you choose a
stamp image that has something to do with the person you are honoring and
explain in the clues the significance of the stamp.



Carving is much easier than you would think. I was a little overwhelmed by
the concept at first, so ordered custom made stamps for the first 3 boxes I
planted. However when I started carving some basic images, I discovered it
wasn't so hard and then progressed to more difficult images. I started out
using a gouge, but now almost always just use an exacto knife. I usually
print my images on a laser printer, then use acetone to transfer the image
to the carving material. If you'd like to see another tutorial about
carving, here's a link:
http://hartx6carving.blogspot.com/2008/03/carving-with-exacto-knife.html



Welcome to letterboxing and to the Great Lakes Group!
