From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-10-21 17:36:56 UTC
If anyone who is coming to the MI gathering at Yankee Springs tomorrow
has a lantern (and necessary fuel) they would be willing to bring, it
would be greatly appreciated.

The shelter has electric, but I could not find any lights. It is in
the trees, there are partial walls and it is all dark brown wood,
which made it seem a bit dark when I was there last weekend. I
thought about bringing some electric lights, but there is only one
outlet (I am bringing a power strip) and as several people will be
using the power for crock pots, I didn't want to overload the
circuit. So I was thinking lanterns would be a good source of light
in the shelter if we can round up a couple of them.

Thanks!

SpringChick

I'm keeping a positive outlook on the rain. All weather reports I have seen for the area around Yankee Springs indicate about a 30% chance of rain after 6:00 p.m., which is a positive change from the predictions earlier in the week. Cool temps won't be so bad -- can always bundle up, put more clothes on, sing and run around in circles. We have a couple of people bringing crocks of soup and chili and the grill will be fired up, so it should be a great fall day regardless of the weather. I'm remembering back to the first Great Lakes Gathering at the end of May 2003 -- rainy and unseasonably cold for the end of May. But it didn't dampen any spirits -- we were having a letterbox gathering, in Michigan.

Sorry you won't be able to join us.

SpringChick
