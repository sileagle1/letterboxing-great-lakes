From: timbertoes33 (toomey@genevaonline.com) | Date: 2004-07-22 15:51:55 UTC
There is cause for worry when any hobby gets mass publicity like the
Time article about Letterboxing. It makes me think of the vacation
destinations listed in Outside magazine. I'm sure the locals hate it
when their favorite hang-outs get publicized to that extent and the
masses swarm in.

I've read with interest all the points of view posted here (after my
own case of freaking out when I read the article) and found some
comfort in yooperann's comments about not keeping to yourself
something others might enjoy. My life would be a lot less
interesting if I didn't have Letterboxing in it. I also saw some
truth in azobox's comments about the article drawing in some amateurs
that run out and mess up boxes (and the environment) in their quest
for the quick, easy finds. It helps to keep in mind that this
pastime requires a LOT of thought, planning and energy to keep on top
of things. If you aren't truly in love with it, interest will taper
off. Like most things, it's not for everyone. Like azobox, I've
told every friend and relative I have about my passion and after a
few questions (mostly about my sanity), they lose interest. I'm
hoping that the article will generate some serious boxers that will
place some awesome boxes and the flash-in-the-pans will fade away
quickly without doing too much damage.

Timbertoes


Letterboxing Publicity

From: cdem_ltrbox (wp4@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2005-09-12 14:29:14 UTC
I was looking through my "Cooking Light" magazine and noticed a great
letter to the editor from a lady in Noblesville, IN. She was
responding to an earlier article about geocaching, and extolling the
benefits of letterboxing: solving clues, collecting stamp images,
discovering interesting sites that make for fun hikes, not needing a
GPS. She mentioned the letterboxing.org Web site.

Seeing this article/letter reminded me of how our family got
introduced to letterboxing. Our kids get Ranger Rick nature
magazine. Last summer, they had an article about letterboxing. In
the ongoing cartoon at the back of the magazine, they even had Ranger
Rick and his friends searching for a letterbox. That piqued our
interest. Even though the first box we went looking for was missing,
we tried again and now we are hooked!
