From: Atom118 (Aatom118@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-12-30 11:39:52 UTC-06:00
I try not to have too many boxes/stamps ready to go that don't have some
'definite' plan. I find that the piles of 'To Be Planted' gets me all
fidgety and overanxious to go boxing (which sometimes isn't appreciated
by Coffee Beans or the extended family). So, right now, I only have two
stamps carved and waiting to be planted. Fortunately, we've been on a
planting binge for the last few months, and we've planted several [I
won't say how many since a few might include undisclosed bonus boxes]
that had piled up. Those didn't bother me so much, because we had a
master plan in the works...just needed to find time to plant them. Of
the two, I have only vague ideas about where to plant them...those are
the ones that get me fidgety.

As for boxes planted but not clued, I don't know how anyone can do
that! I know that I try to get my boxes clued and posted ASAP after
planting. (hopefully not at the expense of the quality of our clues)
I'm learning to patiently wait for finders...but they can't be found if
they aren't clued!

I know its not the same...but I have found an outlet by doing a few
postals and LTCs....

As for the artwork...most of ours we find online...or from a scrap from
a restaurant or tourist brochure we've found. I like to plug the
drawings into a site that someone here mentioned
(http://www.dumpr.net/sketch.php) to convert them to nice black and
white line drawings.

Atom118 of Atomic Beans

From: Gabe (ainawgsd@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-12-30 10:02:59 UTC-08:00
I do have a pile of stamps that I fear may never be planted. Some were carved for purposes other than letterboxing. I did a series of stamps as a final project for my human sexuality class...it's not that the images are inappropriate, they're all PG13 at worst and most are G, I just can't come up with anywhere decent. Then there are my Chinese New Year stamps that I could plant if I were motivated enough to scout out locations, I've even thought about making it a single letterbox where the stamp rotates as the years change. Some were carved for an event and the event is either over or I missed the deadline. Some were planted but have been pulled due to various circumstances (such as Coal Miner's Daughter and Aina's series which will go back out in the spring now that they're not closing the park anymore or my DNAngel series which I pulled after over a year with no visitors and the third box kept going missing anyway...not to mention I only ever
planted half of that series to begin with). I have quite a few containers ready to go too, but with a lack of good hiding places scouted out they're probably going to sit there for a while.

And I have two more images floating around in my head (sort of). One is a box that I have been meaning to make for over a year now, I have a location scouted out and picked an image already I just need to sit down and carve it. The other is a little foggy...I have lots of reference pictures but I've forgotten what my original idea was, although I think I may have remembered the general plan this morning while walking the dogs.

As for images, many of mine I have found online and modified slightly to suit my design ideas. My absolute favorite source is to find several images (usually photographs) and use trace paper to take bits and pieces of two or more images to make my own. I especially like to use photos that I have taken myself when possible. I have also drawn my own if I just can't find what I like anywhere else.

Hey pugnasties! Speaking of those far-traveling pugs, Tiptoe & Tonto and
ourselves were the first to find your Arizona pug. Hopefully he stays clear
of the cactus...

And yes, we DO plant in winter. We probably plant more of our local boxes
in winter than in any other season, and they are outdoor boxes. If you can
find a good hiding spot in the winter, it usually will be very well
protected in the other seasons. Sometimes it can be hard to find winter
plants in the summer, because there is much more growth to get through. We
always keep in mind what season a box was planted when we're looking for
one. It helps to try to picture the environment at the time of the planting
when you are hunting for something.

Since I never replied to the initial post, I'll also add that like Hartx6, I
use Google Image a lot for ideas. I simply go to Google search and type in
something like "Arizona Image" and I'll get clipart, photos, and a variety
of things that I can either use directly or that will at least give me
ideas. I've also used the site Atom118 mentioned to transform images (and
have also used photo programs or simply MS Word to turn photos to black &
white carvable images). I also rip pages out of magazines, theater
programs, stuff that comes in the mail, or whatever. I really should get
all of those things organized! Sometimes I use things just as they are, but
other times I'll modify or combine various clipart to create what I'm
looking for. I don't think I've ever really drawn anything "from scratch".

Like Atom118, I don't like to have too many ready-to-plant boxes hanging
around. I usually carve for a specific place/purpose, then plant fairly
soon afterwards. It's therefore bothering me that I have 5 of the boxes
donated for the WI 10th Anniversary gathering still in the house. However I
want them to go to the part of the state that they "belong" based on their
image. We did manage to get 6 of them planted so far. I also have 2 boxes
ready for whenever we head further south in IL. We had hoped to do this in
July, but after all the flooding we postponed the trip. Then we
optimistically hoped it would be "nice enough" to go down there between
Christmas and New Years, but obviously scrapped that. (Does anyone else
remember how NICE it was 2 years ago over the holidays?? We keep hoping for
that again!)

I do have some EMPTY LnL and assorted containers in a big basket, but I
regard these a staple supply, just like having a pile of PZKut and paper.

Now it's time to get writing the clues for those 2 boxes we planted in
Arizona...

Wisconsin Hiker
