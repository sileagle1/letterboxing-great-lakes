From: Sara Byron (sarabyron@att.net) | Date: 2011-12-28 16:14:02 UTC-06:00
My opinions are biased and therefore you can take them with a block of salt!
My DH has worked for AT&T for 36 years! Can you believe that? In this day
and age?
Therefore, I don't have a choice and we have AT&T. I can say that coverage,
for me, is very good. I have never had problems in WI, even at our house in
Minocqua, where Spring customers (my nieces and nephews) have coverage only
When driving on main highways. Verizon used to have poor coverage way up
north, but they have improved immensely - everyone is happy with them too.
That said, since we have AT&T, I have an iPhone. I used to have three
versions of the Palm treo and a year ago was moved to an iPhone, kicking and
screaming. I have to say that I really love it. I am not a proponent of
apple or mac os - I use a PC religiously, and I do not like having my life
controlled by apple. BUT - I have no criticism of my iPhone.

I have multiple apps that I use for letterboxing - clue tracker, google maps
and map quest, flashlight, and misc hotel apps - particularly find pet
friendly hotels. I use this phone all the time! Constantly.
I also have an Otter box heavy duty case since I drop things all the time.
My nephew told me it made me cool to have an otter box.
Hope this helps a little.

I'm a little biased but I recommend Clue Tracker.
My husband and I wrote it because we wanted an app that could search both LbNA and AQ and save letterbox clues for access when we didn't have cell phone coverage.

We have continued to improve the app over time. The current version (which has only one review right now) includes many features including the capability to record finds and to adjust the location of a letterbox.

If you have specific concerns about Clue Tracker, please let me know. We are always happy to get feedback whether for new features or for reporting problems.

Also note that reviews and ratings on Apple's App Store tend towards the negative side because historically Apple has only prompted people to rate apps after they delete an app.
