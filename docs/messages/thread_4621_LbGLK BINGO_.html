It's that time of year again... time for LbGLK BINGO! The game will
begin on January 1, 2008. The format is the same as last year, but
the word list has been updated with new boxes, names, events, etc.

The instructions, word list and card template for the 2008 edition of
LbGLK BINGO have been uploaded to the "BINGO 2008" folder in the FILES
section of the Great Lakes list.

There are 3 documents you need to print:

1) BINGO1.PDF - The instructions
2) BINGO2.PDF - The word list
3) BINGO-GRID.PDF - The card template

All of these documents are in .PDF format, which will require the
Adobe Acrobat Reader to open. If you do not have the Acrobat Reader,
you can download it free here:

http://www.adobe.com/products/acrobat/readstep2.html

If you have problems accessing any of the documents, e-mail me and
we'll figure out how to get them to you. If you have questions about
how the game works, feel free to post to the list (others may be
wondering the same thing) or e-mail me directly.

Please note that your entry must be received by Dec. 28, 2007, which
is only about 2 weeks away, so don't let it catch you by surprise!

SpringChick

Re: [LbGLK] Re: LbGLK BINGO!

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2007-12-16 11:23:07 UTC-05:00
I tried to choose boxes evenly across the region that were planted during the last quarter of 2006 and the first half (sometimes a little later) of 2007, thus giving newer letterboxers more of an even playing field in this section. But if you haven't been out boxing in a while or have been busy boxing in other alluring places in the world ;-) you may not have visited them yet. Besides which, when you consider that I chose the 45 boxes fairly evenly across the entire 6-state region, there is probably only one or two boxes within an hour from where you live, so it is quite hit or miss.

I added some additional general terms this year so instead of 7 per column, there are 8 per column, which will hopefully help to spread it out a little -- last year it came down to several people all waiting for the same few words.

The one thing that surprised me the most as I was compiling the list -- were there no events in WI this past year? I sifted through the AQ event calendar and didn't see any, thus no events in WI. I realize the one event was held in IA, but there were quite a few boxers from the GL region who attended, which is why I included this one.

The main thing was that I tried to use different boxes, boxers and events than in the previous Bingo games. Although a few of the general terms are new, many are repeats since there are a limited number of these.

As Azobox noted, in the past, choosing all general terms has not been a disadvantage in the least, so don't be discouraged if you find you don't qualify for many of the boxes, boxers or events.

SpringChick
