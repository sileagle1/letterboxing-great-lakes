Funny "cover stories"

From: bindlestiff_boxer (bindlestiff_boxer@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-03-30 04:57:14 UTC

While poking around in the Geocache forums I found some fun messages
about cover stories that have been used to avoid looking suspicious to
non-players (muggles in cache-lingo)

Pretending to use the GPS as a cell phone by either "talking" on it,
fake-sending a "text" message or pretending to forget your glasses
while holding it way out in front and squinting at it.

Another one was pretending to use the GPS as if it were a digital camera.

It reminded me of an experience I had while working out the clues for
a box I had just placed. NOTE: I use the hand-bearing compass from our
boat, which is about the size of a baseball, and bright yellow in
color. While I was getting some bearings I noticed a couple behaving
in a "curious manner" nearby. It didn't take long for me to figure out
they had a GPS and were likely caching. It seemed that they must have
been right on the coordinates because they just wouldn't leave the
area and were doing some discrete poking around with a walking stick
while they kept stealing peeks over at me.

Since I had more work to do in the area and understood they wanted to
get the cache, I just pretended my compass was a GPS and asked what
coords they were showing. LOL ... they looked so relieved to be able
to get the cache.

So now I wonder other what kind of "cover stories" we could share
about our letterboxing trips. Let's please share them!

Enjoy!

someone approaches, esp. if we're already off the

tree or getting that "perfect shot." If we actually

pretend we're having an impromptu picnic - it IS
tupperware and plastic baggies, after all :)
