New Letterboxer

From: Jon Russell (jrussell6@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2004-06-27 22:32:45 UTC-05:00
Wisconsin Hiker and I (Martini Man) spent a lovely afternoon attending our
great-niece's baptism. Her uncle (our nephew) has expressed an interest in
letterboxing for quite some time. So Wisconsin Hiker sent him (and some
younger letterboxers) some clues to a box we hid some time ago and told them
we would get them started today. Well, things went very well. Wisconsin
Hiker, I and our nephew each carved a stamp at our great-niece's
post-baptism party and took off to find a couple of older letterboxes that
we thought might be attainable. The boxes were found in great shape and
everyone had a good time going through the clues and stamping in.



Here's the good part. We watched our nephew (hitherto known as "Fishbone" )
draw freehand a stamp for his lovely intended (trail name is now "Bingo
Babe") and carve it on the spot. The detail was phenomenal and we have high
hopes for the kind of stamps he could create. He already has some ideas for
Waukesha County, Wisconsin so stay tuned.



We had a great day and we look forward to sharing some good experiences with
our new letterboxers in the family.

New Letterboxer

From: Leslie (coyotespaw@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-04-07 01:52:24 UTC

Hiya everyone!

I'm a brand new letterboxer in the southwest Chicago suburbs, and I
found my first 3 boxes today!
This is horribly addicting... I stayed up half the night last night
carving stamps, I have 4 ready to go for boxes I am making :-)

On the subject of box maintenance, my "letterbox kit" now includes a
ziploc baggie with a wet sponge in it so I can keep everyone's stamps
clean that I get when I am finished. (It's also great for cleaning
BEFORE you stamp, especially if the person before you used some darker
ink than you are going to use).
I also have extra baggies and a small spare journal to help out if
someone's box needes it.

Happy letterboxing!

Howling Coyote

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-05-11 23:19:51 UTC
The Lazy Days of Summer Mini-Gather will be held on July 23, 2005 at
Muskegon State Park in North Muskegon, MI.

There is a Gatherings folder in the Files section of this list with
a text file containing some basic info on this event. It is also
listed at AtlasQuest (http://www.atlasquest.com/events/). More
information will be up within the next few weeks.

There will also be a Fall Gathering in MI this year. I am still
working out arrangements for location/shelter, etc. but should have
that finalized by the end of this week. Tentative date is Oct. 22,
possibly the 15th or 29th if those are the only dates we can get the
shelter. Tentative location is Yankee Springs Rec Area -- just off
U.S. 131, between Grand Rapids and Kalamazoo). This is a nice
easily accessible and centralized location for West MI boxers and
there are really great trails here. In the event that this shelter
is not available for any of the dates, we are looking at a couple of
other locations, but they are not as centrally located so I am
keeping my fingers crossed on Yankee Springs. More details as soon
as I have them.

Hope to see you at one or both of these events!

SpringChick
