From: estella_naparella (estella_naparella@yahoo.com) | Date: 2011-04-12 17:41:52 UTC
So, as stated in a previous note, Skootch and I are planning trips this
summer for letterboxing just for fun. To geek out and plan our trips, I
ordered a map of Omaha, NE so I can map out where we need to search for
boxes (grouping them together so I can make a route and itinerary... I
told you, I'm geeking out).

Instead of a map of Omaha, I have now received the children's book,
"Magic Rings and Funny Things". It has a creepy clown lurking on the
cover. I have indeed contacted the seller and will remedy this, but in
the meantime, I think I may just carve up a stamp for a box, which I
think I shall name "Map of Omaha"... although it will be less fraught
with 1970's child-angst as depicted in the book (creepy).

Just wanted to share a (weird) part of our summer adventures in boxing.
It's so safe compared with grade 4 stroller trails!

Cheers!
E.N.

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2011-04-12 12:58:14 UTC-05:00
Odd! (but funny)



We use paper maps for an "overall view", but like to use the MS Streets &
Trips software package (relatively inexpensive) for planning our LB trips.
We electronically "pin" each box with box title and maybe another short note
or two. We sometimes even let it plan a route for us, using one of the
features of the package. We can print maps zoomed in or at any scale we
want. However we don't actually print as many now that we also use a GPS
when driving on our adventures.



We also "geek out" when planning our trips and already have our route
planned for a September trip (a loop through portions of Montana, North
Dakota, South Dakota and Wyoming) and a corresponding spreadsheet. We also
have our spreadsheet and pinning done for a May trip and Martini Man is busy
mapping boxes for an Oct trip to Maine. OH YEAH! We are MORE than READY to
hit the trails!!!! Impatiently waiting for the trip to New Mexico next
month....



Wisconsin Hiker
