From: ainawgsd (ainawgsd@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-04-19 03:17:05 UTC
ROTFL! Flying Dragon and I both blamed the shaking bed on the dog
too! At first I thought it was FD (not sure what I thought he was
doing, seizing I guess). Then we thought it was Tatsu scratching
(poor itchy allergy boy, sometimes he scratches so hard he shakes the
floor in the room) but when yelling at him (which I don't
remember...must not have been that awake yet) didn't stop the
scratching we were both convinced he was having a seizure...and a
violent one at that! We both bolted upright and straight to the end
of the bed...only to find no dog at all! At that point we figured out
that it was in fact an earthquake (even Tatsu doesn't shake the blinds
on the windows when he scratches!). A quick snuggle and Tatsu
completely forgave us for blaming him before we tried to go back to sleep.

Rest assured, many people blamed the dog. My vet woke her husband up
and asked him if it was an earthquake...to which he replied "it's just
the dog, go back to sleep." He didn't remember a thing about it this
morning. I really wished I could have gone back to sleep. I've slept
through earthquakes before, some as big as this one...living in CA and
WA you kind of get used to them. But for some reason this morning's
quake unsettled me and I never did quite get back to sleep...I only
managed to doze and have weird dreams about fast food places running
out of food. FD didn't really get back to sleep either...this was his
first earthquake (or so he claims...although I find it hard to really
believe as I've been through two other earthquakes in this area...both
MUCH smaller). I did not however feel the after shock. This morning
at work was rather hectic and we were all much too busy being run
ragged to notice any slight shaking that might have been going on.

Kimono Girl
