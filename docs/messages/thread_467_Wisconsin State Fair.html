From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2004-08-02 23:05:40 UTC-05:00
These are probably the closest to State Fair grounds:
Squished Pennies
Watery Grave
Miller Park (only if no home baseball game)
Creative Writing: The Never-ending Story
Lucky Leprechaun (some off-trail walking required)

If you want to enjoy some of the Lake Michigan lakefront (only 8-10 miles
from fair grounds) try these:
Calatrava
Green One (high pedestrian traffic area, especially in summer)
Happy Days (2)
Harbor Island Letterbox
South Shore Series (3)
Lake Park Series (3)

Not sure how much time you'll have for boxing, so let me know if you want a
longer list...

Debbie (Wisconsin Hiker)

From: aine_42 (aine_42@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-08-03 20:59:48 UTC
Re: the Harbor Island box -- This one may not be accessible if you're coming up for the
Fair over the weekend. The island is off-limits to the general public during certain festivals
(most notably Summerfest, but any others that use it for fireworks and such during their
weekends). Arab World Fest -- which is a lot of fun -- is this weekend. I don't know if
they're using the island or not, but you may want to take that into consideration while
planning boxes.

Also -- if you're worried about keeping your non-boxing husband entertained, the
Squished Pennies and Watery Grave boxes are down in Wauwatosa's village, which has
some pubs, restaurants and shops within 2-3 of blocks of the areas where the boxes are.
He could easily keep himself occupied while you hunt your treasure. :-)

Czeltic Girl
