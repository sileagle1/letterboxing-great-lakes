Looking for some input please

From: (ducacj@yahoo.com) | Date: 2018-03-22 13:11:06 UTC

 After 14 years of letterboxing I am finally getting all of our stamps and clues together into one album. I previously scrapbooked our finds into our vacation photo albums. Early on I started making 2 copies of every stamp - allowing each child to make a copy when they were younger and now it has just become a habit. I used one for scrapbooking and one remained in the spiral binder.


 I realized as I was compiling printed copies of clues to go along with the stamps for the album that we had only recorded just over 100 of our finds in Atlasquest. I started to go back and add in old finds but some finds not recorded date back to 2007.


Should I go back and enter in finds?


90% of our letterboxing the past 10 years has been done during our summer vacation. We choose a state to visit and spend 1-3 weeks touring the state. During that time we can rack up a lot of box finds! Especially if we get into some of the large cemeteries. I have always contacted a planter if we have found any issue with the box - replaced baggies, logbooks, or noticed something that may influence future boxers, etc. Or if we have been unable to find the box. 


I am just not sure if it is helpful from a planter's perspective for me to add in old finds? 


Any thoughts would be appreciated.


On another note we are planning 2 weeks in North and South Dakota this summer if anyone has any box recommendations for those states.


Thanks,

DUCA Family

Re: [LBGLK] Looking for some input please

From: Dawn Massie (ypsidawnkey@yahoo.com) | Date: 2018-03-23 02:21:56 UTC
I think you should absolutely record your old finds on AtlasQuest if you're so inclined. The main purpose of your virtual logbook on AQ is for you to have a record of your finds. It's true that each find you record will trigger a message to the planter, but I don't think anyone will mind. I know I don't when I receive a find notice from the past. You might copy and paste a brief note when you're doing this, something like "Just getting caught up on recording old finds."

At any rate, congratulations on 14 years of letterboxing and the memories made!

Dawnkey
