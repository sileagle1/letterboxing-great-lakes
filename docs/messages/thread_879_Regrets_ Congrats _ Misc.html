We're really sorry we missed the WI Gathering - we definitely would
have liked to meet some of the new boxers in the area. Our nieces &
nephews would also have enjoyed actually seeing some other LBing KIDS
(there were none at the first WI Gathering).

Congrats to those of you who just reached (or are almost at) the F100
mark! It wasn't long ago (April) that we were thrilled to reach the
triple digits. Since then we've been lucky to take several trips
that have included letterboxing. Last week we were in California
hiking, boxing, and wine tasting (that's why we missed the
Gathering). While in CA we were excited to reach our F500
milestone! Our count is now F552 plus 28 Hitchhikers (We don't count
HH in our regular F count). It's been a lot of fun finding boxes in
different areas (Wisconsin, Minnesota, Illinois, Michigan, Maryland,
Connecticut, Wyoming, California, Hawaii and Scotland) and the thrill
of the hunt hasn't worn out yet - as a matter of fact it just seems
to get more and more addictive....

Changing the subject once more: I saw a few recent posts about multi-
colored signature stamps and thought I would mention something we
noticed at the Monster Mash Gathering in Connecticut last month.
Many people that had multi-colored signature stamps had PRE-STAMPED
their image on adhesive backed paper. They had several sheets of
their stamp, with each sheet containing multiple copies of an
appropriate "label/sticker" size to match the size of their signature
stamp. When doing an exchange, all they did was peel off a "sticker"
with the pre-stamped image and place it in our journal. Not sure if
this would appeal to you, but it is an option to save time at
Gatherings.

To answer another question - quite early on we decided to stick with
green as the color we use for our signature stamps and don't plan to
vary the color. It's gotten to be "part of our signature".
