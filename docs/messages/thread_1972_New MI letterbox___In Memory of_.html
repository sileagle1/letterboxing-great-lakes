Hi
I just placed a new letterbox in An Arbor MI.
It's dog related as many of mine are, but not exactly a notable dog
in the sense that the others have been.
I'm posting the clues here, I'll add the box to my website and
letterboxing clue page when I get a chance.



This letterbox was created to memorialize Sarah, who passed away in
July of 2005. She is notable primarily because she was a beloved
family pet. Sarah was a Border Collie with the black coat with white
markings typical of the breed. She was intelligent, friendly, and
devoted.
When you stamp in to the log book, you are encouraged to add your
memorial to
someone who has touched your heart.
If you don't have a carved image, you may choose to simply write in
their name.

The letterbox is planted in Gallup park in Ann Arbor, Michigan. The
park entrance is off Geddes road, west of the intersection of
Geddes Road and Huron Parkway. The walk to the treasure will take 15-
20 minutes if you walk at a rapid pace, 30-40 minutes if you take a
more leisurely stroll. All travel is on level, paved trails. The
final search may require a roundabout approach due to vegetation.

The clues:

Enter the park, and park in the first parking lot.
Begin your quest at the sidewalk adjacent to the parking lot,Geddes
Road to your left.
Travel south.
Follow the river.
Kids play here, right?
Fishing pond?
Right again!
Gallup Park trail?
Right again!
Two big rocks.
Put your toes where they meet.
Look 260 degrees.
Ancient park resident reaches towards the sky.
Sarah rests there.
