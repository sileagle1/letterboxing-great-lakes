From: Leslie A. Fischer (l-fischer@northwestern.edu) | Date: 2004-04-07 10:00:58 UTC-05:00
Hi Tim,

I was the letterboxer who mentioned the clues at the Outagamie County
Museum. As I mentioned, these do NOT seem to be "our" clues, but a
whole untapped source of boxes not on the LBNA site. The LBNA site
lists only one box for Outagamie county, so it's difficult to imagine
the county would charge $10 for one clue. It seems to be their own
particular program.

Here's the listing and the website:

Letter boxing: Sleuthing Outagamie County
Year-round program!
Hidden treasures await young and old who dare to venture into new
territory... the back roads of Outagamie County! Letter boxing is a
fun way to learn about historic sites, the environment, and
orienteering. All you need is a rubber stamp, a stamp pad, a compass,
a good pair of walking shoes and a keen wit!
Cost: Passports/clue books are available now at the Outagamie Museum
gift shop for $10.00 ($9.00 for members).


http://www.foxvalleyhistory.org/calendar.html#exhibits

Hope this clarifies,
Leslie
(Acorn)
