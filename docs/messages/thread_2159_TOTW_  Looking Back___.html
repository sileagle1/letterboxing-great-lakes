Probably the biggest thrill was finding Green Tortugua's "McNeil Point" box
near Mount Hood in Oregon. Here's a link to the clue (it includes some
photos): http://www.atlasquest.com/lboxes/clue.html?gBoxId=17 The hike was

finally did, we were ecstatic! We were only the 2nd finders in 4 years.

Oregon. We really enjoy the chance to go out boxing with some "locals" when
we travel and also meet them for dinner. The hospitality of people such as
Lightin Bug, Janilla, Irene of Hikers'N Hounds, Funhog & Pungent Bob, Maiden
and all the other folks we met in these 2 states was much appreciated!

We were also happy that the weather cooperated for the Haunted Harvest
Gathering and that we had such a great turnout for the event!

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-01-12 10:52:16 UTC
I too would find it difficult to cite just a single box or event
from the past year. The highlights for me have been:

* My favorite letterbox/hike would have to be WI Hiker's Moose Lift
LB near Champion, MI. This was an incredibly beautiful hike,
particularly on a sunny autumn day.

* Favorite series would be the Bailey's Washington Island Series
(WI) by the Leapin' Lizards. A couple of these challenged us (and
almost made us miss the ferry), but we ended up with them all.
Superb scenery, superb carvings!

* Meeting and spending the entire morning chatting with Norse Gnome
while up in the U.P. this fall. Oh, and that Big Pine letterbox
that had us two-tracking for miles down mud-filled dirt roads (a
couple of times wondering if we would soom be out pushing)... and
we even saw a bear!

* Most memorable is definitely the rock scramble for the Bear Fence
LB in Virginia's Blue Ridge Mountains. We got to this one late in
the day and although we would be tempting dusk (and thus had a
slight concern about bears), it didn't sound like *that* long of a
hike and we wouldn't be back that way the next day. After climbing
and climbing up the face of rocks and narrow ledges, we reached *a*
summit. While breathtakingly high and breathtakingly beautiful
(especially at dusk), this was not *the* summit. We looked ahead on
the trail which skirted the edge of the rocks with a sheer drop-off
along one side. Not being mountain goats, we turned around, reread
the clues and found an alternate way to the box in which we actually
walked on the AT for about a mile, which was very cool.

* Another highlight of the Virgina trip -- finding my first [clued &
planted] Legerdemaine letterbox. Oh what beautiful works of art his
stamps are. Certainly treasures in my logbook.

* How can I not look back and remember meeting Mark & Sue Pepe?
Although that was one highlight of the GLG, it was certainly not the
only highlight as it was great to attend that gathering and meet so
many new letterboxers in the GL region and see all of the wonderful
carvings in Boch's Circus.

* Attending the Mystery Gathering in Rhode Island and getting to
meet so many letterboxers from all over the country, particularly
many whom I've exchanged e-mail with for the past 3 years --
Alafair, Funhog, Turtle McQ, Amanda from Seattle, Trish, Jay Drew,
Wanda & Pete, Warrior Woman, Clueless & Silent Doug, Team Green
Dragon, Always Chaos, Poison IV, Lady P... and of course a surprise
visit from one of Michigan's own LB icons. This was such a
fantastic event -- I highly recommend it to anyone who has the
chance to get to RI in the fall.

What a *GREAT* year it was! Thank you everyone for being a part of
it!

SpringChick
