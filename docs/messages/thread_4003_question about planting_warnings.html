question about planting/warnings

From: baliddle (baliddle@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2007-04-23 18:27:54 UTC-04:00
I have a question about planting. I'm in process of planting a
series of boxes in local cemeteries. There's a cemetery that I want
to use that I think would be perfect. But, a friend of mine is a
county deputy sheriff and one of her co-deps told her that they often
bust people in there for various delinquent activities (drugs,
etc). I've been through there several times in the past few weeks
and have never seen anyone in there, much less people doing these
things. This isn't a highly dangerous/super high crime area. I
think if people go there during the day and use common sense, they
would be safe enough in there. It may not be a great idea to go a
night (I'm not sure if they lock the gates at night here like most seem to do).

I have a very specific idea of the kind of cemetery I'm looking for
and I'm having a much harder time finding planting sites than I
thought I would. This one is perfect in all other aspects , so I
really want to plant a box in there.

My question is, should I:
- not plant it there at all because of this reported activity
- plant it anyway and don't mention anything about it.
- put some kind of vague reminder in the box clues about being extra
careful while there
- put some kind of more specific suggestion about not going at
night/bring a friend with you
- put some kind of really specific warning about possible delinquent
activity and not to stop if there are other people around

What would you do?



Bali

From: Gabrielle Coil (gmc81@randomkitty.net) | Date: 2007-04-23 16:32:47 UTC-07:00
It's up to you whether to plant a box in this cemetery or not. I couldn't say for sure what I would do in your shoes. If you do plant the box here, I think you should include specific warnings. The worst thing you could do is not mention any warning at all. You don't necessarily have to go into great detail about the goings on, but you should somehow warn people that unsavory characters may be about and that they need to exercise extra caution if they choose to go alone or after dark. I think we all know that we shouldn't letterbox alone and that we should be on the lookout for suspicious persons...but let's face it, in the heat of the moment letterboxing kind of takes over our brains and most of us are not as observant or careful as we might otherwise be.

Kimono Girl

From: xxxxxxxx (BrighidFarm@comcast.net) | Date: 2007-04-24 16:00:36 UTC-05:00
Amen to that one. I totally agree. There's one box in a deserted cemetery
here in this area and the last I looked, the clues mention nothing about the
area having safety problems. It's in a forest preserve area where crystal
meth dealers and users hang out (and where a few live) and more than once a
body (a dead body) has been pulled out of the pond that's there. Muggings
have occured, rapes have occured. And it's not just at night. I
personally would never risk going alone in the daytime either. A group of
people are probably fine going in there. Probably 99 times out of 100,
someone can go in there and nothing will happen. I just wouldn't want to be
that hundredth person thankyouverymuch.

Yes, warnings might cause some boxers to decide against looking for a box
that has safety warnings in the clues. And yes, everybody needs to look out
for themselves and use good judgement. But it's hard to use good judgement
when one might not know the history of an area, and the placer will has no
idea who-all is going to be coming from where-all to search for the box.
Notwithstanding the fact that it's not going to look too good if someone
gets seriously hurt going after a box and the police later find out it was
because of a letterbox, and it hits the media that way, I wouldn't want to
be responsible for someone getting hurt without at least having warned them
that problems might exist so that they can make an informed choice. Being
able to make an informed choice is nice. It's the polite thing for a placer
to do in my opinion. :-)

~~ Mosey ~~

From: Becky (imdurangogirl@aol.com) | Date: 2007-04-25 13:25:55 UTC
I have two boxes planted in a beautiful park in Youngstown OH. The
problem with the park is there are a bunch of entrances exits all over
the city. Some of them in less then desirable areas. In my clues, i
simply stated please leave the park the same way we had you enter if
you are unfamiliar with this area. I didn't want to go into great
detail and alarm people, but I also didn't want them exiting into one
of the areas that could be kind of rough. I felt it was my
responsibility to put something in, since im the one familiar with the
park and the surrounding area. I think a cautionary note asking
visiting boxers to be aware of people and their surroundings is a good
idea. ~The Cats Meow~
