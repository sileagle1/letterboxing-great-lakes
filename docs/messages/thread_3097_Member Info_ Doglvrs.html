I knew I wrote this story down recently here on the list here it is again with some other
info added(Mrs. Doglvrs speaking ~ as usual)

Long about May of '04 a gal in my knitting group at church came in totally
excited about this new thing she had done that weekend (Memorial Day weekend) with
her Mom. "It was so fun!" she proclaimed and proceeded to tell the rest of us about it with
intense excitement. One of the other ladies in our group was a rubberstamper and she
asked a few questions...but mostly the rest of us ~ who obviously enjoy entertaining
ourselves while sitting on our butts ~ looked on.

When she was finished showing us the clue sheet and talking about walking for a really
long time for the second box...I looked at her and said "Cindy ~ you mean you tromped
around in the woods for 2 hours and you found a rubberstamp for all your trouble?" Poor
Cindy replied, "Well yes, but it was so much fun!" To which I replied...."Dang! Sounds like
exercise to me." Earned me a round of laughter...which of course is what I was after... ; )

Then around August of afore mentioned year...Granny came to visit. This little family had
no cash to speak of, and a whole 4 days of entertaining of Granny to do. So, I emailed
Cindy and said..."Hey, what was the thingy about the woods and stamp and whatnot?"
Cindy - bless her sweet little heart was happy to oblige. I printed off some local clues and
the kids and I were ready ~ Granny, however - was not. Seems she had the same silly idea
her daughter did...you know, about the exercise...

Jump to Monday - bored kids, school is yet to begin..."Hey," I says "go grab
that compass Dad bought last month and we'll give this letterboxing a try." Off we go to
hunt up a couple of turtles. We followed along and did real
good....when we got to the first box, and it was exactly where it should be...I jumped up
and down I was so excited! (Brings you back to your first find don't it?) My sweet children
looked at me and said "Mom, are you crying?!" Never mind that....onward.

At dinner ~ we regaled Dad with our adventure...along with how we tried and tried to find
a Sphinx in with turtles. On the way home, I happen to glance at the name of the park and
noticed it was not the same as the Turtles home....Dad says "I know that park! I can take
you there!"...all manly like. And thus he did then back to find Who was at Hager too!
Two days later we became the Doglvrs ~ we chose our name because we have 3 dogs
(Emily, Alex & Lana) who we all love dearly ~ and because we had a paw print stamp in the
house. I eventually hand carved one for us and if you've ever seen it, the RASW in the paw
prints stands for Rick, Angel, Stephanie & Wyatt. We live in Hudsonville, Michigan where
we do things like make cheese, teach piano & flute lessons, play clarinet, and battle with
Pokemon cards.

We have letterboxed in 4 states and planted boxes all over our county and a couple of
others. I am the carver/logbook maker/addicted boxer ~ and Mr. gives me ideas. I feel it
is our duty to hide boxes that men want to find thus more hubby's will want to box with
their wives. I know there are a few of our number who have it the other way `round and
for them, I am sorryI could teach all your wives to knit, and then you'll have hats, gloves
and scarves for boxing in winter. ; )

I look forward to each and every gathering and hope to be able to meet many of the new
faces around the list ~ especially those of you who have exchanged emails with me.

Mrs. Doglvrs
