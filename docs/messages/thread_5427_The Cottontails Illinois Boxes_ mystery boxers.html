I think most of these "mystery" boxers are playing by the rules...they just aren't active in the online letterboxing community so we never hear about them until their stamps turn up in our log books. While I certainly do appreciate box reports (someone emailed me a week or two ago that one of my more "far flung" boxes is in desperate condition so now I know I do have to figure out a time to get down there and fix it, without the report it might sit in a sad state for many more weeks or even months before I manage to get to it and even then I probably wouldn't bring adequate repair supplies) I also really appreciate the mystery boxers who log in and don't email me. It makes checking on my boxes a surprise that I anticipate like opening a present...how many people have visited since the last time I checked?

Good point about the money in the boxes being a potential source of mischief, BTW. I hadn't thought of that (not that I really want to encourage people leaving anything other than stamp images or hitch hikers in my boxes anyway).
