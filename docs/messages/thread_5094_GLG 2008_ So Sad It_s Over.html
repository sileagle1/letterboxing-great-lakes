GLG 2008, So Sad It's Over

From: Gooble Gobble (gooble_gobble_3@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-06-01 23:20:04 UTC-07:00
Another GLG has come and gone. I can't help but feel a
little misty eyed. The day was magical to say the
least. What a beautiful day, for the most part (more
on that later) everything was perfect.

Thank you SOOOOO MUCH to The Red Cat for all the work
she put into making the gathering truly special.
Everyone I talked to had a great day. A very special
Thank You to Red Cat's parents and brother for
supplying everyone with a tasty selection of meats to
enjoy, and for cooking them for us. The selection of
food was over the top as always with a huge variety of
things to munch on (I don't remember who it was that
made the delicious pasta salad with the wheel pasta,
artichoke hearts, sundried tomatoes, etc. but please
send me the recipe, I forgot to ask you for it)


It's true that letterboxers are the nicest people
around. As always everyone was walking around with a
huge grin plastered on their face. I really enjoyed
seeing all of you again, or for the first time, and
hope it's not too long before our "paths" cross again.



Thank you to everyone who made boxes for the event.
All of them were great and I will always look at them
and remember how special the day was. It's funny how
even several years later you can look at an image in
your logbook and remember where the box was, what kind
of day it was, etc, without having it written down.
Things like that, to me, are a testament to how
special and unique letterboxing is. I thank God for
being a part of it. I thank God for all of you as
well, for without you it wouldn't be possible.


Now for the interesting part. I don't know when people
left, but I can say with almost certainty that I was
the last one out of the park (with the exception of
Optic and Kayakin' Monkey who were camping) because
when I got back to my car at 9:15 I was the only one
left in our section of the parking lot. Do you
remember where you were and what you were doing at
8:00 p.m.? I do and will never forget it. It was a
truly memorable experience to say the least.


After parting ways with C.C. Family at Campsite #12 it
started to rain lightly. I headed down the bluff to
find the rest of the boxes. It rained a little bit and
then it stopped and cleared up some. The sun came out
and everything was perfect again.


After looking a little while for some boxes on the
south river trail I decided to head back up the bluff
and look for "The World's Largest Ball Of Twine" box.
Having secured that image I was off to find the boxes
by Martini Man and Wisconsin Hiker. I knew exactly
where I needed to go, being very familiar with this
side of the park, and started the trek. What I failed
to notice though were the ominous clouds rolling in
fast.


I was stamping into "The Body" and felt a couple of
light raindrops hitting my head. No big deal I
thought, This is just a sprinkling like before and it
will pass soon. Ummm, that's not how things went down
though. I thought "There's no sense in turning back
now, I'm two miles from my car", and I was going to
find all the boxes. I continued on a little ways to
see how far I could get and what would happen with the
rain. Maybe 200 feet down the trail the sky opened on
me, and I don't mean cleared up. I have never been
outside in the open in rain like that in my life. For
those of you who weren't there I was on top of the
bluffs on the edge of a HUGE prairie when the deluge
came. There was thunder, lightning and a wind that was
unbelievable. I would guess that the wind was at least
30-40 m.p.h. The first thought that ran through my
head was "Oh no!, my logbook will get soaked!"
Immediately after that I sought cover, and fast. I
found a nice big tree to stand behind, which as
everyone knows is the best spot to hide in a storm,
ESPECIALLY one with a lot of lightning : ) The first
mission was to make sure my bag stayed as dry as
possible to protect my collection of treasures, next
was my futile attempt at staying out of the driving
rain. When I say driving I mean driving. It was
seriously coming at me almost horizontally, that is
NOT an exaggeration. With nothing to block the wind
coming across the prairie the wind was raging. I kept
trying to look out from behind the tree to see if
there were tornadoes but it was of no use. As soon as
my head cleared the side of the tree the rain was
blinding it was coming down so hard and fast. I had no
choice but to wait it out. After about 20 minutes of
the Heavenly faucet on "High" it let up enough for me
to give walking back to the car a try, my hopes for
finding all the boxes dashed. I thanked God for my
safety and started on the long, wet trek to the
parking lot. The rain stopped after 5 minutes of
walking at which point I promptly slipped and fell in
the mud : ) I had a GREAT laugh at myself for now
being completely drenched and covered in mud on my
left side. I gave thanks to God for my slip and fall
in the mud because it just seemed like a perfect
ending to a perfect day. As soon as those words were
out of my mouth I was on my left side again in the
mud. Awesome, Just Awesome. I felt like a little kid
who has been splashing in puddles and not caring how
wet or dirty they were. It was a beautiful, liberating
experience to have at the end of such a great day. I
wouldn't have changed it for the world. You could
probably hear my laughter and shouts of joy back at
the parking lot.


Later that night at a small post gathering party C.C.
Family and Hart x 6 told me that there were tornado
warnings in the area while I was out in the middle of
the storm. I felt very blessed to be safe. I'm glad I
had the foresight to bring a change of clothes and
socks with me, "Just in case".


Once again, Thank You to God, The Red Cat, and all of
you. Whether you were at the gathering or not, it's
you who make this thing we call letterboxing so
special. God Bless, take care, be safe, and box on
brothers and sisters, box on.

Gooble Gobble

Re: GLG 2008, So Sad It's Over

From: Sara (saralacey@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-06-02 14:47:58 UTC
Gooble Gobble wasn't the only one that was able to experience the
storm in its full glory. After running in the CC Family on our way
back to our campsite and finding out that the rain fly was put on my
tent (Letterboxers are wonderful people!!) we decided to take the
time to look for some more boxes.

We were sitting on the side of the trail happily stamping Pengi into
our log books when a fellow camper came down with his two dogs. He
was on his way to the storm shelter and informed us that there was a
tornado watch out. I left Mom to put Pengi safely back in his
hiding spot while I jogged (those of you who made it to our site
know this wasn't a fun jog) back to our site to grab headlamps,
something to eat and full water bottles to take to the shelter with
us. I also had to rescue out letterboxes. About halfway up the
hill the rain started. I'd tell you about it, but I can't top
Gooble Gobble's description.

Being the mother that my mom is, she couldn't just sit and wait
patiently for me. She was convinced I had been struck by lightening
and came up the hill after me. We met near the top and started our
slip slide down the hill. About half way down the rain stopped and
we decided there was no point heading to the shelter and trudged
back up the hill to our site. In my mad dash to gather stuff to
take to the shelter with us, I managed to get a bunch of water in
the tent. We realized we had been beat and decided to pack it up
and head home. As we were frantically packing up our gear the rain
started again. There wasn't a dry spot on my body. Optic had been
smart enough to pack a rain poncho and I think a portion of her
shirt managed to stay dry. I was dumb enought to think, it's not
a "real" backpacking trip and we're only a mile from the car, I
don't really need all my normal gear. I don't think I'll make that
mistake again.

It was a day neither of us will ever forget. We didn't get to spend
the night in our cute, little campsite that we were so excited
about, but we did have a wonderful day. We both met some great
people and have spectacular new images in our logbooks. A huge
thanks to Red Cat and her family for all the work they did!! The
only regret we have is that we didn't have time to meet everyone we
wanted to. We're both looking forward to the next gathering.

Kayakin' Monkey




Re: GLG 2008, So Sad It's Over

From: artgekko_mn (artgekko@frontiernet.net) | Date: 2008-06-02 16:46:04 UTC
I had no doubt that GG was still out there when it hit. I was driving
north, heading into the worst of it. I spent about half an hour at a
gas station, waiting it out. Glad I did, as when I finally resumed the
trip I passed drifts of golf-ball sized hail.

The first part of the day was truly wonderfulcouldn't have asked for
better weather or a better group. I know I missed meeting several
groups that were there, but it was such a pleasure to meet so many
people and families after seeing your trail names for so long. Thanks
to everyone who drove or flew a distance to make it.

Extra special thanks to The Red Cat and her fabulous family for
setting up and hosting such a great event and feast! Yay to Greg, Jane
and Brian for all their hard work.

I have to apologize to anyone who tried to find my "A Little Looney"
box. The clues were offI don't know what I was thinking when I wrote
them. A thousand apologies! And to the Cottontails, who did manage to
find it despite that glitch, I hope you aren't cursing me now because
of the poison ivy! When I planted it it some 2-3 weeks ago, nothing
was leafed out. When I got to it Saturday, I realized the stump was in
the middle of a decent patch of it. The perils of planting in the
spring, I guess. I hope none of the other boxes I hid had the same
problem!

Finally, what was the tick tally? I pulled seven off mefour wood
ticks and three deer ticks. Two days later I still have the
creepy-crawlies. :-P

-AG
