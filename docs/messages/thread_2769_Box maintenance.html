Box maintenance

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2006-07-13 03:11:14 UTC
Checked my "Milwaukee Neighborhood" box tonight and it was alive &
well and in good shape. A few people had logged it as "Attempted"
but as many of us know, this does NOT necessarily mean a box is
missing. This is especially true if it is a "mystery" type box. I
know that at least one of the persons that logged an "Attempt" was
actually a few miles away from the correct location.

6 groups have found the box in the 8 months it has been there, but
all 6 visited within the first 2 weeks of it being placed! It's been
lonely for a while, so if you have been delaying hunting it down due
to the "Attempted" reports, have no fear - it IS there!

On the other hand, I just received an email saying my "Who Is It?"
box might be missing - Darn! I'll try to check it out in the next
few days.

Also checked on my "Picnic in the Capitol" box last weekend and it
had migrated from its original spot, but was still in the general
area. I put it back in the correct spot.

My "Door County Tradition" box was in fine shape on June 24.

I will probably be slightly relocating my "Playing Possum" box in
McHenry, IL this weekend since I have had several reports that
a "Restricted Area" is now posted on your way to the box. I'll
announce an update when I write some new clues, so DON'T go looking
for this one Saturday or Sunday.

That's it for now...

I'm very relieved to report that my "Who Is It?" box is in fine shape.
(It is one of my favorite stamps, so I was dismayed to hear of its
possible demise!)

However this is another example of a box that is MUCH harder to find in
the summer. It was placed in February so there wasn't any undergrowth
to contend with, not to mention additional trimming discards that don't
even belong there... This means you'll need to be a dedicated searcher
or you may want to wait until late fall when things aren't so thick!

The box has been found by 5 groups so far.
