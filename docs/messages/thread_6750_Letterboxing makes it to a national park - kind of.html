Letterboxing makes it to a national park - kind of!

From: Doodle & Deedle Bug (doodle_n_deedle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2010-09-30 10:06:45 UTC-07:00
I was reading a copy of the Akron Beacon Journal today, and the cover story for their lifestyle section had the title, "'Questing' enriches area park adventures - New outdoor activity for families to begin" about the Questing program they've set up in the Cuyahoga Valley National Park here in Ohio.

As I read the article, the more I thought, "This is a take on letterboxing!" How could I not think that after seeing the photo of a park ranger by a tree stump where a plastic food storage container, aka a "quest box" was nestled, or after reading this: Questing involves following a rhyming trail of charming clues and a clever map to find a hidden box. Participants sign their names to a log book. They collect unique STAMPS [emphasis mine] in passport-like booklets or on quest brochures. Many get THEIR OWN STAMPS they use to mark the log book.

AND THEN, I read this paragraph at the very end:

"The program is based on a 150-year-old form of treasure hunting called ''letter boxing'' that started around England's Dartmoor National Park."

There's also a paragraph earlier in the article that says, "The biggest problem getting the program started is that no one involved had any experience or exposure to questing. Everyone was a raw rookie." Oh, silly park rangers, all you had to do was ask your local letterboxers to help out!

Here's the full text of the article: http://www.ohio.com/news/first/104062479.html It's going to be interesting finding one of these and seeing exactly how similar and different it is to a letterbox!

Doodle Bug.
