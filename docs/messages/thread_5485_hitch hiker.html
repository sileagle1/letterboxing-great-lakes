From: xxxxxxxx (BrighidFarm@comcast.net) | Date: 2008-12-28 05:42:11 UTC-06:00
I find those all the time. Lots of folks who create hitchhikers have no
interest in listing them online at either LbNA or atlasquest. If it has a
logbook with it and looked like every other hitchhiker, then I'd say just
move it on. If it has a logbook, does it have an email addy for the creator
that you can contact them that you found it? Most do of the ones that I've
found, altho many times the addy is no longer valid. Once a hitchhiker is
placed out there, there's little way for the creator to change the logbook
to reflect a changed email addy.

As far as changing your name, that would involve changing things in your
email system. At least, in my case it would. I use MS Outlook and I put
"xxxxxxx" down in MS Outlook for who was sending email out from this email
address since it's my farm/business address and is used by folks other than
myself at times. So I didn't want it to reflect any one person's identity.
I'm assuming there must be someplace in your email system on your computer
where you can change the name of who is sending out the emails.

But be aware that it'll be that way for any emails you send out that aren't
letterbox related too. At least that's how it works for me in MS Outlook.

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2008-12-30 14:21:36 UTC
There are many letterboxers who create hitch hikers and intentionally
do not list them anywhere. It goes back to the days when there was no
Atlasquest, only LbNA, and it was truly just a clue database, without
the ability to log finds -- people use to just keep track of their
finds in their logbook, and many still do. Since a HH has no clues,
there was no reason to list in the clue database. There was a Yahoo!
list where people posted HH finds and for several years Pete and Wanda
from RI kept an informal listing of HH and their travels, but it was
very manual and labor intensive for them.

Enter Atlasquest... now that LbNA shares listing functionality with
AQ and they both have the ability to log finds, they have also added
the ability to track hitch hikers. However many boxers, particularly
those who have been involved in the hobby for several years, choose
not to use this function, preferring the element of surprise
associated with HH's. Many do not even care to know where their
little HH has traveled -- it is just out there for other boxers to
find and enjoy.

So if you can't find a HH listed, don't think it is odd. You should
still contact the placer if they have provided contact info in the
logbook, but if not, don't knock yourself out over it. Just consider
yourself lucky to have found it, give it any TLC it may need and move
it along to the next box.

SpringChick
