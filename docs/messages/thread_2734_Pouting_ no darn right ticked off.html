Pouting, no darn right ticked off

From: Sherry Lynn (ShersL84Bed@aol.com) | Date: 2006-07-02 15:04:40 UTC
Hi

My sons letterboxing party was a great success, Love all the
wonderful pictures we have of the kids looking for the boxes and
reading their clues and maps.
Unfortunately while I was out planning for the party on the trails a
couple days before, I left my letterboxing journal. :(
I recieved a phonecall from a man who asked for me or my son, as both
of our names were on the journal with our phone number asking if
found please call.
He called alright, but when I asked how far he was away, he said,
that was all relative. I thought that was an odd answer.
So, I asked him if he was close to the preserve where he found it, he
then again stated that was all relative.
The guy was not offering where he lived, or a place that I could meet
him.
Finally I got from him that he lived in a couple cities away. Luck
have it, it was the city my mother lives in. Unforunately for me, he
wouldn't give me his address and was unwilling to meet at the local
market there in town. When I asked him if he would be available to
meet some time on the next day, he says a vague..well I never know
what the next day might bring. grrrrrrrrrr
He told me to give my mother his phone number, and that he would give
her directions only. At this time, I'm wondering if this is some
kind of freak. I'm not going to subject my mother to a possible
lunatic. Why did he bother calling me at all? to taunt me that he
brought it home and I wasn't going to get it back? grrrrrrr

Not that it truly matters but he also implied that he had read it all
page by page. I guess thats not a big deal, but it was something
that my son and I shared together so it is kind of irratating to me.
Am I being a nutcase myself and over-reacting? I'd like to have it
back, what would you do? Start new?

From: xxxx (PonyExpressMail@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-07-02 11:19:34 UTC-05:00
Well, if you're over-reacting, then folks would consider me over-reactive
too. Does your gut tell you that you're over-reacting? I'll bet it
doesn't. :-)

The guy certainly sounds less than all there to me. If it were me, I
wouldn't give out any further information. If he's unwilling to even meet
you at a public place, there's something wrong with that picture. Maybe he
was just looking for you to offer some sort of reward, but didn't want to
come right out and ask for it himself? Who knows? He might not be an
ax-murderer/serial killer sort of guy but why take any chances? Why would
he want to give directions only to your mother? She's just another stranger
to him. But, being your mother, she would be an *older* stranger than you
and thus possibly more vulnerable than you (tho I've met some grannies that
can really kick butt). Hopefully, the journal didn't contain any types of
personal entries that would give him more information about your family than
he has any need to know.

Start over with a new journal.

P.S.: This is one reason why I carry pieces of cardstock with me to stamp
the box's stamp. Then I put the cardstock into my scrapbook/logbook when I
get back home. I have sooo much in the way of personal writings and photos
in my scrapbook/logbook, and also entries that nieces and nephews and
friends' children have made, that there's no way I want some weirdo finding
it.

I had a strange episode about 15 or 20 years ago as a dog trainer. I often
did private sessions in a person's home. Never even thought about taking
someone with me for safety's sake. Half the time I didn't even have anyone
who knew exactly where I was going on what evenings. I had never had any
problems up to that point.

Got a call from a gentleman who said he'd been having trouble getting his
dog trained by trying to do it himself out of a book. Lived out in the
country so wasn't any too close to any group classes so had decided to try a
few private lessons. He sounded totally normal on the phone. Seemed like
the typical nice, well intentioned, but kinda clueless dog owner. So I
agreed to drive out there after work one evening to meet with him and his
dog and give him some pointers. Drove up the long drive. Nice house, but
totally secluded from from any of the neighbors. Got a bad gut feeling as I
was going up the drive. I follow my gut feelings so I turned my truck
around and left. About 5 years later or so, the guy was convicted of having
murdered two women in two separate instances on hiking trails in another
state. Now that's not to say that murder was what he had in mind for me.
But I'm sure glad I turned around that day and left. And I've met a few
more weirdos over the years since then. So I've become very careful over
the years. I don't mind being called over-reactive. I'm alive to hear
people tell me that I'm over-reactive. So over-reactive is just fine with
me. Tho I prefer to call it "cautious" -- very very cautious. :-)

OK, the first thing I would do is google this guys phone number. I
would definately not give him your mothers number. Then I would
google your phone number just to see how much he knows about where you
are. That alone may give you pause! The next thing I would do is
contact the police and tell them about your uneasy feelings. This is
most seriously a situation where your gut feeling of concern is DEAD
ON ACCURATE. The people on this list who have met me could tell you
that I am not a small guy, and as a result things like this tend not
to scare me. However, reading your post sent a chill down MY spine.
You may never get your journal back, but if the police talk to him it
may be enough to make him aware that your guard is up. Hopefully
enough to make him go away. It is unfortunate that there are people
like this in our world. In all seriousness CALL THE POLICE. I am
guessing that he may have been very close when he called you. He was
trying to gage your reaction to his call. Make sure that your
reaction is enough to keep him away.
