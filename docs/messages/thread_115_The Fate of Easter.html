The Fate of Easter

From: Deb (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-04-11 11:55:36 UTC
Got out yesterday with my granddaughter to do the Fate of Easter
series. I had originally thought the 6 box series in a city park
would be perfect for her. When the additional 6 box bonus series
was added I was a bit skeptical as to whether she would stay
interested long enough to complete them all. It was her first
real 'boxing trip, as previously she had only accompanied me to
check on boxes, but never to hunt boxes.

She immediately got excited when we ran into Grandvilla, Mobius
Klein and Azobox on the way to the first box. Her blank white log
book now had 3 exchanges in and she was off to collect more stamps
and fill up those pages. While stamping in at the first box, we
were greeted by BindleBabe who had stopped by to check up for a
minute, so some additional stamping took place there for yet another
exchange. By the time we were headed for box #4, she had snatched
the clue sheet from me and was in charge of which move we made
next. We had a bit of trouble with the 6th box and when I asked her
if she had had enough or wanted to keep going, the response was "I
want to find them all."

So on we pressed... adding stamps from all of the bonus boxes to
our log books and then heading back to eventually find #6. Along
the way she also took over the compass and much to my surprise
learned very quickly how to set a bearing and orientate herself to
head off in the right direction.

All 12 boxes complete, I was ready to head home. She on the other
hand wanted to go find more boxes! I convinced her to save some for
the next trip and she agreed only if I promised to take her with me
next time.

It was funny watching her as we walked the trail on the way back. A
couple of times she pointed to hollow root cavities and old stumps
and said, "that would be a good place for a letterbox."

Thanks BindleBabe for a great series of boxes, some very nice stamps
and the delightful story-style clue presentation. We had a
wonderful time!

SpringChick
