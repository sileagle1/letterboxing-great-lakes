Oh, what fun! We had a great time hunting for all the letterboxes at
the Faire....we know nearly every inch of that property! Meeting
Dragon was a delight! in his velvety green pants in 85 degree heat!
Ugh! I don't know how those people did it back then, and only took
baths twice a year? Ugh again! Do you really think that is true? Saw
everything from soup to nuts when it came to costumes and if you are
a people watcher, it's worth the price of admission to just sit and
watch everyone. I loved that part as much as the letterboxing!
There is lots to do and great food and drink. "Here, here!" We were
cheap and went back to our vehicle for out brown bagged lunch so we
could save a little for the treasures we wanted to take home with us.

Learned a lot about clues and interpreting them and sometimes the
obvious was just that....TOO OBVIOUS! To echo Shutterfly, "What
bell" "Oh, that bell" DUH! I think this should be an annual thing for
doing at the Faire. Great fun and the environment is charming! The
wood sprites were my favorite charaters. Just wish I could do a
British, Scottish or Irish accent well. The cast of players are
great and always in character....and many of the patrons attending
also dressed up and just flowers in your hair would be fun!

Enjoyed meeting Jane the Phool too! She was a stitch. She plays the
perfect part at the Faire, is a stamper and loves to letterbox! If
you decide to go....wear some flowers in your hair, sunscreen on your
person, comfortable shoes, backpack is great for carrying
supplies,and be sure to have some of the vegetable tempura before you
leave! Yum.... Dragon, Thanks for putting together those great clues
and keeping that watchful eye on us! :)
