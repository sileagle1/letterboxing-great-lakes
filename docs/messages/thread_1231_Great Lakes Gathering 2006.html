Re: Great Lakes Gathering 2006

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-04-28 13:40:55 UTC
This definitely sounds like one possibility and the offer is
appreciated, but I would like to open it up to the entire group to
see if there might be others also interested in hosting the 2006 GL
Gathering in their area. In order to allow some of the newer folks
who may not have attended a gathering in the past to get a feel for
what is involved, I would like to suggest holding off with a
definitive plan as to location and date for 2006 until after this
year's June gathering.

When I began the annual GL Gathering, the intention was that it would
move around within the region from one geographic area to another,
hoping that it would end up near everyone at one time or another. I
realize that the state of WI has not hosted the gathering in the
past, but last year's IL gathering was just over the border -- just a
little over an hour from the Milwaukee area. I am also considering
that for some time now there have been enough letterboxers in WI to
support intermediate gatherings throughout the year, giving
opportunity for people who are not able to travel to attend an
event. In other areas where there are not as many boxers, the only
opportunity to attend an event is the annual GL Gathering.

If I remember correctly, at last year's gathering the 2005 event was
put up for discussion, looking for location and host volunteers, and
the reason it came back to MI a second time is because Delirium is
the only one who volunteered. But letterboxing within the GL region
has grown a lot since that time and there may be others interested in
hosting the annual event in their area.

It was also the intention that various people would take turns
hosting the annual regional event. As much as I enjoyed hosting the
2003 gathering and would have been willing to do so every year, I
felt it would foster unity and involvment within the region if
various people hosted the gathering from one year to the next. This
is the reason that I handed over the reins after hosting the first
gathering and the reason I did not step in this year when Delirium
had to bow out. I think it is only fair as we look forward to the
2006 gathering to open it up to anyone who might be interested in
taking a turn. Of course, if there is nobody else interested, I am
grateful that some of the veterans are willing to step up time and
again to share their experience.

As for the date... there is never going to be a date that works for
everyone. When the original gathering was held the Saturday before
Memorial Day, it had been the intention that would become "the
weekend" on an annual basis and people would be able to plan on that
date. For whatever reason last year's event was moved to the 2nd
week in June and now this year we are at the 3rd week in June. The
original premise behind holding the gathering at the end of May was
to avoid the flurry of activities that typically vie for June
weekends. Graduations, weddings and Father's Day are all big June
weekend events that typically cannot be rescheduled, and which most
people would give priority over a letterbox gathering. While it is
certainly a possibility to put the date up for a vote on a year-by-
year basis, it is easier for people to plan ahead if there is an
established weekend that stays consistent from one year to the next.

SpringChick

From: azobox (jparkerg@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-04-28 13:55:59 UTC
Hey Dragon,
I'm thinking that we should maybe hold off a decision for 2006 for a
couple of months. There are so many new letterboxers in our midst who
have not had the Gathering "experience" and I suspect that after they
attend one or more of the many events both large and small that are
planned for the next several months they will have some good ideas and
a better handle on what goes into planning events like these.
I think we may see some new volunteers emerge who can take the group
in even different directions re format and activities.
And it may evolve into a series of smaller gatherings in scattered
locales which could more easily draw boxers who cannot drive great
distances but who might be interested in being involved on a small scale.

Anyone else with thoughts on this?
