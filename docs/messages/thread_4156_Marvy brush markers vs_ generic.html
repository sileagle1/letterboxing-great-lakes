From: mayofamily4 (deannamayo@hotmail.com) | Date: 2007-06-07 17:39:26 UTC
I'm a recent newcomer to this hobby and have been learning lots from
the discussions, etc. After seeing several signature stamps done in
multiple colors and another placed stamp that BEGGED for colors (an
Easter basket!), I decided to invest in a set of Marvy brush markers.
My local Hobby Lobby carries them for $16.99 so I headed there.
However, prominently displayed on the end cap was "Hobby Lobby Brush
Pens" for $1.99! After some deliberation I decided to spend the extra
$15 and go with the tried and true. The others did not mention anything
about being archival safe/acid free. Has anyone tried these? How
important is it to use archival safe/acid free? I didn't one to be
responsible for ruining anyone's logbook!

I haven't tried that particular brand (the Hobby Lobby ones), but I
have tried several others. The thing I didn't like about them is that
they don't stay wet as long, even with huffing on them.
At first I wooried about ruining my good Marvys by coloring unclean
stamps. So I keep the Marvys for my postal boxes and use either Ink
Dots or cheaper markers on the trail.

Also...wait for coupons! It can make those Marvys a lot more
affordable.

Hi!

We use markers out on the trail all the time. There are so many great
stamps out there that we really want to do them justice by using color
when it enhances the image. We carry 3 different sets of Marvy
markers - the Primary, Pastel and Garden.... and I have to admit I
just ordered the Garden set also. If a stamp is dirty, we simply
clean it with a wet napkin before coloring it with our markers.

Of course there are some stamps that seem best with a single color, so
we also carry black, green, blue and red standard size inkpads and
a "palette" of small inkpads that can be removed from a row and used
individually.

In the Milwaukee, WI area the Michael's craft stores and Joann Fabric
stores often have 40% or 50% off coupons. That's when we buy our ink
supplies! However we have found that the Marvey Markers last a long
time - we have stamped a lot of stamps and are still using some of our
original markers from over 3 years ago - so they are worth the
investment!

From: ruhlette (ruhlette@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-06-08 19:40:06 UTC-07:00
I also love the Marvy Markers. I started with the LePlume II dual-tipped
markers, 2 sets - primary and Victorian. I just bought the fatter brush
markers in both sets. I hardly ever used the writing end, so I wouldn't
recommend them over the fat brush type, which inks up much faster. My
markers are still going strong AND I've managed not to lose a single one.

If I could only afford one set, I would choose the Victorian colors. They
seem more woodsy to me. But then again, I'm in love with the Arts and
Crafts Movement.
