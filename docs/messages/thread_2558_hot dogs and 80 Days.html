hot dogs and 80 Days

From: tiptoetonto (tiptoetonto@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-05-30 04:19:59 UTC
WI hiker & Martini Man - We will gladly bring a couple of packages
each of brats/hot dogs and buns - We really appreciate all your hard
work and incredible attention to detail! But I must warn you that
I've sworn off letterboxing...Yes it is true, I quit! ....
Hopefully, a week of frantic end-of-the-school-year-crazy teaching
will offer a little R&R and I will be fully recovered by this coming
weekend! Let me explain...

We also attempted to do as many of the 80 Day boxes that we could
fit in this weekend...That screaming you heard in the state of
Michigan you ask? Tonto is making me confess that it was not a
sonic boom...it was me screaming in moments of frustration, I HATE
LETTERBOXING" - Yes, it is true, I shouted this more than once in
the woods...the shame I'm feeling is more than I can stand. Trying
to get the 80 Day boxes, fighting the mosquitoes, the stinging
nettle, 600 miles in the car, and the 90 degree temps all were a
piece of cake....The stamps were so wonderful that I could hardly
contain myself. We also managed to get a few of the Holy Grail
boxes including the "Migrating coconuts", but then it
happened....one of the 80 Day boxes eluded us....Maddening insanity
swept over me....We even stayed overnight near the eluding box -
thinking maybe we had hit a wall (and darkness!) a good night's
sleep would clear our thinking...back at it in the morning, it still
didn't reveal itself....AGHHHHH
We had to shake the insanity out of each other - there was talk
about getting a chain saw and just mowing down the whole area until
we found the dog-gone box, but common sense prevailed and the only
damage done was a very heavy trodden trail to where the box should
have been!
We felt it best we move on, after all there were more world cities
to conquer and the clock was ticking...Thankfully more success came
in day two - We even ran into Kiss me Kate and Barefeet Ankh (who
wasn't barefoot at all I might add!) - fellow Illinoians trying to
show the Michiganders that we too can scoot around the world in
record time - but then as the sun was setting and the bugs were
again feasting on our sweaty stinkin' flesh ...it happened again! A
second box proved to be our undoing..this time the clues just
evaporated...there was much swearing...and swating!

With tears in our eyes, scratches on our legs and fists shaking at
the sky we headed "back home again to Indiana"...We stumbled into
Tiptoe's parents cottage bewitched and bewildered just as the last
vestiges of light for the day gave up it's last spark. Her parents
scolded that there must be therapy available for this horrid hobby.
Thankfully it was a holiday, and the Indy 500 was still filling much
of the conversation, or else we are certain a family "intervention"
may have been called to convience us that we just have to give up
this maddening addiction...at a weak moment Tiptoe promised to
quit....Again, a good night sleep, (and getting out of hostile
territory) led us to clearer thinking.

We have returned home and read all the exciting posts regarding next
weekend's gathering...Letterboxing smiles are beginning to return to
our faces...But, if you are missing a few hot dogs and brats, know
that an intervention is going on somewhere in either Indiana or
Illinois!
Tiptoe & Tonto...stuck someplace around the world!
