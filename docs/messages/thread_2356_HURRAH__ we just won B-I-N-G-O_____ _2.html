>Pope Gregory XIII introduced a new calendar into the Christian world
>and, in 1852, France was the first country to switch from the Julian
>to the new Gregorian calendar. The significance of this switch was
>that the beginning of the year was moved from April 1st to January 1st.

>However, there were some people who did not hear about the switch, or
>did not believe it, or just refused to accept the change of the date,
>and so they continued to celebrate New Year's Day on April 1st. Others
