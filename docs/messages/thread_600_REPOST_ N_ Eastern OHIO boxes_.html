REPOST: N. Eastern OHIO boxes!

From: Armadillo Jo (jes7o@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-08-26 05:38:30 UTC-07:00
This was on the big list and I thought that I'd repost
it here, as this probably effects many of the placers
of NE. Ohio boxes (on of them being myself). If ANYONE
in Cleveland (Doodle, Deedle?) can possibly do a check
on NE. Ohio boxes (Eddie's Grill, Pymatuning Walleye,
heck even Burton's Log Cabin and the other Geauga
County boxes) e-mail myself off list.
****************************************************
Everyone,

I wanted to warn you all in Ohio about something that
ended up in my inbox
tonight.

Back story:

I was on vacation in Ohio last week, and planted two
boxes near our vacation house on Lake Erie. The clues
were up for a couple of days when I realized
(the hard way) that there's some kind of poisonous
plant near the tree where I planted Box #1. It doesn't
look like poison ivy, but let's just say that the
left side of my face got all red and swollen, but it
didn't itch. When I was trying to figure out where I
picked it up, I narrowed it down to that. On the
day we left, I removed the box, and updated the clue
on the letterboxing.org page, stating that it had been
removed, and why. Box #2 remains in its original
location. I just felt that it wouldn't be right to
send people to this location, after I got sick.

I got an email tonight from someone saying that he was
upset that he couldn't find the Miniature Golf box. I
responded by telling him the story, but said
that I was glad that he had located my Eddie's Grill
box.

He wrote back saying that he was very angry with me,
because he wasted a lot of time searching for the Mini
Golf box. He told me that he was going to
remove my Eddie's Grill box (which he has NO right to
do, but as I live in Connecticut, I have no way of
preventing him). He went on to say that he would
be relocating 15 boxes in eastern Ohio and attempting
to return the stamps to their owners, and that I've
ruined the hobby of letterboxing by removing the box
near the plants that would make people sick.

The only thing I can think of at this point is to
inform you of this, in case anyone here who has boxes
in Ohio wishes to write to this guy and tell him to
STAY AWAY from their boxes. I can forward his email
address. I'm sorry this happened. I really don't know
what else to do about this, but I did email
him and ask him not to remove my Eddie's Grill box, or
anyone else's. I also told him that I stand by my
decision to remove the Mini Golf box, rather than to
leave it in a location that may cause other people to
have an allergic reaction, particularly because I
updated the website on the same day.

Chrissy

From: Doodle & Deedle Bug (doodle_n_deedle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-08-27 13:17:48 UTC-07:00
Letterboxers, I think I may need your help. I've encountered a letterboxer who is taking this game too seriously.

Since the boxes threatened in the below message are my area's boxes, and I don't believe that a lot of the placers belong to the Big List to even know what's being threatened, I took it upon myself to ask Chrissy for the writer's contact info. I assumed it was maybe a newbie who hadn't learned to deal with the disappointment of not always finding what you set out to seek. I searched for the person's trail name, and he's a member of the Big List who's been boxing for a few years, which completely shocked me. Why would someone who letterboxes, and reads the Big List, make threats to destroy boxes placed by people who had nothing to do with his experience? I wrote to him, and this is what his response was. I wanted to quote it directly, because I didn't think I'd get the "mwah-ha-ha-ha" mad scientist tone I'm inferring from it if I paraphrased:

"This Chrissy was the first I've ever encountered who placed a box, posted the clue, and removed the box and left the clue on the list. That is not funny. Nor is what I am going
to do about it!"


Now, this guy lives a few hours away from any boxes in Ohio, so would he REALLY drive all that way just for a sabatoge mission - but he almost sounds crazy enough to do it. (For the record, Chrissy DID post on letterboxing.org that the box had been pulled.) Should Ohio be taking his threats seriously? Or do you think he's simply resorting to terrorist tactics to ruin letterboxing in my state, where we'll all pull our boxes and clues so that he won't potentially harm them? Any ideas on how to handle this? Should I call him out on the Big List, so that anyone who does have their box sabatoged knows who's responsible? The Big List doesn't seem too concerned with the threats in the initial post. Should I just move to Southern Ohio, which he doesn't seem interested in? (But I don't want to root for the Reds...no offense to any Reds fans.)

Thank you for your wise counsel.

Doodle, rather perturbed.
