Letterboxing last night

From: Pale Puppy Posse (advantagek9@voyager.net) | Date: 2005-04-26 08:45:32 UTC-04:00
Naughty dog and I decided it was much too nice to go home and work in the
yard. So we packed a picnic supper and the dogs backpacks and set off for
Ft. Custer in Augusta.
The first set of boxes that we coveted were the Breakfast Elves. Since we
had all three dogs with us I decided it would not be prudent for both of
us to tromps into the woods after Snap! dragging or being dragged by the
dogs. So let's take turns I thought. Being the nice girl that I am, I let
Naughty dog go after Snap! Unfortunately the last people there, in early
April, did not rehide the box but merely laid it on the ground beside its
hiding spot. The box was wet inside but the logbook and stamp are fine.
This box did not have an outer bag to help protect it, FYI Azobox. As we
are sitting there reading the logbook the Posse alerts us to someone trying
to sneak up on us. I tell Naughty dog to stash the box and head over to the
dogs to keep them from tangling the biker up as he descends down the path
with his Brittany off leash! Geesh, Naughty dog is about as covert as a
child trying to hide the fact that he has been eating chocolate!
Delighted in our find we ambled off to find Crackle. When we came upon the
location, forgot to tell you Naughty dog is reading the clues and I am
reading the compass, he sends me off in a different direction then the
clues state. He had this "funny" idea that he would send me off on a wild
goose chase as he ran over to the other side of the trail and snatched the
box. Well, he didn't think very far ahead because he was holding the dogs
and trying to take them to the hiding spot. When I head back to the trail
frustrated for not finding the box I see him and the dogs tangled in bushes
and prickers and they all can't move. He confessed his sin and I could have
beaten him to death if he didn't look so dang funny hog tied there in the
woods. So I untangle them and set off in the right direction. Still I can't
find the box. So I go back and let Naughty dog try. As I read the clues I
discover that he left out an important piece of information!! He is really
dead now!! He finds Crackle in his hiding place which seems to have eluded
many a boxer I am soon to discover.
Off to find Pop! Down the trail we head when we come to a crossroad. As we
discuss which way to go a couple is walking down the path. I head off to
one side of the trail to keep them contained while Naughty dog is hollering
Brenda this way! I shhh! him and tell him we aren't supposed to show them
where we are going!!! He knows better at this point to locate Pop! himself
so he waits for the rest of us to arrive. I hear an awful sound and we must
have disturbed a crane because he sounded mad. But we located Pop! and I
could do my happy dance without anyone seeing..... Both Crackle and Pop
were okay but only Pop had an outer bag to protect it. Finish the trail
and stop at a picnic table to have dinner and bevie of choice :-)
Still light out... can't we try for Kazoo Dog's box, we still have time?
Naughty dog agrees as he knows he needs some brownie points. We get to the
location and spend about 10 minutes arguing about where we should start! I
give up and let him lead. Dang if he wasn't right, I hate when that happens.
We find box #1 and head off for #2. Now it is about 9 pm and it is getting
dark. No problem we can do this, keeping in the back of my mind that the
park closes at 10 pm. We struggled but could not find #2. I am sure because
it was too dark to see. So we abandon the search and head off in what
Naughty dog thinks is the way back. After walking and checking compass for
about a half an hour and me begging to turn around and just back track to
the car, we manage to find a sign that says main trail head and we make it
back with a few minutes to spare.... Wow! what a time we had... but would
do it again in a heartbeat. Made a great ending to the day!
Oh and in box #1 the last people to stamp in were from Colorado.. which I
thought was great, some interstate action!
