From: duncan53211 (duncan53211@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-08-30 23:02:15 UTC
Here are the parts of the "rules" pertaining to the Grail Exchange
hiding question raised recently:

6) Boxes must be planted in traditional letterbox style they
cannot be turned into Postal Letterboxes and should involve at least a
modest hike (at least a 1/4 mile).

7) Clues can be any style, but to ensure that nobody's efforts just
lay in hiding, never again to be found, they should not be total
mystery boxes -- clues should give at minimum a city and site name.
Beyond that, creativity and conundrum are encouraged.

8) Dragon will create a website to encompass the entire project,
which will include the clues to boxes. Once you have the clues ready,
send them to him via email and he will post them.

9) For this project, boxes must be placed in the Great Lakes States,
but any member of the group may participate.

In particular, note in #8 that once you have the clues ready, you
should send them to ME. Do not post them on the LBNA site. One reason
for this is rule #11:

11) For this series, we'll try to keep everything a secret from each
other as possible. Do not tell what you are making, what you are
hiding, and where you have hidden it. This would also mean that the
exchanging of "Holy Grail" clues is forbidden! (This last sentence is
refering to the coded clues to the Holy Grail box.)

Dragon
