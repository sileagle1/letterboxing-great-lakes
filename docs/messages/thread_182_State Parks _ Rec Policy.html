Update concerning letterboxing and the the parks & rec policy.
Quoting Sandrich from the Migo forum,
[quote]
The DNR is aware of Letterboxing, and they have asked me about the
sport as well. Since I haven't done any letterboxing (yet), I felt
that it really was best to keep my conversations with them to be
'caching-centric, and not drift off that point too much. I did refer
them to the letterboxing website for any further questions... My
concern here is that the DNR may wish to also impose a use fee for
Letterboxing as well as caching, and I'm sure we all would like to
avoid that as well!
So, for the time being, I'm going to continue to negotiate with the
DNR as regards to 'caching, and hope that down the road, when we have
the type of poilcy that our community wants, then perhaps someone can
negotiate to have Letterboxing included with caching.
[/quote]

I feel that the best approach to fitting letterboxing into this Parks
& Rec policy is to be up front and proactive about it. I predict that
if a fee were imposed on geocaching and none on letterboxing, then
geocachers would quickly become letterboxers and continue to hide
boxes on Parks & Rec land unmonitored, and the DNR would be right back
to the original problem.

So please log on to Migo and read the Parks & Rec thread in the
forums, and join the conversation. If we can do this policy right the
first time, in a way that encourages people to continue to hide and
seek boxes and addresses the concerns of the DNR about use trails, we
will save ourselves the annoyance of politicking in the future.
