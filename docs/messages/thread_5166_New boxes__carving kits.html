Welcome to the insanity! We do enjoy a heads up about new boxes. You don't have to post clues, just an alert that hey, there's a new box in such and such an area....and maybe a quick note about the placement if it was a particularly exciting adventure.

From: Carol Osada (cjosada@hotmail.com) | Date: 2008-07-07 23:20:50 UTC-06:00
Not all Michael's stores carry Speedball carving rubber; just larger stores. I've never found carving rubber at JoAnn Fabrics. But I will recommend another source for the best carving rubber I've ever used...PZ Kut! It's only available on line and there is a shipping cost. But it is the best for carving fine details. Search on the words "PZ Kut." I buy Grade B White. Grade B Orange is the same product and a bit cheaper, but its' hard on the eyes.

Re: New boxes?/carving kits

From: be_a_badger (schuppner@uwalumni.com) | Date: 2008-07-08 21:25:20 UTC
Artist and Display on Burleigh carries carving supplies. Check one of
the last aisles on the left as you enter the store, just before - or
possibly sharing an aisle with - sketch pads. Carving supplies will be
on your left in that aisle near the linoleum carving materials.

They're a good resource for purchasing markers individually. They tend
to have a better, more consistent selection than other stores in the
area.

They're not always the most economical, but can be convenient if you
need a quick fix.
