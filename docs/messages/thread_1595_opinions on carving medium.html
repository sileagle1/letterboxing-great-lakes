Sounds like a neat series!

MasterCarve is thick but a little softer than some other media. I
think SpringChick just did a double-sided stamp recently on PZcut.

Re: opinions on carving medium

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-07-13 20:54:42 UTC
I did a couple of double-sided stamps for the Great Lakes Gathering
using MasterCarve. You can get it in two different thicknesses --
approx. 1/2" and approx. 3/4". I used the 1/2" thickness because I had
relatively small containers and the thicker stuff just seemed too bulky.

One thing I found when doing a double-sided stamp is to do the image
with the least detail first. The reason is that while you are carving
the second side, the image on the first side gets moved around on your
cutting mat quite a bit and find detail areas can get damaged or work
loose -- this is especially true with the MasterCarve as it is kind of
supple and not real firm.

Another tip for doing double-sided when they will be stamped over each
other -- like if it is two parts of the same image that should be done
in different colors, or two halves of a large image -- basically
anything that needs to be lined up with each other, is to create a
border around at least one of the images to help when lining up the
second stamp.

SpringChick

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-07-13 17:29:40 UTC-04:00
Actually the one I think you are referring to (one of those from the gathering) was done on a Staedler Mars Grand size eraser. This is my all-time favorite carving medium and they were readily available from several online resources when I first began carving. It lies somewhere between MasterCarve and PZKut as far as firmness goes, but is thick like MasterCarve. Unfortunately I have been told by countless art suppliers and craft stores, etc. that this size Staedler eraser is no longer being distributed in the U.S. The smaller size is still available at most art stores and I even see them at Office Max and Staples on a regular basis, but I find them too small for most of my carving. I've also searched online for a German source (that is where the company is located), but have not had any success in that regard either. A couple of months ago, my local scrapbooking store cleaned out their basement and there was one lonely eraser sitting on the shelf when I stopped in there one day. I got all excited and thought perhaps they were on the market again and had the owner of the store check with all her suppliers -- but same deal -- no distribution source in the U.S. If anyone is successful in finding a U.S. or foreign source that accepts U.S. currency or PayPal, please let me know. I would buy a case of these thing if I could find them.

SpringChick

From: LindaFay (coachlindafay@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-07-14 16:31:33 UTC
For small (1" x 1") two-sided carvings, I've very fond of Paperchase
erasers (I got a bunch at the local Borders) - they are translucent and
in fun colors. The material is a litter stiffer than mastercarve, but
not as stiff as the "pink stuff" and smoother than erasers. I really
like colors and began carving using neon "office depot" beveled
erasers - pack of 6 and each a different color. OD usually only has
them during "school supply" time - so I picked some up this week.
Since I do a lot of boxing with the younger generation, the different
colors are easier to say "how's the green stamp?". Oh yea, teams of
youngun's really can't get into a team stamp - they what their own.
And it is really hard to be stealth with 6 wiggly 10 year olds.
