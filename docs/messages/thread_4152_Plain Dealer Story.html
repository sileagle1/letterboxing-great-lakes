My personal opinion, being a Clevelander:

I kind of like letterboxing still remaining "under the
radar" in Cuyahoga County. Especially since so many
of our area's boxes are hidden in the Cleveland
Metroparks. There currently is no letterboxing policy
in the Cleveland Metroparks, even though there is a
geocaching policy. I'm afraid that if letterboxing is
brought to the forefront as geocaching is, the
Metroparks will then instill a "one size fits all"
policy, making the letterboxers fill out the same
paperwork as the geocachers, rather than create
something specific for the letterboxes. Even though
the hobbies are similar, they're still different.
I've looked over the Metropark's geocaching form
before, and in its current state I don't think it
would work well for letterboxing. I'm of the mindset
with letterboxing and the Cleveland Metroparks of
"don't ask, don't tell." It's worked for us for the
past however many years. I'm afraid that an article
about letterboxing in the Cleveland area's major
newspaper would jeopardize so many of our area's
already existing plants.

I also feel that if Mr. Sangiacomo is serious about
doing an article on letterboxing, he'll take the
initiative to contact some of the area's placers.
I've been contacted twice before by reporters from the
area's Sun News paper who took the initiative to seek
out active letterboxers rather than just post a "I
want to do an article on this, please contact me"
message.

Of course, it's up to each individual to decide if
they want to go to the media or stay hidden about
letterboxing. If someone does decide to talk to the
media, please take into account the concerns that have
been posted on this board numerous times before about
previous letterboxing articles, and try to stress to
the reporter that letterboxing is about more than
running through the woods looking for plastic
containers.

Doodle Bug.
