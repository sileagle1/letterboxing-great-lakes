From: Becky (imdurangogirl@aol.com) | Date: 2006-09-01 23:04:05 UTC
I actually had that happen on one occasion. I had recarved a stamp for
a box since I wasn't happy with the first one. Drove out to the hiding
spot. It was a week or so after some bad storms and there were trees
down. With the downed trees it changed the landscape enough that I
wasn't sure I was in the right location. Then much to my
dissapointment I realized that the little den under some uplifted roots
that the box had been hidded in was gone, because the tree was one that
had come down. We searched for the box and best we could determine, it
ended up buried under the tree. So lesson learned, no more hiding in
root dens! LOL

From: ainawgsd (gmc81@randomkitty.net) | Date: 2006-09-02 03:17:34 UTC
Oh yes. The first time I went out to check on my Lance's Fish Tank
series I forgot where I had hidden box #3. I did happen accross a
geocache while thoroughly searching the log where I *thought* I had
hidden it. When I went back and looked at the clues it turns out I
wasn't even close (we're talking at least a five minute hike from the
log to the actual location!) and I had only thought about hiding the
box in that log when I was looking for hiding spots. Turns out it's a
good thing I didn't hide it there, since the last time I went to check
on those boxes that log had been completely burned out!

I also feel the need to follow my own clues at least once a year
(especially for longer hikes) just to make sure that the landscape
hasn't changed dramatically enough to render the clues useless. For
most of my series/longer hikes, I do use short cuts to go between
boxes if I'm just doing a quick check so it's important to travel the
actual route that other boxers will be taking once in a while.

Kimono Girl
