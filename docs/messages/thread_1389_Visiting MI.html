One of the few near Frankenmuth is Cottontail in the Cottonwood near


Bridgeport (off I-75). Also if you have time while you are traveling


between Dearborn and Muth try driving up M-15. You will be pretty


close to Howlin at the Moon, Man on the Moon, and also any in the


Davison Area.






As you can tell there are more boxes in the metro area then up near


Muth.

From: pkleingers (pam@kleingers.net) | Date: 2005-06-07 17:39:58 UTC-04:00
OH gosh, we have a ton of boxes, obviously!



Both Franzsolo and Scoutdogs are excellent carvers.so any of theirs would be
worth the effort. I think they both have kept up the status pretty well,
tho this past winter has been somewhat challenging weather wise. Miami
White Water Park has quite a few very nice stamps. Winton Woods has some
good ones also, but are more likely to be missing. I'll have to check and
see what other ones are must finds.



I would highly recommend you make the effort to head east to Jackson or
Chillicothe sometime. It's like a mini-Connecticut as far as the number of
boxes available. One of my favorites in that area is Leo PEtroglyphs. They
are really cool.

aka Pam in Cinci

Re: Visiting Cincinatti

Our youngest and I recommend the N. KY Archealogy Box (actually a micro) for it's outstanding location in Kenton County, KY. It has a spectacular view of Cincinnati and a nice little museum. And don't forget to pack your picnic lunch.

A great perk of letterboxing is finding the neat places that you might have bypassed.
