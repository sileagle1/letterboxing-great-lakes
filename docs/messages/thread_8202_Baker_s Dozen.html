A heartfelt Thank You to Atomic Beans for creating a wonderful GLG 2015...They did Illinois proud!  The organization was spot on!  Thank you for reminding us of the past gatherings..but the bigger evidence of just how much has happened in those 12 years came from watching the second generation of letterboxers running around carrying on the letterboxing tradition.  We remember meeting many of those very letterboxers when they were just infants!  So fun to see them forging friendships with children of other letterboxers, enjoying the wonders of the outdoors, clues, boxes and each other!  


It is always a delight to see "old" boxers and meet new ones!  


Thanks to everyone for making this still one of the best "hobbies" ever!

From: Atom118 (Aatom118@gmail.com) | Date: 2015-06-15 10:15:47 UTC-05:00
We would like to thank everyone for making this weekend such a WONDERFUL
EVENT for us. Hopefully you all had fun, aren't so sore, and didn't
shake your fists at our clues too many times. The weather over the
weekend (minus the evening showers) ended up being about as good as we
could have hoped for in early June.

It was great to put faces to names (and stamps) for those of you who we
only just met, and as always, a pleasure to see everyone else again.
From our end we wanted give one more shout-out to all the wonderful
carvers who donated stamps, the Wormy Wanderers for helping us plant
some of the boxes, HowDGirls and TJMich for a SUPER AWESOME pre-event
(hopefully someone will post a picture of the stamp and board), Tiptoe
and Tonto for taking the reigns at the grill, WI Hiker and Martini
Man/Hartx6 and TJMich/ and the Christmas Elves for helping pick-up
boxes, and everyone who contributed their festive event boxes to make it
such a great day. (and
I was worried I wouldn't be able to find the letterbox in the letterbox!).

I can only image how some of you might be feeling today. We only ended
up doing about half of the hikes over the weekend (to pick-up and
set-out boxes), but I'm BEAT today. Hats off to all of you who probably
did a bit more.

I'm happy to say, that after Saturday's collection, we came out just
about even on our costs for the event without having to dip into the
reserve pool that the MN contingent paid forward. We'll probably still
be slightly ahead after we mail back a few of the boxes. If anyone is
considering hosting next year (hint, hint), I can say it shouldn't be a
financial hardship, and we'll pass along the leftovers from the previous
two years to get you started. I'll also mention that we followed MN's
lead from last year and made the clues available early for those who
made their donations in advance. Not only did that make it easier for
us to budget the event, but many people appreciated having all day
Thursday, Friday, and Saturday to do the event boxes after traveling
long distances to attend the event, especially since these boxes were
only available this weekend. As a result, I think several people were
able to collect many/all of the event boxes, the special series of 13
extra boxes created for those who donated early, and many others in the
nearby area over the long weekend.

Anyway, we are beat. We will be tiding up some posting of boxes and
pictures over the next several days. BTW, if anyone took a picture of
the Castle Rock sign outside the main entrance, please email me a copy
or post it (we kept forgetting).

See you at GLG 2016 (if not sooner)!

Atomic Beans
