REPORT: KANE COUNTY, IL

From: Mosey (PonyExpressMail@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-05-23 17:31:10 UTC-05:00
1. The Windmill -- placed in the Batavia area by "Susan and the girls."
What can I say? A fun time was not had by all on this search. :-) But
seeing the Windmill was impressive.

It was more than a bit of a physical challenge. This isn't a box for the
physically un-adept or the physically klutzy. Didn't have to use it, but
was sort of glad I had the first aid kit in my backpack "just in case". :-)
And I'm not too sure if anyone too terribly overweight would be able to take
this trek either. But a searcher finds out pretty early into the trek just
what they're in for. Ancient had to turn back. No way would Ancient be
able to "duck thru the hole in the concrete" and then "walk the creekbed."
I didn't "duck thru the hole" either tho. I decided to do a commando
up-an'-over the concrete wall rather than a "duck thru". Of course, then I
found out that the ground on the side I was coming *from* was a little
higher than the ground on the side I was going *to*. Not a pleasant
experience when that happens -- oops! :-)

But what I needed in my backpack more than anything was blood! Good
old-fashioned blood. I had thought I'd sprayed down with Deep Woods Off
fairly good this morning, but the mosquitoes thought otherwise. I think I
lost half my blood on this trek. I more than needed a transfusion or two by
the time I got back to the truck.

When folks go to buy land in a rural area and ask the real estate agent what
the land is like before they drive out to look at it, the answer often is
"Oh, you know. A little high land. A little low land. Just like all the land
'round 'ere." Well, this little piece of woods is part of Kane County's "a
little low land." Lot of muck and swampy type areas. And tons of skeeters
calling it home. The clues were a little confusing, but the box was there.

So I rejoined Ancient back close to the parking lot to where our "creepy
green guy" was sitting in his car. When we first showed up, there was a guy
in a dark green sedan sitting in the parking lot. We parked nearby (about 4
spaces away from him) and when we got out of the truck, he started his car,
hit the accelerator, and disappeared out of the parking lot and down Rte 31.
When we got back to the parking lot after I'd found the box, he was back
again, parked in the same spot that he'd been in originally. Then he
started his sedan and disappeared again. So we pulled out of the parking
lot about 10 minutes later and drove into the parking lot across the road to
get a look at the Fox River at that point, and there he was! Again, he
started his car, hit the accelerator, and pulled out right away. He must've
thought we were stalking him! Poor guy. We were going to invite him to
lunch with us at Sweet Tomatoes, but he flew out of the parking lot too
fast.

2. Oak Hill Cemetery -- also placed by "Susan and the girls" a little
further north in the Geneva area.

This was a whole lot less physically challenging, but a lot more mentally
frustrating for us. Tho it probably shouldn't have been. Everything was
great right up until the last clue. We just couldn't find the box. We did,
however, find a shovel. Finding a shovel in a woods that's right next door
to a cemetery is a little. bit. creepy. So we left to come back and try
another day. We were just brain-dead with the last clue and couldn't find
the box.

3. WERDHERKARWE -- Secrets at the Waterfall

Two boxes here, "Da Fox" and "Da Bandit." Both really nicely carved
stamps -- we loved 'em!!! Placed by Kim of Nuts4Mice. Longish but very
nice hike. The waterfall was sooooo cute. Frankly, I'm predicting that
these boxes aren't going to last long, altho I sure hope they do. To us,
the carvings were well worth the hike. But on the first box, I've got a
feeling a social trail is going to develop and lead folks right to the box,
and the second box is sooo open to sight from people biking at you from
around corners in either direction. We had to be sooo careful not to get
caught. But if anyone in the area hasn't found these boxes (they've only
been out there since May 8th), these hand carved stamps are worth finding,
in our humble opinions.
