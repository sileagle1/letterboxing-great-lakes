Thanks for keeping us updated on this issue and posting the results
of the meeting(s) between the geocache community and the Parks Dept.

I am also disappointed to learn of this new policy and agree with
those who feel it is short-sighted.

>> Seems like they are actually standing to lost money this way. For
one, they need somebody to deal with the permits and fees and
paperwork, and to routinely check out the boxes in the park, whereas
now, they just go about peacefully co-existing. Seocndly, if I
planted a box (for free) and myself plus only 5 other letterboxers
paid the daily usage of $6 for that, they would net $36 without
having to hire anyone to do anything. By instating the charge, I
pull all of my boxes and they get nothing.<<

With such a limited budget, I think the current staff will just be
expected to take on the extra burden in regards to paperwork and
implimenting the new policies. I'm not sure the Parks Dept will be
hiring anyone extra to check out the boxes.

>> On a more positive note.. I got another email from my letterboxer
friend who works at the State Park here and apparently although
there is a policy, there is room for individual decision-making on a
per park basis, and if the ranger decices to, he can look the other
way. Perhaps that is why I have not received the official "come get
your boxes or be prepared to pay" notice.<<

We might get lucky and have the Rangers in the more popular parks
that have a high traffic volume, be willing to leave us alone, as
they are more likely to be able to meet the new self-sufficency
policy.

What do you think, is it a good idea for all of the Letterboxing
community to simply leave our boxes in place until we are asked to
move them, or is the risk of confiscation with possible destruction
too great?
