Re: [LbGLK] Re: Out of Commision Boxes

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2008-11-12 06:49:03 UTC-05:00
As far as I am aware, neither AQ or LbNA will change the status of a box based on reports by finders, except in extreme circumstances. It is not because they want people to continue to look for something that is not there, rather because it would be impossible to know which finders are reporting a truly missing box and which finders just were not able to find the box, even though everything in the clue seemed to match and they were convinced it was the right place. I cannot tell you the missing box reports I receive that are not actually missing boxes, and most of these people were *positive* they were in the right place. It is frustrating enough to me that they then enter an "attempted" status on the box because this often causes subsequent seekers to avoid the box thinking it may be missing, but for a finder to have the ability to actually effect a status change would just be wrong.

While I don't think anyone intentionally sends placers on a wild goose chase for boxes that no longer exist, it is a part of the game. Letterboxers come and go and leave their boxes behind which then eventually go missing, long after they've stopped keeping track of them. Even active letterboxers often have a hard time always having up-to-date status on their boxes due to the fact that they have lives outside of letterboxing that usually takes priority.

When you set out to look for a box, there is always going to be the chance that you won't find it, either because of your own error or because it is truly not there -- it could have been missing for 2 years or 2 days, but it is equally as disappointing to you when you get to the empty hidey-hole. You should always try to contact the placer so they are aware and can check it out. Many people choose to look only for boxes that have been very recently found, as indicated by status updates on LbNA or AQ -- especially if they are doing the hike with young children, as this is usually a pretty good indicator that the box is still there. But as with everything in life, there is not always going to be a reward at the end of the trail. This is where it is definitely a plus if you are enjoying the entire process -- the unraveling of clues, the hike, the time spent outside with friends and family, because even if you don't find the letterbox, you still had a wonderful time and had the opportunity to use the experience as a lesson in disappointment.

SpringChick

From: (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2008-11-13 10:49:05 UTC-06:00
Many letterboxers (including myself) really don't want to see an ability for searchers to comment on boxes. However in the situation you described, here's what I suggest:

If the box is listed on LBNA, send an email to the web masters, explaining that the store is no longer in business and that you have tried contacting the placer so they can update or remove the clues, but you haven't received a response. Ask the web masters to therefore remove the clues from the active list. They may then try to contact the planter themselves and if they get no response, they will probably remove/retire/archive the clue.

Wisconsin Hiker
