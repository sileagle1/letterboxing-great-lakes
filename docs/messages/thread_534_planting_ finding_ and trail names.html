Hey! Another Boxing Girl Scout! What council are you...where's your camp?
My GS friend Pippin and I are working on establishing a bunch of private
boxes at Whispering Oaks (Brookfield, IL) camp, River Trails, near Sheridan,
IL. I've got about 10 boxes out there so far. I'm working on clue books to
place at the various campsites. Just this night I carved a three part
homage to "Three Chartreuse Buzzards" which came out pretty cute (for
buzzards!...hey, now there's a trail name!)

Speaking of those boxes...kind of a cute story. Last month Pippin velcroed
a centrifuge box inside a large railroad bell at camp (used to be used as an
emergency "alarm" to call the caretaker in my pre-cellphone days as a girl).
Lo and behold, not an hour later a group of little Brownies decides to play
some ring-around-the-rosy style game around same bell (which stands about 5
feet off the ground on a concrete pillar-thingie). Papillon and I are
sitting not an arm's length away, when we notice the circle stopped moving,
and one by one the little girls (I guess we weren't calculating their range
of vision when I helped her place it) start pointing up into the bell.
Drat! "Mommy, Mommy!" one girl called "what's that?" The tired, worn out
mom (it was a mother-daughter weekend, and said mother had just about had
enough hiking to last the rest of her life) just blew the kid off..."oh,
that's just there to keep people from ringing the bell." Phew!!!

Sorry guys...got off on a tangent there...

As a military brat, I belonged to several councils
over the years including Commonweath Virginia, SE
Louisiana, and most recently Tribal Trails (central
IN) council as an adult. I have not kept my membership
up because of my own personal war with Tribal Trails,
WHO SOLD OUR CAMP. Thankfully, our camp, Kokiwanee,
was bought by a land trust, who has made into a
wonderful nature preserve. The council didn't give a
rip about what current and former scouts wanted, as
the overwhelming majority wanted the camp to stay
open. Politics. Ug. I've been considering becoming a
leader in the Singing Sands council, where I live
now...when and if my job becomes less demanding.

Anyway, I dedicated a series to Kokiwanee and named
each stamp after a former unit. I also made a
hitchhiker called "the Princess Pat" after my all-time
favorite camp song. What's everyone's favorite camp
song?
