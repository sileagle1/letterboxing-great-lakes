Goofus and Gallant

From: bindlestiff_boxer (bindlestiff_boxer@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-11-03 12:43:51 UTC
Anyone remember the kids magazine called Highlights? Remember the two
characters called Goofus and Gallant?

My recent email brought those fellows to mind, and here's why:

email #1 -- Gallant says: We did the Easter series this spring. My
daughter (7) still talks about "Bad Bill Bunny" when we talk about
LBs. For Christmas, the kids will be receiving LB supplies as we have
several ideas for locations and stories, using your Easter series as
an example. We like those kinds.

I can hardly express how much getting this email meant to me and how
much I value their comments.

email #2 re: some of my clues are at our yahoogroup and not on the
LbNA site --- Goofus says: All I want is clues to letterboxes. Why
do I have to "join" anything??? When I clicked on join, filled out
all the info, and got to the user agreement, I found that yahoo could
start sending me unwanted info. And I don't need another email
address. I just want clues. I'm not even sure if I was in the right
spot for "joining your group". If you have your own site that only
"members" can access clues, then why do you list your letterboxes on
Letterboxing North America? For those that are not computer-savy or
web-site-savy, this is very confusing and even rude. Looks like we
won't be hunting up any of your letterboxes.

Uhmm... where to begin on how I felt when I read this? No, let's not
even go there. However, Bindle Bum is always "accusing" me of being
too optimistic and always finding something good to say no matter what
the situation ... but the only 'positive' I can say about this email
is that I think it's a GOOD thing that someone like this won't be
looking for my boxes.

Oh, and I guess the other good thing is that I truly believe there are
far more "Gallant" people in the world than there are "Goofus" types.

BTW -- anyone want to guess the theme of my next series of boxes, or
who the two main character stamps are going to be? (grin)

Enjoy!
---PJ
aka Bindle Babe




Re: Goofus and Gallant

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-11-03 13:15:47 UTC
I always loved Goofus and Gallant. Highlights was a cool magazine --
the hidden picture puzzles were my favs. I've been trying to think
of a way to incoroporate that into a letterbox for some time...
Goofus and Gallant will make a great box duo.

It's always nice to get heartwarming e-mails from finders where you
feel like you've really given some enjoyment to somebody or an entire
family. It's neat that this series was so inspiring to these folks.

I'm not sure I would call the 2nd guy a Goofus though. I think that
being a tech-savvy kind of person it is very easy for us to
assimilate that onto everyone else. But the truth is that over 40%
of the population are not regular computer users and many more are
only familiar with e-mail -- their particular e-mail program (most
often AOL). With all of the negative publicity about internet fraud,
identity theft and spyware/malware, it can be very intimidating for a
non-computer person to have to "join" anything. Like he said, he
just wants clues, and he probably is not alone in that desire.

While it is everybody's right to decide how they will make their
clues available and I personally think the format you've chosen is a
great tool for doing so as it allows you to easily organize
everything (very easy to find stuff at your Yahoo! site),
the "joining" requirement does turn some people away. Doesn't make
him unworthy of your boxes or a Goofus though. It's all about
accessibility.

Just my 2 cents...

SpringChick

From: Mosey (PonyExpressMail@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-11-03 07:16:46 UTC-06:00
Oh, I looooooooooooooooved Goofus and Gallant as a child! Back in the
1950s, my parents sometimes sold it door-to-door to help make ends meet.
Thus nobody in our little neighborhood went without Goofus and Gallant.

I remember those days fondly. Those were the days of a tiny two-bedroom
bungalow with a hide-a-bed in the living room for our parents. Anytime the
doorbell rang after 8:00pm, my parents had to run around putting the bed
away and getting the couch back in order again before they could open the
front door. The dining room china cabinet had drawers in the lower section.
Nobody knew there was no silverware stored there. It held our parents'
clothes.

Would I want to ever ever go back to those days? Nooooo.....

But I remember them fondly. Funny how the passing years can romanticize the
old days.

~~ Mosey ~~
