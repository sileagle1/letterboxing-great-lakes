Re: [LbGLK] TOTW: Inspiration for stamps?

From: Gabrielle Coil (gmc81@randomkitty.net) | Date: 2004-09-27 15:08:10 UTC-07:00
My inspirations are all over the place. Many of my stamps are inspired by Japanese culture (anime, fabric, art) since one of my other major hobbies is Anime and that has sparked an interest in other Japanese things (like kimonos and fabric and traditions and such).

And a lot of them are inspired by my pets. My personal stamp, for example, was adapted from a picture of my dog in her Halloween costume. And my latest series started when we got the angelfish for our aquarium and one of my first thoughts about the one I picked out was "that would make an awsome stamp" (it's not *why* I picked that one though...I just thought he was the prettiest). From there, I decided that most of the other fish in the tank would also be great subjects for stamps (although some were harder to photograph than others). And the series I'll be working on next is my dog's name spelled out in Hiragana (Japanese characters).

And then there are some that just sort of come out of the blue. Like my "Mudflap Moose" hitchiker stamp...I was on a detour through Canada this summer and several of the semis we passed had little cartoon moose with wings on their mudflaps. When I got home, an internet search proved fruitless so I designed my own winged moose for my stamp. And I'm toying with the idea of making a stamp either for or in honor of my brother using a design that he is fond of (my mother has already turned this design into a little flag/banner thingy for him).

So yeah, my inspirations are all over the place. I tend to do whatever I feel like at the time and then look for someplace to hide it. My letterboxes may or may not have anything to do with their location. The Hidden Villiage series is hidden at Homer Lake because there was enough difference in the terrain to match the different elements/locations that the villiages represent. The Kame Kasuri box and my fish series are along Lake Shelbyville because it's a lake and it seems proper to have fish (maybe not tropical fish) and turtles there. I'm not sure where the dog's name series is going yet...probably just someplace with plenty of good hiding spots.

Kimono Girl

My biggest source of "inspiration" for stamps has been a set of clip
art images I use at work (I'm in marketing/graphic design). I don't
draw well so I depend on other people's artwork to inspire my work.
Usually I work with the clip art to personalize it for the area I'm
planting in. I did draw my own personal signature stamp and it
didn't turn out too badly.

The most difficult part for me is to come up with interesting clues.
I think that sets the tone of the box and I hate to waste a good
location with mediocre clues, so therefore I don't plant nearly as
many boxes as I'd like to. *sigh*

I haven't tried converting a digital photo to a stamp yet, but I intend
to try this weekend. Has anyone used the stamp feature of Adobe
Photoshop who can offer me advice/opinions on it? I just noticed it this

Most of my stamps are designed after I find a cool site to place them.
The series I am currently working on reflects the unique features of
various city parks. The basic design was inspired by the city logo, but
I replace the central leaf with something relevant to the park: a dog
bone for the dog park, a wheelchair for the park with an accessible
playground, etc. I use clipart for most things but do some of my own
sketching, like for Claire's and my personal stamps.

My most recent stamp was actually inspired by a phrase on the website for
one of my more favorite forest preserves: "in search of the elusive
morel mushroom." I think I prefer stamps that either reflect an aspect
of the site or have a personal story that goes with them. A stamp I
found in NY claims to be NY's crappiest stamp and the placer explains
that it was one of his first attempts but he just felt the site really
needed a stamp- even just that much makes it mean more to me.

Re: TOTW: Inspiration for stamps?

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-10-05 15:20:07 UTC

I'm a bit late getting in on this one, but better late than never...

I generally like to theme my boxes on something historical or
interesting about the area where they are being placed. As such I
try to find images that relate to the theme, both in image and style
of the image. I am terrible at drawing so almost all of my images
are from clip-art. I tend to favor elements from nature -- flowers,
trees, leaves, suns, animals, etc. I haven't had much success with
converting a photo into a carvable graphic, although I have seen it
done very well by a couple of other individuals.

I have a huge library of clip-art, both in print and electronic
format. I have also amassed a rather large file of future "image
possibilites" while searching some of my favorite clip-art
libraries. Sometimes I will see an image that inspires a box because
it brings to mind a fitting location. A few times I have run across
images I liked and wanted to carve, however they didn't seem to fit
at any location I could think of, so I made them into hitch-hikers.

Alternatively, I will do up a series of related stamps and place them
together in a park that really has no connection to the theme of the
series. But this is usually only with a series, very rarely do I
place just a single stamp in an unrelated location.

SpringChick
