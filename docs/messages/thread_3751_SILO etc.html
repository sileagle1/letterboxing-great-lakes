Hey, I've been to a few gatherings and I still get dazed and confused...I think it's the combination of ink and rubber gives off some chemicals that cause that "letterboxers' high"...especially since we were in a confined spaced where they couldn't disseminate as readily as outdoors...(jk!).

Don't put down your stamps! No one starts out as an experienced carver. Besides, I thought your Great Wall in particular was pretty impressive, considering you used an ordinary eraser. Once you try a "regular" carving medium, you'll really see things take off. Another thing to keep in mind was that some of those stamps were carved as Event stamps. As such, the artist often tends to put a little more effort than for something placed in the wilds. Not to mention that some were created by long-time carvers, known to be some of the best in the country.
