From: be_a_badger (schuppner@uwalumni.com) | Date: 2009-10-24 15:47:55 UTC
If we don't have a fixed gathering date going forward, we'll need to be careful about the timing for hosting decisions so there's plenty of time to book a park, etc. The rotation should help, but with different booking policies from place to place and a floating date, fixing the timing for hosting decisions might be worth considering.

We don't want to get ahead of things, but it's something to think about. Let's wait and see how the gathering date will be determined and, if it floats, talk about the timing for hosting decisions. We're not overlooking the other parts of the 2010 process, but they seem pretty straightforward.

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2009-10-24 10:59:05 UTC-05:00
Excellent point! In some states/counties, reservations can only be made at
the start of the year (January 1), but in other states/counties reservations
can be made up to one year in advance. Therefore it would be good to get
things rolling regarding hosting a year in advance (for example in May/June
2010 it would be good to make a decision for 2011). If we wait until the
end of the year, some desirable locations may have already been rented by
other groups.
