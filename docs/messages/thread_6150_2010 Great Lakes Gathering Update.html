Thank you SpringChick for your diligence and oversight of the annual Great

ours.


I’m so excited to be a part of next years GLG and I’ve already let my
excitement get the better of me. As my enthusiasm grows over the next several
months, I will be diligent as to not over look any detail.


This gathering is a joint effort between Pitties, Shorty & myself. In
getting to know P & S over the past few years we’ve grown a good friendship and
before even offering to host we decided that each of us have different
talents to offer each complimenting the other, we should work well together in
hosting GLG 2010!


The one talent that none of the three of us have is the talent of creating
a web site. So, I’ve recruited Nitrocat to create a web site for this
gathering. Due to scheduling conflicts the site wont be up until the beginning
of next year. Until then, you can get information from AQ
And from the Great Lakes Yahoo Group where we will be adding
files/databases over the next few weeks.


THEME: Changing the World – One Box at a Time


About a year ago I sold off my coin collection (about 15,000 coins) from
all over the world. Coins are little works of art in your pocket. Anything
you can imagine has, at one time, been minted on a coin somewhere in the
world. Think people, leaders, landmarks, animals, trees, plants, ships,
planes, buildings, themes, abstract designs, etc etc etc.


In doing research for something to carve & contribute you can do an image
search for “World Coins ______” and fill the blank with the image you are
looking to carve. You can also go to your library in the reference section
and look through the Kruse Book of World Coins that has pictures of every
single coin minted from every country going back to 1900 (and they also have
a Pre-1900 book).


Other than an “object of interest” to carve, you can also think of
countries of your ancestry, a significant year for you or your family, a place
you would like to visit or have visited, etc. You can also ask me if I have
any ideas or if you are having trouble finding just the right coin for you.


If you decide to contribute a stamp image please contact Shorty on AQ or
through this message board to let him know what you are carving so that
there are no duplicates. If you are able to send the “whole package” or “box
ready to plant” (stamp, logbook & box) that would be wonderful and very
helpful. However, if you can only send a stamp or a stamp & logbook that would
work out well as well.


There will be no limitations on your creativity, you can carve whatever
size and image you want to be stamped or inked up with whatever color(s) you
want. Though, at gatherings of this size monotone seems the quickest &
easiest for large groups to stamp into. I do ask that you try to carve the
lettering on the coins. Letters do scare off some people but you owe it to
yourself to at least try, don’t worry if they’re not perfect and if you
totally mess them up just cut the words out totally, but please do try.


DATE: June 5, 2010


This date was chosen for two reasons #1 it follows the traditional date of
the gathering – the weekend after Memorial Day and #2 it works well for
Shorty, Pitties & myself.


PLACE: Jubilee State Park just north of Peoria, Illinois.


This is a very large state park that is somewhat centrally located for
those of us who live here in Illinois. They have shelters large enough to
accommodate a group our size. Also, it’s a park not many know about. Unlike
Starved Rock or some other well known parks I wanted to bring attention to a
lesser known park that has only hosted one letterbox (ever) that went
missing before anyone found it.


This park has granted permission to hide boxes and leave them out for 2-3
weeks . . . not permanently L However, I’m sure a few may slip our mind when
we are picking them all up. ;-)


Pitties, Shorty and I are all thrilled to be hosting next years GLG, you
can contact any of us if you have any questions or thoughts. We will be
looking into local hotels/camping etc, and will let you all know how things
progress over the next 8 months or so.

hx6
