From: Charles Zamzow (cerealcityboxers@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-11-17 11:51:42 UTC-08:00
Something to consider for the future. I have a DROBO on my PC (which stands for data robot) and Shadowprotect software. Three times a week Shadowprotect automatically (at 2:00 a.m. in my case) makes a complete restorable copy of my C: drive. Software to do a stand alone restore is also included. I actually had to do this once to recover my PC. When to do the backups is schedulable and is entirely up to you. Individual files are also recoverable or just accessible if you just want to look at an older version. Peace of mind.

You might want to take it to a computer store to see if they can
retrieve the data. We had a power surge a few years back that fried
our computer (and a bunch of other stuff). And we were panicked
because it was more like 7-8 months since we had last saved
everything. We took it to the computer repair place in town and they
were able to retrieve everything.

That did motivate us to be more proactive about saving our data. We
use a service called Foldershare, which is internet-based. It
basically stores all your computer data on another computer, and it
does so all through the day (at least when both computers are
online). Our home computer is linked up with my DH's work computer.
So he has the benefit that work stuff is automatically backed up.
There is more than one company that does this, and I think there are
some that store your files online.

Hope you get all that data back!!!
