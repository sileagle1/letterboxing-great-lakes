Re: Rockford Restaurants?

From: lesann847 (Acorn@fools-errand.org) | Date: 2006-06-22 11:36:06 UTC
OK, I know you asked for "not a chain" but Beef-a-Roo is a Rockford
institution. Bill likes the burgers; I'm a fan of the grilled turkey
sandwich. Lots of fresh salads. Each restaurant has a different
theme; they're on the web. Friendly people, and they even come around
with mints and ask how your meal was. Great place for a quick,
satisfying lunch. I usually go to the one at 6593 Lexus Drive, but
there are plenty throughout Rockford/Loves Park/Roscoe.

In the same neighborhood, but a lot more upscale, is Five Forks
Market. This is a bistro-style restaurant with lots of light, tasty
fare"mostly sandwiches, flatbread pizzas, salads and tapas. Sometimes
we ask them to pack a picnic and take it over to Rock Cut SP. They are
at 6565 Lexus Drive. Oh, and check out the market for a nice bottle
of wine. When Bill wants to treat me, he gets a jar of lavender honey
there.

For Sunday brunch, we always like Thunder Bay Grille at 7652 East
State which offers a buffet. Lots of variety for all appetites at a
reasonable price.

If you're looking for fancy/romantic, I love Cliffbreakers. If you go
to dinner early, you can snatch one of the tables overlooking the Rock
River. 700 West Riverside Boulevard. They also have a Comfort Suites
Hotel there, but it's not like the usual Comfort Suites. These rooms
are furnished with antiques. My sister and brother-in-law enjoyed
spending an anniversary there a few years ago.

Rockford doesn't seem to be a big ethnic restaurant town, but I can
warn you to stay away from any Happy Wok restaurant. Locals
Shutterfly or RaqsEnigma will probably have better information on
out-of-the way ethnic restaurants. If so, I hope they share here.

Enjoy!

Acorn

Hi WH,

Nunzio's (5800 N. 2nd St. in Loves Park) is an Italian restaurant known for
its pizza but we LOVE it for the Steak Sinatra. Our favorite Mexican places
are Dos Reales and Don Pablo's (both on East State Street). Thai Hut on
Sandy Hollow has great Thai food (although the area is a little rundown). I
especially like their chicken pad thai. All of these are casual-dress
types. Thai Hut doesn't have a liquor license, much to my husband's dismay
(he likes beer heheheh).

Bet you're planning on coming to this area for "On the Waterfront"????

From: Angi (ShutterflyIL@aol.com) | Date: 2006-06-23 04:24:12 UTC
Nick (Manic Mechanic) and I have an obsession with Giuseppis. It is at
950 Halsted Rd (that's 61103 zip if you need it) behind a Walgreens.
It's a little hole-in-the-wall Italian place, but the pizza there is
amazingly good. It is also VERY well priced. We go there and get a
basket of fries and a large pizza for around $10. They don't serve
alcohol but really, the food is so delicious, you won't want anything
but water. If you happen to go there, let us know...we might have to be
FORCED to meet you there!

My favorite is Italian, so I could rattle off a lot of places, but
nothing beats Giuseppis. :)
