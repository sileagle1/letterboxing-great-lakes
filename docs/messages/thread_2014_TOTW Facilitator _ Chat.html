Just some thoughts ...

Mark Pepe's newboxers site has a TOTW, but I think that sometimes it is difficult to come
up with new topics regularly. Is is a duplication of effort? Just asking. Maybe a TOTW
more focused on issues within our GL region as a guideline? Or maybe a TOTW in the
database, where the question could be posed and responses logged? Might be a quick
reference? Just a thought.

For example: What time of year do you find boxing the most enjoyable in a specific area?
Why? Or what types of critters are common a specific area? Special precautions first-time
visitors should take? I could see a database forming in this format. I could create a
database shell and see what everyone thinks. Do we have sufficient resources to
accomodate multiple databases?

I read LBGLK, newboxers, travellers, travelling event boxes and the big list. I am starting
to think the big list doesn't add to my knowledge base and maybe I will drop it. I also read
Atlas Quest regularly. I occasionally find time on Sunday to chat (hi bindlebabe) and I
found it difficult to watch several different conversations taking place simultaneously. It's
a newboxers chat, but I'm not sure it remained focused enough for the newest of boxers.
Maybe a more local flavor would enhance the chat experience and generate some new
TOTW's that could be posted either on the message boards or in a database. Or chat
could replace TOTW.

my 2 cents today,
speedsquare





Re: TOTW Facilitator & Chat

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-11-15 10:30:44 UTC
I agree that it can become difficult to come up with new topics
after you've been running TOTW for a while. That is the main reason
that it fizzled out here. I imagine if we began again, there would
be a lot of repeat since there have been so many new members since
then. You bring up a good point about overlap with the newboxers
group -- I don't know how many people here are regular readers at
the newboxers list, if that would be an issue or not.

The database idea is interesting and you are welcome to give it a
try. There is no doubt it would be a great way to organize topics,
but I've found at this group and any other I've ever been a member
of, that very few people actually look through the stuff in the
databases, files, links at a Yahoo! group. With a database you
would lose the benefit of "discussion" which is what really drives
TOTW more than the topic itself.

Another option is to have kind of a freeform TOTW -- we could set a
time, say any time on Sunday or Monday morning, etc. and anyone
could throw out a topic, preceeding their post with TOTW. Once
there is a topic posted, we work with that one for the week and
anyone else who had a topic to post waits until the next week.

I personally have not ever been a big fan of chat for the very
reason you mentioned -- I find it difficult to follow the variety of
threads that get going. It always feels to me like people are
stepping all over each other, not to mention it is only a small
percentage of the chat that is actually productive. My friend and I
have a joke about chat rooms, that sometimes the only conversation
is "hi" so-and-so, "bye" so-and-so and "I'm back." But I know
people who find it a very effective way to communicate on the
Internet and as it is an option with a Yahoo! group, we are
certainly able to use it if there is interest.

I liked the comment at the end of your post -- maybe chat could
replace TOTW, or vice-versa? Perhaps both are not necessary, as
either would provide a forum for tossing ideas around and discussion.

However, I am finding with this, as with the Gathering and other
things that come up, there are lots of people who think they are
good ideas, but when it comes right down to it, there are very few
who have the time or are willing to put the effort into doing it.
So while both of these tools (TOTW and chat) are there if we want to
use them and there has been interest expressed, they are not going
to get off the ground unless there is somebody willing to faciliate
them.

SpringChick

Re: TOTW Facilitator & Chat

From: azobox (jparkerg@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-11-15 11:18:03 UTC
TOTW was/is a good way to bring some reluctant contributors into the
discussion and to share a range of opinions. I noticed during the
early days of this list that a topic would occasionally elicit
comments from people from whom we'd never previously heard a peep.
It seemed as though lurkers would read a topic and realize that they
had an opinion or an idea, and that this was a non-threatening venue
to voice it.

While we could re-run some of the tried and true topics again and some
of the newer members would gain from exposure to the experience of
more seasoned boxers, it is just as likely that the old-timers might
not take the time to chime in yet again on athe TOTW.

"What's in your pack" or "Where are you boxing this week" or "What's
the origin of your trailname" can only be explored so many times....

Having read other regional lists and having tried to come up with
topics for this list awhile back, I think that maybe we need to let it
rise and fall as the desire/need is dictated by the members here.

We are, by and large, a group of friendly and helpful folks. It seems
as though if someone here has a question or concern, it can be posted
and there are likely to be responses. Perhaps adding the prefix "TOTW"
or something like that will alert the rest of us that you'd really
like some comment or input from a broad base of participant experience.

As for chat....I've not participated in those kinds of discussions so
I can't address it with any experience. I do know that I'd not be apt
to sit down at a prescribed date/time to interact with boxers online.

my two cents


azobox
