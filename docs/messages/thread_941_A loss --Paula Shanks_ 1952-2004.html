A loss --Paula Shanks, 1952-2004

From: yooperann (wfisher47@comcast.net) | Date: 2004-12-20 14:59:55 UTC

Those of you who were at the very first Great Lakes gathering may
remember my brother's wife, Paula Shanks. I started letterboxing in
the summer of 2002, when my daughter found the site mentioned on a
blog and discovered there were two boxes in Presque Isle park in
Marquette, which is my hometown and where we often spend lots of
summer vacation time. I passed the mention on to my sister-in-law
Paula, who was planning to join us in the U.P. Paula was a serious
rubber stamp artist, and when she came up from Ann Arbor to join us
she brought along books of clip art, good carving tools, lots of
erasers, and blank journals for each of us. Before the week was
over, we'd each chosen and carved our personal stamps, found the
Presque Isle boxes, and even planted our own box, with a stamp
designed and carved by Paula, on Sugarloaf Mountain.
Paula never letterboxed except with us, but she did come to the
Great Lakes gathering and this summer, when we were all in Wyoming
together, she carved the stamps for four of the seven boxes we placed
there.
Paula died very suddenly on December 2nd. Her personal stamp, a
pair of glasses, graced the front on the memorial program (her name
was written with her own hand-carved alphabet) and the photo on the
back was of her with a rubber stamp sculpture. The link to her
obituary, which includes a photo of the program, is here.
http://paulashanks.typepad.com/my_weblog/2004/12/obituary_john_h.html

I've also posted with a photo taken at the Great Lakes Gathering (and
copied from the LbGLK site but I think it may have been my photo to
begin with--if not, my apologies and thanks). That post is here.
http://paulashanks.typepad.com/my_weblog/2004/12/gifts_from_paul_1.htm
l
John and his daughter Kati (a high school senior) are thirsty for
comments on the blog, so feel free to add one.

And value the time you have with those you love. We never know when
one of our meetings may be the last.
