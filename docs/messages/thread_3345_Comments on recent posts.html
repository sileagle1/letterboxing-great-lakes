Well, it depends on how spread out the boxes are! Even if they are
spread out quite a bit in a single park (someone estimated 14-15
miles of hiking were required to get all the boxes at the Wandering
in Wonderland event in June), you will invariably meet up with other
people on the trail. It's a good chance to chat with other boxers.
However too large of a group descending on a single box require a
wait for stamping in and require extra care & caution when replacing
it, since often the person who actually found the box has already
left the area, passing the box along to the next people who were
waiting. It's always a good idea to be sure people know exactly
where the box should be replaced, and that the information gets
passed along.

If the boxes are spread out across several locations (which I think
will be the case for this event), people tend to disperse a bit more
since driving may be required to get to different areas.

There are usually some boxes placed near the actual gathering spot,
and people try to be somewhat discreet when finding these, to not
spoil the fun for others. However with all the people that are
milling about, it's likely you might be observed by another boxer who
is alert and looking for clues and boxes.

Some people prefer to just hangout and chat rather than looking for
many boxes. This is especially true if the boxes will remain in the
area for a while and the boxer knows that he/she will be able to come
back a different day to look for them.

We're looking forward to meeting you and some of the other newer
boxers in our area!

Wisconsin Hiker

P.S. It is recommended that you ask permission before flipping
through someone else's logbook. Sometimes there are stamp images in
a logbook that may give away the location of a mystery box or a
personal traveler and seeing it would giveaway the secret. If anyone
does have these types of stamps in their current logbooks, one
approach is to paperclip some pages together to "secure" these images
from the casual page flipper.
