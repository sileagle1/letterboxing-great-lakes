From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2009-01-01 17:28:55 UTC
The games don't really go in any particular order, rather are
simultaneous. So from the start, you are trying for any of the
configurations of the interim games -- straight line, "X", 4 corners,
6-pack and 10-spot -- as well as working toward your full card. By
nature of the spaces necessary to get a winner on one of the interim
games, we do tend to see straight line first, followed by 4-corners,
etc. But that is not written in stone and it is very possible that
somebody could get a Bingo with all 4 corners or a 6-pack block before
a straight line Bingo is claimed, etc. Of course, full card will be
last just because it requires all of the spaces.

Just remember... once I begin to call the wild cards (1 each month on
a random Wednesday), you can use your wild card only toward a full
card winner, not in the interim games -- they still require that you
actually have marked off each of the spaces required for that
particular configuration.

SpringChick
