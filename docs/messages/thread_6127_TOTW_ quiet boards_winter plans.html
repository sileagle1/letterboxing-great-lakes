TOTW: quiet boards/winter plans

From: Atom118 (Aatom118@gmail.com) | Date: 2009-10-20 09:20:14 UTC-05:00
SO, it seemed to me that the boards have been quieter lately than they
have in the past. I remember when I first started boxing that I'd check
my mail every day and there would be strings of emails about one thing
or another. After checking the stats of the GLK board, there does seem
to be some drop-off in posts. Sometimes it was just random chatting,
but often it was something directly related to letterboxing. Anyway,
this is my attempt to prompt some chatter.

So how about a new TOTW (Topic of the Week): Winter Plans. Anyone have
plans for winter boxes? Do people like winter boxes?

Personally, I find winter to be a great time to box. The trails are
usually empty, and if you choose your clues wisely, the boxes are easier
to find without all the greenery to get in the way.

As some of you know, we tried to encourage some winter boxing last year
with our 'Monk' series. It seemed to go over well, and we are planning
a new winter series for this year. We just need to find some time (and
carve) to make the boxes! In addition, looking past winter, we're
trying to get plans for spring. At the W MI gathering, we were reminded
of all the fun we had doing Azobox/Springchick's 'Around the World'
series (as well as Dragon's Holy Grail Series)....humm....

I hope to hear from you,

Atom118 of the Atomic Beans

Re: [LbGLK] TOTW: quiet boards/winter plans

From: Katrina Lustofin (drkaty@gmail.com) | Date: 2009-10-20 15:09:31 UTC-04:00
I love winter boxing. The cold does not stop me, though I have had some
difficulties in the past because of the frozen water. There was one
letterbox I could not find because it was very difficult to determine where
the "dry creek bed" was under several feet of snow. There was another box,
which I really need to go after again, that I could see and almost touch -
frozen solid in a block of ice in a tree hole. While I did contemplate
chipping it out, I decided since it is not that far from my house, I can
always return again.

Frankly, I would rather outfit myself with gloves and wool socks than the
plethora of water bottles necessary for some summer hikes. The cold doesn't
hold the dogs back at all, and as an academic, I have this marvelous break
in December during which I traditionally drive across several states and box
en route.

This year I plan to finally plant my Rankin and Bass series, based on my
favorite Christmas specials. I have Rudolph, Snowmiser and Heatmiser,
Nestor, and a Bumble carved and ready to go. I just have to decide where.
And they will be limited time, only available during the holiday season (the
real one, not the retail one). The poor stamps have been sitting in plastic
bags with their logbooks for close to 3 years now, so it's past time for
them to see some action.

Katy

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2009-10-20 16:26:21 UTC-05:00
I think Tiptoe used a good phrase: "full onslaught of all seasons". I am
definitely a warm weather person and consider winter an onslaught (or
sometimes an actual ASSAULT!). However that doesn't stop us from heading
out to do some letterboxing. No bugs, less attacking/obscuring foliage and
less muggles are some of the positive aspects. However we DO have to keep
the ballpoint pen inside our mittens to keep it warm enough to write with.
And sometimes those wind chills can totally freeze our fingers in the short
time we have the mittens off to stamp in. .. Oh, why do we even have to be
talking about this already!! I hate the cold and have been very crabby
about the weather this month. What happened to fall? Why have we had the
furnace on for 3 weeks already???

I definitely prefer fair weather boxing (what did you expect someone called "Sunny" to say? :) ) But my definition of fair weather has expanded. Now it means daylight, dry, not snowing much, not too windy and not extreme cold.
