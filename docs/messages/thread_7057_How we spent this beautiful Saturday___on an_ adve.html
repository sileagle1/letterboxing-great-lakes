We too had a letterboxing adventure today. Fortunately we didn't have to
ford any streams with a baby filled stroller over our heads! Our hats go off
to the Atomic Beans!

Tonto had work in Springfield (IL) yesterday, so Tiptoe decided to go along
with the hopes of maybe getting in a few boxes today before heading back
home. It stormed all night long and we were certain the trails would be
water logged. They did prove to be a bit soggy, but nothing resembling GLK
2010!
All was fine except... (hard as this may be for some of you to believe),
Tiptoe left her glasses at home and couldn't read a thing. But worse, she
left ALL of her letterboxing gear at home as well!
Tonto had an old pair of glasses in the glove box that she could at least
make out some things, a quick stop at Hobby Lobby for new markers, sketch
book, and a piece of rubber...she was carving away in the car in route to
the first box. It is about the worse thing any of you will ever see! Blame
it on the bumpy car ride, shortness of time (minutes), shovel gouge, and
blurry vision! But with the quick carve of "TnT" , we had a "hand carved"
sig stamp and we were ready to go.

We were enjoying the beautiful spring day finding the boxes, but as we were
finishing up one longer trail, we were striping down to the bare
essentials...it had reached 85 degrees! Our bodies were just not used to hot
humid weather! We went from winter to summer in a flash!

We found a few drive by's as we blasted the air conditioner in the car.
Headed for another box in a lakeside park, but it was swarming with so many
people that there was no way we could grab the box without half of the
people in central Illinois witnessing two very strangely "undressed" folks
poking around their picnic/fishing activities.

We also made the observation that LOVE must've been in the air...around many
bends in the trails we ran into young (and old couples) embracing/kissing
etc....we could hear Old Owl telling Bambi, "Yes. Nearly everybody gets
twitterpated in the springtime. For example: You're walking along, minding
your own business. You're looking neither to the left, nor to the right,
when all of a sudden you run smack into a pretty face. Woo-woo! You begin to
get weak in the knees. Your head's in a whirl. And then you feel light as a
feather, and before you know it, you're walking on air. And then you know
what? You're knocked for a loop, and you completely lose your head!"

We also saw a bald eagle soaring right overhead, white pelicans, red bud and
magnolia's blooming! Take heart all of you further north...Spring is coming!
(or should we say Summer!) FYI.....Beware that HOT & Humid stuff is headed
north toward Chicago tomorrow (Sunday)...with possibilities of storms that
could turn tornadic!

A good drive back up to Chicago following old Route 66..eating at Palm's
Cafe (a throw back blue plate special restaurant in Atlanta), picking up
some Funk's Grove SIRUP..and a few more drive by's before heading home, made
for a great day....but now too tired for any twitterpating on our part!
Tiptoe & Tonto
