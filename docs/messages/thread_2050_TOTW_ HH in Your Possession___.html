TOTW: HH in Your Possession...

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-11-28 11:40:33 UTC
Okay everyone -- time to 'fess up! I'm posting a new TOTW in which
we all have the opportunity to come clean and admit to the HH we are
holding hostage in the bottom of our backpacks. The purpose of this
is not to shame anyone and please don't criticize or flame anyone
who steps forward to repent. Rather the purpose is to rattle your
brain and make you remember you have the thing and maybe provide a
glimmer of hope to a HH owner who hasn't heard on the whereabouts of
their little creation in a while.

You don't have to tell us how long you've had the HH or make any
excuses as to why you haven't replanted it yet if you don't want to -
- although funny excuses/stories are welcome. If you don't have any
HH currently (pat yourself on the back), feel free to chime in with
a story about one you previously held hostage.

So, dig down into the bottom of those backpacks and drag those
skeletons out of the closet. There, don't you feel better? Now,
get out and plant the thing!

SpringChick

From: cerealcityboxers (cerealcityboxers@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-11-28 17:05:55 UTC
We held onto of azoobox's HH's for several months during last
Spring. Our excuse was that with the proliferation of micro's and
mini's, we never could find a box big enough or with enough room
inside it to place the HH. When we finally placed it and notified
azoobox, he mentioned that HH's don't necessarily have to go inside a
letterbox. On top of, along side, or even underneath a micro will
all work, if the location will handle it and the HH is waterproof.
There, even less excuses now available for holding onto those little
buggers.

From: bindlestiff_boxer (bindlestiff_boxer@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-11-29 12:59:27 UTC
Team Bindlestiff currently possess no HH belonging to anyone else.

We have however, recovered the recently liberated little HH of ours
that was stolen and used without our permission as an unwilling
participant in a convoluted "experiment" by another boxer from the
Great Lakes region.

We remain firm in our conviction that people who want to conduct
"experiments" and start new trends with HH's are more than free to do
so, but they should choose a willing partner to collaborate with and
respect the right of those who choose not to participate, to decline
becoming involved.

---Bindle Babe

Exactly, make sure that poor HH is triple-bagged or in
its own container. We found a long-lost HH last year
when looking for a mystery box. The mystery box had
been planted about a year earlier, and we were the
first finders (yea us!), and the HH had been included
as a first finder bonus. The HH had been left next to
the box rather than inside, though, and its lone baggy
was not enough to protect it from harsh Great Lakes
winters. The stamp we salvaged, but alas, the log
book had turned to mush. Nothing like seeing little
bugs oozing around the mushy pages of a logbook that's
melded together....Luckily we were able to locate the
HH's creator & send it back to him for some TLC.

As for us hording HHs in our bookbag...glad to report
that we have none right now (I think). Instead I'm
hording the clues to our planted Holy Grail
box...really, Dragon, I'll get them typed up & to you
soon, as soon as I stop coughing up my lungs because I
got sick this weekend *WAHHH*

Although, now planted and on her way again, we did
hold onto the Mrs. Claus HH which we found on the way
home from the Great Lakes Gathering in June a little
longer than we should have...so I understand if we
only get coal in our stockings this year.... :(
