Hello to the group from Jigsaw

From: Tom (t.lewandowski@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2005-08-23 02:35:58 UTC
Hello everyone! I want to start off my introducing myself. My
trail name is Jigsaw and I am 30 years old. I live in Milwaukee,
Wisconsin. I stumbled across geocaching in a blurb in a random post
on some website I don't even remember where, well, after going to
the site and pricing GPS devices, I was like, yeah, thats gonna have
to wait.... too expensive at this time.

But then, I noticed a link about letterboxing. Eventually after
much searching and reading, I really thought that it was something
that I would like and could possibly do... Well guess what? It is
and I do!!

I have only placed one box so far (The Mysterious Woman) with a few
more in the works. I have found 30 boxes. I have met The Christmas
Elves and Sophie & Me (two separate occasions) on the trail. They
are awesome! I rescued The Lucky Leprechaun when he wasn't so
lucky. I will be attending the Haunted Harvest Gathering in
October. I have also been doing some Postal Letterboxes too. This
hobby is so versitile and so fun. And its good exercising without
even realizing it.

Me (of Sophie & Me) made the comment... "Put a gun to my head and
tell me to walk 5 miles, and I would say 'Yeah right', but Put a box
out and tell me its 5 miles, and I'd do it twice!!" I found that so
true. Although I don't think I'd do the entire Cambridge Park
Series in one fell swoop again anytime soon. LOL.

And Ive done all this in only 2 months. This hobby/sport keeps
getting better each day. I am thouroughly obsessed!

If there is anything that is happening that I may be intrested in
(gatherings, series, anything at all) please do not hesitate to drop
me a line and let me know about them. Most likely, I will be
interested.

Until then,

Jigsaw

This paragraph reminds me of a box Deedle & I found in
Southern Ohio back in April of this year. The clues
said it was an easy find. We found the "start" of the
trail that we were supposed to find and began walking.
After an hour or so of going up hills and down hills
and squeezing through muddy narrow parts of the trail,
we just kind of looked at each other going, "This is
an EASY find??" We finally got almost back to the
start of the 5 mile trail (since it looped around a
lake) and the agonzing reality set in - it's an easy
find if you started at the OTHER end of the trail,
which we never saw from where we parked.

But yes, at least we got our exercise that day!
