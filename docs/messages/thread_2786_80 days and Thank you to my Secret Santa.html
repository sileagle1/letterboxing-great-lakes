80 days and Thank you to my Secret Santa

From: pgranola2002 (p2jd@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2006-07-19 15:05:51 UTC
To those of you who have completed the Around the World in 80 days
series...I extend to you the highest praise. I am in awe of you.
This past weekend I dragged my husband (not a letterboxer, he just
goes because he loves me)up to Michigan to finish the 80 days
series. Mind you, this 80 day adventure has now turned into 370
days. I and 3 other victims I was able to convince to travel with
me have made 4 trips to MI to get these boxes, and this weekend was
supposed to be THE ONE, the finish line was in sight, the
satisfaction of a job completed was bubbling up in my soul. Yea
right. Let me give you the whole picture...last fall when we first
started this journey we picked up 8 boxes, which was good we were on
a roll until we got to #9 only to find that the place was closed
(locked up tight)we started to climb the fence but a truck pulled
into the area and although I really wanted the box I don't look good
in orange so we moved on. When we hit box #12 it had gone missing.
At box #14 we spent about 2 hours searching but couldn't find it. We
returned home a bit disappointed but undaunted. We figured that 8
out of 11 was ok considering the circumstances. Next trip up I
picked up #15 and found that # 12 was still missing (or was missing
again). Fast forward, now several months have passed and the area
for box #9 is open again, I convince my son that a 5 hour drive with
mom to go get that one box is a great idea, it will give us some
quality time together and off we go in his car. (Mind you this is
over 500 miles round trip so now in the scheme of things I have
traveled over 1000 miles for this one box) We found the park open
and once stamped in he said "Well we're doing good, lets try to get
boxes #3 and #12" and off we went...until his car started acting a
little wierd. Scratch that idea...we were lucky to get home, his
transmission went out. That brings me to this weekend, 1800 (at
least)miles and one transmission later I was going to get this thing
done. We were doing so well, got boxes 12,13,3 and were sailing
along. And then we get to number 8. We tried and tried and tried to
figure this one out, we know we were in the right place but somehow
we missed something and after about 2 and a half hours later had to
leave empty handed. I actually had tears in my eyes, my husband who
does not want to be a letterboxer but only goes because he loves me
doesn't love me anymore.("If you want to come here again you're on
your own sister!")
To finish this sad tale let's add injury to insult, I did find box
#14 this time but also found a swarm of mosquitos, in fact I had to
play tug of war with them to get the letterbox. I walked out of
there with no less than 28 mosquito bites AND poison ivy. I have
spent the past 36 hours covered in cortizone cream muttering I can't
believe I only have 14...box #8 where could it be?...whose idea was
this stupid hobby anyway?...I can't believe I only have 14.
Just as I was about to throw away my stamps and log books the
postman brought me a surprise, it was from my secret santa and I had
Christmas in July. Whoever you are...I love my box. You couldn't
have done a more wonderful job, it's like you know me. I have a
collection of old Christmas movies but not that one, I love Frank
Perreti, the deer poo was perfect, and a gas card?!! Thank you,
thank you, thank you!
So now I have to make one last trip up to MI before school starts to
find that elusive box #8 and finish this ridiculous journey...anyone
want to come along?

Oh you guys, I am amused reading the accounts of your travels -- amused not
in the sense of laughing *at* you, rather at your sense of humor. Really we
never intended for people to become so obsessed... and it was never our
intention to stump people to the point that they would be coming back 4-5
times for the same box! That #8 box, I really have wracked my brain left
and right trying to figure out a better way to word the clues so it does not
cause such problems, but cannot come up with anything better without totally
spelling it out. I do understand where people are getting confused -- how
was I to know that one little mis-interpretation would bring you to a hiding
place that matched the clues precisely?

Tiptoe, it is a good thing you didn't try to hand-deliver that card to my
house this weekend, because while you were here in MI, OldShoe and I were
over there in the Chicago suburbs -- at least you were smart enough to head
to the cooler of the two places.

P. Granola, I really feel for you on the #8 box, but certainly do admire
your perseverance and am glad to hear the others all fell into place.

Hang in there! And congrats to the latest finishers (I think the official
announcement staff will be chiming in shortly).
