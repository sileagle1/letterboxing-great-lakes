Well, this Email is an answer to some of your questions. The only question I don't have any idea on is the date . . . I'm leaving that up to SpringChick to decide on (unless she defers it to me or to a poll).



This gathering will be a joint effort between Pitties, Shorty & Myself (Hart x6) with extra supporting roles as needed.



The gathering will hopefully be at one of these two locations. Jubilee State College S.P. is my primary interest as it is over 3,000 acres with all kinds of trails. Jubilee is located just north-west of Peoria, IL.



My second choice is much smaller and just north of Morrison, IL. So, I'm keeping my fingers crossed for JCSP.



Jubilee College State Park http://dnr.state.il.us/lands/Landmgt/PARKS/R1/Jubilee.htm

Morrison Rockwood State Park http://dnr.state.il.us/lands/Landmgt/parks/R1/MORRISON.HTM


The theme will be "CHANGING THE WORLD, ONE BOX AT A TIME" with images carved using images found on World Coins.



There is SUCH a huge selection of images to difficulty of images to carve. You can image search "World Coins ____?_____" and find hundreds of images. The "?" can be filled in with ANYTHING; mermaids, trees, fish, elephants, flowers, buildings, planes, people, etc etc etc.



I'm asking that you carve the entire coin (inside a circle). I ask that you at least give the lettering a try!! If you can't nail it, or you screw it up, then just cut it out leaving no lettering. Though, I think you owe it to yourself to at least try.

=0
A
I may step on some toes with this, but there are a few things I ask not be a part of your stamp;



1) If you plan on using metallic's (gold, silver, bronze) please include an ink pad, as not everyone has their own. If you can get away with stamping it with Brown, Black or Gray that would be preferred.



2) Please no HUGE stamps. I was thinking of setting a standard (ie nothing bigger than 3" across), however, I'll leave the size up to you -- but please try to keep the stamps from growing to a large size.



3) Keep the stamps "clean" or "G" rated. There are coins out there with nudity, I ask that you respect the many folks who would prefer not finding a "PG-13" stamp.



Also with #3 we may want to discuss offensive images. One quick example are the German 3 Reich coins all have a swastika on them. If you think the image will offend someone, please find something else.



PLEASE PLEASE Try to have your stamps into us within a month of the actuall gathering -probably next April/May. There is a lot of time between now and then, carve them up and get them to us ASAP so that no one will be left sweating it out at the last minute. ALSO, I will soon be posting this to AQ, PLEASE sign up what coin or set of coins you will be carving so that there are no duplicates.



The exact date is still TBA. Personally, I would like to see it remain the same or move one week later to accommodate those with kids in school. I've been on=2
0this board long enough to know that you can't please everyone!



Here's looking forward to GLG 2010 & getting the park of our choice.



~Hart x6

P.S. The theme was in part inspired by my coin collection that over the
course of the last year I sold off on Ebay. I collected coins in earnest for
about 12 years, and amassed coins from every country in the world.

If you have an idea, and have trouble locating a coin with the image you
want, please contact me & I will work with you in finding the right image.

In addition to Google Image Search, you can try Ebay and look up completed
listings for a specific coin -- you may find a good image there. Your local
Library may be of help too . . . just look for The Standard Catalog of
World Coins by krause.

Trees, Flowers, Animals, Buildings, Profiles, Olympics, Air Planes/Jets,
Presidents, Landmarks, etc etc

From: Kathi Krankoski (krank@ameritech.net) | Date: 2009-08-20 10:55:20 UTC-07:00
For general boxing, color isn't an issue. When you get into the "artificial" boxing atmosphere of an event, the rules sometimes change a bit. The tendency to hit the trails in groups (socializing is the name of the game!) requires a longer period of time for the entire group to stamp in (particularly when lots of colors are involved) and pass everything around ("Who has a red pen I can borrow?" "Where's the cap for the green?"). Add to that the fact that there's undoubtedly another mass of people hot on your heels...you want to hide it and get out of there quickly so they can actually find it hidden and not just do a hand-off. Monochromatic (even if not just one color) stamps streamline the process. I think the suggestion of providing a pad for the stamp color required is a good one (it's not like we are talking permanent placements where doing so isn't practical in our climate).

And then there's those mosquitoes that always live where the stamps that require a dozen colors are placed............. (-:

--klk/fw
