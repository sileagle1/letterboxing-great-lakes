To camo or not to camo

From: Scoutdogs (DogScouts@hotmail.com) | Date: 2007-03-07 10:17:37 UTC-05:00
Good point. If you're hiding in an urban area, you have more to consider than if hiding boxes in the woods. You have to weigh the implications regarding the box being accidentally found if it's not re-hidden properly. It may be better to risk someone seeing it as trash and just tossing it as opposed to someone seeing it, thinking it's a "suspicious package", evacuating the area, calling the bomb squad, etc.

Camo blends in better in the woods and that's what it's designed for, not the urban jungle (unless maybe it's planted in urban vegetation, but that is at risk from landscapers.) I think in an urban situation, better "camo" is actually an untaped box. Because plastic doesn't look as out of place in urban settings as it does "in the wild."

There's been a LOT of discussion about camo tape since the nonsense in
Boston. The concern is that average Joe Blow stumbling across a
'mysterious' package that has clearly been wrapped for disguise will
over react and yell for the bomb squad. Some locations that allow
boxes have already stated they must be clear, so that contents may be
identified without opening the box.

I personally have switched to doing partial camo. I'm wrapping camo
around the sides in a compromise, leaving the top and bottom of the
container clear. Being an urban boxer, I'd really rather avoid public
panics :)

From: pennypenny_sl (PennyPenny@cablespeed.com) | Date: 2007-03-08 19:19:43 UTC
I have thought about this issue (bomb scare, etc.) every time I make a box and think about
how best to hide it. Some hiding places don't need camo on the box, but some do. So...

What has worked well for me with a few of my most "stealth" boxes is to do the camo job
as I like it -- while also keeping some small part of the box clear for seeing through it.

For instance, one of my boxes is held in place with velcro on the bottom, and the rest of
the box is painted black to blend in with it's urban location. I did not paint the bottom...
so just around all the edges of the velcro, when the box is removed from it's hiding spot,
you can peek in and see that there are no wires, switches, or the like(!)

Another I have is painted in outdoor/woods camo paint... and again, I simply left part of
the bottom clear... and hid it with that side to the ground. I keep my fingers crossed that
it's placed again in that same position after finders stamp in and return the box.

So far (knock on wood) I've had good luck with these camo boxes, and hopefully it's
enough to keep me from getting in trouble with any of the authorities ;-)

PennyPenny
