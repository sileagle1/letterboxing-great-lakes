WI - Fun 3-day weekend!

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2004-08-16 04:54:28 UTC
Hi!

We had a great 3-day weekend, letterboxing mostly in Door County,
WI. For those that don't know where that is, it is the "thumb" of
Wisconsin that extends into Lake Michigan, sometimes known as "The
Cape Cod of the Midwest".

We had FABULOUS weather - no rain, not too hot, not too cold! We
found all 15 boxes we set out to find in the county and had many
great views of sun sparkling on the water, great hikes through woods,
beaches, bluffs, etc. Of course the boxes contained many great
stamps to commemorate our journeys. Thanks to all of you who plant
boxes (I can't say this enough!). We also found time to do some wine
tasting and eat some great meals.

We were especially pleased to find some of the oldest boxes placed in
WI, including that believed to be the first box placed in the state.
Before leaving, we planted another box in the county (clues posted
soon), then adopted an older box in the Green Bay area, met some new
boxers and did an exchange (their first meeting with other boxers)
and found 4 more boxes enroute home.

We're looking forward to our next 3-day boxing weekend in MI on Labor
Day weekend. Hopefully the weather will be as good - from what I
have read, I have no doubt that the boxes will be plentiful and the
stamps delightful!

Wisconsin Hiker

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-08-16 18:17:34 UTC-04:00
You simply must visit Al Johnson's Swedish Restaurant in Sister Bay for the
best Swedish pancakes ever. The goats on the roof are kind of a neat touch
also.

There are not a lot of sandy beachy areas such as we have over here on the
Michigan side of the lake -- mostly rocky shorelines there, but you may find
a few. A great lakeside park to visit is Cave Point County Park which is on
the Lake Michigan side, south of Bailey's Harbor & Jacksonport -- there is a
nice scenic drive that runs up the Lake Michigan side of the peninsula.
Cave Point is not as busy as the nearby Whitefish Dunes State Park and has
some very neat rock ledges and rock formations along the water line that are
somewhat like caves (thus the name). Here is a link that gives a nice
overview of the park. It was one of our favorite little out-of-the-way
places in Door County. I believe dogs are allowed on leash at all Door
County County parks.

http://www.doorcountynavigator.com/html/gems/cave.html

Whitefish Dunes State Park, just up the road from there, is somewhat busier
than Cave Point, but it is is one of the great sandy beach areas in Door
County. They do allow dogs on half of the beach area there (I think the
south half). It is supposedly the best place to watch a Door County
sunrise.

Lots of great galleries and shops and so much to see -- do take time to ride
some of the back roads and view the countryside and some of the awesome
barns in the area. And of course the lighthouses are awesome. We took our
boat over and rode out to a few that are on islands in Green Bay. Found out
the hard way why the water up at the tip is known as the Devil's Door -- I
have never been so scared in the boat ever... and it was smooth and calm
when we left land. You can see several of the lighthouses from land though.
Of course, Cana Island is a favorite, but I also really liked the big red
one out on the end of the pier at Sturgeon Bay. The other lighthouse in the
Sturgeon Bay area is used as a summer home for Coast Guard personnel and is
off-limits to tourists. But we asked real nice if we could take some
pictures and they were very friendly.

Door County is one of my very favorite places and I am sure you will have a
great time there!

SpringChick
