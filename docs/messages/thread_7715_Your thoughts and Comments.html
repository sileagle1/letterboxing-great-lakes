Dear Fellow Letterboxers,

I have been letter boxing since 2006. But, I do not know much about letterboxing culture or language. (I just love to be stealthy and feel like I got away with something.)

In 2009 I came across a non-traditional subway series in Toronto Ontario. The stamps were not contained in a box nor a book. These stamps had magnets attached to them and were hidden on metal objects in high traffic areas. Are they considered microboxes? Is there a name for this kind of stamp?

Also, I do like carving stamps. I would like to create a series similar to this in Madison, WI. I feel like this would work in more muggle-infested areas. However, I do know some folks get bummed out when a traditional box is not used. I also would miss reading the logbook. However, I think fun could be had by all. Do you think there would be interest for these type of stamps here in Wisconsin?

Respectfully,
COPPERBONES aka bamugzy

I found plenty of magnetic series in CA. They usually put a logbook in
just the first or last box of the series. A compromise, as it were. Just
be ready and willing to re-carve and re-plant often!

Hi!



If there isn't any logbook at all, they are just stamps, not letterboxes.
However some people might still enjoy finding them.



We personally don't like letterboxing in "high traffic areas" and prefer to
find boxes in more secluded spots. Hopefully you'll consider putting some
of your carvings out on a trail sometime since we are in Madison/Middleton
quite often and would love to hunt for them.

From: Atom118 (Aatom118@gmail.com) | Date: 2013-04-01 10:08:09 UTC-05:00
We've planted/found a few of these in Chicago with small
notebooks/stamps in hide-a-keys. Unfortunately, they didn't last very
long (oddly some of the newspaper bins we hid them in got replaced more
often than we had previously noticed!). While I was excited to plant
them in the locations (given the otherwise difficulty finding an
appropriate place), I'm not sure if we'd do it again unless the spot was
perfect. Even though some of these had been our boxes, and we were
familiar with the area, it always felt odd to get them in such
high-traffic areas. But that's just us...I know some people really
enjoy that aspect of urban boxes.
