Trail Tracker,
This brings up a good topic for discussion. I've often wondered which is the correct choice: adding pages or replacing a full logbook. One of the best letterboxes I've found had 8 years of personal stamps and messages and it was still the original logbook. Now, more people are taking to the trails and some, not all, are in too much of a hurry and may not understand the importance of sealing bags and making sure all four corners of the box lid are secured. But letterboxers who also create boxes become more sensitized when finding and replacing boxes.
Sorry to hear about the water damage. One of my logbooks was found floating in the box. YIKES! I took the logbook home and spread out the pages on paper towels and newspaper and dried them with my hair dryer. (First Aid for Logbooks!)
Then, I dry ironed the pages. I was able to save most of that logbook. But I did discover that some of the inks we use don't bleed while others washed out completely. Permanent ink and messages in ball point pen remained. What we use to leave our mark in the logbook can help too.

Beetle
