From: lesann847 (l-fischer@northwestern.edu) | Date: 2004-08-22 16:09:28 UTC
I have very sad news about Fool's Errand #20 - Judgement. Not
only has it disappeared, but to add insult to injury another box
has been planted beneath the very flagstone that protected
FEXX.

Team Taco and I collaborated on this box, so it is very dear to my
heart. They spent hours and a ton of talent on carving the stamp,
making the log book, scouting the location and writing awesome
clues. I spent hours and hours making a great cover for the
stamp in the form of a book of judgement.

This is very sad.

I am off for a few days to Starved Rock; when I get back, I'll post
that great stamp so you can see what you've missed.

We will plant again.

Acorn

From: moonsunmagik (moonsunmagik@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-08-23 20:25:49 UTC
It was a letterbox. The individual who planted the new box is new
to letterboxing and claimed that she had no idea another box was
placed in the same area. Since the box that we and Acorn had
planted (and is now missing) did not have the name of the town or
the cemetary in which it was planted prominently and explicitly
displayed in the title of the clues, the new 'boxer considered the
area boxless.

Which it was, by the time she got there. It seems "Judgement" was
fine until we allowed Acorn to give us credit for the box. So
whoever has it in for our boxes hasn't forgotten us, which is pretty
creepy if I allow myself to think about it for very long.

I've learned a couple of things from this: one, a shroud of mystery
isn't always a guarantee of safety and security of your clues and
boxes, and two, never underestimate the power of a grandma with a
pencil box and a kitty cat stamp! Those flagstones are HERS now,
and she can definitely have 'em!

Melinda, Team Taco
