Beetle,

Funny you should reply to this string again . . . I thought it was dead.

I have been going through a personal crisis of sorts with this hobby. I love
this hobby and I will never give it up, however, the honeymoon is definitely
over.

I've noticed over the course of the last 6 to 8 weeks that I'm just not as
excited as I once was. I think this is in large part to my over ambitious
nature... when I get hooked on something I go FULL throttle.

For example, our one year anniversary is next week (Nov. 25th), our counts
are P 64 F 243 X 83.

I currently am in the middle of carving my 100th stamp!!

I guess I'm a bit burned out, I never thought it would happen with
letterboxing though. Perhaps some of you seasoned veterans of the hobby can give some
words of encouragement.

I figured with any addiction you have to raise the risk factor to get the
same thrill you once did... so tonight while driving to Oregon, Illinois for
work I decided to find Pastors Wife's "John Deere Plow" in the dark during
hunting season!! There were signs everywhere saying the park was closed due to
the fact that hunters could bag a deer or two. Don't worry.... no one shot at
me, though in the dark I may or may not look like a deer.

Beetle, with a last name like Hart there are many options... I will forgo
the 2nd grade humor though ;-)
I have no problem with changing my signature stamp... though changing our
trail name seems to be a little bit of a snag to me.

I don't know if I draw to much of my boxing "identity" from our trail name
or the tape I may have to cut through on LBNA and AQ to change my trailname. I
must say though, I am very intrigued by "6 of Harts" that you recommended and
I have even sketched out a few possibilities for signature stamp images
going on that name.

Sorry for the long, rambling Email, just kinda down I guess.

hx6
