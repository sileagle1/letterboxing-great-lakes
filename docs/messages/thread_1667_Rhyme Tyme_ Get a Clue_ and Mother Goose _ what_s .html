First of all, since SpringChick and azobox often find themselves
writing to each other in code and intentionally planning to confound
their readers, it's not surprising that confusion has surfaced
with the event planned for October 22 at Yankee Springs Recreation
Area.

So here's a quick breakdown of what's planned and how you can
participate.

First, there is the Fall Michigan Letterbox Gathering, called
"Get a Clue". Like many other gatherings, all are welcome and
there will be a potluck, opportunities to exchange stamps with others,
and letterboxing galore!

An additional activity will be a game based on the board game
"Clue" that we're calling
"Rhyme Tyme Clue Exchange Game".
This is an opportunity for both new and experienced boxers to interact
with each other in a different way. You'll try to solve the
mystery of "Who Killed Mother Goose?" by discovering the
killer, the weapon and the place in which the dreadful deed occurred.
See message 4551 on the Yahoo chat to read Sgt. Grimm's report. A
more complete description of how the game is played and how you can
participate can be found the link below.

FAQ's:
*Can I do one thing and not the other?
Sure. We're happy to have you involved however you'd like to
be.
Come and play the Rhyme Tyme Clue Exchange Game and solve the mystery,
"Who Killed Mother Goose" and then hang around and chat all
day.
Or play the game and then go out to box. Or come and do some
exchanges, have some lunch, and box `til you drop!

*Can I make a box and place it myself at the park before the gathering
or do the organizers have to plant the boxes?
Either way is fine.
You can visit Yankee Springs between now and then and plant the box
yourself. Or you can mail the box to the planners and they'll get
it planted for you.
(Be sure to let us know if it's intended to be a permanent box for
Yankee Springs or if you want it returned to you after the event.)

*Do I have to carve a stamp about Mother Goose?
Nope. But if you want to play the Clue Exchange Game, you'll have
a better chance of having stamps to play with if you bring some. (Read
more about this on the link below.) Plus you'll have the fun of
getting lots of images from other playes. The Mother Goose Murder
Mystery stamps are not regular letterboxes. They are part of the Clue
Exchange Game.

Full information about the "Rhyme Tyme Clue Exchange Game"
and the
rest of the Michigan Fall Gathering "Get a Clue" can be found
at:

http://www.stampinthebox.com/events/051022_main.asp
