From: duncan53211 (duncan53211@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-11-06 00:33:27 UTC
WI Letterboxers,

I hope that I'm not being being too out of line with this, forgive me
if you feel I am, but I felt I needed to make contact today with the
WI geocacher group on our behalf for a couple of reasons.

The primary reason was that after a long walk in the cold rain to get
to the Aslan box in The Lion, the Witch, and the Wardrobe series, I
found that a geocacher failed to notice the large print on the box
saying that it is a letterbox and promply exchanged a travel bug for
the Aslan stamp! (Sorry Sophie & Me!) I'm hoping that the president
of the WI Geocachers can hunt down the geocacher involved, so we can
get the stamp back before it bounces around too many geocaches! (I
should also note that another geocacher had also came by and put a
plastic orc fighter in the letterbox and "took nothing"). As
mentioned below, I know of the geocache in the area, and it is yards
away from the Aslan box. (It is actually only a few yards away from
another letterbox in the area.)

Secondly, as is indicated on their group's website, and as I noted in
an earlier post to this group, the WI geocachers are trying to set
policy for geocaches in WI with the state DNR and parks. As we know,
these policies often get converted to include letterboxes without our
knowledge until it is too late. I firmly believe that the current
problems that the IL letterboxers are having with various preserves
are a result of policies that were inacted for geocachers, in which
the geocachers were aware of what was going on but the letterboxers
were not.

As such, I sent the following email to the president of the WI Geocachers:

>>>snip

"Greetings!

I'm representing a number of letterboxers in WI who
feel there is a need for our two groups to at least
have some contact with each other since our two
activities share similiar concerns. Athough we do not
have a state organization (yet), there are over 100
letterboxers in WI, most of whom are part of the Great
Lakes Letterboxing group.

One area we are particularly concerned about is your
group's discussion with the WI DNR and state parks.
As many states adopt policies restricting Geocaching,
too many of them automatically assume that
letterboxing is the same and force the geocaching
rules on us. Often these infringe on the very areas
where letterboxing and geocaching are different or
have differing philosophies and tend to ruin our
hobby. Before the WI geocachers "strike a deal" with
the state, we would like to have a say in the deal, as
we may not be in complete agreement with you on some
issues. By contacting you, I'm hoping that we can
work together for the good of both hobbies in WI (not
to mention all the other fringe hobbies related to our
hobbies).

I also have another concern I'm hoping you can help me
with. On my letterboxing walk today at Lake Park in
Milwaukee, I found the box I was searching for, but it
had been found by two different geocachers (at the end
of Oct.) who failed to notice that it was a letterbox
and not the nearby geocache (although the box is
CLEARLY marked as a letterbox). As such, one of them
who goes by the name Great Teton 2020 took the hand
carved stamp that needs to remain in the letterbox.
They left travel bug #477229. The other geocacher
left a plastic figure. The stamp that they took means
a lot to many letterboxers and we'd like your help in
tracking down this geocacher and hopefully getting the
stamp back to where it belongs. I also have in my
possesion the items the geocachers left behind, since
although I know there is a geocache near the
letterbox, I'm not sure exactly where it is, so I
couldn't put them in the geocache. (I know about the
geocache because I happened upon a geocaching family
(who are actually friends of mine) as I was searching
for a different letterbox nearby over a year ago). If
you find out who this Great Teton geocacher is, please
have them contact me by email so we can work things
out.

This actually brings up another concern for both of
our groups. Although most letterboxers know about
geocaching and know what to do with a geocache, I've
found that geocachers tend to not know about
letterboxes. Anything that your group can do to alert
WI geocachers about letterboxes so that situations
like this don't happen would be greatly appreciated.
This is the first time I am aware of that a geocacher
has taken the stamp of a letterbox in WI. I have,
however, personally experienced this in many of the
surrounding states on my letterboxing trips. (In one
extreme case, a geocacher actually removed a letterbox
so they could take over the great hiding location.)

Thanks for your efforts, and here's to our two groups
working together in WI!

Sincerely,
Tim Kuehl
"The Dragon""

>>>snip

I will keep you all informed of anything that transpires from this
contact through posts like this to the GL group.

It would also seem prudent at this time for us to officially form a
group. I in NO WAY wish to become a "splinter" group of the GL group,
NOR do I wish us to create a separate Yahoo Talk Group. We should
stay a complete part of the GL group. All I am suggesting is that we
form a "recognizable group" that the DNR, etc. can recognize and
contact. I think it would be nice to have our own webpage, with
information that is particularly for WI letterboxers - particularly WI
newbies. So we all have a voice in whatever policies affect us, we
should join together by creating a membership list of letterboxers in
WI, with contact people, who can turn around and inform the other
members either through the GL group or by email. (I also think it
might be cool if we created a "Letterboxing WI" logo. Maybe it could
even be carved for the traveling event stamps SpringChick is looking
for and/or for our own patches, but I digress.) I don't think we need
to go the route of the geocachers and have a "president", membership
dues, etc. for our group either. Just a couple of contacts on the
website in case someone from the outside need to contact us as a group.

If you don't like any part of this, let me know, but please be kind :-)

If you agree with me that this is a good idea and would like to help
in any area I mentioned in the formation of the group, please let me
know the specific area in which you'd like to help. I definately feel
there is much to be gained by having newer WI letterboxers as well as
established letterboxers joining together to put this group together,
so don't be shy to volunteer!

BTW, if you are interested in visiting the WI Geocachers website, go
to http://www.wi-geocaching.com/about.php On this page is info about
their dealings with the state allbeit minimal info.

Respectfully submitted,

Re: WI Letterboxers - it's long, but please read!

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2005-11-08 16:32:21 UTC
Dragon,

You asked for opinions and feedback, so here is our input:

We personally don't have an interest in becoming "organized" or
having a quasi-official group to represent us as letterboxers. I'd
really prefer that the DNR didn't even know about letterboxing. We'd
rather take it as it comes, adapt to conditions and keep it fairly
low-key and secretive. Maybe this is wishful thinking, but it seems
that as soon as things get organized it's not long before more rules
and regulations start appearing...

This list, the national list, and contact the placer features seem to
work quite well in communicating items of general interest. If state
or local issues arise they could be posted and rather than having
a "single voice" representing an organized group, it might be more
effective to have lots of grass-roots voices contacting the officials
in question if it is necessary.

There are lots of sites that provide good info for new people
(AtlasQuest, letterboxing.info, LBNA and the new boxers list). I'm
not sure we need another one for WI. Those that are more "serious"
about letterboxing will find the existing material. Not sure what
other content might be included on a WI website, but I still prefer
to avoid a lot of fragmentation or duplication with other sites.

If a WI patch was created, we'd probably want one. However we're
satisfied with the identification we have with the regional group.
The only state that I know of that has their own patch is Texas (due
to its size, I think it's comparable to a region!), all the rest are
regional patches. However maybe a trend is starting toward having
separate patches.

Re: Geocachers. We've been lucky not to have had any problems. A
few have logged into our boxes, but not taken anything. One cacher
found my Possum box in IL and contacted me to let me know it wasn't
hidden very well anymore and offered to go back to the park to cover
it better if I wanted him to. I think some cachers have switched over
to letterboxing based on having found some LBs and then learning more
about it. I do think it is a good idea to put a "This is NOT a
Geocache" label on your box as an extra precaution.

Our 2 cents,

Wisconsin Hiker & Martini Man

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-11-08 12:33:31 UTC-05:00
I am not from WI so I have just been watching from the sidelines (unless Milwaukee wants to claim MI also). But I would have to say that I agree with the things WI Hiker has stated regarding "less contact with the DNR is better" and also in regards to WI (and the other states in the Great Lakes region) remaining a part of a regional group. I think taking a more organized approach to letterboxing would be contrary to the nature of the game and would significantly impact the way we play and the freedoms we currently enjoy. If anything I think the answer is for more clandestine modes of play as it is my feeling that a majority of the issues that letterboxing faces today are due to over-exposure and easy accessibility to clues and letterboxes.

Down the road, depending on DNR and other land usage policies and increases in the number of letterboxers, I may feel differently.

SpringChick

From: duncan53211 (duncan53211@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-11-09 01:41:03 UTC
Well, I had a nice long reply to WI Hiker's post, but Yahoo deleted
it in the editing stage before I got to send it. Guess it wasn't
meant to be.

Since I don't have time to re-type it now (let alone write it in the
first place), I'll just point out that I agree to most of what WI
Hiker, Martini Man, and SpringChick posted. Those were the same
sentiments that I was initially trying to send in my contact to the
geocachers. I took it out because it didn't sound pleasant and was
more information than a first contact needed to have. Besides, I'll
take a "very professional sounding" critique anyday!

It seems the geocachers should be made aware that we would prefer to
stay under the radar and not to have their policy be applied to us.
(I'm not trying to speak for the group, I'm just stating what I've
gleaned from what people on this site have said.) My concern is
that in every situation I've seen, geocaching policy has become
letterboxing policy, whether the letterboxers agree or not, and
whether the policy says letterboxing or not. The geocachers are
looking to limit the number of boxes, put a time limit on boxes, and
assess a fee to plant a box (sound familiar?). Do we want these
policies to be forced on us without a say when we have the chance?
Can we make sure that these are only applied to geocaching and not
to letterboxing? Can we get the WI sate parks to be more like the
CT state parks (oh, if only we could!)? I don't know! What are we
going to do? At least let's come to a concensus on direction. We
can also keep tabs on what policy they are working on, even if just
observing under the radar.

I still can see the need for a WI letterboxing webpage. I envision
lots of helpful information not only for outsiders looking for info,
but also for WI newbies and for letterboxers traveling to WI. We
don't need to re-invent the other established pages that are out
there, but there are some unique aspects to letterboxing in WI that
are helpful to know, just like there are for MI, IL, OH, etc. BTW,
if you google "wisconsin letterboxers", you don't find anything
useful. If the DNR did that to contact us, they'd give up.
Outsiders wouldn't know there is a "contact the placer" on LBNA. (I
should also mention that the "contact the cacher" on the geocaching
site is difficult to find, which is why I couldn't find a way to
contact the stamp "thief" directly until SpringChick gave me the
direct link.) Shouldn't we at least be googleable? (New word?):-)

Actually, the preceding paragraph might be best solved in this
group! Maybe each state could note some letterboxing quirks that
are unique to the state, and we could collectively do it for the
region, so letterboxers from outside the region and newbies can know
of the quirks right away. I'm thinking of things like 1 pace = 1
step in the GL region (mostly), bring inking pens - we have colorful
stamps!, bring rags to clean off our colorful stamps!, etc. If we
then put it in the files section with a proper heading, it should be
able to show up on a google search as desired. Just a thought.

Anyway, time to go pack to go to Boston!
Dragon
