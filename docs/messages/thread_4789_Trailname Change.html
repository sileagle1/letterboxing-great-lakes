From: (Mathw613@aol.com) | Date: 2008-02-17 21:09:24 UTC-05:00
About 12 years ago I worked at NAPA Auto-parts.

The city of Chicago was trying to save some money, so they would set out
empty cop cars to sit in the middle of the interstate at various points. This
saved the city from paying a guy to sit in the car.

Problem was the cop cars they put out there stayed in the same spot, without
so much as a dummy sitting in the driver seat (so when you drove by you saw
the cop car was empty). After a month or two, people were speedy by these
empty squad cars as if they weren't there at all.

Every day it was one of my jobs to drive from Libertyville down to
Naperville to the NAPA warehouse.

One day one of my co-workers came to work laughing. After asking why he said
that he bought a box of Dunkin Donuts and placed it on the hood of the
squad. He claimed that he took out only one donut and left 11 in the box.

Well, that day on my way down to Naperville I had to laugh when I passed
that cop car with a box of donuts on the hood. As I remember it that box of
donuts staid there a week or more before it (and the squad car) were moved
altogether.
