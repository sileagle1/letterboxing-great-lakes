Boxacon!

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2009-10-03 23:14:08 UTC-05:00
What a fun event today!! Can you believe it? - The rain held off until
early evening and then it was only a very light sprinkle. That was a very
good thing since there seemed to be an infinite number of boxes to find! We
only managed to find about half of them today, but are definitely planning a
return trip (or two) to hunt for the rest. We think there were probably
about 65 boxes out there today!! Aimlesst and her crew did an excellent job
of planning & planting. It was obvious they put a lot of thought into
creating the clues and placing the boxes. The clues have piqued our
interest in several books we haven't read yet. Many thanks also to those
that contributed boxes to the event. We're glad to see that many of the
local boxers planted boxes themselves so aimlesst also has quite a few to
find in addition to the many she planted.



There was an amazing amount of food at the event and quite a variety. Lots
of main dishes and side salads as well as appetizers and desserts. BBQ pork
sandwiches, chili, soup, meatballs, sweet potato meatloaf, baked beans,
pasta salads, cheese, marinated mushrooms, hot apple cider etc, etc. We
certainly couldn't even hope to taste everything since there were so MANY
things. By the way, who made those yummy brownies? Here again, aimlesst
was very organized with plenty of serving spoons and anything else that was
needed.



It was great to see so many old LB friends from past events and ALSO to see
so many newer boxers. And the truly newest was 2.5 week old baby Henry (if
I remember correctly his trail name is Two Square, a funny twist on his
birth date). The littlest Christmas Elf also made an appearance Friday
evening and was tooling around in her stroller on the trail today w/mom &
dad. She'll be celebrating her 1st birthday later this month. We did
several exchanges with people we hadn't met before, but as usual, there were
plenty of people we missed. It was nice to put some faces to the stamps
we've seen in logbooks or names we've seen posting on this board.



We also had a fun time chatting with folks at both the pre- and post- event
dinners last night and tonight (18 people each night). It's always nice to
have some extra time to chat since the event itself is usually so hectic
with so much to do.



A huge thanks to aimlesst & her friends & family who helped create such a
nice event!!



Wisconsin Hiker & Martini Man

From: Sara Lancaster (sr_lancaster@yahoo.com) | Date: 2009-10-04 09:22:13 UTC-07:00
We couldn't stop talking about the event the entire 45-minute drive home. We always look forward to seeing our letterboxing friends--and meeting new ones--at these events, and Boxacon was no exception.

Our families tease us when they hear we're going to a "gathering" (they must think we're wearing costumes and chanting around flames or something equally as odd). We simply tell themthe gatherings arelike a big family reunion picnic...with the purpose of finding Tupperware containers in the woods. (Ok, I guess thatmight sound kind ofodd a nonletterboxer.) It's always a blast to see everyone, share stories from the trail and enjoy a day a fun, hikingand solving clues.

Many thanks to aimlesst for a fabulously organized and fundayand to everyone who created boxes for the event!

Leapin' Lizards

P.S. We'll be leaving our Lost Seriesin place at least through the month of October if you didn't have a chance to find them yesterday.

From: FungusWoman (krank@ameritech.net) | Date: 2009-10-04 23:23:05 UTC
Did you ever see a grown woman cry over a stick? Thanks to all of you who went searching for my lost (on the LOST train, nonetheless) hiking stick, especially HowD Girl, Lampwick, Diana Dolphin, and Red Sea Biscuit who double tracked four boxes with me looking for it (ever try finding a stick amongst a forest full of them?), but especially to WORMY WANDERERS who found it for me! As I was explaining to some people, it's very sentimental to me. DESTINY CALLED when we saw Julie and her group approaching with it in hand. Even Tiptoe called me to let me know rumor had it that WW had it in their possession. What a great group letterboxers are!

But more importantly, thank you to aimlesst for spearheading this wonderful event, to Library Grrl, and others who assisted. So many boxes, so little time! I only wish we could have stayed longer or returned today.

The food was wonderful (need sweet potato meatloaf recipe!) Thank you to everyone who donated batteries for the troop. Including a donation mailed in by TrailTracker and anonymous donation in the box, we have enough to donate 170 batteries!

I have plans for the Twilight Zone series. Keep your fingers crossed that the person I need to contact will be amenable to the idea.

We couldn't have said it any better - wonderful day at Boxacon! Aimlesst & company did a fantastic job providing for a relaxing, well organized, fun-filled letterboxing day!
