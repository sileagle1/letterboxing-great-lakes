Great Lakes Spring Cleaning

From: Duncan53211 (duncan53211@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-03-28 14:46:29 UTC
I was going to propose this to just the WI crowd, but since it seems
to be happening in MI too, I'll suggest it here.

With the weather getting better and vacations being planned, there
might be some letterboxers from other parts of the country planning
trips to our states to box. I encourage all letterboxers in this
area to set April 30 as a goal to complete spring cleaning and
checking on all of their boxes. I think it is also extrememly
helpful to include in the clues at the begining when the box was
last checked and repaired so a person from out of state knows that a
box is truly "active". (Or do it like I do on the page that lists
all of my boxes.)

If you have boxes too far away for you to check by then, I'd suggest
that others in the area be asked to check on them for you. This
might not take care of ALL of the great boxes we have in this area,
but it should tackle most of them.

Oh! And Matt - please replace Chicago Underground please! It is
such a neat box! Maybe make it a series? Please, please, oh pretty
please! ;-)

Dragon


Re: Great Lakes Spring Cleaning

From: Deb (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-03-29 00:30:13 UTC
Dragon, I think this is a fabulous idea! I was out again today --
our power was out most of the day, and since there is not much you
can do at home without power (except carve stamps), I figured I may
as well run out and replace Ghosts Below and replentish one of the
log books that I found down to only 3 remaining pages yesterday.

I'm sure I can manage to have all of my West Michigan boxes checked
by then, and also will do a day trip up to Traverse City to check on
mine and Esperanza's boxes there -- I can also check on the two 45th
Parallel boxes that Esperanza and Matt the Rat have in place in the
Traverse City area.

I will not be able to check in on my U.P. boxes by then though. I
am really hoping the gathering doesn't end up being on Memorial Day
Saturday because with the extra day, it would be the perfect time to
take a weekend trip to the U.P. A regular weekend isn't really
enough time.

Tom and Jill, I think you mentioned you would be heading up there
soon -- Easter weekend perhaps? I'm sure you will be busy while up
there, but if you have the time and you could check on any of the
boxes along your route (mine or Esperanza's) I would really
appreciate it. That would save me from having to do the U.S. 2
boxes and I could just concentrate on the Lake Superior boxes when I
go. Please don't go out of your way and if you don't end up having
a chance -- don't sweat it, I will get to them.

So let's get out there and get our boxes in shape!

SpringChick

From: Tom & Jill (tj_mich@ameritech.net) | Date: 2004-03-29 06:48:24 UTC-05:00
Sure, we'll be going right past some of the US-2 boxes, including Mighty
Mac, Top O' The Pickle, Seul Choix Point Light, and Kitch-Iti-Kipi, so
we'll do our best to check on them during Easter weekend.

We're also planning to remove our 3 boxes at J.W.Wells State Park along
Green Bay. The park administrator has offered to let us make them into
"virtual caches," in which finders hunt for some existing place in the
park, but there is no physical box to find there. She would keep the
stamps at the campground office, open any day between 7am and 9pm, so
people could stop by after the "hunt" and stamp in. We may take her up
on that offer with at least 1 of the boxes, mainly because we're curious
to see how it might work out. This will require us to fill out the
usage permit, but no charge.

--Tom
