From: golfgalnopar (golfgalnopar@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-10-08 04:41:11 UTC


Wow, almost more info than I can handle as a newbie but....it sure
has given me some ideas. The stamp logbook that we use to collect
the stamp images that we find is one that I made from a notepad with
a teacher/education design around the edges. We all thought it fit
since our group is retired teachers. (Can you picture us stumbling
around in the woods?) Anyway, I used a binder machine from school
and then put a very thin piece of birch bark that we found on our
first letterboxing excursion on the front and back covers to decorate
it. We call it our "Stalking the Wild Letterbox with the
WonderBroads" book. lol Maybe we will do one something like that for
our first letterbox. So, thanks for all the input to my little
question about how to make a log book. I haven't had this much
milage from a question since I left the classroom. :) I think i
like this group !!!!!

Carol

From: Steve and Koreen (col.one923@spwl.net) | Date: 2004-10-08 00:16:16 UTC-05:00
I've been reading and enjoying this group, but haven't introduced myself
yet. I joined a week ago or so, but we have been doing a little
letterboxing for a year now. We are the Puddlejumpers, if any of you
have seen our trail name around anywhere. We "got our feet wet" in
letterboxing last Fall, but didn't come up with our Trail name until
this past Winter and finally got our "family stamp" carved. We kept
busy all summer, but Fall hits and we get itching to hike, so we headed
out to Letterbox and got my brother, KDkadiddlehopper, involved too. We
have planted a few boxes this Fall and our first one, which is very easy
to find by the way, will explain how we came up with our trail name. We
are a family of 5 and the kids are Jacy, soon to be 9, Josh 6 and Jon
just turned 4. It is a family event each time we letterbox and the kids
already have some great stories to tell. Moofie and Blaze, your Pioneer
Village box is their all time favorite so far and they would love to
tell about it sometime. Maybe at the WI Fall gathering if there is to
be one, in Nov.? We would love to try to join in and meet some of you.

Now having said all that, the real reason I posted was to ask Carol if
she happened to be part of the same group of retired teachers we met
while camping at Devil's Lake, WI in September? They were a great bunch
of ladies and it sounds like you would fit in with them wonderfully.
hummmm? Must be something about retired teachers!

Koreen
Puddlejumpers

Thanks for the compliment but we are not the same bunch of retirees.
I'm glad you enjoyed the company of the Wi. retired group however.
All the retired teachers that I have met around the country seem to
be really enjoying themselves. I can tell you that since I retired
(25 of us busted out at the same time) we have been having a blast.
The half dozen of us that make up the "WonderBroads of Grand Ledge"
letterboxing gang are constantly busy doing some fun thing and even
started a Red Hat Society chapter of the same name. Hey, I think
there is a stamp idea in there somewhere. Anyway, we are hoping to
get in a LOT more letterboxing before the snow falls.
