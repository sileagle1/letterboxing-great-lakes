Fall Folk Festival

From: (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2015-10-03 20:45:45 UTC-07:00

Martini Man & I were busy today planting for the Fall Folk Festival event on Saturday OCT 17 - only 2 weeks away!  


Thanks to many contributions by our regional letterboxing friends, there will be 45+ boxes out on the trails. But the hiking distances won't be too long, since there are only 5-6 miles of trails in the park.  Of special note will be the FIRST EVER stamp carved by long-time boxer Tonto.  Don't miss finding Tonto's Tulip! Also of special note are boxes carved by some of the younger letterboxers:  Miss Mo, The Nickerboxer and Samtrack.  We are happy to see new carvers joining the ranks!


There will be plenty of boxes to find and we still have enough capacity at the lodge for a few more attendees, so if your schedule allows, please sign-up to join us at the event.  Since our theme is "Folk Festival" which celebrates many countries around the world, we're asking that all food for the potluck be representative of a certain country.  Just email me to let me know what you'll be bringing and what country it represents.


Now that it is getting closer to the event, I encourage you to look at the event website, particularly the Checklist page and the FAQ page.  Please note that unfortunately the city of West Bend does NOT allow dogs in any of their parks.  [I don't know what's wrong with them!].


Reminder:  You can also bring your Bingo Booty to the event if you want to save mailing costs and get it done before the hectic holidays arrive.  You can also bring any boxes you would like to give to ggghiker for the Fall into Fun event on November 7.


We're looking forward to seeing many of you in 2 weeks!

Fall Folk Festival

From: (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2015-10-18 18:43:47 UTC-07:00

A big THANK YOU to all of you that attended the Fall Folk Festival yesterday!  So many of you contributed great boxes for the event and everyone really got into the spirit of the theme for the potluck.  I think it was the BEST food I have ever had at an event!!  We were also happy to see so many people willing to hit the trails to hunt for the boxes.  It was a bit chilly, but several people told me they were comfortable once they started hiking and the temperatures climbed a little bit.  We were impressed that some people were able to find all the boxes in one day!  We think the total mileage was in the 5-6 mile range, but some have claimed 9-10 miles based on their pedometers.


Since Martini Man & I were hanging out in the lodge all day, I'd appreciate any photos you could send.  I can see the nice photos that people (Sophie & Me, Leapin' Lizards, Pacerkbackers, ggghiker) have already posted on Facebook, but if you post photos somewhere else, let me know where to find them (or email them to me).  If anyone wants to find us on Facebook, you can find us as Wis Hiker and Martini Man.


If anyone is wondering about the name of a bonus box, email me and I can send you the official name if you describe the image.  [There were 5 bonus boxes]  


In case you are wondering how many boxes were available: there were at least 59 out on the trail and at least 5 in the lodge.]


For those that want to post clues publicly for a box that will be staying at the park, I always prefer that people list their own boxes if they carved the stamp. I will put a copy of the clue packet in the Files section of the Great Lakes board tomorrow for anyone that couldn't make it to the event. That way they will be able to see the clues in the "loop" format. People that created boxes could use the electronic file to construct individual clues for their permanent boxes, or they can wait for me to do that in the next few weeks. Right now each box is clued based on the preceding box, rather than a common starting point such as the lodge.  If people are in a hurry to post individual clues, they can construct them based on what was in the clue packet. Otherwise they can wait for me to email them some verbiage, but that may not be until the end of the month.


I received 4 Bingo Booty submissions yesterday.  For anyone else that is interested in playing the slowest BINGO game ever, you can bring your Booty and $2 fee, to the "FrightFest in the Forest " event in Michigan on OCT 24 or to the "Fall Into Fun" event in Illinois on NOV 7.  You can save mailing costs AND avoid the hectic holiday rush by giving Bingo stuff to me at an event.  The game will begin on January 1, 2016.


3 events in 4 weeks - we are certainly a busy letterboxing region this fall!
