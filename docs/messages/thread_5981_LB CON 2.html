From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2009-07-25 16:00:38 UTC
Some of you may have already seen this on another board, but Miss Moon gave me permission to cross-post it. I think she has some very valid concerns.

====================
This is in no way a put down of LBCon or a comparison with L & B but concern with publicity. There have been some discussions lately of the growing concern of National Parks having discussions about the "problems" of geocaching and letterboxing.

In years past before the previously mentioned article on Family magazine, before other spats of publicity there has been about boxing, before scout trooops giving out patches, before mentions on the Today show, before all the other publicity that has brought so many more people into the hobby, this wasn't a problem, or at least certainly not to the extent it seems to be happening now. Boxing was smaller, much more lowkey, and the people who found out about it usually did so by WOM or by wandering by accident onto a web site, first just lbna and now both lbna and AQ.

Please remember that with National "Conventions" and clubs bidding on the chance to hold these conventions comes more and more publicity. More publicity means more concerns from Park Officials, both on a national, local and state level. Making letterboxing more mainstream doesn't guarantee an embracing of the hobby by officials concerned with the land they are striving to protect and preserve.

It has always been traditional to not plant in National Parks anyway but in other woodland areas of different kinds, both local and state. With publicity comes more regulations and even the potential banning of boxing in many of these places where we now plant in peace.

I would just ask everyone to please give this some thought and remember that sometimes bigger is not always better. I love this hobby and hope to be doing it unfettered by an over abundance of official rules and regulations for many years to come. I know that many of us feel the same way.

It's a very special and unique hobby that I would like to see treated with the same kind of concern and respect that we give to the places where we plant. I hope it can continue to flourish without becoming a monster that must be stepped on by officials because of their fear of it.

Once again, this is not a put down of LBCon or the people who worked to make it happen. So please don't put words in my mouth and flame me for loving the hobby enough to speak out and speak my mind. I voice my concerns because I love letterboxing.

Missmoon

From: (Mathw613@aol.com) | Date: 2009-07-27 22:51:02 UTC-04:00
The Con, like Letterboxing, was what you made it out to be.

After my first gathering (SILO 1) when I had been in the hobby for only 3
months, I wanted to get EVERY stamp (exchanges, PT's, event stamps, cooties,
etc). I kept looking over at my daughter Skeeter (who was 5 at the time)
sitting by herself at a table. I ignored her in order to get *every* stamp.

After SILO 1, I would flip through my logbook to look at the stamp images I
had amassed and realized I only really referred back to and "studied" the
images of the stamps I had collected out in the wild & the exchanges (as
they are like autographs). I hardly ever look to the pages containing the
BEAUTIFULLY detailed event stamps!

I decided at that point that all future gatherings I would only exchange --
no PT's, no Cooties, no Event stamps (other than the 1 main event stamp --
saying I had been there).

The GLG gathering in Afton S.P. I didn't find 1 event box . . . I was to
busy exchanging and meeting people. However, after the GLG Gather we did find
a few boxes that had been in the wild for a few years.

So, with that rather lengthy background, you have an idea of where I was
coming from as I drove to St. Louis. Exchange Exchange Exchange . . . there
were boxers from coast to coast, many of whom I may never get to meet again.
Of the near 300 signed up I only met about a dozen previous -- I'd say
about half the number actually showed up, perhaps a tad closer to 200. Either
way, when it was all said and done, I had only met about 10 people before
and ended up exchanging with close to 150 more boxers.

Shorty & I found a few boxes on the way to St. Louis and met up with
Familyof6 in Nauvoo. I planted a box in Alton, IL (still to be listed) so before
we hit St. Louis my count was P1 F2 X1 (not thrilling #'s I know). We got
to the campus after 10:00pm and found 4 Little Piggies from Tennessee.

They let us into the dorms (we weren't scheduled to check in until the next
day), before the night was up we met up with Camp Fire Lady, Ryan &
Amanda, Kreepy Kelli and several other boxers. We exchanged with another 15
folks.

Friday Shorty & I went to Queeny Park . . . where the Queen's ruled and we
only found 5 boxes(and hiked 5 1/2 miles) :-(

Then we went to Fairy Princess Fiona's house (she lived across the street
and down a block from Queeny Park) for an Open House where we had snacks and
met more boxers then we went to the Doom & Pain Pajama Party -- this
precursor to the main Con was instrumental in getting exchanges out of the way.
After Friday night I had exchanged with over 100 people!!

I kindly turned down PT's with only a couple sentimental exceptions. And
Cooties where flicked off my back as they came (truth be told, I only got 1
Cootie all weekend and it was one that Wassa left on my bed. Ironically
enough I was the first person to ever stamp into the cootie that was launched
at SILO 1 by Brat Pack IL!!!)

The "Main Event" had vendor tables set up where you could buy any color of
ink you could imagine from several different companies. There were markers,
PZ Kut, ink re-fills, carving tools and BEAUTIFUL logbooks created by Camp
Fire Lady (I highly recommend you all buy one) all for sale!

There were several workshops on how to make your own logbooks, how to
cipher and decipher clues (by KuKu), how to carve detailed stamps (by Raqs), how
to age paper (by Lnd-Crzr). There was a HH contest and a Logbook swap.

As I got nearly every exchange I could by mid-day Saturday I decided to
find some Event boxes hidden out for the event (it was either that or just sit
on my duff and do nothing) we found 5 AWESOME Lnd-Crzr boxes. That evening
there was night boxing planned. 6 boxes hidden on campus and 9 boxes in a
cemetery.

I've never been night boxing before, and it was different for sure. There
were clues, however, they placed bicycle reflectors near the boxes location
so you could see them with your flashlight (don't know if this is normal or
not).

Shorty, Kreepy Kelli & I spent the majority of our evening (3 hours)
laying in the woods and ambushing people as they walked by. The passerby's were
spread out enough where the next group to walk by us didn't hear the scream
from the group in front of them, so we got everyone good. . . even had two
people wet themselves (that was reward enough!).

That night we staid up until about 3 in the morning just sitting, chatting
& laughing together. Sharing stories and tales . . . it was so nice to have
the time to get to know these wonderful people outside the 'sport' of
letterboxing.

The next day was a bit slower pace as event boxes were being pulled and
people started leaving. A large group of us went out to lunch and really
enjoyed each others company and the 14 of us carved a single stamp and hid a box
right there in the restaurant
_http://www.atlasquest.com/showinfo.html?gBoxId=121150_ (http://www.atlasquest.com/showinfo.html?gBoxId=121150)

I hadn't been so sleep deprived in years . . . every night was a 2 - 4 in
the morning bedtime. So, Sunday, in preparation of the drive home I slept
from 4 - 9 in the evening. Aside from being woken up an hour into my nap --
and being ambushed with ink pads!!!! I woke up and packed up (check out time
was anytime before midnight).

Shorty and I had planed on hitting some boxes on the ride home. Our luck
turned sour on box #1 at 10:30 p.m when a nice officer who hailed from Scott
County Iowa (right across the boarder from where I live) turned his lights
on and put a spot light on us. :-(

We had to leave with a warning. Shorty fell asleep soon after and so I
drove him all the way home without stopping for another box.

I, personally, really loved the convention. Sure, it wasn't an outdoor
gathering . . . but neither was SILO 1 or a Letterboxers Wedding. I've heard of
workshops (how to make logbooks or how to carve a stamp) in other
gatherings just like the Con. And, as I try to only exchange with everyone (and if
successful then I hit the trails) I was in 7th Heaven meeting SO many
awesome boxers from so many different states!!

I would do it all over again! In a heart beat.

hx6

As Miss Moon pointed out, one concern is publicity. Big national
"conventions" tend to get publicity. Apparently this was even organized and
planned for by the CONVENTION Committee (unlike a large state event we
attended 5 years ago, where the organizers specifically turned away requests
for publicity). One post I saw said that "there was supposed to be a media
person covering the Con. Arrangements had been made for them to have a
'press pass'." For a hobby that has its roots in secrecy and discretion,
this is a different path.



The same thing can be said for many other rubberstamping activities that are
using the word "letterbox". Many have more in common with scrapbooking or
other artistic activities, rather than clues & hikes.
