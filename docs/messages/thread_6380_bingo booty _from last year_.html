With our recent winter weather, I've finally had the chance to use one of my
bingo prizes from last year's competition - a hand knit hat in shades of red
and grey. I have no idea who made it but I wanted to say thank you! Not

my other winter hiking gear (what awesome powers of intuition Spring Chick
must have). So thanks!

And to every one playing this year, think what you have to look forward to
in your booty!

> With our recent winter weather, I've finally had the chance to use one of my
> bingo prizes from last year's competition -

Hard to believe (since I've done so poorly in Bingo lately), but I did win some nice booty a few years ago. Most of which I put to letterboxing use, but we did have an odd use for one of the prizes this Christmas. Someone put in teeny-tiny ziploc bags, which have been great for HH and microboxes. But that wasn't the unusual use.

Every year,for Christmas, we find out how a different country celebrates Christmas and incorporate some of their foods or decorations or traditions into our Christmas--esp. when we celebrate with one group of friends. We hadn't done anything from South America yet, so we picked Columbia (and got a lot of jokes from our friends about a cocaine Christmas). For one of our gifts to our friends, I filled those tiny zip-loc bags with powdered sugar as their special Columbian gift.

If we were able to come to the Olympics (which we can't), I was going to pick Columbia as our country and have a few "samples" out at our table.
