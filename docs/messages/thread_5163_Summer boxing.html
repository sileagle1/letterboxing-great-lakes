Summer boxing

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2008-07-07 06:45:37 UTC
Wow! Just got back from a big loop in northern WI to find some of
the newer boxes up that way. We had some nice hikes and found some
nice stamps (more details & photos should be on our blog soon).
However one thing that was NOT so nice were all the mosquitoes and
flies!! Yes, one of the big downfalls of summer boxing in a "wet"
year is a LOT of bugs...

Another NICE thing was to get "Contact the Placer" messages (or at
least a status log) on 18 of our boxes during the past week. It's
obvious that people are hot on the hunt in many states, since we
received reports from CA, CO, MN, IL, and WI.

Unfortunately we also received a few "Attempted" logs this week from
a newer boxer, without any details. However one of these has already
been confirmed "Found" by a more experienced boxer. The same thing
happened with two other boxes in the last few months, with other new
letterboxers logging a box as "Attempted", but other boxers
subsequently finding the box.

For any new boxers on this board, a general guideline is to avoid
marking a box as "Attemped" unless you have already contacted the
placer about where you looked to determine whether you were in the
right spot. The placer may then wish to check on the box to see if it
is still in place or not, and may then let you know. Some people
regard an "Attempted" status as an indication that the box is
missing, so it should not be used lightly. Luckily more experienced
boxers know to take this status with a grain of salt, or as an
additional challenge.

Happy hunting to everyone and DON'T FORGET YOUR BUG SPRAY!!!

Wisconsin Hiker
