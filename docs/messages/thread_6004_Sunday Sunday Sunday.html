Sunday Sunday Sunday

From: (Mathw613@aol.com) | Date: 2009-08-04 22:17:38 UTC-04:00

Sunday was a wonderful day!!

Saturday night Becki suggested we head up to Rockford for some letterboxing
-- isn't she the best!! :-)

So, in a flurry I printed up clues to several new boxes in the
Rockford/State Line area.

Our first stop was a series of 3 boxes based on Arcades of the 1980's --
talk about memories! Walking to the first box, I notice a man at the tree
line right where the first box was. Hmm, he had a backpack on . . . could it
be, only the second or third time since we've been boxing, running into
other boxers on the trail?

By the time we got to the first box, the couple was well on their way to
box #2. I didn't want to shout anything, what if they weren't boxers to begin
with? So, I hurriedly opened the logbook..... Tat2Bob & Kimberly!! I
ditched Beciki & the kids and went running to catch up.

It was the first time we had met Bob & Kimberly, been keeping up on their
exploits for nearly a year now. So what a thrill it was to finally meet
them.

We went back to box #1 & finished the series together. Talking and sharing
tales along the way. It was so refreshing to meet boxers just as crazy as
I am -- I mean that in the kindest way possible Bob & Kimberly :-)

We went our separate ways.... Tat2Bob & Kimberly to their next box.... us
to the McD's drive through.

We then hit the trails again and found a few more nice boxes by
SweetEllGee. And then, who do we run into again? Yup, you know it.

We attempted a box with our friends and then went on to a very nice spot on
the river front where we found another. The kids loved the cool playground
there and we enjoyed visiting some more with Tat2Bob & Kimberly.

After this, we went our separate ways again. We headed to the Wisconsin
Welcome Center Rest Area and found a couple boxes there...... and I was first
finder of a Geocache that was just placed the day before -- I didn't write
my name or exchange any junk. I warned Pastors Wife of her box.... I hope
the Geocachers don't take the awesome stamp carved by Krait!!!

Then we hit the Illinois Welcome Center Rest Area for another box.

We then found 2 great boxes located near a stone bridge built in 1882 . . .
very impressive. Without the boxes, we never would have visited that site.
We ended our day at dusk with one more find and a playground to burn off
the last of the kids energy before the long drive home.

We pulled into the driveway at 11:30 that night and went straight to bed. I
am still recovering from the day -- but was it worth it!! We found 12
boxes and met Tat2Bob & Kimberly -- all in all, it doesn't get much better
than that!!!!

hx6
