In light of the recent postings about "being caught with your hand in
the cookie jar", I thought I'd share something I came across while
looking up clues.

These comments were written by Bindle Babe and included at the bottom of
a set of clues she posted. Since I am giving credit where credit is
due, BB, I hope you do not mind me reposting them here. I think they
are very important for all of us, both new and experienced letterboxers
to keep in mind.

--klk/FungusWoman

And I quote:

"ATTENTION NEW, OR BEGINNING LETTERBOXERS:
Before you begin please consider the effort that goes
into carving a stamp, maybe even hand making a logbook,
and then to find a secure spot to hide the box in the
vicinity of a certain area or site. All of that effort
spent for no other reason that to bring some enjoyment
into the world.

Be aware of your surroundings; enjoy the view from where
you are. Letterboxes are often place in areas for a
certain reason, spend a moment to consider why the placer
may have chosen to share this area with others, by drawing
them here with the lure of a letterbox find.

Please! Survey the area for people who may be watching
what you're doing. Remember, a common activity is
"people watching"... someone may be watching you! Please
don't give away the hiding spot to others who might not
share the appreciation of this activity. If you will use
care when you replace the box in it's hiding space, you
can be sure you won't contribute to a box being lost,
stolen, or destroyed.

Consider that there would be no letterboxing, if there
were no boxes to find. Missing boxes not only deprive
other boxers of the same sense of enjoyment you just
experienced, but the person who created the box has lost
all the time they spent and sadly, may someday choose
to stop placing boxes."
