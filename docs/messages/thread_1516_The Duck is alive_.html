The Duck is alive!

From: Doodle & Deedle Bug (doodle_n_deedle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-06-27 19:50:12 UTC-07:00
Well, as alive as a rubber stamp of a cartoon duck can
be. We checked on our Duck Tape box this past
Saturday, and he was still where we planted him. It
was interesting reading the comments in the log book.
A few people reported that the ground was swampy
during their searches...it's always been pretty solid
for us. Guess we luck out when we go there. The box
was last found on June 4th, and I think those finders
were newbies, because they wrote how hard the clues
were. Shaw, they should try some of SpringChick's
boxes and then whine! :)

We then headed out to Port Clinton, and in the
sweltering heat found Hazard. I really like the way
those clues were put together, making you use
landmarks in order to determine the numbers you needed
to then find the box. We then found Rest In Peace.
This box was hidden in a cemetery. Now, I do
genealogy, and I come from a line of long,
unpronounceable Eastern Eurpoean surnames. So imagine
my surprise when about 4 graves away from one of the
graves listed as a landmark to where the box is
hidden, I see a grave with my mother's maiden name on
it! So of course I copied down that info to determine
if they're more long lost relatives. That's the
problem with geneaology - just as you're trying to
finish determining the link between you & one of your
relatives, 5 more relatives appear in your research.

That's all for now...

Doodle Bug.
