Sunday afternoon in Plymouth, IN

From: Pale Puppy Posse (advantagek9@voyager.net) | Date: 2006-03-27 12:14:24 UTC-05:00
Naughty dog and I had a job to do on Sunday in Plymouth, IN.
Thanks to Armadillo Jo, two bentriders, Flagbearer and Full of Heart we
managed to have a great day letterboxing as well. Here is the scenerio:

First, Potato Creek.... fun little park, kind of expense for out of town
people, $7 for a one day pass! It also seems that two bentriders have moved
to NY and are asking someone to adopt this box. Did the list know this? Has
someone adopted it yet? just FYI as I don't remember reading about it.

Then we had lunch at the Schoop's burger joint which is next to Centennial
Park. We first did Plymouth Rocks! a three box series. Box one is doing
well, box two has a destroyed log book - totally soaked and box three was
not to be found. Hoosier hysteria and My heart of heart are both well.
This park is WAY bigger than it looks!

Off to Magnetic park and Bird park. We were pulled to the magnet without
hesitation but seems the resident of Bird park might have flown the coupe.
Very funny location as we started from both ends of the park.

Next to Oakhill cemetary... this place goes on forever! Lots of neat stones
to see. I was a little upset to see a woman walking her dog there...
Anyway.... Dem Bones series needs some surgery! Naughty dog got no "head"
and Pale puppy posse was minus her "left hand" man. You might want to
rethink your choice of boxes. The M & M containers seem to attract the
critters and the others are just not water tight. Many of the "pictures"
were soaked. Some gave all needs a new home as the box is trashed and
logbook soaked. Make a difference in the world was wonderful.. what a cool
stamp!! I was pleasantly surprised to find all these wonderful stamps hand
carved. With such tough subjects I thought for sure I would find purchased
stamps... ( I have a personal preference for the hand carved ones) So gold
stars to everyone in Plymouth, IN.

Let us know if you get a chance to check the missing ones as we will be
back this week some time.
We missed New beginnings and will hit that next time we are there. Great
day for boxing.. though can't decide if it was spring boxing or winter
boxing.. I prefer to think spring!
