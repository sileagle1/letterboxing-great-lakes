Nice Geocacher story & 3 Little Pigs update

From: (papeseed@tds.net) | Date: 2005-11-27 18:54:23 UTC-06:00
Today, when we returned home from the Twin Cities from Thanksgiving weekend, there was a message on our answering machine from a woman who identified herself as a geocacher. She said she was walking her dog down in the nature area where Three Little Pigs: House of Sticks is hidden, and she found the logbook just lying out in the open! I am lucky she was a geocacher and she recognized it as a logbook and took it home and dried it out. And then she looked me up in the phone book, left a message and offered to drop it off at my house tomorrow! She said she had never heard of letterboxing before until she posted a message on her geocaching board asking what to do with the logbook. Several people responded that it sounded like a letterboxing logbook, not geocaching.


Just thought I should spread the word about some friendly and helpful cachers! Also, don't go looking for the 3 Pigs House of Sticks box just yet...I'm going to have to go check on it and see if the rest of the box is even still there. --Papeseed

Well, Dragon, you better hope it was animals that got the box, because I got the logbook back today and YOU were the last one to sign in!! :-) heh heh heh. Seriously, when I saw that I knew it was probably a "noxer" miscreant who somehow found it by accident. I agree, I don't think an animal could have wedged it out of there.

To add to the nice geocacher story, not only was the logbook in my mailbox when I got home as promised, but the box and stamp itself were in between my front doors with this note on a napkin: "After I put your logbook in your mailbox, I went back out there with my dog to pick up garbage. Guess what I found! I don't know why I didn't see it before. It was laying in the marsh, open. I found the cover several feet away. Maybe an animal got into it??" I can't believe a total stranger was so nice as to deliver my poor box parts to my house...not once, but TWICE. And I can't believe my luck that THE SAME PERSON stumbled upon the scattered contents of my box at different times and places and KNEW THEY WENT TOGETHER!!! I feel like one LUCKY PIG today! :-)

BTW, it was the House of Straw that got blown down, not the House of Sticks like I first said. --Papeseed
