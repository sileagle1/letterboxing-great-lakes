MI - Muskegon Area

From: Deb (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-03-21 13:16:30 UTC
I was able to get out yesterday before Mother Nature shifted moods
and began spewing blasts of artic air and snow from the north, to
check on the Sandy Seasons boxes at Lake Harbor Park in Muskegon. I
knew the winter box was missing from previous reports and had carved
up a replacement stamp with the intention of planting the new box
yesterday.

Once there I was delighted to find the Winter box peacefully resting
in its place, which was a surprise since my visit a few weeks ago
had confirmed it was missing. Unfortunately though the box was
empty and the contents nowhere to be found (okay I admit, I didn't
look very hard -- it is a very steep hill ;-) Since the Sandy
Seasons series had kind of a color-coordinated thing going on
(really I am not usually so anal), I wanted to use the original
box. So I took it all back home -- the empty (very dirty) box and
the new box. I also picked up the Spring, Summer and Autumn boxes,
which were all in good shape. I will get them cleaned up and try to
have them all back in place within a week or two -- depends on the
weather.

While there, I also checked in on Arbo's Beach Box and found it in
good shape. Was surprised to see that although the logs of the
Sandy Seasons series were identical to one another as far as
visitors who had stamped in, a very different set of boxers had been
to the Beach Box.

Happy Boxing!

Deb
