I just finished a personal traveler this afternoon, so if you happen
to meet me on the trail on at a gathering, you have a chance to get
it.
One of my favorite series of books is the Sword of Truth series by
Terry Goodkind. The first book in the series is Wizard's First Rule.
The wizard's first rule gave me a good laugh the first time I came
across it, because I mumble the phrase a lot.
All you have to do to get this stamp is to come up and tell me what
the wizard's first rule is.

Also, because I know she doesn't get online EVER...Wiener Mouse has
one as well...how you get it depends on the situation. If you see her
on the trail, you can get it as her personal stamp. You just say..."I
am a knight of Ni and I request a shrubbery" and she will give you
one. If you are at a gathering with her, the stamp will be hidden IN
a shrubbery...so you will have to find out which one it is in.

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2005-01-17 22:08:10 UTC-06:00
Here's our personal approach, obviously not the only one possible:

When we have obtained an image of someone's personal traveler, we just
counted it as a "Miscellaneous" stamp (yes, an "M" count, as if we didn't
have enough categories already!). We use the miscellaneous category for
personal travelers, traveling event stamps, other stamps laying around at a
gathering, animal signature stamps, etc.

In other words, we count it as an "M" if it isn't:
- a stamp in a box with clues to follow (our "F" count and "P" count),
- the signature stamp of a human letterboxer (our "X" count),
- an official unique stamp made specifically for an event (our "E" count -
we only count one "E" per event we attend, if there are more event stamps we
count them as an "M". We like our "E" number to represent the number of
gatherings we have attended) or
- a hitchhiker (our "HH" count).

Wisconsin Hiker

From: aine_42 (aine_42@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-01-18 16:21:53 UTC

I've been counting personal travelers in my F count if there was some puzzle I had to solve
to get them, in the X count if the only prerequisite was knowing to ask for it and I've
already got the person's trail stamp.

(I like to make things complicated, I guess. *sigh*)

I guess technically they should all be part of an X count, though, as I didn't exchange my
stamp to get the traveler, and according to the FAQ on the LBNA site, "a letterbox can only
count as Found when the letterboxer obtains a stamp image from any stamp associated
with that box, and their personal stamp image has been imprinted (or attempted) in the
logbook or other part of the box."

Hmm. Looks like I'll start counting them all as X. (Does make life easier.)
