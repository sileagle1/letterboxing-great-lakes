Another trip "south" ...

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2006-03-13 03:50:53 UTC
We decided to head south to Illinois today to hunt a few of the boxes
we ran out of time for last weekend.

Started out at "Trolley Days" and said Hi! to a few fast bikers on
the trail. All was well with the box.

Next we headed over to find "It has class" (found) and "Valerie's
Victory Box" (not found). A good walk in the park though, even on a
fairly dreary day. We did see a bunch of robins! Then on to the
new "Catch a falling star" box which had a LOT of visitors yesterday.

Then a nice walk looking for "Squirreling Around" which we extended
by walking over to "Art in the Park". Very interesting sculptures!
Our favorite was the face on the big boulder made by an artist from
Iceland. We were disappointed we didn't run into River Rat who had
also signed in to the logbook today. As Martini Man said, "Rats! no
exchange..." We also enjoyed seeing so many kids having fun in the
farm area on our way back to the car AND we saw our first real
flowers of the year growing on the side of the Mercker cabin. A real
sign of spring!

Next a tricky retrieval for "Viva La Mexico" where we stamped in in
the car. Too bad we couldn't stay for margaritas, but with a long
drive home, we figured it wouldn't be a good idea...

Then another nice long walk in search of "What a Scream!", noting the
budding trees along our walk. We found the box and lo and behold - a
HITCHHIKER!

Last we were on the hunt for Sternennacht. As has already been
noted, box #2 seems to be missing. We believe its home is now the
burned and charred base of a tree. We'll have to make a return trip
when the blue box is available again...

Thanks again to all of you who hide boxes!!!

Wisconsin Hiker & Martini Man

P.S. We hope the "Symbols of the Big Ten" series is replanted - we'd
love to hunt them down on some future trip to Illinois!

I wish I would have met you guys and the trail also. I got an early start yesterday on my way back home to Freeport. I am kicking myself for not having all the clues for "What a Scream" I would have liked to get a hitchhicker, Nature Nerd and I went up to Glenview on Saturday and and found quite a few boxes and also went to Grosse Point Light, what a great view of Lake Michigan. We were spotted by a few bikers who knew what we were doing, I believe they were Honor Guard. Too bad they didn't have their stuff with them, no exchange but good conversation. I can't wait till this Saturday. This is when Nature Nerd, Hiker Babe and I are going to go Easter boxing. I hope for good weather and hopefully some exchanges.

River Rat
