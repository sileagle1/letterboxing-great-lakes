Better late than never!

I stumbled upon letterboxing via the internet in the summer of 2003 through a link at the
Carving Consortium. Like many, I needn't look further than the LbNA home page; I was
instantly addicted. The Smithsonian article seemed vaguely familiar, but I spent most of
the 90's confined to long hours in a small cell (cubicle, if you prefer.)

We've returned to our Hoosier roots, after living years in Chicagoland and God's Country,
Wisconsin. But when we left in 2000, there probably weren't many boxes to be found. It
took a lot of talking ... well, begging ... to convince the silent one (aka lutjanus apodus
indianensis) that this was something we BOTH could do for enjoyment. He has spread the
word at his workplaces and does a respectable and respectful explanation of the entire
hobby. His audience is mostly teachers, so it's not a tough sell. Many of them say things
like "ooh, that sounds so cultural." He responds, "you have the CULT part right." He
enjoys that. He doesn't complain and he keeps me from my stupidity in the woods. Only
bruises so far. But he draws the line at Gatherings. Not that we have cooties, but he
likens it to the GenCon in Indy. He's just not that social with total strangers. He is thrilled
that I have found other friends to go boxing with.

We moved from Indy to Richmond, IN in July. We still haven't sold the old place, so we are
staying closer to home. We're closer to the SW OHIO boxing mecca and I look forward to
getting to other parts of Ohio. And Michigan. And Pennsylvania. And Kentucky. And
back to Chicago and Wisconsin. Did I mention I'm addicted?

I have several boxes to plant and more to come next spring.

For the record, I have exhausted RUSH COUNTY for the 2nd time. There are no more
boxes at the present for me to find. Still have to hit some newer ones in Fayette County.

I have thoroughly enjoyed meeting many of you and look forward to future gatherings.

I could go on and on, but it is getting late.
