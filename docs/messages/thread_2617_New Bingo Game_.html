We were the ones who sent in the jello--it is a Bingo joke in my
family. When I was little, our extended family would always go to
my Aunt Evelyn's house for Thanksgiving. After the big meal, she
would always have Bingo for the kids to play--complete with wrapped
prizes. You would start out with good prizes like Cracker Jack and
gum and candy bars. But she never, ever bought enough of the "good
stuff". Pretty soon, she would be back in the bedroom wrapping up
cans of soup and boxes of Jello to give out as prizes. So you
definitely wanted to win early when you played Bingo at her house!
My mom has carried on the Bingo tradition. Now our whole family
plays at Christmas time. In the midst of the good prizes she always
wraps some soup or jello in memory of Aunt Evelyn. So I carried
that tradition on into Letterboxing BINGO.

BirdWoman/Waltzing Pigs
