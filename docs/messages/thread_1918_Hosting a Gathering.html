Hosting a Gathering

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-10-24 13:06:47 UTC
I received a question about what is involved in hosting a gathering
and I think it would be beneficial to post this reply to the list
rather than individually respond since others may be wondering the
same thing.

Hosting a gathering requires a commitment and some work, but it is
also very rewarding and can be a lot of fun if you just take things
as they come and don't overplan or overstress. It gives you a chance
to meet a lot of people and to try out ideas that you have seen/heard
of from other gatherings around the country.

It would be best to have a couple of teams responsible for hosting --
it is a lot of work for just one person/team. It helps if you have
been to a gathering before, but it is not a necessity as sometimes
going in without preconceived ideas and procedure can generate fresh
ideas. Also keep in mind that there are several people within this
group who have hosted letterbox gatherings and are more than willing
to answer questions and give feedback.

That said... what is involved?

- Scouting/choosing a specific park/location within the decided
geographic locale (1)
- Making arrangements for a shelter at the chosen location (2)
- Deciding on a theme for the event -- this can either be chosen by
the hosting group or suggestions put up for a vote at the list
- Publicizing the event -- posting to letterboxing Yahoo! lists,
AtlasQuest, etc. (get the word out)
- Soliciting letterboxers to carve and send letterboxes to be
planted at the gathering location (participants, area boxers, boxers
from afar, etc.)
- Planting letterboxes that are sent for the gathering and writing
clues to distribute at the gathering
- Making arrangements for traveling event boxes if desired (this
will be more easily faciliated as there is a new Yahoo! group
specific to traveling event boxes)
- Returning letterboxes and traveling event boxes to appropriate
people after the event (some boxes may be planted as permanent,
others may want their box returned)
- Arranging for workshop(s), if desired -- you do not have to
conduct these yourself, but would need to coordinate topics/leaders
- Arranging for any other activities as desired (i.e. kid's
activities, games, special guests/speakers, presentations/displays,
overnight activities -- be creative!)
- Carving or making arrangements for somebody else to carve the
official "event stamp"


NOTES:

(1) Some things to consider about the location:

- Does this location have a specific policy on letterboxing? (i.e.
would not meet at a National Park)
- Does this location have a reservable shelter capable of handling
50-60 people?
- Does this location have a large enough trail system to handle
planting letterboxes that are sent from participants?
- What are the park hours? Shelter hours?
- Is there nearby camping? hotels? other lodging?
- How convenient is the location from major highways? (don't want
too many people wandering around aimlessly looking for the park)


(2) Renting the shelter:

- Shelter does not need to be fully enclosed, but should have a roof
- Shelter should be capable of comfortably handling around 50-60
people
- Also consider space if there will be workshops, kid's activities,
etc.
- Electricity is nice for potluck participants, but not absolutely
necessary
- How close are the restrooms? water?
- You will most likely need to pay for the shelter when you reserve
it -- at the gathering we pass a hat to cover the cost of the shelter


For additional information, you might want to take a look at
the "Tips for Planning a Gathering" article at Silent Doug's website:

http://www.letterboxing.info/articles/00000017.php


Hope this helps!

SpringChick
