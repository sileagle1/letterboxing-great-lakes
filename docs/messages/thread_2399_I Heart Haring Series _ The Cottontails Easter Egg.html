Ok, this is getting just a little bit ridiculous. We can't seem to
place a box ANYWHERE!!!

We went out to Tyler Creek, and oh--the CARNAGE!! Be still our
hearts. Yes, it's true, these boxes have been obliterated by a
controlled burn. For Box 1, even the stump is gone. For Box 2, the
log that was used as cover for our box is gone. All that remains is
the metal coil from the spiral bound logbook. :(

We will carve and replace soon, when we think that the park is done
working up there. 3 park trucks drove up while we were there today.

Stick a Fork in Me is fine, although we are seriously thinking about
pulling it temporarily because the area around it seems overgrown
and has not been burned. We might take precautionary measures.



On a hunch, we drove over to Wing Park to check on our Easter Eggs.
We know that the official Elgin Easter Egg hunt was happening in a
different park, so they were safe from that. However, with all the
bad luck we've been having with our boxes being melted, we thought
we'd go check up on them. . .

It's a good thing that we did. There was what looked like a day
care group, or a neighborhood group with a million little kids
holding easter baskets getting ready for. . . wait for it. . . AN
EASTER EGG HUNT!!! AAAHHHHH AAAAAHHHH!!!!!

We jumped into action and scurried around collecting all of our eggs
before they could be trapped in the clutches of little children. We
only took eggs 1-7 since the others are hidden in a different area
of the park, AWAY from this Easter Egg Hunt action.

We will replace these eggs later today, or maybe Monday, for the
duration of our Easter Egg Hunt.

Frustrated,
The Cottontails

PS, still washing the squirrel hand. eew!
