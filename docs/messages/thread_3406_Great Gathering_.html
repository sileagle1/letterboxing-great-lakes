Great Gathering!

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2009-02-16 04:06:44 UTC
Many thanks to Beetle and Lock, Shock & Barrel for organizing a great
gathering today! Everyone was decked out in their beads and there
were many VERY creative floats in the parade. We saw lots of people
busily stamping the stamps that were cleverly included with the
floats, or otherwise available throughout the afternoon.

We enjoyed catching up with several of our long-time LB friends and
also had a chance to chat with a few new addicts such as Tat2Bob &
Kimberly and Terrio. However we somehow missed Popcorn Lady who we
really wanted to meet! (I counted a total of 14 attendees from
Wisconsin and she is the only one we still haven't met.)

After lunch, Lock gave an interesting presentation on carving and
then it was back to socializing and stamping. One of the newest
additions to the Great Lakes LB family was in attendance, but even
with all the noise, the littlest Christmas Elf managed to snooze her
way through most of the afternoon. We also saw some spirited dancing
by two boys in Spiderman outfits and some of the other kids were
decked out in jester hats and feathers in addition to beads.

A waitress was kind enough to serve as photographer and she willingly
took a group photo with probably a dozen different cameras. Tomorrow
I'll try to get a bunch of photos posted from the event.

Thanks again to the hosts and to everyone that attended!

Wisconsin Hiker & Martini Man

From: Kathi Krankoski (krank@ameritech.net) | Date: 2009-02-16 09:41:15 UTC-08:00
Great job, LS&Bx2 (that's Lock Shock & Barrel and Beetle)! I'm glad I was able to get back in time to attend SILO3. Two letterbox gathering attended in less that a week's time...is life good or what? While the "Fungal Infestation of San Jose" was much smaller than the SILO (hey, 21 people, somecoming from over an hour away on aweek night just to meet up is pretty cool...and I got to meet Wassa and other greatboxers from the Silicon Valley area in person...including Sharuna, a transplant from Lombard, IL...small world!), SILO appears to be gaining a reputation for showcasing some of the area's outstanding carvers...and float creators (have to ask Ryan to create a new category on AQ??).

So what's next year's theme going to be? February 4th is National Stuffed Mushroom Day (ouch!)...the 21st is National Sticky Bun Day (as if inky fingers isn't bad enough...). February is National Condom Month...nope, not touching that one with a ten-foot carving tool. It's also Potato Lovers Month...hey, we could carve potatoes instead of rubber! Feb 12 (1959) - first Barbies sold in stores...Lock could have a field day with that one. The 18th is Pluto Day (too controversial - planet or not?). National Inventors' Day (as proclaimed by Ronald Reagan in 1983, in honor of Edison's Feb 11th b-day)...now THAT could be fun..........
