Weird coincidence

From: TrailTroll (kanderson@american-community.com) | Date: 2005-09-20 12:07:26 UTC
This is a very odd, but very happy, coincidence. Yesterday I went to
check on a box that I placed in early June. I found it totally out in
the open - the logbook was outside of the baggie, and I found a page
from the logbook sitting on the ground several feet away. But here's
the good part: the stray page was from the day before, and the logbook
was dry. Apparently whatever had disturbed my box had happened in the
last 24 hours, so I had the chance to put it all back together before
it got rained on, or worse. And the strangest part is that until about
10 minutes before I went there, it wasn't on my agenda for the
afternoon!

Hope everybody has the forces of nature on their side --

TrailTroll

From: bindlestiff_boxer (bindlestiff_boxer@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-09-20 21:16:55 UTC
Weird is right. Prior to this summer, I've never found a box "out in
the open" like that ... nor had a box report from another boxer that
reported finding a box left out like that.

However, this summer I personally have found 2 of my boxes simply left
"out in the open" (these boxes are along a trail that I frequently
ride my bike along) plus 3 belonging to other boxers AND also I've
gotten reports from 2 finders that they found my boxes out and open
like you describe. (One finder did a re-hide, the other simply left
the box out and sent me a note .. yikes!) BTW - these are all MICH boxes.

Of course the ones I ride by are 'local' but the 2 that I got 'contact
the placer' notes on where NOT local at all. One was up the Lake
Michigan coast and the other was over near Newago/White Cloud.

Not sure what's going on. Wondering if either non-boxers are finding
them by accident and not taking time to read the "about boxing" forms
I leave inside or perhaps there are some new boxers who don't
understand the concept of putting the box back where it was found.

Anyone else have this sort of problem with their boxes?

Enjoy!
---Bindle Babe

We were at the Bristol Faire some weeks ago and were having trouble
finding one of the boxes. Then we noticed a little girl, about 4 years
old, walking around carrying the box. She had accidently found it. We
introduced ourselves and explained to her and her mom about
letterboxing. The little girl even stamped in the logbook with her
fingerprint. They liked it. It was quite a coincident to show up at
that moment. The mom said she hadn't noticed what her daughter was
playing with but she probably would have had her throw it out or just
leave it abandoned.

One of those 'laying out in the open' boxes was ours! When we checked it out, the stump it had been hidden in had been ripped apart by either a bear or a racoon. By the amount of damage to the stump our guess is a bear. The funny part was that every passer-by then took the opportunity to write in the logbook and put the letterbox back on the ground next to the trail until SpringChick came by and re-hid the box. Thanks to SpringChick's email, we hid it further off-trail; but am considering adding a sheet of Bounce to the box. They claim to deter mice, maybe it works for bigger critters.
