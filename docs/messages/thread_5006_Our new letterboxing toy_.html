Our new letterboxing toy!

From: Doodle & Deedle Bug (doodle_n_deedle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-04-29 05:45:31 UTC-07:00
Last week, we ordered an ipod Nano for Deedle. It arrived a few days ago, and he's been playing with all the features, going on and on about how cool it is, how great the sound is, all the extra features, and making me jealous because I just have a first generation ipod Shuffle ;)

But then - then - he discovered the coolest feature about his new toy after all. I remember reading somewhere about someone using their ipod to store Letterbox clues and mentioned it to Deedle. And he figured out how to get them on his Nano. So now, when we letterbox, rather than carrying sheets of paper around we can just carry his ipod with us! This (theoretically) will make us look less suspicious when letterboxing...we're giddy to try it out! So if you're letterboxing in Northeast Ohio and see a man & woman on a trail huddled around an ipod, it's probably us :)

Doodle Bug.

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2008-04-30 05:02:58 UTC-04:00
I do that -- load clues as text docs onto my iPod. I haven't completely abandoned paper clues though -- I usually take paper clues for the boxes I *know* I am going to and rely on the iPod clues more for boxes that I happen to be near that I hadn't anticipated and to keep my own box clues available at any time (as I often need help finding my own boxes). I've been looking around for an iPod application that would let me assign tags or keywords to text files so it would be easier to find/search the clues I have on there, but have not found anything like that so far. In the meanwhile, I have one document that is like an index that provides state, city, county and box name, which makes it easier to find things. Works great!

SpringChick

For those interested, here is how Deedle told me he uploaded the clues to his Nano:

1. Copy & paste text from Letterbox clues into Notepad (Under Windows 'Accessories' tab), and save as .txt file [Doodle note - if you have a Mac, you can also save as a .txt file; if you don't know how email me for directions].

2. Connect Ipod to computer and access it as a flash drive via My Computer [Doodle note - on a Mac, the icon should appear right on your desktop].

3. Double-click the drive letter and it will open up the folder list [Doodle note - on a Mac, just double-click the icon on your desktop].

4. Copy the .txt files into the "Notes" folder.

Now you can access the files on your Ipod under the 'extras' screen.

(In case it's not obvious, Deedle has a PC and I have a Mac at home :)

Like SpringChick wrote about still keeping paper copies, I don't think this is going to make us go completely paperless, but it might help us be more stealthy on the trails when we do use the Nano for our clues. Except for when we're fighting over the Nano in order to read the clues... :)

Doodle Bug.
