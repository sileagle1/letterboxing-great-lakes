I guess I didn't explain the side wagers game completely.  There's not all that much to it, really.  A bunch of people picked dates that they thought the first "full card" bingo game would be won.  They are in the running for a small prize from me, if they are the closest to the day (being on or before the day the game is won).  So if someone picked January 25, and the full card was won any time up to that day, they would win the side wagers pot.  However, if the big game was won on January 26, whoever selected the next date after that would win the sidewagers....even if that person picked April 15.  You can claim a win on any date between the last date someone else chose up to and including the day you picked.  Once your date selected passes by, you are out of the running.

Basically, there's nothing else to do with the side wagers between now and whenever someone eventually wins it.  So, to keep things a little interesting, I will periodically post some random mysterious messages.  The messages will include "on the trail"; "in the car" and "still at home" references which basically just refer to the people who have selected some upcoming dates.  There's no way for you to really tell what date each person has until it gets later in the game where pretty much all or most of the days are selected.  I will post pretty much daily at that point, so it will be easier to figure out who selected a particularly date.

You will need to figure out a code, presumably, to solve the messages.  Once you do, you do not need to do anything with the information, other than congratulate yourself on solving them, and basking in the glow of knowing you know something other people likely do not know.   There is no extra prize or purpose in solving this codes, it's just something to add interest to the game and give you something to do.  All I ask, is once you figure out the key to solving the code, or decipher any answers, please do not post any of that information to this board.  Let's keep up the suspense and mystery for the other people that may be trying to solve them.  You're free to gloat that you solved it, if you wish, just don't give anything away.

Since my first code was accidentally revealed on the board, I will decipher the first clue for you.... I will be redoing all the codes with a new encryption method, so this won't help you in the least!

The first post was encrypted with a standard Polibius Square.  How this works is you have a 5x5 grid filled in with the alphabet {I and J share a square}.   To encode, each letter is converted to a set of 2 numbers, the numbers represent the row/column that correspond to the letter's location in the grid.

So to solve the code I sent, you just need to break the numbers up into sets of 2 and find them in the grid...


For example:

On the trail:  2311424453432453

23 11 42 44 53 43 24 53

   1  2  3 4  5
1 A B C D E
2 F G H I  K
3 L M N O P
4 Q R S T U
5 VWX  YZ

23; 2nd row down, third column over is H
11; 1st row, 1st column is A
42; 4th row, 2nd column is R
etc.... You will eventually spell out HartxSix.

Numbers are a little tricky with this code, so they are spelled out to their word form for easier decoding.

I hope it makes sense to everyone now.  :)  (Oh, and the subject line of the post has nothing to do with the codes within)
