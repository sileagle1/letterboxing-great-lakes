Re: [LbGLK] Stamp Adhesive

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-08-26 10:41:42 UTC-04:00
What was the problem you were having with rubber cement?

I have had great success with rubber cement, although I prefer contact
cement because it seems to go on smoother. The trick with both rubber
cement and contact cement is that you have to apply it to both pieces and
then let them "set" per the directions for a permanent bond -- the time
varies with brand, but it is usually 30-60 minutes -- until it gets tacky.
Then you put them together and they stay. I have used this since the very
first stamp I made, using various carving mediums and adhering to oak, birch
and balsa woods and have never had a stamp come apart, even when it is
floating in water.

Contact adhesives, including rubber cement, are made to allow for peelable
application -- if you just apply it and stick it together while it is wet,
the bond is not permanent and the pieces will peel apart -- they are
supposed to.

SpringChick
