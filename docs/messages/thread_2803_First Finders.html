From: meanderingmelee (meanderingmelee@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-07-21 11:51:52 UTC
My first post!! My family is loving this new obsession, I mean hobby.
We've only been letterboxing since April (officially our first find
although we were introduced briefly last year), and we are ready to
plant our first boxes. We've decided to take Wisconsin Hiker up on
her challenge to plant in new counties and hope to plant two boxes
this weekend.

Is there a tradition associated with this region as to first finders?
It seems somewhat regional from what I'm getting from other message
boards as to what to give. A certificate seems the most common but
I've read about stamps, trinkets, and gift cards to name a few. Any
information would be appreciated. Thanks

Mike of the Meandering Melee







Re: First Finders

From: the_cignacats (mlcigna@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-07-21 12:30:14 UTC
What we have seen most in SE Wisconsin are certificates and
hitchhikers as first finder prizes. When we first started planting
boxes, we would make small certificates so that people could put
them in their logbook. Now we tend to either carve a new hitchhiker
to send off or if we have one in our possession, we leave that so
that the first finder can get a little boost in their find count.
Who doesn't love opening a box and finding a HH?

Congrats on your first plants!
The CignaCats

From: (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2006-07-21 08:40:09 UTC-05:00
Hi! It's good to see you on the message board and I'm happy to hear
that you'll be placing in a new WI county!

First Finder prizes are totally optional. Lots of boxes around here
don't have anything in them. As you've noted, there is a wide variety
of things that are left as a prize if the box does include something.
We've found FF certificates, key chain trinkets, hitchhikers and a very
nice moon jewelry pin (my favorite prize!). In the boxes we have
placed, we've left trinkets related to the box theme and hitchhikers as
prizes.

Wisconsin Hiker
