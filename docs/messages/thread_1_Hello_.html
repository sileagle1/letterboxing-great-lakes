Welcome to the crazy...I mean wonderful...world of letterboxing! Don't worry about questions. They're no bother at all, and we all had to start somewhere.

Pens are useful to have with you for signing into logbooks. The only thing that's really required is trading stamps, but most people also at least write the date they were there and their trail name (unless you were clever enough to carve your trail name right into the stamp...a time saving bonus that not all signature stamps have). A lot of people also jot down a quick message to the placer which could be anything from a comment about how much they liked the stamp, the weather or trail conditions that day, any problems they had with the clues or the trail, etc.

WOM clues are disseminated a number of ways. Some of them truly are strictly word of mouth and you have to find (or email) another letterboxer to get them. Some are "WOM" only in the sense that they aren't listed on either of the two main sites (LbNA and Atlasquest) but can be found on private sites. I believe there are some clues lurking around the files section of this group even.

Hitchikers are also a fairly flexible subject. Usually you take the HH with you and move it to another box, but you don't have to. In fact, if you don't expect to find another box for quite a while (like a month or more) it is more polite to simply stamp into the HH log, stamp the HH into your log, and leave it there for the next finder. And some HH's, like the Migrating Coconuts HH of the Holy Grail series have special rules...in this case, they must ONLY be moved to another box from the Grail series and you need to email Dragon so that their new location can be posted. Often times the placer of a HH has left their email address in the logbook, but they are also tracked on a couple of websites (Wanda and Pete's HH Page (http://alum.wpi.edu/~p_miner/travelers.html), LbNA, and Atlasquest are the big three). Letterboxing.org and www.letterboxing.info both have articles that go a bit more into the specifics of hitchikers (as well as a wealth of other LB topics).

As for gatherings, check out the archives on this group and the calendar on Atlasquest for others. The big one is in June, but there are lots of local gatherings all year (and some of them aren't really so small).

Kimono Girl
