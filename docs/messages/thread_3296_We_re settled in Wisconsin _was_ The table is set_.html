From: tj_mich (tj_mich@ameritech.net) | Date: 2006-11-09 16:04:04 UTC
Hi everyone,

Just recently caught up on the list after several weeks of neglect.
It was nice to be missed! We regretted not being able to make it to
the mystery gathering in October, nor to the southeast Michigan
fantasy gathering in September. Sounds like they were a lot of fun!

We also missed the round of introductions back in September, so for
those of you that we haven't met yet, we are TJ_Mich, originally from
the Upper Peninsula of Michigan*, recently of the western suburbs of
Detroit, and now relocated to the greater Milwaukee area. It's been
a busy September and October, but we are now settled into our new
house, are getting to know the area, and are looking forward to
seeing old and new faces at the upcoming gathering in Waukesha in a
few weeks. Not to mention the *abundance* of boxes in this area:
there must be a couple hundred within a hour's drive! Wow... we
can't wait!

We have been letterboxing for almost 4 years, and are somewhere
around P20 F299 X90 E4. You can see a list of all our adventures at
http://www.geocities.com/tj_mich02 We were lucky enough to attend
the very first Great Lakes Gathering way back in 2003, just a few
months after we got started. And earlier this spring, we hosted a
small gathering near our old home in Michigan. By the way, Wisconsin
folks: we have 8 temporary letterboxes left over from that event and
hope to find new permanent locations for them next year.

In addition to collecting all kinds of stamp images over the past few
years, we've also formed a veritable posse of junior letterboxers--
er, that is to say, we've had 3 kids! They are The Nickerboxer,
who's almost 3; Little WoodChuck, 1 1/2; and 4 month old Sam, whose
trail name and stamp will debut in a few weeks. Our nine-year-old
Sheltie, Maya, also has her own stamp, and we still sometimes bring
her hiking when we are really feeling brave.

For the record -- but please, not to start any conversations again --
we did once meet the family from Livingston County out on the trail.
In fact it was the day after the 3rd Great Lakes Gathering in 2005,
and they were hunting for some of the event boxes, just like we
were. They were perfectly nice folks and had already been 'boxing
for a year or more at that time, if I recall correctly. Finally, I
just can't resist responding to the comment that - 'they don't have
trail names, so must not "get it"' - with just 6 words: Wanda &
Pete. Don & Gwen. Mark & Sue. Enough said.

Best wishes to everyone for a few more weeks of fall fun, and see you
on the trails!
--TJ_Mich
