LOL .. I'm sure I wouldn't do it well, so here's a copy and paste of
their homepage.

"The Society for Creative Anachronism, Inc. The SCA is an
international organization dedicated to researching and re-creating
the arts and skills of pre-17th-century Europe. Our "Known World"
consists of 18 kingdoms, with over 30,000 members residing in
countries around the world. Members, dressed in clothing of the Middle
Ages and Renaissance, attend events which may feature tournaments,
arts exhibits, classes, workshops, dancing, feasts, and more. Our
"royalty" hold courts at which they recognize and honor members for
their contributions to the group."

When Bindle Bum and I lived in Traverse City we hiked into the midst
of a SCA gathering by accident. Some guy wearing tights and big black
boots that looked like they came from the set of a Star Wars movie
told us "Danes" weren't allowed.

We looked at each other, "Huh?" Bindle Bum is of Italian descent ...
doesn't look Danish at all.

Then, seeing our puzzled looks, the fellow explained. "Mundanes," he
said, indicating our decidedly non-SCA hiking outfits, "You normal
folks aren't allowed around here this weekend."

"Oh" we thought, "sure, right, gotcha".

And Bindle Bum laughed about 'normal' people like us -- who live on a
sailboat most of the year and occasionally quit our jobs for 6 months
at a time, just because we can and we would rather be sailing -- being
mundane the rest of the day.

Enjoy!
---PJ
aka Bindle Babe, just another 'dane'
