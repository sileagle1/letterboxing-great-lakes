I may be slow ... but, hey, look at my new logbooks!

From: speedsquare_lbxr (ruhlette@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-02-24 21:54:54 UTC
I would never be able to make a living doing crafty things. I'm so easily distracted and the
time clock would have to be reset over and over.

So I started my GLG 2007 logbook about 10:45am. I liked the size of my shamrock book,
so I thought 5.5x7.5 inches would make a good standard size. Plus, I still have my
template from last night that will work again. I decided to cut all my scrap cardboard the
same size, and I have enough to make 16 more logbooks.

Okay, that's done. Now let me see, let's start with the covers. I went back to Hobby Lobby
this morning and found some bigger needles and waxed linen thread and a large pad of
paper. Oh, and more PAPER! Being the Cluez-In will feature 50's icons, I knew I had to
have the Dick and Jane paper. Bought several sets. You know, in case of goofs. I had to
reverse the inside and outside sheets because the layout would look aweful. Not a
problem.

The Strathmore pad of paper was a good choice. After thinking about all the PT's, event
boxes, exchanges, et al, I decided 48 sides of paper probably wasn't going to do. So I
spent considerable time cutting 96 (out of 100) sheets to size. My fingers are really sore
right now. Each sheet had to be cut twice, and if you've ever made a book, you want the
sheets to be as close to the same size as possible. Tedious.

Then my better half started opening the refrigerator and looking for Girl Scout cookies.
Lunch break.

Back to work. Woo-hoo. I decided on 16 signatures of 6 sheets of paper. Group them
and fold them. Done. Mark them with pencil before piercing, done. Pierce them. Yeah,
we're almost ready to assemble!

Okay Signature one and cover are attached, looking good. Whoops! At the end of
Signature 2, I notice a BIG boo-boo. I forgot to make the second cut on the paper before
lunch. This doesn't look good. If I pulled it all out the holes would weaken the book and
make a mess. But we can't have pages sticking out of the book! So what did I do? I
continued on and figured I would cut the edges when I was done. Easier said than done. I
think 16 signatures is almost too many for my logbooks. My hands almost could not hold
the expanding stack as I assembled it.

Well, you be the judge!!! Check out my photo album.

~speedsquare
