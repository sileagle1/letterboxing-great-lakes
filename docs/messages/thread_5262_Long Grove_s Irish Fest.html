Re: [LbGLK] Long Grove's Irish Fest

From: Carol Osada (cjosada@hotmail.com) | Date: 2008-08-24 22:07:42 UTC-06:00
Yes, I'm planning to be at Long Grove's Irish Fest on Saturday. Long Grove fests are the best! I have several boxes in the Long Grove business district: Long Grove Covered Bridge (since 2006), and more recently three newer boxes: With My Hands I Give You My Heart, Archer School, and Ruth Barn. I plan to be there around 11:00 a.m. because parking is at a premium if you get there in the afternoon. Might I recommend that we meet near my Long Grove Bridge letterbox because there are benches in that park area. We should be able to recognize each other. Letterboxers all have that same 'look' --- hanging around, not appearing to be doing anything, holding a journal but not writing, usually seen with a tote or small back pack, and the ink on the fingers is a dead give away!
Here's an old Irish proverb, also called a seanfhocal, that I just have to share with everyone. Airthnionn ciarog ciarog eile - translated it means A beetle recognizes another beetle. That saying has ME written all over it. The meaning of this proverb is "It takes on to know one" (if used dismissively) or as a sign of camaraderie as in "Birds of a feather flock together." Hey, wasn't that the name of another recent letterbox gathering. Sorry, I missed that one. We should all be able to spot each other. Who else is planning to attend?
Beetle
