From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-09-19 10:01:53 UTC
Well, I am SpringChick, the female half of the SpringChick and
OldShoe team, and definitely the more obsessed of the pair. We have
been letterboxing a little over 4 years now and live in Muskegon, MI
with two pugs (who do letterbox on occasion). We don't keep an
accurate count of boxes, but by rough count it is about F400 P150.
The count is more mine than a team count as OldShoe is not an avid
letterboxer and his involvement in finds and plants is usually
limited to boxes done while traveling. I often letterbox alone and
actually enjoy the solitude of walking the trails by myself. In the
past couple of years I have also been taking my young grandson with
me, trying to instill a love of nature in him. He is just at the age
(now 4) where he understand what we are doing and eagerly looks
forward to the next box.

Most of my letterboxes are planted in the West Michigan area, the
exception being several in the Traverse City, MI area and a sizable
collection of them planted in Michigan's U.P. I have planted a few
here and there across the U.S. while on vacation but am slowly
retiring these boxes as the areas they are in become populated with
letterboxers and letterboxes. As letterboxing grows and more boxes
become available, I find myself adjusting my planting style to offer
more difficult and/or unique boxes, in terms of location, hike or
clues. Mystery boxes are my favorites to plant and hikes of 2+ miles
(mystery or non) are my favorites to find -- although I don't always
have the time for them while passing through an area on vacation.

When I first learned about letterboxing, it seemed tailor-made for
me -- a combination of several things that interested me. I had
been an avid hiker for several years. As a teenager I spent a summer
in Switzerland and did a lot of hiking in the Alps and for many
years previous to learning about letterboxing I had been taking an
annual "hike & write" trip to Michigan's U.P. each fall by myself.
I would hike by day, set up camp and write at night by the light of
the fire. I also am an artsy kind of gal and have more rubber stamps
than any person in their right mind should have, although I had
never heard of carving my own stamps, much less tried doing it. I
was familiar with several types of bookbinding and had been crafting
handmade books as gifts for friends over the years. The treasure
hunt aspect was not new to me either. I've always liked puzzles,
games and brain teasers and one year at summer camp the staff put
together a treasure hunt for us and I was just thrilled. I went
home and made up a treasure hunt for my family and have continued to
put together scavenger hunts/treasure hunts for our annual family
camping trip each summer. As an adult I became a logic puzzle
fanatic and this is one of my favorite types of letterbox clues to
write. Everything about this hobby fascinated me -- the only problem
was that there were no boxes in my area to look for... and that is
another whole story (which can be found in message post #8688).

I am the originator and one of the moderators of this Yahoo! Group,
as well as serving as a moderator at a couple of the other
letterboxing related groups. I was until recently one of the LbNA
web masters, but resigned because it was getting too overwhelming. I
am also the originator of the annual Great Lakes Letterbox Gathering
(held the first Saturday in June each year), and last year began
hosting an annual fall letterboxing event in Michigan.

Nice to meet you all!

SpringChick
