From: Tom & Jill (tj_mich@ameritech.net) | Date: 2004-03-23 06:50:41 UTC-05:00
Hello, my name is Tom and my wife is Jill. We currently live in Canton
Township, Michigan, and share the trail name TJ_Mich.



We first learned about letterboxing through a wire service article in
the Detroit News & Free Press travel section on February 1, 2003. We
carved our signature stamp that week (you can imagine the surprise on my
wife's face when *I* asked *her* if we could go to Michael's to buy the
supplies) and went searching for our first boxes the next weekend. We
were 1-for-2 that first day, but our "success rate" has improved quite a
bit since then.



When we started letterboxing, we were glad to see there were already
several boxes in our native U.P., and we've planted several more there
in the past year. We also have a couple in Ann Arbor, and one each in
Ohio and Wisconsin. Our goal in planting letterboxes is to lead people
unfamiliar with a particular area, to interesting places that they
probably wouldn't have known about or stopped at if they were simply
passing through. We have very much enjoyed the off-the-beaten-trail
locations to which letterbox hunts have led us. Some great examples of
this are the Seul Choix Point lighthouse near Manistique, Michigan;
Crisp Point Lighthouse along Lake Superior; Alum Rock Park near San
Jose, California, and the Eternal Flame south of Buffalo, New York.



Our website has clues for all 18 boxes that we've planted, as well as a
list of our "finds" and our "attempts." It's at
http://www.geocities.com/tj_mich02



Look forward to meeting many of you - either on the trail, or perhaps at
the next Great Lakes Gathering?? The first one was fantastic!
