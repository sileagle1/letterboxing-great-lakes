From: FM Girl (meadow.walk@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-01-16 19:41:16 UTC-06:00
It's funny to hear you say that. When I was getting my degree in
anthropology I had to do an ethnology, which is a research project in
the field [if someone didn't know that]. I chose a bingo parlor. I
spent months at that bingo parlor, doing interviewing of both workers
and players, mapping movement flow, participant-observation, and more.
I did my analysis using a study of game theory from an ethnology done
in Indonesian cockfighting, if anyone wants to know.

Anyway! This is really funny to me. Because some of the conclusions I
drew related to how workers -- that was my name for the administrators
of the game -- differed in their belief system about both bingo and
about the luck vs control of playing the game, versus the same beliefs
held by players of the game, who were trying to win the game. And I am
seeing some of the same belief structures come up in the discussion as
to bingo on here. It's pretty amazing. I don't know why I am so
astounded that I was right in my analysis, but I am. Maybe because I
was only 21 and I didn't think I knew much at 21!

For example, I drew a sharp distinction between the people I called
"workers", anyone who administered the game, and "players", anyone who
participated in the game itself. They had such radically different
perspectives on the game itself, how and why to play it, and winning,
and there were reasons for this distinction that I don't need to go
into. I am seeing some worker-speak in SpringChick's posts. I don't
know why I am astounded, after all it is her game and she is running
it, so she should talk like that, I am just blinking at it. And I am
seeing some player-speak in other peoples' posts too. It's like I
learned a foreign language that no one else knew 15 years ago, and all
of a sudden someone starts using in front of me. And I still remember
it.

Here's an example of what I am talking about...in my research I found
that players had 2 systems for trying to win. I called one Math
Systems, and it included all forms of logic, and the other
Superstitions, which included anything else pretty much. So a player
using a Math System would say, "I always pick cards that have 7's in
all 4 corners, because 7's come up more often." Or, "At this bingo
hall, I always pick these numbers.... because the balls are old and
balls with those numbers have been called more often, therefore, the
balls have been worn away on one side from being in the little holder
more often, and so now they are lighter on one side and will pop up
more often. Here. At XYZ Bingo Hall, their more worn out numbers
are..." I swear I am not making this up, people actually said that to
me, more than once.

Basically Math Systems had to do with numbers, their positioning,
probabilities, and percentages. But at the e base of it, Math was
supposed to be based on some form of logic. If you could say "I do
this and it works because..." and have a reason it was almost always a
Math System for winning. If it has "because" in it, that was a verbal
marker.

Superstitions included anything that had no reason behind it, or any
statement where the player said "I do this XYZ and it always wins. I
don't know why it works, but it does." Examples included having a
lucky elephant statue that always generated a win when its trunk was
turned to face north. Wearing a certain shirt. Sitting in a certain
seat. Having a talisman or setting up an entire altar [I documented
some of there; they were so interesting]. Having rituals.
Superstitions basically were a mythology of bingo.

The workers were very dismissive [at that time and place] of the
players as a whole. There's a whole big reason for that I don't need
to type up here, but it was very interesting to find out what it was.
It was especially interesting bc this bingo was at a Catholic high
school and both workers and players knew each other rand were
frequently friends/friendly on a personal level, but on Bingo Night
those relationships were subsumed to the Bingo Parlor Hierarchy. But
anyway, workers were especially derogatory towards Superstitions or
any player who openly used Superstitions, but they did have some
grudging respect for those using Math Systems. Because there was some
kind of logic or reasoning behind those.

I hadn't really been reading the bingo posts bc I am not playing and I
am not sitting here analyzing any of you here. It was just a momentary
and very strange flashback because I accidental read some stuff that I
had not heard or thought about in over 15 years.

Hm, maybe I should make a bingo letterbox....

I don't know why I am so thrown by this. It's like, what, did I think
anthropology was a joke and I was fooling around when I did it? It was
just very very odd in a funny way to read this!

Freelance Mystic

I read about 1/4 (at most) of the posts here -- mainly due to my
hectic schedule, 3 kids in god-knows-how-many extra curriculars, and
various other daily comittments -- so I don't know what all this
quarelling is really about either. And I did not read every post in
this current thread. But to add my own two cents, I will say that
probably a few people took that initial post a little personally
about why some people chose the middle square, when it was probably
more advantageous to choose a corner square. I will say that I did
feel stupid at first... muttering to myself: "...Hmmmmm.... I always
thought the center square was the best place for a free space?!?!"

Until... UNTIL... I went over to the files section on our list (or is
it database? see... I told you I don't keep up here...) and read over
the rules of the current bingo game. Then there it was: AHA! Not only
can you win up and down, diagonally, and "cover all," which is all
the bingo I ever learned growing up... but you can also win 4 corners
and perimeter too! I had never even heard of the last two!!! So I
personally chalked it up (my choosing the center space rather than a
corner space,) to my own stupidity of not reading the rules of this
particular game in the first place!

I dare say, however, that there were probably a few others besides
myself that didn't fully know of the other win "options" and simply
chose the center square because that's typically the best spot in
traditional bingo. Asking why someone made a choice, and then further
stating that a different choice is perhaps better, is always going to
stir things up just a little bit with some people. I didn't take it
personally however.

Have a great day!
