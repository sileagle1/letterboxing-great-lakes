I agree with Acorn - we wear Cool-Max liner socks and SmartWool socks
when hiking in both summer and winter.

I am usually colder than most people, so I will wear multiple layers
with the top layer being a down coat from Lands' End. I also have some
winter "climbing" pants (shaped for mountain climbers) that are really
warm. Bottom layers are lightweight or midweight hightech long
underwear that wicks moisture. I also really appreciate the down
mittens I have!

I am usually warmer than most. Hiked this morning, wind chill -9. wore Smart Wool socks, no liners. (as I do year round. I find I don't get blisters with smart wool as I do with cotton socks) Wore reg. Nikes. If you have cold feet and you are just sitting, get up and walk around for a while so you get the circulation going.
