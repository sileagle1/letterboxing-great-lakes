Holy Grail Clues: The Boat to Castle Aaaaagh

From: Doodle & Deedle Bug (doodle_n_deedle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-07-18 08:11:15 UTC-07:00
We finally had a chance to type up the clues for this
Holy Grail Box. Since Dragon's on vacation, he asked
us to post them directly to the list until he has a
chance to add them to his website. I forget who
created this box (we actually planted it a while ago,
then misplaced the notebook that had the notes to
write up the clues during the wedding and moving
mayhem), but you'll be given your due on Dragon's
website, since I sure hope he kept track of these
things! :)

Doodle & Deedle Bug.

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

The Clues to The Boat to Castle Aaaargh:

Location: Berea, Ohio

Since a boat is what you seek, find the place where
boat rentals occur at Wallace Lake in Berea. Leave
your steed in the parking lot and begin your quest
at "Richard's Rest." From here, head south towards
the
woods. When a doorway to "Danger" rears its ugly
head, be like Brave Sir Robin and flea straight
ahead, up the dirt path and over the hill.

Cross the field southeast to the pines. At the
bench take a rest and a reading of 130*. You'll see a
narrow path. Follow it down, if you dare. It twists
and turns, snakes and curves, until you end up at
water and a square rock (and perhaps a few dead
fish, no doubt victims of the Killer Rabbit!).

Ready to launch? Standing at the square rock, with
the rock to your back and water in the front, walk 5
paces to the right. Head up this path; about 4 paces
in the path comes to a fork (the right branch may not
be noticeable given the time of year). Walk 3 paces
along the left branch. Look to your right. See the
Siamese Tree? Your vessel is docked here at its base.

If after finding the boat you chose not to sail but to
return to your trusty steed instead, head back to the
water. Face the path you were just on. Take 3 or 4
steps to your right (towards the square rock).
There's another path that leads straight up to the
road and a dirt Bridal Path. Head north on this path
back towards your steed.
