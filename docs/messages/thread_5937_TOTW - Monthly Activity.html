Oh my! Does not having a TV all these years do this to a person? Words like "pivot sheet", "spreadsheet" and "graphs" is just way too left brained for us! In matter of fact, the thought that we even wondered about a "statistical" question is scary in it's own right! Way too much math for us!

We basically counted up our finds and sorted them into the two groups. (We used our own "spread sheet" method - flinging the wrinkled, inky, smeared clue sheets into either a warm pile or a cool pile.) To our surprise we had a fairly even amount in both groups, but winter boxing was the winner with 67 more boxes. Thanks in part to winter trips. We also realized that we seem to prefer cooler temps to be outdoors, along with the fact that the warmer months bring more weekend distractions (like holidays, weddings, family parties etc.)
We'd give you a more concise account of the status of our boxing habits - like how many more boxes do we find when there is a full moon, but we have an uncanny need to go watch mindless TV...maybe this is why we've done so poorly in math all these years!
Tiptoe & Tonto

From: Coffee Beans (coffeebeans06@yahoo.com) | Date: 2009-06-11 13:15:57 UTC-05:00
Well, WiHi isn't the only one who's left-brained and
statistically-minded. ;) I worked up the Atomic Beans spreadsheets and
made pretty graphs following her format and uploaded them as a pdf in
the files section too. Just for kicks, I also made a pretty pie chart
showing our breakdown of boxes found per state. If you check out the
file, you also might happen to notice that we are nearly at a major
boxing milestone... hopefully over this weekend or next. :)

In case you don't go look at it though, we definitely have more finds
Apr-Sep than Oct-Mar -- 67% vs 33%. I imagine those who travel away
from the chilly/snowy midwest even for a week or two of vacation during
the winter months are able to get more winter finds that way. Our
winter traveling involves visiting parents, which as you may know, live
in WI and MI so definitely snowy there and no increase in opportunity to
box. As for plants, we're very close, 49% vs 51%, although the
distributions are pretty inconsistent overall, and a couple of 10-box
series planted in July and Dec skew the results a bit.

Coffee Beans

My letterboxing statistics are not as sophisticated:
