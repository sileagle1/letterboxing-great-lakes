From: TJ_Mich (tj_mich@ameritech.net) | Date: 2006-01-31 08:21:59 UTC-08:00
Besides LbGLK, I also keep up with 6 or 7 message boards on AtlasQuest... mostly just the main AtlasQuest discussion page, and general topics such as Stamp Carving, Event Planning, Premium Member page, etc. I really like how the message boards on AtlasQuest allow you to display dozens of messags per page -- makes it easy to skim the postings, reading only the ones that catch your eye, instead of having to open each message separately. Also, I appreciate that the boards tend to stay pretty well on-topic, so that you can follow the areas of interest to you. Finally, it's nice that AtlasQuest remembers which messages you have already read -- even if you log in from different computers.

Another nice feature on AtlasQuest is that the "My Page" shows not only the new postings from your selected message boards, but also shows all new articles from SpringChick's and Mark and Sue Pepe's letterboxing blogs. So I can keep up on those too, without having to check them individually every day.

I used to follow the "big" list (Letterbox-USA) but there were so many flame messages, uninteresting tangents, etc. that it got hard to keep up. I enjoy our regional list a lot more. The volume is manageable, and I can read and enjoy every message.

--Tom of TJ_Mich

I subscribe to many of the boards, the "big" national board and several
regional boards. I get individual emails from most of them that I
belong to. I usually join a regional board when we're planning a
vacation trip to that area since they discuss the local conditions,
etc. It has also proven to be a great way to get advice on where to
go, where to eat, any known missing boxes AND make plans to get
together with some local boxers when we're in their area. Sometimes
after our trip I change my settings to "no email", but sometimes I
continue with the emails because we've actually met some of the people
that are posting and we like hearing what they are up to. Some of the
groups (Mid-Atlantic in particular) are quite amusing. Right now I
read the following regional boards: Great Lakes, Pacific Northwest,
Mid-Atlantic, Southeast (might make a trip down there in April) and
Texas (waiting to hear when/if anyone finds the 2 boxes we planted in
November, then will probably drop these emails).

I also check in on AtlasQuest fairly frequently. I like the ability to
see what blogs have been updated and link to them directly from AQ (in
addition to Mark Pepe's & SpringChick's, I also enjoy reading the Green
Torgtugua's and dvn2rckr's blogs). I also check the AQ Events page to
see all the upcoming events around the country in case some of them fit
into our vacation plans (or we MAKE a vacation plan around an event!).
Usually also scan the "recent messages", but don't follow any
particular boards.

I also like to check on Pete's Hitchhiker site occasionally to see
where the various HHs we've found have traveled since we saw them.

Yep, I'm addicted to the hobby!

Wisconsin Hiker
