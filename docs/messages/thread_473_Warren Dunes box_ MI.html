Warren Dunes box, MI

From: Armadillo JMO (jes7o@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-08-03 06:01:37 UTC-07:00
Yes, the clue is very vague for this letterbox. It
makes it seem that the box is just a hop, skip, and a
jump when in reality it's about 2 1/2 miles away over
a sand dune. The mileage on the clue is incorrect
according to the trail map. AND if you want to go back
to your car you either have to A: go back the way you
came (back over the dune)or B: go the alternate route
which will involve begging a park ranger to take you
back to the car if you parked at the nature trailhead
in the first place. But you're correct, I probably
shouldn't have publicly suggested to give an alternate
route. I think I will probably e-mail the placers and
see if they might want to post the alternative
themselves or at least amend their clue to place a
degree of difficulty on it. 'Cause it ain't easy
folks!!! I really had no idea how difficult this one
was, and I'm one to do a lot of research before
heading out.

However, it was a beautiful spot with beautiful scenes
of Lake Michigan!

Armadillo JMO

From: Bill and Ann Fisher (wfisher47@comcast.net) | Date: 2004-08-03 08:30:48 UTC-05:00
I've been in the vicinity and not tried for it after I emailed the placer
about its current status and got back a warning that I should be prepared
for a long hike. I was grateful for the warning and decided not to try to
fit it into an already busy day. After hearing these details I'm very glad
I took a pass. On the other hand, there are times when knowing that a box
is a challenging hike would make it all the more attractive, so I think it
would be great if the clues were amended to provide that information. I
also think that in these post-Time magazine days, a box that's a good
challenging hike has much more chance of being there when we go look for
it. (Though, of course, we'd be all the more disappointed if it were not).
Yooperann
