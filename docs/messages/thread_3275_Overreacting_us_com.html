From: Brad Maynes (Brad.Maynes@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-11-06 13:22:02 UTC-05:00
I'd just like to make a few points that seem, at least in part, against the
prevailing winds post-Press and Argus article.

1. Newspapers/Magazines/Publicity is/are bad.

Well, now this is just silly. How many of us CAN'T trace our involvement in
letterboxing back to a) an article or "other" website (yahoo featured site)
discussing letterboxing or b) WoM from someone else who can trace themselves
back to this. I really don't see any problem, at all, with articles, etc.
regarding letterboxing, PER SE. Poorly written or flawed articles are
another matter.

2. We should limit access to clues.

I can't disagree with this any more strongly. If an individual placer wants
to keeps clues limited/secretive, that is their call. I know many prominent
letterboxers that have some public clues and some more private clues and
some totally secret clues. While I'd like for people to be informed, I don't
want to place artificial barriers to keep people away for the sake of
keeping people away. Furthermore, some of the most careless/obnoxious people
I know are quite blessed with persistence and would be a problem regardless.

3. We should make people sign a waiver/disclaimer.

To what end? So that we can yell at them if they breach it? Can't we yell at
them anyway? So we can ban them from clues if they breach it? I'd rather not
be the person spending my leisure time deciding if someone else should be
banned from spending their leisure time letterboxing, thanks.

4. Kids are a problem.

Uneducated kids are a problem, just the same as uneducated adults are.

When Jen and I first came into this hobby, we found it to be totally
accessible and easy to discover what the "rules of the road" were. I think
it's a shame if we make ourselves less newbie friendly, and make less boxes
available out of fear.

This is just my $0.02, and not reflective of the views of J.

B of J&B Books

Exactly!

Sending rude or obnoxious email or letters to reporters who have
already printed their stories will not do any good. They will just
ignor it and block you from further email. Calls, especially
threatening, may bring negative publiclty or, in some cases -
depending what words were used, the police to your door. You may
want to contact them after it prints and let them know you saw the
story and you would like to give additional or a view from a seasoned
letterboxer since most of these articles seem to be about newer
boxers, notice I did not say newbie.

Now as far as contacting the family, be nice. Polietly explain how
you feel, especially now that you have published their name, address,
and phone number to all of the internet. Yes I know with a little
googling we all could have found it, but now someone who didn't cool
off first may have already made contact. You might have well as
printed all the answers to the mystry boxes that are listed.

From: pell_lake_girl (mishiekins@prodigy.net) | Date: 2006-11-06 19:33:30 UTC
Thank you so much for speaking in the voice of reason.

I would like to affirm that I do not have children, I am a grown woman, and I letterbox
alone. No one I know letterboxes or had even heard of it before I started doing it [now I
have gotten 3 other people to start as well before I realized it was supposed to be a "secret
society" or whatever].

And that I do not enjoy nor want to do any kind of search that takes me 6 months to figure
out. Or 6 weeks. A lot of people really enjoy figuring out mysteries or puzzles. I personally
find them annoying, that's just me. And many people like hiking 4 hrs into the woods to
find one box. I would never do that, that's just me.

But I *do* like walking in the woods, taking scenic photos, and following simple directions.
That is a big source of my enjoyment. I love having a reason to go outside and find new
places and enjoy the fall sunshine. I hope to have reasons to enjoy other seasons as well.

I know people spend time and money and effort to make boxes -- even easy ones. I really
appreciate it, a lot, and I ignore the "let's keep it a secret" aspect bc I want to share my
appreciation with everyone I know. I like showing my logbook to my family and friends and
they are interested in hearing about my outdoor adventures.

And I really appreciate anyone who puts out "easy" boxes. It is not just families with little
kids that enjoy them. Some adults do as well. Not everyone gets a kick out of being
stumped and frustrated.

I'm not saying don't make it hard if you really enjoy that. I am saying THANK YOU to
people who don'tmake it hard arbitraily.

and also, please, could we just not be so outraged that *gasp* OMG people actually enjoy
they boxes that you create and want to share their appreciation? I am sure this family just
thought the people who placed the boxes would get a kick out of getting "credited" by
them and hearing of their appreciation.

Freelance Mystic
