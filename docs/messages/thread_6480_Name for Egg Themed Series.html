The basket stamp is carved, but readying the box came to halt when it came to name the box. My mind is racing with ideas for boxes, many of which do not have an Easter/spring them. I'd love to read your thoughts about what to call the series - please share here or via email. Here are some ideas:

Egg Land
Egg Land, WI
Egg County, USA (our county used to be known as Cow County, USA)
Eggsquisite
Egg Peak (there's a peak in the park I intend to plant at)
Eggtopia


BTW, when boxes are not necessarily intended to be found in one trip but share a theme, is that a series or something else (if so, what is it called?)?

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2010-03-30 16:19:55 UTC-05:00
If the series consists of boxes by a variety of planters, planted at
different times, I like it when each box has its own listing. This way the
planters can maintain the clues themselves, rather than relying on a single
person to keep clues updated for a group of disparate boxes. You could have
a common prefix and then subsequent egg planters could use that same prefix
when they name their box (similar to the "I94 Traffic Crawl - ...." series
of boxes across several states). However if you do it that way, it would be
best to keep the prefix relatively short.



In addition to the names you have listed, "Eggstravaganza" is another idea.



Looking forward to going Hippity-Hoppity down the hiking trail!



Wisconsin Hiker

WiHi - I was already thinking along those lines about the naming convention - thanks for putting it into clear words.

"Basket Case" - I can definitely see the possibilities there. Even other baskets! Yes, I was planning on this being a year-round series.

While hiking on the bike trail (IAT connector), I filled six text-note pages on my phone with ideas for this project. In my mind, I can think different groups of boxes:

1) PUNNY EGGS - plays on the word "egg" like "Egg-stra Special Delivery" (stork bringing an egg)

2) EGG RAINBOW

3) LB EGGS - Martini Egg, Spring Chick Egg, etc.

4) FOOD - SunnySideUp eggs (of course!), Peeps, etc.

5) EGG TOWN - Sheriff Egg, Teacher Egg

6) WISCONSIN EGGS - Cow Egg, A Packer's Dozen, Bucky Egg

7) CHARACTER/"LITERARY" EGGS - Sherlock Egg, Green Eggs & Ham
