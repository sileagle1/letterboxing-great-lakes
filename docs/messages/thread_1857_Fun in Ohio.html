Fun in Ohio

From: TrailTroll (kanderson@american-community.com) | Date: 2005-09-29 13:49:14 UTC
I want to thank northern Ohio letterboxers for a wonderful trip.
I went through Toledo/Maumee last weekend, and picked up many
letterboxes along the way. There were a variety of styles and
placements, but they were all fun. A special thanks to LindaFay, who
is especially creative. Her Maumee Dogwalk series allows you to
create your own picture, The British are Coming was a really fun
puzzle to solve, and Aunt Phoebe's letterbox is unique (and a little
spooky). The one that my family liked hearing about the best was Is
It Time For Tea? - it requires you to go in a small teahouse and ask
for the box, and the two women working there were really tickled to
see me. One of them had no idea about letterboxing, so the other
woman and I explained it to her, and then we all had a good chuckle
about what would have happened if the novice had been behind the cash
register when I got there.

Another standout was ateam's Farnsworth Metropark series. Very
interesting park, good clues, and well-hidden boxes. And Roxxzilla's
boxes were cute because of the puppy pictures in them. I got most of
the boxes in the Toledo/Maumee/Waterville area, as well as some
farther south in Bowling Green and McComb. A great letterboxing
trip, and I just want to thank all of the letterboxers in that area
for their contributions!
