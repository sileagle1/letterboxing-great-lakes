From: Pam Donovan (p2jd@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2008-07-11 14:10:10 UTC-07:00
In the past there have been a couple of boxes there. I found one of them a couple years ago. But they have since disappeared. I know I spoke to a ranger friend of mine at one time regarding placing boxes in DuPage county and he sited Churchill woods as a reason why they needed to control box placement. Apparently, on the island (accessed from St. Charles road) there had been 5-6 geocaches/letterboxes in a very small area which created a lot of traffic in a small area. Of course the letterbox placer did not know the geocaches were there but that was of little meaning to the ranger powers that be.

To my knowledge there are no boxes there now. Hope this helps.

From: xxxxxxxx (BrighidFarm@comcast.net) | Date: 2008-07-12 16:37:52 UTC-05:00
We had two mystery boxes there a few years back -- personally I thought
Pippi's clues were really great but then she's my sister :-) -- but then
someone else, after finding our boxes, planted two of their own boxes within
about a hundred feet of our boxes and their clues gave away the location to
our boxes.

So we pulled both of our boxes since they were no longer "mysteries". The
family that planted the other two are, to my knowledge, either no longer in
boxing or maybe they moved away or something. Anyway, their boxes are no
longer out there and I no longer see their stamp in any current logbooks.

I've toyed with the idea of putting ours back out there but the
powers-that-be that control the licensing for the letterboxes in DuPage
County tend to make it take forever to get boxes approved in DuPage's forest
preserves so I probably won't bother. We're hoping to move to eastern
Tennessee maybe by the end of next year and by the time the boxes got
approved in DuPage, we'd probably have been in Tennessee for a few years
already. :-) Plus, unless they've changed the rules since they first wrote
'em, the rules for planting boxes in DuPage's preserves were kind of out
there and unrealistic and unneccessary. It was a little too much.
