Holiday Boxing Tour Report

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2007-01-03 03:25:25 UTC
Well, today we were back to "real life", but we had a great 6-
day "Holiday Boxing Tour" from DEC 27 thru JAN 1. I was joking when
I said we were "heading south" to describe our trip to Illinois, but
in reality the weather on 2 days DID make us feel like we had headed
much further. It actually got up to 60 degrees on DEC 29 & 30 and it
was so nice to be outside in weather like this!

We started out with a gorgeous sunrise over Lake Michigan as we left
home on Wednesday DEC 27. We started our boxing as soon as we
crossed the state line with a bunch of Cinemaboxer boxes. We had
found some of their creations before, so we were looking forward to
adding more to our logbook. We had also heard good things about
the "Trip Across America" series and were excited to have a chance to
start our journey. Temperatures were in the 30s but a nippy wind
made it feel colder. However with no snow and no rain to deter us,
we were able to attempt all 3 available 5-state sets. However the
recent rains had caused extensive flooding of the Des Plaines river
and that prevented us from being able to access Maine & Missouri. We
didn't go any further than that since we decided to postpone Set #2
until the waters receded. We loved the images Cherokee Rose 2
selected for the states we did find and are looking forward to
continuing our trip later this year.

The next day was bright & sunny with temperatures near 50, and a
perfect day to spend in the Kickapoo State Park. Since it was the
site of the 2005 "Mining for Boxes" gathering, we had a wide variety
of boxes to look for. This was a very pretty park with lots of
hills, water, and hawks screeching overhead. The park also has an
interesting history and it was amazing to see how well nature could
recover an area used for coal mining.

The next 2 days were quite warm and we were down to only a light
jacket (or no jacket) and enjoyed a picnic lunch outdoors. Not a
normal December day for us! We bopped around one day finding a
variety of boxes scattered about. Since it gets dark so DARNED
early, we had a chance to use our new Christmas presents from Martini
Man's brother & sister-in-law: headlamps! They worked great,
allowing us to have our hands free for logging and stamping, yet
still gave us the ability to focus the light where we needed it.
These will definitely be staying in our packs and were a great gift!

Our favorite day was Saturday when we did the Lance's Fish Tank
series by Kimono Girl. As many of you know, we like to hike, and
this series allowed us to be out hiking for 4 hours with nice water
views and trails (and stamps). This was much better than being in
the house or car on a nice day, and given the time of year, we had
the whole area to ourselves - PERFECT! Later that day we saw a
bluebird flitting through the woods, something we don't think we've
seen before.

Unfortunately the last 2 days were quite rainy, so our boxing was
limited. However we did manage to get to the Boar's Head Festival
series before it gets pulled this weekend. Luckily the weather dried
up late in the afternoon as we were heading home. This allowed us to
reach a major milestone on the Hunt for Pink Panther series - we
found our 2,000th box! A nice way to celebrate the start of the new
year. We hope the rest of you all had a nice holiday and wish
everyone a happy & healthy 2007!

Wisconsin Hiker & Martini Man
