We absolutely agree...we do have some boxes...several that are outside our
normal traveling range. It's awesome to have people who can check up on
boxes and do any light work on them..changing torn or broken bags or
replacing a box that has been cracked, etc. We have had people do this for
some of our boxes and it's a great help to not have to drive 100 miles to
replace a bag. Even when boxes are close, we have found that it's just
better to repair a box...notify the owner. We're already there...no sense
in having someone else who's busy have to run around too. We tend to think
that things will become more like this in the future...locals who don't
really adopt the boxes, but take care of them as if they had....It takes a
village to raise a letterbox. :)

Box Maintenance

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2006-05-14 03:42:39 UTC
Hi!

We led a hike for our hiking club today that followed the same route
as our Norwegian Woods series (5 boxes on a 9 mile hike). Since the
weather wasn't too good today (i.e. almost constant rain), we had a
small turnout for the hike, but on the bright side, this made it
easier for us to check on our boxes. 3 of the 5 boxes were in great
shape, but 2 had a bit of moisture which we tried to dry out. If you
haven't done the series yet (EVEN THOUGH SOME OF YOU WERE NEARBY TO
PLACE/FIND THE TROJAN RABBIT), rest assured that all 5 boxes are
still in place and in fine shape.

Unbeknowst to us, this was the same weekend that there was a 50-mile
run on the Ice Age Trail and a shorter 18-mile run on the Nordic
Trail circuit. As we were walking the 9-mile blue loop, we passed
some of the same people twice that were running. We were amazed when
one hardy runner asked us "Are you letterboxing?". We answered in
the affirmative, but didn't have a chance to find out who this astute
runner was. The parking lot had cars from several states, so we have
no idea who asked us this question.

The 9-mile hike was the easy part. While doing box maintenance we
found a HH that had it's own box and was the largest HH package we
have ever seen. We brought it home with us and will be placing it
somewhere later this month. Just goes to show that there IS a
benefit to checking on your boxes....

After the hike we went to find the Trojan Rabbit (Great stamp!).
Replacing the TROJAN RABBIT box was a horrible exercise in
frustration. It just so happened that we arrived at the cemetery on
the same day that 2 guys were doing grounds maintenance, even though
it was raining. We had no problem retreiving the box, but waited 30-
45 minutes before we could replace it because these guys were too
nearby hoeing & seeding. Drove us crazy!!!!

Wisconsin Hiker

P.S. We have noticed that less than half the Monty Python boxes have
clues posted. Do any of you have any idea when all 40 boxes will be
available?

Box Maintenance

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2007-09-15 23:42:34 UTC
It was a sunny but cool day here in the Milwaukee, WI area today.
Martini Man & I decided to check on some of our series that we hadn't
heard much about, and/or had received "Attempted" messages.

I'm happy to report that all 4 boxes im my "Christmas Botanicals"
series (4 mile hike) are in place and in good shape. Most haven't
had a visitor since last November.

We were also pleased to find all 4 boxes in Martini Man's "Field
Survey" series. Many were damp inside, but since the baggies were
sealed well, the contents were fine. These were a bit of a challenge
to find in late summer (trail conditions are quite different now) and
we saw that some seekers only found some in the series. However we
were impressed that someone found all 4 last month. Martini Man has
added a brief note to the original clues and may add more updates in
the near future.

We then headed over to Lapham Peak to give Double Dragon's "White
Knight" a more watertight home. After that we checked on my "Tweedle
Dee & Tweedle Dum" box and SpringChick's "Humpty Dumpty" box. Both
were in fine shape. We ended our loop by checking on Bindle
Babe's "DRINK ME" box and were happy to find it right where we
expected and in great shape.

A nice afternoon outside, hiking 7-8 miles and finding all 12 boxes
we checked to be in good shape!

Wisconsin Hiker
