From: Deb (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-04-20 09:43:11 UTC
SpringChick has kind of a dual persona. Every summer we go on a
boat trip with some of OldShoe's buddies and their wives. I am the
newest of the bunch and the first year I went along the guys had a
hard time remembering my name (yeh, I know -- DEB can be so
confusing). Anyway, I quickly became "Spring's Chick" in reference
to OldShoe's last name. It kind of stuck into the next year and was
eventually shortened to just SpringChick. I liked it, not because
of the possessive nature of its origin, rather it reminded me
of "Spring Chicken" and it was about the time I hit my 40th b-day.

When I first was beginning letterboxing, I was at the stamp store
with my daughter looking for a sig stamp for our upcoming trip to
Chicago (pre-carving days). I didn't really know for sure what I
was going to use for my trail name, but had a few options in mind
depending on what I found for a stamp. SpringChick was not even one
of the ideas, but my daughter found a stamp that actually said
Spring Chick on it (actually it said "from one Spring Chick to
another" but I sliced off the other words). How unlikely is that?
It seemed destined to be my trail name.

SpringChick

*Big sigh* This is where Tom and I reveal our lack of creativity.
When making a quick signature stamp in the rush to get started with
this new hobby we had come across, Tom carved a neat rendition of
the state of MI, and added our initials (T and J) to it. Hence the
TJ-Mich name. We really are more interesting people than our trail
name might lead you to believe, honest :)! But once we got started
as TJ-Mich, it seemed somehow disloyal to come up with something
more creative; it's amazing how quickly you can become attached to a
name or stamp. Of course now that our 'boxin doggie Maya has her
own carved stamp, we figure that adds some fun to our sign-ins in
the logbooks...
