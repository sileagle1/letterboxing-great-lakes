Our floating Logbook

From: cdem_ltrbox (wp4@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2005-09-28 03:49:16 UTC
Last week, Team Taco nicely emailed us to let us know that the lid
to our box holding Cemetery Stroll #1 was cracked. However, we
dilly-dallied about and didn't get over there and then the
torrential rains came down on Saturday. Well, Ball Magnet and I
went over there on Sunday to replace the box. He went to retrieve
the box. Imagine my horror as I saw a stream of water pouring out
of the crack in the box as he walked back to me. Then imagine my
increasing horror as I opened the box and discovered it was full of
water and the contents were floating!!

Thank goodness Team Taco (the last finders) had zipped all the
baggies up tight and things were dry. Our logbook was floating, but
at least it wasn't waterlogged. I guess we need to find more
durable containers, especially with some of the recent stories about
critters crunching on boxes. Any suggestions on durable containers
and where to get them without spending a fortune? Since I'm asking
for suggestions, where do people get camouflage duct tape--I've
looked at the local hardware and sporting good stores without luck.

Thanks,

BirdWoman

One of my favorite places to get containers is Dollar General. All of the ones here in Champaign/Urbana carry a brand called Aero. I think they sell the larger of the two sizes that I use 2 for $2 and the smaller size 3 for $2, but it's been a while so I don't really remember. I have found them to be quite durable...even when chewed! Several of my Hidden Village boxes have chew marks on them, but no punctures. As for duct tape, I usually get that at Walmart. This time of year, they should have some in the sporting section with the hunting stuff.

From: LindaFay (coachlindafay@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-10-02 14:41:26 UTC
Hi Birdwoman,
I've tired lots of differnt boxes. My favorites du jour are either the
Country Lemonade or any type of powdered drink mix containers. I run
them through the diswasher at least 2 times. Wrap them in camo tape
and then they are ready. I have also cut the top lip down so that I
could get a larger log book into one of these - other wise you either
need a flexable one or small. Haven't had a problem with critters
eating through, yet.

I get my camo tape at Cabelas - mail order - saves driving around
http://www.cabelas.com
