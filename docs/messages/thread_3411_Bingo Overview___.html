Okay, for those who were not around last year, and even for those
who were, as there are a few changes this year -- here is a brief
overview of how the Bingo game will work (complete instructions can
be found in the BINGO1.pdf and BINGO2.pdf files in the FILES section
of the Great Lakes list).

- Anyone who is a member of the Great Lakes Yahoo! group can play

- Each person who is playing will fill in the provided card template
(BINGO-GRID.pdf) with words of their choosing using the word list
provided. There are 24 word choices for each column, B-I-N-G-O.
The words you can choose are a mix of letterbox names, trailnames,
event names and general terms relating to letterboxing. Some of the
words have stipulations attached with using them -- i.e. to use a
letterbox name, you must have either found that letterbox or be the
person who planted it, etc. But don't worry -- there are plenty of
general terms so that even a brank-spanking new letterboxer who has
never found a box, planted a box, exchanged with anyone or attended
a gathering, still will have some choices and enough options to fill
their card.

- A copy of your card gets mailed to me along with an item to go
into the prize pot, otherwise known as the Bingo Booty. There are
examples of things people sent last year in the instructions, but
feel free to get creative -- anything a letterboxer could use or
enjoy while letterboxing. I *MUST* have your entry by Dec. 27 -- NO
EXCEPTIONS!

- Beginning January 1, I will draw a word each day (there are 120
words total) and announce it in a post at the GL list. If you have
that word on your card, you cross it off.

- There are 5 games, for a total of 7 winners:

* The "big game" is full card -- first, second and third places
will be winners
* 4 interim games where (first place winner only on each) -- 4
corners, straight line, "X" and perimeter.


This was a lot of fun last year and helped to pass the cold winter
months here in the GL region. If you have questions after reading
through the instructions, feel free to post to the list as somebody
else may be wondering the same thing.

Best of luck to all!

SpringChick
