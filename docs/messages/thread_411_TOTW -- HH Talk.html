From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-07-14 10:01:21 UTC
I have been a bit preoccupied and completely forgot about TOTW...
Since I will be out of town this coming weekend and Monday, thought
I would post this now and run it through the end of next week.

On another list I am on, the topic of HH came up and I thought it
warranted more discussion. The question that was asked pertained to
HH size and container -- should they have their own? How big?
Other considerations?

As we try to make our boxes smaller and our hiding places more
discreet, the opportunity to place a HH either inside the container
or along side it in the hiding spot diminishes. Likewise, I am sure
most of us have been "stuck" with a HH that was so large it was
difficult to find a place to stash it. I know I carried one around
in my pack for nearly a year before ditching it at a gathering...

What are your thoughts on HH? And, more importantly -- where do you
think they all disappear to? I envision a large pile of HH partying
off in netherland somewhere, right alongside the gathering of
mismatched socks looking for mates.

SpringChick

From: (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2004-07-14 12:42:58 UTC-05:00
We've found 8 HH so far and one WAS in a rather large cylinder. It wouldn't fit into any boxes we found, so eventually we just used a large freezer bag to hold both the HH and the box we had found. We figured that whoever found the box couldn't miss the HH that way. Afterwards, I read on one of the message boards that some people think it is a bad thing to put a box in a bag, so perhaps what we did wasn't the best approach.

One idea I've had is to place a larger HH in a spot near a box, then put a note in the box with directions on how/where to find the nearby HH. This assumes the HH is in a waterproof container.

Generally, I think it is probably best to just use a sturdy baggie to hold the HH and its logbook. That minimizes the space required and therefore maximizes the chances of placing it in a box.

Where do they go? I've assumed they're just slowly circulating around. Unfortunately not everyone sends an email to HH originator or to Wanda & Pete so the HH website can be updated. Using that website, we like to check on those HH we've found to see where else they've ended up.

Wisconsin Hiker

From: Doodle & Deedle Bug (doodle_n_deedle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-07-15 07:11:04 UTC-07:00
You know, I've been dealing internally with this question for quite some time now (since about April, when I came up with an idea for a letterbox but decided it might make a better hitchhiker since the concept was supposed to be all about letterboxer unity, etc etc, and I thought a hitchhiker would get it a bigger audience). The stamp is carved, but I've now been debating how to create a logbook. I could just staple some sheets of paper together, but sometimes I'm a horrible perfectionist (just ask Deedle how many times I've stamped and restamped images to get the "perfect stamp" for our collection while on the trail ;) and want the log book to look semi-professional...or at least, ensure that it won't get crumpled or damaged too badly in its travels. And, because I'm so protective, I've been trying to search for a container that I could put a hitchhiker stamp in and a logbook and have it small enough to fit inside larger containers. Film canisters immediately come to mind, but
then the logbook would have to be rolled, and from what I've seen on the trail, rolled logbooks can be hard to stamp into, and/or the first page gets ruined from residual ink left on the stamp. I think I finally found the perfect box - a plastic container that travel Q-Tips came in. Maybe 2 by 3 inches, but that's just a guesstimate...I haven't actually measured it yet, but it looks like it would fit inside the containers we're planning on using for our full-fledged letterboxes. I'm not sure how well the contents would fit, though, with constant packaging and repackaging. So maybe I'll just have to give in and put the hitchhiker in a plastic baggie...

I know, I'm sure that doesn't help anyone out :) As for where the missing hitchhikers go, hmmm....I could see them very well partying with the missing socks and the lost car keys in a field somewhere....

Doodle aka Diane.
