10th Anniversary Gathering -- Is our dog welcome?

From: Kermit (kermitboxer@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-08-18 17:13:18 UTC-05:00
Hi everyone --

On a serious note here (for once, for me): My husband and I are planning to
attend the 10th anniversary gathering in October at Whitefish Dunes SP. We
have a dog, she's about 40 lbs and a Shepherd/Shiba Inu/Chow/Dingo mix (as
far as we can tell -- she was a stray I adopted). Nala is our best friend,
and our frequent companion letterboxing.

We would love to bring her to the gathering -- she is very friendly with
people, excellent with children. If there is food nearby she will beg
shamelessly. :-) She is not voice controlled, and so we NEVER allow her
off her leash or out of our control. We'd enjoy having her with us on the
trails.

However -- I know that many people are quite afraid of dogs, or very
uncomfortable around them. I wanted to ask everyone who is attending if
they are comfortable with our dog attending, or they prefer that she remain
with her grandma here in Milwaukee.

Please feel free to e-mail me privately with questions or with your
preference. We will definitely respect people's requests for this to be a
pet-free weekend.

thanks so much.
-mph (Kermit)

RE: [LbGLK] 10th Anniversary Gathering -- Is our dog welcome?

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2008-08-18 20:12:45 UTC-05:00
It does look like Whitefish Dunes allows dogs, even on a certain section of
the beach. However it does prohibit them from several locations, including
a.. Buildings
a.. Picnic areas and picnic shelters
a.. Playgrounds

Here's a link to all the rules about bringing pets to Wisconsin State Parks:
http://dnr.wi.gov/org/land/parks/pets/

And another link about pets on the beach:
http://dnr.wi.gov/org/land/parks/specific/whitefish/policies/pets.html

Also, since the DNR website for Whitefish Dunes only shows photos of the
shelter in winter, I've uploaded some shots we took last fall. If you'd
like to check out the location, you can see them here:
http://ph.groups.yahoo.com/group/LbGLK/photos/view/3582?b=1&m=s&o=0

It is a very pretty spot!

Wisconsin Hiker
