Great Lakers, I posted this over on the big list but I know that many
folks don't read that group as frequently these days.
So I'm posting here, too.

Here's a question that'll likely get a variety of reactions.but here
we go:
What are recommended responses to encountering dog(s)on the trail?
Here's the situation: The park does not require leashes or have any
posted dog related rules. I was walking on the trail when I saw an
energetic, young hound-type dog with another hiker who was far behind
me. I heard the dog running up behind me and then past. He stopped
and then turned and ran back to me and jumped with his front paws on
my chest.
I sharply commanded, "down"! Eventually he ran away. But then twice
more during this short hike the same dog did the same thing.
What would you have done?
I admit that I am not a dog-person.
I'm especially interested in what ideas you dog lovers would suggest.
Thanks,

azobox

From: Delirium (donaldson_home@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-05-03 06:25:07 UTC-07:00

That's a perfect case of poor dog ownership. The hiker should A) have control of his/her dog at all times, even if there is no leash involved and B)have apologized to you and then made sure the dog didn't approach you again.

As a dog lover, I would never put my dogs in danger by allowing them to approach stange people. Sure, you're a well-mannered human, but there are plenty of people out there who, in the same situation, would have responded with kicks and punches- not something I'd like my dogs to go through.

But, the owner you met obviously doesn't share the same philosophy as I do, so I guess YOU have to be the responsible party. If you're the brave type, you could talk to the owner (if you can see them), and explain what that dog did, and could they please make sure it doesn't happen again? This is, of course, assuming that the wayward dog owner is simply not aware of the ruckus their dog is causing. Some people, I'm sure, know, and just don't care. In which case, I suggest you just start screaming in a high pitched voice, and yelling "I'm a lawyer, I'm an injury lawyer! Get your dog away from me!" *grin*

I know someone on the other list who carries pepper spray just for that reason, but again (as a dog lover), I'd hate to suggest that.

Cheers,

Delirium

From: Brendaj@Ac- (Brendaj@Ac-Claim.com) | Date: 2004-05-03 15:19:29 UTC-04:00
For those of us that are responsible dog owners, we would try to never let
something like that happen. Unfortunately, there are a lot of people out
there that just plain do not think. My husband is not a dog lover as he was
bit as a child. This can be a very traumatic thing for the rest of ones
life. I have taught him several ways to counteract when something like this
happens (depending on whether the dog is friendly or not). You will never
be able to stop people from being stupid but that is what gives you the
right to ask the owner to please keep his dog from "attacking" you. Pet
the dog or hold his collar till the owner shows up and explain that you do
not appreciate his dog mauling you even if it is in a friendly manner.
Maybe it will sink in and maybe it won't but at least you tried. Don't let
one bad apple spoil the whole bunch, Okay?
