Based on an earlier recommendation from Gooble Gobble, the location

I'll take any help I can get!

From: (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2008-01-22 10:37:48 UTC-06:00
Here's some more info about the park that makes me even more excited - sounds like a very SCENIC area for hiking (and of course you all know that we'll plant our boxes on one of those "challenging trails").

Located just forty minutes east of the Twin Cities, the park offers excellent opportunities for hiking, crosscountry skiing, swimming, picnicking, and hike-in camping in a quiet, natural setting. To preserve the natural character of the park, development has been kept to a minimum. The campground, swimming beach and interior of the park are accessible only by trail.

The steep terrain offers challenging hiking and skiing plus the promise of a rewarding natural experience. Afton State Park lies on the bluffs overlooking the St. Croix River. The park is cut by deep ravines which drop three hundred feet to the river. Outcrops of sandstone jut from the side of the ravines. The rugged terrain affords spectacular views of the St. Croix Valley.

From: Gooble Gobble (gooble_gobble_3@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-01-22 16:16:23 UTC-08:00
Just you two wait!!! Challenging is just the
beginning! Those 300+ foot elevation gains might not
sound like much until you consider the angle of the
slope you're walking/climbing up. "The Wall"??? Lapham
Peaks infamous hill has nothing on some of the butt
busters you'll encounter at Afton State Park. As if
one huge hill isn't enough, try going up and down, up
and down, a few times. I'm going to start training
tomorrow.

For those less masochistic, don't worry, the terrain
is quite varied and I'm sure the boxes will be planted
all over the place. There are 20+ miles of trail after
all.

The views of the river valley are beautiful, and it
makes all those climbs worth it. Even getting to the
trail head is very scenic. You walk down past
playgrounds and picnic tables at a meandering slow
decline. There is a great scenic outlook and then
down, down, down you go to the main trails and the
beach area. You'll find picnic tables right on the
river and trails going everywhere. There's a really
cool old bridge made of R.R. ties that oozes tar when
it's hot out and fills the air with a wonderful pine
scent on your way. All in all it's the perfect place
to have a gathering. It has everything you could want
in a park and then some.

I want to start carving right now, but we don't have a
theme yet. So..... ideas anyone?


Gobble Gobble

I have one that I got kind of excited over at work (I was a little
bored today). The theme I came up with was "Westward ho!" Minnesota
is the western-most state in the Great Lakes area, so most people
would have to head west to come to the gathering. The park is located
on the St. Croix River, just a few miles north of where it meets the
Mississippi and both rivers were instrumental in the development of
the state. Minnesota also turns 150 this year (May 8th I think).
Laura Ingalls Wilder lived just across the border in Wisconsin and
later in the western half of the state. The last stand of the James
Gang was in Minnesota. You can still see paddleboats on the rivers.
The letterboxes could be prairie/frontier/Old West themed.

Anyone have any other ideas?
