IL Boxing Weekend Report

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2005-10-16 22:09:28 UTC-05:00
Wow! What a great weekend for boxing - sunny, clear blue skies, great
temperatures for boxing and no bugs - what more could we ask for? Well, the
"more" that we got was a box-rich area with lots of good carvers!



We planned our weekend around the Haunted Trails series. After meeting
CMoon, Kookaburra and Squeek at the Haunted Harvest Gathering, and seeing
their cool signature stamps, we figured they probably had some cool stamps
in the boxes they planted. We were right! The Haunted Trails were the
first boxes we looked for on Saturday morning and we were very pleased to
find them all and see the interesting stamps. We also REALLY enjoyed the
Biker Chick series. We found one by walking (is there a true story behind
the "Tandumb" box?!?). We then found a gas station where we could get air
put in our bike tires. We found the other 4 in the series (and the Barn
Theater) by biking the trail. It was great to be out on our bikes on such a
great day and such a pleasant trail. Another box that we are wondering
about is the "Little Box on the Prairie". What WAS this area? Why are
there sidewalks here?!? Two "indoor" boxes we found also provided an excuse
for treats. Yes, we were the happy consumers of caramel apples (Happy
Apple) and pumpkin ice cream (Come Grumpy, Go Happy)!



One amazing thing about Saturday is that we actually found EVERY SINGLE box
that we looked for. That doesn't happen to us often. Another highlight was
that we hit our F1000 mark. And last, but not least, is that we had a
fantastic Chinese meal at Lao Sze Chuan. Finding this restaurant is also
due to a letterboxer, but I don't remember who. In the early days of this
discussion group, SpringChick asked us all to "introduce ourselves".
Someone mentioned that they liked food and found the chowhound.com site
useful. Well, I'd like to thank whoever that was because ever since then I
research restaurants for our travel destinations using this site. It has
been a source of many good meals for us.



Today we got to see another "haunted trail". The trail that the "Girl with
a Sword" series (great stamps) is on was the site of a Halloween event
yesterday. We therefore got to see all the spooky effect this morning as we
strolled the trail. We found several other nice boxes but then hit our
first "couldn't find" in Springdale Park. We searched every tree for
"Softball Anyone" with no success. However as a substitute, what did we
find? Prairie Dog! We had noticed she had stamped into several of the same
boxes as us with today's date and hoped our paths might cross. After doing
an exchange, she showed us the tree she thought she had found the box in
earlier this year, and confirmed that it looked like it was missing. Oh
well, it's probably impossible to have TWO perfect days in a row!



We were impressed by how much Forest Preserve land there is in this area.
Very nice, especially with the trees beginning to change colors. Two of the
non-forest boxes that we really enjoyed were the Cemetery Stroll series by
the Waltzing Pigs. The clues were very interesting and informative!



We'll have to plan some more IL trips. Too bad the daylight is diminishing
and the weather will soon be changing to something less enjoyable. Next
Sunday we're off on a "Boxes, Cheese and Beer" excursion to Monroe & New
Glarus with a few friends!
