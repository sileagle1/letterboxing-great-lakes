Store-bought vs. hand-carved

From: blueskyinkazoo (blueskyinkazoo@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-09-06 20:04:01 UTC
After following the discussions on hand-carved stamps vs. store-bought,
I felt I had to bring up the opposite situation. I have planted 4 LB
and have a 5th ready to go. I jumped right in and hand-carved all my
stamps (which really is fun and not too difficult). I expected because
my clues specify my stamps are hand-carved, I would attract those whose
own signature stamps were also hand-carved. I check my boxes fairly
reguarly and I would say only about 1 in 10 of the log-book stamps are
hand-carved - I don't know if this is typical or just reflective of my
area. I think people probably chose my LBs based on location - maybe
after you have developed a reputation for nice hand-carved stamps,
people actually go looking specifically for yours - I'm a long way from
that yet! Because I don't have a lot of opportunity to do as much
finding as I would like, I look forward to seeing the stamps in my
planted LBs and it is much more fun to see the hand-carved ones!
Blue Sky in Kazoo

I'm glad someone else noticed this too. I haven't planted yet but
starting with my second box (the first I was to excited to remember to
look through the logbook) that most the boxers had store bought
stamps. I also felt this was a let down I really wanted to see others
art as well, even if it wasn't my own book.
I was originally looking to have my personal stamp custom made but
decided to carve on my own cause I wanted to get out there and start
faster and since that first one I'm hooked. I love to carve and I love
to see others carvings, I have never been disapointed at a hand carved
stamp no matter how "hacked up" some have been. I can't wait to get
the money to start planting my own boxes, I'm just mostly worried
about planting to many boxes so close to each other because I love to
carve. (this will be a topic I bring up when I get closer to planting,
probably this winter.)

Re: [LbGLK] Store-bought vs. hand-carved

From: Drucilla Prince (wwlilsis@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-09-08 14:42:51 UTC-07:00
DUH!!! You need to consider the following: most of us who get into this want to get out right away and find boxes. People, newbies, are not familiar with the whole stamp carving deal, what it entails, where to get the stuff. When you are in the know, you have to wait several days for materials to arrive etc. you just want to get out there and do it so people go to the craft store and get a stamp. Now of course, later, one should try and carve their signature stamp, but I, for example, could not possibly carve my signature stamp with its detail. It also after all the boxes I have found really is a part of me and I don't really want to change it. It is neat to see who came to your box but the real treasures are the stamps you put out and find. Keep carving, there's really no excuse to not to be carving stamps!!
