MILWAUKEE LAKESHORE BOXES MAINTENANCE REPORT

From: agentink_and_zfan (agentink_and_zfan@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-11-14 00:01:44 UTC
Hello everyone:

Today was a perfectly eerie day along Lake Michigan -- inspiring clouds, strong winds,
crashing waves -- so we decided to perform annual maintenance of our lakeshore series
boxes. All of the following info has been updated on the corresponding LBNA clue pages
as well. Here is the update:

1. South Shore Series

The south shore series boxes are missing. The City of Milwaukee or County of Milwaukee
appears to have removes 100s of trees, poured an asphalt path, and rebuilt the
breakwater along what was a wonderful natural area on the southern shore south of
downtown. This is the new "extension" to the Oak Leaf Trail and the bicyclists do indeed
have a nice path to get them from point A to point B. Unfortunately, in the process all
three boxes were destroyed by the workers. The box status has been changed to REPAIR
on the LBNA site and we will replace the boxes in the next few weeks.

2. Lake Park Series

The Lake Park Series was our first placement of boxes. This is one of our favorites places
in Milwaukee and so we were glad to see that 2 or the 3 boxes are intact and in-place.
Maintenance was done on box 1 -- new camo-box, new logbook, and double-bagged
contents. Box 3 was similarly maintained and verified. Box 2 is missing. The box will be
replaced in the next few weeks and the LBNA site will change to reflect the replacement.

3. Shipwrecked Series Box 1: Spiderbox

Our favorite plant is still in place -- healthy and warm -- while crying out an SOS for
visitors. Great walk along the lake today -- the winds were pushing the water up toward
Michigan ( storm surge for sure, maybe a seiche? http://www.geo.msu.edu/geo333/
seiches.htm ) and the beach was well exposed for walking. No shipwreck souvenirs but
lots of rocks and shells.

4. Shipwrecked Series Box 2: The Lost Schooner

The Lost Schooner was confirmed even more lost -- missing with no sound of a mayday to
be heard. One can only guess that it has slipped below the crashing waves of Lake
Michigan and now lies at the bottom of the brink. For those of you that visited this great
shipwreck along the shore, you know the STRONG ATTRACTION that the box had for the
location. Thus, we are convinced that the box was taken because the forces of nature
should not have been able to remove the box from that spot. Lily stopped to visit and she
decided to come home with us for some hot tea since the wreck was no longer present.
We hope to find the wreck and return it to the shore but salvage operations will take at
least a few weeks.

Agent Ink and Z-fan
