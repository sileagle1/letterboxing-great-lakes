Snow, Snow, More Snow and Lakeshore Flooding?

From: lhallaxs (lhallaxs@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-02-09 19:37:33 UTC
I don't think that I have posted before but we have only been letter
boxing for a few months and it got put on hold because of our
wonderful Michigan weather. It got too cold after our first find in
November and now we have way too much snow on the ground and adding
more ever day and today they are calling for 50 mph winds along Lake
Superior and lakeshore flooding - who the heck has ever heard of
Lakeshore flooding in February. Anyway what do you tell a 2 1/2 year
old when they keep asking to go letter boxing again. I keep telling
him we have to wait till the snow goes away but they just can't quite
grasp that concept. I guess I should be using this prime time to
carve stamps. I don't mean to sound like I am complaining but I am so
jealous (at the moment) of those folks who live in warming climates.
By the way the event held in Grand Rapids last weekend sounded like
a lot of fun but since we live way north in the Upper Peninsula it was
a little too far for us. Love reading all the posts!

Mama Bear and Big Foot Bug

Well we're also sick of winter, but have managed to make it out a few times,
mostly to plant boxes. We aren't responsible for any little people, so we
have a bit more flexibility than some others. It was a nice afternoon
today, so we were able to get out to plant 2 more boxes. We saw LOTS of
people out enjoying the 16+ new inches of snow. They were ice fishing,
sledding and cross-country skiing. The sun even peeped out occasionally,
which made it really bright, reflecting on all that white snow. We put on
our boots & gaiters and tromped through the snow to get the boxes planted.
Temperatures were reasonable (34 degrees), so it was a good time to be out.
Tomorrow on the other hand will be a different story - "Highs around 1
below. ... Wind chill readings 29 below to 39 below zero." Yuck!!

I think you're looking for cleats rather than gaiters. Gaiters go over your
shins and prevent snow from getting into your boots. Here's a link as an
example: http://www.rei.com/product/743529

Cleats (or traction gear) usually fit over your boot to provide tractions
when walking on ice or slippery snow. Here's a link an an example of one
type: http://www.rei.com/product/760280

Both should be available at outdoor sporting stores, if they aren't already
sold out.

Too cold to go out today though - stay safe & warm!
