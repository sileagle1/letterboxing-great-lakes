I found some stuff at a Dollar Tree store which is working great for me (and
of course the price is right). It is called Fix All Adhesive and is made by
the Super Glue Corporation. It must be some sort of contact cement, because
it utilizes the same dry-first step SpringChick described. It says use a
thin coat on each piece and let dry for 10 minutes, with maximum strength
achieved after 24 hours. For stronger bonding they say apply a thicker coat
and let dry for 30 minutes. (I gently take my embossing gun to it to speed
it up a little. No, I don't inhale the fumes!!)



I've been mounting my stamps with thin basswood. It gives it just a little
extra body without adding too much bulk or weight. I seal the wood with
clear nail polish, just because I'm usually working on stuff in the wee
hours and it's a lot easier to just use it than deal with spraying stuff
outside. An alternate mounting method I use utilizes this self-adhesive
cardstock I get at work. I apply several layers and trim close. Works
well.
