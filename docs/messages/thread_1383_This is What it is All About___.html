'boxing in Cemeteries (was Re: [LbGLK] This is What it is All About...)

From: Doodle & Deedle Bug (doodle_n_deedle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-06-05 09:06:21 UTC-07:00
I loved reading SpringChick's blog post about her
recent letterboxing experience. I know that people's
thoughts about hiding and/or searching for letterboxes
in cemeteries can be varied, but really what it comes
down to is the same as hiding/searching for boxes in
any location - being respectful to the land and your
surroundings. In college I referred to myself as a
"cemetery walker." I went to a smaller college in a
more rural part of Ohio, and the cemetery was just on
the outskirts of town. Often times I'd take walks
there, either with another friend or alone. When I
was upset it gave me a calm, serene place to just be.
Other times, esp. when walking with my one friend, we
liked to read all those old tombstones, admire the
artwork of some of the monuments, and wonder about the
histories of all these now departed people. Many
people are surprised to learn that cemeteries used to
be treated akin to public parks. People would bring a
picnic lunch with them and spend the afternoon at
their departed loved one's grave. I've noticed a
trend back towards this behavior recently, which I
think is nice. I think it's so sad when I pass an
old, apparently forgotten about cemetery that you can
tell isn't tended to any longer.

I also like to think that my family properly instilled
in me respect for cemeteries (which probably also led
to my liking to walk around old, rural cemeteries).
Often on Sunday's we'd go on a ride with my
grandparents to the cemetery where some of my
relatives were buried. We had our specified route to
drive through the cemetery and stop at everyone's
graves. We'd bring a bucket full of flowers, divy-ing
them up between all the graves. We'd also bring my
grandfather's box of gardening tools to trim the grass
so that it wouldn't grow over the stones, etc. And
teaching by example really works. Deedle's and my
grandparents are now all passed on and buried in the
same cemetery. We visited their graves on Memorial
Day weekend, and commented how we needed to come back
with gardening tools to keep the grass from growing
onto the stones....(although, we won't bring a
weedwacker like we saw someone else using that day).

Doodle's rules (from her grandfather and mother) for
being in a cemetery (whether letterboxing or just
visiting):

- Never play your car stereo inside the gates
- Don't run (even if there's a really wonderful hill
right next to your great-grandfather's grave that's
just asking for little kids to run up and down it)
- Don't step on the grave stones

:)

Doodle Bug.
