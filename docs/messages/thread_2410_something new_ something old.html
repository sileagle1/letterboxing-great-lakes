From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-04-21 00:33:52 UTC-04:00
Ahhh, so it is magnet - "backed"? I was having visions of baking some kind of dough imprinted by a rubber stamp, and not quite sure how that then became the stamp. Technically it would not be a find per LbNA guidelines as there is no logbook (just like finding a box with a missing stamp is technically not a find). From the LbNA FAQ:

"a letterbox can only count as Found when the letterboxer obtains a stamp image from any stamp associated with that box, and their personal stamp image has been imprinted (or attempted) in the logbook or other part of the box."

I guess if you stamped your personal stamp image onto the back of the rubber stamp itself you could count it, but that would get kind of messy after a while if everyone did that ;-) But, a lot of people don't keep track, and even those who do, it comes down to whatever each person calls a find.

SpringChick
