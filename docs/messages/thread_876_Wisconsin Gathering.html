Re: [LbGLK] Wisconsin Gathering

From: Steve and Koreen (col.one923@spwl.net) | Date: 2004-11-15 13:47:47 UTC-06:00
We want to echo this as well. We had a great time! Meeting everyone
was an added bonus for us too, because I think it "validated" the hobby
of letterboxing with the kids a bit more. I think when they met some of
the people who planted the boxes we have been finding, or who have
stamped in those boxes before us, they somehow became more excited about
letterboxing. The kids talked all the way home about the day, who they
met, etc. (Many of you made really nice impressions with our kids.)
When we got home they sat around the table looking at their logbooks to
see who they may have missed, which stamps they liked best and which of
the hunts we did yesterday they liked best. At the gathering, our six
year old son took his log book and wandered around seeking others to
exchange with all on his own. Everyone was so friendly and encouraging
with the kids and I really appreciate that.

Dragon, we did find your Environmental Classroom box while there, but it
was dark by the time we did. I think we were some of the last few to
come in after dark to get something to eat. Our very last find was the
White Horse and for some reason that stumped us. We looked around one
last time and found it at last and it was hard to keep quiet about it,
'cause we gave it away to Czeltic Girl! Yes, Leapin' Lizards, we have
to work on our "stealth mode".

Thanks everyone for a great afternoon!
The Puddlejumpers

From: jkgrage (jkgragee@aol.com) | Date: 2004-11-16 18:58:23 UTC

We would also love to chime in about the wonderful time we had
Sunday. I also second the notion about how seeing all you wonderful
letterboxers 'validated' this hobby especially for my daughter. My
children often look at me and roll their eyes about my activities and
at least they now know we're not out there with our stamps, log
books, ink etc.... etc... etc... all alone.

A major thanks to Dragon for planning this gathering and for all of
you who placed boxes for us to find. What a different experience
waiting in line to stamp in, or hiding around the bushes so we can
wait, and find the box ourselves. We are most often all alone on the
trails and sometimes the only car in the parking lot of the nature
preserve (o.k., maybe we shouldn't letterbox in the rain anymore).
Thanks again to everyone who shared with us and we hope to meet up
again.
