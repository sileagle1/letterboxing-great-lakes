WI 10th Anniversary Event

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2008-08-30 02:30:14 UTC
Yay! I just finished carving my "Malted Milk" stamp for the event,
which is now only 5 weeks away. I have also received 5 stamps from
other out-of-state contributors who carved images related to either a
Wisconsin "First" or an impression of Wisconsin. A few more guest
carver contributions are expected for the event, so we should have a
nice variety of carvings when we find those boxes.

We're looking forward to a nice time in the always scenic Door
County, and are especially looking forward to seeing several of our
LB friends to hear about their summer adventures (such as CR2 &
Hawkeye).

We're also hoping to meet some of the newer boxers in our area. So
far I see Kermit signed up. We're looking forward to meeting this
dramatic playright. What about Popcorn Lady? or WalesFlyerMary?
Terrio? It also looks like someone from Texas is signed up?! Snow
Cat?

Hopefully everyone will be able to get out this holiday weekend to
find a box or otherwise enjoy the great outdoors as the summer starts
winding down!

Wisconsin Hiker



Re: [LbGLK] WI 10th Anniversary Event

From: Carol Osada (cjosada@hotmail.com) | Date: 2008-08-29 22:18:38 UTC-06:00
I have complete a couple stamps for the 10th Anniversary Gathering too. They are ready to mail off to The Dragon soon. Not sure if we should mail a freezer container too, or just the contents. I hope Popcorn Lady signs up. I have been contacted by several new letterboxers in the Lake Co. area of NE Illinois too. I love to talk with them about letterboxing because their enthusiasm reminds me of how I felt when I first started to find boxes. Years later, I still get a rush when I find a box. Letterboxers are good people!

For the Door County Gathering, I have already made reservations at The Wagon Trail Resort in Rowleys Bay, WI. for Fri-Sun. Looking forward to three days of fun, friendship, boxes, and relaxation in that peaceful area.

Several people, including the Brat Pack, are planning to meet in Long Grove at Village Tavern at 4:00 on Saturday. Some new boxes are anticipated, so I'm looking forward to finding them. We can enjoy the Irish Fest all afternoon and then, after we meet and eat, there is a men's best legs in kilts contest. LOL! Just some additional silliness for the day.

Looking forward to seeing other letterboxers, newbies too, at Long Grove tomorrow.

Beetle
