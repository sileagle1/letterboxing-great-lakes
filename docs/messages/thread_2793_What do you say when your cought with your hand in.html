I've almost always got the dogs with me so they provide good cover. If
I'm caught searching, then I say that the dog pooped somewhere around
here and I'm trying to find it to clean it up. No one ever offers to
help or lingers over that one. Both dogs have backpacks and carry their
own gear (water, snacks, first aid, etc) and for most people, the sight
of a dog in a backpack is distracting enough that they hardly see me at
all. I've only once had some one look beyond the dogs to wonder what I'm
doing, which caught me off guard but my current excuse is that I'm
keeping a log of the dogs' miles for their backpacking titles (not
untrue, but I don't take the log with me).

I've been caught scouting for places to hide a box, sans dogs, and
generally tell people I'm an entomologist, which I've found is a good way
to end conversations, since either they don't know the word or they find
bugs icky.

Katy
