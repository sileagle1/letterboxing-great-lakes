Great Lakes Gathering (long, but please read)

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-11-11 02:10:01 UTC
It looks like the majority of people like the idea of keeping
the 'official' regional Great Lakes Gathering alive, and I would
agree with that. I am also very happy to see so many state and local
gatherings popping up throughout the region and as WI Hiker, feel
that all gatherings are open to anyone and hope that these smaller
gatherings do encourage folks to make a trip over to another area
and meet letterboxers there as well as do some boxing in a new place.

There is never going to be a place or a time for the regional
gathering that will please everyone and the best we can do is strive
for consistency. One suggested option was to set both date and
location to remain the same from one year to the next. Making the
date consistent is certainly a step in the right direction, but I
hesitate about keeping the location the same year after year. Not
only does this have the potential to become stale, it also runs the
risk of becoming an event localized to the state where it is being
held every year. So I've decided to take the suggestion of rotating
between states according to a predetermined schedule. Having a
preset schedule will enable people to make advanced plans, while the
rotation will provide variety, greater accessibility and
availability of hosts.

The date will be moved back to that of the initial Great Lakes
Gathering -- the Saturday after Memorial Day. Hopefully if this
date does not work for you this year, it will work out in a future
year. We all have jobs and kids and commitments and busy lives and
what works for one person invariably causes conflicts for another --
it is just the nature of the beast.

I have not yet set a rotation schedule for location as it will
depend on who is willing to host the 2006 event. I am throwing
aside all previous input and starting new with suggestions for
location as I am not sure how the established date may affect
previous offers. If you are willing to host or assist with hosting
the 2006 Great Lakes Gathering to be held on June 3, 2006, please
contact me offlist. After this year's location has been decided, I
will set the rotation for future years and add it to the files to
have on record. Going forward, each year during the month of June I
will ask for volunteers to coordinate the gathering for the
following year -- to be held on the Saturday after Memorial day in
the state next in the rotation.

Thank you everyone for your input.

SpringChick

From: duncan53211 (duncan53211@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-11-11 03:49:11 UTC
It seems to me there hasn't been enough people heard from about this
yet. Less than 24 hours of requested input is not enough. Some of us
are on vacation and others don't read the list every day. The polls
to decide all this were up and gone before some of us even knew they
existed!

This would have been my 2 cents, or rather a reminder, that this HAD
been all discussed and decided on just after the IL GL gathering. It
WAS decided by the group at that time that we would have a GL
gathering every mid-June - date to be set by the host. It was decided
that the GL gathering would bounce back and forth east/west among the
states each year on a rotating basis. It was also decided that
individual states would have their gatherings in the fall so as not to
interfere with the GL gathering. This is why WI has had their last
two gatherings in the fall. Reading through the posts, I couldn't
believe no one mentioned any of this!

Please, if we are going to re-work all of this gathering stuff, let's
take more time and input before making final decisions!

Dragon

From: (katy@dsa132.org) | Date: 2005-11-11 04:20:34 UTC
I like the idea of varying location. To me, having the gathering in the
same place every year is a bit too exclusionary. Yes, we can all travel
to that place, but is it really fair if the gathering is always 10 hours
away from Letterboxer X and only 30 minutes from Lb'erY? Not to mention
that for me, at least, a big part of the fun is exploring a new location
and seeing new sites.

I also like the idea of having a set date, in theory, but I suspect that
it won't work well in actuality, and that more people will be
willing/able to host if there is some flexibility to the date.

Katy

From: azobox (jparkerg@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-11-11 10:03:30 UTC
Having wrestled with the pros and cons of places and dates for the GL
annual Gathering, both publicy and privately, I have to say that this
decision is likely the most workable.

As SpringChick notes in her post, there was no combination that was
going to please everyone. However it seemed as though a consensus was
emerging to support the decision she made. We've been tossing this
around for nearly a month in various threads and it was time to get it
settled.

Although the event is more than six months away, many people are asked
to reserve 2006 vacation time at the first of the year. Now at least
we know which weekend to hold.

So, SpringChick, thanks for undertaking this responsibility. Getting
agreement from a diverse group of letterboxers, especially a group of
independent thinking upper-midwesteners is like herding the proverbial
cats. (I don't think we're all in the corral yet, but we're heading in
the same direction).

I look forward to seeing the plans for GL 2006 emerge and hope that
I'll be able to participate.

azobox

Re: Great Lakes Gathering (long, but please read)

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-11-11 12:07:39 UTC
There were no polls. My earlier efforts to go the democratic route
with this were met with resistance. Due to changes in circumstances
and rising questions as to the future of the event, a decision
needed to be made. I realize there was only a single day for input
and there may have been folks on vacation or just away from their
computer, however it was important to me to get a general feel for
what people were thinking, not hear from every single person.

I am sorry, although I was in attendance at the IL gathering, I
honestly do not recall any of the conversations you refer to. I
would never have agreed to the date moving around like that as it
has always been my intent to have an established date for this event
to aid people in making plans. As for more time in making the
decision, I think that this has dragged on long enough and needed to
be settled.

As the founder of the Great Lakes Gathering, I have made the
decision as to the future of this event based on input from the
group. Hopefully this decision will work for the majority of
people, although I realize that with a group of 200 members, it
would be very difficult to meet the needs of every person.

SpringChick
