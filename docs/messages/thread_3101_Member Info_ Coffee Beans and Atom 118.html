Hi everyone,

Atom 118 and I live in Chicago, and we are science geeks who work in a
lab at the University of Chicago where we met about 3.5 years ago. We
started boxing in January 2006 after Atom's sister Fermont told us about
it. She was coming to Chicago to visit the next weekend, so we knew we
had to fit some letterboxing into our weekend activities. But first, we
needed some signature stamps. Atom and I wanted to take advantage of
our geeky creative sides, so we made our our first sig stamps by carving
our images out of bars of soap and pouring polydimethylsiloxane (PDMS),
a polymer substrate we use frequently at work, onto the soap and then
pulling out the PDMS mold once the polymer had cured into place. It
sounds pretty good in theory, but didn't work quite as well as planned.
My first stamp fell apart after the first weekend, but my replacement
lasted a couple of months. Atom's also lasted a couple of months, but
then we decided that we could handle the carving and bought some
MasterCarve and Speedyball cutters, and the rest is history.

Our first letterboxing trip with Fermont took us to Lincoln Park to
check out three boxes there. The clues were very clever, but
unfortunately the boxes had gone missing (not surprising since, as many
of you know, Lincoln Park is a very popular place to visit!). We didn't
give up however and drove a bit west to find our first box, "Down by the
Lazy River," just after it got dark. After finding just one box out of
four, we were hooked. :) We did manage to go three for three the next
day though, and we were stoked for our new hobby... even enough to brave
one incredibly cold Wisconsin day about a month later to box in
Menomonee Falls with Fermont in about 10 degree weather and 4 inches of
snow!

Anyway, the aspect that really got me excited about letterboxing was
figuring out the clues and then finding the hidden treasure out in
public places -- I've always been a sucker for treasure hunts. But as
we got more into it, we also really like having the excuse to find new
places to explore and go hiking. We've done a majority of our boxing in
the Chicagoland area, but since Atom's family lives in SE Wisconsin and
mine lives in SW Michigan, we've done a lot of boxing there as well.
Since we're big science geeks, we're also pretty into data analysis
(yes, go ahead and laugh), so we have some pretty thorough spreadsheets
and graphs detailing our letterboxing adventures. So at the risk of
sounding too obsessed with stats, we are currently sitting at F312 (17
are HH), P50 (17 are HH), and X58. Our average number of boxes
found/month is 36.125, with an average HH finds/month of 2. The
majority of our exchanges are from the Great Lakes Gathering this past
June, where we had a blast and really enjoyed meeting a lot of you.

Thanks to everyone for making this a great hobby!
