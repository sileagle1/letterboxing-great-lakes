Re: [LbGLK] Supplies at Wal Mart

From: Delirium (donaldson_home@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-09-10 04:38:03 UTC-07:00
I don't know if Wal-Mart carries carving medium, but I'm sure they sell erasers, and those things work well in a pinch. Those pink pearl ones do well. You won't be able to get the biggest stamp ever, but you will be able to avoid a 60 mile drive.
If you ever do internet shopping, www.dickblick.com sells huge blocks of Mastercarve that will delivered right to your door. I'm sure Speedball stuff can be found in the same manner.

As for images, I've always done either a google image search, or www.corbis.com has lots of nice pictures. When I'm in a pinch about proper images, I go bother Artistamp. She has the BEST ideas for stamp pictures.

Good luck on your first carved box- that's a great step!
Cheers,
Delirium

I've never seen carving materials at my Wal-Marts here in Cleveland (yes, that's plural, because I'm a Wal-Mart addict and have been known to go to all three area Wal-Marts in the same day - I know, I need help.) Since you said "the closest craft store is 60 miles away" you probably don't have a Joann, Etc. close by - I saw Mastercarve & the Mastercarve carving tools in one of our area's Joann's.

That's where I bought our speedball carving set from, because the Michael's didn't start stocking it until 3 weeks _after_ I bought the set online *grumble*grumble*grumble*shipping fees could have been avoided*

Concerning designs, MS Word's clipart has provided me with some workable images.

From: familia steibel (evailejp@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-09-10 15:03:52 UTC-07:00
What can I say it wasn't said before by experienced people?

I am new to letterboxing and I am just trying to carve a decent stamp for my first box...

I started a practicing a couple of weeks ago with an Xacto knife and a couple of erasers from walmart...

then I ordered a speedy cut tool and some media from dickbliss, the order was 14 but 21 with SH...

In the mean time I visited one of those "Dollar castles" and with the "back to school" offers I've got 5 white erasers (aprox 1 by 2 inches, maybe just bigger) for 1 dollar...

they carve like butter, not crumby at all and 1/4 inch thich...

I am using them to practice and because some stamps look OK maybe I'll save them for a box.

so I would say I could alternative media and do fine to start... but the cut tool... Y find it very handy! does not replace the old Xacto, but does some unique work though.

Images for carving?
well, I like animals and plants, so I used outdoor magazines that ussually inlude logos with sketches of fish, deer, birds, etc. They don't have much details but are nice silhouetes. They are quite standard, but to preactice they are just fine...

my 0.02

JP (of Eva-Ile-y-Juan team)
