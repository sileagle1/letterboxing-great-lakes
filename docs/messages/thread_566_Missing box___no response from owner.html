Re: [LbGLK] Re: Missing box...no response from owner

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-08-21 10:31:30 UTC-04:00
Yeh, missing boxes are just a part of the game. Even when a placer is
diligent, checks frequently on their boxes, posts status notes on the clues
and has every reason to believe the box is intact, it could go missing the
day before you get there.

I realize the more nagging problem are those boxes that have been missing
for quite some time and the clues still remain, with no indication as to
status of the box, due to the fact that the placer is no longer letterboxing
or simply has been pulled in other directions for a while. In answer to
your first question about how long it will stay on the clue site -- probably
for a long time. The way it works right now, a box is only removed if the
owner removes it, or in extreme cases, if it has been confiscated by
authorities and the owner does not come forward.

In answer to your second question, how many are there? It would be
impossible to know, especially since seekers often report boxes to be
missing only to have a subsequent seeker find the box. Relatively speaking,
the number of non-existent boxes whose clues are still listed and provide no
indication that the box is missing is only a small percentage of the overall
total of boxes listed on letterboxing.org.

It can be frustrating to drive a distance to look for a box and then be
certain you are at the right place and find no box. But you are correct --
you still found a wonderful new park or city and most likely enjoyed a nice
hike with family, friends, etc. If you do know ahead of time where you will
be headed, especially if it is a distance away, it is perfectly acceptable
to contact the placer and inquire as to the status of a box. Most placers
are okay with this and have experienced the frustration of missing boxes
themself. If you do happen across somebody who doesn't care to provide that
information, you probably just won't hear back from them -- nothing lost.

SpringChick

From: azobox (jparkerg@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-08-21 21:02:56 UTC
Speedsqure,
On the LbNA site, any placer can edit or delete a box that he/she has
placed. Edits can include information like "this box is reported as
MIA - unconfirmed" or "This box has been removed temporarily for
maintenance" or anything else the placer wishes to post.
Or the box can be deleted and it will no longer show up on the list
at all.
Many placers use the edit function to update prospective seekers
about last known findings or other reports that may affect a seekers'
plans.

Probably I don't understand what the placer of D'ville had in mind,
but the whole entry can be made to go away with a simple click by the
placer.

The weak link in this information chain is that some seekers print
out lots of clues ahead of time and stash them away for a future
trip. Then the box status changes before the hunt is made.


azobox

From: azobox (jparkerg@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-08-22 10:04:48 UTC
On the surface your idea for a button for a finder to click makes
perfect sense. In practice it's fraught with pitfalls.

There have furious debates on the big list over the years about the
collection of data and the appropriateness of anyone other than the
placer adding information to a clue-based site.

Without going into the minefield of history, suffice it to say, all
points of these arguemnts have been aired ad nauseum. The gist of it
is that seeker-based information cannot be supported on clue pages
because it quickly compromises the placers' intentions. There were
experiments launched by groups of seekers to classify boxes (by *all*
kinds of criteria) and the revolution by placers still rumbles
through the underground.
Anyway......interesting thought(and not illogical at first glance),
but simply not feasible.

Your plan to contact placers is your best bet for a positive
experience. The problem of unresponsive placers will probably always
be with us.


azobox
