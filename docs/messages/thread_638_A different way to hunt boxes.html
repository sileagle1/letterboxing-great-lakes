A different way to hunt boxes

From: silboxer61364 (dispatcher_riz@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-09-05 10:31:23 UTC
Has anyone thought of doing an in-the-dark letterbox hunt? We
have a park near town that has well marked trails and is open after
hours with a special permit. Just wondering what everyone would
think if there was get-together with an in-the-dark hunt, basically
it would be that you could look for the letter boxes at night, and
only use flashlights to decipher and locate the locations of the
clues to find the boxes


Re: [LbGLK] A different way to hunt boxes

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-09-05 07:05:35 UTC-04:00
At the Great Lakes Gathering this past June there was a group that did night
boxing -- waiting until after dark to do the Vampire series. Just last
night I tried to persuade OldShoe to come letterboxing in the dark with me
at a cemetery box, but he wasn't seeing the fun in it.

Sounds like a lot of fun, but I would think you'd have to be particularly
careful of vegetation, etc. which would be more difficult to see at night.
I am sure some boxes/locations are more suited to in-the-dark-boxing than
others.

SpringChick
