An idea...

From: Paula Collins (boxer@cinemaboxers.com) | Date: 2006-08-15 13:28:27 UTC
Hey all...
Im debating on planting a "Geocache Letterbox" A box that has both
clues, AND Coordinates. (Along with a logbook, and trinkets) My
intent is to place a PROMINENT writeup ON the logbook to explain to
local Geocachers WHAT a letterbox is, and to NOT ever remove the
stamps IF they should come across any others. What does everyone
think?? (Obviously the letterboxers will know what it is before they
head out - it would be in the clues!)

I know that I have read MANY times about stamps being 'traded'
accidentally and perhaps, if everyone who HAD the ability to plant
a 'Geocache Letterbox' would, we could prevent it from happening, at
least on a small local level.

As an example, we just discovered that we have a plant in the same
place as a Geocache, far enough away for us not to be concerned, but
I have seen others even closer.

Bad idea? Good idea? Any feedback is appreciated.

Paula
CinemaBoxers

From: Doodle & Deedle Bug (doodle_n_deedle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-08-15 06:48:08 UTC-07:00
There are several Geocache/Letterbox hybrid boxes out
there already. We found one in Michigan one summer.
I think there were actually two logbooks - one for the
geocachers to sign, and another for the letterboxers
to stamp, and like you suggested, there was a
statement on the logbook explaining not to take the
stamp. Another idea would be to spell out in the
clues that it's a Geocache/Letterbox hybrid and
explain what letterboxing is and that the stamp is to
be left in the box.

Good luck!

Doodle Bug.

From: R (ontario_cacher@yahoo.ca) | Date: 2006-08-15 09:54:19 UTC-04:00
Works for me. Most of my letterboxes are also posted on geocaching.com. Here's an example:
http://www.geocaching.com/seek/cache_details.aspx?guid=286f64a1-ff2d-4808-8203-5eeacd46165e

I've been doing this for over 2 years and have never had a stamp traded out and very rarely do I find something in the box when I do maintenance visits - usually if something is left in the box it's a signature item.

I ask geocachers to get creative, if they don't have a stamp I ask them to try a nature stamp, or a drawing or some other creative things. So far geocachers have left lots of cute stick figure drawings and simple line drawings. Some have made custom signature stamps, a couple have carved their own and some use store-bought sig stamps. You'll get a real mix. Go for it. :)

JARS

From: ruhlette (ruhlette@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-08-15 12:28:48 UTC-07:00
Hoosier Honey Bee and I found a hybrid last January. Hi! You've Got Mail
- We weren't the first to find it as a cache or letterbox, and the finders
did seem to understand not to take the stamp and letterbox items, as they
were clearly identified. It was cleverly planted in a large rural mailbox
spray painted as camouflage.

I would have a separate logbook for each activity and mark the logs IN
LARGE PRINT. Having an adequate size container to house both activities
will probably help. You might not want to encourage HH's to this hybrid
box, because all the explanation in the world will go out the window if
they confuse the box stamp and the HH's.

Do you know the members of the local geocaching community? How
aware/respectful are they of letterboxing? You might want to get some
feedback from them.

Just some on the spot thoughts,

speedsquare
