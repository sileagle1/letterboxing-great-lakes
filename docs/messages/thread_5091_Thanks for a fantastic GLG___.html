RE: [LbGLK] Re: Thanks for a fantastic GLG!!!

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2008-06-02 01:03:07 UTC-05:00
Hey Optic! We were thinking of you & Kayakin' Monkey at about 8 - 8:30 pm
when we were hanging around the parking lot chatting and the fierce weather
rolled in. We hoped you were OK. We stopped by Campsite #12 late this
afternoon, but it was deserted. Glad to hear you survived intact!

We also would like to thank The Red Cat, her parents, her brother and the
other local assistants that helped create such a fine event. The weather
was great until late in the day, the food was fantastic, the stamps
interesting, placements very well done, and we got a good workout on the
hikes!

We enjoyed seeing some old friends (after almost 5 years of boxing they are
now too numerous to mention) and meeting some new folks. It was really nice
to see how many WI folks made it to the event this year. Gives a lot of
credibility to "The Coolest Thing About Minnesota" box (very funny, Walking
Stick!!)

We really enjoyed spending Saturday afternoon on the trail with
PackerBacker, her young ladies Tayler & Lacey, and the "mom" portion of
Partyof6. We were all dismayed to see Po6 take a tumble over a tree root in
the trail and hope her ankle is healing quickly.

Today we were back at the park to find more boxes. We had GREAT weather all
day and met a few other groups of boxers out on the trail. It took us until
6 pm, but by the end of the weekend we managed to find all the boxes in the
clue packet, several of the handwritten "posted" clues, plus the Empire On
Rails box, all the Snow Friends boxes AND all 4 of the marathon Gooble
Gobble boxes. Congrats to Wormy Wanderers for being the first to complete
the GG series! I wore my pedometer today and we hiked 13.6 miles. If we
would have figured out all the clues correctly the first time around it
would have been less, but we were just happy to EVENTUALLY get everything
figured out.

We should have some photos posted and our blog updated within the next day
or two.

Now we're looking forward to the Birds of a Feather event near Rockford, IL
on June 21!

Wisconsin Hiker (& Martini Man)

From: be_a_badger (schuppner@uwalumni.com) | Date: 2008-06-07 01:40:40 UTC
We rolled home from the Gathering on Monday with our pages filled with
terrific images. We spent less time than usual exchanging and more on
the trail. To those we didn't meet, we hope to see you at another
event!

Thanks to The Red Cat and the rest of the planners and carvers for an
excellent weekend. A special thanks to mom, dad, and brother of The
Red Cat who offered to help and did a great job greeting, grilling,
and keeping things humming at the Visitor's Center.

While hiking the long gentle ascent to The Body on Sunday, we were
lapped by a much older gentleman. He trotted past with his hiking
poles and as we reached the top he was on his way back down, so we
asked how many trips he makes. His answer? 2-3.

On Sunday, the park was hopping with the usual traffic - people of all
ages, dogs, and horses. Folks were traveling light or with packs,
strollers, etc. There was one unusual pair - a man and a long-haired
cat. The cat rode the man's shoulder (sitting or laying) and jumped
down from time to time to explore on its own. The man would continue
down the path and, if the cat remained far behind, he would backtrack
to the cat.

For those who couldn't make it to the event and for those who log
their finds on LbNA, our clue is now on LbNA.

The Christmas Elves

Re: Thanks for a fantastic GLG!!!

From: Brandicat (cherylw77@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-06-07 14:18:52 UTC
What an amazing day! I met so many wonderful people, enjoyed
delicious food,and hiked down beautiful trails until I was ready to
drop! I can't believe that I boxed for over 8 hours! I followed the
clues to 25 letterboxes (which means of course, that I will have to
return to the park to find the rest....) As I was stamping my last box
of the day-Coolest Thing About Minnesota-dark clouds were rolling in
and the thunder was rumbling ominously. I high-tailed it back to the
Visitor Center, and got there just as the rain started to fall. (I did
see the horses around 7 pm- one of the riders remarked "looks like we
might get wet") As I drove out of the park, there were black storm
clouds on one side of the road, bright sunshine on the other, and rain
pouring down overhead. A beautiful rainbow appeared in the sky just as
I passed the park office.

Thanks to everyone who helped make this such a fun day--those who
planned the event, carved stamps, hid letterboxes, grilled the food--a
lot of work went into it, and I think everyone who attended had a
memorable experience.
