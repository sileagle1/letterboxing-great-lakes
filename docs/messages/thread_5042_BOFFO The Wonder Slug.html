BOFFO The Wonder Slug

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2008-05-18 00:39:20 UTC
I saw this on the personal blog of a local letterboxer (42 Pines). A
year or so ago, I encourged these boxers to join this regional group,
but I don't think they have. Therefore I'm cross-posting the info
here in case anyone knows the boxer/stamp in question:

====================

To my fellow Wisconsin and nation-wide letterboxers:

We have a problem our unsurpassed community of knowledge really needs
to pull it together tight in order to solve this dilemma. While
replanting "To Judge a Book by its Cover", we found something while
trying to define where the original hiding spot was for our
letterbox. What was this find? It was someone's personal stamp! The
stamp was immediately recognised, as it was one of the last
letterboxers to have stamped our book. Out of respect for the trail
and its culture I will not upload a photograph of the stamp itself
but I will enlighten the community of what it looks like.

Its got an insect-type creature on it and the text reads "BOFFO", and
in smaller text "THE WONDER SLUG". If anyone can help find the owner
of this stamp so we can arrange a way to get it back to them it would
be great. Please leave a comment here to get ahold of us, or find us
(42 Pines) at LbNA or AtlasQuest. I'm putting a post here hoping that
someone finds it, and so that there is some mention of this before I
get a chance to sitdown and really try to seek this letterboxer out.

This stamp must have fallen out of their pocket/bag while they were
stamping our box, and it survived a rough-and-tough Wisconsin winter.
It deserves to get back home.

=====================================
