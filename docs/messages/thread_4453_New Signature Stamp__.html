From: lcoil79@ameritech.net smokey (ainawgsd@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-09-21 17:14:11 UTC-07:00
Remember, there aren't any hard and fast rules for letterboxing (other than being considerate of others)...do whatever YOU feel is appropriate. When I recarved my signature stamp, I went for a totally different image. My original stamp was based off of a picture of my dog Aina wearing her kimono. My current signature stamp is based off of a picture of me wearing a kimono (with a lot of artistic license taken with the kimono itself and the addition of some maple leaves). The stamp I mostly use to stamp into log books is a "min" version of my signature stamp and uses a motif from Aina's sig. stamp (a geometric shape...that also happens to be in my kimono but got cut off entirely when I "miniaturized" my image). For me, this motif is important...so all of my signature stamps from now on will incorporate it somehow. But beyond that one motif, I can't say that anything else will stay the same next time I decide I need a new signature stamp.

Kimono Girl
