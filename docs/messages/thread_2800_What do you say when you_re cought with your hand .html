From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-07-21 06:58:59 UTC-04:00
Yeh, what she said...

I agree that there is no reason to outright lie, particularly if you are
with kids as it does set a bad example, as you say. But I think that most
people really don't want to hear the whole spiel anyway. It's like when
somebody says, "how have you been" and they really don't want to hear the
details of what's been happening in your life for the last year. Add to
that it is a little *different* and most people aren't going to *get it* and
it just seems easier to skirt the issue rather than go through the
explanation. It is possible to not lie without telling the whole truth.
The scavenger hunt explanation does seem to work better as most people can
relate to that (although it doesn't explain the rubber stamping part if
you've already begun the stamping process). If somebody stopped and asked
and genuinely seemed interested in what I was doing, I might tell them,
guess I'd play it by ear -- but that has never happened to me.

While providing an explanation *may* be an opportunity to introduce
somebody to letterboxing, that is not generally the case and to be honest, I
am not out looking for converts. If somebody finds the game through an
online site, or another participant or even a publicity piece and they are
intrigued enough to research a little and then follow through with it,
that's great and welcome to the game, but I don't go out of my way to add
sheep to the flock.

I would have to agree that the *secretive* aspect of the game adds to it for
me.

SpringChick
