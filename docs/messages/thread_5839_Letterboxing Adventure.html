Letterboxing Adventure

From: tiptoetonto (tiptoetonto@yahoo.com) | Date: 2009-04-26 00:31:36 UTC
We have been up to our eyeballs with aging parent issues, but managed to get out for a quick LB hunt yesterday. Diversion tactics have been discussed on this board in the past, but Tonto used one that we won't soon forget.

We decided to "kill 2 birds with one stone" and do a little morel foraging and letterboxing...What better box to go after than Shorty's "The Morel." We were looking for the box on the Hennepin Canal yesterday and couldn't nab the box due to a fisherman right by the bridge and box. He certainly had his eye on us wondering why we kept returning to that spot. Just as we were about to abandon all hope, he caught a huge carp and was having trouble landing it. Tonto being the good sport that he is, quickly went to his aid and sprawled his long body over the side of the lock with a net to help bring in the big fish affording Tiptoe an ideal time to quick grab the box, stamp in and return it without any question. Success on 2 of 3 accounts- the fisherman got his fish, we got the letterbox, but no real morels. One other bad thing...Tonto had carp blood on his hiking pants rest of the day - no fancy restaurants for us!
Tiptoe & Tonto
