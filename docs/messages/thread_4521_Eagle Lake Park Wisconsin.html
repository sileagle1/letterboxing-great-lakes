Hi all,

We had a report or two that our Eagle Lake box was
missing. We found that what actually happened is that
a squirrel had ignored one of the main rules of
letterboxing. "Do not bury them." At first glace,
the box appeared to be gone. I felt there was no
possible way for that to happen without someone
removing it from the base of the tree on purpose. So
I started scraping out the dirt inside the tree until
to my surprise I saw a little blue and clear plastic.
Since the box was placed in 04, the original tight fit
has become significantly tighter. It took some
pulling and wiggling, but we finally got it out. We
rehid the box so that it's sticking out a bit and then
covered it with some other logs for right now, but we
may have to move it in the future or the tree may not
let us have it back!

We wonder how many "missing" boxes are actually there
buried under a couple of years of forest litter.
