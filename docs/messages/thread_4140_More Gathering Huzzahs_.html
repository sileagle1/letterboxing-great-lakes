More Gathering Huzzahs!

From: Doodle & Deedle Bug (doodle_n_deedle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-06-04 08:52:32 UTC-07:00
As we told Eagle Eyes & M&8X a few times in person,
they did an awesome job putting this gathering
together. Where to begin?

First, let's not forget Rosemarius and the Friday
night sock hop. We loved having the opportunity to do
some exchanges beforehand in a more relaxed (and
indoor air conditioned) setting! Thank you, Rose, for
organizing that! And if anyone took a photo of Perdu
and her red glasses, email it to me! :)

When I first learned that the potluck was going to be
around dinner time on Saturday, I thought that was
odd, but it really worked out well. Like someone else
already posted, this gave us more time to spend
dedicated to box hunting and not worry about missing
out on food or other things (like the group photo we
missed in 2005 :). And it was a nice way to reunite
everyone who was still there in the evening. Whoever
had the bagpipes, that was amazing!!!

Now, during the day we did stop back at the shelter
around the time of the "cooling summer rain" to
regroup and get a few more exchanges before heading
back to the trails. There were still so many people
we wanted to meet but missed - hopefully we'll get to
meet another time! That reminds me - TrailTracker,
did we ever meet back up with you to finally do an
exchange? I don't remember if we did.

The placement of the boxes was also great. Like
someone else said, there wasn't a lot of bunching up
since the boxes were spread around the park in
different areas. And the few times we did start after
the same box as another group it was nice meeting some
more people and getting a few more exchanges, but we
never felt like there were other boxers right on our
tails. Well, except for the Burma Shave series. Mrs.
Doglvrs, hope you get a kick out of the comments we
wrote in the logbooks!

Now, I do have a confession to make about the
placement of the Memphis Drive-In box. Although we
created The Memphis Drive-In box, we did not plant it;
we had sent it to M&8X ahead of time for planting. So
the credit for the unique hiding that we heard so much
about goes to her and her planting crew, and if
someone who took a photo of the hiding spot can email
it to me I'd really appreciate it!!!

And this leads me to our most memorable moment of the
weekend - we fell victim, and fell hard, to the "Oh,
we have time for one more box!" syndrome. We decided
to stop for the The Christmas Elves' Mr. Potato Head
box en route back to the potluck dinner, since we were
driving right past that trail anyway. We were the
first finders. At around 6:30 at night. Were we the
only ones at the gathering who didn't get the memo
that this hike was long enough to sing "99 Bottles of
Beer on the Wall" in its _entirety_ on the hike to
this box?? :) What a way to end the boxing day!!

I do believe, though, that between Friday afternoon,
Saturday and Sunday morning we found all but 3 boxes
placed for the event (Wisconsin Hiker & Martini Man,
we never did make it to your boxes Sunday morning -
our feet were just too tired after all the walking
Saturday and then the '50s Jukebox series).

Oh, and don't let me forget the raffle prizes - wow!
And I'm not just saying that because we won one of the
backpacks (hey, we deserved it after the Mr. Potato
Head hike!! ;). The pack we won had some handmade
logbooks, a block of MasterCarve, a Great Lakes logo
bandana (perfect for me who's always wearing one on
the trails!), a water bottle, a few bottles of water,
a couple containers for new letterboxes, some Ink
Dots, and I forget what all else - it was just great.
And, in addition to singing 99 Bottle of Beer in its
entirety this weekend, I also learned that Deedle
Bug's arm is one ticket length shorter than Eagle
Eye's :)

Thanks also to everyone Saturday night who shared
their campfire with us as we went campfire hopping for
a few more exchanges before calling it a night. We
cleaned the Jeep out when we got home Sunday, and I'm
happy to report that it arrived home cootie-less. Not
sure about our letterboxing bag, though....

Now back to work & the real world. Thanks again to
everyone for such a wonderful weekend!!!

Doodle Bug (& Deedle Bug, too).

From: Barb Funk (mmfunk99@greenapple.com) | Date: 2007-06-04 12:52:25 UTC-04:00
NO!! We did not Doodle and Deedle Bug! We did not exchange after all
that. There were several key folks I wanted to get exchanges in with
that I missed. I'm so sorry. I hope to exchange with you both sometime
in the future for sure!

I also have a couple more things to add to the post-gathering posts.
Thank you so very much to all of you who donated yet more money and
postcards for our troops! I am just this morning opening envelopes and
adding up the cash donations that were given to me on Friday and
Saturday as I just kept putting them in my "safe place". I am so
grateful and humbled one more time. Letterboxers are the best. I
cannot say this enough. I have felt more support from our letterboxers
than in any other organization that I am heavily involved in---no
contest. This means the world to me and most importantly to our
soldiers who are really feeling the burden right now. I promise you
this money is going to good use!! Right now the top two requests are
beef jerky and those Crystal Lite To Go packs. They are not getting
enough protein and drinking water temps for those on the front lines in
undisclosed locations reaches 110!!! Using evaporation tricks, they
are able to get water temps down to 80 in the wee hours but having the
Crystal Lite to add is like heaven to them. So any money that was not
specifically designated to go to the St. Barbara medals, will go toward
the Crystal Lite in this next round of care packages for June!

***So my big question is---who is the VERY generous letterboxer who gave
me cash that was put into an envelope-- taped shut and made from a cute
piece of stationary paper with a blue house and blue picket fence on
it? On the outside it simply said, "For care packages! and a smiley
face" ??? Please, please, let me know either here or off post---which
ever you prefer. I don't believe you got ANY stamp for this and I want
to be sure to take care of that---but I also would like to thank you
more appropriately!!! Everyone's generosity is amazing. Congrats to
Scoutdogs too for inching ever closer to being able to buy that K-9
vest!! Gunner has his patch on his bandana---granted it is only stapled
so far. I will sew it on very soon! :)

My congrats to speedsquare as well on her 500th find!! Dr Watson just
had her AQ anniversary cake yesterday AND I wanted to throw in some
special cudo's to a few first time campers who did REALLY well!
Primitive Cin and TrailStomper and her daughter Emerald Frost and
friend, Carrot Top! Congrats and we celebrate this with you as well!

Trail Troll---I never got to meet you! TrailStompers and I were hoping
to have a grand meeting of the Trail-people but we will have to put it
on the agenda for another event! I heard your campsite was a hoot with
all the trolls you hung! I will be checking out the photo posted in the
album!

Last, if you did not see, Mrs. Doglvrs' stamp for the collection of
items in honor of Hartx6's Vietnam Vet father going to Grand Rapids, MI
---was STUNNING!!!!

Trailtracker
