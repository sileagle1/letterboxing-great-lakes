Hello everyone!

Sorry if we've been MIA for some time - the holiday season ate up
more time than planned this year (what with the baby, my extended
christmas work schedule, and a couple sicknesses), but Team Rabbit is
back, and ready to answer ALL of your Gathering questions!

Congratulations to both Del & Mr. Del and Tom & Jill on the
new 'boxers! Wow! Also, thanks to everyone for the offers of help
with the Gathering. We certainly appreciate it! Now, question time!

A Mr. Dragon from Wisconsin writes, "I've heard that the Gathering is
going to be held on June 18. Is that true?"

Why, yes, Mr. Dragon, what you've heard is absolutely correct! This
is the date decided upon at the last Gathering...

Our next question comes to us from a Ms. S. Chick from Muskegon, who
asks, "Just where exactly will the 2005 Gathering be held?"

Well, Ms. Chick, that hasn't actually been decided upon yet. Our
best rabbits are ready to scout locations (as soon as it warms up a
little, and they get a sitter for the baby bunny).

The top criteria for deciding on a prime location are: no entry fee,
easy access from main roads, and (very important) some sort of
pavillion under which we all CAN GATHER. Interestingly enough, we'd
also like the location to be further west than our "territory" (since
we're practically in Canada), which could place it near what was
(until just recently) known as Delirium-town.

Any other questions? Yes, D. Bug? "I understand that this year's
theme will be Mo' Boxing in Motown, right?"

Also correct! I've already got an event stamp in mind too!

So, please let us know any further questions any of you may have. We
would also REALLY appreciate any suggestions, ideas, hints, etc...
I'm sure that many things will arise that we can't ever foresee at
this time.

Your humble servants,
Team Rabbit
Brown, Gray, & little B&W






Re: [LbGLK] Re: June 18 - GL Gathering - Location?

From: Cari Donaldson (donaldson_home@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-01-19 18:14:55 UTC-08:00
My suggestion (since it's going to be closer to my old stomping grounds) is Hines Park. Those of you not familiar with the park (and between there and Kensington, I don't think I'll ever have a love affair with a piece of earth like those), it's a long strip of riverfront along the Rouge. Yes, the Rouge. As in, "Wasn't that the river that set itself on fire in the 80s?" It's a lot cleaner now. And there are plenty of little park areas with pavillions, trails, and no entry fee. However, if the 18th is on a Saturday (and I think it is), the park is closed to car traffic from Ann Arbor Trail to Outer Drive from 7-2ish. This isn't that big of a problem, since there is a big pavilion at the Ann Arbor Road (Nankin Mills) entrance, with wooded areas, and a parking lot reserved for people who got themselves a permit for the pavillion. Thus, some degree of privacy.

Or there is Crosswinds Marsh, which has a big covered pavillion area and lots of trails. This one would probably be less populated on a nice June day. Also free entry.

Websites follow:
http://www.digitalcity.com/detroit/entertainment/venue.adp?vid=172363 (shameless promotion of Hines)
http://www.waynecounty.com/parks/cwinds_park.htm (info on Crosswinds Marsh)
http://www.waynecounty.com/parks/nankin_park.htm (info on Nankin Mills, part of Hines)
http://www.waynecounty.com/parks/pickpark.htm (general info on Hines Park areas)

Ok, all this brainstorming is leaving me mighty homesick. Hope that gives you a jumping off point. WAYNE COUNTY RULES!

Cheers,
Del
