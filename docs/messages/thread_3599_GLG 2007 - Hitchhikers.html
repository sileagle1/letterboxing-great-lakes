really appreciated the reports I get on their travels.

A HH Hostel is a special letterbox that is meant to hold hitchhikers. There can be from one to several HH's in a hostel. The rule of thumb is that you can visit the HH Hostel and take one of the HH's only if you bring/leave a HH.

For a more information, see the LbNA glossary:

http://www.letterboxing.org/GlossaryList.php

Many of the major letterboxing websites have excellent glossaries for
letterboxing terms you may not be familiar with. Here are some links
to them:

http://letterboxing.org/GlossaryList.php

http://www.letterboxing.info/glossary/

http://www.atlasquest.com/aboutlb/glossary/


Basically a hitchhiker hostel is a letterbox that in addition to
having a typical box stamp, also has enough space to hold several
hitchhikers. The theory is that if you have a hitchhiker, you can
bring it to the hostel, leave it there, and take a different
hitchhiker. In other words, a "planned acquisition" rather than
a "surprise". Oftentimes people will stamp into all the hitchhikers
currently in the box at the time they visit. You are not supposed to
remove a HH from the box unless you leave one in its place.

Wisconsin Hiker
