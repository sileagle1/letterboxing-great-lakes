Mystery Letterboxers

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2008-11-30 03:22:16 UTC
It's been mentioned on various message boards over the years, but
today we saw more proof that there are a LOT of letterboxers who
aren't as "hard core" as those of us on this list. These people
simply lookup some clues, find the box and go about the rest of their
lives. They don't plant boxes, they don't log their finds on-line,
they don't participate in letterboxing boards, etc. Who ARE all
these mystery letterboxers???

What was the proof? We checked on one of my boxes at Devil's Lake
State Park (Demon's Dwelling). It's been in place for over 3 years.
In that time I've received 10 notices or emails from people that have
found the box. Imagine my surprise and amazement when we looked
through the logbook this afternoon and counted up 43 different
groups! Many of these groups appeared to be families and it was nice
to think about all these people hiking up the bluff and then
succesfully finding the box. Most of these were stamps we had never
seen before, nor were we famililar with the trailnames. Therefore we
consider them "mystery letterboxers".

It was also great to find the box in near perfect condition. All
baggies were sealed well and the box was completely dry inside. The
olny thing we need to do is bring another logbook, since the first is
almost full!

Wisconsin Hiker

P.S. No one left any money in this box!
