Re: Dragon at Bristol

From: Irish Bain (irishbain@rocketmail.com) | Date: 2007-07-23 21:57:54 UTC
We searched for Dragon all day yesterday and came up empty, despite
running into parents of one of his students. THEY had an awesome
logbook.

Wizard's Wardrobe was NOT to be found as of 6:30pm. Worse, the shop
owner appears to find that her favorite spot to sit (a tall, leggy
blond of 30-40ish years). I had to circle back six times and still
came up empty.

The Pig'n'Whistle pole is in the right place, but the bench had been
confiscated and relocated about 15ft away. Without the bench, I
couldn't tell where to search and so had to let that one go.

As for security, they kept a good eye on me right up until I let them
know I was on a treasure hunt. Apparently there were two other
assorted hunt type activities in progress. Five or six times I was
studied, watched, approached, and then given the amused smile and left
alone. This was NOT while stamping in, but simply doing the visual up-
and-down from the clues to locations. Maybe it was the way that made
my hat bob :)

The Spell of Incantrix was placed late. I actually got to be a first
finder! Woo! Never did THAT before!!!

The brother of Grainger says the clue needs serious updating. The new
watchword is Lupin Hop. Never could catch him off stage.

Barefoot Ankh is not in the shop listed. KissMeKate(according to her
bf, a delightful gentleman in blue brocade)is apparently all in
chocolate fabrics this year, but walks faster than I can. We were
*moments* behind her in four locations!

One more comment: The clues to one box refer to a concrete cover. It
is NOT. It's plaster. If you know the Faire, then you know that means
it's also WET plaster. A fistful of towels would be well advised.

The weather was perfect! 70 degrees, breezy, and dry! Talk about a
great time at the Faire. Thanks, Dragon!

Ish
