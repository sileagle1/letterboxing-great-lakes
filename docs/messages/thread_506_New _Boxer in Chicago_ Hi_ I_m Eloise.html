From: Eloise Mason (nee Beltz-Decker) (eloise@ripco.com) | Date: 2004-08-08 18:32:41 UTC-05:00
Dagonell the Pirate took me letterboxing wiht him this past
weekend as his native guide (he's from New York, and had no idea where to
even START figuring out where some of the Chicago cluetrails start), and
got me well and truly hooked. Of course, it's only fair, as I'm the one
who hooked him on letterboxing originally ... wait, I should explain. :->

Back near the beginning of the year, or even before (I can't find
the relevant reference in my online journal), I posted a link about
letterboxing, which I thought was Very Cool. I got moderately obsessed
with it for a while, buying erasers, carving some stamps, thinking about
tupperware and logistics and whatnot. Did I actually go find any boxes at
that point? I did not. I did, however, posta bout it a lot in my online
journal, which Dagonell reads. So I hooked him on it, and HE folloewd up,
and now he's dragging me around (ok, he didn't have to twist my arm very
far at all), and I'm re-hooked. So fair's fair. :->

After all, when I've only been a real letterboxer for a single
weekend and my count stands at P0F7X1, that's a sign of something, isn't
it? To be a bit more detailed (and because I know you're all interested),
my Found list is as follows, to date:

Hurray for Hollywood
Historic Chicago Box #2: Columbian Exposition
Chicago Underground
Grosse Point Light
Fish Out of Water 4
History Tower
Schoolhouse Rocks!

In no particular order. We also tried for, and couldn't find,
several, and we wonder if perhaps they're missing. What's the protocol on
that, post it here and expect people to read about it, or email the
placers directly?

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2004-08-08 18:46:07 UTC-05:00
Welcome to the LB world and the Great Lakes Group!

My own "protocol" is to email the placers directly both when we find AND
don't find a box. As a planter of boxes I like updates on the box status
("in good shape", "needs new book", "box is cracked", etc) so assume other
people may appreciate the same feedback. If a box is in good shape, I don't
necessarily need to go check on it for a while. If it is in bad shape
and/or possibly missing, it alerts me to go check on things. [Note: I
didn't initially have this protocol, but after we started placing boxes I
realized how nice it was to get feedback, so then started emailing even when
a box was in good shape.]

I also email the creator of a hitchhiker when we find one since they
normally like to track the HH travels. I usually copy "Pete" on the info so
he can update the HH tracking page.

Wisconsin Hiker

Re: New 'Boxer in Chicago: Hi, I'm Eloise

From: dagonell2001 (salley@klaatu.canisius.edu) | Date: 2004-08-10 18:58:28 UTC
Greetings to all from Dagonell the Pirate!

I see Eloise has already posted. I knew she was well and truly hooked
when I pulled out the first hitchhiker and she squealed like a school
girl! :D

Da Chicago Report:

Clark House: We couldn't find it. I would have said it was missing,
but I read the placer's post that it's still there. I guess we just
missed it.

Columbian Exposition: Present, but not safe. The "hedge" has been cut
all the way down. We tried to re-hide the box better than we found
it, under grass clippings and leaves, but it really should be covered
in camo tape. If the groundskeeper had been a little more diligent, it
might have been disposed of as litter.

Forest Goblin: We couldn't find it, however the clues needed
re-arranging. When we got to the field, we discovered a *large*
family celebration taking place. I decided if I couldn't be discrete
I would pass it up rather than reveal its location, however the point
was moot because the outhouse was gone! We tried to extrapolate, "If
it was here, then we would go..." but it didn't work. We couldn't
find it. I see the placer will be updating the clues shortly.

Planes, Sand and Stars: Without giving too much away, I could never
have checked the hiding place if I had been solo. Working together,
we still couldn't find it.

River Trail: We got to the site just as it was closing and couldn't go
on the trail. Could the placer please insert into the clues that the
park closes at 4:30? We were tackling boxes from outside into
Chicago. If we had known of the time limitation, we would have hit it
earlier.

Grosse Point Lighthouse: Safe and sound. Nice site, cool hiding place.

Schoolhouse Rock: Wonderful site. The logbook was so busy, we hung
around for a short while to see if we could spot anyone counting steps
up the trail! :D

History Tower: Safe and sound

Hooray for Hollywood: Safe and sound.

Fish Out of Water #4: Very cool stamp, nice historic site. The
letterbox was actually out in the open, plainly visible! We put it
back where it belonged and spent a minute with a bark chip making a
cover that exactly fit the hole! It's there, but you can't tell it's
there! You have to feel around for it.

Chicago Underground: Possibly THE COOLEST urban letterbox hunt I have
ever been on! The trail was wonderful, the clues were great, the
hiding place was boss! When I read "something to eat, something to
eat, something to eat and something to drink" I started looking for 3
fast food joints and a bar! When I realized what the clue actually
meant, it was a genuine forehead slapper! Matt the Rat deserves a
medal for this one!

Oh, The Great Serpent Mound HH and E. Tuttle HH? Well, I could tell
you, but then I'd have to make you walk the plank! :D
-- Dagonell the Pirate
P.S. This coming weekend, Syracuse NY!
