DesPlaines River Flooding & Letterbox

From: cdem_ltrbox (wp4@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2008-10-02 14:34:05 UTC
I've been putting if off because I feared the worst, but I went to
check on my UGRR:Broken Chains letterbox which is hidden along the
river. This letterbox lies between the river and a neighborhood.
This neighborhood was flooded for 3 blocks from the river and some
residents were evacuated by kayak--not good. As I walked along the
trail, I noticed the mud sediment on the plants going up about 2 feet-
-not good again. I got to the hiding spot and noticed that at least
the large and small log hiding the box were still there. This
surprised me since the small log over the box is basically just a big
stick. My hopes are rising. So I got a stick and pushed around in
the hiding spot--nothing. Unfortunately, it was what I feared.

Then I looked around the area, hoping that maybe the box was lodged
somewhere else. Wait...is that a camaflouged Lock-n-Lock? It was!!
It was lying on the ground about 10 feet away. I scrambled over to
retrieve it. It was dry as a bone inside! So back it went in to its
hiding spot.

As I was driving home though, I started thinking. How did the box
get out of its hiding spot, when the 2 logs holding it in place were
still there in their original spot? And how did it manage to land 10
feet away--out in the open--and not get swept away. There was an
empty liquor bottle right next to the hiding spot. Could it be that
the drinker of that bottle somehow found my box and tossed it 10 feet
away (which means it did survive the flood in its hiding spot!)? But
why would he/she/they do that? Now I am wondering if I should go
back and relocate it--but it is such a great spot.

BirdWoman/Waltzing Pigs
