picnic and to see the water falls - and to buy pasties in Ironwood.

OH - Well, here it breaks down. Since I am an Illini, I am not fond of

Hooray for the Great Lakes!

Wisconsin - born, raised, nearly always lived (except for college on the
East Coast) on the 60-ish mile line from Madison to Milwaukee. Love to be a
cheesehead and all that quaint stereotypes that entails! Majority of my
letterboxing.

Michigan - my husband hails from West Michigan, so we spend a lot of time in
the Holland to Grand Rapids area. Astounded to discover that there was a
letterbox mere blocks away from my in-laws. It made me almost want to
visit! Planted my first out of state box in MI too! Have yet to make it up
to the UP but that is definitely in the game plan.

Iowa - my sister spent six years of college there, which entailed multiple
visits and fun stops like Amana and whatnot. Also have several friends that
lived/attended college there. (I enjoy mocking them, I'll admit it. WI
pride.)

Illinois - sister and the boyfriend are now in the Chicago area, and I spent
a summer doing contract archaeology in the suburbs of the suburbs of Chicago
(Plainfield, anyone?). It's kinda like letterboxing but with no clues and
far fewer finds. You spend a lot of time watching your feet while walking
through corn fields. Chicago is the constant bane of my "driving from
Madison to Grand Rapids" existence. Who else wants a chunnel under Lake
Michigan?!

Indiana - drive in, drive out. No really, I very nearly went to Notre Dame,
and recently spent a lovely weekend watching the sandhill crane migration in
Jasper-Pulaski ... 10,000 plus cranes all in one little field put up quite a
racket! Have also spent time at the amusement park that promised me
"there's more than corn in Indiana!"

Ohio - drove through multiple times on my way to Boston. Also came once for
a good friend's wedding and enjoyed spending time on the shores of yet
another Great Lake.

and Minnesota - visited friends in Minneapolis, took a high school spring
break trip to the Mall of America (yikes), and watching a cop pull a 180
turn up and down and through a divided median to pull my husband over
shortly after entering Minnesota from the more speeder-friendly South Dakota
(where I DID letterbox).

- Archaeofrog
