From: Gooble Gobble (gooble_gobble_3@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-11-08 13:06:38 UTC-08:00
I wondered if someone could tell me when the Great Lakes letterboxing group came to be and WHY wasn't Minnesota included in it? We do have one of the Great Lakes in our our state. I feel kind of left out (not really, but I think we should be included in the Great Lakes Patch, etc, etc, etc). On a lighter note, I'm planning the first ever (to the best of my knowledge that is) Minnesota gathering to be taking place in Spring of '06. All are invited, even you exclusivist great lakers : ) Since none of you really know me yet, that was to be read with ALOT of sarcasm ; )I look forward to meeting all of you I've come to know through your boxes and/or messages I read on this group. I will keep everyone informed of the proceedings. So far I have a location chosen but not a date. A HUGE state park with 20+ miles of trails and plenty of facilities.I will say more later as I don't want the D.N.R. to "accidentally" find out my plans. Although the MN. D.N.R. only says geocaching is forbidden,
not boxing (ah yes, I love semantics). Anyway, I digress, My name is Gooble Gobble, or I suppose Chris if you really have to. It's a pleasure to meet all of you and consider this your official, albeit early and uncertain of the details, invitation to Minnesota this coming spring for the first annual Mn. Gathering. Until then, may the road rise up to meet you, may the wind be always at your back, may the sun shine warm upon your face and the rains fall soft upon your fields.



Sincerly, Gooble Gobble

Welcome Gobble, Gobble (I used to have a letterbox with that name ;-
) Even though MN is not on the home page graphic, you are certainly
welcome here, because yes, Minnesota does border one of the Great
Lakes... but then so does New York... but somehow I don't find it
odd that they are not on the GL map.

Anyway I digress... for the beginning of the "Why is MN not on the GL
Map?" debate, see message #236 (April 2004). The bottom line -- we
really don't have any hard and fast state boundaries. Anyone who
letterboxes and feels comfortable in this group is welcome here.

There have been a few other folks from MN who have been on the list
from time to time. It is great that you are planning a gathering for
next year!

SpringChick
