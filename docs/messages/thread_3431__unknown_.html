That's interesting. We have the BEST luck with stamps that have NO backing and the worst luck with wood backing on hand-carved stamps. (Store-bought stamps seem to stamp well, even with wood backing, but we much prefer to find a hand-carved stamp.) The most disappointing are when the image has nothing to do with the box title or the location.

was started in America. Some guy durring the Civil War wanted a
pickle as his last meal but ended up living. He attributed his

his tree every year.

Consider me a Letterbox snob too!!

We started boxing the last week of November 2006 -- my first gathering was
Feb 2007 (SILO). I was SO overwhelmed with exchanges/cooties/event
stamps/personal travelers that I didn't have much fun at all. Not to mention every
time I looked over at Skeeter (age not-quite-five at the time) she was
just sitting there by herself. I didn't have any time with her and just didn't
enjoy the
near-heart-attack-at-all-the-stamps-to-be-collected-must-not-miss-one atmosphere.

Shortly thereafter I decided that I would no longer do ANY cooties (I just
give them to the kids to pass onto the next), PTs or event stamps.

I get the main event stamp, saying I was there, then I don't do any. I much
prefer to meet people and visit and make memories doing that. The Event
Stamps that remain as permanent plants I will go back and get someday.

Now that I've been to 16 gatherings and am just a gathering or two from
meeting my 400th boxer I have found this to be a breath of fresh air. As I've
already met 60-80% of the people there I only have a few new people to
exchange with and visit with old friends, play with my kids and just lay back
and enjoy the day. This past SILO was one of my favorite events yet -- I had
a blast with my daughter and with those in attendance.

Even if someone offers me a PT, I kindly turn them down . . . its like
offering an alcoholic a sip of beer -- if I get just one I will become that
raving lunatic again.

hx6

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2010-08-09 22:57:00 UTC-05:00
Hear, hear! At the 2010 SILO, we stamped the event stamp + one other
miscellaneous stamp. In 2009: event stamp + 2 Miscellaneous and in 2008,
just the event stamp. Each year we do a few more exchanges during the
get-together, but mostly we just spend our time chatting with people we have
met in previous years (if they are not too busy collecting stamp images and
are willing to talk to someone that doesn't have a PT). I do REALLY
appreciate it when people who made those excellent carvings for the indoor
event plant them in boxes on some trails so we can go hunt for them outside
at some later date.



However at outdoor events w/trails, we DO go out w/various people to hunt
down the boxes - we can socialize while we are walking! It is nice when the
boxes are able to be in place for at least the entire weekend or preferably
even longer. That means we can leave many of the boxes to hunt down at our
leisure after the party is over.

From: Angela (justhave2teach@yahoo.com) | Date: 2010-08-10 18:39:16 UTC

Welcome to the hobby!
A PT is a stamp that a boxer carries with them on the trail or to an event. The only way to get it is to know what you need to do to get it. Usually in the clue it will tell you that you need to ask them a question, give them an answer to a question, or sometimes just ask for it. Usually they are stamps that are meaningful to the boxer and help you get to know them just a little bit better...but not always :)
Happy Boxing

Amen! Several years ago I began requesting "no table boxes" at the events that
I planned. At the time it was met with yays from one camp and contempt from the
other and inevitably people still bring a table box now and then. If I know
about it ahead of time, I usually ask them to please hide it and write up clues,
even if it is just in a bush outside the shelter.

I've been to events where there are a couple dozen boxes out on the table, boxes
that have nothing at all to do with the theme of the event or are not any kind
of special series or traveling series and there are some people who enjoy
sitting inside stamping these boxes all day. While the special series are nice
once in a while (Boch's Circus, etc.), it's not really letterboxing and I would
rather have my events be about getting out on the trail to find boxes and/or
socializing with other letterboxers.

I've participated in a few music-related postal rings on AQ this past winter.
While they are fun in their own way, I by no means consider it letterboxing and
it bugs me when it is referred to in that way.

If that makes a person a letterboxing snob, so be it... you're in good company!
