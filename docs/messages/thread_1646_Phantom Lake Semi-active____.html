Phantom Lake Semi-active....

From: (col.one923@spwl.net) | Date: 2005-07-26 18:43:27 UTC-05:00
After checking on our Phantom Lake box, I made the following note in the
clues......

NOTE: 7/26/05 We are making this box semi-active for awhile. After two
reports of poison ivy near this box, we decided to take a look for
ourselves. To our shock, we found the trails nearly grown over,
requiring some major bush whacking. We planted this box in the early
spring, but now in the middle of summer the paths that the clues lead
you on are almost nonexistent. Not wanting to lead my 7 year old
through the overgrown path, we back tracked to another path to get to
the box from another direction. We found that path with a huge newly
fallen tree over it which would require climbing to get through, as well
as more bush whacking. We never did reach the box to verify the
location of the poison ivy. Because we want all of our boxes to be
"family friendly" and this box no longer fits our criteria, we are
choosing to notify you that this box may be more trouble than it is
worth. Of course if you are adventuresome and willing to deal with the
poison ivy, the bush whacking and the now hard to follow clues then by
all means search for this box. In the mean time, we are going to wait
until Fall before we try to access this box again. We figure if we are
unwilling to go to the box location ourselves, we want to give fair
warning to anyone wanting to hunt for this box. You've been warned....



This was disappointing for us, but a I guess a lesson learned in just
how much things can change in an area from one season to another. My
son was just in awe over how much things had changed. And we thought
this was going to be one of our quick and easy boxes to check on because
it is so close to home.


The Puddlejumpers



RE: [LbGLK] Phantom Lake Semi-active....

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2005-07-26 21:48:47 UTC-05:00
Yes indeed, we have also been amazed at the difference in seasons at some of
our box locations. I did check the date your box was placed while we were
on the trail and realized that things would have been much different then.
We've discovered that checking the month a box was placed gives us a good
indication of how much variation in conditions there might be from when we
are searching. We hid a bunch of boxes in January, so I suspect things are
VERY different now (I know a certain woodpile that houses one of our boxes
is virtually obscured from view by vegetation now - requires much more
diligent searching). A good tactic might be to look for boxes in the same
season they were placed, to have better luck recognizing landmarks
mentioned. Of course a lot of snow could even mess this up...

Wisconsin Hiker

I have to laugh a little bit. We found Tunnel No. 3 in our early days
