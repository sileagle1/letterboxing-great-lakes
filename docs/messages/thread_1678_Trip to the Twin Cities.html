Trip to the Twin Cities

From: Dick and Anne (rhesse34@hotmail.com) | Date: 2005-08-04 17:16:20 UTC
This past weekend was my Mom's 90th in the Twin Cities, so we
included ( of course) a bit of LBing. Here's what we found (and
didn't):
*Elk Mound - neat spot and the box is still in fine shape.
*Fish Out of Water 8 - Merlin grew exploring the Bass Ponds and it
was great to be back, but MUCH changed. The box is fine, but don't
leave it there if there are heavy snows this winter: the spring flood
WILL carry it off.
*Mountain Top Cache - good shape.
*Grand Excusion - Needed to avoid "Ribfest", but the box is fine.
*Minnesota History - A fun series. Boxes 2 and 3 are interchanged.
*45th Parallel - STILL in good shape... even with a hole in the
box.
*The Crystal of MIC - Neat place and in good shape.
*Suburban Wildlife - All in good shape.
*Little Brown Dog's Delight - The breeze off the lake was
delightful. Box #2 is perilously close to a vast and healthy stand
of Poison Ivy.
*Little Brown Dog Goes to School - Thanks to a heads up from
Wisconsin Hiker we were first finders and the first finders "prizes"
are a great idea... we'll probably use it, too.
*Catty - Stumped!! :-( "7"??? "Garden Gateway"??? Maybe next
trip.
*Mazomani Trail - Another great spot, but the "Overlook Sign" seems
to be missing, so we had a bit of a hike before we backtracked.
*Woman Bathing Among Reeds - We wonder who was watching us?
* Goldie Gopher - Merlin enjoyed being back on campus again... and
the river flats where, a goodly time ago, he used to park his car.
*Karl Oskar House - A very interesting spot. Wish it had been the
weekend so the house had been open. Oh, and a surprise first find.
*Lumbering Along - We couldn't find it. Pretty sure we had the
right place, too. :-(
*Chippewa Valley Choo-Choo - Neat spot... wish the train had been
running.
*JakerJaz Series - A day behind Dragon. The big tree has been wind-
thrown: the box is fine.
*Rocky Arbor - Amazing place! The box is fine.

A couple of things we noticed: Many of the boxes were very near
Poison Ivy, something we make an effort to avoid when looking for a
hiding place. Knowing what it looks like in all seasons helps, and
is probably a worthwhile bit of knowledge for all LBers. Another
thing we noticed was that a fair number of the LBs were in very
public and/or hiding places that didn't protect the LBs from the
elements or chance discovery very well. But, it was GREAT to see to
see so many LBs in Minnesota... an up and coming LB area. We're
looking forward to our next trip.

Merlin and Ardea
