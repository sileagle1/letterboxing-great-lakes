We like the idea of State and local gatherings. The group size is not
overwhelming and the drive is manageable. As letterboxing gains in
popularity, this will probably end up happening anyway. Of course,
these types of gatherings should remain open to anyone who wishes to
make the effort to attend. Perhaps a region-wide gathering could be
initiated to take place at the same time each year, say, the 3rd
weekend in May (for example) and rotate from State to State each year
(initial rotation schedule to be determined by lots). This would make
it a fixed-in-time event and each State would get a chance to host
it. It might even turn into a letterboxing convention. HOO-AHH!

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2006-10-02 22:00:57 UTC
Hi!

I know there are some newer people on the board, so I just wanted to
remind everyone that anyone and everyone can plan gatherings with
whatever theme and whatever date they'd like. No voting or prior
approval necessary!

The Great Lakes region does have an annual event that SpringChick
started, and she has stated that it should always be the first
Saturday after Memorial Day and the location should move around
within the region.

However, any OTHER things you'd like to organize would probably be
welcomed with enthusiasm by the boxers in your area! Last year
Martini Man and I hosted a Haunted Harvest event in October just
because we felt like it. If you check out the events section of
AtlasQuest you'll see that there are lots of other events going on in
other areas of the USA at all times of the year. Some are very
simple and informal and some are pretty elaborate. SpringChick and
Azobox are hosting an event in MI this fall but that doesn't mean
there can't be other events in WI or IL this fall or winter. Since
the June regional event is too far away for some people to travel to,
and since most of us like gatherings, I'd like to encourage all of
you to think about hosting something at some point. Remember - it
doesn't have to be elaborate! Some winter events in other states
don't even include much "boxing", but focus more on social
interaction or perhaps some sharing of tips & tricks. I know we have
a lot of enthusiatic and creative people in the region, so I'm hoping
something might materialize in the WI/IL area before the 2007 GL
event in Ohio (because that seems SO far in the future!)

Wisconsin Hiker

Welcome! Here's some info I posted as a "Frequently Asked Question"
on a gathering web site in 2006:

I've never been to a Gathering before. What should I expect?

* Friendly folks - Don't be shy, just walk up and say hi!

* Tasty food

* Lots of boxes, some will be WOM boxes (Word of Mouth) so stay alert
for verbal hints, unusual questions or clues that get distributed in
ways other than the clue booklet.

* More tips can be found here:
http://www.atlasquest.com/events/tips2.html

We hope to meet you at one of the gatherings!
