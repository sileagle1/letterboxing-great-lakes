Re: Land Usage Policies >> State Parks

From: Deb (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-03-23 11:47:05 UTC
Hey Tom... I have received the same information as you about the
new State Park policy. One of the employees at the State Park here
who is a letterboxer, tipped me off that the ranger there had shared
the new policy with her. It was my understanding that it was not
yet a sure thing and it wouldn't be happening until next year. But
that could have changed since then.

It is actually the reason I have been keeping tabs on the geocaching
site. I wanted to see if there was discussion of this there, but
have not see anything thus far. I would assume that the policy
would include both letterboxes and geocaches.

Of course I am not happy about the policy and think it is a shame
that they would charge people to use the parks in the way they were
intended to be used -- for recreation and outdoor activity. I will
not pay to place boxes and when the time comes, will remove my boxes
from the parks and find new homes for them. There are many miles of
beautiful trails around -- if the State Parks don't want people
coming there (not to mention the revenue and awareness it helps to
generate), then I will go elsewhere.

I was wondering if they will also begin to charge people to fish, to
play volleyball and to fly kites on the beach?

I think it all comes down to greed and ignorance.

SpringChick

> have multiple letterboxes planted. She let me know that there is
a new
> policy on geo-caching at Michigan State Parks. Essentially,
they've
> decided that a Use Permit must be filed for each letterbox or other
> physical object placed in a park, and a $35 fee must be paid. The
> letterbox can remain in place for a maximum of 3 months.
>
>
>
> This is quite a bit different than the existing State Park
practices
> which are shown on the Michigan Geo-Caching website. Perhaps this
> policy has not been communicated to many people yet. Even though
I was
> disappointed to hear about it, I was glad that this particular
State
> Park employee brought it to my attention in such a friendly,
positive
> way. That will give us a chance to remove our boxes the next time
we're
> in the area; perhaps we'll be able to find a non-State-Park
location for
> them in the future.
>
>
>
> Below, I have pasted the message from State Park Headquarters,
which the
> employee forwarded to me. She also forwarded the 2 documents
mentioned:
> the new policy, and a sample Use Permit. I'll place those 2
documents
> in the GLK group's Yahoo! database for anyone who is interested.
>
>
>
> My reason for bringing this up, is not to start a flame war on why
this
> is or isn't a good policy. in fact, I was very excited to see this
new
> Great Lakes Group was started, since I know this group will be
able to
> have a much calmer discussion about such topics! But I wanted to
alert
> you all to it, in case other State Parks don't take the time to e-
mail
> or call you, like they did for me. As you'll see in the policy,
they
> have the authority to remove "unpermitted" boxes as of 6/1/2004,
and I
> would hate to see any of you lose the stamps and log books that
you have
> out there.
>
>
>
> --TJ_Mich
>

> Attached is a new policy addressing geocaching on PRB lands. This
is a
> new treasure-hunting type "hobby" made possible through the use of
GPS
> units and the internet. In a nutshell, a geocacher places a
container of
> interesting items (?) in the park, then posts hints and GPS
coordinates
> on the geocaching website. Geocaching "hunters" look up these
items on
> the internet then go look for the cache. Typically, the finder
will sign
> the required log book and may exchange some items. We already have
a
> number of these geocaches on our lands.
>
>
>
> Another form of this activity is known as "letter-boxing". While
this
> policy refers to geocaching, any activity which involves the
placement
> of items on PRB lands for others to find would be covered by this
> policy.
>
>
>
> Virtual caching involves the posting of hints and GPS coordinates
to
> direct "hunters" to an existing feature. It may be a sign, a
waterfall
> or other natural feature. There is no actual placement of
materials on
> site and there is no Use Permit fee charged for such virtual
caches,
> although a Use Permit is still required. Virtual caches are
preferred
> over physical caches (and there is a financial incentive, too!).
>
>
>
> This policy is to take immediate effect. A sample Use Permit is
also
> included to provide guidance. Please distribute to your respective
> units.

Hi all.

There is a discussion about this going on in the MI-geocaching forum right at this moment. Apparently, there is a meeting scheduled for tomorrow between MI-GO folks and the parks and rec people. The goal being to try and sway them from implementing the $35.00 fee and to try and reduce the movement of placements from every 3 mths to 1-2 times a year.

MI-GO is also trying to tally the $$ spent by folks in state parks to try and provide an estimate of revenue that boxing/caching brings to the parks, so please consider adding in your numbers. To view the discussions on the forum you do need to register, but membership is FREE.

Please consider taking a look!

THANKS!

Re: Land Usage Policies >> State Parks

From: LeapinLizards (emptyenergy1@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-03-25 04:53:27 UTC

Hi all,

We were very disappointed to hear about the efforts of the Michigan
State Parks to charge for letterboxes placed in the parks. On our
trip from Wisconsin to Michigan last spring, (for the specific
purpose of going letterboxing), we were very happy that there were
boxes in the state parks. We've found that we would never hear about
these places if it weren't for the letterboxes. It's free
advertising for the parks....and they are going to charge "us" to do
it for them. Amazing! $35 per box! Per 3 months....I don't know of
anybody who would pay that. Part of the benefits of letterboxing is
how inexpensive it is to do...unless you're taking 5,000 mile
roadtrips down Route 66 to do it;)
The parks are seeing it as a source of more revenue....I think it
will have an opposite effect. The placement fee will erradicate any
boxes in the park. Less people will go to the parks. Not enough to
close them down obviously, but less will go. Missing out on the
great things to see. I think of our trip through Texas and our stop
at Palo Duro Canyon....never would have known about it if there
weren't two letterboxes that were just 25 mins south of Amarillo.
Very cool park. In Tulsa, OK, there was the Oxley Nature Center
which basically sponsors its own series...very cool idea. And don't
forget Connecticut...who's state parks have their own statewide
series.
We think Springchick is right. Ignorance and greed. We'll
happily pay the $20 to go to the Michigan State Parks...as we did
last spring, but to be completely honest, we'd skip them to go to a
free park elsewhere for the letterboxes...it just seems like more fun.
As for the limited placement time....I couldn't imagine the placer
having to mess around with clues and placement that often. Not to
mention, we don't really care to try to keep up with clues that
change every 3 months, 6 months...year...5 years...It's cool to find
the old boxes....We can't wait til some of these boxes start pushing
10-15 years in existence....maybe more. The older boxes kinda have a
badge of honor to us.

I guess it's just sad that the commercialism of our society has to
invade even the most benign, wholesome fun....

Intro to come later:)

Leapin' Lizards
John and Sara
