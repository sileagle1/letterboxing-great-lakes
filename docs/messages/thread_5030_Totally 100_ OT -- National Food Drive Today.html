From: xxxxxxxx (BrighidFarm@comcast.net) | Date: 2008-05-10 09:06:05 UTC-05:00
!!!!! FOOD DRIVE ALERT !!!!!

The Postal Service employees hold an annual food drive asking all our
patrons who can afford it to leave some boxes or cans of food in or next to
their mailboxes today, Saturday May 10th for pick up by their mail carriers.

Tomorrow is Mother's Day. Think of the single moms who are just managing to
get by and need some help from your local food pantries. Think of the
families, even, who have both parents working but who who are still just
managing to scrape by. Think of people in this country with no health
insurance who have to decide whether to pay for the prescriptions that month
or buy food. The local food pantry helps keep them going.

Even if it's just a can or two or a couple of boxes of mac & cheese mix, if
you can afford it, please consider donating. The food stays in the community
because the mail carriers from each community see that the food gets
delivered to that community's food pantries.

Tons of people donate during the Thanksgiving and Christmas seasons. This is
the time of year when food pantries are traditionally running low on food
and will greatly appreciate every single can donated. Please consider
helping out and donating.

!!! Thanks !!!

~~ Mosey ~~

RE: [LbGLK] Re: Totally 100% OT -- National Food Drive Today

From: xxxxxxxx (BrighidFarm@comcast.net) | Date: 2008-05-11 08:33:04 UTC-05:00
Yep, when we got finished up for the day, we were down too. 6000 pounds
last year compared to 4900 this year (Lombard, IL -- area of probably about
45,000 to 50,000 people). Times are getting rougher. But it's 4900 pounds
that the pantry didn't have on Friday, so I'm EXTREMELY grateful to our
patrons for contributing. Five LLV mail trucks filled to the gills.

I take the day as a vacation day to be able to just concentrate on the food
drive. A lot of carriers who are on vacation, are retired, have the day
off, etc. come in on Saturday just to drive around and pick up any food they
see on porches so that the carrier doing the route has a little less to have
to carry. Plus we go out and pick up the food from the patrons of carriers
who don't cooperate with the drive at all and won't pick up the food from
their patrons. In driving around Lombard, plucking food off porches, twice
I almost picked up a load of dirty diapers. Now, if they'd been clean
diapers I would've grabbed 'em as a donation. :-)

Folks donated some usually-not-thought-of items too.........someone donated
a bunch of hearing aid batteries (might come in handy for folks who visit
the pantry & wear hearing aids), a few people donated new children's toys,
lots of soaps, shampoos, pet food (poor folks often have pets too), the
kinds of things that can't be purchased with food stamps. Some patron
must've gotten a good deal on VO5 shampoo because one patron donated 50
bottles. Or maybe they work for the company.

And then...........

then...............

someone donated a package of opened hot dogs in a tupperware bowl.
OKaaaaaaaaaaaaay.

And we got some candy with an expiration date of 2006. OK, that's gonna be a
wee bit stale. :-)

My huuuge downfall was the M&Ms. I'm an M&Maholic and carriers were
bringing in bags of donations that had those really large bags of M&Ms in
them. Our maintenance guy was helping us pack the food in tubs and I made
him take them and hide them all way down in the bottom of our packing while
I kept my eyes closed so I didn't see where he put them. Making sure that
M&Ms get to the pantry is my only downfall in food drives.

You mentioned pantries needing food all year long -- that's why I like
having a national food drive at this time of year. Folks tend to think of
their local food pantries around Thanksgiving, Christmas, etc., and this
time of year supplies can get tight for the pantries.

!!! ThankyouThankyouThankyou !!! to everyone who donated.

~~ Mosey ~~
