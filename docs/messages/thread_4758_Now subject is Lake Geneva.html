From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2008-02-06 14:24:46 UTC-06:00
Our club always does it as a 1-day hike. We start out in Williams Bay at 8
am, heading southwest. We take a short break in Fontana, then stop near a
boat launch spot were we eat our lunches. We then continue into the town of
Lake Geneva, where most of us buy ice cream cones as a mid-afternoon treat.
We then continue on the last stretch along the north shore. Most people
finish at around 4:30-5 pm. Contrary to some publications, it really isn't
a 26-mile hike. Many years ago our club took a measuring wheel along and it
showed only 22 miles.

Wisconsin Hiker

P.S. I've also done the "around the lake" hike 5 times. My mom has done it
3 times with us.
