Hitchhiker angst

From: Heather M. Fieldhouse (bunnyhugger@ameritech.net) | Date: 2006-09-06 18:48:19 UTC-04:00
My husband and I (actually, it was mostly his creation) recently
planted a hitchhiker to commemorate our 50th find. (I know that
doesn't sound like very many but we're occasional boxers so it took
us more than a year to do it!) We're really, really proud of it --
it's got a handmade log, a custom box, and a great stamp carved by my
husband. It was really hard to let it go! I wrote my email address
on the log and noted that it is listed on LbNA.

Well, I see from LbNA that at least one person has subsequently
reported finding the box that I left it in, but no one reported
finding my hitchhiker. Is it common for people to not report finding
hitchhikers? Should I worry? I would hate to think it's already
gone astray. I guess I'm a little concerned because I know there are
a lot of new letterboxers out there right now and I'm afraid that
someone might not have known what it was or what to do with it.

This is our second hitchhiker -- the first disappeared off the radar
shortly after I placed it (the first person to find it emailed me,
but then I never heard from anyone again). Since it's been well over
a year since I last heard from that one, I am sure it's gotten lost.
I would hate to think that a similar fate has befallen our new one.


Heather F.

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-09-08 19:25:35 UTC-04:00
Yeh, I have a couple like that also. Surfin' Santa was one that made his
initial appearance at the first GL Gathering back in 2003 and I get a couple
e-mails each year about him while there are others, more recent, that I hear
nothing of.

As disheartening as it may be, when you launch a HH, you have to accept the
fact that you may never hear anything about it and treat the messages you do
get as nice little bonuses/surprises. If that isn't something you can do,
then it's probably better to put your effort into a traditional letterbox
rather than a HH.

SpringChick
