Our oldest plant that still has the original stamp and logbook is "Leelinaw", near Ironwood MI. It was planted on June 29, 2003, and is still active as of this summer.

In close proximity are "Go Blue" in Ann Arbor, and "Cut River Bridge" in Epoufette MI - both dating to August 2003, still original stamps, but each needed new logbooks due to filling up. Our oldest box is "Poplar Point" in Henes Park, Menominee MI, from April 19, 2003, still active but that stamp has been replaced twice.

My oldest still-active box is "Rock Cut", planted in August 2004.  The original store-bought stamp was replaced long ago by a hand-carved one, but the logbook is still the original.  I planted this along one of my favorite equestrial trails and used to check on it every time I rode there.  Eventually my horse Lark would just stop at that location without me needing to "whoa" him :)
 
This is the very first box I planted and although I have planted many others that have met unfortunate ends (muggles, geocachers, flooding, animals, over-zealous park rangers) this one has rested unmolested for over 9 years.
