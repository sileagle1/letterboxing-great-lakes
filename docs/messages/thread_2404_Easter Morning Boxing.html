Easter Morning Boxing

From: karen grage (jkgrage@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-04-16 20:06:17 UTC-05:00
Well, I must say, it's no easier to be a first finder on a box 30 miles
north of Chicago than it is closer to the city. I decided to spend Easter
morning (also my birthday) letterboxing. I was going to try the Easter egg
series but after reading that some may not be there I decided to postpone
that trip and stay a little closer to home. How happy I was when I found a
new box placed at a location just a few miles from home.

After opening Easter baskets and piling everyone into the car we made our
way out to the new Fort Sheridan Letterbox. It is a great location and
without giving too much away I want to mention that with almost no one out
this morning, we run into the only two people in site, right by the
letterbox! We waited them out, kind of invaded their space and went to find
the box. After we retrieved it we found we missed being first finders by
two hours!!!! That's what we get for opening Easter baskets before boxing!

On a brighter note, we headed to another location for another box (it had a
playground next to it and I promised my son). While enjoying some time on a
bench as he played at the park a woman with a piece of paper passed us by.
My husband looked at me and said 'I think she's a letterboxer!'. We watched
as she looked at the place for clue one, then she kept walking, looking at
the place for clue two. She was a boxer!!!

We stalked... ummmm... I mean followed her for a little ways and finally
approached her and introduced ourselves as fellow boxers. To top this off,
she was none other than Prairie Dog, the boxer who beat us out for the first
find on the box earlier in the morning!!! I must say, this was a much
better treat, I've been a first finder before but have never met a fellow
boxer on the trail. We continued on together to find the box, enjoy some
boxing conversation and exchange stamps. It was a perfect birthday treat!

Happy Easter to all and Happy boxing!

jacks
