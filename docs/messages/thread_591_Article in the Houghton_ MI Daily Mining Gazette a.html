Hooboy! I was just coming to post that the article has been picked
up by the AP and showed up in today's Marquette Mining Journal. But
that one doesn't mention me so I was pretty surprised to see that I
was mentioned in the original which I hadn't read till just now.
And speaking of UP letterboxing, which I did some of last week, I'm
happy to report that my Sugarloaf and Mystery Model Town boxes are in
good condition and well-visited (it's so nice to place boxes and
still have them there two years later!). I was also only the second
person to sign in at the Hogback box--a great hike I hadn't done in
40 years. It says something that it and Sugarloaf are essentially
across the street from each other and yet it's only had two visitors
compared to at least a dozen at Sugarloaf. (What it says, of course,
is that you can do one in 20 minutes and the other took us 2 and a
half hours!)
On the way back through Wisconsin I also picked up Red Arrow Park
in Marinette and Badger Park in Pestigo. Both in cities I'd been
through dozens and dozens of times. Both in beautiful city parks I
hadn't previously even known were there. Of course that meant it was
6:30 in the evening before we left Peshtigo, giving us a mighty late
evening arrival back in Chicago, but sleep is overrated anyway.
