Mn. Fall Gathering planting update

From: Gooble Gobble (gooble_gobble_3@yahoo.com) | Date: 2009-09-15 15:26:26 UTC-07:00
Why do I always seem to pick the hottest day to go for a five hour planting binge? I just got back from the park and what a day! Kudos to Red Cat for the park selection. It's SO beautiful. There is a nice range of hardwood and pine segments as well as some nice prairies. There really is something for everyone. The sheer variety of mushrooms I found today would make even Fungus Woman jealous! Among others, I found two choice edibles: Boletus Edulis (Porcini) and Chantrelles. MMMMM Mushrooms.

I hope that everyone has a great day on the 10th of Oct. There should be plenty of boxes to find, and a nice choice of environments to plant them in. I recommend going out to plant as soon as you can if you're not familiar with the park. As I said, the terrain is varied and there are some really cool areas just begging to be stocked with boxes. Go and familiarize yourself with the park, it will help out on the big day so you have a general feel for the layout of the park. It's not going to be here soon enough, it never is.
Happy Trails to all.
Gooble Gobble

Yes, I did a little bit of research on the park this morning and found this
description:



"Cottage Grove Ravine Regional Park consists of 506 acres of hills and
dramatic, heavily wooded ravines with occasional clearings of old field and
prairie. This varied topography and landscape types means that its slopes
receive different sun exposures. This creates an unusual variety of
vegetation and habitats. Look for grouse, ermine, cottontail rabbits, deer
and a number of bird species, including the pileated woodpecker."



I also found a map that shows the trails which I figured would be useful:



http://www.co.washington.mn.us/client_files/documents/pkp/Maps/PKP-CGRskimap
.pdf



We're looking forward to being there! (for a while at least, until we need
to head to our niece's wedding.)
