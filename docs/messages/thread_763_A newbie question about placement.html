Rules? What rules? Bike paths are certainly fair game for placing boxes. There is a series around here that is on (or rather just off) a bike trail that we found fairly enjoyable (even if we only found two out of four boxes and were plagued by mosquitoes and rabid woodchucks...it was still a very nice hike). As for a rule of thumb for box proximity...there aren't really any. I will however say that I have never seen boxes placed that close together. Then again, I'm new to the hobby and haven't found a great deal of boxes yet. If you really want to place some boxes (or a series) even if they are kinda close together...go for it. Or, you could always narrow your choices down to one or two that are absolutely perfect for a box. I come across good hiding places for boxes all the time, but upon closer inspection many of them are not as great as I originally thought.

From: Bill and Ann Fisher (wfisher47@comcast.net) | Date: 2004-10-14 22:04:19 UTC-05:00

Some of my favorite boxes were in places that weren't particularly
special--until I read the clues. Take a look at the clues Birkiebob wrote
for his "Boyhood Adventure" boxes in
Milwaukee.
http://letterboxing.org/BoxView.asp?boxnum=2987&boxname=Boyhood_Adventures
One of the boxes is in a nice enough park, on the lake, glad to be
there. The other one, though, the one that's missing, is really in a very
ordinary playground with a drainage ditch and a little patch of
woods. Nothing special--but the clues make it clear how special it once
was to a young boy. Matt the Rat's Chicago Underground box,
http://letterboxing.org/BoxView.asp?boxnum=836&boxname=Chicago_Underground
is another example of a spot no one would think to visit if they weren't
tracking down a box.

Lots of things other than a good location can make a box special--an log
book or stamp made with extra care, mystery or bonus clues, or just a good
story. As an urban boxer who often must hide boxes in very public places,
I have to avoid the top tourist spots simply because the boxes would never
last there. It's a lot more fun to find the places people wouldn't
naturally visit and then make them glad they made the effort.
