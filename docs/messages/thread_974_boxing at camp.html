boxing at camp

From: Kathi LK Krankoski (krank@ameritech.net) | Date: 2005-01-16 23:54:33 UTC-06:00
Brrr...hard to type with cold fingers.

Just got back from a weekend at GS camp. My buddy Pippin was the chief cook
and I was at her beck and call (it's a joke) as her assistant, so we stayed
warm in the kitchen (she makes one heck of a great spinache lasagna, may I
suggest you talk her into making it for a future Gathering??).

We had 31 Cadette/Senior level (though we aren't supposed to call it that
any more...but "11-17 teen Scouts" sounds goofy to me) girls for what was
called "Me Weekend", as in "all about me" (=them!). They were allowed to do
whatever they wanted (within reason!), but we did provide some suggestions,
and one thing of course was introducing them to the various boxes we've been
hiding at camp since summer. Most of the girls did participate, and they
really enjoyed it. Carving stamps and coming up with trail names ("Lemon
Sniffer"?!?!) was their favorite. While we didn't use "real" carving
materials, we had a variety of regular eraser materials which suited their
carving mania quite well. I provided a history of the hobby and basic
"rules" (both verbally and in the form of handouts for them to take home).
I found some dual tip art makers on sale for 45 cents (!!!) so each got one
to take home. I included in the handouts some of the more winter-friendly
clues (indoors and/or near the lodge we were staying in) and hid three
special-for-the-weekend stamps ("WhatZITtoya" was an empty Stridex jar
containing the log and stamp of a girl's face with a red zit on her nose.
They had to ask me to borrow my jar of Stridex to obtain it.). That wasn't
enough for one group in particular, who managed to hunt out several others
which were frozen but they managed to pry loose (had to double-check that
they hid them well again!). That group also placed their own box to add to
the collection.

Since we haven't been announcing the boxes at any of our council leader
meetings or anything, we thought this would be a good way to get the girls
themselves interested, and then they can disseminate the info to their
troops (this was a council-wide event, so they were from a number of
individual troops). Watch out for a bunch of new boxers in the near-west
Chicago suburbs. I only hope they learned their lessons well, and that I
instilled (=pounded into their heads) the "rules of the sport with no
rules"!

Note to CMoon: I found S'mores...first finder! And a whole slew of finders
right on my heels! Great hiding place!
