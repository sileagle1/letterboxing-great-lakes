From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-09-07 12:37:52 UTC-04:00
I have a partner who is not always quite as enthusiastic about LB as I am also. Sometimes he is eager to go, sometimes not. Same with gatherings. My feeling... if he doesn't want to go to the gathering, I don't beg. There are bound to be lots of people there I will enjoy meeting and hanging out with and I would rather go alone than with an unenthusiastic companion who is inconspicously rolling his eyes as we tell trail stories and recount moments of letterboxing enlightenment, and counting the minutes until we can leave.

If she doesn't want to come with you, just come alone. We will be nice to you and perhaps the decision for you to go independently and the respect you give her by not forcing the issue will make her see how important this is to you and encourage participation in the future.

SpringChick

It sounds like she likes letterboxing. Perhaps she is uneasy about meeting
"a group of strangers"? If so, I can guarantee that you will be welcomed
like long lost friends when you show up. Last year we attended the huge
Monster Mash event in Connecticut and didn't know a soul there when we
arrived. We just hung out the whole day at the event, chatting with lots of
boxers, meeting their babies and dogs. We really enjoyed it! This year we
traveled to Pennsylvania for 10 days and Oregon for 9 days. On both trips
we blindly met "strangers" that we only knew through email and postings on
letterboxing boards. We shared some great hikes and nice meals with these
people and now consider them friends. Our experience has been that the
letterboxing community is like a big extended family, very welcoming to
fellow addicts!

However if your wife still doesn't want to come to the gathering, I agree
with those that suggested coming alone. In our travels, and even here
locally, we have met many other people whose "significant other" just isn't
as obsessed as they are. In other regions these "singles in letterboxing"
seem to search each other out to share hunting expeditions, then happily
return to their significant others to resume the rest of their "normal" life
activities. We're currently encouraging a lot of single women to
start/continue boxing and have planned a few joint excursions with them.
We're hoping that this area will develop a sense of camaraderie that will
allow the "single" boxers to communicate with each other on-line to plan
impromptu excursions to hunt boxes together.

Hope to see you on OCT 1!

Wisconsin Hiker
