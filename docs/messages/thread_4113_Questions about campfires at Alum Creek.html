Questions about campfires at Alum Creek

From: trailtracker99 (mmfunk99@greenapple.com) | Date: 2007-05-29 19:53:11 UTC
Hi everyone,

I went ahead and called Alum Creek Camp Grounds today about what
campers can and cannot do regarding wood used to build fires at the
Columbus Cluez-In event this coming weekend. Here is what I found out:

No wood can be brought in from another state at all. No (random) wood
can be burned that is laying on the ground in the woods or near the
camp sites. Wood from Ohio can be brought to the camp site to be burned.

There are private people who sell wood in the area just outside Alum
Creek grounds for $4 per bundle. Any wood brought into the camp site
either must be burned or cannot be taken back out once brought in.

I thought this might be helpful to know since not everyone is under
these same current restrictions.

Won't be long now! :)
Trailtracker

Thank you for your post. No worries at all--more than once I have done
the same thing!!

I do want to let everyone know that I went and checked out the campsites
today. The tent camping and the camper ones are all very nice and clean
and well-maintained. They each have a picnic table and fire ring. Many
of the tent camping ones have some larger chunks of partially burned
logs in them so that is good!

I am going to bring firewood so if anyone needs some, I would be more
than willing to share---and/or share my campfire too. I am right on the
end of the M row of tent campsites and there is plenty of room if anyone
wants to pull up a chair.

I was also thinking that it would be nice to put a sign with our
trailname, signature stamp and what state we are from--at our
campsites. We don't do that very often when we post messages here and I
would love to associate what state folks are from with trail names.
Just a thought--if anyone else would like to do this too. I printed off
the suggested GLK 2007 logo and will just draw a line to the state of
Ohio with my other info.

Last---and perhaps I'm the only doofus that would do this, but for those
of us who are camping---we are at the regular campsites---and NOT the
group campsites off Lewis Center Road?! (Can you guess what I did
first???) :P The entrance to our regular campsites is off Old State
Road and it says "Campground Welcome Center" on the park map. (Yes! I
do this on occasion with hunting letterboxes too!)

The restroom facilites were clean at the campsites as well---but I did
not see any showers. I have not checked to see if there are any
elsewhere.

Are we there yet??
