From: Atom118 (Aatom118@gmail.com) | Date: 2011-08-16 15:30:44 UTC-05:00
Sadly, our 'Memories of the White City' seems to have disappeared. It
has hidden in the same stump for the last 5 years, nearly as long as
we've been letterboxing, and it was my first plant. It had been
replanted (slightly) in the same spot after 2 of the original series of
3 disappeared, but now the lone hold-out is gone too. Given the age of
the first plants, I don't think I'm going to replace it. Anyways, I'm a
bit bummed it disappeared, but I'm also surprised it lasted as long as
it did. It is still a nice walk, but if you have the clues, I doubt
you'll find a box.

Atom 118 of the Atomic Beans

Re: bummer/lost box

From: klk-fw (krank@ameritech.net) | Date: 2011-08-16 20:54:29 UTC
I was the person looking for it today and broke the news to Atom. When he explained the actual hiding place to me (I was at the correct stump) I feel it might be there but out of reach due to advanced rotting. Besides this, there were a number of new shrubs recently planted within a foot of the hiding place. If it had been visible at one time, undoubtedly it would have been removed.

But I did have some Adventures in the White City today, despite the lack of letterbox....

-- The Kid (the 21 year old formerly known as Papillon) actually went with me! I think I guilted her into spending some time with me, but besides she just likes to go into the city....

-- The Osaka Gardens were beautiful; it was neat to imagine walking the grounds of the Columbian Exposition. I felt I should have been wearing a long dress and carrying a parasol....

-- As we walked around Promontory Point, we saw:

---- A grandpa in a Speedo (Ewww...nothing against grandpas or Speedos, but in this case they should not have been one and the same.)

---- A nude (male) sunbather! (Yikes! And he was lying on his back!)

---- The "rescue" of a duffel bag from the lake which contained what appeared to be the belongings of a homeless person (all sorts of strange odds and ends: fake holly, windchimes, candles, mismatched drapery panels, t-shirts, a baby blanket, ads for psychic readers, a chain letter, etc.). Kind of sad...hopefully the owner didn't undergo the same fate as the bag.......

---- The nude sunbather...again! (and there were LOTS of people walking around too!)

Back to boring Suburbia........ Thanks for the adventure, AB!

--klk/fw
