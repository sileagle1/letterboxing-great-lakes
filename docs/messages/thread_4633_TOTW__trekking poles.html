From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2007-12-25 19:50:11 UTC-06:00
Merry Christmas! We can't wait to meet Logan - he sounds like a great
addition to your team.

Both Martini Man & I have used trekking poles for several years, but we only
use them in the mountains. We primarily got them because of the downhills.
Using poles allows our arms to take some of our weight, instead of our knees
bearing the full burden as we go down steep descents. However I have found
them also very handy on steep ascents - almost like having handrails to help
haul yourself up. It's a good workout for your arms, so a hike now benefits
your WHOLE body! (You may have noticed our poles in some of our blog
photos.) Poles are also useful when doing stream crossing if you are trying
to hop from rock to rock.

Usually we adjust the height of the poles depending on what we are doing -
shorter for the uphill and longer for downhill. They telescope to a short
enough length that we can pack them in our suitcase. We've used our poles
in the midwest very rarely, such as if we are in the Kettle Moraine in early
spring when there is still snow and/or ice on the trails. However other
people in our hiking club use them more often on hillier trails in the area.

I guess the only con is carrying them along if you don't really need/want to
use them. However when we are doing mountain hiking, we are always wearing
our backpacks and have some straps that we use to secure the poles to the
pack if we won't be using the poles for a while.

Wisconsin Hiker
