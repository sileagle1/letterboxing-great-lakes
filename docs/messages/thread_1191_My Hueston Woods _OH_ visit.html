My Hueston Woods (OH) visit

From: corvisia (bunnyhugger@ameritech.net) | Date: 2005-04-18 22:58:43 UTC

This weekend my husband and I drove to the Cincinnati area to
visit Kings Island. Before going home we stopped at Hueston
Woods State Park. It's a sentimental spot for us because we
used to live nearby and often visited the park to picnic and fly
kites. We didn't have a lot of time but we did look for a few
letterboxes in the area.

First we looked for and found Seuss #1. We were the first
finders on that one. Then we looked for, but were unable to find
Sno. I hope we just overlooked it. Finally, we looked for, and
found the Thrasher. As an added bonus, it had a hitchhiker
hiding in it.

Two new boxes plus a hitchhiker for us -- and a fun day at Kings
Island. I'd say the weekend was a success.

On Friday before we left, we went and checked on the new box
we planted last week at the Capitol in Lansing. It had only been
five days since it was planted, but we were already antsy to see if
it was still all right -- we're like new parents! Anyway, it was fine,
still in its hiding spot, although no one had stamped in yet.

Heather F.
