Geocacher story

From: azobox (jparkerg@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-11-17 20:54:09 UTC
In the past there have been occasional comments about geocachers on
this list. I want to report that I recently received a very friendly
message from a cacher who found one of my letterboxes. He 'stumbled'
upon a bonus box and after borrowing a stamp from another cacher and
stamping in, he contacted me. He was very respectful of letterboxing
and was curious about why he couldn't find the clues to the box he'd
found. He apparently went on the various clue sites and searched. But,
of course, since it's a bonus, there were no online clues. He wants to
find the main box now.
But he's left with a "reverse-challenge". Since the bonus is attached
to a mystery box, he'll have to discover which box the bonus is
attached to. Some of you who have searched in W Mi may know of which I
speak. I'd say, in fact, that his chances are slim.
I responded with the particulars of bonus clues and a bit more. It
turns out those geocachers are now intrigued with letterboxing as
another activity to get them outdoors and into nature.

azobox

P.S. It seems that the geocacher thought he had found the perfect spot
for his micro-cache only to discover that a letterboxer had also
thought it was just right. Hmmmm....maybe we do think alike.
