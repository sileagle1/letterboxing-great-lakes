The Hoosier Heckmans are Ed, Robin, & their 3 kids, ages 15, 8, & 4. I
discovered
letterboxing thanks to the article in Family Fun magazine this past summer &
thanks to Cindi of the DUCA family when she mentioned on another board that
they were going to Michigan to letterbox over the weekend. (Thanks,
Cindi!!)

We have actually gone out boxing twice. The first one we found was
Armadillo Jo's Seven Pillars of the Mississenewa. (I look forward to find
her Angry Woodchuck too, as I know the general area he's in!!) The kids

We do all of our boxing together. Ok I did do one box by myself but that means that Jeff dropped me off at one end of the walkway and we worked thru the clues via phone until I found the box and stamped in and then we chatted until I walked to the other end of the walkway. So it was by myself right?? We would have done it together but the kids were sleeping and we weren't really sure where we could legally park??

Anyways we have one sig stamp for the family. When Nick got old enough to want to stamp in on occasion he wanted his initial so I just went and bought an 'N' stamp. He decided for Claire's birthday she should get her own stamp too so that's what he got her. Neither of them have gotten to the point where they want to stamp into every box but they carry their own backpack/fanny packs with their own logbooks, ink dot and stamp. I think Nick gets more excitment writing his name in the logbook when I let him. :)

It's all a personal thing. Do you think you'll be going as a group all the time?? If you do then maybe one stamp would be best. Maybe they could all have their own logbooks but not stamps that way they could write or take pictures of whatever they want to do in it.

We have both. When all 4 of us are boxing together, then we stamp in
with our family stamp (which is also the stamp we use when placing
boxes, since that is a family affair). We each have our individual
stamps as well, which we stamp in with when we aren't all there.

It is nice to have the family stamp, because it really shortens the
time spent stamping in. Plus it doesn't take up as much room in a
logbook.
