Spirit of Letterboxing

From: bindlestiff_boxer (bindlestiff_boxer@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-09-25 01:15:22 UTC
Just wanted to take a minute to thank Brian (Robin of Loxley) for
taking the time to do some maintance on one of my boxes. Since I
won't be near this box anytime soon, he not only gave the box some
much needed TLC, but will be mailing me the old logbook.

Earlier this summer, Grandvilla and Mobius Klien did a rescue on
another of my boxes that had been "evicted" from it's hiding place by
some kind of critters-that-chew. This one was quite close by so they
mailed everything back to me so I could do maintance and replace it.

These are true examples of the spirit of letterboxers helping one another.

Thanks also to anyone else who has ever performed these sorts of
letterboxing maintance and acts of kindness. I can't even imagine how
many times someone has done simple things like: wiped the condensation
out of one of my boxes, cleaned off a stamp that had gotten all
"gooked" up or replaced worn out baggies for me.

Enjoy!
---PJ
aka Bindle Babe

From: Howling Coyote (coyotespaw@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-09-26 10:52:40 UTC-07:00
I bought some stamp cleaner too at my local "Stamp Store", and they showed
me how to properly clean stamps so that they are not damaged: gently blot
the cleaner on to the stamp, let sit for 10 seconds, then blot off with a
paper towel. Repeat one more time if necessary.
I agree though that you still have to be really careful with that one kind
of white carving medium, it crumbles like a cupcake.

Howling Coyote
