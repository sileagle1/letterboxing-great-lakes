From: Carol Osada (cjosada@hotmail.com) | Date: 2007-07-24 11:03:40 UTC-06:00
YIKES!!! 150 missing CT boxes!!! I'm speechless!!! Do you think the Dept. of Homeland Security is on to us? LOL!

Some of the boxes I place are 'drive by' or require short hikes. I think there should be a wide range of letterboxes for beginners, people with limited mobility, as well as for the adventurous hikers who will hike miles and enjoy the journey even if the box is missing. So I assume some of the risk if a box is easy to find and ends up being short lived.

At times like this, I pull back and rethink the true nature of this hobby. It's sooooooo easy to blame others, but there IS a solution. This hobby was intended to be a challenge for adults. Children were welcome to come along, but I doubt young children or first timers would pursue this hobby if more of the letterboxes were more challenging.

I haven't read about this epidemic in CT, but I will. This is one reason I created a hand carved stamp and logbook called "In Memory of All Missing Letterboxes." It was available at local gatherings upon request to anyone who has ever lost a letterbox. Maybe CT folks would like to create a similar tribute to those lost boxes and the people who created them.

Beetle
