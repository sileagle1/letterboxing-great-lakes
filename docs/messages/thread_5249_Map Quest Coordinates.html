Map Quest Coordinates

From: p_skultety (pskultety@aol.com) | Date: 2008-08-14 05:44:43 UTC
I did some letterboxing this past weekend in the Galena and Dubuque
areas. When I was printing up my from clues AQ I saw that some of them
had a link to Mapquest. When you printed the map it gave you some GPS
coordinates. This is the first that I noticed this feature. I thought
that this was a great idea and that it would make it somewhat easier
and get you somewhere near the boxes you were looking for.
Unfortunently for the couple that I tried (they were in some smaller
towns that I was traveling near) the GPS coordinates we no where near
where the boxes were located. They put you in the right town but at
least in one case we were miles from the box. Does anyone know how this
feature comes up with the coordinates they give you?

From: Kathleen Brade (brade@comcast.net) | Date: 2008-08-14 08:31:07 UTC-04:00
Recently, when I placed a box at a local park, I had a similar problem
(although it was GPS coordinates for Google Maps). The link showed the
box 6 miles away (near a highway) rather than at the address I
provided. I "fixed" the problem by misspelling the name of the park and
including an address.

I think this is a bug in AQ; some locations don't map to the correct GPS
coordinates so Google Maps and MapQuest show the wrong location. I
think you can send email to AQ and they'll fix it.

From: baliddle (baliddle@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2008-08-14 09:21:58 UTC-04:00
The coordinates are based on the location that is entered in AQ. If
they just put a city or zip code, it'll center the coordinates in
that area. If the planter gives an exact address, the coordinates
should be at that exact address.

Here's some more
info:
