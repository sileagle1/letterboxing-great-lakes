From: duncan53211 (duncan53211@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-06-15 03:35:25 UTC
While working on the WI LBs of Antiquities pages, I've been given and
found some information I thought I'd better pass on before the pages
go "live".

We now know the reason for the sudden disappearance of the Wehr and
Boerner boxes (and possibly the Attack Goose box). Capt Jim, the
owner of the first two boxes, was contacted by the grounds keeper at
the park and was told to remove the boxes as the constituted an
unacceptable activity. Jim promptly removed the boxes and still has
them, but they are retired for good.

Curiosity got the better of me, and I did a search for the policy of
the Mil Cty Parks that this grounds keeper seemed to be trying to
enforce. I have found no policy for or against
geocaching/letterboxing on the Cty Parks land or in the state of WI.
I did dind out however, that the WI Geocachers Assoc. is trying to be
proactive (without our input) in establishing a policy with the DNR.
So far the DNR hasn't seemed to give them the time of day and doesn't
seem to care. Some of the policies the geocachers are proposing to
the DNR might concern us though, such as limits on numbers and
required forms with fees. I don't know why the geocachers are trying
to ruin something that isn't broken. Anyway, right now they seem
content to let it lie. In specific cases with state parks,
particularly Pike Lake and Harrington Beach, the rangers are actually
very supportive of geocaching, which is good news.

We might want to discuss this situation at some point.

Dragon
