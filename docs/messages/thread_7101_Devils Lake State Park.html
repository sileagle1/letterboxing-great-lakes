From: duncan53211 (duncan53211@yahoo.com) | Date: 2011-05-05 01:31:31 UTC
If you are spending the day there, I would suggest hiking around the whole lake along the upper cliff trails and grabbing all the boxes along that circle. Start with the "vertical climb" on the SE side while everyone is fresh, and take plenty of snacks and fluids. Take your time and enjoy the views, etc. The hike will be good training for the 16 yo CC runner...:-)

From: sunnysideupm (sunnysideupm@yahoo.com) | Date: 2011-05-11 02:38:01 UTC
We made it all the way around! Can hardly believe that was only 3.5 miles - with the elevation changes it seemed much farther. Alas, the concessions weren't open yet for the season. Might have to go back for that malt. DS said we should go back so DH and I can go LBing and he and his sister can go kayaking/canoeing. LOL. We found 2 of the four LBs we sought. At the park entrance I did pick up postcards and a DLSP Centennial MUG!

The next day I went out closer to home determined to get some mystery boxes - I got them (4) and 14 more! It was neat to get my first TattooBob and Kimberly boxes; see the evolution of Lock Shock and Barrel's carving; follow nearly the same route Dragon did a few weeks ago; be the FF in five years for three boxes; and go through a labyrinth for F1000! :)
