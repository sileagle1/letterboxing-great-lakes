Introduction

From: Angela (missangi1210@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-04-05 07:08:59 UTC
Hello everyone! My name is Angi and I just started letterboxing this
week after hearing about it for the first time...last week. I found a
link to the letterboxing.org site while surfing around on the
internet and became very interested in it. When I got the chance I
went out and bought a stamp kit and made my first stamp. It ended up
being pretty big. It just barely fits on the tiny logbook page as it
is about 2.5 x 4 inches. Oops. I am probably going to be working on a
smaller one sometime soon.

When I found out that there was a box not very far from my house, I
decided to start this weekend. I was originally going to start once
school got out because all the other boxes would be like a day trip
for me. Anywho...my first box was found today (or yesterday as it is
2:00 in the AM now) and was the Red Rover box (Midway Village) in
Rockford, IL. I didn't really pay much attention to the stamps in the
logbook, but am I right in thinking there was one from Leapin
Lizards? It was fun looking through the stamps.

So I have all this fun looking for my first box...and now I have to
wait a month to go again. ::cries:: I can't wait for this semester to
be over with.

I hope I am making sense...it is way past my bedtime...lol.

Introduction

From: Barefoot Lucy (barefootlucy@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-06-05 21:10:35 UTC-05:00
Hey Y'all!

I thought I would poke my head up long enough to let y'all know I couldn't
stand it anymore - I had to see what this group is all about. Several folks
I know are already on this list, and since I like them so well, I figure it
has to be a good place to hang out.

Anyway, I'm from Texas and have been boxing down here for almost four
years. I also co-moderate the Texas list with Silver Eagle. I normally box
with my 11 year old son, the very cool Lightning, and sometimes we take my
husband/his dad, Fearless Fox, along if he's very good. I have a 19 year
old daughter who is a waylaid boxer and a carver extraordinaire. She's busy
at the moment being 19 years old and on her own, so she doesn't find her way
out to box very much unless she's hanging out with her old mom and her
little bro.

Aside from letterboxing, my other passion is scouting. I'm the Cubmaster
for one of the local packs and love it - and it flows very well with my
letterboxing activity as I usually manage to slip out on campouts and hikes
for a little boxing wherever we go! Lightning, unfortunately, is growing
older and he just crossed into Boy Scouts and he and Fearless Fox left me
alone to play with the little guys.

I was actually born in Wisconsin to temporarily displaced Texans and I spent
my first three years there until they moved us back to Texas. I did another
brief stint in Indiana when I was about five, so your neck of the woods
isn't entirely foreign to me!

I've been jealously reading about your gathering this weekend and it sounds
like a good time was had by all. I might eventually actually make my way
north to a gathering, but in the meantime, here I sit. Thanks for letting
me eavesdrop!

--
Barefoot Lucy
"It's not about footwear, it's about philosophy"

From: Jay (jayvz@tell-my-mom.com) | Date: 2009-06-23 16:53:53 UTC
After an e-mail conversation with Wisconsin Hiker, it was suggested that I send a note introducing myself to the group. I have been lurking here for a few months, learning, but now should at least introduce myself to the group.

I am new to letterboxing as of April of this year and together with my children, have fallen in love with the hobby. We've been out letterboxing at nearly every opportunity and have found many of the hidden gems within our community. We also plan to do some trips with the kids this summer to find the gems of other areas as well. My children like going on the "treasure" hunt and have told and invited many friends to join us. We've even set up a series and taken my daughter's fourth grade class on a letterboxing quest their last day of school (unfortunately it rained and we had to move it indoors).

I have been a stay at home dad for 6 years with my children age 6, 10, 16 and 27. My two oldest children are do not join us on our quests, but the two younger love to figure out the clues and find the boxes. My 10 year old daughter has also become a fan of the wonderful carvings out there. She has also tried to carve her own signature stamp.

In addition to the Stay at Home Dad career, I am a part time paramedic (working nights only) and teach a number of parenting programs for hospitals around the area. I have been a girls scout leader for 7 years, and do at risk mentoring and literacy counseling at school. My wife and I have also done Foster care for pre-adoptive newborns for the last 4 years, but have recently retired to focus more on our children's adventurous needs.

I have become quite fascinated with carving my own stamps and placing letterboxes. I have been working hard to make the plants enjoyable for everyone. If anyone has any suggestions, I'd really like to learn. Most of my plants at this time are pretty short trips, because I need to plant them without too much complaining from the kids that "I'm tired." While they are short, they are great for boxers with kids.

You will find that I'm a bit of a history buff and have done extensive genealogy research. Many of the boxes that I hope to create have some sort of a history lesson to help the children put history into understandable terms.

I look forward to meeting many of you on the trails and am hoping to attend some of the gatherings with my children. If you are coming to the Green Bay area, please contact me. Time allowing, I'd love to go letterboxing with others and have my children learn from their adventures.

Thank you to all of you for providing the opportunities to explore with my children.

Jay
AtHomeDad with Bidder Bear & Smiley Riley
Suamico, WI

We also plan to do some trips with the kids this summer to find the gems of other areas as well. My children like going on the "treasure" hunt and have told and invited many friends to join us. We've even set up a series and taken my daughter's fourth grade class on a letterboxing quest their last day of school (unfortunately it rained and we had to move it indoors).

I have been a stay at home dad for 6 years with my children age 6, 10, 16 and 27. My two oldest children are do not join us on our quests, but the two younger love to figure out the clues and find the boxes. My 10 year old daughter has also become a fan of the wonderful carvings out there. She has also tried to carve her own signature stamp.

In addition to the Stay at Home Dad career, I am a part time paramedic (working nights only) and teach a number of parenting programs for hospitals around the area. I have been a girls scout leader for 7 years, and do at risk mentoring and literacy counseling at school. My wife and I have also done Foster care for pre-adoptive newborns for the last 4 years, but have recently retired to focus more on our children's adventurous needs.

I have become quite fascinated with carving my own stamps and placing letterboxes. I have been working hard to make the plants enjoyable for everyone. If anyone has any suggestions, I'd really like to learn. Most of my plants at this time are pretty short trips, because I need to plant them without too much complaining from the kids that "I'm tired." While they are short, they are great for boxers with kids.

You will find that I'm a bit of a history buff and have done extensive genealogy research. Many of the boxes that I hope to create have some sort of a history lesson to help the children put history into understandable terms.

I look forward to meeting many of you on the trails and am hoping to attend some of the gatherings with my children. If you are coming to the Green Bay area, please contact me. Time allowing, I'd love to go letterboxing with others and have my children learn from their adventures.

Thank you to all of you for providing the opportunities to explore with my children.
