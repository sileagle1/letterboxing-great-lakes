Ooooh Wick's Sugar Cream pie! Even my crapster IGA-ish
(it's IGA-ish because it's small like an IGA, but not
even cool enough to be an IGA) grocery has them in the
freezer...AND THEY ARE YUMMY. By sight, they are
foul...a greyish custard on crust...but it's heaven in
the mouth.

I think another Indiana thing is the breaded pork
tenderloin sandwich (which is usually just called a
tenderloin). It's basically a pork fritter, breaded,
fried and slapped on a bun with pickles, cabbage, and
some electric yellow mustard-like sauce called "Wally
Sauce". I'm not a fan...but it seems that people eat a
lot of them around here. Every dumpster diner has them
prominantly on the menu.

Older Hoosiers refer to lunch as dinner and the last
meal of the day as supper. They also refer to US
highways that became four lanes 30 years ago (such as
US 30 and US 31) as "the bypass" even today.

Isn't it amazing that in a 5-7 state area there is
such variation in food, vocab, traditions?

--Armadillo Jo, who stays off the bypass and takes
"Old 30" to work every day.
