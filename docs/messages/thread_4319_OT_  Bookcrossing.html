The problem with finding books is that they are meant to be picked
up, so unless you get out to find one right away, it may get picked
up before you get to it. One of the taglines talks about making the
whole world a library!
The website is at bookcrossing.com - membership is free and they are
good about privacy issues. Any messages are totally blind, so your
email is never exposed to anyone. If you join you get sign up to get
email alerts for any books that are released into the wild for the
towns that you chose from your local area. Or there is a Go Hunting
link on the sidebar that lets you see where books have been
released. There is also something called an OBCZ - Official Book
Crossing Zone. These are usually places like coffee shops that have
a bookcrossing shelf set up, those are usually kept stocked with a
variety of books.
http://homepage.mac.com/pachydomo/OBCZWorldMap.htm
This is the link to the world maps of OBCZs, you can click into to
your area to see if there are any locally. Other than that, just
keep an eye out for books roaming free in the wild. Usually they'll
have a post-it or some note on them saying that they are a
bookcrossing book. I've had books from Milwaukee go to Iraq, New
Zealand, Sweden, England amongst other places. It's my other big
obsession besides letterboxing!

From: xxxxxxxx (BrighidFarm@comcast.net) | Date: 2007-08-01 23:30:33 UTC-05:00
Having a book "stolen" is always a risk. I put everything in/with the book
for the bookcrossing site, and I have maybe a 25% sucess rate. I've
released 40 books "into the wild" so far. 10 of them were later logged in at
the site and 30 have never been heard from again. So I don't do it all that
often anymore.

It would be nice if people who didn't want to play the game would just leave
the books for someone who does want to, but lots of folks just see the
availability of a "free" book.

And I suppose folks also mean to get around to reading
them...............someday..........and until they do, the book just sits on
a shelf.........someplace....and sits....and sits.

Personally, I prefer it if folks who went around actively seeking the books
just did one at a time and didn't pick up another one until they read and
released the previous one into the wild again, but I know some folks who
actively go looking for them who have found 4 or 5 in one week and then the
books just sit until they get around to them.

For places like Starbucks or other shops, if you look towards the bottom of
the listing, it'll often tell ya where in the store you can find the book.
I like to leave them at interstate waysides to see how far away they might
end up.

From: mkeaims (aimlesst@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2007-08-02 12:06:47 UTC
I usually don't ask anyone to hold a book unless there is an OBCZ
there. I have heard from others that sometimes workers pick a book up
and toss it in the lost and found, so it never hurts to ask. If you
have a relationship with them since you already have a box there, I
would probably just ask them to leave it out where it is visible.
My catch rate - rate of books released that ever get logged in on the
web site - runs between 10-20%. I'm a lurker and will loiter hiding
behind my newspaper to see what happens to my book whenever I can. I
know that most of my books get picked up. I've even had books that I
got entries on over a year after I released it. I think you just need
to be willing to let the book go once you release it.
Good luck!
aimlesst
