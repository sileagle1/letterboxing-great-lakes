Random drivel of the day:
I'm finding that i'm enjoying planting more and more.
My stamp carving ability is improving with each and
every stamp. I find myself keeping an eye out for box
materials every time I go shopping. I've been debating
on which is the better carving medium: the pink stuff
or the Staedlater carving block. I've used both, and
both have their merits.
Last weekend I bought the wrong size containers for
the Kokiwanee series...so in my head i'm thinking,
"These boxes MUST be used!" I'm planning on planting a
few boxes during my Ohio trip next week...one in
Lorain County (box finished, just need to find a good
spot where finders and myself won't get shot) and
probably one in Ashtabula County near the Pymatuning
Reservoir.

I finally got a chance to read the Time article. My
co-worker brought in a pile of magazines for the lobby
and the issue with the article was in it. I thought it
was a little cheesy. In my opinion, it did not capture
the spirit of this activity AT ALL. It made it seem
so...surburban...which it is NOT. The article really
didn't capture the artistic angle as well as it could
have. No WONDER everyone was concerned.

Oh, and I deleted a letter from my trail name...so now
i'm just Armadillo Jo, cause it just goes with the
flow....! When I was planting boxes at my old Girl
Scout camp this weekend, I remembered my trail name
for camp...Scooby. Scooby. The most un-original trail
name ever! Only given because I happened to have a
Scooby-doo hat on during orientation. My best friend
was Pebbles because she had red hair. I guess there
were some cartoon junkies at our camp that year!
Armadillo Jo is just more ME! What are some of your
previous trail names?

Armadillo Jo
