From: sl-pennypenny (PennyPenny@cablespeed.com) | Date: 2009-12-01 19:28:20 UTC
OK, another topic -- Image transfer!

I used to do the iron transfer with fantastic results, but in the past few years it has been really, really hard to get the image to transfer. What I understand is that the newer inks used today in most printers have "bonding" qualities that don't allow them to transfer to the rubber like they used to.

Acetone transfer isn't working either -- I suspect for the same reasons.

I prefer to create my images using photoshop and so I really do need to transfer my image rather than trace or draw it out.

Does anyone have any sure-fire method of doing a clean, dark, and smudge-proof transfer? What carving material do you use with your method (as I'm sure that matters as well.)?

Thanks in advance for any hints and tips you can share!

PennyPenny

Carving Media

Ditto to A118's comments. We had the same progression - started with pink &
MasterCarve w/gouges and really liked MC. Moved to blades and really
preferred white Grace B PZ-Kut. (Luckily I still have a good supply -
bought a bunch JUST before it disappeared!). Never bought orange since it
didn't seem like we'd be able to see the image transfer as well while
carving.



Image transfer

I never had much luck w/iron transfer (tried twice) but have good luck with
acetone. As you said, you need to have the right toner, which is generally
found in older laser printers and older copy machines. Perhaps you can
print your image at home and take it somewhere that has an old copy machine
(at a school or library??). Luckily Martini Man's laser printer still has
the "good" toner, so I'm still getting good transfers.

RE: [LbGLK] Re: Iron transfer frutstration...

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2009-12-01 19:22:03 UTC-06:00
There are probably instructions on some website or another for acetone
transfer, but here's a simple description of what I do:



1. Print image from computer on laser printer.

2. Cut image out from paper.

3. Place paper w/image onto carving media with image facedown.

4. Put a small amount of acetone (very cheap at a drugstore) on a
small area of an old washcloth.

5. Firmly pat the washcloth w/acetone onto the back of the paper.
Don't rub, just repeatedly pat/push firmly down, rewetting with acetone as
needed.

6. Peel back a corner to see how well image is transferring. If good,
peel off. If not good, try more acetone and pressure.



You can do the same thing with an inkjet printer IF you take the printed
image to an older copy machine and make a copy. Then use the photocopy for
Steps 2-6. The key is that you need to have toner.
