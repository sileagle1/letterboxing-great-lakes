I've also wondered if there is any "certain type" of person that is drawn to
letterboxing. It definitely does seem to be of interest to teachers (at
least 4 of the Wisconsin crew). However it also seems to attract technical
people. The teachers I know of that LB in Wisconsin are not English
teachers, but math/science oriented: nursing, math, computer
science/electrical engineering. Others are not teachers, but have science
related jobs. Neither my husband nor I teach, but we do fall into the
technical area (my bachelor's was in Electrical Engineering and he works in
Information Technology). Note: One of the 4 is an Art teacher.

Since LB combines many different aspects, that may be why it appeals to a
wide range of people. Martini Man & I love puzzles and games, so the "clue
aspect" is a major draw for us. When I was a kid, I would play spy games
and in grade school we were always devising codes and writing secret
messages rolled inside fountain pens (yes, I'm old enough that we actually
did use fountain pens in Catholic grade school). When we first started
LBing last summer we laughed at ourselves for playing "secret agent man" in
our late 40s. However we had too much fun to let that stop us. Now we tell
people that LBing is "hiking with an intellectual aspect" to make our
playing sound a little more serious! However most people still think we're
nuts...

Wisconsin Hiker
