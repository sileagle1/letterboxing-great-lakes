From: Atom 118 (hersh118@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2007-07-26 14:42:04 UTC-05:00
I had emailed one of the guys from SPY this morning. He had found one
of my boxes last weekend, and he seemed very nice.

"Are you part of the Sunfield Area SPY's? Could you give me a little
information about your group and your letterboxing hunt? I ask because
your treasure hunt (and help line) has been brought to the attention of
the area's letterboxers. Although your email to me was very warm and
helpful, some of the rest of your group has been portrayed in a negative
light. Your group sounds like it does a large amount of work that
benefits the local community, and before I jump to any wrong
conclusions, I wanted to get some information from you (as you seem to
be a key player).
Thanks,
Atom "

Here is what he responded.

"We have somewhere around ten or so teams and there's a summer long
'treasure hunt' of boxing going on. We are all new to boxing this
summer. We have a tip line which helps people by warning them when
boxes are missing or when the landmarks have changed and they need to be
a little creative when searching for a box. I certainly hope no one
from our group has done something unethical boxing-wise or any
other-wise or has offended anyone in any way. I can relay info to the
guy who is running this contest, and we can correct it. Can I ask what
the problem is?"

Before I respond, do you have any key points I should hit? I was going
to reiterate the stealthy aspects, express our disdain for the help line
(as it is contrary to the sport), and our concerns of large groups. In
addition, I am most interested in the 'help-line' for reporting missing
boxes as many boxes have lasted for years and should not go missing so
often.

Atom

Leave no trace!
Don't create a trail to the box.
Re-hide the box and its contents as well or better than you found it.
Re-seal and double bag contents (if applicable).
Don't handle the box if you have food smell on your hands.
Make sure no one is watching you.
Help-line okay for reporting problems with boxes (flooded area, park
closed, box possibly missing) - heck we report those things here - but
not okay to give clues, locations, or info to find boxes (spoilers)

In addition to what Dragon suggests, you may want to suggest that they reiterate some of the basic rules of letter boxing (especially leave no trace/respect the environment, be stealthy when retrieving and rehiding boxes, hide boxes as good or better than you found them) to their teams. It is apparent from the number of "new team" announcements that not all of the people playing this game attended the orientation meeting held this spring, perhaps they did not get quite the same message as those who attended the meeting.

I would also be sure to point out the general concern over the hint line, since that really does seem to be the biggest concern. While it's ok for things like noting poor trail conditions, potentially missing boxes, and things like that letterboxers should still be encouraged to try contacting the placer first. You should point out that it is generally against letterboxing ethics to offer hints on other people's clues as well as mentioning mystery boxes without the placer's permission. I don't mind giving hints for my own boxes if someone emails me and tells me they're having a hard time finding them, but I really wouldn't want people to be able to call a "hint hotline" to get hints to the clues. Some clues are deliberately tricky and it may be the placer's intention and desire for them to be worked out without the assistance of others.

Kimono Girl

Leave no trace!
Don't create a trail to the box.
Re-hide the box and its contents as well or better than you found it.
Re-seal and double bag contents (if applicable).
Don't handle the box if you have food smell on your hands.
Make sure no one is watching you.
Help-line okay for reporting problems with boxes (flooded area, park
closed, box possibly missing) - heck we report those things here - but
not okay to give clues, locations, or info to find boxes (spoilers)
