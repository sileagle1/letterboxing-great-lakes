From: Barb Funk (mmfunk99@greenapple.com) | Date: 2007-06-23 11:00:32 UTC-04:00
A very wise letterboxer from the Cincy area shared with me that the
Metro Parks in Ohio are willing to allow letterboxes but only with their
specific approval and then they must also be checked and monitored by
the placer on a very regular basis. Safari Man found out some specifics
about this as well. He and I have both had boxes pulled by the Metro
Park folks as well, but the letterboxer in the Cincy area explained that
it is because of the increased problems they are experiencing with drug
dealers using the Metro Parks as drop-offs and exchanges. :( They
also explained that putting an innocent letterbox out by someone can
eventually slip into becoming a drug drop off later.

Once I heard this, I was a whole lot more understanding. Just the same
it's sad. I guess this is why I always feel like letterboxing is
operating on "borrowed time" and try to appreciate each box and
experience while I/we can.

Trailtracker
