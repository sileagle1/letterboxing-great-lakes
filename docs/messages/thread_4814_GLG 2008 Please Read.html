From: Gooble Gobble (gooble_gobble_3@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-02-29 23:34:50 UTC-08:00
Hello everyone! The nice weather we've been having
lately and the late nights of carving have me VERY
excited for this years GLG. I've noticed that there
hasn't been alot of registrations for the event thus
far. That got me thinking about why. Obviously some
won't be able to make it due to prior commitments,
etc. I also thought that maybe some people weren't as
excited about the theme this year as they were about
others in the past, maybe I'm wrong. I know that if
the event was in (insert state here) and it was about
all things related to that state I might have a hard
time coming up with ideas for stamps and/or getting
excited about it. This is just my opinion, yours might
differ. If however, you need ideas for stamps and/or
need a little something to spark you're interest
please take a look at the huge list of ideas that
follow. I tried to think outside the box and find neat
things that were related to MN. Please give them a
look and see if anything sparks your interest.

On a quick side note before the list, Red Cat, M.C.
Bear and myself got together a few weeks ago to talk
about the gathering and brainstorm on what we could to
make things fun, new, and exciting. I wish I could
divulge some of the things here right now, but I
cannot, some things are better left as a surprise. But
I will say this, there are several things I have never
seen or heard of at a gathering and I hope/know you
will all find them fun and different.

I hope to see a great number of you in May. I look
forward to seeing old friends I've only net once, and
many new ones. I hope this list isn't too long for
you. Enjoy!

If you need more info on any of the things on the list
just Google them with Minnesota in the search, I'm
sure you'll find them.


We'll start with Places:

Split Rock Lighthouse
Fort Snelling
State Capital
Cathedral Of St. Paul
St. Paul Winter Carnival (Ice Palaces, etc)
Weisman Art Gallery
Mall Of America
MN. State Fair
Valleyfair


People: (Born In MN.)

Bob Dylan
Judy Garland (Can you say "Wizard of Oz") plenty of
ideas there
Charles Lindbergh
Paul Bunyan and Babe The Blue Ox
Jesse Ventura
F. Scott Fitzgerald
Ann Bancroft (Polar Explorer)
Sinclair Lewis
Jeanette Picard (First woman to pilot a hot air
balloon into the stratosphere)
Prince
Winona Ryder (BeetleJuice, BeetleJuice, BeetleJuice)
Will Steeger
Laura Ingalls Wilder
Dred Scott
James J. Hill

Northfield on Sep. 7, 1876)
Andrew Volstead (Mn congressman who Prohibition was
named after "The Volstead Act")
Charles M. Schultz (Alot of ammo there)


Things, etc:

The Kensington Runestone
One of Minnesotas first nicknames was "The Bread and
Butter State" other nicknames are/were "The Beaver
State", "The Gopher State", "Land of 10,000 Lakes",
""The North Star State"
In-line skates were developed in MN.
Water skiing was invented in MN. on Lake Pepin in 1922
During the 30's MN. was a hideout for Gangsters
including John Dillinger, Baby Face Nelson, and Ma
Barker to name a few.
The Gopher is the official mascot of the University Of
MN.
Holidazzle Parade
Jeffers Petroglyphs
Minneapolis or Saint Paul Skyline
When the civil war broke out MN. troops were the first
to volunteer to fight for the Union
In 1881 Pillsbury opened the world's largest flour
mill
Viking Ship A Heritage Hjemkomst Interpretive Center
in Moorhead
Greyhound Bus lines started in MN.
MN. Renaissance Festival
International Eelpout festival in Walker
Rocky and Bullwinkle say their hometown is Frostbite
Falls which is based on International Falls
26 foot tall statue of Smokey the Bear in
International Falls
U.S. Hockey Hall of Fame in Eveleth
Real Danish Windmill in Moorheads Viking Ship Park
Tonka Trucks are made in MN.
Huge iron ore freighters that dock in the Harbors of
Duluth
Snowmobiles were invented in MN.
For four years starting in 1873 millions of
grasshoppers invaded Mn fields eating everything in
sight including clothes hung out to dry
General Mills based in MN.
Land O' Lakes dairy based in MN.
MN produces more turkeys than any other state
Nordicware headquarters
The Bundt pan was invented in MN.
MN leads the nation in the production of Sugar Beets
Vikings (see Kensington Runestone)


This is by no means a complete list of everything that
MN has to offer, but as you can see there are ALOT of
great ideas just waiting to be discovered.

Thank you for your time, see you all in 91 days. Think
Spring.


Gooble Gobble

Re: [LbGLK] GLG 2008 Please Read

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2008-03-01 13:13:51 UTC-05:00
Thanks for the list of ideas! I'm sure it will be very helpful and was very thoughtful of you for those who are not so familiar with MN.

As for the gathering, I would have to agree with Kimono Girl, distance is likely keeping some people away or at least from committing early. It would be a similar situation if we were to hold a gathering in the MI U.P. or the eastern edge of Ohio, etc. One thing to consider, and you guys may have already thought of this, but just because it is the Great Lakes Gathering, does not limit it to Great Lakes area folks. Since MN is on the edge of other regions, perhaps there are boxers in IA or even the Dakotas who may be interested in coming. I haven't kept up with other areas enough to know how regular gatherings in those areas are, but I know there were a lot of G.L. folks who attended a gathering in IA last year.

As for me, I would love to come, but as with last year, the job I am currently in requires weekends beginning in late May through the summer. The pay-off if well worth it to me, but unfortunately it keeps me from a lot of other fun weekend activities.

On a positive note, a smaller gathering is not necessarily a bad thing. The first couple of years, the G.L. gathering was around 50 people, give or take a few. There were plenty of awesome boxes and we all had a great time. Because there were fewer people and boxes, there was actually time to chat with everyone and find all of the boxes. May not win an award for the most awesome *mega gathering* but there is nothing wrong with that. It comes down to the place and the people who are there.

SpringChick
