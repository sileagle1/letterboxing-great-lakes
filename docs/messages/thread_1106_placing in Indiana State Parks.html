From: klustofin (katy@dsa132.org) | Date: 2005-03-28 20:14:19 UTC

I know that there are a few people on this list from Indiana and I'm
hoping that they can give me some state-specific advice. I want to
plant a series at Shades State Park. I went to the IN DNR's website
and read their policy on geocaching and also reviewed their permit for
geocaching. I couldn't find anything about letterboxing and those two
documents are much too specific to be applied to letterboxing (for
starters, I have no way of providing the DNR with exact coordinates
and I don't plan to post it on the geocaching site). So, those of you
with boxes in Indiana State Parks: did you ask permission, and if so,
of whom? Have you had any boxes pulled by park managers or other DNR
employees?

Thanks!

Katy

Interesting question. Since I found my first boxes in IN State Parks, I didn't question that it

very far off the trail. It might still be an under-the-radar activity in this state. Wonder
how long that will last?

I'm not from Indiana, but I agree with what Armadillo
Jo & speedsquare already wrote about planting boxes in
general. A large chunk of letterboxes hidden around
Cleveland are hidden in our area Metroparks, and when
Deedle & I were getting ready to place our first plant
(which was slated for a Metroparks park) I went to
their main website to see if they had a policy. Same
as you wrote - they had a geocaching policy, and a
form to fill out if you were placing a geocache, but
also too specific to be applied to letterboxing. And
since there were already so many boxes planted, we
didn't want to jeopardize those boxes by bringing
letterboxing to the attention of the powers that be.
We figured as long as we're not planting them in areas
that will harm the area, it was better to keep it
under the radar as long as possible.
