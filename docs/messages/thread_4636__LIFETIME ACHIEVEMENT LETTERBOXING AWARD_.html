"LIFETIME ACHIEVEMENT LETTERBOXING AWARD"

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2007-12-27 19:47:58 UTC
Many of you have probably heard of Pete & Wanda, boxers in Rhode
Island who are great hikers and who have found a kazillion boxes.

In 2005 they decided to publicly recognize those letterboxers that
they felt had made a significant contribution to letterboxing, and to
do this on an annual basis.

We were happy to see that "The Dragon" of Wisconsin was one of those
that received the award this year. He certainly was instrumental in
getting us fired up about the hobby, since he planted so many
creative boxes in this area. Congratulations Dragon!

If you'd like to read more about Pete & Wanda's award and who has
been recognized in 2005, 2006, and 2007, just click on this link:

http://alum.wpi.edu/~p_miner/LLAA.html

We hope you all are still enjoying the holidays and that you get a
chance to get out to do some boxing (or stay in to do some carving)
as this year ends and the next one starts!

Wisconsin Hiker

P.S. Only 5 more days until our first BINGO word...

Re: "LIFETIME ACHIEVEMENT LETTERBOXING AWARD"

From: p_skultety (pskultety@aol.com) | Date: 2007-12-30 18:46:57 UTC
Congratulations Dragon!

Thank you so much for being out there and hiding all of those little
boxes for the rest of us. As a newbie to boxing I really feel honored
now to have found several of you Door County Boxes and your Adoptees
up there.

I just started letterboxing this past September on our annual trip to
Door County and your boxes accounted for over half of the boxes that
we found while we were up there. Your Sherwood Point Box was our
first attempted box. Unfortunately due to the trickiness' of the
clues at least for a beginner (getting dj vu, finding the correct
trail sign, and sighting the reverse azimuth of B) it ended up being
our second find. Our first box was the Door County Tradition by
Wisconsin Hiker. (I know that he will be proud after reading this).
His was on the same trail as yours and was an easier find. But we
didn't give up. So yours are officially our 2nd, 3rd, 4th boxes that
one being The Cave Point Box which I have since read is one of the
oldest box in WI which really makes that one special. Then you were
our 7th box the Mink River Box, which by far was the most challenging
of my finds and therefore another notable one. Last but not least you
get credit for our 10th, 11th and finally our 12th the Sturgeon Bay
Shipping Bay Canal Box that we just had to grab on our way out of
town. I just couldn't get enough of that stamping stuff.

So please keep on hiding them and we'll all keep on looking for them.

Congratulations again on your achievement.

Northstarr and Twinkles
