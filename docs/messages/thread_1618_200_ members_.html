From: Angi (ShutterflyIL@aol.com) | Date: 2005-07-20 22:58:33 UTC
It is so good to see you on the list! You would be amazed at how easy
stamp carving is for the non-artistic type. I use the computer to get
my images, either from clip art or just altering regular images (like
combining a camera and butterfly for my stamp). I guess actually
creating the image and getting it on to the stamp material is the
hardest part. Once it is on there, all you do is take away what you
don't want. Don't rush and if there are parts that might be harder to
carve, start with those first.

Good luck! If you need any help, I'm sure you can find one of us from
the area to help you out!

Shutterfly

carving? Any tips?


Here's a good spot to find links to lots of carving related sites:
http://www.letterboxing.info/links/?id=77

Everyone has their individual preferences, but you might want to start with
a Speedball Stamp Carving kit (available at Michael's Craft stores & don't
forget the 40% off coupon in Sunday paper if you have them in your area).
That will give you some tools (#1 blade seems to be most popular) and some
carving media. If you want to start out really basic you can use a rubber
eraser and an x-acto blade. (I now prefer the x-acto blade.)

You can get your design images from anything - draw something, use clipart,
copy/modify a neat graphic from some other print media, etc.

I've personally had the best luck transferring an image by making a
photocopy of my drawing/image, then cutting around the image on the copy
paper, placing it upside down on the carving media and using a cotton ball
soaked in nail polish remover to "pat" the paper. Doing this transfers the
toner from the paper to the carving rubber. To use this method you must
start with something that has toner (copy machine or laser printer, NOT an
inkjet printer),

Good luck!
