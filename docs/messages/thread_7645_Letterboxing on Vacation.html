Letterboxing on Vacation

From: rhondapetsch (rhondapetsch@yahoo.com) | Date: 2013-02-10 18:33:05 UTC
How do you organize your clues and plan on which boxes to find? I was so excited to be going to New Orleans a couple of years ago that I printed all of them. I then put them in a 3-ring binder and spent months reading & decoding them. When the big day arrived I was unprepared for how large the city was. I was so focused on one box, (it was the hardest clue to solve and I never found), that I spent the day with my face in my binder and wearing out my family. I ended the day with one box and my family upset and worn out from walking from one district to the other.
I am now planning a trip to Hawaii. I will be going to Waikiki on Oahu and Kona on the big island. My family has already let me know this trip is not about letterboxing. I wish there was an app so that whenever I was close to a box I could go after it. I don't want a repeat of my last vacation. What do you do?

From: rei5kids (5reikids@gmail.com) | Date: 2013-02-10 19:14:03 UTC
I use the Box Radar app on my phone, and do my research before hand. I've boxed on 5 different vacations we've been on...3 different locations in Florida, our yearly migration to Missouri & back and once in Wisconsin, and I'm already researching for my trip to NC at the end of March. March I'm going to be in a time crunch so I'm focusing in on two boxes...I've already solved the clues, confirmed that I solved the clues correctly with the box owner who was gracious enough to confirm that I solved it right and if we have time while we are in town (we'll only be there 2 days for a 5K and Half Marathon I'm running) then we'll grab them...if not, then no skin off my teeth. Will I be bummed that I didn't get a chance, yes, but still it's not the end of the world.

For me personally, I only look for boxes within a 5mi radius of our hotel...sometimes even closer depending on how my husband's mood seems. I also usually only go for the "grab n go" boxes since I'll usually have at least 6 people waiting for me in the car and they don't wait patiently for very long. I do print off lots of clues...but sometimes things just don't work out. For example, on our way home from Florida in December I was driving when I realized I was about 10 miles past a rest area that *THAT* was the rest area that a box was at that I wanted to stop for. Ooops. My bad. Oh well. I wasn't expecting to be driving that stretch of highway...I figured my husband would be and I could navigate him to stop there. Turned out that I made up for it when we decided to stop at the Louisville Slugger Museum and there happened to be a box there...stopping there hadn't originally been in our plan but we figured it would be neat, fun for the kids, and a good chance to get out of the car for a few hours.

~Mel (5reikids)

From: louannewinkle (louanne.winkle@gmail.com) | Date: 2013-02-10 20:13:47 UTC
Hi there,

If I can, I do prepare ahead of time when planning to do letterboxing while vacationing, even down to the point of mapping out all the boxes ahead of time so I know which order to do them in. However, I've found 2 other things to be helpful:

1. If you have a Smartphone, download the Box Radar app. I've used it several times and it's been great for letterboxing. There is also another letterboxing app that I've seen others write about, but I haven't used it and can't recall the name of it right now.

2. I have posted on the state board on AtlasQuest for wherever I'm going. I ask for boxing suggestions or specific questions about the area. If there are active letterboxers in the state, I have found them to be very helpful.

3. The third thing I do is "make a deal" with my husband or family members about the amount of time allotted for letterboxing. I think it's fair to just establish that "Tuesday morning is all about letterboxing." Then we're all clear on the amount of time and everyone seems to have a better outlook on it. I know my husband enjoys letterboxing if he's planning on doing it.

Enjoy Hawaii! It was one of my best vacations, although I went there before I was a letterboxer.

Angel Winks

From: Sara Byron (sarabyron@att.net) | Date: 2013-02-10 15:06:02 UTC-06:00
I use Clue Tracker on my iPhone. I couldn't find Box Radar in my app store.
It's an android app. So use one of them.

When we box and travel, I also make deals with my family and try to be
FLEXIBLE. My homework includes identifying boxes that are located in places
that we want to visit anyway. For instance: My DH has a goal of visiting
the graves of every dead President. Therefore finding a cemetery that has a
dead President AND a letterbox is a win-win. i.e. Richmond Virginia has
cemeteries with many letterboxes, as well as US and Confederate Presidents.
Adam, Davis, Monroe, Tyler. Indianapolis (Harrison) etc.

Drive-by's are also appreciated. But you have to remember to be flexible.
While in Tulsa, we located good boxes, but the park was infested with ticks,
they were falling out of the trees on us. We had to give up on those.

In Massachusetts we found a HUGE box of stamps in memorial to children who
had passed away young. There were at least 20 stamps in this box. But the
mosquitoes were SO BAD, that we allowed the boys to go back to the car. My
DD and I had used repellant and were fine.

Stamps by the ocean are popular. Go for the easy finds until they get
sucked into the obsession.

From: Estella Naparella (estella_naparella@yahoo.com) | Date: 2013-02-10 20:00:37 UTC-08:00
When Skootching Dog and I go on vacay, we plan boxes into our trip, plotting ahead, printing out clues, putting them in a color-coded, alpha-numeric order with as precise location as I can muster on a map of the locality. Then I find the best deal on either a campground, condo or hotel in the area, as centrally located near as many boxes as I can.

We then plan out which boxes we will get on which days, according to when areas might be open for operations or not (some are closed on various days of the week), and a place to have lunch out, or dinner, if we're eating (in the car, in a hurry to get to the next box) on the road.

We'll often do research on various weird/cool/interesting things to see and do in the area, and will pre-carve a box ahead of time to plant while we're there.

We don't get out much.
Obviously, we need a life.
:)
