From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-05-15 05:33:20 UTC-04:00
There is nothing that says you can't use wood if you would prefer to use that for carving your stamps. I have never carved wood before so I don't know if it is difficult. I am also wondering as to how the wood carving would take ink. One of the first stamps we found was not made from rubber, rather a curious metallic material that looked a lot like the leading used in making stained glass windows. It wasn't actually a carving, rather the material had been applied maybe from a tube over a template sketch. It was a nice piece of work, but the ink didn't take very well.

I am not aware of any carving classes in West Michigan, but if you are haing specific problems, feel free to post problem/question and we will try to offer suggestions. You mentioned faces and I am supposing it is because of the detail. Are you using the carving tools or an Exacto knife? If you are not already, try using an Exacto knife -- I find this much easier to get precise, detailed cuts.

SpringChick
