>>When you guys are going to a town you aren't familiar with to go
letterboxing how do you prepare?

Our method is very similar to others mentioned. Mapquest and Google
usually help us reach locations that arent detailed on state maps or
are outside the scope of big city maps. Also, we have county maps
for taking long weekend trips closer to home.

For the June gathering, were using AAAs trip planner. It only
allows 21 stops per trip, so we broke the big trip into a few
sub-trips. We moved the stops around for the most effective order.
We'll print text directions, which use only a few pages. We wont
use the turn by turn maps, but well use the zoom to print maps for
each stop at one or two detail levels - depending on what we need for
each stop. Regularly congested areas and construction are noted in
the text directions and construction is also marked on the maps.

Our spreadsheet is very similar to Wisconsin Hikers. We add columns
for:
- location details (the name of a park, address, etc.), which we use
mostly as a quick reference while planning the route.
- status, which is often added or updated just prior to our trip with
the date found, attempted, etc. An attempt doesnt discourage us
since - often it encourages us to look since we have had reasonable
success with boxes that have been listed as attempted. We use this
column to decide whether to send an alls well report. We send
status reports on our finds when: maintenance is needed; the last
hunter logged an attempt; its been a while since someone else logged
a find (because we dont log ours); or theres a specific request for
status in the clue. That leaves some of the element of surprise when
the planter visits.
