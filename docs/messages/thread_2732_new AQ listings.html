From: xxxx (PonyExpressMail@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-07-03 22:32:08 UTC-05:00
Same here. I tried Oak Hill Cemetery about a week or two after it was
placed -- I know the place so well I figured it would be a piece of cake --
nope, couldn't find it. Got up to the cross part and then drew a blank.
We tried every conceivable definition of the word cross. Then we decided to
forget the "cross" part and search for any conceivable type of coffin. No
luck at all.

Speaking of Civil War graves, there's a great site in Milton township that's
an "abandoned" cemetery in the Wheaton area. Woods have grown up around it
and in the middle of it are a bunch of civil war graves plus quite an
elaborate family plot. Milton Township folks were in kind of a fight to get
the rights to the land to restore the cemetery. They did a ton of
absolutely beautiful work restoring a cemetery to the north of this one that
has the gravesite of a Revolutionary soldier, so I'd love to see them go to
work on the Civil War one.

But I don't know if I should draw any attention to the cemetery at all that
has the Civil War gravesites. It's been vandalised as it is -- by the
neighbors actually who don't want it restored because they feel it will hurt
their property values -- and might not need any more attention since right
now it's not really under anyone's "control" legally.

The woods are rather dense, but the light comes thru the trees at just the
right spots for me to get some great photos. I always go out there when I'm
in an Eeyore sort of mood. It's that kind of place.

Give a holler if anyone ever finds Oak Hill. I went back a couple of times
but finally gave up. I never did contact the placer because I figured there
had to be something I was missing. But the same placer planted the Windmill
box and that was a physical toughie -- that was physically much harder than
we expected it to be, with a lot longer distance than we expected. After
doing the Windmill box, I always thought that there might be something more
physically difficult that we were missing about the Oak Hill box. :-)

~~ Mosey ~~
