I have tons of plasticware and logbooks for making up letterboxes, even an
entire block of PZ Kut awaiting my leisure. I plan to put out a series of
boxes at a local nature preserve based on invasive plant species in Ohio;
the images are even printed out and sitting in my end table next to the
sofa, awaiting good lighting (which I got for Christmas) for carving. I was
beginning to plan a series based on the strange creatures of Roald Dahl, but
might just watch Willy Wonka again and contribute to PPP's exchange
instead. Or maybe both. And I told myself that I would design and carve a
team stamp for me and the dogs before the GL Gathering. The problem with
that is deciding on a team name; I have several ideas but everytime I think
I'm close to choosing one, I come up with more ideas. My current favorite
is to be team YUCK - for YUki, Claire, and Katy - spelled out in insects (I
am an entomologist, after all).

I also have some Rankin and Bass Christmas characters to be planted
somewhere. Sadly, I don't even remember how many of them I actually
carved! Snowmiser is in Illinois somewhere, because I couldn't even find my
own box when I went to pull him. I know for certain Heatmiser, Rudolph, and
Nestor are in the file cabinet, but I don't remember if I ever carved that
Bumble or not. Sad, isn't it?

If only letterboxing were a valid career instead of just a hobby.

Katy

PS One of my New Year's Resolutions is to find a box a week until I have all
the local boxes in my logbook. The joys of living somewhere new!
