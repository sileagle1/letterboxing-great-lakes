From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-09-06 16:39:22 UTC-04:00
To create/start a HH, you simply carve up a stamp and create a log book just
as you would a letterbox. The difference is that this "box" is not going to
have a permanent home or clues.

As you create your stamp and book, keep in mind that the smaller the
package, the easier it will be for people to move it along from one box to
another, so trying for a streamlined package is a good idea. Most HH are
either in a very small container (i.e. a 35mm film canister) or simply
contained inside a ziploc baggie, although using a larger container is not
breaking any rule -- just makes it more difficult for people to relocate to
another box.

You should identify the HH inside the logbook -- the name of the HH, your
trailname and contact info (e-mail address is fine) and the letterboxing.org
website URL. Also, you should clearly label the outside of the container or
the baggie that this is a HH and put the name of the HH. It is not a bad
idea to include a short paragraph about what a HH is and what to do with
them inside the log book in case the finder is a new letterboxer.

Once you have it all ready to go, take it to the box where you want to start
it out, stamp the HH stamp into the box log and the box stamp into the HH
log and leave it there. Your HH is now officially on its way!

For tracking purposes, you may want to post the release of your HH to the
letterbox travellers Yahoo! list :

http://groups.yahoo.com/group/letterbox_travellers/

and/or send the information to Pete and Wanda for their HH tracking page:

http://alum.wpi.edu/~p_miner/travelers.html

It is completely up to you if you want to do this or not. Some people send
them off and don't have any interest in knowing where they are, although
most people want to hear.

You might also check out Silent Doug's page about HH for additional
information:

http://www.letterboxing.info/articles/00000010.php

Hope this helps!

SpringChick

p.s. all of the above links can also be found in the links section of this
list.

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-05-03 19:51:34 UTC-04:00
There is a Yahoo list meant for announcing new HH and HH finds -- the Travellers list. The Pete and Wanda list tracks the travels of a HH -- Pete generally monitors the Travellers Yahoo list so if you post a find there, you don't usually have to e-mail him. But for a new HH, you should e-mail him to get it added to his page -- because some people prefer to not have their HH travels monitored, he doesn't automatically add the new ones when they are announced on the Travellers list.

As an alternative, you can list HH on AtlasQuest.

The Travelers Yahoo list:

http://groups.yahoo.com/group/letterbox_travellers/


SpringChick
