Feeling Discouraged

From: Steve & Christa (sbellock322@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-06-15 04:20:29 UTC
I am very sad to report that our first box, and my(Mr.)first carving
have gone missing! The "What a Scream" box is no more, and I am not
sure if I will recarve and replace. Frankly, with this box and
reports about "Vive Monmartre" we are both getting very discouraged
about placing boxes. On a more positive, and slightly weird, note I
recieved an email from a woman who works with the homeless in Elgin
the other day. One of the people she works with, a mexican man who
speaks english but has trouble with the nuances of the language,
stumbled upon the third box of our Sternenacht series. This is what
we consider the "holy grail" box of the series, as it was the
hardest to carve and also contains the logbook. He understood that
is was not trash (whew!!!), but also did not know what to do with
it, soooo he brought it to her. She is now very interested in
boxing and coincidentaly will be traveling to England next week!
Anyway even with this "good" news we are feeling a little snakebit.
Has anyone else gone through periods like this? In all we have lost
quite a few boxes, Box 2 of Sternenacht (twice), Box 1 and 2 of I
heart Haring, 1 box that won't be named, three easter eggs (no big
deal there), and more than likely box 2 of Vive Monmartre.
AAAAAAAAAAHHHHHHHH (pulling hair out!!!)

Anyway, just needed to vent

From: Angi (ShutterflyIL@aol.com) | Date: 2006-06-16 00:58:42 UTC
Yes, I have! **raises hand in the air and waves it like crazy** I like
to call my period of letterbox depression...2005. Yes, all of 2005
simply bummed me out. Most of my boxes were either: A) Taken by the
Winnebago FP people... B) Not rehidden well by certain people and then
found by animals/people or C) Lost by any other means. Though I did get
three of them back from the FP office, I was still really annoyed with
the concept of letterboxing. I honestly thought I wouldn't get back
into it again. But look what the gathering has done to me! I'm totally
loving the hobby again. :) Let me speak from experience...don't give
up! Think of all the fun people have had hunting for your boxes!
Recarve and replace! (I shouldn't talk...none of mine have been
replaced...lol) You will get back in your groove soon enough. :)

Shutterfly
