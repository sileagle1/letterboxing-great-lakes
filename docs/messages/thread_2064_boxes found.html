From: (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2005-12-02 11:11:25 UTC-06:00
Hi!

One way to check on activity on boxes is to look at the status logging
on LBNA (keep in mind that not everyone logs their attempts of finds,
but you can usually get some sense of activity based on those that have
logged).

Here's one way to do it for the specific question you asked:

Go to LBNA
Click on Search for Boxes
Click on the dropdown list for Placer and scroll to KazooDog
Click on Search Boxes button
You should now see a list of all the boxes this person placed
Click on the green flag to the right of the Nearby City for each box
and you should see a date, the name of a boxer and whether they found
the box or didn't (will show up as "attempted" if they were unable to
find it. (You'll sometimes see that even if a few people logged a box
as "attempted" it doesn't mean it is missing, because someone else has
logged it as "found" with a more recent date.)

Hope this helps!

Wisconsin Hiker

From: azobox (jparkerg@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-12-02 19:06:26 UTC
To others who have sought the KazooDogs boxes.....

PalePuppyPosse and I have been corresponding on this issue for quite
awhile. PPP has contact with the placer through another venue. Here is
what we've learned:
KazooDog is apparently not an active letterboxer, at least not
presently. She has boxes in greater Kalamazoo and at Fort Custer, near
Battle Creek.
There have been problems reported to me and others about some of the
clues, particularly at Fort Custer.
The one at Al Sabo Preserve in Kalamazoo was found (as reported on
this list) by JBBooks just this fall. The Fort Custer #2 box has an
incorrect clue (compass reading).
Since it is not my box, I am reluctant to "correct" the clue publicly.
However, the box HAS been found by a number of boxers (who noted their
frustration in the logbook).
Here's what I would suggest: if you're seeking KazooDog's box #2 at
Fort Custer, use your imagination as you use your compass.

Good luck,

azobox
