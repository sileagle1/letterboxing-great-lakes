And on our ride home...

From: Doodle & Deedle Bug (doodle_n_deedle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-06-20 08:54:40 UTC-07:00
Just thought I'd share a quick recap of the box
hunting we did on our ride back to Cleveland on
Sunday. After we found a few more boxes at Hudson
Mills, we grabbed lunch and went to Hidden Lake Garden
to find the Big Rooster. And per the Letterboxer's
Law, when we got to the parking lot, there was another
van there (they didn't look like fellow letterboxers,
though). We pulled out the camera and went over to
the tree to take some photos in that "really neat
looking tree" while we waited for them to leave.

Then we travelled to find the Lake Erie Creature.
Reading the clues on the way there, I saw the line
"Pass the turtle crossing sign. Pass the snake
crossing sign." I looked at Deedle. "Snake crossing?
Do they mean a Slippery When Wet sign?" Nope, they
meant a snake crossing sign, and I'm like, completely
paranoid of snakes, esp. reading warnings about the
Michigan Massasauga Rattlesnake all weekend long. And
that morning, in Hudson Mills, I saw not a
rattlesnake, but a garter snake or something like it
(thin body, black with a green or yellow stripe) when
I went to look where I heard rustling along the path
as we walked. I think I handled myself very well, I
didn't scream, I didn't jump into Deedle's arms and
make him carry me the rest of the way. But he did get
to retrive all the rest of the letterboxes that day.

But back to the Lake Erie Monster...looking over the
clues, it was a short walk, on a paved path, no reason
we should actually come across any snakes, right?
Until we got to the part where the box was actually
hidden, and we'd have to walk off the path. Of course
_I_ was not going. So we studied the best path to
take to the hiding place, and once the coast was
clear, my brave Deedle, going all Army-Comando style,
grabbed his walking stick in both hands, and ran to
the hiding spot, causing a great russle in the
foliage. He figured that if he made enough movement
and noise any snake would high tail it out of there.
He checked the spot, grabbed the box, and then ran
back to the path the same way. Oh, the things he'll
do for me! He also had a snake-less return trip when
he replaced the box to its hiding spot. And thus
ended our weekend as we headed back home to the
promise of Chinese for dinner for his efforts :)

Next weekend we're doing some letterboxing in the Port
Clinton area. I'm not entirely sure how many boxes we
found this weekend that will be added to our F count
(since we need to sort through which stamps were
exchanges, which were from the circus), but I think we
may just hit F 100 next weekend. We shall see...

Doodle Bug.
