I'm not actually a scrapbook person by nature but I am as far as
letterboxing goes. I use one, two, or as many as it takes, pages of a
scrapbook for each stamp.

However, I don't take the scrapbook out into the field with me. Never ever.
:-) One of my worst nightmares is having my car broken into and the only
thing taken is my letterboxing scrapbook. I also don't want it in the field
with me because it could get damaged by inclement weather, lost, etc. And
frankly, the pages are just too large to conveniently carry around. I have
a clipboard with cut pieces of cardstock that I carry in my backpack. I
stamp the cardstock, bring it home, then make the scrapbook page around the
stamp image on the cardstock.

I include a photo or two if I happened to have my camera with me. I include
any journal type notes. I also include any maps or driving directions just
in case I end up looking for another stamp that was placed after I'd already
been there the first time or if someone thinks the box is missing and since
I've been there already, they ask me to go and check it out for them -- I
can quick re-familiarize myself with the area just by opening the scrapbook.
Google earth has great aerial views too that I often print out and put in
the scrapbook.

With many stamps, there was nothing really of extra-special interest to the
hike or search itself, so it may only end up with one page in the scrapbook.
Other hikes I've been on, like our recent trip to Mattheissen State Park or
my rather intense notes on the history of the Portage Canal that's in this
area -- those stamps actually end up with several pages of my scrapbook. At
the very least, I include a copy of the clues (which includes the name of
the box), the cardstock with the stamp image on it, the date I found it, and
a map. At first I was putting all the hitchhikers on one separate page but
when that page got full, now I just put the HH stamp image on the same page
as the box I found it in.

Then I keep a little index on my computer and printed out listing each box's
page in the scrapbook so that I can find the page quickly if I need to.

I've seen other boxers, tho, who merely have a book or artist pad they carry
around loaded with stamp images -- no notes, no dates, no nothin' other than
the stamp images. But that's soooo not *my* style. :-)

From: Cynthia Duca (ducacj@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-09-17 12:11:47 UTC-07:00
Well I think you'll find as many different answers as you the number of people who answer this.

We always make 2 copies of the stamp. One goes into scrapbook pages doing exactly what you posted. It will have the clues, date found, stamp, journaling, pictures, etc. I rarely seem to keep it to 2 pages but that is my goal. :) The second copy goes into our 'logbook'. It is basically just a large spiral artist pad that holds 2 stamps (average size) per page. Above or below the stamped image I write the name of the box and the date found.

When it comes to exchanges when there is time I ask for 2 copies so I can scrapbook pages of the gatherings along with all the different stamps and still put a copy in our logbook. If there is not enough time than I just scrapbook the exchanged images. I'm considering photocopying the images for our logbook or just leaving them out of the logbook altogether.

Hope that helps. Remember to do what you want to do.

Oh one thing I've found since I don't stamp onto the clue pages is to write the box name and date off to the side of the image so if you're scrapbooking them weeks or months later it's a quick reference. Comes in handy when you have a great finding day and putting the image with the box name.

Cindi _ DUCA famy

No, I don't go to gatherings. I kinda have mixed feelings about gatherings
because I really really love the secretive nature of letterboxing. That's
part of the fun for me. I'm not really sure I want to know what everybody
else looks like, talks like, etc. But I've never really had to think about
it all that much because I work Saturdays anyway and it seems like all
gatherings are held on Saturdays. I don't know if I'd bring my scrapbooks.
I could just see me having to tote a scrapbook into the bathroom at a rest
stop when I stopped for a pit call because I'm so paranoid it might get
stolen out of the van. :-) But actually I probably would do just that if I
did ever go to a gathering.

My scrapbook pages are really too big for my scanner but the next time I
borrow my sister's digital camera, I can take a few photos and post them as
photos. I can either do some of the pages that don't necessarily include
the stamp image or I can cover the stamp image if I can't get hold of the
placer.

I have fun with it. When I get some spare money, I go over to Archiver's or
Creative Scrapbooking and get some little embellishments and stuff. Instead
of the usual white scrapbook pages, I'll often cover the white page with
"outdoor" themed paper or "seasonal" themed paper. Or I'll do a watercolor
background myself and use that as the backing page. When I think about it,
I probably often spend as much time (or more)on scrapbooking a find as I do
on the search itself. And my letterboxing scrapbooks are really quite
simple. Nothing fancy. Some folks who are into scrapbooking are really
INTO scrapbooking. And make me look very elementary. So it's really kinda
nutty, I know. I've been told that scrapbook folks ARE nutty. But I'm not
a "scrapbook folk"! I swear I'm not.
