A twist on problems with placing boxes

From: (duncan53211@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-04-07 01:10:49 UTC
As it seems the places we love to plant boxes (i.e. state parks, et
al) are becoming hostile to us, can someone think of a way we could
advertise our "service" to other places that may be "overjoyed" to
have our boxes (and people come visit for the boxes)? Is there
somewhere, somehow we could advertise/offer to hide a box at a
certain place so 1) we would have an "okay" place to hide a box and
2) help others out by bringing in more people?

Any thoughts?

Dragon


Re: A twist on problems with placing boxes

From: Angela (missangi1210@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-04-07 05:47:49 UTC
I might be running a temporary greenhouse all summer for the place I
work at. My first thought was, "wouldn't it be cool if I could put a
box in the greenhouse and meet everyone who comes by?" But then I
thought about it a bit and didn't think it would work out. Has
anyone ever put one in a store or anything like that? I know it
would be private property, but if the owners would approve, wouldn't
it increase business as well? Just a thought.

There are some at the Bristol Faire right? Maybe other places like
that would want to take part in it. Maybe there should be a way for
people to contact potential box placers. Like if there was an event
and they wanted to draw in some extra people, someone could put an
ad out for placers in the area.

Just random late-night thoughts.

Hi Duncan,
Here is a site that seems to be working from a different angle. They
don't really seem to be affiliated with any of the hobbies or
interest that they promote, but they seem to have some sort of
business or commercial interest. On the surface I suppose it's a
good enough thing. I personally ran far and fast when I found out
that no one who actually letter boxes is familiar with it in this
area....but it may be of use, I wonder what folks in other geographic
areas and/or hobbies have found with it...and the commercial places
that are listed as potential meet-up spots....just something to kick
around. I hesitate to become "commercially" involved with anything
that I really like. It's a little like doing what you love as a
career - there is a reason they call it work, and so it stands there
is a reason they call it selling out ;)

Thanks, it's good that your thinking with a creative mind about

From: John J Foerch (jjfoerch@earthlink.net) | Date: 2004-04-08 05:01:01 UTC
MiGO did just that for two of its events. Winter Social 2003 and
Winter Social 2004. We played a game called Poker Run, where the
object was to visit some caches to collect poker cards and make a
hand. Bonus cards were placed at local businesses (Mt. Pleasant in
2004, Owosso in 2004). After both events, the various shops involved
gave high praise to our group for being their customers.
