found 8 of our boxes. Unfortunately we also received 2 reports that
our "February Frolic" box could not be found. We won't be able to

knowledge NO ONE has found it since we planted it in February.

We were surprised to find a stamp carved by Keddy Writer this
weekend. Why? Because we weren't in Illinois, we were in Georgia!
Martini Man was in the Atlanta area on business last week, so I flew
down to join in for the weekend. We thought we'd be enjoying some
southern sun, but we were WRONG about that! My flight Friday night
was delayed due to big storms in Atlanta and then Saturday it rained
all morning. We didn't head out to do any boxing until almost 1 pm.
Due to the still "iffy" weather conditions, we only did some short
walks and had to make use of our umbrellas occasionally. Today was
better - no rain, but overcast. We were able to spend the whole day
at Little Mulberry Park, hiking 8+ miles and finding lots of boxes.
Despite the weather, we had a fun birthday weekend!

Re: [LbGLK] Re: Spring is Here

From: Coffee Beans (coffeebeans06@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-04-07 11:03:57 UTC-05:00
Along with many others, we too had an awesome letterboxing weekend.
When we saw the weather forecast, we couldn't resist and decided to
forgo all non-letterboxing plans we had for the weekend. We have a lot
to catch up on now, but it was worth it.

We decided to spend Saturday finding boxes, so we drove northwest and
found 14 boxes in/around Wauconda, IL -- Cherokee Rose 2's recently
planted Trip Across America 7 being the draw. :) Luckily, we hadn't
been to that area before so there were several other boxes nearby for us
to find also. And to top it off, we ended the day with our 700th find!
It was a fairly old plant from 2004 by Renfield called "All the World's
a Stage: Kindergarden," and boy did we have to work for this one! It's
noted in the clues that the park may not be upkeeping this particular
trail anymore, and I definitely agree! We had to backtrack several
times and use all of our creative clue interpretation skills to find it,
but luckily Atom's keen eye spotted the correct tree and the box. :)

On Sunday, we went back out to check on some of our more threatened
boxes along the Des Plaines River Trail. We successfully found most of
the boxes we looked for, but 2 of the 4 in the HHH series were
questionable as the trail leading to the boxes was flooded over. We're
not entirely sure since we couldn't get close, but the boxes are likely
underwater as well. :( However, both survived the severe flooding back
in the fall, so I still have hope that they're still there. We'll be
keeping our fingers crossed and making notes on the clues.

Happy spring and happy boxing to all!
Coffee Beans
