Questions about wood carvings

From: duncan53211 (duncan53211@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-10-26 17:20:05 UTC
Since my whole team is going to state, that does mean that there will
be 7 new boxes for the "KML Going to State" series as well as having
the previous three boxes fixed and updated.

My dilemma is that I cannot possible carve 7-10 "from photograph"
stamps by the time I leave to go up there this week. I could however
have a laser cutter from our school's tech ed dept. carve the stamps
for me in a day. I tried to create a stamp using the pink stuff with
the machine, but it didn't work. I could have it make the stamp as
wood carvings very easily however.

So, my two questions are these:

1)Would the fact that the stamps are machine produced prevent any of
you from seeking them out? (Any strong purists out there?)

2)Does anyone have any experience with the durability of hiding a wood
stamp carving in a letterbox? If so, what might I do to keep them
from rotting too quickly, and what is the best way for letterboxers to
get a good image of the stamp? What kind of wood might work best?

Thanks!
Dragon

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2008-10-27 06:08:09 UTC-04:00
Dragon,

Have you tried using a lino block on the laser cutter? They are firmer (and thicker) than our soft block materials but yet have more give than wood. There is a gal up in the U.P. who carves some of her stamps using lino blocks. Although it is more difficult to get a *perfect* image than it is when using rubber, it produces much better results than wood, especially if you take the time to ink it really good and have a hard surface to stamp on. The stamps seem to hold up fine despite the weather up there.

Congrats on the team's great season and good luck at state!

SpringChick
