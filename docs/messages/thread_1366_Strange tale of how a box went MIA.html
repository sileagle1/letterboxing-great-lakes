Strange tale of how a box went MIA

From: bindlestiff_boxer (bindlestiff_boxer@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-05-31 12:25:52 UTC
Not long after hiding some of the boxes in our Hauntings series,
several of them in the Traverse City area went missing. We planned to
re-carve and replace them to keep the Hauntings series alive.

Today I found this in my email box:
"My name is (removed on purpose) and I am a student at the University
of (removed on purpose). Every year we do a Scavenger Hunt that
involves items around Chicago and even around the US. This year part
of our team had to drive around Lake Michigan looking for certain
items. One item they had to find was the Traverse City State Hospital
Letterbox. However, we did not totally understand what letterboxing
was and accidentally took the box. We feel very badly about this and
would like to know where we can mail the box to so that it can be
returned to the State Hospital Ground. Please let me know so that I
can mail the box to you or to someone else in the area. Again, we are
very sorry that we took the box."

Of course I sent a nice note thanking them for the honesty and giving
a mailing address as well as a link to some letterboxing info.

However, I'm still sitting here feeling stunned.

I have questions like, "Who the heck was responsible for organizing
this Scavenger Hunt?" and "Why on earth didn't they provide info to
the participants about letterboxing if they were including boxes in
their hunt?" swirling around in my head.

And I'm a little bit mad because ALL of my clues include
this, "Copyright (year): Please do not publish or post this
information anywhere without obtaining prior permission." So I wonder
what part of that statement was not clear to whoever decided to add
my letterboxes to this hunt?

At least I have a chance of getting one of the boxes back. Of course
it means a 3 hour drive to replace it. Opps .. shouldn't grump about
getting a MIA box back! Thanks for letting me vent.

Enjoy!
---PJ
aka Bindle Babe
