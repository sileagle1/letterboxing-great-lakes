first letterboxing success

From: thrzabox (thrzabox@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-05-19 02:38:24 UTC
time and circumstances recently allowed our family to enjoy our very
first letterboxing trip!

since one member of our family is a scientist and the twins are ardent
fans of the nascar scene we selected a series that was inspired by
some science experiments.

we are most happy to report that we were successful in finding *every
single one* of the boxes in the series.

while we were not the first ones to complete the series, we remain
quite proud of ourselves for this accomplishment, as some of the
logbooks contained comments that indicate others had been unsuccessful
with some parts of the series.

since we are new to this activity, can anyone who has completed this
series and has an understanding of what we mean by *every single one*
tell us if this is a common situation or were we simply fortunate?

familia thrzabox

Hola, and welcome to letterboxing. I must say that I am honored
that you selected my series for your first experience.

Congratulations on your success with finding all of the boxes.

Based on email from you and SpringChick, I've updated the clues to
this series. Sometimes when I'm in the woods, what seems an obvious
landmark to me, really isn't obvious at all. Also, as I placed these
boxes on different days and from both ends of the trail, I hadn't
realized there was a problem using the rojo object in the way I had.

As for the *every single one* situation, I think you were fortunate
that you found something a little less than "ordinary" on your first
trip out. (grin) BTW - Bindle Bum was reading your email over my
shoulder and when he got to the part about your youngest and the lemon
pie, he laughed so hard he was given a "Shhh!" repremand by the Head
Librarian. (heheheh)

Enjoy!

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-05-20 12:23:41 UTC
Hey BindleBabe, thanks for revising the clues to the Fill 'er Up
series! I was able to find the boxes that had eluded us this past
weekend, "every single one of them." Actually I was the first person
to stamp into all of them. There were a couple other people who had
found some of the boxes and each of them had been found by at least
one person, but I was surprised to find that nobody had stamped into
all them (sounds like a logic puzzle, eh?).

Thrzabox, I was disappointed not to see your stamp in any of the log
books -- perhaps we all assumed incorrectly and you were actually
referring to a different letterbox series? If so, please dish -- I
am always up for a challenging series of boxes! If this was the
series you were referring to and you just didn't have a stamp yet
(you did mention it was your first outing), just wanted to let you
know that you can feel free to just sign into the log books until you
get a stamp.

Happy Trails!

SpringChick

From: thrzabox (thrzabox@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-05-20 20:01:41 UTC
You are correct that it was the Filling Up series and that we do not
yet have a family stamp. We went to Michaels but could not agree on
one. We are grateful for the discussion regarding carving materials
because we picked up a piece of the pink Speedy while we were there.

Thank you for the information that we could have simply signed the
log
books. We did consider it. However, an outting of familia thrzabox
is,
well, picture this:

Mom is carrying baby in backpack and holding the hands of overly
eager
twins who are loudly WHISPERING to each other about being sneaky. Our
oldest, in a wheelchair, proudly waving the freshly printed clue
sheets and holding the dogs leash. The dog is at the end of the
leash,
sniffing for a good place to urinate after ridding in the van for an
hour. Dad is pushing the WC while carrying a diaper bag filled with
all the usual items, plus special snacks and water to be used as
distractions as needed.

At the first box, when we discovered we hadn't brought something to
write with, it took all of our effort to restrain the twins! One was
screaming, "I'll go look in the van!" The other was preparing to
right
straight through the woods back to the van; to heck with library
patrons and dog walkers.

How lucky to have a quick thinking spouse who yelled over all, "NO!
We
don't need anything else for our nature walk. Today, we were JUST
GOING TO BE QUIET on our walk, RE-MEM-BER?"

familia thrzabox
