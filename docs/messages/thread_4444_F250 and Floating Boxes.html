F250 and Floating Boxes

From: cdem_ltrbox (wp4@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2007-09-18 00:54:29 UTC
We were participating in the Heritage Days 3Kwalk/ 5Krun up in Long
Grove last weekend. After our walk, we did a little more walking to
find some letterboxesall with a covered bridge theme. We found
Beetle's box first with a nice stamp and HH. Unfortunately, the
interior of the box also came with some mud and water. We were able
to leave it in a drier and cleaner condition, fortunately. Then it
was off to find 2 of Mosey's boxes. We couldn't find the first box
we looked forwe were the "experienced" boxers she mentioned in her
post. If she discovers that the box really was therewell, it won't
be the first time we've missed a box! Then it was on to her second
boxwhere we did find another nice stampand which happened to be
F250 for me! (the rest of the family is a little or a lot behind that
number).

Reading FungusWoman's updated clues for 2 of her boxes (basically,
get in a canoe and start paddling) made me think of those 2 boxes.
Right after we found our first box, we went on an afternoon of boxing
basically finding a lot of FungusWoman's boxes (and being amazed at
the beauty of hand-carved stamps.) One of those finds was "Swinging
Bridge" --the 3rd box we ever found. "Hoffman Towers" (a gorgeous
stamp) was F50 for me.

Fortunately, our boxes near the DesPlaines River had NOT floated
away. However, I will be updating the clues for UGRR #1Broken
Chains because there are some big fallen trees totally blocking the
path.

Well, enough of the rambling note (I need to do more rambling in the
woods looking for boxes!)
