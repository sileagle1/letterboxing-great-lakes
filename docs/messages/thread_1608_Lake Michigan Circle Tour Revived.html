Lake Michigan Circle Tour Revived

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-07-17 21:45:16 UTC
After considerable thought (and a little arm-twisting) I've decided
to resurrect the Lake Michigan Circle Tour project -- with a few
modifications from the original proposal. I'm working on details and
will post them within the next week or two.

To get you thinking (in case you weren't around when this was last
discussed or if you can't quite remember what it was all about)...

There is an official driving route around Lake Michigan through MI,
IN, IL and WI. See this site for info:

http://www.great-lakes.net/tourism/circletour/michigan/.

The goal of this project is to identify existing letterboxes and
place new letterboxes that are located within a short distance of
the official tour route and put together a listing of these boxes.


A few guidelines:

- This is an open-ended project -- it will be an ongoing thing with
boxes being added and removed as necessary.
- Boxes can be existing or newly placed for this series.
- Boxes do not need to meet any specific theme.
- Planters must be diligent about the status of their circle tour
boxes and be willing to follow-up on missing or damaged box reports
in order to keep the list as accurate as possible and the boxes in
good condition.
- Boxes should be within 10 miles off any exit from the official
Lake Michigan Circle Tour route.
- Boxes will be organized by "leg" of the route -- I am working on
these divisions. There will also be a "mystery box" category for
boxes that meet the "within 10 miles of exit" criteria but do not
specify exactly where they are located.
- With the exception of the mystery category boxes, clues for
included letterboxes must specify point of exit from the tour route,
distance from point of exit to park/location and an approximate box
retrieval distance or time.
- Included boxes must be listed on at least one public site, such
as letterboxing.org or AtlasQuest. It is okay to just list the box
and then link to clues that are on your own web site as long as your
web site does not require membership to access clues.

**I am also considering a sublist of "by request only" clues that
meet the criteria but are not publicly accessible. This will depend
on interest.**

Watch for further details and instructions on how to get your boxes
included in the tour listing.

SpringChick

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-07-19 19:07:45 UTC
Although the Letterboxing 'Round the Lake project is originating here
at the LbGLK group, one does not need to be a member of this group to
access information regarding the project, submit boxes, view the list
of boxes or obtain the clues to letterboxes included.

While joining Yahoo! groups and creating user accounts at web sites
may seem second nature to you and me, there are many people who for
whatever reason are not comfortable joining online groups or web
sites. It has always been the policy of letterboxing.org to provide
letterbox clues without requiring a person to create a user account
at the web site. Although a person must create an account to list
their letterbox clues, the clues posted at letterboxing.org are
accessible to the general Internet public regardless of membership
status (with the exception of clues linked to a membership-required
web site or Yahoo! group). Likewise at AtlasQuest -- clues listed at
this site are accessible to any person using the Internet without
having to join the site or create a user account.

While I certainly support various and alternate methods of clue
delivery and protection, including WOM, limited access and membership
only (free or otherwise), I feel that for a project of this nature,
where there is a high degree of potential for non-letterboxing folks
to want to "give it a try," clues should be readily available to the
general public without requiring an individual to join a site or set
up a user account.

That being said, I am willing to entertain alternate ideas, perhaps
some kind of a graphic indicator to show which clues require a
website/group membership to access them. Would this be an acceptable
compromise to you?

SpringChick
