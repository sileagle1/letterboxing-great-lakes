Is this box cursed?

From: TrailTroll (kanderson@american-community.com) | Date: 2008-04-23 16:56:14 UTC
I have a letterbox that seems to attract the unusual. It's in Paoli
Indiana, so it doesn't get visited very often. It was originally
planted under a deep bed of myrtle. Last year, a prison work crew had
come through and cleared all the brush. Fortunately, a nice inmate had
signed in with his inmate number, and he moved it to a hollow tree
nearby. This year, I went back to check on it, and there was a duck
nesting next to it! I thought the duck had adopted the container, so I
apologized for interrupting it and went to get my camera. When I
returned, I realized that the little nester hadn't moved.....at all. A
couple of pokes with a stick confirmed that the guardian of my box was
DEAD. Yuck. I promptly moved the box, and have updated the clues.
The name of the box is Morel Madness - I hope the duck didn't eat a bad
mushroom! Anybody else ever find a corpse on one of their boxes?

TrailTroll

From: Martha Hyland (martha.phillips@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-04-23 16:35:37 UTC-05:00
One of my first letterboxes this season was found in a tree -- and not
ten feet away was a dead and badly decomposed dog. I managed to check
(visually!) to see if there was a tag or collar, but found neither.
Poor thing, Made me glad I hadn't brought my own dog on that walk.
(Not that she'd be traumatized, but she's not anthropomorphized enough
to recognize that she shouldn't LICK it).

As for interesting finders, I've never had a prison inmate sign into
any box I've found, but back in 2004 when I started boxing I found
these two messages in two different boxes:

"Navy Seal on night training mission, the water is cold!" (A box near
the shore of Lake Michigan, underneath dense brush).

"7/9/04 -- Had fun finding this, but not who we are looking for --
Milwaukee Police." (hollow tree in forest)

Didn't even care that they might be fakes, I laughed at both.

-Kermit
Milwaukee, WI
