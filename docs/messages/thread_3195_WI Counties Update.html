Woo Hoo! We have 2 more "pieces of the puzzle" filled in on the map
of Wisconsin. The Y's Guys recently planted a box in Lafayette county
(the last southern county) and the Meandering Melee just posted two
boxes in Langlade county.

end of June, boxes have been placed in 14 new counties. That is
fantastic!

Re: [LbGLK] WI Counties Update

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-10-20 06:00:12 UTC-04:00
Okay, I am taking this as a challenge and hope that other MI letterboxers will rally behind me to get letterboxes in those boxless MI counties. I'm knee-deep in last-minute gathering details at the moment, but after it passes, I will color up a map for MI like WI Hiker has done for WI.

What constitutes a planted county? If there is a listing specifying a county at either LbNA or Atlasquest for an active letterbox, it counts -- doesn't matter if there are any status reports from the last year, etc. Just has to be a listed box that is believed to be active. I realize there may actually be boxes in some counties that are WOM or mystery boxes that do not indicate the county -- because they do not have a publically specified county, these don't count.

As was previously mentioned, the U.P. already has a box in every county, so the remaining counties are all in the lower peninsula. So get out the map and dig around a little to find something unique, scenic or interesting about a boxless county that inspires you to plant a box there!

SpringChick
