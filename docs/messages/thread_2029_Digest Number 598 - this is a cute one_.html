Subject: Re: TOTW: You Know You're Addicted...

Oh, this one is great!

Isn't it amazing the things that run through your mind while you are
sleeping? When I was in college, I used to joke that I debugged my
COBOL programs in my sleep... never failed if there was one that was
giving me fits, it would come to me while I was sleeping.

Reminds me of this summer when we were in the U.P. I spent a couple
of hours trying to figure out a LB and was making a really stupid
mistake (really, very stupid). It was 1 of a series of 3 and I had
got the other 2 boxes, so I really wanted to find this one. But,
finally after walking around the entire park a couple of time (a few
miles), I gave up. We were about 45 minutes up the highway en route
to the Porcupine Mountains when I suddenly realized where I went
wrong. I blurted it out, but thought it best not to ask to go back
as we were already way behind schedule due to the time I had already
spent looking for that box. Imagine how thrilled I was when Old Shoe
turned the truck around and headed back down for it! (What a guy!)

SpringChick

This is exactly the "bad rap" (and I appreciate you mentioning this CCB - I
mean no insult to your response) I was reading on the big list when I
started boxing myself that made me such a crusader in the way I teach girls
about boxing. Yes, indeed, we preach a GS leaves a place better than she
found it, and I pound it into their newbie boxer heads that they are to do
the same with letterboxes!! I can't speak for Michigan area GS, but I must
say those here in Illinois who I've dealt with are very responsible. I'm
sure those who have learned from leaders taught by Beetle are also just as
responsible.



BirdWoman, CMoon is right, the award is only at the Teen level (an Interest
Project, not a badge), but your girls can work on it as a bridging activity
once they're ready to move up out of Juniors!

Message: 2

Date: Tue, 15 Nov 2005 20:46:34 -0000

From: "cerealcityboxers"

Subject: Girl Scouts and letterboxing



If anyone is involved with the Girl Scouts, we would ask you to stress

the importance of rehiding a letterbox after it has been found. We

have recently found several letterboxes basically out in the open, and

the last stamp-ins were from Girl Scouts. The Scouts have a slogan

where they always leave a place in better condition than when they

found it. Apply that to letterboxing. Always hide a letterbox better

than when you found it. Or at least just as good. Is this part of a

written letterboxing code of ethics somewhere?
