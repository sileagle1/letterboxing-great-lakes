Spring Chick and others,

I do appreciate your comments, but as others have emailed things have changed for the better so there is no good reason not to register ordinary, mediocre or crappy LBoxes and HHs as AQ and LBNA are now soul mates. About the only good reason not to register HHs is if they are such special treasures that knowledge of there whereabouts is only spread by WOM or private emails so as to only be found among a select group of artisan LBoxers who have created little treasures.

However, the two HHs of which I speak are open for public inspection. They are nothing to brag about (one entry is in ballpoint pen with no stamp and somewhat illegible). I wouldn't call these HHs crappy, but they are ordinary. There is no reason they should not be registered at LBNA/AQ. The tracking that is done is only when found ~ not where they are placed. In this MInneapois area there is not a whole lot of LBox activity in the summer let alone the winter. So, in this particular instance more good than harm was done by me mentioning where I had placed "Christmas Swish Tree." You are correct is saying that most HH are a "surprise" and not a "treasure" as some would have you believe. If they were a "treasure" they would not have been placed where I could find them in LBoxes registered via public knowledge at LBNA and AQ. The bottomline is that there is NO GOOD REASON why Bindle Babe should deny finders the opportunity to record "Christmas Swish Tree" at Atlas Quest. This is an
example of not being a good team player.

The following is an email reply I made earlier to Ryan at Atlas Quest that I have pasted word for word. I have nothing but GOOD to say about how very, very helpful, unbiased and expedient he has been in responding to all of my emails.

"I retrieved my first two LBoxes and have changed their status to "missing." Another LBox that I had planted and was going to register with LBNA/AQ today is on hold as well as about seven more I had planned to place this year. The reason being that the contents are of a quality that is too good to list on a public website. My background is in bookbinding, papermaking, letterpress printing, foil hotstamping, screen printing, engraving, photolithography, still photography, cinematography, graphic design, audio-visual training materials, instructional technology, and broadcast television. I carved my first linoleum block over 40 years ago. Most LBoxers don't even know the meaning of type high as it relates to the thickness of linoleum blocks or why they are impractical for 97% of LBoxers who are better off using pink putz if they don't have their own little pilot press, proof press or larger Chandler & Price printing press.

I will be downgrading my two "missing" letterboxes to a quite ordinary status. Also, I'm
coming to the realization that women LBoxers are not near as active during winter months. I will buy some cheap little notebooks, carve a quicky counterfeit and get some smaller inexpensive containers, and edit my entry as appropriate. Will try to have these crappy LBoxes back in place in a week just in case one or two searchers come calling in the next 6 months.

Thanks to your advice and that of others I will no longer register my artisan quality LBoxes with LBNA or AQ."

"Thanks Again ~ Journey"
