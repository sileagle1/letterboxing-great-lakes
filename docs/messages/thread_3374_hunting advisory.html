From: Amy Theisen (aimlesst@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2006-11-30 05:36:34 UTC-08:00
I visited the Sir Robin Box near Middleton, WI on Thanksgiving Day. They had an advisory sign up that they were doing an extended bow season there due to herd reduction efforts to contain chronic wasting disease. I had blaze with me, but was still a little nervous. So I kept a running commentary going the whole time, just to be sure I couldn't be mistaken for a deer. "Hmm, wonder if I'm going the right way. Lovely day. Lovely shrubbery here. Not a deer, please don't shoot me." I saw no hunters, but did see 4 does. (Just in case anyone heading in for the gathering was planning on grail boxes along the way).

From: Melisa Hills (mshills1@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2006-11-30 07:55:05 UTC-06:00
Glad you were safe! That would have been a bit scary. We wondered,
when we drove into Menomonee Park for some boxing on Thanksgiving, if
hunting was allowed there (since they have the archery range in the
park). But, since there are horse trails (and people on horses riding
around), we assumed not, but with the children with us, we were ready
to turn around and head back out if there was hunting.

A related funny here, we were driving home on Sunday from a friends
house about 15 min. before hunting season is over. Driving from by
the Waukesha Airport back towards Sussex - the over the river and
through the woods route (there is a farm with sheep, chickens and a
cow that the children like to see). We come around the first curve
and we see a guy in blaze orange along the edge of the woods and the
cornfield on a walkie talkie. We go a short bit further and another
guy in blaze orange on the other side of the road in the
woods. Then, we go about a half mile down the road and there are 5
deer - Standing in someone's front yard - as if they know that when
dusk hits they are safe to go back into the woods, but until then,
they are safe in this person's front yard. We were laughing
ourselves silly at the concept. If they would have gone a bit
further, they would have been safe within town limits, LOL! Guess
you had to be there, but it cracked us up!

From: Barb Funk (mmfunk99@greenapple.com) | Date: 2006-11-30 13:46:57 UTC-05:00
The deer hangin' out in someone front yard while the deer hunters
hunting not far away reminds me of a Far Side Calendar page! Cat
Herder, your description was hillarious!

I want to throw one more caution out there about hunters and hunting
season. ( I hunt as well and so this is in now way meant to be mean in
any way or to anyone.)

For those of us who letterbox in the dark, sometimes hunters are there
in the dark too. Responsible hunters never shoot unless they SEE and
confirm their target as the animal they intend to shoot, but sadly the
very first shot taken in Ohio for gun/deer season was one half hour
before the season began and was in the dark. It resulted in a 20 year
old nephew being shot by his uncle. The uncle shot at the noise. The
nephew "misunderstood" where he was supposed to be flushing out the
deer, "according to the uncle". In other words the uncle rationalized
it was "okay" to fire at an uncertain target because he thought he knew
who was and wasn't in the woods. Luckily most hunters are very safe,
but it was a sobering reminder for me on both sides of the fence that
things do happen.

I hope everyone has a great and safe time at the first snow event this
weekend in WI! I know all of us in Ohio are jealous. I hear that same
weather will be hitting us starting tomorrow! This is our last 66
degree day for quite some time, I'm sure.

Good luck to the Brat Pack too! Way to bring another letterboxer into
our world!!

I guess the deer safety council did a good job with their memo that was sent to all the midwest deer populations. The deer took their advise and precautions!

On the way from Marietta to Columbus, we also did some letterboxing on Thanksgiving Day. We were driving down some back road and came into this little town. There was a group of guys on one corner, all leaning up against a truck, drinking beer, and waving at all the passing cars. We kept going and right around the next curve in the road was a deer. He was just moseying along like he didn't have a care in the world. Not only was he in town limits and in someone's front yard, but he also had a strip of what appeared to be bright orange caution tape wrapped around his neck! We laughed so hard we almost wrecked, then we turned around and took a couple pictures of him.
We still think it was some kind of strange Brotherhood-of-the-Deer initiation thing.
"Hey Buck...I double dog dare ya to put this around your neck and go walkin through town. It'll drive Bubba and Clem crazy, cause they can't shoot ya! That'll teach 'em for eatin poor Mama."
