REPORT: DuPage/Kane Counties / IL

From: Mosey (PonyExpressMail@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-06-15 12:17:18 UTC-05:00
Had the day off and it was wonderfully overcast, cool, and breezy -- perfect
for letterboxing rather than housecleaning.

1. & 2. Stopped first at Oakhurst Forest Preserve, near Aurora, Kane
County, for two boxes there planted by Howling Coyote. Nice little lake --
the history behind how the place was developed was interesting.

Unfortunately, couldn't find Box #1. The clues were pretty basic, so I
can't imagine that we weren't in the right area. We found two possibilities
for the "happy couple" that the box was supposed to be near, one
"vegetable", one "mineral." We checked out both. There are some smallish
rocks scattered about in one place that look like they might have been the
"cover" for the box. About a foot from the rocks was an empty liquor bottle
in a brown paper bag. Looking around the area, it seems like it might be
rather popular for hosting "get-togethers" -- water bottles, soda cans, the
liquor bottle. We were sure we were in the right place, but hopefully we
weren't. Or we were but the box is just hidden better than we were looking.
We thought we did a thorough check, but ya never know.

Had a nice walk by the lake to Box #2. Egrets, herons, and barn swallows
galore. Momma Goose, Papa Goose, and three little goslings were snacking by
the side of the lake. Didn't get too close -- the parents didn't look
welcoming to human guests -- if looks could kill, momma and papa would've
succeeded in burying us. From the logbook, it appeared we were the first
finders. But the container was in a plastic bag that was very badly ripped
and torn already, even tho the box was only planted a couple of weeks ago.
We didn't have a gallon size bag, so we used the next size down, and it fit
and closed, but barely. So maybe if someone else heads out to snag the box,
they can bring a gallon size bag along if they have one?

We picked up a "Hitchhiker Book" in Box #2 -- more about that in another
post.

3. Then drove down to snag "As the Dragon Flies" at the DuPage River Park
near Naperville, placed by the Weekend Warriors. Leave it to wealthy
Naperville -- fancy "outhouse," free poop bags for the dogs, even a pay
phone there. Tho I would imagine by now that everyone in Naperville would
have a cellphone. Quite a few folks were there with their dogs, playing in
the pond -- the dogs were playing in the pond, not the people. One yellow
Lab was busy retrieving a branch almost as large as a tree, and at least
twice as big as him. Nice hiding spot for the box. Box was in great shape
and seemed happy. Once we saw the stamp, the title of the box made sense.
More barn swallows galore.

~~ Mosey ~~
