GLk 2010 Preliminary Report

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2010-06-06 05:05:10 UTC
An exciting day! The morning was mostly rainy which meant slow boxing w/muddy trails and umbrellas, ponchos, etc. Then we were all pleased to see the clouds clear and the sun appear!! Lunch & group photo w/sunny skies. Afternoon changed to overcast again, but not much rain at all. Boxers however were still dealing w/VERY muddy, slippery trails. Lots of mud covered boots, legs, arms, packs, and even eyeglasses from the numerous falls (photos coming soon). Unfortunately Joe Boxer sustained a sprained ankle (at least) and was out of commission.

However that was only the beginning... later in the evening the clouds formed into tornados and the rain came belting down. Most (probably ALL) of the campers abandoned the campsites and either headed home or to hotels. And FungusWoman suffered enough worrisome symptoms to warrant emergency vehicles and a trip or two to the hospital (she called later in the evening to say she was doing OK and was at a hotel w/the HowD Girls). Probably dehydration or heat exhaustion... I'm sure she will provide more details later.

Nonetheless, these were GREAT LAKES LETTERBOXERS, so nothing deterred many of them from meeting at Buffalo Wild Wings at 9:30 pm to exchange stamps & stories. Roly-Polies even managed to carve up a tornado event stamp that got passed along the tables at the restaurant!!

At 11:20 pm there were still 30+ boxers at the restaurant when we said our good-byes (so we could hit the muddy trails again tomorrow).

Thanks to Hartx6, Pitties and Shorty for organizing an event with so many wonderful carvings and so many "adventures"! Luckily everyone kept up their good spirits and I'm sure this event will be a very memorable one for all who attended.

More later...

Wisconsin Hiker

From: (RobinBarton@integritivity.com) | Date: 2010-06-06 08:44:50 UTC-06:00
Cheesapalooza and I got home safely at 1:30 a.m. I hope that everyone else
go home OK.

The weather we drove through was terrifying! I had planned to drive south
through Springfield to avoid the worse stuff coming from the SW, but as we
were heading east for Peoria to get to 155, the news was saying that the
worst was IN east Peoria and there were tons of cars heading in the
opposite direction. So I turned around and went back to the campground,
but headed for the Quail shelter we used today, thinking it was the
closest (but it wasn't) and we stayed in the bathroom for while. Then we
left again, heard on the radio that there was still 5 more minutes of
warning near us, stopped at the closest campground loop and spent some
time in their bathroom. Then we got on the road before the next wave go
to the highway, but drove through some really dicey stuff even though
supposedly we weren't in the tornado area anymore. Once I pulled off the
highway and then under an overpass to wait it out and tornado sirens were
going off. I thought of pulling under a bridge a few other times when the
rain was so heavy but there were already cars under the bridges and no
room for me. After a while we were coming up on an edge of the storm and
there was so much lightning that we were getting closer and closer to that
I was pretty freaked out but also was having trouble seeing. I pulled to
the side under a bridge and then doubted myself for a bit because no one
else was stopping and it was not raining at all. Then my decision was
affirmed as it started POURING and blowing horizontally from the opposite
direction (from the north) and ALL cars stopped, whether they were under a
bridge or not. For 5-10 terrifying minutes I sat there praying that our
van would not be blown into the ditch (but at least that's probably as far
as it would go). I even told my daughter to lay down on the seat because I
was afraid the back window might blow in or something. The van was shaking
and then finally the wind backed off. I waited until the wind was
definitely better, then took off into surprisingly still heavy rain.
Fortunately, it lessened after 10 or 15 minutes and then by Springfield it
wasn't raining at all anymore. Then I just had to get us home with my
exhausted nerves!

I wish now that we had found a hotel in Peoria because today should be
beautiful and we didn't get much yesterday (due to my daughter's lack of
enthusiasm) and I planned to do a lot today. But I wasn't sure what to do,
didn't know where the hotels were, and it sounded like we would just be in
the basement of a hotel much of the night. But it sound like things were
OK after 8pm, so I'm glad you all didn't have to go through that again.
Maybe I can convince my family to do a little letterboxing around here
this afternoon to make up for it (after we dry out the tent and everything
we tossed in the car!).

Thanks for a wonderful gathering, we really had fun during the day!

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2010-06-07 00:26:52 UTC-05:00
We just got home tonight around 11 pm safe & sound. Due to all the weather
excitement, we invited Hartx6 to use the sleeper sofa in our hotel suite
last night (we hated to think of him out in his car!). We slept in a bit
this morning since we didn't want to disturb him too early. I'm sure he was
exhausted, so we finally snuck past him down to breakfast where we chatted
with several other boxers. Those that were at the hotel last night were
apparently asked to congregate in some "safe" areas and the TJMich parents
told their boys that everyone was having a cookie party so they didn't get
too worried about the storm (our hotel always had cookies available in the
lobby). We heard that the LBers at the hotel took the opportunity to do a
few exchanges at this time (Great Lake boxers are always prepared!). I'm
curious how the hotel contacted people. Since we were out to dinner w/Tiptoe
& Tonto at the time, we weren't part of the hotel corridor/laundry room
party. (By the way, at Buffalo Wild Wings, someone who works there did tell
us the best place to go in case of a tornado was the restaurant cooler room.
This was probably the same thing as the Steak & Shake freezer, except we
didn't actually need to go into it! Instead we stood around and watched the
storm reports on the large screen TVs, then sat down to eat & socialize. I
heard the people that ate earlier at the Jubilee Cafe went into the basement
for safety, but they also got free pie while there!)



Hartx6 eventually became conscience by 9:30 am, and Martini Man & I were
heading back to the park by 10 am. Sunny skies, great temperatures, but
trails were still quite muddy and had lots of water in some areas. Our
first sign of a LBer was a note posted by Dartmoor Dreamer on a trailhead
sign warning people about 16" of water on a trail and a map suggesting an
alternate route. A bit later we saw SunnySideUp & Peabody , but then it was
a LONG time before we saw anyone else. Today we did the Australian, etc.
loop and the European loop. We were impressed to discover that all the
boxes were totally dry inside, even after the torrential downpours
yesterday. Due to water on the trails, we found a route across the corn
field at one point (rather than the trail), only to discover that the next
trail we wanted was quite flooded. This meant taking an alternate route
around, then coming backwards along the KME trail. But we were able to do
this ONLY after we acquired a few logs to build ourselves a little bridge
across a creek. Late afternoon we ran into Pitties, Shorty, Hartx6 and
Tiptoe and Tonto. They were nearing the end of doing our Coin-Operated
series. Unfortunately they had failed to find box #4 and we had heard that
several others had problems with this box. However we do know that Leapin'
Lizards & The Dragon did somehow manage to find it yesterday, so perhaps
they can post some hints or clarifications if the clues need some help.



After finishing up the two loops, we had time to do the first box in the
clue packet (carved by FungusWoman and possibly illustrating "I'd Rather be
Fishing"'s suggestion for how she could find some letterboxes). Hooray!! We
managed to find all the boxes listed in the clue packet (of course we didn't
need to do our own long series, so that made it a bit easier). We left the
park at 6:45 pm and headed back home to Wisconsin.



Many thanks to Hartx6, Pitties and Shorty for hosting the event this year!
The path tags were very nice, there were a ton of excellent carvings, and we
appreciated the longer hiking trails. Thanks also to everyone that carved
the stamps - there were many interesting images.



It was great fun to see so many people that we have gotten to know over the
past 7 years and to also meet a few new people. We were very happy to see
so many WI boxers at the event and also happy to see more from MN were able
to make it this year. A bonus was to have some MO people attend. (However
we were sorry to see that Azobox did not make it this year. He was the ONLY
person that had made it to ALL of the previous GLk events). Hopefully we'll
see some of you at other events later this year.



The still addicted & obsessed,



Wisconsin Hiker & Martini Man
