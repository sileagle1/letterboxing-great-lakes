================================

That's exactly what we're hoping to do!

We did some boxing on our way to celebrate Christmas Eve with Martini Man's
side of the family in the Madison, WI area. We found "Peas in a Pod" and
"Lions" and also put another logbook in our "Picnic in the Capitol" box
since we had received a report that there were only a few pages left in the
first book (Thanks Christmas Elves!). Christmas morning we headed back home
to celebrate with my (Wisconsin Hiker) side of the family and found 3 boxes
by the MonkeyHunters (Messy Trails Hybrid Cache, Skybrid Cache and Quest for
BigTooth). We were pleasantly surprised to discover that the hybrid boxes
contained HAND-CARVED stamps. All the other combo geocache/letterboxes that
we have found before only had an off-the-shelf, store-bought stamp. Glad we
decided to give another hybrid a try. The Quest for BigTooth box was along
a great trail that followed a creek and crossed it several times. Very
pretty on a winter day, with some light snow falling occasionally. Also a
good way to start each day since it gave us a chance to get outside and get
a little exercise before all the holiday eating & drinking began! [P.S.
All the boxes were in great shape - totally dry inside.]

Now we're planning another 2-day visit to northern IL to find some boxes.
We're hoping to be there Thursday & Friday this week and the "Christmas
Around the World" boxes are definitely on our list. We had hoped to also do
the "Big Ten" series, but when we checked the clues yesterday we found out
that they have all been pulled for the winter. Oh well, we have other ones
we can look for! One of them is the latest Cemetery Stroll box. We're
"making our list and checking it twice" before we head out.

We hope you all have a chance to do some boxing this week. It is still
pretty on the trails and there are a lot less "scratchy things".

Wisconsin Hiker
