From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2009-11-23 09:14:21 UTC-08:00
While feedback from finders is great and can be a wonderful tool to improve your plants, there is a fine line between using them as a learning experience and being a target for somebody's bad day or poor finding skills. Obviously if you are getting a lot of similar feedback about a particular box, there is probably something you want to re-examine, but don't let an isolated negative comment dishearten or deter you too quickly.

As a new letterboxer it is natural to have some uncertainty about if you are doing it "right." Stick around and you will quickly find that there are a hundred different versions of "right." While letterboxers like to claim there are "no rules" and there is no formal "how-to-letterbox" rulebook to guide a new letterboxer, there are many unwritten rules... but be careful, because even these are subjective, differing from person to person or region to region. It really is something that comes with experience.

You will never please everyone simply because everyone is looking for
something different from a letterbox -- some like long hikes, some like
drive-bys; some like mystery boxes, some like a slight challenge and
others want gimmes; some expect hand-carved stamps and handmade
logbooks, others just want a stamp and a notepad; some want a location
with a view, others could care less about the surrounding area...
plant boxes that are enjoyable for you and chances are they will be
enjoyable for a hefty segment of the letterboxing crowd, even if not
for everyone.

That being said, if there are specific things that you question or have concerns about in the feedback you receive, please feel free to pose a question to the list and you will get plenty of feedback offering various perspectives from boxers in our area.

SpringChick

Re: [LbGLK] Virgin letterboxer....trying to learn more

From: Kathleen Brade (brade@comcast.net) | Date: 2009-11-23 13:16:55 UTC-05:00
Hi!

I'm not sure exactly what issues people might have.
Off the top of my head, here are a few things:

1) Place / Location is relatively safe (not on railroad tracks or middle
of a road, not in a patch of poison ivy).
2) Place / Location does not harm the surrounding environment.
3) Clues: Have someone else (letterboxer or non-letterboxer) follow
your clues to be sure they are correct. For example, make sure every
compass direction is accurate. I know some boxers who avoid letterboxes
by certain letterboxers because the clues tend to have LOTS of mistakes
in them.
4) Stamp: I find it helpful to know if the stamp we are seeking is
hand-carved or not but that is due to my highly-biased son. You don't
need to state what type of stamp you have included.
5) Container: Don't use food containers that have ever contained food.
Make sure they seal well. OK, there may be some exceptions to this.
6) Use of baggies: use them inside your container. Ideally you don't
need to put your container in a baggie; if so, rethink your container.

I think others already replied with other suggestions / tips.

-- MichKathy
