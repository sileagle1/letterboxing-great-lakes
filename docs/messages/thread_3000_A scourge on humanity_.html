From: Scout Dogs (DogScouts@hotmail.com) | Date: 2006-08-25 17:11:55 UTC-04:00
I agree! You should try getting them out from between the pads of a dog's
paws!

A friend's dog got them in his feet and was so traumatized that he had to be
muzzled and pinned down to get them removed (his feet were covered with them
and then he got some in his mouth trying to remove them from his own feet.)
We had to get the ones out of his mouth first and that's when he was sure we
had lost our minds and were trying to kill him.

Thankfully I moved quick enough not to be bitten before all of the ones in
his mouth were out. And thankfully we had our first aid kit in a backpack
and that included an emergency muzzle and pliers. It pays to be prepared!
I'm sure someone would have been seriously bitten if we hadn't had the
proper supplies. No way to carry a 120 lb dog over a mile out of the woods
(or safely remove burrs from the mouth of a dog in that condition without
tools. As it was, we needed the bandages that were in the kit too because
some had gone deep enough to cause bleeding. It was also a blessing that no
one else was around because anyone coming up on us would have thought for
sure we were torturing this dog and killing him slowly.
