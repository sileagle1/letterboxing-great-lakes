a stamp making tip

From: John J Foerch (jjfoerch@earthlink.net) | Date: 2004-03-22 15:54:44 UTC
Here is how I get a design from computer (or anywhere) onto a stamp block.

1. Print the design, actual size.
2. On the back of the printout, trace the design, using a soft pencil.
3. Lay the paper on the stamp block, trace side down, and scribble to
transfer the pencil marks to the block.


Stamp making tips, anyone? I'm new to this.

Re: a stamp making tip

From: Deb (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-03-22 17:23:24 UTC
Hi John, I think you want to pencil trace on the printed side of the
paper and lay that side face down on the rubber and rub to transfer
the pencil markings. The design transferred to the rubber will then
appear backward on the rubber, which is how you want it so that it
will be the right way when you stamp. If you pencil trace on the
back of the printed image and transfer, the design on the rubber will
be facing the right way and the stamped image will come out backward.

A couple of other ways to get the image onto the carving material
which will work if you are using a photocopy or a laser-printed image
(i.e. doesn't work with inkjet prints), is to simply lay the printed
design down on the rubber and dab it with a cotton ball that has been
wetted with acetone. Can also be done without the acetone using an
iron set at very low heat -- just be careful that you don't directly
touch the iron to the rubber around the edges or you will have a
sticky mess!

For more carving tips, check out Ruthann Zarhoff's carving tutorial
here:

http://www.ruthannzaroff.com/carving/

There is also a downloadable .PDF file of the handout I used for the
stamp carving workshop at last year's GL Letterbox gathering
available here:

http://www.confessionsofdecember.com/lbna/events/030531_materials.asp

Have fun!

SpringChick
