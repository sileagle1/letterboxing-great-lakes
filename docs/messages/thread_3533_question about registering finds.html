Just like so many things about letterboxing, where, or even whether at all, you register your finds is up to you. As Dragon pointed out, both LBNA and Atlas Quest have ways for you to log your finds. Re: [LbGLK] question about registering finds

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2007-01-07 17:06:35 UTC-05:00
Registering your finds is totally optional and boxers who do it have different reasons for so. Some people do to keep track of the boxes they have found; other people do it as a way of letting the planter know that they have found the box; and some people don't do it at all.

Both LbNA and AtlasQuest provide the ability to register finds (as pointed out by Dragon). As you continue letterboxing you will probably find that neither site lists all of the boxes you have found, so using it to keep count of your finds will not necessarily give an accurate count. Registering your finds will give the planter an idea of when their box is being found, but it doesn't give the opportunity to provide feedback, which most planters love to get. As an alternate or additional option to simply registering the find, you can send the planter a note ("contact the placer" option on LbNA or "send AQ mail" on AQ) to let them know of your experience with their box.

As you can see, there are many different ways to do it and all are optional -- just choose what works best for you!

SpringChick

Re: question about registering finds

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2007-01-07 23:22:46 UTC
There are many letterboxers, especially those who are active on the
talk lists, who would agree that it is courtesy to let the placer
know you've found their box -- either by logging status or a
personal note. But it is not a requirement of the game and there
are many, many letterboxers who just happily go about printing clues
and finding boxes and you never hear a word from them.

Letterboxing is different things to different people and everyone
has a different level of involvement in the game. As a placer I love
to receive personal notes from finders and *try* to reply to each of
them (am not always as good at that as I should be); in turn I try
to send a personal note to a planter when I find their box. I know
of many casual boxers who never do this -- that is their choice.

SpringChick

Good point. I do greatly enjoy hearing that people have found my boxes...but more often than not when I do get a "contact the placer" note it's to let me know that people had trouble finding my box or that it wasn't in good condition (and I do also enjoy recieving these messages too, as they provide invaluable information to me when deciding what letterboxes to do maintenance on and when).
