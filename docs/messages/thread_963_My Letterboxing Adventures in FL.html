My Letterboxing Adventures in FL

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-01-08 14:15:58 UTC

I was down in FL for a few days, in the panhandle, Panama City Beach
Area. Before I went I checked for boxes because I knew I would have
a day to explore and do a little boxing. There were 6 boxes in the
surrounding counties, 4 of which indicated they had been washed away
by the hurricanes and not yet replaced. I headed down with clues to
the remaining 2 boxes.

On Monday, I headed out in shorts and Birkenstocks (not my usual
letterboxing garb, but hey, it was sunny and 77 degrees there -- a
record high for this time of year -- it was freezing rain and very
cold where I had come from). I drove to the next county over to the
city of Destin -- a place with the most wonderful location and a
disgustingly commercial tax base. Box #1 -- I find the park, I see
the hiding spot before I am even out of my car yes, a drive-
by... I even see the blue rim of the box top peeking out from
behind the rock as I am getting out of the car. Hey, at least the
box is here, I thought as I pulled it out from under the
footbridge... my first FL letterbox. I actually felt a little bit
of a rush -- until I opened the box and realized there was no stamp
inside. Just a logbook and a little bag of gold-foil wrapped
chocolate coins. Huh? WTF is this crap? Geocachers, maybe?
Disappointed, but not willing to go home without some proof that I
had been there, I inked up one of the coins and stamped my book.

Off to box #2... This one was inside a ritzy, glitzy resort. I
tell the guard I am there to visit the Village and he gives me a
pass, no questions asked. I drive around the golf course and into
the Village, parking near the footbridge to this tropical-paradisey
island. Very cool. I follow the clues around the island and
eventually come out at the bay... turn left and there ahead is the
swing I am looking for. Oops, there laying in the sand under the
swing is pair of sleeping bodies, half intertwined with one another,
half intertwined with a sleeping bag, half naked -- and sleeping, or
least pretending to be sleeping. There is no way I can get to the
box location without walking over them, so I continue on and then
back down the trail a bit, and I wait... and I wait... A few times
I thought about just leaving, but hey, I had no more clues and I
wasn't leaving FL without a stamp -- and I did have a book with me,
and it was a really gorgeous place... So I waited longer, and a
couple of times I walked toward them, shuffling my feet along the
wooden boardwalk, hoping to wake them up. After about an hour and a
half, they arose, dressed, wadded up the sleeping bag and left. I
made my way back to the swing... there was the pine tree and the
little bush thingie next to it. Supposedly the box should be under
this bush/tree thing in some native grass and pine cones. Don't see
any pine cones, just a lot of poison ivy and some briars. Carefully
stepping over the briars and around the poison ivy (remember...
shorts and sandals), I looked under every nearby tree. Then kind of
behind one of them, I see a pile of grass and a couple of pine
cones... Hmm, maybe the box lies within. So, I begin clearing away
the grass and what do I find hiding there? One very large copper-
colored snake who did not appear to be happy I had upset his world.
Back through the briars and poison ivy I went, this time with less
care than on the way in... and I'll be, but that darn snake
followed me! So, up onto the swing goes me, my backpack and my
scraped legs. Once I decided he wasn't coming any closer, I left.
No box. I did however bring home a souvenir -- little red poison
ivy bumps all over my feet and ankles.

I had thought about planting a box while down there and decided
against it -- hey, if they can't plant their own boxes, why should I
do it for them?

SpringChick
