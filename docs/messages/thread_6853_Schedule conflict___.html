From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2011-01-05 03:00:28 UTC-08:00
It was not posted to a board on AQ, it is on the event info page on the event
calendar. I can only speculate that it was not announced here as there has not
been any change from the original announced date.

It is unfortunate that there are two events in the region that conflict this
year, but I think going forward as letterboxing continues to grow and events
become even more prolific, it is likely to happen from time to time. There was
no intentional overlap, but there are only so many weekends in that area of time
and working around the schedules of those doing the planning and location
availability limits that even more. As we have learned, you are never going to
be able to accommodate everyone -- even if it had been possible to change the
date, it is likely there would be other folks negatively impacted by the new
date. It was agreed by the group to make the date of the GLG "host's choice."
While that offers the benefit/flexibility of acquiring a desirable location and
moving the date around to make the event more accessible to folks who have
personal conflicts with any set date there may be, it also opens up the
possibility of other scheduling conflicts that are more likely to occur when the
date does not remain constant from one year to the next.

SpringChick

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2011-01-05 16:14:37 UTC-08:00
The first couple of years the date of the gathering was random and then once it
was apparent this was going to become an annual affair, we set up the state
rotation plan and decided to stick with the weekend after Memorial Day so people
could plan on a specific date. This was the date for a few years thereafter,
but it always caused conflicts for teachers as it was at the end of school.
Also, as the gathering grew it became more difficult to find locations that were
suitable for a group our size, especially having a specific date to work with.
So once we went full circle with states hosting, we voted here at the Yahoo!
group about the possibility of changing the date.

There were a few different options, one to leave it on the Saturday after
Memorial Day, a few others that weren't very popular and then the option of
making it "host's choice" within the time frame of May - September, hoping a
flexible date would make it easier to secure a location and also reasoning that
moving the date around a bit would make it possible for people who had conflicts
with any set date to be able to come at least some of the time. The "host's
choice" option was by far the popular choice and last year was the first year
under the new date guideline, although I think it still ended up being the
Saturday after Memorial Day.

While changing the date addressed some of the problems, it did open the door to
new ones... nothing is perfect I guess. I think it points out the importance
of lining up a host as early as possible, so that a location can be secured and
a date set early enough that conflicts will not be an issue. I have
traditionally waited until later summer or early fall to do this, but I think in
the future, it is something that should be done as soon after the current year
gathering as possible. My only hesitation with choosing a host too far out is
that people come and go and lose interest in the hobby, especially over the
winter, and I would hate for somebody to volunteer to host and then fizzle out
before the event came around.

SpringChick
