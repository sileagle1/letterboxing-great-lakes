Hauntingly goooood time!

From: bindlestiff_boxer (bindlestiff_boxer@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-10-02 13:00:08 UTC
Ten cool things we talked about last night as we reviewed the day:

1. What a wonderfully well organized event (we'd like to echo Fungus
Woman in giving thanks to our Hosts!) and how ideal the location
was... beautiful park and having the building was super!
2. How MANY people turned out for the gathering ... way more than
anyone anticipated! (we really enjoyed getting to meet so many new
boxers, put names and faces to trail names we've seen online, as well
as doing exchanges with the chance to see some beautifully carved
signature stamps ... and even to collect a few personal travelers)
3. Trail memories by Mrs DogLvrs include the time 3 teams of boxers in
the woods... one of those moments when the noise level drops ... and
out of the silence Fungus Womans voice floats back, "Oh look, I found
some mushrooms! They're so cute."
4. The huge number of boxes that were available for finding.
(published, WOM, temporary and same-day placed ones) As I was talking
with WI Hiker during the day, she said they figured that using a 10
minute stamp in (not counting any hiking or clue solving time) there
were enough published boxes to keep a person busy for 7-8 hours!
5. The potluck food was great ... thanks to all who shared food and a
big thanks to whoever brought bottled water! (Mrs DogLvrs and I had no
idea the drinking water in this area was 'hard') (And Tipto ~ thanks
for giving me the finger! ~Mrs.)
6. The Group Photo event ... hehhehe .... two shots each with about 15
cameras but everyone was a trooper and put up with it nicely. (I
didn't see one single pair of finger 'bunny ears' behind anyones head)
7. The fun we had watching the Great Altoids Gum Tins Collection Event
(thank goodness some helpful kids gathered them all up for us so we
didn't have to go in search of those 12 tins before we could leave!)
8. How much fun I had teaching the Kids Logbook workshops. I really
enjoyed watching them learn how easy it would be to make their own
logbooks. I especially liked when 2 kids from my last session wanted
to push the learning up a notch and go onto a little more advanced
level and learn how to make the logs using quatro signatures. I loved
that Drama Mamma (I know you're out there lurking!)sat in on one of my
sessions so she could learn and take back the lesson to her groups of
Cub Scouts. (A special thanks to everyone who brought supplies and
loaned us class equipment ... what a generous bunch of people!)
9. How perfect the weather was. Whoever put THAT requisition in
deserves a huge pat on the back. LOL
10. And finally ... our own "Adventures in Just Getting To the
Gathering" (Lake Express ferry was cancelled, rented a car and drove,
paying for wi-fi at a truck stop so we could download some clues to
boxes along the way..heheheh) But that's another story. LOL

Thanks again everyone for sharing your time with us - and now we have
to pack everything up to catch the ferry....we hope!
---Mrs DogLvrs and Bindle Babe

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2005-10-02 23:19:45 UTC-05:00
Hi!



Wed like to thank everyone who attended the Haunted Harvest Gathering
yesterday! A grand total of 80 people (and 5 dogs) attended the event. We
had a fantastic time talking with so many boxers we hadnt met before and
touching base again with those wed met at previous gatherings. It was nice
to see so many kids enjoying the activities (boxing, logbook making,
Halloween crafts, and just running around the beach having fun). The dogs
also provided entertainment, especially that funny Scout who DIDNT want to
walk on the floor in the building. The food was delicious and we couldnt
have asked for better weather!



We appreciate the help provided by Merlin & Ardea, Tabby Tales and JAB in
preparing for the event, Bindle Babe for running the popular kids logbook
workshop AND for taking the group photos, and Mr. Puddlejumper for helping
out so much with post-event cleanup.



We owe a special thanks to those who created boxes for the event. Some were
by local or regional boxers, a few of whom created their very first boxes
for this event. We also had the wonderful Phineas Bochs Traveling Circus
boxes, and several other boxes generously created by people from other areas
of the country, including CT, NY, PA, TX, and OR. There were published
clues for 38 boxes in the gathering handout.



There were also several boxes with clues distributed by various other
means. These included: Boo, Eensy Weensy, Felka Lechsinska, Mr.
Pumpkinhead, Halloween Letterbox Surprise, Samara, Psycho, Haunted Harvest ,
personal travelers, and probably even more!.



The creators of the boxes that will remain as permanent placements
(approximately 18 boxes) will be posting the clues on the Internet in the
near future so they will be available for people who were unable to attend
the gathering.



In addition to the boxes, there were at least 3 cooties infecting the crowd,
so those of you who were there may want to check your belongings to see if
you went home with anything extra.



Speaking of something extra - when we were cleaning up, we discovered the
following items that were left behind:



Large square Glad storage container

Large rectangular Ziploc container

1 knife (used to cut apples?)
2 smaller white serving spoons

1 metal serving spoon w/cutout in handle

1 black plastic serving spoon w/red accent in handle

1 compass



If any of these belong to you, please contact me off list and we can arrange
something to get them back to you.



I hope to have some photos posted soon (within the next few days) on the HHG
site. If anyone wants to send me any photos they took, please do so! Id
especially appreciate any family or small group photos and photos taken out
on the trail, but any and all photos are welcome.



Thanks again to everyone who made it such a fun day!



Wisconsin Hiker & Martini Man





Miscellaneous Memories:



Hubbub of activity at registration area. Many people were attending their
very first gathering, so there were explanations of stamping onto event
poster, stamping a name tag, stamping event stamp, and handing out clue
sheets & maps.



Quiet of mid-morning when almost everyone hit the trails. That gave us time
to find the 12 Altoid tins!



Group photo and Bindle Babe telling us we had to get a little closer, a
little closer, a little closer. This was a bit more of an effort for those
that were sitting or kneeling in the sand. Our motivation for getting the
photo done was lunch!



It was Kimono Girls birthday so they brought a delicious ice cream cake.
After singing Happy Birthday to her, she then announced that anyone who had
sung to her could stamp into a special box that was out near her car. Wow!
Did those kids ever stampede out to look for that one! (and it was a VERY
cool stamp).



The pleasure some of the kids got from creating Halloween foam craft
projects and one girl intently working on the Letterboxing Word Search
puzzle.



Watching the kids play with their glo-sticks out on the beach after it got
dark.



Cigna Cats coming back to report that they had found 29 boxes. Quite a feat
since they are expecting their first baby boxer!



LeapinLizards and Dragon returning from the trails at about 8:30 pm to find
a few boxes in the beach area and then heading out shortly before 9 pm to
find one more box. Since it gets dark by 7 or 7:30 pm, they definitely
were doing some flashlight boxing!

Re: [LbGLK] Re: Hauntingly goooood time!

From: SKJJJK (col.one923@spwl.net) | Date: 2005-10-03 23:27:56 UTC-05:00
Hello everyone!

We just wanted to add our thanks and thoughts on the gathering as well.
We had a great time! The kids had a blast! And Scout is completely wore
out! You know he was getting tired when he tired to "cat nap" each time
we were stamping in.

We approached this gathering with great anticipation and especially
looked forward to meeting some of the boxers we have never met before.
We've been boxing for 2 years now, hunting many boxes planted by WI
Hiker and Martini Man and this was our first opportunity to meet them
and it sure was a pleasure! It was nice to see boxers that we met last
year and it was even more fun to meet other boxers.

Here's a list of highlights and things that stood out for us....

The tremendous amount of effort in putting this gathering together was
very apparent! The blowing of the noon whistle, to call us in for the
photo and lunch, proves WI Hiker thought of everything! ;o)

The Haunted Harvest web site was very helpful to know what to expect for
the day. We informed the kids ahead of time to be on the look out for
secretly hidden boxes around the pavilion or even cooties. While we
didn't get any cooties, Thunder was the first to find the Psycho box and
then the kids started bringing us all these altoid tins! Thank You Mrs.
Doglvrs and Bindle Babe, our kids say finding all the altoids was one of
the best parts of the day for them.

Mrs. Doglvrs asked us for our first exchange of the day and it was so
much fun meeting her. We discussed later, on the way home, the dilemma
of wanting to box and yet wanting to use exchanges as an opportunity to
meet and chat with other boxers. We found it hard to do both. We got a
fair amount of exchanges, but the one regret we had over the day was
that we didn't get a chance to meet more than we did. In two years, we
have only been to two gatherings now and I think we will look at them as
a rare opportunity to meet and chat in future years. We can box every
other day of the year right? In a sentimental kind of way, we would
like other boxers to know how much we appreciate their efforts to plant
boxes, because letterboxing has become our family hobby and the focus of
most of our weekend recreation and vacation time. Our kids are young
and we are building memories and whether you folks realize it or not,
you and your letterbox plants are going to be forever etched in the
childhood memories that our kids take with them into adulthood. Meeting
the Dragon last year was a larger than life experience for our kids
because he sent us on that Tunnel No. 3 expedition. That letterbox hunt
stretched all of us especially me as a mom, as the kids kept saying
"mom, I can't believe you are letting us do this" as we walked the top
of that wall and then bushwhacked our way for what seemed like miles!
It is just impossible to express or share with you folks all of the very
special moments we have shared as a family because we were hunting for
your boxes. Each of our letterbox finds are scrapbooked with photos,
clues, journal notes etc. Okay enough of the sentimental mush....

We were delighted to stumble across Samara without even knowing of the
clue! After all those altoid tins, we just didn't have anytime to
strategize our letterbox hunt, so we just walked across the beach
thinking we were heading for a trail. When we met up with the closed
fence, we decided to circumvent the fence to get to the trail on the
other side. If anyone had fallen into the lake, that would have gone in
the books as a Puddlejumper high adventure event, but we all
successfully stayed dry. However, while discussing what we were going
to do with the fence in our way, I stuck my head in that stone column
and spied a suspicious looking stack of stones that only letterboxers
recognize! We stamped in thinking this day of letterboxing is going to
be easier than we thought.

We went on to Search for Scaredy Cat and came up empty handed! That
burst our bubble pretty quick. We did meet up with Punxsygal and
Aimlesst and we found Loser together.

It was about that time I remembered that in all the excitement I plugged
in the crock pot, but forgot to turn it on! Our sloppy Joes were cold.
Thankfully there was a microwave available.

Other highlights? A police officer pulled over at the side of the road
seemingly watching us as we were retrieving Beelzebub. But ,we didn't
see him until Mr. Puddlejumper, who prefers to stay on wide paths far
away from any poison ivy, told us after we emerged from the area. We
assume he decided a couple of middle aged people with young children
were not your typical troublemakers and he moved on without speaking to
us.

We really enjoyed meeting AJ out on the trail. We got a chance to talk
with her personally about our "high" adventure while searching for her
EZ Calf box.

Little Green Monkey found a woolly worm, adopted her, named her Lily and
brought her home. Unfortunately, we had tears when Lily escaped in the
car before we left for home. Once at home though, we found her and gave
her a nice home in a glass jar. Little Green Monkey plays with his
woolly worm all the time. He has discovered she can climb up his shirt,
but can't climb up the window.

The kids really enjoyed working on the crafts and especially enjoyed
playing on the beach with the other kids and the light sticks. Thanks WI
Hiker and Martini Man for everything!

Overall, we just had a great time. The chatter in the car on the way
home was lots of fun too. The kids had lots of fun getting exchanges
and meeting people. They talked nonstop about this exchange, that
letterboxer, etc. I also wanted to mention we really enjoyed meeting
and conversing with The Cottontails, Sophie and Me, the CC Family, Drama
Mama and the Cat Herders and others. A bit of trivia.....Can anyone
guess what Drama Mama, the Cat Herders, the Frog-a-Nobies and the
Puddlejumpers have in common? I'll give you a hint. Personally, the
Puddlejumpers' letterboxing jaunts have a dual purpose that make a
perfect fit with this commonality.

We waited until tonight to send this email, because we planned to return
today to get all the boxes we didn't get on Sat, including Scaredy Cat,
which still challenged us today! As always, when we found the right
spot, the clues made complete sense! We found another out of place
evergreen but couldn't make the clues work for that. . We completed
the circus series and all of the other boxes listed in the clue sheet.
After having time to study the clues a bit before returning today, we
found the hunt much easier. We even met JAB out on the trail again!
After Sat, finding Bluebird at Lake Kegonsa on Sunday and then the rest
of the Gathering boxes today, we added about 60 boxes to our count! The
count doesn't matter so much, but after hunting boxes placed in close
range like that, I think it will be hard to go back to regular boxing in
Wisconsin. It is also going to be hard to get the ink off our hands!

Thanks for a great time everyone!

The Puddlejumpers
Hyacinth
Blue Thunder
Little Green Monkey
and Scout
