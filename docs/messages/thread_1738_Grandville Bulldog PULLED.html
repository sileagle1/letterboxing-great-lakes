Grandville Bulldog PULLED

From: (papeseed@tds.net) | Date: 2005-08-21 14:31:37 UTC-05:00
Just in case anyone has printed clues off and intends to soon search for GRANDVILLE BULLDOG...stop! After reports that the rest area where it was hidden had been razed and access blocked due to road construction, I pulled the box this morning.

The former rest stop is a very cool location, and still accessible as of now via a back gravel road. However, it appears the statue of Jean Nicolet will likely soon be moved to a new area recently acquired and under preparatory construction by the Brown Co. Historical Society. I may re-hide the Bulldog there when it is finished, if an appropriate hiding spot is available. I intend to place a bonus stamp inside the box then, so all of you who have already found it will have an added incentive to search for it in its new location.

As for the old location...I'll be keeping my eye on that back road and what happens with it. I'd like to place another box there sometime if it remains semi-accesible to the public.

Stay tuned...and a big congratulations to those who found the Bulldog despite the construction!!

[Coincidentally, the box with the stamp image I carved of Jean Nicolet and sent to Doodle & Deedle Bug as part of the Hometown Landmarks exchange has gone missing. I am thinking dear departed Mr. Nicolet -- the French explorer who first landed at Green Bay and "discovered" Wisconsin -- is unhappy with what is going on at his memorial site, so has taken off exploring again. Perhaps I will create a hitchhiker, so he can keep on going...]

--Papeseed
