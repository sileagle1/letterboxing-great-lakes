Road Trip! ISO drive-by boxes GR to K'zoo

From: bindlestiff_boxer (bindlestiff_boxer@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-03-18 17:19:46 UTC
Next month is a long-planned letterboxing trip with a very dear friend
and member of our group...Mrs DogLvrs!

Our current plan is to spend an overnight in the K'zoo area hunting up
boxes, since this is pretty new boxing area for both of us, and it is
a "target rich" enviroment. (grin)

Does anyone know of any quick and easy drive-by boxes on the way
between Grand Rapids and K'zoo? You know when you put the two of us
together, we can just be driving along somewhere and find ourselves
overcome with the NEED to get out and find a box. Heck we even pulled
my laptop out of the trunk at a truck stop and paid for a quick
internet fix so we could find boxes on the way to the WI gathering
last year!

Also, and K'zoo area boxes that should go on our "must do" list?
Please keep in mind that one quarter of our team simply refuses to do
uneven ground such as sand dunes or hikes much more than a mile
round-trip.

Thanks in advance for any and all help in planning our Spring get-away
letterboxing adventure.

Sail on!
---Bindle Babe
(only 16 days to go and still counting down!)




Re: Road Trip! ISO drive-by boxes GR to K'zoo

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-03-21 13:03:18 UTC
I think most of the boxes en route between GR and Kalamazoo are at
Yankee Springs and not really drive-bys.

As far as Kalamazoo area boxes, my recommendations (taking into
consideration that I have not done every single box in the Kalamazoo
area)...

My absolute favs are the Slim boxes, but they may be a bit of a hike,
so other than those...

First in the Zoo
Zoo Two
Woods in the City I and II
Celery Flats
Asylum Birds
Asylum Lake Preserve

The last two are a bit further hikes, but I don't think a mile and no
significant hills -- a very pretty area though.

Have fun!

SpringChick

Do NOT do any boxes by KazooDog half are missing. I would suggest
Azobox's and if you want alot of boxes in one area I would go to
Saugatuck Dunes park!!!! Spring chick has some there and there is an
awesome walk on "The Spine of the Dragon" (if you do this one, just
a clue when in dought go up). You said some in the group don't like
to walk on dunes and stuff, well if these are adults you can drop
them off down the road in downtown Saugatuck. There are lots of
shops and places to grab a bite. The Saugatuck Park is a MUST see!!
I really loved this park, beautiful! Hope this helps!!
