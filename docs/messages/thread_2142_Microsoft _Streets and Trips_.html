Microsoft "Streets and Trips"

From: cdem_ltrbox (wp4@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2006-01-08 02:29:44 UTC
When we were at the Haunted Harvest Gathering this fall, Wisconsin
Hiker and Martini Man had their laptop out with a copy of
Microsoft "Streets and Trips", which is kind of like your own
personal Mapquest with memory and the ability to plot many
locations, not just one. I didn't think I would ever use anything
like that, but I was wrong! When I was digging through our computer
disks, I discovered that we already had the program from when we
bought our computer. I decided to give it a try and it has been
great.

I put in our house and the houses of various relatives in other
states with one color "thumbtack". Then I started plotting out
where various letterboxes in these (and nearby) counties were and
saved them with a different color "thumbtack" on the map. When we
went to Ohio for Christmas, I noticed that there were several
letterboxes near my folks--a perfect way to fill the time before all
the siblings and cousins showed up. When the kids and I went on our
big letterboxing trip to Lake County, I printed out a detailed
street map with all the letterboxes marked. We didn't get lost
once, and that is very good for me (usually, if my husband isn't
sure which way to turn, he asks me and then does the opposite). Now
if we are boxing, I can just print out a map and print out the
clues, instead of looking at all the clues and wondering where they
are and which ones are close to each other... Now I just check for
new letterboxes on the Web site and put them into this program if
they are in a county we visit.

Thanks to Wisconsin Hiker and Martini Man for making boxing a little
easier and convenient.

BirdWoman/Waltzing Pigs

Hey, glad to hear you like it! We found out about MS Streets & Trips from
LightinBug, a letterboxer in Pennsylvania. We've been using it since April
and it has been EXTREMELY useful. We use it all the time and make maps for
each area or state we will be visiting. It is much quicker and more
flexible than an on-line mapping tool. We use the basic pushpin symbol to
mark boxes we haven't found yet, then change the symbol to a little flag
once we have found the box. Unfortunately we sometimes just change the
pushpin color to show we looked for it but didn't find it. We've also found
the software useful in locating hotels near boxes we want to look for when
we are traveling. We usually print overview maps that show all the boxes to
hunt for as labeled pushpins, then detailed maps of the area near each box.
The one thing we're trying to remember to do is also print a map showing in
enough detail how to get from one box to another.

As a matter of fact, we used MS S&T today. We printed an overview map of the
area north of Milwaukee that had labeled pushpins for several boxes we
hadn't looked for yet - all MATHMAN boxes! Off we went, and we're happy to
report that we found Mathman 8, 11, 14 and 18. We're pretty sure we were at
the right spot for Mathman 2, but unfortunately did not find a box.

These were our first boxes of 2006 and Martini Man also got his first BINGO
word yesterday. I haven't crossed off one single thing on MY Bingo list
yet. Maybe I should have guessed a May date if everyone is off to as slow a
start as I am.... I didn't see a word posted for today yet though, and I
figured today was going to be my lucky day - maybe it will be!

Wisconsin Hiker
