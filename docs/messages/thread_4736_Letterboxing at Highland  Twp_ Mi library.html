Letterboxing at Highland Twp. Mi library

From: heather (heatherstreets@comcast.net) | Date: 2008-02-03 17:52:32 UTC
Last week I participated in a LB program at the Highland Twp. Library.
The librarian is a beginning letterboxer, so she asked me to show my
stamps and logbooks. She had the kids make logbooks, and they
brought their own stamps.
It was a neat way for the kids to learn about the library and have
fun. Some of the kids were so into it.

I attached an email I just got from her.
Looks like it was a big success and she is interested in possibly
having an event there!

Heather,

I wanted to thank you again for your help with the Letterboxing
program! We had SO MUCH positive feedback from both the kids and the
parents that I think we will need to do another program sometime.

You mentioned that there are letterboxing events. I wonder if we
could arrange something here that would have a family friendly
focus. We could then possibly invite any interested patrons. I am
just thinking out loud...I have not talked to my supervisor to see if
she would even allow such a thing. However, we do have a great
community room where the letterboxers could meet to talk and exchange
ideas, etc. We also have a nice outdoor space.

I know you are possibly starting a new job so you may not be up for
anything more. If you are available though I would love to ask for
your help with any future programs. It was very kind of you to drive
all the way here and good of your husband to babysit while we had the
class. The kids really enjoyed seeing your logbook and stamps and
the stories of your adventures inspired both parents and children to
give letterboxing a try!

Brenda Dunseth
Youth Services Librarian
Highland Township Public Library
