From: lcoil79@ameritech.net smokey (ainawgsd@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-06-04 14:56:36 UTC-07:00
Well, Aina tried to eat someone every day of the weekend except Sunday (she's usually good, but she can get fear-aggressive in the car apparently...next time she comes with us we'll have to remember to be sure to roll the back windows up enough she can't get her head out). But at least on Sunday she let several people pet her on the trail!
Then there was Sunday night. I think we were the only ones left in that part of the campground. Even the geocachers in the big RV in site M10 had left when we got back to camp. It was kind of eerie and strangely quiet after being full all weekend. We bought enough firewood to get a good bonfire going and even scavenged some wood from the empty fire pits in the M loop. Then it started to rain, then let up a bit, and then it poured! We found out that our tent is still waterproof (mostly, the seams around the windows need resealing). Around midnight (local time) the thunder was so loud that we decided we'd be better off breaking camp and wasting a few hours at Cracker Barrel. So we started packing our junk inside the tent and waited for the rain to let up. As soon as it did, we got everything else together and broke camp at 1am. Then we were off to Cracker Barrel, which was open 24 hours last time I ate at one, only to find that they had JUST closed and wouldn't be
opening again until 6am! So, with only about 4 hours of sleep, we decided that we should skip the boxes we had planned on for the morning (the ones by the dog park :( and a grail box in the Dayton area) and drove straight home, bypassing all of the construction traffic along the way. Ironically, by the time 6am Eastern rolled around, we had literally just crossed the Illinois state border and the local Cracker Barrel was still closed for another hour. We kept looking for an IHOP or something similar along the way, but breakfast just wasn't to be. Pulled into the driveway at 6am Central time and collapsed until later this morning.

Even with the rain and the cranky dog it was a wonderful weekend! We had a great time thanks to our wonderful hosts and all the great letterboxers who attended.

Kimono Girl
