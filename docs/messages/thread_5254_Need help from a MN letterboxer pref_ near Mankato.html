Here's a cross post from the North East area boxers..... lets hope the MN
park rangers take a page out of the MA rangers guide book;

Hi Folks,

Some exciting news! I don't know if anyone has mentioned this, but there are
some really terrific hand-made stamps that have just been put out by the MA
DCR - they have management of many parks in MA. The series was produced by 2
rangers that were inspired by the CT park service and their passport
program.

The stamps are part of a 'passport' promotion encouraging families to visit
the vast system of parks. They are hand-made by a children's illustrator and
they are all self inked. The passport books are available free from the DCR
at parks and on the website. I have visited over 31 sites with my
letterboxing buddy Ginny. It is actually better to use your own paper or
letterbox journal as the stamps are large and they bleed through the
passport book paper (and are hard to line up). There are 5 regions with 76
stamps in total. If you complete sections, you can get a t-shirt with the
stamps on it. We were the first to complete the central region!

Here is the website for all of you that are interested:

_http://www.mass.http://www.mass.http://www._
(http://www.mass.gov/dcr/passport/index.html)

There are pictures of the stamps online. We have bumped into Geo cachers - I
wanted to let the letterboxing folks know about this - the stamps are more
like our hobby. Also, they are drive-bys- you find the box at the park and
open it with a combination listed in the passport book.

It's a lot of fun and sometimes quite challenging. Some of the parks have
admission fees, but I have taken out park passes at my library or asked the
folks at the entrance to stamp in. Most of the parks are really cool places
to know about.

I hope we bump into some letterboxers.

Best regards,

Jil
