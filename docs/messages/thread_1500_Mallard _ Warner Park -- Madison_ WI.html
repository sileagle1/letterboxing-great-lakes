Mallard / Warner Park -- Madison, WI

From: m_clan_lbers (joshmonty@hotmail.com) | Date: 2005-06-24 01:39:20 UTC
Last fall, a box popped up for Warner Park in the Madison area. It
was called something along the lines of "Home of the Mallards". We (M
Clan) tried to find it shortly after it popped up and found the spot,
searched trees everywhere, almost bumped into a homeless man sleeping
in the woods, but never found the box. A copule days later the
placer's said the box was missing. Dragon, are there any plans to
resurrect the box as part of the WI Letterboxes of Antiquity. I'm not
sure anyone ever found the box and I'm not sure who the placers were,
but we'd be willing to help relocate the box within Warner Park if the
clues are out there still.

M Clan

From: (col.one923@spwl.net) | Date: 2005-06-23 21:23:35 UTC-05:00
I remember that! Take a look at this archived thread where we asked a
question about permission to place boxes...thread starts with post #
2163. In that thread with post # 2176, blueheron492002 responded and at
the time I think I made the connection that they, The Madcats were
referring to the Warner Park box. I don't remember the name of the box
now either. I don't know that they have planted any other boxes, but I
think they are still out letterboxing? I don't know.

Anyway, maybe that bit of info will help. The box I would like to see
replaced is the E-Way Trails Letterbox. It was our first find and I saw
that it was also WI Hiker and Martini Man's first find as indicated
here http://firstfinds.blogspot.com/ We would be willing to do that,
but just haven't yet contacted the original placer about resurrecting
that box. I just remember the excitement when we first learned about
letterboxing, checked the list and found E-Way Trails was so close to
home. We buzzed over there that very day and found it. We were hooked
after that.

The Puddlejumpers

From: (col.one923@spwl.net) | Date: 2005-06-24 08:40:22 UTC-05:00
Oh, that is good news! We will definitely look forward to that. Since
we didn't even have a stamp yet the first time we found it, we will want
to go find it again.

I guess I haven't looked at your (Dragon) antiquity page recently or
haven't looked at it close enough. I always wondered what happened to
the Castle Rock box since we didn't have a chance to find it. We were
"up north" over Memorial Day Weekend and found both Elk Mound and Bell
Mound. It was fun to read the entries in the Bell Mound logbook since
they went all the way back to 2002. 2002 doesn't seem all that long
ago, but I think that was one of the oldest boxes we had found to date.
Dragon's entry mentioned the MPs at Castle Rock and I thought he was
kidding! I think that experience might have scared us away from the
letterboxing game altogether! Wow!
