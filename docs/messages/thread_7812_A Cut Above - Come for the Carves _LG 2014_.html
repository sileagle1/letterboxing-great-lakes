A Cut Above - Come for the Carves

Great Lakes Gathering 2014

Saturday, June 28, 2014

9 am – 6 pm

Wild River State Park

Center City, Minnesota

hosted by the Minnesota Carving Consortium (MCC, formerly the Minnesota Camping Contingent)

Come to Minnesota; Come for the Carves!  The 2014 Great Lakes Gathering will feature the best stamps that each letterboxer can submit. 

The naturalist at Wild River is excited about letterboxing because seekers do not destroy an entire area when out boxing.  We have been given permission to permanently plant 6 boxes at the park.  We are allowed to temporarily plant 24 boxes at the park.  Wild River SP has 35 miles of trail along the St. Croix River.  Minnesota state parks have very strict rules about letterbox placement and we want to continue the excellent relationship that we've started at Wild River.  All permanent boxes will have to be planted by the MCC.  We can and will plant boxes outside the state park in a nearby county park, along a bike trail, etc.

Donate Stamps: So, sharpen your carving tools!  We would like stamps submitted by March 1st (so we have time to make fabulous logbooks to go with them).  All ability levels are welcome.  There will be a special carved-by-kids section, too.  Carve any image, but keep the images family-friendly.  Send the stamps to Dartmoor Dreamer; 2887 Lake Blvd; St. Paul MN  55109-1620. 

Donate Cash: At Great Lakes Gatherings in the past, there has been a donation bucket available (to help defray expenses for shelter rental, printing, containers and mailing).  For this event, the MCC is suggesting a donation of $10 or more per adult letterboxer.  The MCC is offering an incentive to those that send their donations before May 1st.  What kind of incentive, you ask?  (packet available on Friday night and a special WOM clue).  Any money collected beyond 2014 expenses will be donated to the 2015 GLG organizing  group.  Contact Dartmoor Dreamer for payment options DartmoorDreamer@hotmail.com.

Important Info: This event and the positive interaction with the Wild River SP naturalist are the first steps in educating all Minnesota State Park personnel about the low environmental impact and family-friendly nature of letterboxing.  We are counting on everyone to make this a great experience at the park and for the park staff.  We want to continue to expand letterboxing legally in Minnesota State Parks.
