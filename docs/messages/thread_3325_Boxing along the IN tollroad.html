Boxing along the IN tollroad

From: cdem_ltrbox (wp4@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2006-11-15 20:31:20 UTC
Last week, we went on a little road trip from Chicago to Pokagon
State Park in Indiana. Along the way, we picked up a few boxes.
That mapping feature on AQ was nice for plotting out some boxes.

Our first stop was a pretty little park to pick up "Century of
Progress", by the Fairy Stamper. We didn't recognize the many
Hoosier stamps, but it was nice to see a few stamps we recognized
from our neck of the woods. The stamp in the box was quite nice.
Then it was on to Maple Woods Nature Center for 2 wonderful little
boxes by Tiptoe & Tonto (I liked the stamps and really liked how
they did the covers of the log books). The entire forest was
blanketed with fallen leaves, so it was a challenge to find and stay
on the trail for the "Fallen Leaf" box--but we managed it. Then it
was on to the Sap Bucket Box. We were surprised to see the word
Letterbox freshly carved into a tree, with a pointing arrow.
Fortunately, it was pointing in the wrong direction for the box
(which was in fine shape). We poked around in the direction of the
arrow, thinking perhaps there was a Bonus box--which there wasn't.
Tiptoe & Tonto were surprised to hear of the carving, so not sure
who did this. Ball Magnet wanted to try and remove the carved word,
but we figured that would do too much damage to the tree, esp since
the only tools we had were rocks and sticks.

We stayed at Pokagon State Park--gorgeous park. I vaguely remember
someone on the list proposing this park as a possible gathering
site. I would definitely vote "thumbs up" to that. The inn at the
park is wonderful and is right on the beach of Lake James. We
didn't get out on a lot of the trails, but the ones we were on were
nice. When we were at the Nature Center, they had a display about
Geocaching, so they obviously allow these types of activity. We
looked for the "Family Reunion" letterbox without success, but I did
add 2 new birds to my life list so that was good!!

They have a 1/4-mile toboggan run that operates all winter, so the
kids definitely want to make a return trip.

Not a lot of boxes found, but a nice little trip.

BirdWoman/Waltzing Pigs
