April 15 - The Cat in the Hat

From: Adam E (Aatom118@gmail.com) | Date: 2019-04-15 07:05:47 UTC-05:00
We had a new baby,
But we still wanted boxes to do.
So we’re starting some series,
Of plants that were new.

Each of them was doable,
With a stroller on the go.
I guess we should clarify,
Just so you will know.

The conveyance we used,
Was not of the umbrella style.
You had to use something for trails,
For these trips some took awhile.

They were not drive-bys,
So don’t get confused.
To help you along,
There was a scale that we used.

(1) were the most basic,
A paved trail you would find.
Then we got to (2),
If unpaved paths you didn't mind.

When we got into (3),
You might thought us crazy.
As you would find,
To do these you couldn't be lazy.

On this scale of ours,
You could count on bumps, that they’ve got.
Or if they are ‘smooth’,
Then that meant flat they were not.

At (4) we ended our scale,
To note trails beyond three.
How can we describe them,
For how difficult could they really be?

Now are babies have grown, 
The stroller has gone to disuse,
We wish for fun travels ahead.
As they both grow into youth. 

N - Strollerboxing

When families attend my informational letterboxing sessions, I use the term strollerboxing with your family in mind.  I hope attendees are encouraged to start letterboxing while their children are young.  And, we always have a few boxes planted for youngsters to find to prove strollerboxing is possible.

Thoughtful poem today- I am sure your family has some endearing memories tucked away from these days.
