Ok, for the record, let's just get the facts straight.

Yes, the original poster acknowledged that he had no affiliation with
the commercial firm touted in the message. But that was *not* known
from the outset.
Since the poster was a new member to this group and Yahoo groups have
been beset by auto/spam membership requests (and subsequent ads for
who-knows-what), we always review both the membership request for its
authenticity *and* the member's first post. And, yes, trolls do join
under the cover of well-meaning letterboxers. And, yes, YOU were
likely moderated on your first post.
All first posts by new members are now moderated.

So when the question was asked, the poster immediately disavowed any
commercial relationship with the company mentioned, and the post went
through.
End of story.

It is a common practice on lists like this for posters to acknowledge
whether or not they stand to gain financially from mentioning a business.
Is it a rule? No.
However, it's a courtesy to disclose those relationships up front.

It's not unusual to read mention of specials, coupons, or someone's
favorite carving medium's source, but it is deceptive if one stands to
gain financially and that fact is not public.
