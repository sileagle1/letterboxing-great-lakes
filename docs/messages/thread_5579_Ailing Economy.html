Re: [LbGLK] Re: Ailing Economy

From: Carol Osada (cjosada@hotmail.com) | Date: 2009-01-25 22:01:34 UTC-06:00
WI HI - Sorry to hear about the layoff. Ailing economy is an understatement!

After 37 years, my husband was part of the fourth wave of layoffs/reductions at his company. At least he had received his lump sum pension in hand previously, and then received a severance package at the time of his layoff. That was last April! And he's not finding any offers. Those head hunters who use to call and call and call aren't calling anymore; businesses aren't hiring and the head hunters probably lost their jobs too. Didn't think those head hunter calls would ever go away, but they did. It's a scary time.

The problem in this country is that we have been outsourcing jobs to foreign countries for so many years in the interest of corporate profits vs lower payrolls and no benefits, that there simply aren't any good jobs left in this country. High tech jobs have gone oversees. Made in the USA is not something we see anymore. This country has some major policy decisions that need to be addressed, and jobs and the future economy should be at the top of the list. No single answer. But those of us who still have a job are glad to have a job even if it's not the ideal job or what we were looking to do long term.

Businesses want us to spend but that's not easy to do if you don't have income and you don't dare run up those charge cards. We're just holding our own.
