Re: [LbGLK] Re: patches

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2007-01-16 21:17:59 UTC-05:00
I sew mine on also -- this way they are transferable if and when I ever come across that perfectly designed backpack. I just use a couple of straight pins to hold the patch securely in the position I want it, then whipstitch around the edges in a thread color compatible with the border color. I have found that the heavy duty type thread or quilting thread holds up best to repeated washing -- if using regular thread, try double-thickness. It takes less than 10 minutes (okay a little longer if you count the time it takes for these old eyes to thread the darn needle).

SpringChick

From: Scoutdogs (DogScouts@hotmail.com) | Date: 2007-01-16 22:16:26 UTC-05:00
Patch Attach! My dogs have many badges and I DON'T sew :-) Patch attach allows you to attach the patch with an iron and if you want to remove it later- reheat and peel it off. It will leave a mark on the fabric, but the patch won't be effected and can be attached to another fabric.

You can get it for less than $5 in Wal-mart or most craft/fabric stores.
