Good Manners

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2007-06-12 21:26:18 UTC
Just wanted to let anyone reading this board (especially the
Investigator) that good manners seem to be alive and well in the Great
Lakes region. In the span of 2 days I received 7 "Contact the Placer"
messages regarding 6 of my boxes. Several of them are from very new
boxers and it is so nice to hear from them and know they care about the
finer points of letterboxing etiquette!

Wisconsin Hiker

From: perdu_watcher (perdu_watcher@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-06-13 00:24:52 UTC
I never really thought about it until recently when it was brought up
on one board or another about making comments when logging in a find
(though I don't log all my finds), I suppose I thought it was there if
there was a problem, but that the logbook was for making the
comments. I am learning and trying to make a comment with each log,
when I log them, even if it is to say "Thanks," because people don't
have to plant boxes for us and it is awful-gosh-darn-nice when they do
so that we can hike out there and find them.

Thanks for the reminder of the importance of being a well-mannered-
boxer.

~Perdu

Re: [LbGLK] Re: Good Manners

From: (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2007-06-12 19:43:25 UTC-05:00
Well I don't log my finds on-line (but Martini Man does). However we almost always send a little note about whether the box was in good shape (or not) and any other comments we might have (such as if we particularly liked the stamp, hike, view, etc) A lot of time the notes are very short.
As Placers of boxes we do like the fact that people let us know they found a box by logging it as Found and/or sending us a note. Some do neither, some do one or the other and some do both. The notes are more fun to read, but even the logging lets us know if the box is OK. This is important to us since we have planted 90 boxes and we don't do regular maintenance on all of them. Instead I keep a log of when each box was last found, and those that haven't had activity in a while are those that we check (of coursed in addition to any that someone has reported a problem with).
Since we don't see the logbooks real often, it is especially nice to get the little "Contact the Placer" notes. Here are some we received this week
=========
Hello Wisconsin Hiker,

Today we found our first letterbox and want to thank you for a great hiking experince. Your box is clever. We enjoyed the goat.
=========
Just wanted to let you know that we found your box on June 10. It was a long hike, but a good one! Our kids were just about to poop out, then we found the box and they livened back up again! Someone else had found it in October and had placed a "hitchhiker" in the box! The "Traveling Pants" hitchhiker started in Wisconsin last year and has made its way around WI before jumping the river into IA in Oct. We are planning on taking it to either IL or SD. Thanks for a great historical recount and a challenging letterbox hike!
========
We forgot our compass, but didn't have much problem with the box. Thanks for the plant!
======
we are sad to say it seems your site for this letterbox has turned into a party site. The place matching your clues exactly is now home to a fire pit and many empty booze bottles and beer cans. We dug around as much of the rusted stuff that we could, but it's apparent they cleared out a circle in the middle of the rusty fenced area and made a fire pit. There was a bag of fresh fire wood and several new chairs. We scoured the area and found no letterbox among the rusty stuff.

We truly hope we were in the wrong spot, but I don't think we were. :(
=============
Wisconsin Hiker
