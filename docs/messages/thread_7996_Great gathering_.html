From: Atom118 (Aatom118@gmail.com) | Date: 2014-06-30 09:41:22 UTC-05:00
Hats of to the Minnesotans!

The Atomic Beans had a wonderful time over the weekend, and now we are
all tired!

We arrived on Friday afternoon and quickly bumped into a few of our
hosts. Tie Dye Sue quickly put me on notice when she told me to keep
the ticks I found (on myself) so I could win her twisted childhood
game. (EEK!) I'm happy to say, that turned out not to be a problem for
the Atomic Beans (although we did collectively find three) as much as
the mosquitos. [Our poor children are a bit tasty for the bugs is
seems, and no matter how many how many layers of spray/wipes we applied,
the children are polka dotted today. Fortunately, it didn't seem to
phase them much.]

We started Friday afternoon (after wrestling with the new tent) by
pondering Pingo....kinda like Bingo but with stamps hidden at the
various pottys around camp. It also proved to be a nice opportunity to
bump into several familiar (and new) faces around the campgrounds. We
had already had a long day to get to MN, so we going to turn in early
after a festive campground dinner...but oops...kids like the smores, and
they were on a bit of a sugar high afterwards. Fortunately, we bumped
into the younger portions of the Boston Terrorists at the nearby
sandbox...and the kids had a fun time in the sand and doing daredevil
jumps off the large rocks. That did help to spend a bit of the kids
energy, but as some of you might have noticed while walking to the MN
pregathering campsite, it didn't zap them off all of their energy ("BUT
I WANT TO STAY UP LATE!").

Saturday morning started out beautifully. We walked from the campsite
over to the gathering and managed a FF along the way. After a few brief
"hellos" we set off on the trails. We did a few sets by ourselves
before meeting Martini Man, Wisconsin Hiker, Hartx6, the Hills, and
Lewis (of Lewis and Clark) on the trails. The stamps were great, and the
company was festive.

We returned to the shelter for lunch (Dartmoor Dreamer asked if we found
any Bonus Boxes "DOHT, WE KNEW SHE LIKES BB...OOPS!") at which point we
were already a bit wiped out (oh how the children have made us soft).
Fortunately, there was a 'kids only' section near the shelter and we
were happy to send our oldest off to box while the younger one napped so
we could have a few moments to ourselves.

The sky was seemingly starting to darken, so we opted to go try some of
the 'area' boxes and do some driving while the kids relaxed in the AC
and watched TV (BTW, it has been awhile since I noticed/loved AC as much
as after hiking several miles with kids in tow and then jumping into a
cool car). Hartx6 had invited us to dinner in North Branch so after a
few finds we took him up on his offer...and just in time. As we entered
the restaurant, the skies opened up. "We did zip up the tent, right?!"
After 30-60mins of torrential rain (sometimes coming in nearly
horizontal), the rain slackened. At this point we were unsure if our
campsite would still be there when we returned (Hartx6 recalled stories
of gatherings past filled with tornadoes and naked DD).

Fortunately, we had zipped up the tent and our campsite was rather dry
(at least inside). The kids zonked out after playing a bit more, and we
followed soon thereafter.

Given the long drive home, we packed our wet gear and started the long
drive south...tired, but happy. Sorry for the long report, but needless
to say, a WONDERFUL gathering! On another side note, our campsite
letterbox turned out to be a very nice excuse to meet several new
people....it is much easier to identify unfamiliar letterboxers as they
were finding our letterbox!

So, anyone volunteer for 2015? It seems like IL might be up in the
rotation...the Atomic Beans has formed an exploratory committee to do a
feasibility study if no one has claimed dibs yet.

A of Atomic Beans
