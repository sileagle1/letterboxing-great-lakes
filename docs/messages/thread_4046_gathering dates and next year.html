I've been thinking about the size and nature of future gatherings as well.
I plan to take notes at the Cluez In for logistic purposes. Indiana
hasn't hosted "the big one" yet either. And now that I know next year's
will fall on the last Saturday in May, I may have trouble attending. But
I'll worry about that later.

Originally I was going to see about hosting one some year at Pokagon SP.
It's easy to get to from the Toll Road, has an Inn and restaurant,
campsites, trails and water activities. And my family's lake house within
easy driving distance. But there is a lot of space that is "off-limits"
to letterboxes and geocaches. With the potential number of attendees
doubled what I originally anticipated, I don't think Pokagon would be the
best site.

I will still go and talk to someone this summer and get their impressions
and expectations for a large group gathering. I will also look into Brown
County and Turkey Run. I think they are the largest state parks in
Indiana. Another thought would be to look into Whitewater State Park,
just south of me. Maybe not the most spectacular park, but it would be
central to the boxing mecca of Cincinnati and all those boxes in Rush
County, IN.

If there are others that want to help me plan a big one, I will need it.
I think the park system here allows one-year-in-advance reservations. We
might be a little late in getting something for 2008. I will be at Clear
Lake some time during the first 2 weeks of July. We're planning to day
trip into Michigan a couple of times. We'll also be going to Louisville
for a couple of days. I could host an organizational meeting some time
later this summer while the water toys are still available.

This may become an issue as we move forward.
As groups become larger, the capacity of any park to handle gatherings
in a way that fits with the discreet nature of letterboxing becomes
problematic (not to mention Leave No Trace).

I am *NOT* suggesting any change in the way we meet or gather at this
point, but I'm more and more aware of the impact that large groups
have on any environment.

The Great Lakes Annual Gathering will always be "The Big One" for our
region.

Worth pondering.
