Re: Ren Faire on August 17??

From: calico_krys (krystal_lazuli@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2013-07-24 16:09:39 UTC
I am there every day. :-) Just check at the Friends of Faire garden as I'm usually there periodically throughout the day. I can send you a PM with my phone number, but I don't always hear it. DO check in with Bristol Stitch at Mockingbird Studios. If you need help, she is the go to person as she planted the boxes this year. There are also at least 2 others who are there most days that I know of.

I highly suggest you invest in a Friends of Faire 'day pass' at the garden. For the cost of $5 per person, you get access to the garden which has doubled in size this year so plenty of space to sit, relax and eat meals without dealing with the crowds. The cost also includes FREE water and gatorade (bring a mug or bottle to refill). That alone is worth the $5 as water to purchase is $2.50 a bottle at the faire. They also have clean privies with usually no lines. Great value!

Calico Krys

From: Krys (krystal_lazuli@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2013-07-24 18:36:49 UTC
I have heard RenQuest is fun - it is a hunt (like letter boxing) so you might not have time for it and boxing in the same day, although the boxes are easier to find this year. They also have the past two years boxes out so if your CO friend does not have those, might be worth finding as well.

Pub Crawl --- I've seen it - loud and raucous. They all have fun, I'm just not a beer drinker so not my "cup of tea". But they hit each pub and get a sample at each one. If you like beer, it is a fun event.

I do a few boxes a week, so I tend to wander about quite a bit just seeing things, shopping, visiting with friends, catching a few shows and just having fun. :-)

Krys

Re: Ren Faire on August 17??

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2013-08-21 03:22:21 UTC
We had a great day at the Faire last Saturday and wish to extend a BIG thank you to Dragon, BristolStitch and Gibson Girl for the very excellent carvings!

Merlin had done an excellent job of preparation and had mapped the likely location of all the boxes. As a result, we were able to find 34 boxes on Saturday! It probably also helped that we had 5 people/brains searching. We surprised another LBer (AltChar) when she was replacing a box we were looking for - this meant we also got an unexpected exchange. We missed seeing Calico Krys, but did run into Gibson Girl and saw BristolStitch in her shop.

It had been 10 years since we had been to the Faire and we won't wait that long again. There is so much to see, and in our quest for boxes we didn't see as much of the entertainment as we could have. However we did enjoy a few performances, some food, and managed to make a few purchases. I particularly liked seeing all the human-powered amusement rides!

Thanks again to those who have planted or assisted with boxes at the Faire!

Wisconsin Hiker
