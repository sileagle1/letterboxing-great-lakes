It was a gorgeous day in West Michigan, sunny and on the cool side -
perfect for walking in the woods. So I took the challenge of the
week and headed out with OldShoe to plant 3 boxes that have been
sitting on my dining room table for a while now. No mystery boxes
in the lot, but not your typical follow-the-direction clues either.

These three boxes are all located in the Whitehall/Montague area, in
northern Muskegon county.

From: aquamarine31577 (karajmcc@aol.com) | Date: 2004-08-23 20:07:26 UTC
Fortunately I was free yesterday, and with my mom in tow we set out
to find your three new letterboxes. We had a lot of fun deciphering
the visual clues, and absolutely loved the books you've created to go
with these stamps. Of all the years we've lived in the Muskegon
area, my mom and I had never been on the White River Trail, had never
hiked in the vicinity of East Meets West, and had never visited
Meinert Park (and I even worked for the Muskegon County Parks system
for a couple of years!). We were the first finders for all three
boxes (a thrill for us), and we even found a geocaching box along the
way (stamped before us by Bindle Babe & Bum). We also found the
Indian Point stamp (that one proved a bit of a challenge) and were
surprised to find ourselves at another Muskegon County location we
had never visited. That's one of the things I'm loving about
letterboxing - visiting places (sometimes so close to home) that I
hadn't visited or didn't know about. What fun!

Thanks, SpringChick -
Keltic Kara (and Sisu Sue)

Congrats, Kara! At the moment, I remain a first finder virgin. (grin)

Bindle Bum and I haven't been 100% the last few days. The story on
the front page of the Chronicle Saturday about the Deputy and a
passing motorist pulling a woman from a burning van to safety
involves a member of my family.

Sunday during her surgeries, we took a break from the situation at
the hospital and drove up here to distract ourselves with a quick
look in search of East Meets West. Even though we were pretty sure
we had the clues down, we saw no box. Unless the two women sitting
on the side of the trail, down the way, were you and your mom and
the box was out of its hiding spot for a few minutes ???? (of course
you all know it took 3 tries for me to get the darn Indian Point
box, so we can't discount that I wasn't looking in exactly the right
place!)

We were also unsuccessful with the White Trail box. Interesting
thing, here ... one of the 3 boxes I have placed recently; but don't
have the clues posted for; is also in this area. How fun that you
also found the geocache. (BTW, there's another cache near where the
clues start, too.)

We didn't attempt the little flower box in the park.

Looking forward to hunting for all these new boxes being placed. I
may not have time to finish up some super stelthy clues, and end up
putting some 'plain old follow the directions' clues up instead.

Enjoy!

From: aquamarine31577 (karajmcc@aol.com) | Date: 2004-08-25 01:52:08 UTC
PJ - You and Bindle Bum provided a lot of excitement for me and my
mom (we felt like spies!). We were indeed the two women sitting
alongside of the trail at East Meets West. We were suspicious that
you and Bindle Bum were out at the same time we were. We had found
the geocash near the White River Trail Box before finding the actual
letterbox and saw your stamp in that logbook. At that point we were
sure that you were ahead of us and had gotten to the WRT letterbox
first, and were surprised (and a bit thrilled) that we found it
first.

We were at East Meets West with the box in our lap when we noticed a
suspicious couple (you) further down the trail. I remember walking
past Bindle Bum and saying "Hi" - taking him for a casual stroller.
At that poilready had the box out and were trying to be discrete.
When we saw you leave one side of the trail and appear down the road
a bit at the other road crossing we were pretty sure it was you and
we wanted to approach you to exchange stamps, but we didn't have the
box rehidden yet and didn't want to give away its location. You
know, in my haste to rehide the box I forgot to put the stamp back in
it's baggie. I figured we'd find you further down the trail waiting
for us to vamoose, and that I'd be able to pass off the baggie to you
to put back in the box! (Dont' worry, SpringChick, I went back to
put the stamp in its baggie ;) You were definitely close, but you
didn't pass by the hiding spot while my mom and I had the box in our
possession. We were actually wondering if you hadn't decided to go
on to Meinert Park to try to be the first finder at Little Flower
Creek, figuring that someone had beaten you as first finders to East
Meets West. When we got to the park we scoped out the parking lot
trying to find a vehicle similar to the one we saw drive away at EMW
with no luck.

Amongst all our excitement, I'm sorry to hear that your visit to
Muskegon was prompted by a family member in the hospital. We saw the
article in the paper...I hope she's going to be okay.

Best wishes, and thanks for the excitement. I'm looking forward to
finding your upcoming boxes as well.

Peace,
Keltic Kara
