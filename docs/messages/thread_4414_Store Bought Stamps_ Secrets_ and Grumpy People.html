Sorry about that one Kimono girl! It bothers me that someone took my stamp, but I always just assumed it was an un-informed geocacher or new letterboxer. Thank you for the compliments on my stamps! Just in case someone hasn't seen my work and wants to, take a look at my Picture Trail. www.PictureTrail.com/scoutdogs I've pulled my stamps (except in TX) so you won't get a spoiler.

I never cared for the store bought stamps when I was letterboxing. When I started, I had never attempted carving anything before, but before I tried placing a box, I decided to try carving. All the stamps in my area were hand carved. What I found through letterboxing has been a real LOVE of carving. I find it very zen and a good way to relax. So, as the saying goes... TRY IT, you might like it! or more modern... "Just DO it!" :-) And don't feel like no one will like your carving because it isn't perfect. That's NOT the case!
