From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-08-10 16:32:09 UTC
I would hate to have to choose between placing and finding, but if I
did... I would have to agree with you. I love coming up with a
theme, researching the history of a place or a person, creating a
stamp image, making a logbook, tramping through the woods looking
for just the right place, and creating a variety of different clues.

As for finding... I especially enjoy the boxes with hikes and those
such as mystery boxes, that require reasearch and/or puzzle-solving.

Good thing we all do this for different reasons, or there wouldn't
be that balance between placing and finding boxes.

From: Kathi LK Krankoski (krank@ameritech.net) | Date: 2004-08-11 00:29:39 UTC-05:00
I do, because I don't have to be smart enough to figure out everyone else's
clues!!!

Well, that's part of it...!

For me, placing is less of an out-of-the-house time committment. I can
carve my stamps, write up my clues, prepare my boxes, etc. any time of the
day...like now, it's 11:30 pm...safe and sound at home. My available time
to go wandering is very limited (I work lots of weekends), and as stated
many times before, my husband is not really crazy about me galavanting all
over tarnation! So I can use bits of time to place my own, and live
vicariously through you guys finding them! I enjoy going out to check on my
babies when I can, and when I do get out to actually find someone else's
boxes, it's an even bigger treat!

This Friday night I'm having my GS troop over for a carving workshop (blind
leading the blind!) and to teach them the rules of boxing. After I take
them out to find my local boxes, it will be a good excuse to plan excursions
elsewhere to find other boxes!
