Illinois Treasure Hikers

From: Stacy (stjohn116@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2009-05-07 12:39:15 UTC
I don't know if you guys have been following the chat on AQ about the pathtags or not, but I wanted to let you know that I will be coordinating for Illinois.

Here are the basics:

1. You can earn pathtags (quarter sized medallions) for hiking in Illinois. There will be tags for 25, 50, and 100 miles. More later if there is interest.
2. You do not have to be a resident, but miles must be hiked in Illinois. Yes, your kids and your dog can earn the pathtags.
3. Hiking must be done after Illinois' start date of 4/24/09. Each hike must be a minimum of 1 mile. You cannot string shorter hikes together. You can however, use partial miles. 1.6, 2.4, etc...no rounding up though.
4. All miles must be in pursuit of New letterboxes, either finds or plants. Maintenance does not count.
5. Once you have 25 (or more), email me here or at AQ and send me a list of the miles and the boxes that qualify the miles. I'll do a quick check (I'm not going to be the TH police though) to verify. Then I can mail your tags. There is a small fee ($3) to cover costs, but believe me, I will not be making any money on this!

If you want more information, you can visit the website that Dixie created for the Treasure Hiking: http://www.dixiegraphix.com/treasurehikers/main.html.
There is also a message board on AQ just for Treasure Hiking.
I'd be happy to answer any questions that you might have, as well.

I really hope that many of you will be encouraged to get out and hike, find and plant new letterboxes, and enjoy this opportunity. Our pathtags have been ordered and I will post a picture just as soon as they arrive. Meanwhile, you can look at the bluepring here: http://www.atlasquest.com/gallery/viewphoto.html?gPhotoId=69531

Stacy

We started our Treasure Hike program here in Ohio as well on
3.01.09---with all of the same basics with one exception. We decided to
do patches rather than pathtags. Mama Stork, becohio and I are the
state motivators and if anyone would like to start a program in their
Great Lake state that does not already have one, please let any of us
know and we will get you started! It has already had a wonderful impact
on getting folks to do more hiking on trails either for finds or placing
of letterboxes in Ohio---and in other states as well!!

I'm glad you posted this here Stacy! I should have done the same a
couple of months ago!! Thank you. =D
