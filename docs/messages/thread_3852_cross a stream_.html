From: xxxxxxxx (BrighidFarm@comcast.net) | Date: 2007-03-15 18:38:26 UTC-05:00
As regards posting warnings in clues.........

Something for new folks to always keep in the back of one's mind is when the
box was planted.

Waterfall Glen in Darien, IL comes to mind. It has some wildly fluctuating
terrain. During the fall and late winter (especially if it's been a winter
with little snow cover), there are many parts that can be easily walked on
and one's tootsies will never come in touch with a single drop of water.
During the spring, those same areas will have a person sinking, oh, maybe
ankle deep in mud. In summer, those areas will be knee deep in good ol'
water.

I always like to check the placement date on the clues to see what types of
vegetative landmarks might be missing because of the season I'm searching vs
the season the box was planted (small trails that were so easy to
distinguish in summer can seem completely missing when the leaves fall and
cover everything later in the year), but I also check the placement date to
note if the type of terrain might also have changed......from dry
land........to swamp.......to pond......

Drought is another thing to keep in mind. There's a box in Kane County, IL
that basically has a person walking up a creekbed. In some of the droughty
summers we've had lately, there's really not much danger and it's fairly
easy to walk up the creekbed. The creekbed, however, is rock, and not flat
rocks at that, with a lot of fallen branches and logs to trip over, so at
the times when the creek has water in it, even if it's only ankle deep or
maybe calf deep, it's going to be very slippery and dangerous. Breaking
thru the brush to go up via shoreline is a possibility but it's tough going
and going up via creekbed will seem deceptively easy but actually could be
quite dangerous.

But whoever planted the box may never have been there when there was water
in the creek to realize that and I know that when the box was planted, it
was a baaad drought and there wasn't a drop of water in the creek.

~~ Mosey ~~

I was overthinking the poll question! I was anticipating someone devising
a very treacherous adventure and testing to see to what lengths we
addicted ones would go. If it's in my area, you bet I'll look for it, but
it might take more than one try if I am not totally prepared for the
challenge. That's why I said extreme letterboxing.

The other thoughts I had were prompted by my return trip from Indy today.
Riley Park in Greenfield floods after most rains. It was especially high
today and Brandywine Creek was rising and swiftly moving. Even though
most would not call it a river, children and adults have gotten swept away
in these small creeks around farm fields. I was just in Southwestway Park
on Sunday and the area was just thawing out. the grassy trails were still
waterlogged and sloshy. Mowizliz found the area flooded and frozen one
week prior. (neener, neener -- we found all the stamps there.)

I try to temper my enthusiasm in tricky areas by asking myself ... yeah,
we can probably get to the spot, but can we get back out safely?

See ya Saturday!
