We absolutely agree...we do have some boxes...several
that are outside our normal traveling range. It's
awesome to have people who can check up on boxes and
do any light work on them..changing torn or broken
bags or replacing a box that has been cracked, etc.
We have had people do this for some of our boxes and
it's a great help to not have to drive 100 miles to
replace a bag. Even when boxes are close, we have
found that it's just better to repair a box...notify
the owner. We're already there...no sense in having
someone else who's busy have to run around too. We
tend to think that things will become more like this
in the future...locals who don't really adopt the
boxes, but take care of them as if they had....It
takes a village to raise a letterbox. :)

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-04-06 19:05:21 UTC-04:00
Yes, I couldn't agree more -- sometimes people don't realize how important those "found your box" reports are. As far as maintenance, a found report is just as good as a visit to the box. Of course I still like to go check in on them occasionally to see the stamp-ins, but that just depends on how much time is available when I am in the area.

I do routinely check up on other boxes when I am out boxing or checking in on my own boxes. We need a few more people here in West Michigan so this will be more of an all-around kind of thing.

I'm thinking over the "box rotation" idea -- I may do that in a couple of places. I am also retiring a few that are kind of off on their own not real close to home. I finally got around to picking up the Turkey Day boxes last weekend and had fun looking through those log books. In the next week or two I will be permanently retiring Surf~ Sand, Blue Lake and Duck Lake. I think Duck Lake is missing, but the other two were alive and well as of late fall. If you don't have them -- it is supposed to be a nice weekend so you should have a chance to get out and look for them before they disappear.

On that note... got out to Lake Harbor Park in Muskegon to check in on the Sandy Seasons boxes among others -- it was nice seeing all the stamp-ins from the Sweetest Day Gathering in the log books. All boxes are in good shape and ready for the season. I did notice that the geocache that has plagued my Winter box for the past two years is gone though.

SpringChick
