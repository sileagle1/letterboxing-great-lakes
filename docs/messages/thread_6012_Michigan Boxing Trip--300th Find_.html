Michigan Boxing Trip--300th Find!

From: cdem_ltrbox (wp4@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2009-08-12 21:28:41 UTC
We recently got back from our vacation to northern Michiganand I was able to get a few boxing trips in. We were staying in Arcadiaand there were 2 boxes planted there. They were planted at 2 great places: the North Bluff overlook and the gravity-defying "Spook Hill". While we have been to both of these places a number of times, it was nice to have a stamp there nowand nice that other people will be lured to these spots {lured in a good way of course, not lured like Tiptoe when she was supposedly hanging out at trailheads waiting to pounce on unsuspecting boxers ;~) }. While in Traverse City, we headed out to the old insane asylumto check out a relative's winery, and to snag a few more boxeswhich were excellent. Some of the old buildings had great atmosphere.

Researching boxes steered us towards a horse stable. We got the box and then went for a horseback ride. The only instructions we got were pull the reins right or left to turn and pull back and say Whoa! Not a pretty sightI'm sure Trailrider would have been horror-stricken to see us. We were doing pretty good while the horses walked. When they broke into a trot we were bouncing all over the place and holding on to the saddle horns for dear life (or at least I was). The ride ended prematurely when another person on the ride went over the head of her horse (the horse had stopped abruptly and put its head down) and did a face plant onto the trail. She seemed to be OK, but that was the end of the ride.

Our last box was "The Reaper" in Manistee by Familyof6. The kids were really looking forward to the box, due to the dire warnings in the clues that the stamp might be too scary for small children. It was raining, so we tried to follow the clues while driving in the car. That didn't work, so out we wentand found the box and its excellent stamp. And that just happened to be my 300th find!! (Plus I hit 250 species on my birding life list after finding 2 new birds!)

This trip just reinforced to me the wonderful variety in letterboxing. Some stamps were handcarved and others were store-bought, but they all fit the box/location wonderfully. Some were drive-bys and others were short hikesall took us to interesting places. Some clues were tricky and others were more straight-forward, but we were able to find the boxes. I just wish we had more time on this vacation to get more of the boxes in the Traverse area and up by the dunes. Oh well, another summer.

BirdWoman/Waltzing Pigs
