The forecast for next weekend is exactly what we experienced yesterday
at Alum Creek for the big Columbus Cluez-In event! Many of us ran into
each other placing boxes! It was the mini-gathering BEFORE the event.
It was a good thing too because that way we knew which trail each other
was using to place boxes and which were still open. Anyway, yesterday
we did get a major downpour after it being super sunny and gorgeous for
most of the day---then it all cleared up again. (True to the Memorial
Golf Tournament tradition!!)

I also wanted to give everyone a heads up that there is a lot of poison
ivy in the Alum Creek area and now some of those black flies as
well--but the black flies weren't too bad. I bought a new product that
I tried out yesterday and it works even better than the packaging says!

It is "Cortaid, Poison Ivy Care, Toxin Removal Cloths" "Clinically
proven to wipe away poson plant toxins before the rash occurs."

It comes in a blue and green box and has 6 pre-moistened cloths
individually wrapped and I think they cost $5 or $6 per box. VERY much
worth the cost.

The cloths are generous in size and you just open the package and wipe
the entire area down. There is some sort of soap in the cloths that is
very soothing. You wipe with one side and then you fold it in half and
rewipe with the clean side and dispose. Then you need water to wash
off. I always carry a wet (black?!) clean washcloth in a zip lock when
letterboxing for my hands instead of those scented wet wipes and so I
just used that to clean off.

This morning I have no rash when I KNOW I was in a lot of poison ivy
yesterday AND---the poison ivy I got a week ago is gone from my hand
too! By all means the poison ivy on my hand should have been worse
today because I was hot and sweaty yesterday causing it to spread. My
skin is super smooth today on top of it--where I used the cloth!

Okay, enough of my info-emmercial! :)

I saw zero evidence of cicadas! So that is good! :)

Last, in talking to both M&8X and Eagle Eyes yesterday (our Columbus
Cluez-In hosts) they have really outdone themselves in preparing for
this event!!! They are two of the most organized people and have things
down to a science! They picked a perfect place to hold this event and
we will be able to blend in as "hikers" throughout the area with no
problem. Thanks for all of your hard work over the past year--in
preparing for what is sure to be a very successful event!

We, here in Ohio, are very much looking forward to meeting and seeing
everyone soon!

From: Barb Funk (mmfunk99@greenapple.com) | Date: 2007-05-28 13:29:09 UTC-04:00
There are tons of places to hide boxes. I didn't think so at first, but
there are lots of trails that are not marked on the map as M&8X
mentioned yesterday and she got full clearance to use any trail for
"hiking" (that's what we are all doing) even if the gate is closed at a
trail head.

Alum Creek State Park is huge. One suggestion on a place to plant---I
hid 7 boxes on the trails by the park office (not the visitor's
center---but the office). I used the Pine Loop and the outer Lake View
Trail only. I did not use any of the "inside" trails. I will also
throw one more thing out about this trail, if you plant on the trail
that runs parallel to, but on the opposite side of the pond and Park
Office---you will be seen and so will those who are hunting. This is
because the bank of the Park Office side of the pond gets LINED with
folks fishing---and lol they definitely look straight across and see
what you are doing?! (((big honkin' smile here from my experience
yesterday))).

I would also suggest that you do wrap your boxes in camo tape etc if you
have time.. I normally do, but ran out of time for these. Camo tape or
at least spray painting them would have helped. There are mostly only
sticks to cover with and some pine needles. I never found any rocks in
the area by the park office on the east side nor on the west side of the
lake either.

Very sorry for the scare! I promise you that was not intentional!! :)
