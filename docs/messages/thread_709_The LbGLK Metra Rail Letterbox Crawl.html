The LbGLK Metra Rail Letterbox Crawl

From: Kathi (krank@ameritech.net) | Date: 2004-09-30 06:32:38 UTC
I don't know how many of you have ever heard of a pub crawl...I didn't
until a few years ago (and no, I didn't participate!). A ski club
around here runs one twice a year. They get on the train in Chicago
and take it out to Aurora, stopping at every stop to sample the wares
at a nearby pub.

You know where I'm going with this....

Why not letterboxes!?

I started out with Sit...Sip...Stay...Box located at the Hollywood
stop on the Burlington Northern Santa Fe line. I added Come Grumpy,
Go Happy near the Riverside stop. I've spoken with a few local
boxers already about placing some, and now I'm posting the idea for
all to play along. Lord knows there are enough railroad tracks in the
Chicago area...heck, you can even take a commuter up to Kenosha! Just
think...all the boxing you could do on a $5 unlimited weekend pass!

I created a folder in the files section that contains a document with
introductory guidelines. I also posted pics of the contents of the
two boxes I mentioned above to help give you an idea if you want to
look at them. Since these boxes will all be placed with the blessing
of the propietor of an eating establishment, the pics can also help
you explain to them what you want to do.

I mention in the document a database to record all these boxes in one
place, but I see the database section is restricted. SpringChick or
Dragon, can one of you start a database to the specs listed, or open
up the database so I can set it up? It would help us to see what
stations are taken in a table format.

Anyway, take a look and come join the game! With all the stops
available, we may end up placing enough boxes to make Connecticut
stand up and take notice!!

--klk/FungusWoman
"If you place them, they will come...."
