Thought I would join the "goobling" about box huntings and mushroom hunting.

I have not yet tried mushroom hunting, BUT I came across a awesome article in our local community magazine about Morel Mushroom Hunting (May 06 Good News Magazine, Adrian Michigan).

It gave a great description on how to find mushrooms. What I thought was the most interesting, is the article mentions to put your mushrooms in an old "onion" bag or mesh bag when out hunting, because it helps the mushrooms drop new spores to aid in replenshing for next years harvest. :)

For the "peeps" thinking about hunting and not sure, this little rule of thumb may help you from picking the wrong mushrooms on you hikes.

Morel Mushroom Rules
#1. When in doubt, throw it out!
#2 If it ain't hollow, don't swallow!
#3 If it's wavy, don't make gravy!
#4 If it's reddish, you could be dead-ish
