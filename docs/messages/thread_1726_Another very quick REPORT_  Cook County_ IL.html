Another very quick REPORT: Cook County, IL

From: ponyexpresscarrier (PonyExpressMail@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-08-18 19:24:49 UTC
Found the following boxes this morning before the skies opened up and
the rain came pouring down -- not that I'm complaining one single bit
about the rain.

1. Cemetery Stroll #1: Showmen's Rest, at Woodlawn Cemetery in
Forest
Park. I've always lived this part of this cemetery. A truly tragic
happening. Box was in good shape, cute carving.

2. Field of Dreams, Bob Mann Woods in the Westchester/LaGrange Park
area. Box was in good shape except for the "Everything" bag (which
holds the logbook bag and the stamp bag), so I replaced
the "Everything" bag. I enjoyed the clues to this one.

3. Forest Hills, in Western Springs. Splash, you did a nice
carving.
Congratulations on placing your first box.

4. Softball Anyone?! at Springdale Park in Western Springs. Rather
than finding this box in the hollow of the tree, it was sitting next
to
the tree, out in plain view. But box was otherwise in fine shape.
So
I gathered up some extra leaves and such to give it more cover.
Obviously a very popular park with the kiddies. Interesting chain in
the tree. Nice carving by Splash.

Was also going to go looking for Where's the Springs? in Western
Springs, but the clues said the box is missing already. Bummer!

~~ Mosey ~~

From: Mosey (PonyExpressMail@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-08-18 17:32:22 UTC-05:00
Happy boxing Flash! A loooong time ago a friend asked me to help her
research that tragedy. And, except for Cirque du Soleil, circuses aren't my
thing at all. What happened was pretty awful. I drop by there often to pay
my respects, so I kind of feel it's another home. I sort of became attached
to the troupe.

I could picture right where the box was. Only problem was avoiding any
questioning glances by the maintenance folks. Had to go find Field of
Dreams and come back because they were working down the road, and even tho
they weren't close, there are areas in a cemetery where people can draw
attention to themselves just by being in those areas. I've never met
anybody over at Bob Mann Woods -- nobody two-legged that is. Plenty of deer
there. So went there first and came back. And maintenance men had gone
elsewhere happydance happydance.

That'll be a relatively easy one for your friend to get started with.

~~ Mosey ~~
