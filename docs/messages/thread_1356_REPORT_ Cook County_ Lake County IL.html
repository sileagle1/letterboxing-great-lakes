REPORT: Cook County, Lake County IL

From: Mosey (PonyExpressMail@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-05-29 15:31:23 UTC-05:00
1. & 2. River Trail placed by Pico and Little Red Fox placed by Clue Reader,
both at River Trail Nature Center, in Northbrook.

Had a wonderful Sunday morning hike going after both these boxes. Both
boxes found in good order. Employees or volunteers seemed to have worked at
keeping the nature center nicely rustic. Chipmunk Heaven. Chipmunks just
simply all over the place.

When we went looking for the Little Red Fox, we were sitting on a bench by
the pond quietly talking and heard a commotion on the trail further down.
Even tho dogs aren't allowed, along came a gentleman with both a Doberman
and a Greyhound. At least he had them both on lead. He was practically
dragging both dogs along however. Both wanted to keep going back in the
direction they had come.

So we looked down the trail behind them and we did a double-take at what was
coming along behind the three of them. A White-Tailed doe. We couldn't
believe it. The doe was about 20 feet behind the man and the two dogs and
actually following them. He'd stop to get better control of the dogs, she'd
stop. He'd get the dogs moving forward again, she'd move forward with them.

Then Ancient and I made a mistake. We moved. That caught her eye. She
started over to us. We rose and began walking down the trail in the
opposite direction from the man and dogs. We stopped. She stopped. We
walked. She walked. I'm well used to Holsteins doing this, but
White-Tailed deer??? She stopped at the edge of the woods tho, she didn't
follow us out into the parking lot. Which was good because it's one thing
to try to explain how a dog followed me home but.........

3. Virgo, placed by Flying J at Ryerson Woods Conservation Area in the
Deerfield area.

We drove up Milwaukee Ave from Northbrook. Passed a lady walking a
three-legged Groen down Riverwoods Rd when we got close to Ryerson. Being
owned by two four-legged Groens, I was going to stop and say hi, since Groen
folk often do that, but if I'd tried, the car behind us would have ended up
in the bed of my truck. The day was going too nicely at that point, so we
didn't do that. We waved enthusiastically, the lady probably thought we
were totally nuts, and we kept going.

Being totally, 100% directionally-challenged, one look at the Ryerson map
told us we were going to get lost. This place has quite a few trails. But
it also told us that if we did get lost, there was a ton of stuff out there
to explore.

Another mature woods Chipmunk Heaven. We explored the dam and the Ryerson
cabin, we explored the Smith River Cabin, and down the trail north of the
Smith cabin we ran across a tree with the largest burls we've ever seen in
our lives, we explored up and down the Des Plaines River where there was a
lovely batch of brilliant yellow iris. We walked over to explore the roots
of a huuuuge uprooted tree and ran smack-dab into a grey squirrel
convention. We promptly and loudly got told off by said squirrels. We
promised to come bearing gifts of sunflower seeds on our next visit, but the
squirrels weren't about to be appeased by a promise of future gifts. So we
quietly took our leave of their convention.

And yes, we even found the bridges we needed to find to locate the box.

By 2pm, it had started to rain while we were playing around by the dam and
watching a blue heron on the other side of the river. The walk back to the
parking lot was great. Even with the rain coming down fairly good, the
canopy was thick enough that we were barely getting wet. We did, however,
need to make a run for it from the edge of the woods to the truck.

And what topped the day off was that Ryerson Woods even had beautiful flush
toilet bathrooms attached to the Visitor Center. One for boys. One for
girls. Yes!
