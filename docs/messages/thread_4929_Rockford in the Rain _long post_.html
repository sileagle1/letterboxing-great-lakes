Rockford in the Rain (long post)

From: beckers410 (Mathw613@aol.com) | Date: 2008-04-12 03:22:36 UTC
Several weeks ago my beloved wife, Becki, and I decided to go to
Rockford for our 9th anniversary (April 10) and spend the night,
leaving our 4 ones with Grammy & Granddad.

Little did we know that Rockford was in the eye of the storm and was
to be pummeled by rain!!

We decided to go through with the trip, rain or no rain, letterbox or
no letterbox . . . after all this was our anniversary and all that
mattered was spending time together ~ without the children.

Driving up the wipers on high, water on the road, can't really
see . . . can't our wipers go any faster??

We brought with us 2 huge umbrellas, we were going to try
letterboxing in the rain (something I *never* thought I'd do... too
worried my logbook would get wet).

By the time we got to "Irish Penny" the rain had subsided some and we
were able to grab it and log in in a little shelter house there.
Everything was going just fine, perhaps we could do this after all.

We next went to "Swooop" the rain picked up, but we couldn't miss
this chance to pick up one of the Illinois Road Trip boxes. Was it
worth it!! A wonderful hand carved stamp by Krait ~ A Blue Diamond on
Atlas Quest.

"Grab" on the other hand seemed to be a walk, and the wind and rain
picked up considerably. We were being blown around a lot and the path
next to the river was washed out, but we found an alternative and got
the box. Another Awesome stamp by Krait made this well worth the
soaking to get it.

Becki would stand over me as I stamped in trying to keep the rain
away. I kept my umbrella over my back to try to save my backpack and
its contents . . . by the time we got back my front was 100% soaked
through.

Next we drove into town and found "Rockford Peaches" a wonderful box
with a great overlook of the ball park where they played, thanks to
Nature Nerd.

We then attempted "Ode to a Rose" and now the rain was pelting us.
Soaked and freezing we walked on and found both of the "Den Grimme
Aelling" boxes and stamped in under the little shelter there.

As we were walking to the second "Den Grimme Aelling" box Becki says
to me, "Matt, I love you, but no stamp is worth this."

Though I beg to differ I couldn't complain, I promised after this we
would go and get a bite to eat and dry off some and perhaps our day
of boxing was over.

After eating at one of my favorite places ever (Cajun Chicken from
Popeyes -- we need a Popeyes in the QCA!!) we both felt better and to
my surprise Becki picked up the map and clues and looked to see what
box we could get next -- have I told you yet of how much I love my
wife?

Off to "Jake's Third Birthday" and what a nice surprise to see River
Rat's log in that said, "This is my first letterbox ever!" talk about
a historical box LOL.

After that we drove to the "Goose Barn" a much appreciated indoor box
where we exchanged with the owner Dumpster Diva.

We then decided to find "Fish Tank: Castle" after arriving we saw the
tree it was hidden near about 300 yards out down a paved bike path.
It was still raining good and I told Becki, "300 yards never looked
so far" we both looked at the "No Motorized Vehicles" sign and she
glanced at me and said, "I wont tell if you don't."

We looked back and forth and it *was* a quite road we were on. So I
did what any weary, wet, tired guy would do . . . I put the car in
drive and down the path we went. I ran to the tree as Becki got our
stampin' gear out so that we could hurry up and log in before the
cops showed up. We logged in in record time and I through the car in
reverse... ahh, just what we needed a shot of adrenalin.

We then drove to "Know You're Place" by Shutterfly, as we neared the
park I commented to Becki, "Well, we must be getting close, the rain
is coming down harder." once there and wandering around a bit in the
wrong direction we figured out our error and found the box, with only
a little help from Trail Rider who had me on the phone figuring out
our meeting that night.

Becki & I had figured on going over to Boone County and finding the
couple boxes over there before the sun went down. But I relented and
made the alternate suggestion of going to the hotel, checking in and
drying off!!

Trail Rider and her husband NamVet showed up a little alter and we
chatted about an hour. I was honored to have gotten to know TR better
and meeting Russ for the first time. We had a great time chatting for
about an hour or so.

After they left we hopped in the van to go to dinner -- at something
past 8:00 pm.

After the day was over I looked through the logbook and counting the
couple of mystery boxes we managed 12 boxes and 2 exchanges in the
pouring rain no less!

Today we had some high hopes again, and started with "Fish Bowl:
Bowl" that is listed as "missing" with no finds since 2006. We found
the "hard to find" park and couldn't believe our luck, the sun was
out!

We followed the clues and there was the box!! Right out in the open!!
it was replaced by Camp Redwing Flyers on August 10, 2007 and no one
has logged it as found since late 2006! CRF made this stamp with some
foam cut and glued in the shape of a fish bowl... a little small but
manageable. A Very nice surprise - and a great way to start the day!

Off to Boone County for the 3rd and last installment of the "Fish
Bowl" series -- the fish! And we found that Camp Redwing Flyers had
replaced this box as well... only with a store bought stamp.

Then one of the boxes I really wanted to find [side note: last Sept.
we found a box that had Cookoo Clues inside that told us to take the
clues if we planned on finding this box . . . so I did!] finally, now
time to find the box! We found "Darting Dragons" just as the rain
started to fall again :-( Will it ever end?

We decided to head back and try "Ode to a Rose" and "A River Runs
through it" again. Having troubles with "Ode to a Rose" for the
second day in a row (and knowing it was just found a couple weeks
ago) I called Trail Rider for some help, she asked where we were and
said, "Don't move, we'll be there in 2 minutes."

before we knew it she and Pastors Wife showed up! Taking a walk over
their lunch break. We chatted as we went to the *other* side of the
park to the box . . . something's not right! I took out my compass
and with Trail Rider, Pastor's Wife and Becki as witnesses it was
pointing DUE EAST instead of due north!! What!?!? Its a wonder I've
found any boxes at all with a bum compass.

We walked down the trail to a "River Runs through it" I consider this
the "event stamp" of our weekend as Rockford, Illinois is carved in
it and it has water in the stamp.

Then for the thrill of the trip, we followed Trail Rider to her place
of employment where we had the wonderful opportunity to meet the
master carver himself . . . Krait!!

We chatted, showed off our stamp work and talked techniques. I've
found so many of his stamps and they are all such works of art, this
was a definite highlight -- helped in part that it took place
indoors -- Krait is a wonderful gentleman whose stamps are all worth
the hunt!!

Afterwards we had a bite to eat before heading out for our next
box . . . we followed the clues up until the point the path we were
walking on was submerged with water!! The wind picked up so fast we
thought a hurricane was coming, we could hardly walk back to the van.

This whole area seemed a flood plane and we decided to forgo our next
attempt at a series of 5 boxes. There is always next time right?

We did add another 5 finds today for a total of 17!

My wonderful wife was just that, wonderful!! Thank you Becki for
putting up with me . . . even in the rain and mud, wind and all!!

Of course, I had to promise for our next anniversary we were doing to
the Container Store and Old Navy!!

hx6
