Merry Merry/the great big southern lie

From: Delirium (donaldson_home@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-12-22 18:43:59 UTC-08:00

Hello list!

Well, we're here in Mississippi, the house is (mostly) set up (ready or not, here the holiday visitors come), and most importantly, the computer was hooked up yesterday. So I check my million and two emails, and see a thread about you poor, poor kids up there in the snow. And I figure, hey, if I was ripped away from my native soil, at least I get to gloat about the weather, right? At very least I get to send a witty email about how I went letterboxing today in 60 degree weather, and fretted about not bringing sunscreen, right? 'Cause everyone knows that's the big drawing feature of the south- the weather.

WRONG! I am "snowed in" my house right now (snowed in, of course, meaning that there's half an inch of snow on the ground and that the local Wal-Mart has run out of milk, bread, and butter because holy cow there's been two snowflakes spotted out there)- but if you don't count the pathetic attempt at snow, there's a pretty wicked ice storm tearing through here. And it's cold. Now, not the winter of '94, when it got to wind chills of -70, and MSU actually cancelled classes for one day, but pretty blustery. So instead of getting to gloat, I have to haul out MY carving materials and dream of warmer days (which my neighbors assure me will come sooner than I want). I keep telling my better half that he promised crippling heat, and gosh darnit, I want crippling heat! But so far, all I've got is an entire state of nervous nellies who drive with their hazzards on as soon as there's a single flake spotted.

Cheers,

Delirium, who doesn't understand half the things people say to her down here.

From: Armadillo Jo (jes7o@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-12-23 04:23:01 UTC-08:00
Occasionally, say....once every 12 years...the deep
south gets snow. When it happened in '90 in New
Orleans...it was the first time many of my friends had
ever seen it. They were sledding down the levee on
trash bags! We got a whole whopping 1/2 inch and
school was cancelled. There's no such thing as salt or
snow plows and the last thing the authorities want is
a bunch of half-drunk cajuns sliding around on the
ice.
It never lasts, though...generally winter in the South
consists on 3 months of rain. My "winter coat" was my
Levi's jean jacket. It rarely gets colder than 55,
which would be a "heat wave" up here. I used to
complain about how "cold" it was when it was 55. Now I
only feel the cold if the wind chill is in the
negatives. You get used to it.
You'll also figure out what those Southerners are
saying soon enough. Get the book, "The Sweet Potato
Queen's Book Of Love." Jill Conner Brown writes like
most Southerners talk. Not to mention the book is
hysterical, about a troop of middle-aged ladies who
dress up and parade every St. Patrick's Day in
Jackson, Mississippi.

Sympathizing with your culture shock,
--Armadillo Jo

send us home soon - they just paged for all the

our carving materials are at Deedle's place, not mine!
