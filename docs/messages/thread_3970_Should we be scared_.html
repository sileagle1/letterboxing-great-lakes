Should we be scared?

From: Doodle & Deedle Bug (doodle_n_deedle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-04-17 09:12:57 UTC-07:00
I receive emailings from the Discover Ohio tourism
website, and here's the text from the one I received
just now:

No matter your age, youve no doubt been on a treasure
hunt before. But now, theres a whole new way to enjoy
this age-old favorite. Its called geocaching, and
its a great way to combine time in the beauty of
Ohios outdoors with quality time with the entire
family. Geocaching is a high tech, kid friendly sport
that involves using a handheld global positioning
system (GPS) to find a geocache, usually a waterproof
container holding trinkets, hidden in natural areas.

With more than 7,000 geocaches hidden throughout Ohio,
theres literally adventure to be found at every turn.
If youre looking for popular hotspots for geocaching,
youre sure to find it at one of Ohios 74 State
Parks. In particular, youll want to focus on Salt
Fork State Park in Cambridge, Ohios largest state
park. Home to more than 2,000 geocaches, you and your
family can easily spend days happily hunting the area!

Its easy to see theres no better way to find outdoor
fun than in beautiful Ohio. And dont forget youll
receive future emails with even more ways to enjoy
your time in Ohio!

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

There was even a link in the email for a special
"geocaching package" at Salt Fork State Park. Could
you just imagine if "geocaching" was replaced with
"letterboxing"? Publicity can be good, but this just
makes me want to shrink back into my little
letterboxing hole and have people say the secret
password if they want to enter. I don't want to
re-open the whole "Letterboxing & Publicity, good or
bad?" can of worms, I just thought others would be -
um - interested in reading this, too.

Doodle Bug.
