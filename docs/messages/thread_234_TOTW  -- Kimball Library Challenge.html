Instead of a TOTW, this week I am presenting a challenge...

#592 on this list)? In glancing at the list of names, none looked

also in the LINKS section of this group.

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-05-24 22:12:41 UTC
I've heard from two people who have sent their Kimball Library
Letterbook solutions to Tom Cooch. Don't know yet who was first, or
if the solutions are correct. Kudos to both Delirium and Tom and
Jill!

Anyway, if you didn't get a chance to work on the challenge yet,
don't give up just because of that -- it is still a lot of fun and a
great brain-teaser. And if you solve it, you get the Kimball
Library stamp image from Tom and your name on his successful solvers
page, which is quite an accomplishment as there are less than 50
people who have solved this thing!

I've heard from a number of you (a couple in frustration) who really
enjoyed the diversion from the customary TOTW. I am glad it is
being enjoyed! My hope is that working through clues like this may
get you thinking along the lines of some more challenging and
cryptic clues of your own.

Now, I need to do some work on this thing myself ;-)

SpringChick

There are 2 time frames, one happens in the 60's where this reknowned scientist is part of a group working to crack the DNA code. Flash forward to the early 80's, where Franklin, a former art PhD (all but his dissertation is done) works the night shift overseeing some computers with this funny old man, who Franklin discovers used to be a reknowned scientist in the area of DNA who dropped off the face of the earth. He seeks out the help of a research librarian to learn more about his co-worker. There's also a lot of technical jargon, because the book also deals with the concept that this one musical composition was composed in the same manner as human DNA. (I skimmed through most of the technical
stuff when I read it back in college.)
