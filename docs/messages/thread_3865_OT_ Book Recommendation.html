From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2007-03-17 12:24:37 UTC
Thought I would put in a plug for a book I am just finishing. "Last
Child in the Woods: Saving Our Children from Nature Deficit
Disorder," by Richard Louv explores the increasing disconnectivity
between today's children and the natural world.

I remember as a kid hours spent playing at a nearby park, exploring
in the woods, building forts, fishing in the pond, wading in the
creek, playing outdoor games, camping & campfires, summer camp,
horsebackriding, etc. These are some of the most vivid and memorable
experiences of my youth. Now a 20-minute school recess is the most
some children see of the outdoors for days on end. Too often free
time is now spent watching television & movies, playing video games,
chatting and surfing the internet. While it is true that our world
has changed since I was a child 30 years ago, it simply requires that
we employ different strategies, not change our priorities.

This book first came to my attention through a website associated
with the ACA (American Camp Association) while I was compiling
information for a project at work (I work for a summer camp
organization), but I see it was also recently referred to in an
article on letterboxing in Metro Parent Magazine (Detroit area
publication).

This is something near to my heart and I am sure as parents,
grandparents, educators and concerned adults, there are many here who
share my feelings and would truly applaud this book.

Link to this book at Amazon.com: http://tinyurl.com/34nold
