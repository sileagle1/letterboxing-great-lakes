BINGO wrap-up

From: (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2016-05-04 15:04:37 UTC-07:00

Thanks to everyone that played Great Lakes Letterboxing BINGO this year!  This was the 11th year of the game that SpringChick began in 2006.  It certainly does give us all a little bit of fun during a Midwestern winter!  I think it may be the slowest Bingo game in the world, but we are easily entertained in the winter.


Below is a final list of the winners.  I will be at the Great Lakes Gathering later this month and can deliver prizes to any winners that will also be attending.  Or if you live nearby or I will see you somewhere else in the near future, I can hand deliver your prize. Savings in mailing costs will go toward additional gift card prizes.  However if none of those options are good for you, I will mail your prize.  Just send your mailing address to kotlarek@wi.rr.com  Please let me know how you want yours delivered.


While BINGO is still on our minds, I created an on-line poll to get your input on the game for next year. Mailing actual items for the booty is getting to be more expensive and then mailing the prizes also requires a fair amount of money (thus the extra $2 fee to play).  An option worth considering is to make the game "All gift cards" next year.  We could standardize an amount and then everyone could simply use an envelope to mail a gift card from a place of your choosing (gas, food, crafts, or ??).  No additional fee would be required. However if most people prefer to keep it the way it has been, that's fine also!  Please vote in the poll to let me know your preference.  Letterbox Great Lakes

From: Barb Gecas (bski43@yahoo.com) | Date: 2016-05-23 05:09:11 UTC-07:00
Thank you to everyone for an amazing bingo prize. I was blown away by what came in my box! You all are so generous. There are things in here that will help my small quest of introducing young people to the fun and excitement of letterboxing. You see, I run a small resort in northern Minnesota, and I have a short letterboxing trail on my property for the youngest guests. I show them how to carve a rubber stamp, and then they go off with a little journal that I have made for them, to find my boxes. If they and their parents are so inclined, there are longer hikes in our woods that have a few boxes for them to discover, too.

My hope is that I can get the kids hooked on both letterboxing and hiking. Not sure how many I have gotten to do this beyond my place, but I am hopeful that the "treasure hunt" aspect of it will stay with and encourage them.

If anyone has suggestions for me on this endeavor, I would love to hear them! Thank you all for not only my prize, but this great community. I look forward to the day that I can meet you in person.

Sincerely,
Barb G
aka Gunflint Knitter
