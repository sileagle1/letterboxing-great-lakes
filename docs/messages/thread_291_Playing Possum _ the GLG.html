From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2004-06-14 22:37:41 UTC-05:00
Thanks to both of you for your kind words about the stamp! I am definitely
a beginner carver - that stamp is only my 4th attempt at carving. Martini
Man & I certainly saw plenty of excellent examples of detailed carving in
the stamps available at the event and will continue to practice (after my
arm is healed of course!) I can't wait to get this splint & sling off to
try the letter carving tip SpringChick so graciously shared, even though I
couldn't fully participate in her great seminar. I was impressed with the
wonderful variety of images she provided as practice images.

Martini Man & I both wish to give congratulations & thanks to Dragon for
organizing the event and to Acorn for planting so many of the temporary
boxes.

It was great to meet more LB addicts - we added 20 new exchanges, including
The Travelin' Trouts as their first official exchange. We were also lucky
to meet the Lucky Charms and their charming niece & nephew (aged 4 & 2).
Skyler eagerly asked her uncle if it was time "to go on the hunt"! We saw
DebRocks' dogs (Jo & Tuck) stamped in to some of the box logbooks and were
really sorry we didn't get their stamps in OUR book as an exchange. Maybe
next time ...

It was also interesting to see some of the "famous" traveling stamps. The
Marilyn stamp was fantastic, as was our very own GLG 2004 event stamp
created by Leapin' Lizards.

Once we were out searching for boxes in the afternoon we had fun seeing the
creativity and skill of more carvers. We especially enjoyed the Fool's
Errand series - the great images & the thrill of being first finders at the
Moon box and discovering a nice moon pin which I promptly attached to my
sling. Although we didn't find all the boxes available at the Gathering, we
were pleased to end the day with 18 found plus 2 of the egg hitchhikers. We
hope to get back another time to do the vampire series.

Like everyone else, we were besieged by mosquitoes throughout the day and
used up our entire can of bug spray. We did get a few bites but the major
annoyance was them swarming around us constantly, trying to find a spot that
hadn't been sprayed, or where the spray had worn off due to the heat. Yes
it was HOT!

One last note: the food was delicious and I also would like to ask John of
Leapin Lizards to post or email the recipe for the yummy cheesecake dessert.
I'd like to make it for an upcoming family event later this month.

Based on the wonderful time we had, we're looking forward to attending more
events in the future!

Wisconsin Hiker

P.S. I'll be posting the clues for the "Playing Possum" box on the LBNA
site since it will remain as a "permanent" box.
