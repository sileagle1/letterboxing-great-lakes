We're sorry to hear that it seems that several boxes in the region are now
missing. We've been planting boxes for 3 years and have only lost 2 so far
(that we know of) out of 74 placed. Like some of the rest of you, when the
first one was missing, I went back twice just to see if maybe it "had been
returned". No such luck, so I eventually replaced it. I have to admit it
was NOT a good hiding spot or box (BRIGHT purple!?!?). However another box
was missing when I checked on it, but I went to double-check after the snow
was gone and, miracle of miracles, someone had placed it back in the hollow
log (they noted that it had been laying out in the open). Martini Man has
recently lost a box, but I think we should maybe go back to see if it has
been returned (we've been lucky before....) Other boxes that we planted
have been reported as possibly missing but either other boxers have
subsequently found them and/or they are in the proper spot when we go to
check on them ourselves. So yes, the moral of the story is that just
because someone THINKS a box is missing, it isn't necessarily so.

We had two other boxes strewn about by what were probably humans. Luckily
the next letterboxers on the hunt for them managed to round up the pieces
and the boxes were replaced with new logbooks (and one in a slightly
different location). We've had NUMEROUS other boxes opened or gnawed on by
animals. Luckily all of these continued on OK, because the next boxers put
things back together and/or contacted us about needing any replacement
items. Only one stamp has some toothmarks, but we haven't had to replace
any stamps or logbooks on the animal boxes. Boxes themselves are another
story - interesting how much they like that blue Rubbermaid rubber or other
plastic....

As others have said, we figure that when we put a box out, we may never see
it again. However we have the image in our logbook and on an index card
that we keep in a special album just for our planted boxes. That is our
"souvenir". We don't feel compelled to replace our boxes anymore, we may or
may not. If we don't, we just figure it was a "limited edition" box and
have no desire for anyone else to try to duplicate a replacement box. Of
course we hope that a number of people were able to add it to their own
logbooks. We delete our clues so the area appears "open" for new
placements.

Hoping for the best for ALL of the region's boxes over the winter, which
seems to be arriving WAY TOO early this year!

Wisconsin Hiker
