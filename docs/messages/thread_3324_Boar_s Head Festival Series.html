Boar's Head Festival Series

From: cdem_ltrbox (wp4@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2006-11-15 20:17:11 UTC
We enjoyed placing a Christmas series last year, so we decided to do
it againbut what to do??? Then it came to me. Why not do the
series about the Boar's Head Festival that we are all participating
in this yearI find it rather amusing that 4 Waltzing PIGS are
participating in a BOAR's Head Festival :~) Anyway, the Boar's
Head Festival is a Christmas celebration dating back to about 1340
A.D. in England. It gets its name from the boar (the most ferocious
beast in the forest) which symbolized evil and deathand was
defeated by the birth of Christ. The first part of the program is
set in a medieval manor house complete with lords and ladies, court
jester, serving wenches, dancers, wassailers, good King Wenceslaus
and his page and lots of entertainment. Then the mood becomes more
serious as the Christmas nativity is enacted, with angels and
shepherds and magi coming to see Mary, Joseph and the Christ Child.
In a grand finale, the medieval court joins the scene to pay homage
to the newborn King. The actual clues explain things a little
more.

We are 4 of a cast of around 100. I am a lowly serving wench, Ball
Magnet is a singing wait (demanding his figgy pudding from the
King), MoneyMan is a shepherd and Do-Re-Mi Gal is the Star of the
East. Each stamp in the series reflects one of our roles. The
fifth stamp will only be available on the two days of the
performanceand will be at the location of the performance (still
scouting out a place for that).

This festival is an awesome evententertaining and also very
moving. This is definitely not the type of Christmas program that
only a parent could enjoy. Theater professionals are involved40
voice choirorchestragorgeous costumes-etc. With all the chatter
this summer about the RenFaire, I thought there might be interest in
a medieval Christmas program. So, on to the shameless advertisement

Performances will take place on Saturday, Dec. 9 (at 4 and 7 pm) and
on Sunday, Dec. 10 (at 4 pm). It will be held at St. John Lutheran
Church, 305 Circle Avenue in Forest Park, IL (a suburb of Chicago).
Advance sale tickets are $12 for adults and $10 for seniors/students
and can be ordered by calling (708) 453-2654. Tickets will also be
available at the door for $15.

So, we encourage you to look for the boxes in the woods of River
Forest. And then come to one of the performances and look for the
fifth stamp. Maybe you could think of it as an event stamp, but
without a gathering.

BirdWoman/Waltzing Pigs
