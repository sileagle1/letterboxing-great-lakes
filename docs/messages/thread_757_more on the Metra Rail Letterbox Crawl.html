more on the Metra Rail Letterbox Crawl

From: Kathi LK Krankoski (krank@ameritech.net) | Date: 2004-10-10 22:17:58 UTC-05:00
After "talking" with CMoon and Timbertoes about the Metra Rail Letterbox
Crawl, we've decided to expand things a bit. First, the hiding place
doesn't have to be a food establishment, though we think it best we stay
with independent (non-chain) establishments. First we figured they'd
probably appreciate the patronage of their places more, and dealing directly
with the owner is probably easier than a manager who has to consult with
higher-ups concerning company policies. Plus it's often more fun to
discover lesser known places!

We also thought we should include any box within reasonable distance from a
train station. That would, for instance, include CMoon's History Tower, and
my Hurray for Hollywood (can't get much closer to a station than that one!).
What do you think...within a half mile? Mile for the fast walker? Which
brings us to our next idea...

We think prefixing the box title with the initials "MRLC" would help
identify the box as part of the crawl. So Sit-Sip-Stay-Box would become
MRLC: Sit-Sip-Stay-Box. A statement to the effect "Part of the Metra Rail
Letterbox Crawl" can also be included in the clues, as well as distance from
the station (assuming of course it wouldn't compromise the clues
themselves.) Of course, designating a box part of the crawl would entirely
be at the discretion of the placer. But as we all know, the more boxes are
identified in a region, the more likely they will come looking for them!

We also thought of having a link cross-referencing a file or something
explaining the concept of the crawl might be helpful to boxers not part of
this group who wouldn't know what we're talking about.

Anyone have any opinions on this or other ideas?? As I told the two of
them...I am not claiming ownership of this project or anything like
that...just starting a game we can all play together!
