Great topic! I would say that recently, our most memorable outing was
while letterboxing in CT in September. The landscape is just so
different from Michigan. We were on the Metacomet trail and we had to
scramble over rocks... and then boulders... and we were constantly
going up, up, and up. When we finally reached the top (after a good
hour-long hike) the view was spectacular. You could see for miles and
the trees far below were tiny. It was one of those hikes that made me
glad I letterbox, because I almost certainly would never have taken a
walk like that (which ended up being close to four hours) without the
motivation of finding a series of boxes.
