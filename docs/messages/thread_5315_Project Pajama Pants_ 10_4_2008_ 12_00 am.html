Project Pajama Pants, 10/4/2008, 12:00 am

From: (LbGLK@yahoogroups.com) | Date: 2008-09-27 04:03:32 UTC
Reminder from:   LbGLK Yahoo! Group
 
Title:   Project Pajama Pants
 
Date:   Saturday October 4, 2008
Time:   All Day
Next reminder:   The next reminder for this event will be sent in 4 days, 23 hours, 56 minutes.
Location:   At WI 10th Anniversary Gathering
Notes:   "Part of the mission of Operation Support Our Troops-Illinois is to support our wounded heroes. We have been contacted by personnel working in the hospitals in Iraq, Afghanistan, and at Landstulh Medical Center in Germany with a special request. Our injured troops come in and their clothing is usually cut off, just like a hospital ER here in the U.S. Instead of wearing hospital gowns, they would appreciate drawstring pajama type pants (sizes L and XL are most in demand)."

"We also welcome letters to the troops. Every care package includes at least one letter from a citizen, and these letters are highly prized by their recipients. Since we send to every branch of the military, and not all personnel are called soldiers, we prefer the more generic greeting of Dear military person in the letters. Please do not seal the letters. They do not require postage and if you would like the individual that receives your letter to possibly write back, include your name and address."

"Also, please save the comics from your Sunday papers and/or comic books. The troops love to receive and read them!"

So here's my idea: bring and donate a pair of pajama pants to the gathering, and you can receive the image of a special "Project Pajama Pants" event stamp. If you bring in comics and/or a letter, I'll have my "Love Letters" event stamp
available as your reward. (Even if you've already received that image,I hope you'll still bring a letter!)

No time to shop for pajama pants? Never fear! Place a donation in an envelope and give it to me at the gathering. I will buy a pair for you. The canteen store at Hines VAMC sells them for $9.00 a pair, but any size donation is appreciated.

(Quotations above from http://www.osotil.org/ )


--FungusWoman
