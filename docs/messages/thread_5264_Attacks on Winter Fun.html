Re: Attacks on Winter Fun

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2008-08-28 09:06:46 UTC
My theory is that certain types of carving rubber are more prone to
absorbing smells. A few years ago I had put out 4 boxes for an event,
all in completely different areas of a park and every one of the
stamps (the pink stuff in this case) was gnawed completely off the
wood backing within the first week of being placed. I was totally
perplexed because I had been using the pink stuff pretty consistently
and not had any problems with critters getting at the stamps before
that. When trying to figure out what was different about these
boxes/stamps that would make them so enticing to critters, it occurred
to me the I had made fish one evening while the stamps/boxes were in
process on my dining room table and the whole house had smelled like
fish for a couple of days. I am always very careful about food smells
on my hands when handling boxes, but had never given any consideration
to smells the rubber may be absorbing even before being put into the
box. Of course I'll never *know* that was the reason those stamps
were mauled, but all other things being equal it was the only thing
that differentiated them from others I had done. It may explain why
the pink stuff, being more supple more porous than PZKut, therefore
more apt to absorb smells, seems to be more of a target for critters.

SpringChick
