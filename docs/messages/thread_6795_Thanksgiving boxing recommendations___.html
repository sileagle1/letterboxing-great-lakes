RE: [LbGLK] Re: Thanksgiving boxing recommendations???

From: Laurie Marselle (ltmarselle@msn.com) | Date: 2010-11-14 10:51:46 UTC-06:00

We had a great time in Hocking Hills!! We rented a cabin from Lazy Lane Cabins - had a great experience! Logan is not far from the parks, but we cooked in the cabin most the time. Favorite restaurant was the Italian Cafe that was located just west of Logan on the main hwy. It is in a shiny silver dining train car and they had amazing food - many unique dishes too. Most cabins have hot tubs and fireplaces, too. There are tons of boxes in the area and the scenery was beautiful. Our favorite boxes were ones by Trailtracker and SitsnKnits, but all were nice. A few of the seven parks are connected by trails, so people who enjoy a longer hike can hike between parks. My favorite park by far was Cantwell Cliffs - awesome scenery and stairs/passageways weaving in and out of the cliffs. Made it to all parks except the Rock House park. We tried for 42 boxes and found 40 in 2 days time. Pretty good odds! Check out my logbook for the ones we found. Have fun and let us know if you have anymore questions! I have visitors guide and map of area that I can mail to you.

From: Doodle & Deedle Bug (doodle_n_deedle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2010-11-16 07:19:34 UTC-08:00
I just have to second Hocking Hills as a wonderful place to visit when anyone gets the chance.  Just a gorgeous, gorgeous area.  We went there a few years ago for our anniversary (and did some letterboxing, of course) and are talking about going back with a group of friends possibly this summer.  I know Pitties already mentioned one place to rent cabins, but if you like to explore options, here's the place we stayed that we highly recommend:  http://www.ashridgecabins.com/  Be warned, though, that it is a steep hill to drive up to where the cabins are. 

I hope TrailTracker doesn't mind me throwing her name out here, but if you are going to Hocking Hills to letterbox, contact her on AQ and I'm sure she'd be glad to give you some advice about what boxes are currently available.
