Discretion... Please

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2007-09-17 18:17:46 UTC
I know there are a couple of different schools of thoughts about
asking permission when it comes to letterboxing. Some people feel
"better safe than sorry" and ask before they place a box on park land,
etc. Others feel that when it comes to non-private land, if
letterboxing is not specifically prohibited, then it is better to go
with the "don't ask, don't tell" philosophy. The feeling on this is
that when put in a position to *have* to say yes or no, many places
that do not have an official policy would opt on the side of caution
and say no. On the other hand, if they are not put in a position to
*have* to commit one way or the other, many just look the other way.

Which way of thinking you subscribe to when placing your letterboxes
is your business. However, when seeking boxes you must respect the
fact that the placer may be playing under the radar and that by
mentioning letterboxing to park rangers, etc. you very well may be
putting their letterbox in jeopardy.

I received an e-mail today with a much appreciated condition report on
the of one of my letterboxes that has been peacefully existing in an
area park for over 5 years. In the e-mail there was an additional
comment about being careful for the park rangers because they said if
they found any letterboxes they would confiscate them. I guess I'm
not sure why anyone would be having a conversion with a ranger
regarding letterboxing when they are looking for one of my boxes in
the first place.

Please, please, respect those who have placed boxes by being discreet
while letterboxing.

This has been an unpaid public service announcement. I will now step
down off my soapbox and cross my fingers that this will eventually
sink in... and hope that my box is still in place when I get over
there in a couple of weeks.
