Topic: Difficulty of clues at a gathering leading to longer times out on trail...

Our opinion...Yes, some of the clues yesterday were difficult, but that is fine! - especially if
the clues are going to be in place permanently. The problem as we see it, is that when you
are handed a book of clues at a gathering, the letterboxing saliva starts in and you want to
tackle all of them at once. We are always overwhelmed by the task. We never do very well
at gatherings (we found 7 yesterday...and 2 we had to reconfirm that we were in the right
spot and had to go back for them and dig deeper). It takes a lot of time to trace your
steps and them retrace them..we were on one trail 3 times. The price for spending that
much time is that we hardly got to meet & exchange with anyone. Tiptoe was
disappointed..Tonto and Kemosabe (who enjoyed his first gathering very much!) wanted to
spend all of the time out on the trail and were very happy. In spite not meeting very many
new boxers, we thoroughly enjoyed the trails, the boxes and the old friends!

Some suggestions have been to have easier temporary boxes for the kids...that sounds
great..as long as the likes of T&T can have a crack at them too! But to label boxes "easy"
or "difficult" is a little more tricky. We have found that some boxes that others found to
be easy were in fact difficult and the reverse has played out too. One boxer's idea of "easy
can be another's "hard"...and that is what makes the game interesting. Frustrating at
times? You bet! But, that is what keeps us pursuing the challenge... and how sweet it is
when we meet it!

Less boxes at a gathering would afford more exchange time, but could be less
challenging for the high voltage boxers....(thinking of Leapin' Lizards here) A better
suggestion is to provide therapy at the pavilion for those of us that think we MUST find
ALL of the boxes in one afternoon!

Yesterdays boxes were very well hidden...in some cases involving more digging, debris
moving, etc. than we like as it causes the site to be compromised with all the removal of
stuff, but still we enjoyed them. The question begs itself as to the perfect amount of
coverage, protection, how far into that hole do you shove it?????....Not too much and not
to little. A difficult balance indeed. On the 2 boxes that we had difficulty, we were at the
right location, but due to the hiding spot being buried we were actually standing on one of
the boxes and not knowing it!

What do all of you in letterbox kingdom think about the clueing of clues at a gathering?
Should they be easier or remain challenging? The number of boxes? How to enjoy the best
of both worlds..the finding of boxes and the meeting of others? Maybe this is too many
topics, but they all are related to one another. How to best enjoy a gathering!

Tiptoe & Tonto

Nice post, interesting observations and questions, but you didn't answer the big question: Did you get the SOCKS?! LOL

Now to your musings about gatherings: Whenever possible, we try to make a whole weekend out of an event. That way the "gotta find these boxes" urge has enough time to be satisfied while still allowing us time to socialize during the event. If clues are made available on a Friday evening or early Saturday morning, then the early birds can get hopping after the "worms" (boxes) and get a boxing fix in the morning, then spend time socializing during the afternoon. That also leaves Sunday for more boxing. This of course assumes that the boxes will remain in place for at least the entire weekend.

Another thing that we're seeing more of in recent years is a pre- and/or post-gathering during the evening. This is a GREAT idea as far as we're concerned because it's getting dark then and not so good for finding boxes anyway. We then are not torn between being out "on the hunt" and chit-chatting with our LB friends.

Regarding clues: we like them at least a bit challenging/interesting but at an event they hopefully won't require Google research to figure out. Hiding spots: we've been boxing for almost 5 years and have always read that you never "bury" a box, so wouldn't think to start looking for something buried. Covered with a rock or sticks, etc. but still somewhat noticeable when you got to the right spot.

We've got 3 events coming up in the next 8 weeks, so we're really looking forward to doing some boxing AND socializing in Iowa, Minnesota, and Illinois! I do hope the forecast for Decorah, IA improves for Saturday.... but if the weather is really bad we'll just spend more time socializing at the comfy cabin and hit the trails on Sunday!

Wisconsin Hiker
