Sharing the Hobby

From: silboxer61364 (dispatcher_riz@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-03-15 05:20:29 UTC

Has anyone ever thought about sharing the hobby of letterboxing?
Our town (Streator, IL) has a local hobby council that meets on a
monthly basis and is a group of people with different hobbies.
Every 3rd weekend in April, the hobby council puts on a hobby show at
our local armory. This year will be the 56th show. It is considered
the only Hobby Show in America I was contacted today by one of the
members to see if the group would want to participate and show the
hobby of letterboxing. There is no fee to display and the event is
open to the public. The only rule is that if you setup, you can't
tear down your display until the closing night of the show which is
Saturday. I told them I would find out and let them know next week
if people are interested.

Some of the exhibits that are displayed in the show are:

John Deer Tractors
Model Cars
GI Joes
Tea Cups
Coins
Stamps
Quilts
Model Airplanes
Amatuer Radio Operators
Dinosaurs

Xena Princess Warrior Items
Eagles
A Custom Chopper Bike
Baseball Cards
A Cane Chair Maker
WW II Collection
Lego's
...and many more, overall there is usually about 70-100 displays each
year.

If anyone is interested in participating in this let me know. I was
thinking about maybe posting some letterbox history, sample clues,
and showing off stamp carving.

Who knows? Maybe there will even by a special event stamp for those
who make it down.
