From: Gabe (ainawgsd@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-12-24 01:36:34 UTC-08:00
Anyone can play. You don't have to have found any of the boxes, done any exchanges, or attended any events at all! If you haven't found any of the boxes listed (and there are so many boxes to choose from in the Great Lakes region that many of us who have been letterboxing for years only have one or two boxes on that list...I myself don't have any I don't believe) or done any exchanges or gatherings on the list then you simply use general terms instead.

The words you pick can go anywhere in the column, you could put a general term in the upper left corner or a letterbox in the lower corner if you like as long as they stay in the same column. If they had to stay in the same row (top row letterboxes, second row exchanges, and so on) not only would people who didn't have any of those terms not be able to play, but the game would be over pretty quickly indeed and there would be a lot of multiple winners because there would only be a few ways to organize the card.

And yes, in order to exchange signature stamps with someone you would have had to meet them in person.

Kimono Girl

From: Familyof6 (familyof6portland@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-12-24 11:58:38 UTC
Actually, you can still participate in Bingo by sending Springchick
$1, a booty bingo item (prefereably something a letterboxer would
use), and a copy of your completed bingo board.

In response to your questions: Yes..in order to use a letterboxer's
name on your card you must have personally met that boxer and did an
exchange with them of some sort. For an event, you must have
personally attended the event. To use a letterbox, you must have
physically gone out and found that box and stamped in (and no....I'm
not sure it counts if a friend did it and you weren't there). So, if
none of these apply to you then you will need to move on to the
general items and fill your card in with those. Just remmber to only
fill in the B column words in the B column, and etc. or else you will
never win. Don't worry though...these words are called too jst as much
as the others, if not more. I suggest rereading the FAQ about BINGO
that SC has posted to clarify.

So, in regards to your other questions: No, the top row is not all
letterboxes, the 2nd row is not all trail names, the 3rd row is not
allevents, etc. So yes, jumble it up.

And if I may say so, welcome to the BINGO game. This is also my first
year playing, although I've closely watched and played off wager for
the last two years. It is definitely the SLOWEST bingo game you'll
ever play, but I think everyone will agree that it's more social than
anyhthing.

Check the boards daily...these people are nuts!(LOL) Hey, aren't we
all though. I mean really, we do all this to find a rubber stamp and
logbook, fighting the weather, deciphering clues, and fending off
other crazy letterboxers and wild animals (no pun intended, Mrs.
Doglvrs...lol).....and it sure is FUN!!!!!

Furthermore, you should definitely attend an event and meet some of
these people (especially a SC event!) Welcome aboard!

-Familyof6
