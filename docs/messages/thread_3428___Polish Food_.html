My mouth is watering!!! My mother-in-law is Polish and she makes pierogis for every holiday. She will make three large serving platters piled high with those little dough dumplings. Each platter contains a different filling: sauerkraut, mashed potato with onions, and shaved roast beef. Very gourmet!

My grandmother (my dad's mom) was born in Lithuania. She made different pierogis and we simply called them Lithuanian Dumplings. She rolled out the dough but never too thin. They were suppose to fill you up...and they did!!! She filled them with pan fried ground beef and pork and onions. She boiled them the first time, but we would reheat them in a frying pan with melted butter. OH! My heart! She always made a white sauce with mushrooms (sorry Fungus Woman!) and poured that over the dumplings. MMMMM!!!! I learned to make them just because they are so delicious to eat! Flour all over the kitchen....a big clean up mess....but soooooo worth the effort!

Now I'm hungry!!!

From: pell_lake_girl (mishiekins@prodigy.net) | Date: 2006-12-08 17:08:44 UTC
If you buy the Kasia's they are very very good.

Not sure if they are anywhere but in Chicago though -- Kasia's is a Polish deli
on Division. Kasia's freezes their signature pierogies and sells them in local
grocery stores. Actually I have 3 Polish delis near me and they all sell pierogies.

Plus paczkis on paczki day! [fat tuesday]. I fry them in butter with a little bit
of water mixed in with a lid on the pan. Then they are both steamed and
they get that nice brown crispy texture too.

If anyone comes to Chicago and is dying for good Polish food, The Red
Apple (or Czerwone Jablusko) has an absolutely amazing buffet.

http://www.planet99.com/chicago/restaurants/2147.html

They start you with zupy (soup), usually rosl (chicken noodle soup --
made with parsley) and grzybowa (a creamy mushroom and noodle
soup). Then you hit the buffet for salatki na zimno -- the salad course.
The Red Apple is famous for their mouth watering salads. I like to
start with plain buraczki (beet salad -- good for the blood) and they
also have it w zalewie octowym (with vinegar), as well as salatka z
porw (egg and leek salad), and they usually have salatka z makaronu
(macaroni salad), salatka fasolowa (bean salad), kiszona kapusta (cold
sauerkraut), and czerwona kapusta (red cabbage) to name a few,
plus regular salata z dressingiem (salad with dressings) and all the
add ons you would need -- cottage cheese, sour cream, apple sauce,
etc.

Then we have dania gorace -- the hot dishes, and the meat course.
yum yum, my favorite is kapusta z kielbasa (sauerkraut and Polish
sausage) which we also have at all holidays, papryka faszerowana
pomaranczowym (baked ham, carved off the bone), ryba pieczona
w sosie cytrynowo maslanym (baked fish in lemon butter sauce),
and kaczka pieczona z jablkami (baked duck with apples).

And if you don't want to be a total carnivore, like me you'll have
some jarzyny na goraco (hot veggies) -- ziemniaki (potatoes) of
course, both gotowane (boiled, with parsley) and pur, or mashed,
as well as zielona fasolka (green beans) and kukurydza (corn --
on the cob).

But the verrrrry best part of Polish food is, as we've been saying --
heavenly! fried! carbs! placki ziemniaczane (potato pancakes), which
I swear is nothing more than a blob of hot grease held together with
a few shreds of potato, mmmmm.... and racuszki (apple pancakes),
both with apple sauce, nalesniki (cheese blintzes) and of course
pierogies.

And then dessert.... I love kolacky of course!

Unfortunately they do not have the Traditional Polish Winkie at The
Red Apple. They have some sad little impostors there, but I pass them
by. [I have a picturre of a Winkie ot share but my host site won't
load today]

Also no where in here do I mention the dreaded czarnina. Duck Blood
Soup. That stuff is nasty.

Now I am starrrrrrrvvvviinnnngggggg........
