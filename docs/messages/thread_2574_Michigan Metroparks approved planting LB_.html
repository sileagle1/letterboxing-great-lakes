Hi to all the Southeast Michigan boxers out there (and those Boxers
wishing to plant in Southeast MI regardless of your dwelling
location!) -

I have succeeded in getting official approval from the Huron-Clinton
Metroparks System for us to place letterboxes in the parks :) This
means that the park rangers are aware of the sport of Letterboxing,
know where the boxes are, and won't remove or dispose of them.

The Metroparks will be adding a Letterboxing dropdown on their
website (www.metroparks.com) as well as other fun goodies!

The only thing they ask in return is for us to fill out a permit. The
permit is free and you can either e-mail, mail or fax the completed
permit with clues to Tim Philips. Once the permit and clues are
approved, you can then place your box. (They just want to ensure it's
not placed in a sensitive area, etc.)

Tim's contact info is:

Tim Phillips
Huron-Clinton Metropolitan Authority
Administrative Offices
13000 High Ridge Drive
Brighton, MI 48114-9058
tim.phillips@metroparks.com

Just wanted to share this happy news - yahoo! Now if I could just get
the Michigan State Parks to endorse LB (like the CT State Park
systems did!)....I'm working on that too!

I'm so excited!:)

And here is a cross-post of my reply at letterbox-usa.

************************

Please don't take this wrong, but some things really are better left alone. The publicity from the occasional newspaper or magazine article is damaging enough, I can only imagine the impact of a letterboxing link on the home page of the Metroparks.

Same goes for the MI State Parks. This is a very delicate issue and it is currently on the books that both geocaching and letterboxing are not allowed in MI State Parks without a permit and a fee, although few parks have the resources or the inclination to enforce this. The Michigan geocaching organization has been working very diligently with the MI DNR on this issue. Although pressure and requests from others could have a positive impact, it could just as easily have a negative impact, losing progress that MIGO has already made.

For the past several years, we have chosen to peacefully "co-exist" with the parks systems in MI -- for good reason -- it works this way. If every letterboxer does their part to ensure that they are not placing boxes in eco-sensitive areas, we are in essence policing ourselves and not drawing undue attention to letterboxing.

Just my 2 cents...

SpringChick

Re: [LbGLK] Re: Fwd: [LbNA] Michigan Metroparks approved planting LB!

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-06-02 13:06:39 UTC-04:00
The MI DNR land usage policy for State Parks can be found in the Files section of this group.

No apology needed -- no unwritten code has been violated. Everyone sees this issue differently and I can understand the perspective from which it does appear to be a good thing to have park approval. However, that opens up a whole can of other issues. There are certain organizations that have taken a firm stand against letterboxing (i.e. National Parks) and for the most part, we respect that position. But it is generally felt that when there is not a clear policy or a policy that is not actively enforced, it is better to ask forgiveness than permission. Putting a park in the position to have to say "yes" or "no" often results in a "no" just because nobody wants to be the one to say "yes" and have environmental and/or liability issues then rest of their shoulders. On the other hand, many parks are very aware of letterboxing even though they have not formally approved it, but because they have not been forced into a decision one way or the other, we peacefully co-exist. Even when they have made a positive decision, that can be changed at any time and because they have implemented a permit system, they know where the boxes are and who has planted them.

A better way to ensure that unsuspecting park rangers do not find and/or discard your letterboxes is to not make them obvious or easily stumbled upon. I doubt many park rangers have time to sit at their computer and seek out letterboxes in their park. For the most part, boxes that are found are accidental finds out in the field or boxes that are in sensitive, actively watched areas. Tougher clues, longer hikes, better hiding places, mystery boxes are all good ways to increase your chances that the boxes will not be accidentally found and disposed of.

SpringChick

Re: Fwd: [LbNA] Michigan Metroparks approved planting LB!

From: tj_mich (tj_mich@ameritech.net) | Date: 2006-06-02 17:47:41 UTC
Did the Michigan DNR give any further specifics as to what they mean
by "permitted" as it pertains to Michigan State Parks? At the time
that they first created a geocaching policy, which was January 2004,
they were charging $35 for the permit, and the cache could only be left
in place for 3 months. Is that still the requirement?

They didn't mention letterboxing in that 2004 policy, but they did
somehow let the park administrators know about it, because we were
asked to remove 3 letterboxes we'd placed in a state park in 2003. We
replanted them all in various county parks and rest stops since the 3
month limitation wasn't feasible for us. Our situation was the only
such removal request I've heard of in Michigan, though; other
letterboxes have remained 'under the radar' and haven't appeared to
lead to any problems.

On the other topic, the Metropark approval and acceptance is not
surprising, since the HCMA (the Huron-Clinton Metroparks Authority...
they administer 13 parks in southeast Michigan that are not state
parks, but are similar in size, amenities, etc.) have planted their own
geocaches in each Metropark and promoted programs related to them
("find all 13 and get a free annual pass," etc.). I think they still
have a posting on their website with this information.

--TJ_Mich
