GR Public Museum Event -- Help Needed in Carving Room

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2008-01-05 01:08:12 UTC
As many of you already know, the GR Public Museum is hosting a
letterboxing event on February 2. One of the things going on that day
will be a stamp-carving room -- this is the area I am working to
coordinate. I am envisioning that this area will be open all day and
people can wander in and out and try their hand at carving if they
wish. I would also like to try and run 2 structured carving workshops
-- one in the morning and one in the afternoon.

I am looking for help in the following areas:

* Instructor -- Anyone who would be interested in leading one of the
carving workshops. I would be willing to do one of the workshops if
necessary, but really would prefer not to do both of them. You do not
have to be an *expert* carver to do this, just able to show other
people how to go through the carving process.
* Anyone who could work a 1-2 hour shift in the carving room, just to
be available to answer questions and assist people who are giving it a
go on their own.
* Anyone who could work a 1-2 hour shift in the kids' carving area
(intention is to make foam stamps).

Also is anyone aware of a carving video/DVD in existence? I have a
written tutorial, which is great for a workshop set-up, but thought it
would be nice to have a video running during the non-workshop hours.

The museum will be supplying the carving products. They are also
providing free museum admission, parking and lunch to everyone
involved with working the event. So you could help out in the carving
area for a couple of hours and then spend the rest of the day at the
museum and get the letterboxes that will be planted there all free of
charge!

If you are interested in helping out in this area, please contact me
offlist.

Thanks!

SpringChick
