News from Delirium

From: azobox (jparkerg@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-09-01 14:24:53 UTC
Last night I was sparked to action by Dragon's question about the
well-being of our friend Delirium.
I wrote to her. This morning she responded and I'm happy to report
that they are all doing well.
By the time Katrina reached their part of Mississippi, it had been
downgraded to a tropical storm. She said the winds at 50 mph were
still fierce and at one point she feared they'd lose their roof. But
they suffered no serious damage and everyone is fine. A small silver
lining is that they saw rain for the first time since mid July.
In typical Delirium fashion, she regaled me with stories of a Michigan
woman who is out of her element. (Mostly it had to do with snakes and
locusts).

For those who are newer to this list, a little background: Delirium
was an active Michigan letterboxer who found herself, somewhat
unhappily, transplanted to the South when her husaband's job took them
to Mississippi. Her posts were pure entertainment.

She says that the presence of copperhead snakes makes box maintenance
more of a challenge than she's willing to undertake, and the
temperatures/humidity preclude much outdoor activity.

But she's fine. And so are the kids.

azobox
