Clearly more pleading is required, so here we go:

There once was a man from Nantucket...
(Oh wait, this is a family-friendly site, can't use that one.
Okay, let's try again.)

There once was a young man from Portland...
(Frap nothing good rhymes with Portland. Back to the drawing board.)


(Here we go - with sincere apologies to Edgar Allen Poe)

Once upon a morning deary while I pondered weak and weary
Over many a quaint and curious word I'd not seen before
Alice DeGarmo, queen of the Big Top?
And Gitchee Gumee made my heart drop
Did I have them? Nevermore.

Highlighter poised & at the ready, I tried to hold my poor hand steady
Searching for surcease of sorrow from the selections at my door
"Be these words our sign of parting" I longed to shriek upstarting
"Since my losing spell's unbroken!
I shall read your words no more"

Yet in my deepest yearning
I knew that I was learning
To be patient with the bingo fates
I am their servant, nothing more.

So I linger with my sorrow
wondering what will be tomorrow
As I perch here in my chair
Prepared to amble out the door.
Am I hopeful? Evermore!



Lady TrailTroll
Court Poet and Royal Sucker Upper
