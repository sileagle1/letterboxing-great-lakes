They've played the footage of both the houses collapsing and the houses
floating down the river over and over and over and over and over and over
again on TV here in the Chicago area. Every time I watch it I feel worse
and worse and worse for the people.

The front page and inside pages of the Chicago Tribune had lots of photos
today, including one that showed a farm from an aerial view -- the house,
silos, all the outbuildings, trees, etc, under water. Surreal, like an
exceptionally bad nightmare. Subject line actually should say "Rain, pain,
go away!"

Much as I wanted to build a house on a river in Wisconsin, I picked which
river verrrrry carefully, with much thought even tho it was only a very
small home just for summer, winter skiing long weekends, etc. Even at that
though, if there'd been the rain in northwest Wisconsin that there was
elsewhere in Wisconsin, I don't even know if that house would've survived
since it's neither an ark nor a houseboat. There's something to be said for
living on a houseboat I guess.

Talking about kayaking, a few years back our house here made the front page
of the local paper when one of the paper's photographers took a photo of
kids canoeing down our street when this area of Illinois got hit bad.
Unfortunately, the kids in the canoes were right in front of our house when
he snapped the photo. What a way to make the front page.
