Great Lakes Gathering 2006!

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-10-24 11:30:51 UTC
It's time to think ahead to spring! The area fall gatherings behind
us and the winter months bearing down on us, I would like to square
things away for the 2006 Great Lakes Gathering before people begin to
hibernate for the winter.

I am looking for:

- Possible locations
- People who are willing to host/assist (locale you would consider)

Please e-mail me offlist with input on either of these. Early in
November I will put up a poll of locations. Note that only locations
that appear to have a potential hoster will be put up for vote. You
can still suggest a location without volunteering to host, or vice
versa -- offer to host/assist without having a specific location in
mind, but when the poll goes up, only the locales that have a willing
hoster will be considered.

I will also be putting the date up for vote, taking into consideration
past input. I do understand the school year issue, but also know that
holding out much past the middle of June we begin to run into
vacations, so it will go up for a vote to be as fair as possible.
This past year, the date was Saturday, June 18th, which was a week
later than the previous year, which was a couple of weeks later than
the year before. I would like to see it remain within the first few
weeks of June but am open to other suggestions.

If you have any other feedback or ideas concerning next year's
gathering, please feel free to post to the list of contact me directly
via e-mail.

Thanks!

SpringChick

From: tiptoetonto (tiptoetonto@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-10-24 15:42:53 UTC
Pokagon State Park would be a wonderful park for the GLK gathering!
Tonto and I would be willing to throw our hats in with Armadillo Jo
and anyone else willing to share in the responsibilities...Although
we are technically Illinois boxers, our parents have a home in
Lagrange County...and Tiptoe is a born and raised Hoosier!
We hosted a fiftieth anniversary celebration at Pokagon a few years
ago and it turned out to be a terrific event due to the pleasant,
accomodating staff and surroundings. There is camping for those
that prefer roughing it, and the Inn is wonderful too! As I recall,
last winter, they had a news clipping posted in the nature center
promoting letterboxing. So, I would think they would be supportive
of us planting boxes so a large group could hit their trails. If
anyone is interested in checking the park out before voting...in the
winter they have a quarter mile refrigerated tobogan track that is
way cool! Also, great cross-country skiing trails.
It is an easy park to get to off main roads - we can get there in 3
hours from the south suburbs of Chicago....
Tiptoe & Tonto

Re: [LbGLK] Re: Great Lakes Gathering 2006!

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-10-25 06:19:08 UTC-04:00
I certainly consider WI to be a strong candidate for the gathering and have had a couple of other e-mails in addition to your post with this suggestion. I think the IN proposal also sounds like an excellent idea. It is my feeling that the face of letterboxing changes throughout the course of the year and to accomodate new people and concentrations of people, it is the most fair to put this up for suggestions and a vote around this time every year for the following spring/summer gathering. I also think it is important that different people be able to take turns hosting the gathering if they want to. We are fortunate to have a few people who have put together wonderful events in the past and would be willing to host again as a fall-back, but in my mind, new blood keeps things fresh and makes people feel involved.

As for the date -- I certainly do understand your feelings about mid-June or later for teachers. On the other hand -- those of us who are not teachers work a 40 hours week every week, year around and would have to make the necessary arrangements to attend the gathering despite our jobs. So while this is certainly a consideration, it is not the only consideration as far as the date goes. When I originally set the date of the gathering to be the end of May in 2003, the intention was for it to stay that weekend. The following year that got moved to the second week in June primarily for convenience of the event planner. Now last year it was moved again -- to the 3rd Saturday in June. In addition to this weekend being a perennial conflict for me personally, last year we ran into several people who were already starting into vacations and therefore not able to come.

It would be my preference that we choose a weekend and keep it consistent -- but previous attempts to do that have not held, so again I will be putting the date up for a vote.

SpringChick
