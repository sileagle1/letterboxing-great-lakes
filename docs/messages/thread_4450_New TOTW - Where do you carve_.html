I have an large armoire-style computer desk that has a table surface
that flips out to my right when I am using my PC. I have my nifty
multi-hinged arm, lighted, magnifying lens mounted to the table so I do
all my carving at my PC desk. Face front & center & I'm looking at my
monitor and typing on the keyboard; swivel to the right and I'm carving!

I don't think I could carve without the lighted lens Martini Man got me
a few years ago. My eyes are bad and I have gotten so spoiled with the
amount of detail I can see with it that I wouldn't try carving without
it. Of course I could unclamp it and move it somewhere else, but since
it is fairly large, I don't bother.

From: xxxxxxxx (BrighidFarm@comcast.net) | Date: 2007-09-20 17:36:55 UTC-05:00
I use my father's old art studio, may he rest in peace. He was practically
blind.......and I don't mean legally blind, I mean *blind* blind. The kind
of blind that glasses can't help.

He made these monstrous welded statues -- used to haunt auto junkyards for
parts -- some of his stuff, like his dinosaurs, were a couple of stories
high. Picture someone who's blind using a welder. Picture the neighbors
quickly leaving town when they'd hear the "whooshhhh" of the torches
starting up.

But since he could see shadows, and differentiate much better in extremely
strong light, his studio lacked for nothing, absolutely nothing, in the way
of light. And he had tons of magnification stuff for the smaller art that
he made.

So I design the stamps in my own art studio, then head on over to his to do
the carving. Since, because of my health, I can't stay in one position all
that long, I brought over a bunch of my exercise equipment so I can carve a
bit, exercise a bit, carve a bit more, exercise a bit more.

I put a TV in his studio -- which my father is probably turning over in his
grave about. Ooooooooooh, the idea of corrupting an art studio with a
television set! I really think he would have liked 'Meerkat Manor' tho on
Animal Planet. But 'Survivor'? I wait to watch things like 'Survivor'
until I get back home........don't want Dad haunting me any more than
necessary. Besides, it's too close to Halloween -- I don't trust Dad. :-)

~~ Mosey ~~
