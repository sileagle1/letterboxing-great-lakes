West Central Wisconsin Trip: Letterbox Report to Owners

From: agentink_and_zfan (agentink_and_zfan@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-11-27 04:16:39 UTC
Hello fellow boxers:

We just got back from a great long weekend in the Wisconsin Dells. We spent some time
letterboxing in the Dells region as well as along the Mississippi. This email will serve as a
report to box owners as well as update others on the status of boxes.

Friday started out gray, but no snow yet in the Dells. Agent Ink and Z-fan packed up the
car, everything in good shape, and headed out like two good ole boys on a long road trip.
They made their way over to Lacrosse. It was snowing by Sparta but they braved the roads
on into Lacrosse and up to Perrot State Park, but alas the park was closed to all patrons
EXCEPT deer hunters. Ah yes, blaze orange was everywhere in the woods this weekend --
including on Agent Ink and Z-fan. (No, they weren't hunting, but they WERE smart enough
to take orange along on the last weekend of gun deer season in Wisconsin). Turning
around, they made a treacherous drive on slick roads back down the Mississippi to Goose
Island. After enjoying the island birds they headed to the zoo in Lacrosse. There, two
animals made them laugh by an old well. The animals were playing in the fresh snow!
Agent Ink decided to play too and made snow angels on the ground. The snow started
tapering off as they finished the visit to the zoo so they decided to see the riverwalk
before heading back to the Dells. There were a few fisherman in boats and Z-fan even
saw a bluegill swim up right by shore. A long drive later -- they caught the snowstorm
again around Sparta -- Agent Ink and Z-fan let dinner, drinks, and music on the jukebox
helped them end the night back in the Dells as they watched five Canadian Pacific trains
haul tonnage over the trestle bridge across the Wisconsin River.

Saturday arrived as a crisp, blue-sky day in the Dells. After a hearty breakfast, Agent Ink
and Z-fan decided to go crawl along with some deer hunters into a rocky gorge that sports
a pine forest. This rocky arboretum was especially tranquil as the sun started to warm the
freshly fallen snow from the night before. Nature was at it's finest. The only signs of
humans were the footprints made by the hunters and letterboxers. After finishing a
coffee thermos while walking in the gorge, Agent Ink said "hey, let's go round the Bend in
the Wisconsin River." Z-fan though this was a cool idea. The Bend was a sacred place for
a culture older than the native indians -- so sacred that the peoples of that era buried
their dead near the Bend to rest for eternity. Z-fan was content to just stay near this spot
and watch trains go by all day but NOOOOOOO! of course not -- Agent Ink wanted to go
rock climbing. Now, you may be thinking "what in the DEVIL'S NAME would they be doing
climbing rocks after a fresh coating of ice and snow!" Exactly! See -- trains would have
been much safer! On the way to the rock climbing they got diverted by a bear cub spotted
just along the Baraboo river west of the town itself! The mother of this cub was not in
sight, but the cub was nestled up in a den hibernating in the cold air of winter. The
excitement of the bear made Z-fan hungry. There was a cool place to eat by the train
tracks but NOOOOOOOOOOOOOO -- we have to go ROCK CLIMBING! UP UP AND UP they
went. And UP AND UP AND UP AND UP AND UP AND UP AND UP AND UP -- dwelling at the
top of the rocks to admire the view. Agent Ink said "hey -- what's that OVER THERE?" So
down, down, down, down, down, down, down we went. Z-fan was disappointed that no
trains were in sight, although there was track OVER THERE. Down, down, down, down,
pant, pant, pant, pant, pant, down, down, down, down, pant, pant, pant, pant. FInally, a
beach. Wait, we have to walk all the way OVER THERE!??! An eternity later, they crossed
the train tracks that WERE OVER THERE but now WERE HERE. Egad, Agent Ink spotted more
rocks to climb and this time the REAL vertical ascent into snake country began. Billy goats
could not have climbed better in the snow and ice than they did that Saturday. Stabilizer
muscles working with each step. Legs carefully balancing the weight as they slowly
climbed higher and higher. UP AND UP AND UP AND UP AND UP AND UP AND UP. Pause,
look down -- no DON'T LOOK DOWN!!! UP AND UP AND UP AND UP. Finally, next to a
dried up well at the tip-top of the rocks, Agent Ink said "wish we had some food." "But
there was a cool place to eat back OVER THERE by the train tracks!" was all Z-fan could
exclaim. "Well, then, let's get going. Watch out for rattlesnakes." was all she said as she
took off. There she went: Agent Ink, blaze orange umbrella up in the air -- to prevent
getting shot at by the deer hunters, remember?? -- taking off down the trail. Z-fan stood
there in silence wondering how a train fan could ever have gotten UP HERE!??? With a
grunt and a grumble under his breath he took off after her -- his wife, soulmate, and best
friend. She sure looked great with that umbrella. He smiled. Down, down, down, down,
down, down, down, down, down they went. Back at the car there was just enough time to
head toward Lake Wisconsin and a bite to eat before starting back to Milwaukee. The
ferryboat was running so they stood on the shore awhile and watched it haul cars back
and forth across the Wisconsin river. Agent Ink said "funny isn't it, how this part of
Wisconsin is so different from ours." Z-fan smiled and said "Yeah. This lake is small and
you can see across it. Our lake is big and you can't. But, it also has a ferry boat. A high-
speed one. Hmmmm.... wouldn't that make a great letterbox!"

Hours later, Z-fan finished stuffing his face with pizza back in Milwaukee. A train whistle
blew in the distance. They were home. All was well...

Happy Thanksgiving 2005 everyone!

Agent Ink and Z-fan
