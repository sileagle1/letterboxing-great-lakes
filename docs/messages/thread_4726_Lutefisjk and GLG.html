Being from Minnesota, I knew some folks who liked Lutefisk, but
they're dead now. However, they did live well into their 90's.
Maybe it's a secret Scandinavian "Darwinian Delicacy"?
Here's and old Norwegian folk song to the tune of "Oh, Tannenbaum"

Oh Lutefisk! Oh, Lutefisk!
How fragrant your aroma.
Oh Lutefisk! Oh, Lutefisk!
You put me in a coma.
You smell so strong, you look like glue,
You taste yust like a overshoe.
But, Lutefisk come Saturday,
I think I eat you anyway.

Oh Lutefisk! Oh, Lutefisk!
I put you by the doorway.
I wanted you to ripen up
Yust like they do in Norway.
A dog came by and sprinkled you.
I hit him with my army shoe.
Now Lutefisk well I suppose,
I eat while I hold my nose.

Oh Lutefisk! Oh, Lutefisk!
How well do I remember.
On Christmas Eve how we'd receive
Our big treat of December.
It wasn't turkey or fried ham,
It wasn't even pickled Spam,
My mother knew there was no risk
In serving buttered Lutefisk.

Oh Lutefisk! Oh, Lutefisk!
Now all the world discovers.
How Lutefisk and Lefsa make
Norwegians better lovers.
Now all the world can have a ball,
You're better than that Geritol.
Oh Lutefisk with brennevin,
You make me feel like Errol Flynn.

Merlin
(BTW brennevin in a rich butter sauce and if you can't avoid
eating Lutefisk use LOTS of it.)
