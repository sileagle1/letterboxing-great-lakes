From: klustofin (drkaty@gmail.com) | Date: 2009-03-03 22:50:01 UTC
I just got a letter from a friend who works for the Urbana Park
District, saying that one of my letterboxes was found by a grounds
crew this past week and is in her office awaiting me. For some reason,
the box was in a flower bed, which means it was halfway across the
park from where it should have been! Oh, well.

But the more interesting part of the email was that indicated that 30
boxes are going to be planted by the park district as part of the 30th
anniversary celebration. This has me a little concerned. Any one
have any experience with "official" letterboxes? Some how the park
district having official, advertised boxes worries me, but maybe I'm
over-reacting.


Katy

Re: official boxes?

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2009-03-04 01:55:58 UTC
The staff at Porcupine Mountains State Park in Michigan U.P. have planted 8 letterboxes in their park. They are listed on letterboxing.org and I believe also at AQ. They have a display at the visitor center about letterboxing and have all of the clues available there also. They also allow letterboxers to plant "unofficial" boxes in the park without a permit as long as they are placed responsibly (i.e. not in an are that would harm the seeker or the environment) and maintained. I am very encouraged by their involvement in the hobby and their willingness to just play along rather than take control.

SpringChick
