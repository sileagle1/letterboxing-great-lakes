From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-05-04 19:17:56 UTC-04:00
I agree it is a grueling trek to the top of those stairs -- this coming from a person who climbed them 3 times in search of that box. But a letterbox is supposed to have a stamp and I would be disappointed if I got to the box and found stickers instead of a stamp. I could understand somebody putting in stickers temporarily while they carved a new stamp, but not as a permanent replacement. My feeling is that if the stamp has gone missing it either needs to be replaced or the box needs to be retired. Obviously somebody went up there to put the stickers in the box -- it would have been just as easy to put in a new stamp. Guess I find it odd that somebody would have taken that stamp anyway -- it was a store-bought and nothing special in the way of design.

When you plant a letterbox, you have to be willing to make the trek to the hiding spot now and then to check on the box. I remember whining to OldShoe shortly after I placed a letterbox at the top of one of those huge sand dunes at Hoffmaster SP. Somebody had reported that they couldn't find it, so one summer afternoon we shored the boat up and climbed the dune looking for the box. It was there, just very well hidden. I made him swear never to let me place another box at the top of a huge sand dune. I ended up having to check that box on a regular basis over the past two years due to frequent missing reports -- the tiring part was that it was always there when I went to check on it -- until this past time. I checked just a couple of weeks ago and confirmed that this time the box does seem to be missing. I won't be replacing that one.

SpringChick
