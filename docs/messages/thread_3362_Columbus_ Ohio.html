From: Barb Funk (mmfunk99@greenapple.com) | Date: 2006-11-28 17:22:04 UTC-05:00
Doodle Bug,

The Columbus Zoo Wildlights is simply gorgeous! Also, one of Safari
Man's boxes in his Pride of Columbus Series is right by the Columbus
Zoo! All his stamps are great! You will be somewhat close to a box in
Dublin, Ohio (Columbus area) that Walkabout carved and placed. It is
super quick and very well worth going to find as this is at a waterfall
well hidden from traffic just yards away! This box is called the Blue
Bird Letterbox .
Also there is a brand new box posted right in the same area--Dublin. It
is called Hayden Falls. Leatherlips and the Watchhouse are both part of
the series that go with Field of Corn and they are in Dublin as well. I
thought these locations were exceptional. If I recall, you have to do a
tiny bit of research before going to those two boxes. These last two
are relatively quick finds---20 minutes tops including stamping and
getting back to your car.

I hesitate to suggest more boxes not knowing how much time you will
actually have to go hunting, but the boxes in Delaware might be a good
option as well. There are quite a few.

We are all glad you are both coming down our way!!!
Trailtracker

Re: [LbGLK] Columbus, Ohio

From: Autumn Emigh (bunnyslippers@columbus.rr.com) | Date: 2006-11-28 17:44:16 UTC-05:00
There are a number of boxes that are in Dublin, very near to the zoo. We
have enjoyed Watch Tower, Leatherlips, Field of Corn, Hayden Falls, and
Blue Bird Letterbox. There is one in the Powell area, also near the zoo,
called Connecticut State Bird Nest in Ohio. It is a gorgeous stamp - and
not a real long hike.

If you go to do Ace of Aces in Greenlawn Cemetary, be sure to check out The
President's Men which is also there. They are not real close to the zoo,
however, so it may be out of the way - up to a half hour depending on traffic.

I hope you enjoy the Wildlights - we were just there this weekend and it
was great. :)

Smiles,
Bunnyslippers
