Ever think Mother Nature has it out for you?

From: Sean Curry (seanchiro@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-07-22 21:16:12 UTC-07:00
I have to share my experiences from the last three weeks with everyone, because those that know me will really die laughing, and those that don't will find it too.

I have officially discovered that the outdoors doesn't like me. It started a three weeks ago, when my wife and I were traveling to find some Grail boxes, and successfully found all of the Adult Swim letterboxes (Thanks Worms!!) Upon such quest, we came upon a ravished hoard of mosquitoes, and a burning tree. After stamping in, and loosing a pint of blood each, we put out the burning tree (a sign from God?) and went to lick our wounds. (Finding an official count of 142 bites on me alone).

Fast forward a week and I being the only adventuring one, traverse a nice grove of shiny three leaved plants that seemed to enjoy my company and latched onto my legs. Yup a quick visit to the dermatologist confirmed that it was infact poison ivy. One Cortisone shot later and not only did the poison ivy start to go away, but the itching from the bug bites went away too. (let me tell you a bug bite on top of poison ivy does not cancel eachother out.)

Now today (and I am writing this after being released from the emergency room) I decide to go out without my wife to find a couple of boxes that Atom 118 planted and I end up getting stung twice by a wasp. Within ten minutes of stamping in, I broke out in hives along my upper body and arms, my face started to get puffier, and sweat began to pour down my face as my arms felt like they were on fire.

Well 911 put me on hold for ten minutes and everyone along the path seemed to avoid me because I had my dog with me. As I was sitting there on my gurney, I remembered that I had forgotten to read the waiver of responsibility agreement that comes at the bottom of all of our clues.

Lesson of the day: I should have not worried about the twenty minutes to get to Elgin from Des Plaines. I should have gone with plan A and tried to get to the Cottontail's for thier garage treasure stamp, and forgotten thosr local stamps all together.

Thanks for reading guys. Don't take it out on me if you didn't like this post, just keep it on and relay any funny injury related trail stories, so we can all learn from each other's mistakes.

Chirodoc, aka Sean

P.S. I should have gone to the Ren Fair....


The difference between nothing and something is one.
