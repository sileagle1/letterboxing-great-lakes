From: pennypenny_sl (PennyPenny@cablespeed.com) | Date: 2007-08-03 15:23:32 UTC
Hi friends,

Well... my 3rd South Lyon box has been vandalized; just got an email
saying there's a bag with pages left but nothing else. Real similar to
the last lost, so I'm really suspecting a single person doing this.

Because of it, I've decided not to post any clues for the 4 SL boxes
on the national websites, and I will also pull the others if they have
the same fate.

I think I'll go strictly by word of mouth (or word of email!) and only
give out clues to trusted LB'ers that I already know and who've
already found my other boxes with valid names/stamps/finds as I determine.

This really stinks to have to do this, but I think for a while it's
the only answer unless I want my stamps to keep disappearing.

Bummed in South Lyon,
PennyPenny

From: pennypenny_sl (PennyPenny@cablespeed.com) | Date: 2007-08-03 19:53:58 UTC
Hi Flotilla...

You know, I'm not sure! I actually don't even know many people in this
town and I'm a pretty nice person (I think! LOL...) so I know it's not
personal. I really think it's something like this: Either someone saw
someone else retrieving a box... and they then have been doing this.
Or it's a KID of someone who's parents took them boxing (I've got lots
of stamp ins from kids that are in groups, like a girl scout group, a
school group, a summer camp group... and then they haven't kept quite
about it all. Most likely they live right in the area. I do see a good
amount of young punk "riff-raff" later in the day at the park near the
Depot (I take my 5 year old there often)... so I really suspect a
teenager or young person doing this... especially since the last box
gone is in a spot where it is really easy to find/put back the box
WITHOUT being seen. There's no other reason than vandalism that THAT
one would be gone (You've been there... the one within the bridge;
inside the lower beam... it's really in a good area for a box.)

Anyway... as I said in the post, it really sucks to have to do this,
but I think after the almost 3 years I've been doing this now, I have
a pretty good list of people like yourself that I can safely share
clues with, and then hopefully they will likewise share with only
people they trust. As I talk to more people about this, it sounds
like that's the way some have already gone.

I also am going to work on placing more boxes in places that take
longer to get to (like your A Christmas Story, for instance :-) I know
that will probably help a lot. My creepy little cretin trashing my
boxes in South Lyon will probably not have the transportation,
motivation, or stamina to take a hike over 1/8 mile long, lol!!!

Thanks for your concern... as always, if you have any ideas, I'm all
ears :-)

Penny

Hi Bali... there's many "regulars" on this list (you included) that
I'd have no problem passing clues on to. I'm keeping the site up, but
on the page for those particular boxes in South Lyon, it simply says
I've pulled all the boxes. That will remind others to ask about them
(so I'm thinking) and then I'd pass along the clues. So when you're
ready, let me know ;-) BTW... Take your time, as there's only one
ready right now, since I have to re-do and re-carve the others.

Oh yeah... and on the ballpark one you mention: I did figure out that
the hiding space was JUST high enough for a hardball (not a softball
though) to slide under... and when I first placed it -- I should have
tested it -- I thought it too small a space for a baseball to go
under. But being that a ball could fit under, it was only time before
some kid had to retrieve a ball and find the LB under there. So that
one WAS bad placement on my part... even though it was hidden far back
and near a grassy side of the box. That will definitely go elsewhere
in the park, LOL!
