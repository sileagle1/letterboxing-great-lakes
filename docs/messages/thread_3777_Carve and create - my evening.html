Carve and create - my evening

From: speedsquare_lbxr (ruhlette@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-02-24 03:25:52 UTC
I spent this evening making a new book with coptic stitch. I really enjoyed making the
different books and I've been planning to make more. Tonight I took the plunge. Visited
Hobby Lobby without my coupon, but I came away with just 8 sheets of paper (my dh was
there to keep me from buying one of each). I scarfed some cardboard from various sources.
The thread I bought wouldn't fit into my largest needle so I had to settle on embroidery
thread I already had on hand. I also had exactly 12 sheets of leftover paper that is perfect for
the application. As soon as I can find my camera, I'll post a picture.

I didn't fuss over the dimensions and it looks great. The outside cover has shamrocks and
the inside has plaid lines. 48 pages to fill; I plan to take this little book to the March 17th
gathering in Indianapolis.

Now that I've started a new tradition, I think I'll have to make a much bigger one for June.

~speedsquare


Re: [LbGLK] Carve and create - my evening

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2007-02-24 07:07:03 UTC-05:00
Speedsquare, your book sounds great! I have started the same tradition - an individual handmade book for each gathering. It will be fun to have this collection of unique books filled with stamps specific to that gathering. The one I made for the Carve & Create last week was only 4 thin signatures and still has a lot of empty pages because we were a small group, but June will require a thick book for sure! I did double needle coptic stitch for the binding and was really happy with the results. I may even get more creative on the next one and add in a couple of envelopes and perhaps a different type of paper to mark sections -- i.e. event boxes, exchanges, etc. Can't wait to see photos of your new book!

SpringChick
