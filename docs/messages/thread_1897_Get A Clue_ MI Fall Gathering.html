Get A Clue! MI Fall Gathering

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-10-17 00:55:11 UTC
Well, it's less than a week now until the MI Fall Gathering --
Saturday, 10/22 at Yankee Springs Rec Area near Middleville. I'm
really looking forward to seeing everyone! I was out at the park
this weekend and it is gorgeous there -- the color is really
beginning to pop and the lake is beautiful.

I've received a lot of the Clue game stamps in the mail and have
received word from several others that they are one the way. We
should have plenty of stamps for extra players and the game promises
to be a lot of fun!

I've also received a couple of boxes for planting and am aware of
other folks who have planted boxes themselves. Remember, if you
planted boxes yourself -- bring clues to the gathering to pass out
to the other attendees. I am only planning to bring clues for the
boxes that I've planted (and probably the Election Series since I'm
not sure if Lucky Charms is around or not). If you are coming and
would still like to plant a box, there are a lot of wooded areas
right around the shelter. You should easily be able to find a spot
in the general vicinity once you get there without heading out onto
the hiking trails.

After much debate (and many good suggestions) for the workshop
topic, we have finally decided. Since we have a mix of new
letterboxers and those who have been to previous gatherings, we
didn't want to repeat the stamp carving or logbook workshops. So
we've decided to try something a little different. Instead of doing
a workshop, we will be hosting a forum. We're calling it "Building
a Better Letterbox." The forum will cover topics like:

- Ideas/themes for your letterbox
- What to include in your letterbox
- Containers that work well (and those that don't)
- Camoflauge ideas
- Choosing a location
- Hiding techniques
- Clues

The idea of a forum is a group discussion and sharing of ideas
rather than one person dishing out all the information. So, come
prepared with questions and ideas and be ready to participate in the
discussion. Let's all use our experiences and help each other place
letterboxes that are lots of fun for folks to find and stand a
better chance of survival.

So, where do you go when you get there? (if you've looked at the
map, Yankee Springs is a big place!) If you are coming off U.S. 131
(either north or south) take exit 61 and follow M-179 east about 7
miles, following signs to the Gun Lake Unit of Yankee Springs. Once
past the little toll booth (you will need either an annual or daily
MI State Parks sticker - I believe a daily pass is $5.00), the road
splits - campground is to the right; you want to follow signs for
the beach, boat launch and shelters to the LEFT. Pass a long wooden
bridge/boardwalk on your right, the fishing pier on your right, the
beach house on your right, and then the shelter will be on your
left. There are two picnic shelters in the park - both are out on
the peninsula that ends at Murphy's Point - one is partway down and
the other near the tip. We are in Shelter #1, which is the one that
is partway down the peninsula. We will be there around 7:00 a.m.,
but the gathering doesn't officially get underway until about 8:00
a.m. The shelter has electricity, but the water has already been
shut off for the season. There is a grill there and we are planning
to grill hotdogs for the group, but you could easily throw on
something else if you'd like.

One last thing... there are areas of Yankee Springs Rec Area that
allow hunting and it is hunting season. Although hunting is not
allowed in the hiking areas, the trails are posted as pass-through
hunting areas and some of them run adjacent to game area land -- you
will hear gunshots in the area and it might not be a bad idea to
wear bright colors on the trails. Also, I saw quite a lot of poison
ivy, so be watchful and if you are especially susceptible, take
necessary precautions.

If you have any questions, please do not hesitate to e-mail either
myself or Azobox. See you Saturday!

SpringChick
