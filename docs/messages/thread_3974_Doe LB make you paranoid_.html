Doe LB make you paranoid?

From: baliddle (baliddle@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2007-04-17 19:55:06 UTC-04:00
Ok, seriously, I don't recall being overly paranoid in my life. But
something about this hobby makes me overly so. Even out hunting, I
start thinking that everyone is being overly suspicious of
everything. Are we standing in this one place too long? Are they
looking at the paper I'm holding in my hand? Are they wondering why
I have that big backpack for this not too long walk? And on and on.

But, today was something else. I think I had legitimate reason to be
paranoid. I was out doing some planting today. I was in a cemetery
that is a pretty visible place, but I don't think I was looking
overly suspicious. I think I looked like I was just walking around
looking at the stones. But, I was SURE these people were watching my
every move while I was there. I was right by their fence line for a
little while and she came out of the house right then "looking at a
flower bed by the fence". While I was there, I made a special point
to stop and read lots of stone. I was pretty far away from them
while I was doing the actual plant and I don't think they could see
me put the box away, so I'm not too worried they'll go find it. I
think they were kind of watching the whole time I was there,
though. As I was leaving the place, I think the guy got in his
truck, drove around and followed me. The road I was on was split
so that to go north, you have to go south first and turn around. We
both did that, then both turned left on the next main road. I turned
into another cemetery that was around the corner (just in case he was
watching me, I figured it would add credibility to my first visit. He
bypassed the cemetery and kept going. I turned around and was
pulling back out on the main road. As I was doing so, he came back
from the other direction, so he obviously turned around somewhere
back down the road. He then proceeded to turn down his road and went
back to his house. He did not have time to go anywhere else on this
little trek. He didn't drive into the first cemetery at all before
he went home. Isn't that weird? I am being paranoid, or do you
think he was following me or that he was planning on coming to the
first cemetery to see what I was doing, but I left before he had a
chance? Or am I completely crazy and whatever he was doing had
nothing to do with me?

Does anyone else get these paranoid feelings when you are planting or hunting?


BNL Bali Grl

From: xxxxxxxx (BrighidFarm@comcast.net) | Date: 2007-04-17 20:07:55 UTC-05:00
Y'know, that's not really so odd with a cemetery. Was the cemetery kind of
on the small to medium side?

There's soooo much vandalism and theft going on in cemeteries nowadays that
a lot of people who work for cemeteries or own/manage them or who live
bordering them or across the street from them, etc. are often starting to
keep a much closer eye on things.

One thing I do when heading out for either a medium-sized or small cemetery
is to always carry a camera and a clipboard and I don't dress too
grungy-looking. Then if anyone looks unusually interested or worried about
why I'm there, I engage them in some sort of casual conversation.

A lot of times, before I leave for the cemetery, I'll look it up on the
internet and see what I might be able to find about it's history.
graveyards.com is a pretty good place to start. Finding out a bit about the
cemetery might give me something to ask a question or two about or might
present with me with some subject that I could pretend to be interested in.

Sometimes I also introduce myself as a member of RAOGK (Random Acts of
Geneological Kindness), an internet group of research volunteers who assist
folks around the country in finding ancestors, getting copies of records,
taking photos of gravesites, etc. Which I am actually a member of.

Or sometimes I'll stop at the grocery store and get a small bunch of cut
flowers to leave on a grave, pick a grave that looks like it needs some
visiting, make sure the worried person sees me set the flowers there, and
then maybe wander a bit looking at headstones. But it shows them that I was
there for a purpose other than to harm their cemetery, even if I'm not
really there for the purpose I'm making them think I'm there for. And if
they're close enough, I might engage them in a bit of casual conversation
about how interesting the cemetery looks or now nice the maintenance seems
to be, etc, just in case praise helps allay their worries.

Instead of flowers, sometimes I'll bring a medium-size pebble along in my
pocket to set on a gravestone and stand there a bit as if I'm visiting that
gravesite. Pebbles left on gravestones are a traditional "I was here to
visit you" thing.

Sometimes, however, engaging worried people in conversation can backfire,
and I can end up spending far more time at the cemetery than I planned to if
the folks actually taking a liking to me, and relax, and want to show me
this or that that they feel I might be interested in. Which, if the boxer
is a person like me, who happens to love cemeteries, I'm just fine with
anyway.

I actually became very interested in the Civil War because of something like
this. I was out taking some photos for an RAOGK request for a family who
wanted photos of a relative's gravesite. The cemetery was locked up,
surrounded by an 8 foot fence, so I had to get a township employee to agree
to meet me out there and unlock it for me to go in and take photos. When
they heard it was an RAOGK request, a gentleman with the local historical
society met me out there because he wanted to show me that they also had a
gravesite there of a Revolutionary War soldier. So I also took a bunch of
photos of that.

When he saw I was interested in the Revolutionary War soldier, he told me
that they also had a small totally secluded cemetery of Civil War veterans
that they were in the process of restoring and would I like to see their
work so far? I said ooooooooooh, of course! The cemetery was completely
off the road and in the woods, couldn't be seen by road or air, so over the
river and thru the woods we went, and, at the age of 50, I suddenly ended up
interested in Civil War history. I'd love to plant a letterbox there but I
know the historical society people would do me severe bodily damage if I
did. :-) It's a verrry fragile site.

And sometimes there's a problem because some cemeteries look "public" but
are actually "private" and people who aren't directly visiting a gravesite
of a friend or relative aren't really welcome there, visiting by "strangers"
is discouraged. Tho, if it's not posted, it's not considered trespassing,
tho they might ask a person to leave and then person has to leave, no
choice.

There's a cemetery here in Lombard, IL that's like that. It's not posted,
but it's considered private by the owners. It's not protected by any
fencing or anything and they've had serious problems with vandalism and
theft in the past. Thankfully, it's pretty visible from a busy road so the
police drive by and keep an eye on it and if people drive by and see someone
there, they might call the police on their cell phone and the police will
pay a visit and tell the people to skedaddle. But the day I was there, the
police dropped by but allowed me to stay, so they're selective in who the
chase off.

~~ Mosey ~~
