St. Nick's Gathering-Dec. 2

From: Carol (carol_beetle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-11-28 05:30:33 UTC
The mild weather we are experiencing is predicted to drop into the
colder temps by the end of the week. But since the weathermen seem to
be wrong more often than right when it comes to predicting the weather
from day to day, let's just keep our fingers crossed. Regardless,
letterboxers know how to dress in warm layers and come prepared for a
fun afternoon of letterboxing and friendship.

Wisconsin Hiker's chili will fill our tummies and keep us warm. But
warm beverages will be also be needed. I can bring a propane stove,
cans of propane, and two large pots to heat hot water for beverages.
I will bring a box of tea bags, sugar, hot beverage cups, and spoons.
I'm not sure if anyone would like instant coffee (not my favorite),
but maybe someone would like to bring some instant coffee and cream,
and maybe hot chocolate mix and marshamallows or whipped cream too.
Just some thoughts to keep us warm.

Also there may or may not be a source for water because water access
may be shut off for the winter months. Does anyone know if there will
be water at the shelter? If not, maybe several people could haul some
bottled water.

Beetle

Hi Beetle,

The bottled water is a very good idea. While there are water
fountains at that shelter (if I remember right from this fall). The

I just got a Weather Alert from Weather Underground (my favorite
weather site) stating the drastic weather change will be here in
about 48-60 hours. Our local weather people should consult
Wunderground for the weather, LOL, because more often than not, they
are right on target - go figure!

Anyway, see you all there! My husband just finished up carving our
first carved stamp last night. We had been using a really, really
old stamp that I had owned since the 90's, LOL! So, we will have
that stamp with us to use for the first time on Saturday -
Hurray! It is a little bigger than I expected, but pretty fun (I
think). Mr. Cat Herders put all his drawing skills together with some
really old carving skills to do it. It may get re-carved one more
time on the pink stuff between here and there because he is a
perfectionist, but oh well! As long as he is not making me handle
the sharp things, we are good, LOL!

The Cat Herders
