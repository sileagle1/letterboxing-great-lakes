From: LbNA

Sent: Fri, Oct 26, 2012 10:49 am
Subject: [WM-LbNA] Indiana DNR Caching Policy

Message to Webmasters,

From: Brad Bumgardner:

Message:
Greetings,
The Indiana DNR, in collaboration with local geocachers, has revised the
recent
geocaching policy for caches on DNR owned and managed properties. This
policy
also applies for letterboxing activities. Most changes to the policy
benefit
geocachers, but one aspect shared among the two policies is the licensing of

physical geocaches on DNR lands.

The Indiana DNR is currently working with the various geocaching websites to

remove all geocache listings online pending a new license is submitted.
Similar
to the other sites, the Indiana DNR is seeking the archival of DNR land
geocaches and letterboxes currently.

The full text of the policy and licensing process can be found on one of our

newer web pages at www.geocaching.dnr.in.gov.

If there are any questions, please do not hesitate. I will be overseeing
the
permitting statewide through the end of the year and will be able to provide

assistance in identifying caches in need of the new license.

Thanks


From: Brad Bumgardner, E-mail: bbumgardner at dnr.in.gov
The request was made from 108.59.48.1

From: drewclan11 (drewclan@aol.com) | Date: 2012-10-26 20:30:19 UTC
Thanks for forwarding this (I sent it to the wrong yahoogroup...)

fyi, the Indiana DNR plans to ask LbNA to archive every "unlicensed" box on DNR property into the LbNA Basement. If you have a box on DNR property, or know of one, please get a license to keep it. Being good neighbors with the people who administer public lands is both a good idea and required by the LbNA Terms of Use, which we all agree to when we post clues on letterboxing.org.

The Indiana DNR website to get that done is below.

Jay in CT
