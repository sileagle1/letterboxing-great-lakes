From: estella_naparella (estella_naparella@yahoo.com) | Date: 2011-04-05 16:05:21 UTC
Hi all!
Skootching Dog and I are planning a summer of short road trips to go
boxing. I'm loving the planning of it all (really geeking out on it),
but I do have an odd question (probably been asked when 1: I wasn't
boxing yet or 2: not paying attention). It's a question of taste and
manners.

If you are from WAY out of town, do you seek out the newest (just
posted) letterboxes to get the first finders, or do you let the home
team fight it out first and pick it up on your way out of town?

We've gotten to know many (most) of the Minneapolis/St. Paul
letterboxers, and quite a lot of my entertainment is seeing who got to a
new plant first. It's been a while since I haven't recognized a name on
the FF post, while it wouldn't offend me at all to not know the FF
(locally), I like seeing who got it from among my friends.

What are your opinions on this?

Cheers!
E.N.

From: (Mathw613@aol.com) | Date: 2011-04-05 21:29:15 UTC-04:00
I haven't read only of the replies to this message yet, so this is my
opinion.

While I wouldn't specifically look for new boxes that haven't been found
yet, I wouldn't let it deter me from being FF to one (or more).

Yes, I have a little evil laugh when I am FF on a box not in my typical
stomping grounds.

If it's out there and clues are posted, it's fair game.

Again, I wouldn't look exclusively to be FF and rule out any other boxes --
if it's the FF prize you're after, you may be very disappointed. I don't
usually put in FF certificates or anything. I have found certificates,
bookmarks, trinket, LTC or even a Pathtag used as FF prizes . . . but the VAST
majority of the boxes I've been first to don't have anything.

What I do have though are bragging rites :-)
