From: Kurt and Janna (ode@comcast.net) | Date: 2009-01-25 21:31:54 UTC-06:00
I recently met a wonderful group of women of all ages who gather on the 4th
Saturday of each month to scrap till their heart's content! It's an all day
event, 9am-5pm, with a potluck that usually has a theme. They meet at the
Westchester library service center in Chesterton (not the library itself).
It is loads of fun! I hope to see some of you there. I know I hope to be
at the next one!

I graduated in '82. I've been back a number of times--one of my
college friends is now a professor there. And it is hugely different.
Most of old campus is gone--lots of open space. Alot of the frat
houses are no more--just empty lots. The new library--not so new
anymore--is gorgeous. And they just put a new union in where the
library used to be. And the area around campus--oh my goodness. I
remember that we had to go to Merrilville if we wanted to do much of
any shopping. Campus is now surrounded (no wonder some college kids
have trouble with credit card debt): restaurants, Starbucks, Kohls,
Walmart, etc.

I graduated in '04 and was there for a lot of the stuff going up, the
new library, the new Met building behind Scheele, a lot of the
shopping, and still a lot has changed in less than 5 years. Although,
I'I'm amazed that nothing has gone in near Walmart where that
entertainment place used to be. IS the new union as cozy in areas as
the old one? I rather liked that there was a fireplace in the old one
even if it didn't get used. I'm also curious about the flags and the
Round Table area, not to mention Jesters. As for Walmart, I'm GLAD it
was there. It was one of the few things that a lot of us could go to
when we were there without cars. Those night Walmart runs were great
for relieving boredom and such. Even got to see a ghost while walking
beside the cemetery on one such trip.
