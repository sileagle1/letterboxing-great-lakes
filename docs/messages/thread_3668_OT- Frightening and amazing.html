I wanted to share a website with you that is a good idea to visit. It is www.familywatchdog.us
When you visit this site you can enter your address and a map will pop up with your house as the small icon of a house and red, blue, green, dots surrounding your entire neighborhood

When you click on these dots, a picture of a person will appear with an address and the description of the crime he or she had committed.

The best thing is that you can do is show your children pictures and see how close these people live to your home or school.

This site was developed by John Walsh from Americas Most Wanted . Another tool to help us keep our kids safe.

From: xxxxxxxx (BrighidFarm@comcast.net) | Date: 2007-02-01 18:33:28 UTC-06:00
And the bad thing is that so many of these predators leave and go elsewhere
without notifying the authorities so that there can still be plenty in an
area that *aren't* on the map. I think it was in the news recently here in
IL that with something like 30 percent of the sexual predators, the police
actually don't have a current address on them.

I live in DuPage County in IL and the county police also maintain a website
for citizens to check out what sex offenders are living in the county. And
there's also www.mapsexoffenders.com which is a site similar to the
familywatchdog site.

The principal of one of the schools in my area was telling me that they only
receive updates from the police every 3 months. So the person could have
lived there for quite awhile before the police notify the school. So he
keeps checking the site himself to see any updates that might be there.

And a lot of us mail carriers also check the list as to who's living on our
routes so that we can keep an eye out in case we see any of them attempting
to get together with any children. Especially those of us with schools
and/or playgrounds in our route areas. I have 3 schools on my route and
it's astounding the number of convicted predators living just outside the
boundaries. In IL, they cannot reside within 500ft of a school or
playground. 500ft ain't much. With the width of lots around here being
around 50ft, that's only 10 houses away. That's, like, right down the
block.

Something I've never understood, however, is why residential homes that are
also schools for mentally handicapped children don't qualify as a school if
they have children going to school there that are minors. In Lombard, there
are two sex offenders living right across the street from a home and school
for mentally challenged children. And it's not just a residential home --
it's also a school for children who live at home with their families. So
why wouldn't that qualify for the 500ft distance? To me, all schools should
qualify -- public, private, special ed., etc. But these two predators are
right smack dab across the street. The school does seem to keep a good eye
on the kids. I don't doubt they know those two guys are right across the
street.
