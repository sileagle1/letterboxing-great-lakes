From: enigma3742 (Enigma3742@hotmail.com) | Date: 2008-02-17 17:19:31 UTC
Hey you Great Lakers! Wow, it's been a long time since I logged on
to this group - way too long! I guess that's sort of par for the
course when you move across the country, to lose track of what's
going on in your former back yard.

I wanted to pop on today though to mention We Live and Breathe
Letterboxing, the weekend long gathering Der Mad Stamper and friends
are holding out here in the Portland, OR to celebrate 10 years of
American Letterboxing - or, as DMS thinks of it, 10 years since his
life was changed by that Smithsonian Magazine he picked up in a
doctor's office waiting room.

We opened up registration for the event yesterday, and wow, has there
been a massive response! We have tons of confirmed attendees from
all over the United States...Florida, Rhode Island, Georgia, New
York, you name it.....only as we began processing the bombardment of
online forms, I couldn't help but think, "Where the heck are all the
Great Lakers? Feel free to pipe up if I'm wrong, but I didn't see a
single trail name I recognized from Indiana, Wisconsin, Michigan, or
my home state of Illinois.

So I just had to swing by, and make sure you guys knew what was up.
The official website for the event is here:
www.weliveandbreathe.com

There are two yahoo groups devoted to helping attendees with similar
needs find each other for roadtrip buddies, or room on a Portland
letterboxer's couch if you want to stay for longer than just the
weekend. One is here:
http://groups.yahoo.com/group/NeedaBedGottaBed/

I'll post the other as soon as I can remember the url.

This is quite literally going to be the LB event of the decade, so I
sure hope I get to see some of you there - we still have room right
now, but registration is filling up FAST so I don't know how long
that will be the case. You won't want to miss it!

-RaqsEnigma

Ok, here's the other yahoo group I mentioned:
http://groups.yahoo.com/group/Liveandbreatheroadtrip/

This one is devoted to finding people to drive with if you want to
make a roadtrip out of coming to the event. Mainekokopellian started
it for the East Coasters (there are people driving to Oregon from
Connecticut!)but it's purpose has been expanded to help people in all
states find potential travel buddies.

-----
I didn't mention before the part of the weekend that I'm probably the
most excited about - we're having a real Letterboxer's Masquerade
Ball on Saturday night, where participants are invited to dress as
their trail persona, or wear what ever other silliness they feel
like. It's truly going to be a night to remember!

If anyone has any questions or concerns or just wants more
information, feel free to email me at enigma3742 at hotmail or just
ask away right here and I'll be happy to address that for you!
