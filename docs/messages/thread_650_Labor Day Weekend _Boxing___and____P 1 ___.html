Labor Day Weekend 'Boxing...and....P 1 !!!

From: Doodle & Deedle Bug (doodle_n_deedle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-09-07 07:37:17 UTC-07:00
Deedle & I decided to hit Geagua County on Saturday for some weekend 'boxing. On the way out there we found the Euclid Creek box.

Box #2 on our list was Big Creek Park. I swear, someone up there must know when we plan on going letterboxing because it started to pour as we tried to find the park's entrance (which was a little difficult because the road leading to the park was closed on both ends for construction!). We finally found a way to get to the park, and thank goodness we bought those rainjackets. We ate our picnic lunch first, and by then the rain let up. Beautiful park, and we passed a pond filled with lillypads. Being the city girl that I am, I'd seen lots of lillypads but never actually seen a frog on any of them before. Much less seven of them.

Box #3 was supposed to be Burton's Log Cabin, but given the warning about "trail might be muddy after rain" in the clues, and references to the, um, atmosphere of this local on this list by previous finders, we decided to skip that one for the time being. It was also about 2 PM by this time.

So next stop was the Punderson Park box. Lesson learned: cross country ski trails are always going to be soggy and buggy. Well, the fact that it poured a few hours earlier didn't help, either. We finished up with West Woods. All-in-all, a nice afternoon, but to all the power boxers out there - are all ya'all crazy? We only found 4 boxes and were exhausted by the time we got home (whcih was 7 PM). On paper they seemed close together, we just didn't take into account all the half hour hikes....or the humidty...

So then Monday we finally did it, we placed our first box! Kumbaya is resting peacefully in Strongsville, Ohio, and we'll get clues posted tonight.

Back to reality (aka work)

Doodle aka Diane.

From: Timbertoes (toomey@genevaonline.com) | Date: 2004-09-07 16:22:02 UTC
Doodle & Deedle,

Congratulations on your first plant!! Isn't it a great feeling to
know you've put something out there that will bring such enjoyment?
I especially love checking on my boxes after a while to see the new
stamp-ins. It's also fun to find out what your local boxers think of
your new boxes (i.e. clues, stamp, overall experience). I often
imagine (especially when I'm at work!) people hiking the trail and
deciphering the clues - were they too hard or too easy? Is the trail
in good shape? Has the park run out of trail maps (which actually
happened recently)? Great fun!

Now that you've broken the ice I'll bet you've got plans for lots
more!

Timbertoes
