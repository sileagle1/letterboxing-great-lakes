the return from OH

From: Armadillo Jo (jes7o@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-08-24 16:56:15 UTC-07:00
Boy. What a day. We didn't get any boxing done on
Sunday...but yesterday we hit Geneva-on-the-Lake to
first find the Eddie's Grill Box. Fantastic view of
Lake Erie from this park. We couldn't find the other
Geneva box. It has probably become a victim of poor
hiding. Under bushes do not make good box hiding
places. Planted the International City box in
Lorain...found the French Creek box. It's funny, my
mom grew up in Lorain and had never been to French
Creek before yesterday. This park had the most visible
wildlife variety of any park we've been to so far. We
saw: huge butterflies, a baby snake, the most unusual
helicopter insect I've ever seen, and right in front
of the box, a turtle standing guard on the trail. Very
cool. We then got ambitious and headed towards the One
Hundred Acre Woods. Oh dear. This is the kind hunt
that is great in the late fall and winter when the
ground freezes. In the summer, it is mud and mosquito
HELL. We found 4 of 5, box #2 was being guarded by a
SNAKE. Ug. I'm sad to say that Piglet's logbook is
kaput. It was so wet that all of the ink practically
disintegrated and it's just a multicolor blob of
paper. If anyone goes to the area, bring a fresh log
for poor Piglet! We didn't have any extras along.

-Armadillo Jo

ps- Sorry I couldn't meet up with you Doodle, our
schedule was completely bizarre for the entire trip!

From: Timbertoes (toomey@genevaonline.com) | Date: 2004-08-25 02:45:59 UTC
Speaking of that S-word, a couple weeks ago I was attempting to find
one of Dragon's Vampire Series boxes and, while on the wrong trail
WAY too far past where I should have been, I came across TWO snakes
hogging up the entire path! I was already freaked out, because no
one knew where I was and there were three or four suspicious-looking
fishermen hanging about (more the "I have nothing else to do today so
I guess I'll fish"-type than the outdoorsman-type). I had read some
of the TOTW safety tips the night before and did have a whistle with
me, it was the middle of the day and I tried to look like I knew
where I was going (see, some of the tips stuck with me!). So here
are these snakes and they didn't look one bit scared of ME, so I
jumped clean over both of them, Olympic-style. I never did find the
box, because I tried to cram the find into about 25 minutes from
leaving work to picking up my kids - and that included changing from
work clothes into casual clothes. Man, what it takes to get a few
minutes of letterboxing time! (smile) Once the boxes are back from
the Monster Mash, I'm going to give it another go - after the snakes
hibernate!

Timbertoes

From: Doodle & Deedle Bug (doodle_n_deedle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-08-25 07:13:26 UTC-07:00
Glad to hear you had a great time in Ohio! The 2nd Geneva-on-the-Lake box had been pulled already because of possible poison ivy in the area where it was planted, so don't worry, the planter was the reason it was AWOL (she notes this on the clue page, which of course was after you'd already set out for your trip).

Can't wait to go hunt the box you planted, and I'm happy to hear that French Creek is alive and well. Deedle & I tried to find it earlier in the summer but had no luck...we decided we'll go back in the fall when all that foliage stuff is dead. Maybe that turtle will still be there to help us out....

And how sad that Piglet's logbook is kaput...I'm not sure if the placer, JillBear, is on this list or not. But, imagine trying to find all those boxes in the POURING RAIN, which were the conditions when Deedle & I made our first attempt. :) So glad that snake wasn't guarding box #2 when we went the second time....I much rather deal with that ghost we found instead of any snakes. Hearing about all these snakes is really making me wonder _why_ I'm willing to walk through the woods to find plastic containers....

Doodle Bug.
