though. Anyway, I thought it was a rather economical solution to what we're
trying to do for the girls!

Re: [LbGLK] erasers and other carving stuff

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-05-18 05:37:36 UTC-04:00
It is called "eraser carving" because long before Speedball made the pink
stuff or Staedler made Mastercarve, people were carving on Staedler and pink
pearl erasers. Eraser carving is actually derived from other, more
historical forms of block carving -- namely wood carving and linoleum
carving. When people began using erasers, they called it "soft-block"
carving. Many people still use erasers for their carving medium, and
anything will work with varying degrees of success. I have actually had
pretty good results with those cheap 8 for $1.00 white rectangular erasers
from the dollar store. The biggest problem with erasers is that the size is
limited. Staedler used to make what they called a "grand" erasers -- about
2 1/4" x 1 1/2". The consistency was somewhere between Mastercarve and the
pink stuff. I loved them and several of my first letterboxing stamp were
done on these (i.e. Chinook Pier). But they stopped distributing them at
some point last year. They still make a smaller eraser that has this same
consistency but I find the size limiting and they are rather expensive.

Anyway, that is the scoop with "eraser carving." Use whatever works for you
and is cheap!

SpringChick

Kathi,
Adding onto what SpringChick notes,
you may also read about carvers using rubber plumber's gasket
material. I haven't tried it myself, but I've seen wonderful results.
Check out Ruthann's carvings at:

http://www.ruthannzaroff.com/carving/

which is also available via Deb's site along with other good carving
stuff:

http://www.confessionsofdecember.com/lbna/lbcarve.asp

My very first stamp was carved from a squareish green rubber eraser
about 25 years ago. I've still got it.

I've tried those tiny, round, plasticky ones, but did not have much
success.

It seems that one's choice of material is a pretty personal thing.
I love PZKut.
