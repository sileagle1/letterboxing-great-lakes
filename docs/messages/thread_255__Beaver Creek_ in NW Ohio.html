From: cerealcityboxers (cerealcityboxers@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-06-04 15:42:52 UTC
Does anyone know "An Unexpected Party" who placed the "Beaver Creek"
box in Williams County, Ohio, near Bryan? We found the remains of
this box and put it back together, sans stamp, but I expect its
location has been compromised. I tried "contact the placer" on the
LBNA web site, but have received no response.

In our limited letterboxing activity, we traded stamps with azobox on
one occasion. He's fairly easy to spot with his big compass around
his neck and his hands full of paperwork. We also ran across a Grand
Rapids family over by Lake MIchigan. They were looking for the same
Spring Chick box we were looking for, but they forgot their compass.
We helped them out and traded stamps. This same family stamped in a
box in Northen California that we found last year. We recognized
their stamp. We've also seen Spring Chick's stamp in a letterbox
over by Cleveland. We believe trading stamps when meeting in the
field is a great way to tie the sport together. Letterboxers are a
small family, and collecting stamps is what it's all about.
