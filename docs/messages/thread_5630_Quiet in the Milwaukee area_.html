Quiet in the Milwaukee area.

From: kermitboxer (kermitboxer@gmail.com) | Date: 2009-02-04 18:22:25 UTC
I was just in Eastern Massachusetts for vacation. I couldn't walk a
mile in any direction from my parent's home without tripping over a
letterbox. And here's the thing -- they were *active*. People were
letterboxing in the dead of winter, logging in, moving hitchhikers
around the area and around the country at Hitchhiker Hostels...the
place was (figuratively) packed.

Makes me realize how quiet Milwaukee is. We post finds on our boxes
maybe three times per summer or so. There aren't THAT many of us...I
recognize that Massachusetts has a far greater population density, and
that contributes to it...

Should we quietly, cryptically, teach more people about our sport?

Or are we just inviting box monsters? (those who destroy after finding?)

I have an idea, but I wanted to get some public feedback first.

In secrecy,
-Kermit
