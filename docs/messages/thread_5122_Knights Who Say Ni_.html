I have a question for all the GLK LBers out there who have found the
Monty Python Letterboxes planted throughout the Great Lakes States,
specifically regarding Knights Who Say Ni. Before Gooble Gobble's
extended hiatus from Letterboxing, I had asked him about Knights Who
Say Ni coded message (Cryptogram)?, and had been meaning to discuss it
with him @ GLG 2008 but didn't get time. I am usually pretty good at
figuring out coded messages but this one has me completely stumped!
Since Gooble Gobble won't be back for who knows how long, are there any
of you who could help me understand how to solve the code?
Also, FoundUnicorn and I live very close to Megasoma Punctulatus
(Rhinoceros Beetle) Letterbox planted by Gooble Gobble, and sometime
this fall we will check on the LB to make sure it has enough paper,
etc.. in Gooble Gobble's absence.

From: Coffee Beans (coffeebeans06@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-06-17 16:34:56 UTC-05:00
Here is an excerpt from The Dragon's Holy Grail website that explains
the code part:

"Being a GL group project, all of the letterboxes in this series are
scattered around the Great Lakes region. It is your quest to find each
of the 40 letterboxes and the series hitchhiker and collect not only the
stamp images, but also the encrypted clues they contain. Once you have
collected all the clues, you can put them in order to decipher the clues
to the location of the Holy Grail box. Since accomplishing this task
will be a huge undertaking, there will be prizes for the first three
individuals that complete the series in its entirety.

For the sake of those questing, please do _/*NOT*/_ distribute secret
information about the series (via WOM, email, etc.). Do note that the
*_/location/_* of the series hitchhiker _/*should*/_ be regularly
reported, and that the hitchhiker _*MUST*_ be placed only in HG series
boxes (excluding the Grail box of course)."

More info about the whole series is located here (if the link will paste
right):
http://www.geocities.com/the_lb_dragon/Holy_Grail_Series/Search_for_the_Holy_Grail2.htm

Hope that helps,
Coffee Beans
