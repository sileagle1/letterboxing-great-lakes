Oldest box in WI

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2007-09-26 13:05:28 UTC
Just wanted to mention that today is the 9th anniversary of the oldest
box in Wisconsin! The original placer (a "Steve") planted it on Cave
Point on September 26, 1998. He is no longer in the game, but Dragon
adopted the box several years ago (as he did with several other boxes
in Door County).

Martini Man & I stopped to check on the box this weekend and things
were in pretty good shape. The box itself has some small cracks that
have been duct taped, but we didn't have a large enough spare box with
us to replace it. The ORIGINAL logbook is still in the box, and 2
other logbooks have been added to the box over time. The original
custome-made stamp is also in the box. Over the course of 9 years, 80
different individuals/groups have signed into the logbooks.

Does anyone know what the oldest boxes are in the other Great Lakes
states? Are they still active?

Wisconsin Hiker

There has been a fair amount of debate on this subject in past on
various boards. [Note that this is different from ADOPTION, where the
box/stamp still exists, but instead is REPLACEMENT of someone else's
box.] Some people think of it as "preserving history" while others
think it violates the original planter's intentions and prevents
others from planting newer boxes in the same area.

My personal opinion is that I'd rather that my boxes NOT be replaced
by someone else. If my boxes disappear for some reason, they can be
thought of as a "limited edition" that is now closed, unless I myself
decide to replace it. Other than some ESPECIALLY significant boxes
(such as the first box in a state), I really don't see the need to
create a lot of "replica" boxes, especially without the original
planter's permission.
