bonus boxing story

From: klustofin (katy@dsa132.org) | Date: 2007-07-03 02:37:00 UTC
While I was in Georgia last month, I stopped at a park to find a
particular letterbox on my way back to the hotel from work. I brought
dinner with me and was sitting at the picnic table eating when an
older gentleman got out of his car and started down the trail. He was
the only other person I saw with a backpack and I noticed there were
patches/badges on it, but I couldn't make out what they said. I
thought to myself, "Gee, he looks like a letterboxer." Then I
finished my dinner and thought no more about it.

Two miles later, I went through a tunnel and across a small stream and
started looking for the clearing the letterbox is in. As I approached
the clearing, this very same man came out of the bushes - but no where
near where I thought the box should be. I didn't want to ask him,
because if he'd been taking a leak and not finding a letterbox....
But I was curious enough to look in that spot after he left - and I
found a completely different letterbox than the one I was after, one
for which I cannot find clues online. The last stamp indicated it was
found a few weeks prior, so I'm guessing he is the placer and now I'm
kicking myself for not risking the potential embarrassment and talking
to him.

The recent talk on the list about stealth has me wondering if I'm
somehow being obvious in ways I've never thought of. Other than the
fact that we were the only two people with backpacks in the entire
park, there really isn't any reason I can pinpoint for IDing him as a
boxer; any one else ever just get the idea from looking at a person
on the trail that they might be boxing, too? And what gives it away?

Katy
