I like the Le Plume II markers and I think they are fairly popular
among letterboxers. They are thin enough to be able to color fine
detail and they come in over 100 colors. They also color well most
of the time. If you have the 40 or sometimes 50% off coupons from
some of the major craft/hobby stores, they cost isn't too bad
considering that they last a long time.

Re: Marvy Markers-A Question

From: Irish Bain (irishbain@rocketmail.com) | Date: 2007-12-19 15:56:36 UTC
I've been using the Marvy set from the raffle at the Chambana
mini-meet last January. They're the larger tipped dye based markers
and are a big improvement on Crayolas, but I've kept looking for
something happier. The tips are very large, the ends pop off if I'm
not careful recapping, and the ink does indeed run if wet.

So I invested in the LePlume pigmented double ended markers last
month. Wow. These are marvelous (so far!) and I'm really enjoying the
change. The brush tips are considerably smaller. The writing tips are
just what I wanted AND don't get lost separately in my bag! (ever go
digging for that pen you KNEW was in there..somewhere...?) I haven't
done a drip test yet, but I'm hopeful :)

Ish

From: p_skultety (pskultety@aol.com) | Date: 2007-12-20 04:08:41 UTC
Team Rabit, I know where you are coming from. I to went to buy a set
of Marvy Markers only to find that there were soooo many choices. I
picked up a set of the water based Brush Markers. It was the Primary
Color Set. A word to the wise you should carry some water and paper
towles to wash the ink off of the stamp before you color it with your
markers. Otherwise the ink from the stamp gets into amd mikes with
the ink on your markers. Someone suggested using a spray bottle of
water to clean off the stamp. I just put one in my bag and plan to
try it the next time that I get out.

From: Kathi Krankoski (krank@ameritech.net) | Date: 2007-12-21 12:04:24 UTC-08:00
Personally, I like Tombows or Staedtler Mars if you can find them (more at art supply stores than mass-merchandisers like Michael's), but yes, LePlume's are great markers too. The brush ends are a little softer and "juicier", and the fine tip is a little less fine (not as good for writing, but better for small coloring - Marvy fine tips are harder and don't color as well).

From: Kathi Krankoski (krank@ameritech.net) | Date: 2007-12-21 12:40:11 UTC-08:00
I believe Le Plume's are only available in dye, but Marvy does carry a line called Marvy Memories / Artist. Reading one right now, it does say "pigment" and also has "Quality Memory Book Markers", "Archival qualitye ink - acid free/non-toxic - photo activity tested (photo safe) - lightfast - smearproof when dry - water resistant". They are dual tipped, broad (not brush) and fine. I only have one which I accidentally bought, and honestly haven't used it on stamps (the non-brush tip isn't as easy to use), so I don't know how it would perform, but think it would be a good idea for logbooks which might potentially get wet.
