Re: well? How was the SILO? and Crop?

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2007-02-19 03:36:01 UTC
We just got home from Illinois, since we decided to make it a full
Letterboxing weekend, in honor of the event.

Beetle & FungusWoman did a fantastic job with the SILO event! So
many interesting Chinese decorations, lucky envelopes, and oodles &
oodles of boxes. I know we didn't even stamp into half of those that
were available.

We enjoyed meeting some of the newer boxers for the first time. The
Brat Pack are incredible! How can a family have so many kids, yet
find the time to make such cute gifts for everyone attending the
event? The crafty oragami-type animals matched the theme of the
zodiac animals and the little Chinese word symbol stamps were
enclosed in fabric covered containers that reminded me of fortune
cookies. So clever! It was fun to meet some of the others that are
as obsessed (I mean enthusiastic) as the rest of us. HartX6 and
Chirodoc come to mind! Of course it was also nice to meet the famous
Miss Emily in person (although she seemed to be a good sleeper even
with all the commotion).

Speaking of cooties - we arrived with 5 and went home with 6. I
later realized that I should have just started handing them to the
1.5 year old Brat Pack member. He probably would have taken them
willing. Skeeter would have also probably taken them. That's my
plan for the next event - just keep handing them to the little kids
(and don't leave my backpack open & unattended!).

I loved looking at the photos FungusWoman posted - yes we were a sea
of red yesterday afternoon!

We're looking forward to hopefully seeing many of you at the regional
event in June.

Wisconsin Hiker & Martini Man

P.S. On Saturday morning, and all day today, we were out boxing in
the significant snow that had fallen in the area. We did 6+ miles
yesterday and probably 8+ today. Some trails were already a bit
packed down, but on some we were blazing a trail through snow a foot
or more deep. Quite a workout for the old legs - like walking on
sand... Thanks to Cherokee Rose 2 & Hawkeye and Atom118 &
CoffeeBeans for hiding some series of boxes on longer hikes - those
are our favorites!
