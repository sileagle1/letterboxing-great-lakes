Oh, no! Sorry to hear your box went MIA. Yes, I agree, we know
that's one of the risks of this activity, but geez oh pizza ...
don't you hate when that happens?!

One thing about your post that I noticed; it sounds as if you placed
the entire letterbox into an additional plastic bag. Did I read that
correctly?

I haven't put any of my boxes into an exterior plastic bag. I'm
afraid it would arouse the curiosity of little critters plus give
them an easy way to drag it around with all that loose plastic to
get little paws on or sink teeth into.

I have only come across a couple of boxes that were contained within
an exterior protective plastic bag layer. Like yours, they were
near areas in which flooding was a possibility. Just wondering how
many others on the list do use exterior plastic bags around their
letterboxes??? What kind of experience have you had doing so???

Enjoy!
---PJ
aka Bindle Babe

From: azobox (jparkerg@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-04-13 12:07:31 UTC
I used to put a bag around the outside of my boxes, but observing
exactly what Bindlestiff noted -- the bag being the
convenient 'handle' for the critters to pull, drag and move the
bagged box -- I have not bagged my boxes for quite awhile.
However, in a couple of cases, I have noticed during maintenance that
well meaning finders have added a bag to the outside of my box
(presumably to be helpful). I s'pose I could include a note asking
finders not to do that but I haven't.

azobox
