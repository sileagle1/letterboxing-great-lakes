From: Matt (ratpatrolhq@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-08-16 16:17:08 UTC
Last Friday I took a little bike ride north from Chicago to check on
some letterboxes and scout out locations for new ones in Kenilworth &
Wilmette. The Illinois #1 box has been missing since last year, but
I'd hoped that it was simply misplaced in the bushes nearby. This time
I did a thorough search of the area: it is gone. That box was placed
in 1998 by someone named Rachel and was the only one in northern
Illinois for many years. I'm sad to remove it from the LbNA website,
but I don't plan on replacing it.

Also, a reminder to newcomers and old-timers alike: be careful when
you rehide boxes! I stopped by Grosse Point Lighthouse and could
easily see it in its hiding place. Way too easy for kids or random
hikers to find. Make sure there is no way that anyone can see that
shiny plastic box or plastic bags inside glinting in the sun, cover it
completely! Only the real letterboxes will know where to look behind
that rock or that piece of loose bark to see what is inside, it
shouldn't be at all visible to just anyone passing by.

Well, I ran out of time and forgot to check on the Magic Hedge. Its
meant to be a challenge, so I'm not surprised that a number of people
can't find it. You might be within a foot of it, but unless you look
in an unexpected direction, you won't find it.
