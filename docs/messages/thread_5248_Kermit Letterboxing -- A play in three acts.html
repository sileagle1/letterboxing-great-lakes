Kermit Letterboxing -- A play in three acts

From: Kermit (kermitboxer@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-08-13 18:12:46 UTC-05:00
The mostly true and accurate re-telling of a particular box search this
afternoon.
Warning: Language, stupidity.
*
Act I: Initial mis-reading of the clues*

*Scene I*
K: Huh. 10 paces at 100 degrees leads me into the middle of a field. Box
can't be here, can it?.....
*{pause}*
K: Oh, I'm supposed to start over THERE.

*Scene II*
K: OK. 10 paces at 100 degrees now has me in front of this tree. There is
nothing else around. Just a tree. In the middle of a field.
*{stares hard at tree}*
K: Maybe it's up there, in that hole there in the trunk.
*{continues to stare at tree. Tree is frustratingly unresponsive}*
K: How high up do you think that is? Ten feet? Twelve?
*{shuffles through clues to see if placer has indicated that they are eight
feet tall}*

*Scene III*
*{mother and two small children are now staring at Kermit as she stares at
an unresponsive tree}
{Kermit notices}
{exeunt stage left, away from tree, back towards the middle of the field,
trips over nothing, nearly stabs self through spleen with compass}
{children in stitches as mother herds them away from crazy lady}*


*Act II: Our heroine discovers another clue within the clue*

*Scene I*
K: OK. 10 paces at 100 degrees from HERE is where the box should
be........stupid box......*
{aims compass in a chillingly dispassionate fashion}*
*{compass indicates a path straight through heavy undergrowth up a steep
hill}*
K: Dammit!
*{fifteen minutes of thrashing through underbrush, returning frequently to
start point to see if 100 degress magnetic has changed in the past few
moments}
*
*Scene II**
{incipient madness due to lack of box-finding}*
K: Perhaps all these random golf balls lying here in the brush are a
clue?????

*Scene III*
*{pause}*
K: Oh, wait...maybe I'm supposed to start down THERE...

*
Act III: The triumph of persistence, the folly of delay*

*Scene I*
*{Kermit discovers a hollow log 10 paces away at 100 degrees}*
K: Ha! Damn right! Letterboxing queen right here, bitches! Yeah!.......
*{stick inserted into hollow log reveals no letterbox}*
K: What the.......?
*{turns slightly to her right}*
K: Oh, sweet merciful Zeus, NO!
*{discovers only the lid of a Tupperware container. Alone. On the ground.
Covered in camo duck tape.}*

*Scene II*
*{clutching the remains of a muggled letterbox to her chest}*
K: I need to see if I can find any other remains. Maybe the stamp
survived.....
*{digs through the undergrowth}
{finds another golf ball}
{sees a tree a little further away with a brick underneath it}*
K: You're effing kidding me.....I mean, do I believe mine own eyes???
*{moves brick to discover whole and intact letterbox}*

*Scene III*
K: WHOOOOOOOO!
*{realizes now that the lid was the lid of the OLD box that had been muggled
two years ago!}*
K: WHOOOHOOOO!!!!
*{does the happy boxer dance through the underbrush}*
K: YYYEEEAAAAAHHH!
*{sits herself down to stamp in}*
K: That's right, bitches! Queen of the letterboxers! Bow down in
reverence, peons! I am invincible! I am indestructible! I am.....
*{thunder roars}
{raindrops attack in force}*
K: dammit.

--End--

The mostly true and accurate re-telling of a particular box search this

afternoon.

Warning: Language, stupidity.

*

Act I: Initial mis-reading of the clues*



*Scene I*

K: Huh. 10 paces at 100 degrees leads me into the middle of a field. Box

can't be here, can it?.....

*{pause}*

K: Oh, I'm supposed to start over THERE.



*Scene II*

K: OK. 10 paces at 100 degrees now has me in front of this tree. There is

nothing else around. Just a tree. In the middle of a field.

*{stares hard at tree}*

K: Maybe it's up there, in that hole there in the trunk.

*{continues to stare at tree. Tree is frustratingly unresponsive} *

K: How high up do you think that is? Ten feet? Twelve?

*{shuffles through clues to see if placer has indicated that they are eight

feet tall}*



*Scene III*

*{mother and two small children are now staring at Kermit as she stares at

an unresponsive tree}

{Kermit notices}

{exeunt stage left, away from tree, back towards the middle of the field,

trips over nothing, nearly stabs self through spleen with compass}

{children in stitches as mother herds them away from crazy lady}*



*Act II: Our heroine discovers another clue within the clue*



*Scene I*

K: OK. 10 paces at 100 degrees from HERE is where the box should

be........stupid box......*

{aims compass in a chillingly dispassionate fashion}*

*{compass indicates a path straight through heavy undergrowth up a steep

hill}*

K: Dammit!

*{fifteen minutes of thrashing through underbrush, returning frequently to

start point to see if 100 degress magnetic has changed in the past few

moments}

*

*Scene II**

{incipient madness due to lack of box-finding} *

K: Perhaps all these random golf balls lying here in the brush are a

clue?????



*Scene III*

*{pause}*

K: Oh, wait...maybe I'm supposed to start down THERE...



*

Act III: The triumph of persistence, the folly of delay*



*Scene I*

*{Kermit discovers a hollow log 10 paces away at 100 degrees}*

K: Ha! Damn right! Letterboxing queen right here, bitches! Yeah!.......

*{stick inserted into hollow log reveals no letterbox}*

K: What the.......?

*{turns slightly to her right}*

K: Oh, sweet merciful Zeus, NO!

*{discovers only the lid of a Tupperware container. Alone. On the ground.

Covered in camo duck tape.}*



*Scene II*

*{clutching the remains of a muggled letterbox to her chest}*

K: I need to see if I can find any other remains. Maybe the stamp

survived.... .

*{digs through the undergrowth}

{finds another golf ball}

{sees a tree a little further away with a brick underneath it}*

K: You're effing kidding me.....I mean, do I believe mine own eyes???

*{moves brick to discover whole and intact letterbox}*



*Scene III*

K: WHOOOOOOOO!

*{realizes now that the lid was the lid of the OLD box that had been muggled

*{sits herself down to stamp in}*
