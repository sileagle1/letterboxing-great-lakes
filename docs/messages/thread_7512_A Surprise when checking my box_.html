From: mcpastorswife (Campbell@Barnabas.org) | Date: 2012-07-15 22:52:47 UTC
Today we stopped along I-90 to rehide my I-39 Series: Welcome to Wisconsin box because the rest area has been redone, and I've heard from several people that though my box was there, it was out in the open. I found it, and when I looked inside everything was there ... including a cylinder I assumed was a Hitchhiker. Since people were coming down the path, I brought the cylinder back with me. When I opened it, I found a paper advertising Geocaching and asking people to sign in on this paper! Has anyone else had a Geocache actually put into their box?

Re: A Surprise when checking my box!

From: Mary (drewes.mary@gmail.com) | Date: 2012-07-16 02:09:15 UTC
Well, I found my Mary's Mugs: Four Queens stamp and logbook safe and dry inside a geocache. My box was gone - there were chew marks on my baggies. Thanks to a reported concern from WH/MM, I went to check on it. The area around this stump had been cleared of tree fall and underbrush, in preparation for the restoration of Native American mounds - one of which was about a foot away from my box. I had no idea the mound was there. While I was sorting through things, a park worker came by and told me what was going on. I'm sure he saw the stuff I had laid out, but said nothing. I don't see how they could have not found the geocache. I removed the LB out of respect for the area. Plus I thought a new hiding place would be a lower risk for accidental finds.

The box will be replanted - once the weather and my work/family responsibilities cooperate. Looking forward to that day!

Happy Trails!

- Sunny
