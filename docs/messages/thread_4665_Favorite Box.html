Favorite Box

From: Jon Russell (jrussell6@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2008-01-06 11:58:17 UTC-06:00
When I read "What is Your Favorite Box", I thought of boxes that were
unusual in their design or construction, well beyond your standard log book.

A standout for me was a box we hid for the Haunted Harvest in October 2005.
The box was called Dogbones and was based on the skeleton dog in Tim
Burton's film, "The Corpse Bride". The logbook itself was a dark green, but
was so unusual is that the carver had taken chicken bones, dried them up,
and then arranged them to look like the dog. The bones themselves were
lacquered to the book cover. I can remember getting this box in the mail to
place for the gathering and marveling at the creativity that went into that
logbook.

The carver was Scherezade from Michigan. Alas, she wanted the box pulled,
which we did, and I am thinking she is no longer an active boxer.

Very unusual.

Martini Man

PS: We have seen soooooooo many good stamps over the years that it would be
extremely difficult to point to one stamp as a favorite. I might publish a
list of favorite stamps in the future, but I am thinking that is going to be
one long list.

I will respond to my favorite boxes in an Email in the near future.

Martini Man's Email focusing on "Logbooks" a couple of logbooks come to mind
for me... Diamonds in the Rough and Big Purple Foot do a really great job on
their logbooks!!

However, as far as "above and beyond" I'd have to say "Smurf Village" in
Grants Pass, Oregon. The logbook was about 8 x 12 inches in the shape of one of
the Smurf's mushroom shaped homes. It was a wood carved front and back cover
with the details burned into the wood. It had actual hinges on the front cover
for it to be opened.

Truly a wonderful logbook.
