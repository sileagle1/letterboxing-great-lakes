Tonto had work in Springfield (IL) on Tuesday, so we went down a little early to pick up a few
boxes - specifically the three IRT boxes in that area. It felt good to do a little boxing - even
if it meant some major muddy boots! So muddy, that we had to offer to sweep the little
historic area in the "Maid-Rite" where we realized a little too late that we were in fact the
culprits who had deposited a ton of mud on the floor!
We also took in the Lincoln Library and Museum - something we would encourage everyone
to do. We realized that since letterboxing has come into our life, we've been remiss in
visiting some of the places that we used to enjoy. Balance seems to be the key word here.
Turned out that it was a very full and successful little trip to the capital - a great way to wile
away the long winter letterboxing wait!
Tiptoe & Tonto

From: sarabyronjeffers (sarabyron@att.net) | Date: 2009-03-01 23:04:23 UTC
I think the strapless gown is a Vera Wang influence. Indeed many of
the gowns now are so much more elegant than the over-laced, ruffled
frou-frou of the '80's (with the obligitory brain squeezing headband!)
But I do tend to think that all the brides look alike nowadays. And
lord help you if you have heavy upper-arms. They still seem to go with
the strapless. My husband says that the plus side is that at almost
every wedding that we've been to in the past 10 years, someone loses
their top while dancing. He and my brother place bets on who is going
to be flashing first. Odds are best when the bridesmaids also have
strapless gowns! BTW - I had long sleeves in December - kinda a Grace
Kelley type gown, no brain-squeezer - but the bridesmaids had the
ruffles on the butt dresses of the '80's!
Sara
