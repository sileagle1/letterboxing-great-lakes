Greetings to everyone!
I am sharing this information with my friends, neighbors, Girl Scout friends, camping buddies and letterboxers.
A friend wrote to me and here is what she told me about Listerine and mosquitoes! I'm not sure why it works, but I'm willing try it. Does anyone know what is in Listerine that might repel or kill mosquitoes?
Beetle


The best way of getting rid of mosquitoes is Listerine, the original medicinal type. The Dollar Store-type works, too. I was at a deck party awhile back, and the bugs were having a ball biting everyone. A man at the party sprayed the lawn and deck floor with Listerine, and the little demons disappeared. The next year I filled a 4-ounce spray bottle and used it around my seat whenever I saw mosquitoes. And voila! That worked as well. It worked at a picnic where we sprayed the area around the food table, the childrens swing area, and the standing water nearby. During the summer, I dont leave home without it.....Pass it on.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
I tried this on my deck and around all of my doors. It works - in fact, it killed them instantly. I bought my bottle from Target and it cost me $1.89. It really doesn't take much, and it is a big bottle, too; so it is not as expensive to use as the can of spray you buy that doesn't last 30 minutes. So, try this, please. It will last a couple of days. Don't spray directly on a wood door (like your front door), but spray around the frame. Spray around the window frames, and even inside the dog house if you have one.

Speaking of DEET..............

Does anyone know the "safety" level for the human body and DEET? Regular
OFF has something like 15% DEET, and Deep Woods Off has something like 25%
DEET. OK, fine. I've been using those two products for years and no ill
effects to date. I haven't died of cancer or seizures yet and I'm 54.

But the flood water skeeters around here have been horrible lately,
particularly in lower lying woods areas, because of the August rains. I
have a spray that I've never really used because I didn't look at the DEET
content until after I bought it. It's a spray for human skin, not just
clothes. (I do have a product that's only meant to be put on clothes and
should never be put directly on human skin.)

Anyway, this stuff I found at Walmart has 40% DEET. Anyone know if that's
actually OK to use? I would never ever use it on kids........I wouldn't
even recommend the Deep Woods Off for kids.......but what about adult
humans? In the territory we were in this past weekend, the Deep Woods Off
made a really good attempt but we still got eaten.

Regular


The concentration of DEET correlates to the length of time that it is
effective. Higher concentrations = longer length of protection. An
article I looked at (from Annals of Internal Medicine--so I think
that should be pretty reliable) mentioned concentrations of up to
100%. Here's a link to the article:

http://www.annals.org/cgi/content/full/128/11/931#T2

The article seemed to be saying that higher concentrations are OK if
the conditions warrant, but daily use of a high concentration
probably isn't a great idea. It also mentioned that the Am Acacemy
of Pediatrics suggests no greater than 10% on small kids.

BirdWoman/Waltzing Pigs

I got camping so I need the strong stuff! I bought a small pump spray bottle of Repel 100% DEET with no other ingredients listed. I smear Off insect repellent on my skin but I spray the Repel on my clothes; my bandana, collar, shirt cuffs, pant legs, socks. It is suppose to keep mosquitoes away fro 10 hours. I hesitate to spray it directly on my skin. But I does work. It kept the mosquitoes off me for an entire day while I conducted an outdoor education training course for Girl Scout leaders. The woods were buzzing with the pesty little blood suckers, but they didn't get any free samples of me. With all the West Nile hype, I do what I need to do to keep them off me, but I'd recommend something less toxic for children.
