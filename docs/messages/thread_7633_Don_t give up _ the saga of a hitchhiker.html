Dont give up  the saga of a hitchhiker

From: azobox (jparkerg@yahoo.com) | Date: 2013-02-05 15:55:44 UTC
Back in 2003, I launched a hitcher ("How `bout A Ride?") in Kalamazoo, Michigan in a mystery box of mine ("Kalarea").
It was quickly picked up by some veteran Michigan boxers, Soleil & Odenate, and moved on to another west Michigan box where it wintered-over and was found in the spring, by SpringChick!.

The next few months were hectic, but it finally found itself in the hands of well known CT boxers, Mark & Sue Pepe. A number of east coasters moved the little guy along, including at stop at the Mansfield Monster Mash Gathering of 2004.
Then it experienced a few hand-offs in NY before it again made a Great Lakes appearance (via a TrailTroll connection).
It next traveled to Florida where it was placed in a mystery box.
There it sat.
And sat.
And sat.
It apparently rested for SEVEN years before it was found!

I recently had a report from Candy Girl (from GA) that she had found it.
I asked to have it returned to me so I could review the logbook. I had no idea that I'd find such an interesting history.

Bottom line: don't give up on those mystery boxes that never get found, or your wayward hitchhikers.

And, BTW, the stamp and logbook were in pristine condition.
For old-timers on this list, it was the same type of container that was used for "Around the World in 80 Days".
