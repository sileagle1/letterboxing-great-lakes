From: ladynorelle (ladynorelle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-08-24 15:17:25 UTC
A few weeks ago I was traveling in western Pennslyvania. My
traveling buddy is not a letterboxers, but I don't collect Hard Rock
t-shirts, so we've learned to make compromises. We decided to make a
side trip to Pittsburg. I mapped our route to the Hard Rock and a
couple of letterboxes.

Only after we were on our way did I realize that one of the boxes I
planned to visit was about twenty minutes out of the way. My
traveling companion said it didn't matter to her, and off we went.
it was a beautiful summer day, and we were both enjoying the drive
through some territory that wasn't nearly as flat as Illinois.

The box we were looking for was at a rest stop. We reached our
destination and began our search. We did not find the box... I blame
the clue. Turn right. ok, but it doesn't say which way I should be
facing when I turn. we tried both ways. And I never saw a group of
three trees -no matter which "right" we tried. After a few more
minutes of futile searching i suggested we go inside and use the
rest room, let the clue swirl around in our brains, and try one more
time before we got back in the car.

When we entered the building, there was a bit of a traffic jam
leading into the ladies room. An elderly lady was trying to make her
way in, but was very unsteady and taking the smallest of unsure
steps. Her husband had led her most of the way, but had to "release"
her at the door to the ladies room.

One of my first jobs was as a nurse aid, and helping people to the
bathroom was part of that job. We mustered her into the stall,
leaving the door unlocked. After "resting" ourselves, I waited for
her to finish, helping her getting her clothes resituated and back
out to her husband who waiting just outside the door. Before we said
goodbye, she called us angels.

We never did find that letterbox, but providence must have led us to
that rest stop at just that moment. i'll trade a stamp for that
memory any day.

BigPurpleFoot
