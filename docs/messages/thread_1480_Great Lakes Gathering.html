From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2007-05-17 19:52:11 UTC-04:00
There is never going to be a date that will work for everyone. Having it on a consistent day each year allows people to plan in advance rather than having to wait to find out the date for a particular year. It also eliminates the hassle of annually trying to find a date that will work for as many people as possible (that is a debate I personally don't care to see every year). It is set to be the Saturday after Memorial Day because going later into the summer is bound to conflict with graduations, weddings and summer vacations and holidays for many people, not to mention park shelters are much more difficult to reserve once you get into June, July, etc.

Most people I know don't go to school on Saturday so I'm not sure I see the problem with having it before school is out, rather would think that since school is still in session, people would tend to be home and available for a gathering rather than off on vacation and other summer pursuits.

It only stands to reason that as the gathering moves from one state to another, it is going to be more difficult for some to attend due to distance and the necessity to stay overnight, etc. But as it becomes more difficult for some, it will inevitibly become easier for others who may have experienced that difficulty in a prior year. It all evens out over time. Fortunately there are many, many gatherings throughout the region during the year so those who are not able to make the annual GL Gathering one year or another have other events to consider. Many states have started their own annual gathering to make it easier for those in their area to attend (i.e. the annual Michigan Fall Gathering).

SpringChick

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2007-05-20 10:04:23 UTC-04:00
I really do agree with a lot of what is being said about the annual gathering date. Many good comments and observations have been made, but only go to demonstrate that there is no date that is going to work for everyone every year.

Case in point -- it was mentioned that the weekend after Memorial Day is *prime* graduation day. It is apparent this varies by state or perhaps even region within state as here in West Michigan I have never been to a H.S. graduation this early, rather they tend to start around June 7 -10, with open houses claiming weekends throughout the month. College graduations tend to be earlier but are so scattered that you cannot even attempt to plan around them. I do realize that this is an inconvenient time of year for teachers and it is not intentional. While mid-summer would be much better for them, this would create conflicts for many others who take advantage of the time their kids are out of school to vacation, etc. I realize that it is difficult for people when the gathering is not in your home state and you have to travel or even stay overnight. Consider this -- what about people who usually work on Saturdays? Should we then vary the day of the gathering to accommodate them? And what about those who live in MN or MI - UP or even the eastern areas of OH? I daresay attending the gathering is an weekend/overnight trip for them every year. Not to mention, as life goes on, situations change and what may have been a workable date for some, now is not -- or vice versa. Personally, the first Saturday of June could not be a worse date for me in the job I am now in and hope to be in for many years. But, I am only one person. There are many people who attend the gathering each year, regardless of their situation -- some are able to make it work, some are not. And so goes life...

It is true the gathering seems to have grown from a one-day get-together to an all-weekend event, and this is great that letterboxers from across the region are able to spend this time together, but it only makes it even more difficult to accommodate attendees as rather than shooting for a *day* that works for everyone, we now are shooting for a *weekend* that works.

I think you have to go into it with the attitude that you may not be able to attend *every single year* and that is okay. Letterboxing is not life and sometimes life takes priority, that is just the way it is. Again I stress that there are many regional gatherings throughout the year and this is a good thing because it offers different dates and locations and makes a letterbox gathering accessible to more people. If later in the summer is a better time for you, plan a gathering for that date. Judging by the attendance at some of the regional gatherings (Haunted Harvest, St. Nick's, etc.) I am sure there will be a great turnout. There are many people who even prefer the smaller gatherings over the huge crowd at the annual GLG.

I honestly do not think there is a better solution without going through a chaotic voting process every year, and even then, half of the people would not be happy with the outcome. This is the date that was chosen and for consistency, it will remain.

SpringChick

wouldn't be in this mess every year, but then maybe not.

Considering everything that has been said, I suggest that the best
solution is this:

1) The date for the official gathering be as it is set now -
essentially the beginging of June.**
2) That it be established that all the boxes for the GL gathering,
both permament and temporary (those listed in the gathering clues
packet, i.e. other than WOM, PTs, etc.) remain at the gathering site
until at least the end of June (4-5 weekends). Temporary boxes would
then be picked up and sent back to their owners.
3) That the gathering clues packet be distributed on the GL site on
the day of the gathering or there abouts for all to print out as
needed during the month of June.

This way, if someone cannot make it "the ONE weekend", they can still
get most of the boxes on another weekend that they CAN go on and know
that they will be there (aside of muggles). If teachers or some other
group wanted to have one of those other weekend be a special weekend
for them to gather - great! If the MN boxers wanted to carpool to OH
on a different weekend - great! They can decide for themselves
whether they want to rent a shelter or meet at a restuarant or
whatever so there would be no additional work for the host of the
official gather. It wouldn't exclude others from coming to the site
again for exchanges, to get boxes they missed on the official date,
etc. In a sense, we'd have a month of weekend gatherings, with only
one being the "big one". (I'm still debating in my mind Azobox's
concern about too much traffic as to whether this idea would help or
hurt or make no real difference as far as that is concerned. I'm
thinking it'd be about the same, maybe not so much damage happening on
one day...)

Considering much of this idea is already done, just establishing it as
policy would at least allow those who can't make it on THE day that
year to know we can still plan a trip there in June to get the boxes.
I would love to know that this year's gathering boxes would be
available until the end of June this year. I would go in mid-June at
the drop of a hat, maybe carpooling with the Lizards! (Of course the
whole new house thingy is interferring now...)

*I still think having it on one date limits both those who can host
(who really want to) and the locations available to host. I still
would rather see us say that the gathering is on a Saturday in June to
be determined by the host before Nov. 1 of the previous year. In my
senario, the boxes in the clues packet would still be hidden and the
clues packet up on the GL site for at least the entire month of June.
If some can only go before the official gathering - great! So what
if the gatherers are not the first finders!

Just something to consider...

Yes, it comes up every year, which quite frankly is getting old. Of course not everyone is going to be happy and it will be questioned, but if you will notice it is a very small number of people who are opposing the current practice. To me, that does not necessarily indicate that the current practice is inadequate, only that it is impossible to please 300+ people with one solution.

Whether or not the boxes stay in place is typically the decision every planter has a right to make when they send their box to a gathering and it is my feeling this should remain such. Likewise, hosting the gathering, getting all of the boxes planted and clues written is a huge undertaking in itself and I do not feel it is fair to require a host to go back to the gathering site at some later date to retrieve boxes. If they choose to leave the boxes in place for a certain length of time, that is certainly their perogative and that is great, but to require it, I think is asking a bit much. I know the times I have hosted gatherings, they have generally been at location several miles from my house and just getting everything in place requires a few trips there and back.

There are hundreds of letterboxes in our region alone, I doubt that anyone would be at a loss for boxes to hunt if they couldn't make the gathering and the boxes were not left in place afterward.

If a group wants to host another gathering later in the summer, that is fantastic, but it seems they should then solicit and hide boxes specifically for that gathering.

Just my two cents, for what it is worth.

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2007-05-21 21:39:57 UTC
The date of the gathering does change from year to year, just as the
date of Easter moves around. Likewise, they are both determined by
the date of another event. In the case of the gathering, it is the
Saturday after Memorial Day (not always the first Saturday in June,
but often it will be). In the case of Easter, it has to do with the
spring equinox and full moon and can be determined according to this
calculation:

http://www.jimloy.com/math/easter.htm

I am sure there are a lot of people who would like to change the date
of Easter so they could actually wear those cute little spring
dresses and hats without freezing their butts off, but it is when it
is and we work with that.

Some folks have singled out teachers, claiming they are being
excluded. I do realize it is a busy time of year for them as they
are getting ready to tie up loose ends and end their work year. But
we all have jobs -- many of us work at them year-round and some even
on weekends, each with their own busy seasons and days and each with
their own level of stress and commitment, etc. There are many
teachers who have been coming to the gathering year after year; there
are many people who normally work weekends who make it a point to get
to the gathering; there are many people with kids who come. I'm
tired of hearing that one group or another is being excluded.
Granted, it may be a more difficult time of year for teachers than
for snow plow drivers, but there is no intentional exclusion -- they
just happen to be a group impacted by this date. If we change the
date, there will likely be another person or group of persons
affected by that date and feeling excluded.

Some people want it before school is out so the kids are still home
and not tied up with band camp or cheerleading camp or soccer camp or
summer camp and visits to grandma and vacations, etc. Other people
want it after school is out so they don't have to take the kids out
of school to come for the entire weekend festivities.

I honestly feel like a broken record saying this yet again, but there
is not a date that is going to make everyone happy. There are many
things we could do differently -- we could put this up for vote to
change the overall date, we could rotate the weekend from year to
year, we could vote on it every year, etc. I am not convinced any of
the above would bring us to a better solution -- better for some
people perhaps, but inevitably worse for others. Not to mention, the
last time we attempted the democratic route, it dragged on for a
couple of months of sarcastic e-mails back and forth with nothing
being resolved. I am not up for that again so I have no intention of
doing that.

The annual Great Lakes Gathering is held the Saturday after Memorial
Day and as long as attendance is up in the hundreds, that appears to
be an okay date with many people, so it shall remain. Anyone is
welcome to host an event that would stand on its own as another
regional event, at any time that works better for them.

The end.

SpringChick
