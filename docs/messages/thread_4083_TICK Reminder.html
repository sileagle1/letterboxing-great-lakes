From: Barb Funk (mmfunk99@greenapple.com) | Date: 2007-05-22 06:30:22 UTC-04:00
The Cat Herders,

I completely agree~ticks are out in full force this year already! We
have had more already this year than in the past 10 years combined. I'm
not kidding. We've had 5 bite in and 4 more that didn't get that far.
This is combined from hiking in both NC and OH. And I completely forgot
about the lint roller method!!

I will add that Dr. OZ was on Oprah sometime last week and he said that
it is okay if, when you pull on a tick that has bitten in, the head
stays buried when you pull on the body. (Not what I learned as a
kid---so I listened for further explaination.) He said that the
"poison" is in the body and only when you cause the tick to spit that
out into your skin is that a problem. Most often if you pull (and NOT
SQUEEZE with tweezers) there is no harm done at all and your body will
chuck the remaining head. Using tweezers could force the poison INTO
your body and he did not recommend that method. That's why it sounds
like the lint roller is a perfect solution---especially since you don't
have to touch the little "thangs" and they won't get away from you either!

From: LAURIE MARSELLE (ltmarselle@msn.com) | Date: 2007-05-22 10:00:17 UTC-05:00
I just heard of an alternative to pulling out the ticks, we have yet to try
it, but the email I received swore by its effectiveness. If a tick is
embedded, put a glob of dish soap on a cottonball and hold it on the tick
for 15-20 seconds. The tick will release and attach to the cottonball.
