From: luckycharms9595 (merley95@aol.com) | Date: 2004-07-25 23:27:55 UTC
When does one become an "old boxer"? We found out about letterboxing
from a friend who heard it from a friend... That was earlier this
summer. We have found many boxes, been to a gathering, planted some,
and even found our first HH today. We would still consider
ourselves "newbies". However, even as new boxers, we have treated
the boxes as our own. We carry an emergency box repair kit to help
out others... During our recent hunting, we have noticed boxes in the
open, some with bags not zipped up, and some have been wet.

What we are saying is that we should expect some mis-treatment of
boxes with the new publicity. We think that the folks that don't
take care of the boxes will not last long. The true boxers will
probably end up carring a repair kit, like we have! We have to ask
ourselves, do we want more boxes to find? If we do, there needs to
be at least some publicity- and as we now know, this may have some
pitfalls.
-Lucky Charms
