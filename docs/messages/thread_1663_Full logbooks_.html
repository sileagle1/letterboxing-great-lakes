Here's one for some of the more experienced letterboxers in our midsts:
What do you do with your FULL logbooks?

I've recently done some maintenance and had to replace a number of
logbooks which were full of wonderful signature stamps. With new books
in place, I've settled in the living room with the full books. I've
pored over them, studied the stamps, wondered at the various
conditions described by the finders.....now what?
Do you cut out the pages and make a collage? Just put them on the
shelf with all of the other important stuff of your life? Box them up?
Or, horrors, Do you pitch 'em out?


Just curious,

azobox

P.S. I've been very curious about some of the finders from way distant
places, too.

Re: Full logbooks?

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-08-01 00:20:02 UTC
I have a few that are full and have been replaced and a few from
boxes I have retired. I haven't really done anything with them --
they're just in a drawer in my art room right now. Every once in a
while I sit down with them and browse the pages. Funny how a stamp
will catch my attention the second or third time around that didn't
get a second glance on a previous look... sometimes because the
name is now familiar or even the design one I've seen recently, etc.

My favorite one are the two full books from the Bridges of Lancaster
letterbox in PA. Thanks to the kindness of [previously] unknown
letterboxers, I've been able to put new books into this box twice
now and they've sent me the old ones. So neat to look through a
book from a far away letterbox. Not many stamps I recognize from
boxes around here, but many names I recognize from various talk
list, etc.

I kind of like your idea of making a collage of them, except that a
lot of the pages are stamped on the back... hmmm, have to think
about that a bit.

SpringChick
