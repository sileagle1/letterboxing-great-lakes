From: tiptoetonto (tiptoetonto@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-07-26 03:53:22 UTC
We also want to thank Spring Chick for her wonderful gift to those
of us nutty enough to have been letterboxing in the heat and
humidity this past Saturday in Muskegon! So much work goes into
planning, carving, hiding, clue-writing and hosting - And all of it
is simply for our pleasure! To all placers everywhere - a
big "Thank You!"

Even though it was a last minute trip for us (along with the
frustration of not remembering the pack of clues we ran off!) -
Spring Chick and fellow boxers made it so worth while. We had to be
in eastern Indiana by dinner and were only able to box in the
morning, but were able to visit Keltic Kara's first box in Muskegon
Preserve on the way out of town. Interesting side note - as we were
inking her stamp, the famous Blue Angels flew over in formation
directly overhead! A little deafening, but certainly a first for us
to be divebombed by aircraft rather than mosquitos- I guess the rest
of Muskegon were watching the air show while we were happily
letterboxing!

We missed the rain, but had black clouds and thunder hovering
overhead as we were boxing on the run at one last stop before high
tailing it to Indiana - Azobox's "Asylum Birds"! I think it was
appropriate that we barely escaped there before being commited on
charges of letterboxing lunacy! But, we did prevail! We found and
loved the stamp (even if we were bad and didn't follow the
directions like we were supposed to!)
Thanks again to all who made our Saturday a relaxing, enjoyable
wonderful day!

OOHH -a personal thanks to Dragon for the PI - and not the itchy
kind!
Tiptoe & Tonto
