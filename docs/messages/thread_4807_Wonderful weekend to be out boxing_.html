I hope everyone took advantage of the wonderful weather we had this

Ah, no letterboxing for us this weekend. We just bought our first house and in our excitement have already started packing...unfortunately the letterboxing stuff was one of the first things packed. It all fit in one box! Along with the cardmaking and embossing stuff!! But we did check on the Coal Miner's Daughter box on Thursday and scout out some new hiding spots for a series that was born from the stamps that were supposed to attend the SILO II event (we didn't make it, by the time we decided we weren't too sick to go it was too late to make it up there in time...but that was the day we decided to buy our new house, so it wasn't a total loss!). It was a lovely hike, with just a little snow falling, and I found plenty of hiding spots...so when we do get moved in and unpack the carving tools I have a bunch of new stamps to carve. And the dogs and I did go out hiking in the snow on Saturday. It was so WARM! I had to take my jacket off because it was way to warm over
my fleece. Meditating in the snow with the warm sun shining down on my face just melted all of the stress away.

Yes, we were happy to be out most of the day on Saturday! We did a 2-mile
hike to find 3 boxes, then another 2-mile hike to find 2 boxes, then a
shorter hike to look at a box solidly frozen in place. Due to the rain the
prior weekend, a large puddle had formed in the area of the box.
Unfortunately the ice was so high, it was even covering the flaps of the
Lock'n'Lock container. We quickly realized there was no way we could safely
get it loose, nor get it open. I regret not taking a photo - it was quite
funny to see the camo box top in easy reach, but no way to actually get to
the contents! We'll have to return another day after a thaw... On the
bright side (pun intended), there was so much sun reflecting on the white
snow that we both got quite a bit of color. At a party that night, everyone
kept asking where we had been to get so tan!

Wisconsin Hiker
