From: (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2007-08-07 17:02:37 UTC
Oh it varies greatly depending on the size of the stamp, the detail and the material I am working on. I find I can carve much faster on PZKut because it is my preferred material and I am not trying to "fix" things I don't like nearly so much as some of the other materials. Even my frame of mind can affect how quickly I carve -- there are times when I carve because I just want/need to get something done and my heart is not really in it. Those take longer because I tend to get up and putz around with other things and then come back, etc.

But in general, for a letterbox stamp, I spend an average of about 4 hours carving. Then another 1-2 hours making the logbook...

From: lcoil79@ameritech.net smokey (ainawgsd@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-08-07 13:06:13 UTC-07:00
I have a short attention span and I don't like to start a project and then not finish it, so I spend a relatively short amount of time carving most of my stamps. Usually I spend about an hour on carving. There may be more than an hour of prep work to pencil out the image I want...but actual carving time averages 30minutes-1hour. The longest I've spent on a single stamp so far has been about two hours for my large signature stamp and I was more than ready to be done with it by the time I was finished. My last stamp I also spent a little more time on than usual...maybe an hour and a half...because it had letters and many of the letters were rounded (like o's and g's and s's). I am very impressed by those with enough patience to spend hours on a single stamp. I know that my stamps will never be quite as awesome as some who've spent hours on their stamps (like Scoutdogs...her stamps are AMAZING!). But I'm satisfied knowing that I can still carve a pretty cool stamp with
the right dynamic image.
