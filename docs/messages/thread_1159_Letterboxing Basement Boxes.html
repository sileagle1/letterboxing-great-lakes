Letterboxing Basement Boxes

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-04-09 16:23:31 UTC

Back when the letterboxing.org site was converted from the old site
to the new site about 2 years ago, everyone was asked to move the
clues to their boxes over to the new site. Boxes that were not
moved over were put up for adoption and after a period of time,
boxes that were not adopted were put into storage in the
letterboxing basement.

Most of the boxes that ended up in the basement were those that had
been placed by somebody who left the hobby and didn't remove the
clues to their boxes from letterboxing.org. In many cases nobody
was interested in adopting a box simply because nobody was near the
location it was planted. Some of the boxes may be still active,
others are no doubt missing.

There is a current effort underway to clean some of these boxes out
of the basement -- remember, an abandoned letterbox is litter.
There are a lot of new letterboxers in the past couple of year and
there is a good chance that some of these abandoned boxes may be
conveniently located to one of them.

Please take a minute to check out the letterboxing basement and see
if there are any boxes that you may be able to check on or adopt.
If you check on a box or are interested in adopting one, please
notify the basement list owners so that it can be removed from the
basement.

The letterboxing basement is a Yahoo group and can be accessed by
following this link:

http://groups.yahoo.com/group/letterbox-basement/

Thanks!

SpringChick
