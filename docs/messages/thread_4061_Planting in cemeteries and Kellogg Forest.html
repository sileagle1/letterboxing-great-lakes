seem to have several active boxers in our area (Yeah!)

Thanks!

We have planted several letterboxes in the western suburbs of
Chicago. Some are planted on the periphery of the cemetery, but one
is right in the middle. We use trees, plants, rocks to hide our
boxes--we don't plant them on an actual gravesite or use someone's
gravestone or mausoleum as a hiding spot. We have alot of old
cemeteries near us, and it is fascinating to look at the old
gravestones (plus there are usually lots of birds to watch, too).
The older cemeteries were originally designed to be parks as well as
graveyards--families would come out from the city and spend the day
at the cemetery and eat their picnic lunch, etc. In the recent past,
I have seen picnickers in one of the cemeteries where we have planted
(they were even playing soccer). Obviously, I don't think
letterboxes in cemeteries are a bad idea, as long as they are done
respectfully.

If you are thinking of planting in an active cemetery, I would
suggest that you include a note in the clues to be respectful of
graveside services and to be on the lookout for cemetery workers.

BirdWoman/Waltzing Pigs

gravestones, etc., and as long as the box is planted

respectful there shouldn't be a problem.

gravesite reminded me of something I saw over the
winter while Deedle & I were visiting our
grandparents' graves - it was the weekend of a big
Ohio State game, and there were a group of people, in
their OSU garb, bundled up with a portable TV by the
one grave, having a tailgate party with their deceased
relative. Quite an interesting sight....

From: Doodle & Deedle Bug (doodle_n_deedle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-05-18 06:48:14 UTC-07:00
Catholic cemeteries! (I'm Catholic, my whole family's
Catholic, almost every known deceased relative of mine
is buried in one of our area's Catholic cemeteries).

And with that said, I have a feeling that if the
cemetery by you is like the big Catholic cemeteries by
me, those signs might be more about what visitors are
allowed to put by the graves. No glass jars,
Christmas decorations must be removed by March 15th or
they're going in the trash, not allowed to plant
personal plants around the graves, etc. etc. Does the
list of restrictions look anything like what's on this
webpage?

http://www.clevelandcatholiccemeteries.org/decoration_visitation_policies.html

Now, if this is a functioning, well-maintained
cemetery - Catholic or otherwise - even if you plant
your box on the outskirts of the cemetery there may
still be a chance that the maintenance crews find the
box while doing landscaping.

Use your best judgment!

Doodle Bug.
