A Special Day F=100

From: lininil (letterboxing@mac.com) | Date: 2005-07-20 22:11:17 UTC
A few weeks back Kookaburra and I counted up our finds and have been counting down to
100 ever since.
Today we made it! We choose the GL 2005 Exchange Box - Barn Theater to be our 100th
box. Carved by Pale Puppy Posse and Naughty Dog and placed by our local letterboxing
friend Funguswoman.
We made it between the only raindrops we have had in weeks but were determined for this
to be the day. Buccaneer was with us enjoying his 70-something find and
Kookaburra's pup, Doodle the Burr Ball, enjoyed his 20-something find.
Thanks to all of you who make this activity so great.

CMoon, Kookaburra, Buccaneer and Doodle the Burr Ball.




RE: [LbGLK] A Special Day F=100

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2005-07-20 18:01:50 UTC-05:00
Congratulations!! That's a significant milestone here AND in Dartmoor, the
birthplace of letterboxing. You'd now be eligible for the "one hundred
club" since they do keep counts in England. Here's an excerpt from a
Dartmoor related website: "The one hundred club is specifically for those
people who have found over 100 of the boxes. You get a cloth badge and a car
sticker, and are able to attend the bi-annual meeting at the Dartmoor Prison
Officers Social Club in Princetown, where a special letterbox makes an
appearance!" This same website includes a lot of interesting statistics
related to box counts and other aspects of letterboxing.

Did anyone else listen to the BBC broadcast about letterboxing that was
on-line earlier this month? I thought it was hilarious!

Wisconsin Hiker
