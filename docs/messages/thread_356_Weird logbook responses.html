Weird logbook responses

From: Delirium (donaldson_home@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-06-29 05:50:07 UTC-07:00

I went to go check on my boxes the other day (mostly out of a faint hope to run into other letterboxers while doing so than any real fear that the boxes are in danger), and I found the oddest message in my "Girly box" logbook. Now, bear in mind, this box is fairly new, and doesn't have many finders yet, but there's already some interesting oddities there:

1) most of the finders don't have a stamp

2) the messages are brief to the point of cryptic- example: "I found it. Yay."

Which set me thinking. I know I've left my share of short messages in logbooks (Dragon's "The Gathering" box being one- so many mosquitoes I really only had time for one word- "Hagus!"). What's the strangest entry you've left/found in a logbook?

And, does "I found it. Yay." truly have sarcastic overtones, or am I just jaded and mean-spirited?

Cheers,

Delirium (just because you're paranoid doesn't mean they're not out to get you)

From: Doodle & Deedle Bug (doodle_n_deedle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-06-29 07:24:46 UTC-07:00
I forget which box it was (one of the northeastern Ohio on the west side of Cleveland boxes) but a couple used their cat's paw print as their stamp -- as in, it looked like they actually had the cat with them on their hike rather than having made a carved stamp out of a copy of the cat's paw print while at home.

The very first box Deedle & I found we noticed that quite a few people had signed the log book stating that they didn't have a stamp yet. That was the Funshine Bear box in Berea.

Don't worry, the Girly Box is one of the boxes I told Deedle I want to make sure we find when we're in Michigan in August. We'll try to leave you a nice, long message in the log book :)

Doodle aka Diane.

Here in Western New York, some friends of mine are Search and Rescue
folk. They set up a training exercise for the dogs in a park. One of
the dogs gave the sign for "scent found" and started going the wrong
way but clearly on the trail of someone. The dog found a letterbox!
Thinking they had found lost property the S&R crew opened it looking
for identification. From reading the logbook they got the general
idea of how the game is played and had the dog "stamp in" as a finder!
-- Dagonell the Pirate

From: Tom & Jill (tj_mich@ameritech.net) | Date: 2004-06-29 19:41:36 UTC
We've had a couple of unusual responses:

Just yesterday, we found out about a response in one of our boxes
from someone who didn't know anything about letterboxing, but
was "collecting bark" (?) and came across our box, hidden underneath
bark at the base of a tree.

Earlier this year, we found a response in another of our boxes that
was plain old profanity from someone who had apparently stumbled onto
the box. We simply removed that page.

Haven't seen any cat paws, but there have been a few people who
didn't yet have a stamp, so instead used their inky fingerprint!

--Tom (of TJ_Mich)

I don't think it is sarcastic. I know Wiener Mouse and I have been

From: merlinandardea (rhesse34@hotmail.com) | Date: 2004-06-30 13:15:23 UTC
This is what made me a bit paranoid about finding a "good" place to
hide a box...
The box was placed in the base of a hollow tree that was just over
the crest of a fairly steep hill and at least fifty feet from the
trail that got you into the area. The opening in the tree faced
downhill, away from the trail. Someone had written in the logbook
that they had seen the box while "walking in the woods", wasn't aware
of letterboxing, but thought it was a fun thing to do.
Now, the only way they could have "seen" the box was to have looked
up, into the tree while climbing up a steep hill covered with large
rocks and brush. A walk in the woods???? It must have been a
friendly masochist.
Merlin
