From: speedsquare_lbxr (ruhlette@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-11-14 22:59:05 UTC
Hello, Folks!

I need to apologize for not posting more information earlier, but here's what I can share at
this time.

I reserved one of the large (max-150) shelters at Pokagon State Park for Saturday, May 30,
2009. Pokagon is located in the NE corner of Indiana, just south the Indiana Toll Road (I-
69) exit. I posted the event at AQ last night - just as a Save-the-Date notice. I plan to
communicate most plans here, but the routine logistics will be posted there as well.

If you are not familiar with Pokagon, you will be pleased with the amenities. There are 9
trails, camping, swimming, boating, biking horse trails, camp store, etc. There is also an
Inn on site (for those of us that don't do tents). There is a restaurant inside the park as
well.

There is a caching/letterbox permitting policy for all Indiana State Parks. Due to the
sensitive areas (several endangered species) within the park, the maximum number of
permits is limited to six (6) and they are all currently taken. The park interpreter has given
me the okay to have event-day letterboxes that would not require the regular permit.

I will be at Clear Lake the weekend before the event. I am hoping to have the event boxes
planted several days in advance of the event. I have some ideas for box themes, and I will
create a poll for the Event, and database for letterbox submissions later tonight.

If you think you'd like to be involved in the planning, let me know. I have many ideas
already, but I'd love to delegate some things.

~speedsquare aka Karen

Great Lakes Gathering 2009 - Indiana

From: speedsquare_lbxr (ruhlette@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-11-16 04:00:31 UTC
Great Lakers:

I posted the event at AQ, and some of you have started signing up. Thanks.

I tried to create a couple of databases here, but I think we've maxed out. Here are some
ideas to toss around until Spring Chick can free up some space. I am struggling with an
OFFICIAL event theme, so I am hoping you can inspire me and others with 3 or 4 ideas. We
can vote as soon as we get some workable ideas. My thoughts for event boxes center
around some of my favorite themes. I want to incorporate each of the Great Lakes states,
so with that in mind, I thought these could be used:

Our favorite Great Lakes state parks -- The Conservation Movement -- Flora and Fauna of
the Great Lakes -- American life in the 1920's and 1930's (Between the Wars) - I'm
especially fond of the work done by WPA artists and artisans --Others? - please add your
suggestions.

The only idea that comes to my mind at the moment is "Save Our Parks" - not very
glamorous, but in light of tightening budgets everywhere, it doesn't seem as weird as I
once thought.

I'd like a separate database for box submissions as soon as space permits. The shelter
house will accomodate 150 people, but I'd like to keep the boxes to 15-20 out in the wild.
Depending on your response to boxes, we may have just a couple of boxes in the shelter
that will require some sort of mental challenge. More on that later.

At this time, I am going to ask that additional boxes not be brought to the event/shelter.
There will only be one shelter and space will be at a premium. Personal travellers and
LTC's that you carry on your person are fine. If you need further clarification, email me
off-list.

I am planning a bit cautiously at this point. I will unveil some other activities for the day
after I see the numbers. The park is about 3 hours from my home, and I will be making a
trip up there shortly to take pictures and get a better feel for the actual space. I've been by
it, but I want to count tables, check the grill size, the walking distance from the camp
store and campgrounds, walk a few more trails, etc. I'm not sure the cost of travel will be
an issue and affect attendance this year. My preference would be to not have a mob event.

After we vote on the event theme, I will be asking someone to carve the event stamp. I'm
also interested in having an event shirt made. More on that later too. If you'd like to be
considered for the event stamp, let me know. I just know I must delegate that artistic
endeavor.

I hope to have an answer back shortly about extending the boxes' availability through
Sunday.

It's getting late. I'll be back tomorrow afternoon.

Karen
