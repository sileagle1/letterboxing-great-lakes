Muskegon this weekend

From: Sara Byron (sarabyron@att.net) | Date: 2010-09-16 14:34:08 UTC-05:00
We are going to Muskegon this weekend for an Irish Dance competition. Then my daughter is dancing at the Gaelic Storm concert - then on Sunday we are going to visit WMU in Kalamazoo.

I'd love to squeeze in some letterboxes - but time will be limited. We are renting a small cottage on Beach street in Muskegon - believing that we'd be close to the state park. BUT! There doesn't seem to be a way to get to the park from the cottage! Mapquest says it's 20 miles / 40 minutes around the lake to the park.

Is tree some local trick to this? Does anyone know? We can almost touch the state park from the cottage, BUT WE CAN'T GET THERE? Is there any other way? No foot bridge? Or local ferry?

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2010-09-17 08:25:01 UTC-07:00
Unfortunately there is no way to get across the channel. The only way to get to
the State Park from the south side of the channel is to drive around. I don't
know that it would take 40 minutes, but probably a half hour. From the south
side of the lake, you are actually closer to Hoffmaster State Park, which is in
Norton Shores, just on the southern edge of Muskegon. There are a couple of
nice boxes there.

SpringChick
