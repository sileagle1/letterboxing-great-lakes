Thanks Atom 118...and coffee beans!

From: tiptoetonto (tiptoetonto@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-03-11 02:35:32 UTC
We haven't been able to get out and box since our Florida trip in
January...but finally the weather in Chicago was a sunny 55 degree
today! We decided to do Atom 118 and coffee bean's "Hunt for the
Hidden Hollow" series and a few extras in the same area...Nine miles
and seven out of eight boxes later..we can report that it felt so
good to be outside...at least from the knees up!
Now from the knees down it was a totally different story. Starting
with the first attempt we realized that all of the snow and ice
accumulated over the winter had to go somewhere as it melted under
the higher temperatures...the paths were all rivers of running water
and mud. We've never encountered ankle deep mud EVERYWHERE like we
experienced. But, we figured once we were covered in the slime and
slush what did we have to loose.
We saw at least a hundred deer throughout the day (I'm not
exagerating!)...some within a few feet of us..Obviously, this area
has more Bambi's than people...and they are certainly not afraid of
the two legged folks!
One box was in a stump completely surrounded by a pond in twelve inch
deep water - an island! Tonto braved the water balancing on fallen
logs to recover the box (which amazingly was high and dry!) and then
replace it. All he needed was a tutu as he tiptoed across the
tightrope log to enhance the balancing act! I was very proud of
him! It was a boxing feat that will be hard to beat!
As for the eighth box attempted...it was buried (if we were in the
correct location) under rushing torrents of the Des Plains River..It
was Atom 118's "A Future Noble"..all we can say is that these
two "masses" looking out on all of that "liquid" and ran out of "gas"
searching for the Future Noble!
We highly recommend this fun series and other close by boxes!...But,
give it a little time to dry out unless you really like mudboxing!
Back at home, we took all of our sopping wet clothes off as we walked
in the back door...our feet looked like prunes, but our hearts were
humming with letterboxing happiness. Thanks Atom 118 and coffee
beans for a wonderful day of mudwalking!
Oh yeah! We checked the Bingo words when we got back and to end our
perfect day...we had "Atom 118" in our corner B slot giving us a
Bingo diagonal...now wishing for the other part of the "X"!
Oh happy day!
Tiptoe...and a drying out Tonto

From: Atom 118 (hersh118@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2007-03-12 10:13:21 UTC-05:00
CB and I are happy you had such a fun time with our boxes. Yea, we are
starting to realize how much the Des Plaines River Trail area floods.
The Future Noble box isn't in a flooded area...but you do need (as we
have since found) to walk across a flood-prone stretch to get to
it....on one of our visits to it, I fell in the river while trying to
jump across one of the side streams...and got coated with mud!...

...as for March being box maintenance month...we did get out on the
trail to reposition a few of our Singing and Boxing boxes. 'Six
Languagues' (with the bonus box), both Wicked Stamps, and 'A Holy Grail'
have been removed. The 'Holy Grail' is now in the SPAMalot box (two
stamps for one box) and one of the Wicked stamps will be replanted
someplace else along the trail soon. SO, with the flooding...and it
only being early March....'Magic Foot' was encased below 6 inches of
ice!...hopefully some warmer weather this week might make it possible
for us to finish our maintenance along this part of the trail.

ON A MORE AMUSING NOTE, while searching for our SPAMalot boxes, we saw a
group of people walking through the woods in a very odd fashion...they
seemed a bit drunk as they would walk one direction, stop, turn,
duck...walk another direction...at first we thought they were looking
for a Geocache which we had heard was nearby (actually one of the
reasons we were moving this box)...it dawned on us that this is what we
must look like to others as they pass us on the trail...a slightly
sobering thought. So we asked...'you caching?' to which the young lady
said, 'similar but different...its called letterboxing....' So, they
were, of course, looking for OUR boxes. After a few laughs about the
whole thing, we did a few exchanges with Cherry on Top and Music Nerd
who were out boxing with their mom. They had thought a bit more about
early-March that we had...and had brought along a small shovel to dig up
the boxes....(sorry to all of you who had mentioned digging up boxes...I
thought you had been kidding!)....We spent the next hour-or-so boxing
with the three of them while they tried to find a few of our boxes
before we removed them per our plans. So, thank you to the three of
them for making our afternoon such fun! (and sorry any incoherent babble
about boxes I may have made....I was perhaps a bit to over-eager and
excited about meeting folks on the trail!!!!)

In addition to maintenance, we managed to find FM 'Little Lost Stella'
and Kiddy Writers/Hawkeye's 'Boots are Made for Walking'....a fun boxing
day all around!

And YEA FOR ME!!!!! with the extra B box....I now have a straight line
Bingo...and I'm starting to feel better about my card again. I hope I
was able to help a few others!

Atom 118
