Re: UGH! Beetle's N. IL snow report

From: Carol Osada (cjosada@hotmail.com) | Date: 2008-03-21 21:04:46 UTC-06:00
What's the weather like in the northeast IL area - north of Chicago? Well, the sun is shining, the birds are singing, the daffodils are in bloom...and I am in total denial! What is all this white stuff? Where is spring? Winter is suppose to be over. GRRRRR....

The weathermen estimate that this area was suppose to get 5 inches of snow, then revised their estimate to 9.5 inches of snow fall. But the actually on-the-ground accumulation is about 10-12 inches. It's wet and heavy and it's still falling. It's difficult to push the snow blower through this stuff. And because it's wet, this slop is forming a bumpy, under layer of ice on the roads.

Yesterday, before this snow started coming down, I was on the trails. I have several new letterboxes ready for placing. Now, I guess I will have to wait to place them. I doubt you would be able to find the trails with all this snow and ice.

My recommendation: wait a week. By the end of the week, the temps are suppose to warm up to the 50s and maybe all this snow and ice will melt away. I'll bet that many of the boxes in the area are not accessible or frozen to the ground.

But when this snow and ice are truly gone....we'll be tripping over each other on the trails.

This is a time to carve and plan some new boxes.

Beetle

Re: [LbGLK] Re: UGH! Beetle's N. IL snow report

From: (Mathw613@aol.com) | Date: 2008-03-22 11:16:30 UTC-04:00

Ok, we are all now awake -- 10 am!



Last night I learned that I had to go to Geneseo (30 minutes drive . . . hour round trip) and that I might have to "drive to Wisconsin," to Orchard Manor.



"Where's Orchard Manor?" I asked.



"In Wisconsin," was the response. Well, I knew that... where in Wisconsin . ... "don't know."



Flashforward and "Wisconsin is cancelled."



"I don't have to go?"



"no."



"Is this something that I might have to do over the weekend."



"no, I got it all taken care of," says the pharmasit



Off to Geneseo I go, whistling a happy tune when my phone chirped.



"Hello."



"Matt, you have to go to Wisconsin tonight."



I'll spare you all the nitty gritty details as to how it went from a "no your not going," to a "you have to go tonight" it was already nearly 7pm and though we had ZERO snow here in the Quad Cities (hint hint all you boxers can come here to search . .. drop me a line and we could meet up).



I didn't get back to the office from Geneseo until about 8pm and learned that Orchard Manor is in Lancaster, WI [[Hart family history alert]] My Grandfather went to Dubuque Seminary and my dad was born in Dubuque. The first church Grandpa pastored was in Potosi, Wisconsin where there is one letterbox (the Channel Cat) then Grandpa pastored the Presbyterian church in Lancaster where my uncle was born . . . in the early 1950's my grandparents auctioned off all the owned and became missionaries to Lebenon until Grandma got polio (Christmas Day 1954) and they moved back to the states.



So, I was excited to once again go up to this part of Wisconsin.



By the time I hit the Dubuque airport it was 28 degrees out and the fine misty rain was freezing to the road. An on-coming car spun out right infront of me!!



I had brought the clues to the Channel Cat box in Potosi though, I wasn't sure if I'd be able to find it.



After the drop off at Orchard Manor I found this small park in Potosi. The clues directed me to the furthest parking area . . . have you ever seen semi trucks full of plowed snow driving through town? Have you ever wondered to yourself, "I wonder where they dump all the snow?" Well, the furthest parking spot at Hickory Hill Park in Potosi is where ALL that snow gets dumped.



I had the Rocky Mountains in front of me!! Of course, I had to climb them to get to the box!!



I had just worn my tennis shoes, it was after 10pm and off I went. My foot dropping through to my knee... one time my ENTIRE leg fell in to my pelvis . ..  but I was determined and perservered!



Once past the mountain range I found the box!! After stamping in, it was back to the car for the 2 hour drive home.



I got home at just past 1am a happy (and tired) camper ~ I realized that this was my *first* box that I found in the state of Wisconsin!!



Thanks Aimlesst for replanting this wonderful box!!



hx6

Ok, we are all now awake -- 10 am!

Last night I learned that I had to go to Geneseo (30 minutes drive . . . hour round trip) and that I might have to "drive to Wisconsin," to Orchard Manor.

"Where's Orchard Manor?" I asked.

"In Wisconsin," was the response. Well, I knew that... where in Wisconsin . ... "don't know."

Flashforward and "Wisconsin is cancelled."

"I don't have to go?"

"no."

"Is this something that I might have to do over the weekend."

"no, I got it all taken care of," says the pharmasit

Off to Geneseo I go, whistling a happy tune when my phone chirped.

"Hello."

"Matt, you have to go to Wisconsin tonight."

I'll spare you all the nitty gritty details as to how it went from a "no your not going," to a "you have to go tonight" it was already nearly 7pm and though we had ZERO snow here in the Quad Cities (hint hint all you boxers can come here to search . .. drop me a line and we could meet up).

I didn't get back to the office from Geneseo until about 8pm and learned that Orchard Manor is in Lancaster, WI [[Hart family history alert]] My Grandfather went to Dubuque Seminary and my dad was born in Dubuque. The first church Grandpa pastored was in Potosi, Wisconsin where there is one letterbox (the Channel Cat) then Grandpa pastored the Presbyterian church in Lancaster where my uncle was born . . . in the early 1950's my grandparents auctioned off all the owned and became missionaries to Lebenon until Grandma got polio (Christmas Day 1954) and they moved back to the states.

So, I was excited to once again go up to this part of Wisconsin.

By the time I hit the Dubuque airport it was 28 degrees out and the fine misty rain was freezing to the road. An on-coming car spun out right infront of me!!

I had brought the clues to the Channel Cat box in Potosi though, I wasn't sure if I'd be able to find it.

After the drop off at Orchard Manor I found this small park in Potosi. The clues directed me to the furthest parking area . . . have you ever seen semi trucks full of plowed snow driving through town? Have you ever wondered to yourself, "I wonder where they dump all the snow?" Well, the furthest parking spot at Hickory Hill Park in Potosi is where ALL that snow gets dumped.

I had the Rocky Mountains in front of me!! Of course, I had to climb them to get to the box!!

I had just worn my tennis shoes, it was after 10pm and off I went. My foot dropping through to my knee... one time my ENTIRE leg fell in to my pelvis . .. but I was determined and perservered!

Once past the mountain range I found the box!! After stamping in, it was back to the car for the 2 hour drive home.

I got home at just past 1am a happy (and tired) camper ~ I realized that this was my *first* box that I found in the state of Wisconsin!!

Thanks Aimlesst for replanting this wonderful box!!
