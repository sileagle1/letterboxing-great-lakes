From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-08-08 23:34:48 UTC-04:00
Wow! That is quite an impressive list of boxes! I noticed that there were
no "Mirkwood" boxes on your list and checked out the clue site to see if
they are even listed and see that they are not. I think that they never
made it over from the old LbNA site to the new one since she kind of fell
out of the letterboxing picture.

For those who are not familiar with the name Mirkwood -- this is Ruthann
Zaroff, a veteran SE Michigan letterboxer and stamp-carver extraordinaire.
When I first began 'boxing, we corresponded a lot and she was instrumental
in encouraging me to populate the West Michigan letterboxing void and also a
huge help in getting me going on stamp carving.

I see that clues to her boxes are still available on her own web site. Does
anyone know if the boxes are still there? If so, it may be worth your
effort to attempt at least one of these -- there is something kind of prized
about having a Mirkwood stamp in your book. As for stamps, I would
recommend Jabberwocky -- it is one of my favorite stamps to this day. You
might try e-mailing her to see if the boxes are still in place.

Her site is here:

http://www.ruthannzaroff.com/letterboxing/

SpringChick
