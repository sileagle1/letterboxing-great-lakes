REPORT: IL: Bundle of Joy / FOOW 5 / FOOW 9

From: Mosey (PonyExpressMail@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-05-08 12:14:31 UTC-05:00
Ancient and I decided to treat ourselves to a morning of letterboxing this
fine Mother's Day. The weather here this morning was just perfect for it.

1. Bundle of Joy in Hinsdale, IL

Found the box doing quite well, but I THINK the bag to the stamp was ripped
on the bottom. (We can't remember for sure whether it was Bundle of Joy or
FOOW 5 but we'd say that we're 80% positive it was Bundle of Joy. We folded
the bottom of the bag up so that, temporarily, it should be just fine.) We
carry bags with us but none near small enough to be able to replace it.

Very nicely done stamp. But I forgot to pack pink ink as suggested!

2. Fish Out of Water 5: Swinging Bridge, Riverside, IL

We were amazed that we were only the second finders! On the other hand,
maybe it's not so amazing. Maybe finders before us went mad on the streets
of Riverside before finding the bridge.

If I never ever see another curving street, it'll be like one century too
soon. Only the Marquis de Sade could have designed the layout of Riverside.
That took either an evil mind or an alcohol-laced one. Being
Directionally-Challenged, at least we had a compass with us.

Found the box easily once we found the swinging bridge and the box was in
good shape.

Went east along the river to sit down and stamp in. A family with a couple
of pre-teens slowed down to watch us, so we pretended the letterbox was a
water collection tube and began talking about measuring water pollution and
such. The father called out, wanting to know how bad the water was.
Ancient called back "That's what we're going to find out!" They moved on
across the bridge and outta sight.

3. Fish Out of Water 9: Hoffman Tower

Stupidly, we decided to take the route with less hiking since we'd spent so
much time with wandering the streets of Riverside up to that point. So what
did we decide to do? Take the "shorter" alternate route that involved
driving instead of hiking to the box, and we ended up wandering the streets
of Riverside once again!

The second time around was no better than the first. We ended up somehow
going around in a circle and coming right back to the fire station again,
which is where the swinging bridge was. OK. We asked the nice firemen how
to get to Hoffman Tower. I'm sure they gave us excellent directions. Being
Directionally-Challenged in every single meaning of the words, we still got
dreadfully lost yet again. So we decided to just wander around and look at
the incredible houses around the area. And lo and behold, all of a sudden,
looming up ahead of us was Hoffman Tower! OK!

We wandered down the path, being quiet as church mice so as to not disturb
the men fishing. No women were fishing. It's Mother's Day! They should be
home with their mothers! Or did their mothers kick them out of the house?

We were the first finders to the box, and all was well with the box.
Further into the woods was a very nice heavy horizontal log close to
waist-height to use for stamping in. Beautiful stamp! (This lady can
carve!)

Started walking down the path back to the car, again being quiet as church
mice. Suddenly a slightly heated argument broke out between two men fishing
on the bank. So much for not frightening the fish.

Saw two mallards, a male and a female, actually standing together in the
fast-running water going over the dam. Don't have the foggiest idea how
they managed to balance themselves there, but they seemed to be having a
good time. I don't know whether the argument bothered the fish or not, but
it didn't bother the mallards. She seemed to be having an enjoyable
Mother's Day. Also saw some flashy tanagers.

Took us awhile to find our way to back 31st St., but came home west on 31st
and stopped at Scott's Pet Store at Wolf & 31st. It's not the Shedd, but
it's still fun to check out every so often.

Did our best to avoid ZooTraffic. Figured that everybody would be bringing
Mom to the zoo today. And even tho we had started out early this morning,
by the time the Riverside streets finally released us, it was close to noon.
Yep, lots of ZooTraffic. But saw a lot of dads alone with children --
holding the hands of little ones going across 31st, and dads pushing
strollers. Possibly dads and kids heading off to give Mom some
well-deserved AloneTime?

Happy Mother's Day to all the Moms out there in Letterboxing World!
