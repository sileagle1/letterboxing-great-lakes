Chicago area box maintenance

From: Coffee Beans (coffeebeans06@yahoo.com) | Date: 2009-01-02 10:32:00 UTC-06:00
Happy New Year! Atom and I enjoyed a brisk morning winter hike
yesterday checking on a few of our boxes along the Des Plaines River.
First, we went to Catherine Chevalier Woods to check on 3 boxes we had
planted there. We really like this preserve and some of our first boxes
were planted here. We had gotten an attempt report on "Squash" and
although we found the box in its proper place, the box was cracked and
the insides partially frozen/wet so it was removed and we'll see how it
thaws/dries and decide later about replanting. Next, we found the last
of our HHH boxes still out there, and this box was found open and
contents scattered across the ground. It was also removed and will not
be replanted. Finally, we found my "Beautiful Flower" box safely tucked
into the log where it belonged -- a good sign until opening, when the
insides were found to be one frozen brick of logbook/stamp/ice -- the
contents for this one are unsalvagable I think, so it will likely not be
replaced. So a pretty sad day for our almost-3 year old boxes in
Catherine Chevalier Woods. :( I can understand the first two getting
wet since the boxes were damaged/open, but I'm pretty baffled by the
last one, since the box was sealed up and tucked away in the log -- the
only thing "wrong" with it was that the last finder had put the baggie
on the outside of the box and the contents directly inside the
lock-n-lock with no other protection. But oh well, c'est la vie...

We also checked on a few of our Broadway series starting from Beck Lake
-- this trail was hard to navigate because it had a lot of ice still on
it. But we found "The Lion King" (box is fine -- there's a new picnic
table right near it though that may make it harder to retrieve if people
are there but it does provide a good place to log in now), "Aida" (saw
and touched box but it was frozen to the ground under bark so we
couldn't move/open it), and "Spamalot" (also fine, but we did move the
final placement of the box and updated the clues on AQ). We also went
and decided to remove the "Bear Down" box since we didn't really like
the final placement of the box (busy spot constantly populated with
people and dogs) and we think the last part of the clue was throwing
people off since this box only had 1 finder in 2 years. We might
replant it, might not -- otherwise, congrats to Cherokee Rose and
Hawkeye for being the only finders!

Unfortunately the little park we were going to find boxes in yesterday
was all gated up, so we were unable to get our first finds of 2009...
but maybe we'll get one tonight! ;)

CB of Atomic Beans

Happy New Year to Atomic Beans!

With all the rain and thawing snow and ice, I have been concerned about all the letterboxes out there. I received a report that one of my boxes was frozen; a block of ice! But the finders did some first aid and the box is still alive and well. I'll check it soon. This has been a difficult winter and it's just beginning. Unfortunately, I don't think these will be the last reports of frozen, damaged boxes. If the stamp survived, I encourage you to replace the box.

Beetle,

Its funny you mention a block of ice. We were going to test the quality
of a few of the boxes we rescued yesterday. We are thinking about
dunking them in a bucket of water, leaving them outside to freeze to a
block of ice, melt them, and then see which of our boxes stay dry. When
we get around to our experiments, we'll likely post some pictures to
share with the group. (we thought it would be fun/interesting)

However, I wouldn't be overly worried...as long as finders don't force
boxes out of their spots when frozen...I think many boxes are more
sturdy than you think. While out yesterday, we noticed an area that
once held a box (A Future Noble) was covered with a few feet of water.
We commented that out box had likely been under a similar amount of
water during the winter it was still in place. When we did end up
retiring it (after we realized that it spent many weeks under feet of
water), it was still very dry inside!

Atom118 of Atomic Beans
