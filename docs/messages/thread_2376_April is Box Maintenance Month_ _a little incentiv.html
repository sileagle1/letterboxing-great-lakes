April is Box Maintenance Month! (a little incentive)

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-04-08 22:20:11 UTC
Those of you who have been around the list for a while will probably
remember that a couple of years back Dragon officially dubbed April
as "Box Maintenance Month." The idea is to get out to check on as
many of your boxes as possible and get them spruced up for another
letterboxing season.

As a little incentive, I started a database (click on "Database""
over on the left-hand sidebar menu) called "April Box Maintenance."
For each of your boxes that you yourself physically visit and/or
replant during the month of April, make one entry.

At the end of the month, I will put each entry into a drawing and
pick one out at the Michigan Spring Fling gathering on May 6. The
winning entry will win a *fabulous* prize (oooh, aaaah...)

Can't make it over to Maybury State Park in Northville, MI for the
TJ_Mich Spring Fling on May 6? Don't worry, you need not be present
to win! The fabulous prize will be mailed to you if you are not
there ;-)

Just get out there and check on those letterboxes!

SpringChick


Which Boxes Qualify for Entry into the Drawing?

1) It must be your own letterbox or a box that you have officially
adopted (i.e. you maintain the clues for this box)
2) You or a member of your team must be the ones to physically check
on the box
3) It must be checked during the month of April
4) It must be a previously planted box (not a new plant)
5) It must be either checked/cleaned/okay or replaced (cannot count
a box that you went to check on and found missing unless you replace
it during April)

Okay, this is supposed to be fun and I don't want to get into
technicalities about which boxes count and which don't.

I realize that some people maybe checked boxes last month (or the
month before that...) or simply cannot get out within the next few
weeks and just because a box is missing you still put in the effort
to go out and check it...

I had to draw the lines somewhere and as long as everyone is playing
by the same rules... It's just for fun anyway and to provide a
little kick in the back end to those of us who did not have the
gumption to get out and check boxes yet.

So, to answer the questions posed -- boxes checked during March do
not count and boxes confirmed as missing do not count unless they
are replaced within the month of April.
