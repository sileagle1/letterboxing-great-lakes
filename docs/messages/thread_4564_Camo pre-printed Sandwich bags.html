Those are cute. There's an awful lot of clear plastic showing though.

I just made a whole bunch of camo pouches for the Creepy Crawl
event. They worked pretty good, although, you really need to make
sure they seal properly to close them. I didn't use extra baggies
inside, but I should have. Even in the 4 days they were outside,
they got a little damp because they weren't sealed all the way.

I couldn't possibly begin to tell you how to find it now, because it
took me forever, but there's a tutorial on AQ somewhere on how to
make them. I didn't do anything as in depth as the tutorial showed,
though. Get a hard plastic work surface, like a cutting board. Lay
the baggies on it and cut strips of tape about 2-3 inches longer than
the width of the baggie. Starting from the top, lay the strips
across the baggie onto the cutting board... line the first piece of
tape up with the top lip of the baggie, then overlap each successive
piece about 1/4-1/2" until you get to the bottom. All your tape
strips should overhang the sides about 1-1 1/2 inches and the bottom
piece should overlap the edge of the baggie about an inch. Carefully
peel this off the cutting board and cut out the corners of the excess
tape to create flaps, be careful not to cut into the baggie when
you're doing this. Flip each flap up to the opposite side of the
baggie. Flip the baggie over and do the same thing to the other
side. You could be done now. Once I finished this part, though, I
added some tabs to the opening of the baggie to make it easier to
see/open. I also added some velcro to keep the baggies in a tighter
package when close. I don't like how that worked very well, so I'll
let you figure out your own way to close them up. One I saw "in the
wild" was just tied up with a piece of twine and tied to a bush branch.

From: TrailTroll (kanderson@american-community.com) | Date: 2007-11-09 15:24:05 UTC
I experimented with using camo-covered Ziplocs for a couple of boxes,
and it didn't work out very well. Even with the camo tape covering
it, the bags don't hold up very well. They get torn, or people don't
close them tightly. I am in the process of replacing the few bags I
have out there with conventional containers. This is a good method
for short-term placements, like a specific event, but I don't think
they'll stand up long-term.
