From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2008-02-05 06:11:43 UTC-05:00
This brings to mind my first altered book experience. A few years ago I hit the local thrift store to find a good book to use for my first altered book. I was mostly concerned with binding style, size and title. I found a book with a firm, sewn binding that was the size I was looking for with the title, "Buried Dreams" which fit my purpose well.

I got it home and began working in it and some bit of text caught my eye and I started reading further only to find out it was a biographical work about John Wayne Gacy. My first thought was disgust and I contemplated whether to even work any further or just ditch the book. But then I got hooked on the book and read it from cover to cover, minus the couple of pages I had already slathered with paint. I will have to say as sick and twisted as the subject is, I was absolutely fascinated by his story and his intelligence. Mostly I was aghast at how "normal" he was in his public image. It is truly a frightening thought that criminals such as this live among us and are crafty and manipulative enough to fool the most perceptive of us.

Anyway, I continued working in the book (it is my technique and practice book), figuring it was a good thing to be recycling such an awful story into something artful. No thanks on the stamp though.

SpringChick
