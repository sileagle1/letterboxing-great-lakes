IL Weekend

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2006-11-05 03:08:34 UTC
Well our IL weekend started out great (except for al that traffic
congestion on I294 last night). Finally made it to Elgin a bit
before 8 pm last night and met the Cottontails for dinner at a
brewpub. We enjoyed exchanging lots of letterboxing chit-chat with
them. They seem to be doing great with less than 2 months to go
until the twins emerge. It sounds like they have been busy getting
the house rearranged in preparation for the big event, but we did see
them out on the trail today on the hunt for a new series in Elgin!

We started our morning today in search of some of the fantastic
Cottontail stamps we hadn't looked for yet, including two more in
their artist series. GREAT!, as we expected. In addition to the
great stamps, we really enjoy reading about the history of the
artist.

While in Elgin, we also found 5 of the 7 "Things that Ruin a Picnic"
boxes. Didn't realize until late in the game that some were simply
in a baggie, not a box, so it is possible we missed the last two. (We
didn't do the series in sequential order). If you are a more diligent
searcher than we were, you might be able to uncover the bear and
skunk.

Also in Elgin was the great new "Nurtured by Love" series. I think
these newer boxers used the Cottontails as their example -
interesting background clues, great stamps, and very well put-
together boxes!

Enroute to DeKalb we looked for the Campton Preserve box, but no
luck. We suspect it may be missing.

In DeKalb we found 4 boxes by Hercules and another Grail box we
needed. More nice stamps!

So even on a damp, drizzly, dreary November day, we managed to find
19 boxes and left a HH in one of them. Nothing like a full day of
boxing to make it a fun day, regardless of weather! Tomorrow we plan
to "tackle" the Big Ten series in the Wheaton area.

Wisconsin Hiker

P.S. Stay tuned Michigan! It's not over, til it's over!
