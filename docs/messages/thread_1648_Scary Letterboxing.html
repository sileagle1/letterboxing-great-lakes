OK, so snakes in the trail do not scare me, but this did:

I'm walking along yesterday afternoon looking for a box, and I come around
a curve in the trail, and I see a man's naked butt - his pants are down
around his ankles, and he's just standing there on the side of the trail.
THANKFULLY! he was facing AWAY from the direction I was coming from and I
was walking along fairly quietly.
Talk about doing a QUIET and QUICK backwards walk around the curve I came
from and a sprint to the car.

I was brave enough to drive back a little later, with dog in tow - plus
there were two groups of people with kids and strollers just heading down
the trail, so I felt safe enough to follow behind and go get the boxes.

Anyone else run into scary people out on the trail??

From: christine borleis (sophiebeans@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-07-28 17:14:06 UTC-07:00
Yeah. Me too. I was going to plant a box in honor of Les Paul in the city of Waukesha. I was walking along the New Berlin bike trail, looking for a good hiding place, and noticed a footpath just off the trail. I thought, "Here might be a place to hide the box! Now all I have to do is find a good tree or a cluster of rocks...." Well, I ended up finding a pile of at least 15 odd pairs of little girls' underpants. I turned around and went home and called the police. I never did end up planting the box because, who wants to hide a box where some possible pedophile is lurking around? So sad!

Another time, while I want hunting for the Calatrava box in the woods across from Lake Michigan, I came across a discarded women's purse. Make-up, wallet and other odds and ends were laying scattered about. I appeared as though someone had stolen the purse and then took it somewhere to pilfer through it..... or even worse.....

All I can say is that I always take my dog with me when I go out letterboxing and a cellphone, because you can never be to sure or be too trusting.

Have fun, but keep safe!

Sophie & Me

When my friend Lilly (corbynnme) and I were looking for the Humming
letterbox, there was a man walking behind us the whole time. Whenever

middle of one of her very graphic speeches, he took off in the other
direction in a jog. It was great!
