Traveling to another state & wonder about finding local boxes?

Well, wonder no more! Bindle Bum and I are considering a trip to WI
before the Lake Express stops running for the season. I had wanted
to check out some of the WI boxes, but not being familiar with the
area, it was more difficult that I wanted to work with at the time.

Serendipity strikes. While cleaning up some old messages from my "in-
box" today, I came across a good one that I had forgotten to file.

Link and tip compliments of GreyCrazy in SC as posted on the big
list some time ago.

http://quickfacts.census.gov/qfd/index.html

Click on the state you want, or choose from the drop-down list on
the left side of the window, then click the blue "Go" button.

Look near the top of the next window for a small icon in the shape
of the state you've requested.

Directly below that icon is a tiny line that says "___ (state)
counties -- view map"

Click on the little link that says "view map".

Hint: check your printout when you're done, you might have to turn
it landscape to get it all one a single page.

Enjoy!

From: lesann847 (l-fischer@northwestern.edu) | Date: 2004-10-31 17:11:23 UTC

Another tip using these maps: I use them in my "big clue binder" as
separators before the clues for each state. I color in the counties that have
letterboxes (more and more these days), and when I'm travelling it's just a
quick glance to see which counties I'll be travelling through. It's easy then just
to pull the clues I need.

I also place the list from the LBNA site listing boxes in each state in the binder,
updating periodically, and checking off what I have.

Yes, it sounds tedious, but it really saves time for this naturally disorganized
letterboxer.

Acorn
