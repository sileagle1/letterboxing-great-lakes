Event Thanks

From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2007-06-06 01:21:34 UTC
I'd like to also offer a belated THANKS! to everyone involved with
the 5th Annual Great Lakes Gathering!

We got to Columbus later than expected Friday night, but managed to
get our boxes hid in the last bit of daylight. Unfortunately we
forgot our compass and should NOT have guessed on directions,
especially in the dark. We'll have better clues posted on LBNA soon.

After hiding our boxes we had a little bit of time to attend the Sock
Hop. We enjoyed meeting a few Ohio boxers and doing some exchanges
before the "big day". Very nice event stamp for this get-together!
Sorry we didn't have a chance to be there longer to enjoy the
festivities.

The next day we arrived at the shelter early to drop off copies of
our clues and pickup our packet of other clues. M&X8 and Eagle Eyes
sure had things organized! An amazing attention to detail made
everything go smoothly a major feat with so many people in
attendance. We spent most of the day out on the trails, with a few
stops back at the picnic area for snacks, chatting, exchanges and
tickling baby Emily's feet. It was great to see many of the boxers
we've met at past events including Azobox, the Cottontails, Wormy
Wanderers, TJMich, Team Rabbit, P Granola, the crazy Hiker Babe,
Nature Nerd and River Rat, Kimono Girl & Flying Dragon (& dogs),
Atom118 & CoffeeBeans, Fermont, Christmas Elves, Funguswoman, Doglvrs
and Speedsquare, I think we had 7 from Wisconsin attend the event
this year. We also enjoyed meeting some Ohio boxers and added many
new exchanges to our book. Hopefully we'll see them at future events
so we can start remembering what names go with who! It's always such
a whirlwind at these big events It takes us a few times to really
remember people.

We made it back to the shelters for a delicious potluck dinner, the
group photo and the bagpipes. What a nice way to end the day!

Sunday we were out on the trails again and met a few other boxers
along the way. Imagine our surprise when we did the 50s Jukebox
series that there were still a few prizes left! That meant we
enjoyed some good 50s hits as we drove around on Sunday and Monday.
What a creative idea Safari Man!!

We finished all the boxes hidden for the event on Sunday and hunted
for some older ones later in the day. We also did several boxes in
the area on Monday before we returned home that night. We'll be
sending "Contact the Placer" messages in the near future. Some
highlights on Monday were seeing a bluebird and visiting the rose
gardens which were in full bloom!

Thanks to everyone that contributed boxes to the event and a special
thanks to the hosts for putting together such a terrific event!

Wisconsin Hiker & Martini Man
