From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2008-12-13 18:59:53 UTC
I have uploaded the files for Bingo into the 2009 Bingo folder in the
files section of the list... there are 3 document:

1. The Bingo card/grid
2. The Rules
3. The Word List with instructions

All documents are in .PDF format. You will need the Adobe .PDF reader
to open them -- if for some reason you cannot open any of the
documents, let me know and we will work something out.

If you have not played Bingo with us before, please read through the
instructions carefully before making up your Bingo card as there are
some restrictions governing which words you can use.

Changes for 2009:

- There are 135 words instead of 125, with 10 general terms instead
of 8 for each letter. This will give newer letterboxers more general
terms to choose from and will hopefully help to prevent everyone
waiting for the same couple of words at the end of the game.

- There are 5 interim games instead of 4. Three of them are the same
as in previous years (straight line, 4 corners and "X"); two are new
(6-pack and 10-spot).

- Instead of a free space choice a few days into the game, you will
be playing with a Wild Card this year. There are 6 choices for your
wild card -- I will choose one wild card word per month (therefore not
all 6 will be called during the course of the game). Once your wild
card has been called, you can then use it to substitute for any space
on your card as you try for a full card Bingo (the wild card cannot be
used toward a win in the interim games).

Please get your submissions out in the mail this coming week to ensure
I have them by December 30.

Have fun making up and cards and good luck!

SpringChick

It's that time of year again -- time to get your card filled in and submitted, along with your booty, in preparation for the annual Great Lakes Letterbox BINGO! Anyone who is a member of the Letterbox Great Lakes Yahoo! group can play, regardless of where you actually live.

I have uploaded the files for Bingo into the 2010 Bingo folder in the
files section of the group... there are 4 documents:

1. The Bingo card/grid
2. The Rules
3. The Word List with instructions
4. FAQ with General Info

You will need the Adobe .PDF reader to open the documents -- if for some reason you cannot open them, let me know and we will work something out.

If you have not played Bingo with us before, please read through the
the FAQ, the rules and the instructions carefully before making up your Bingo card as there are some restrictions governing which words you can use.

There are no significant changes for the 2010 game (sorry, my creativity is on overload in other areas right now), which means:

- There are 135 words total, you will choose 25 for your card, per the instructions.

- In addition to full card, there are 5 interim games -- straight line, 4 corners, "X", 6-pack and 10-spot.

- We will be using the "state" wild card again this year, which can earn you a free space toward the full card game.

Please get your submissions out in the mail this coming week to ensure
I have them by December 30.

If you have any questions, feel free to post them to the list because somebody else could very well be wondering the same thing. Have fun making up and your cards and good luck!

SpringChick

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2009-12-14 07:28:05 UTC-08:00
"Monkey Journal" is a type of handmade log book.

"Purple Skull Guy" is a phantom stamp that has shown up randomly for a few years now across the country in logbooks in boxes and people's personal logbooks at gatherings, but nobody really knows who it is or where it comes from (it's a little purple skull).

"Centipede" is a common word used in clues to describe a fallen tree with many broken limbs along its length (typically a pine tree).

SpringChick

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2009-12-19 12:50:32 UTC
Yes, that's correct. The general terms are available to anyone, but to use terms from the other 3 categories (letterboxes, events, letterboxers), one must meet the criteria of having found (or planted) the box, attended the event or exchanged with the letterboxer (or be that letterboxer).

Most people will qualify for only a very small number of the terms in the letterboxes, letterboxers and events categories, if any at all. Don't be discouraged if you are limited only to the general terms -- any of the veteran players will tell you that it really doesn't make a lot of difference and people have won the games with only general terms many times.

SpringChick
