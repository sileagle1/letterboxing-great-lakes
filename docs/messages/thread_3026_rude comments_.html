Re: [LbGLK] rude comments?

From: Doodle & Deedle Bug (doodle_n_deedle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-09-01 07:01:48 UTC-07:00
We've thankfully never gotten any rude comments via
email. I remember checking in on our (still hasn't
been replaced) Duck Tape box once, and a few people
writing things about the clues being hard. I laughed
and thought, "They think THIS box was hard to find?
They should try finding a SpringChick box!!"

I tend not to put ratings on our boxes such as "good
for kids" or "easy find" or "3 on a scale of 1 to 5"
because, well, honestly, I don't know how others will
react. My easy may be someone else's hard, and since
we planted the box and knew exactly what we were
thinking when we wrote the clues of course we think
that all our plants are easy. I hope that they'll
read over the clues and use their best judgement. Of
course, if someone were to email me and ask about a
box's difficulty I'd try to help them determine if
it's right for them.

I remember one box we found in southern Ohio last year
(and when we finally found the log others did the same
thing we did) where it would have been a really easy
find - had we started on the correct end of the trail.
But the clues weren't incredibly specific, and we
weren't familiar with the park, and what could have
been a 10 minute hike turned into an hour and 10
minute hike because we ended up going the way that
took us 3 miles around the lake. But we didn't write
anything rude in the logbook about how the placer
needed to be more specific - that's letterboxing, and
it's the placer's prerogative to be as specific or
vague in the clues as they want.

Doodle Bug.

From: (kevjean@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-09-01 19:31:45 UTC
We haven't planted any boxes yet, but making rude comments about them is ridiculous! The fun part of if it is that it's like a scavenger hunt or a wild goose chase! We spent 8 hours trying to find the 9 deer, but we just email the placer about where we were and if we were on the right track. We found out through others that found the box before us that there was a mis direction in there, and after that, we found it no problem. I had to laugh when we went hunting for the Rodent gets an extreme home makeover. We were EVERYWHERE on this path, and looking at everything, and finally the kids were gettting tired, so I told my parents I was going to head back to the van with the kids, and that maybe we were missing something. Well, I sent my oldest boy back to tell Pappa we were heading back, and then 2 things caught my eye. Well, my parents were heading back with my son, and I pointed them out to my dad. I asked him could this be what we're supposed to be looking for. Well, low
and behold it was and we found the box. I laughed and told him after 50 finds I finally figured one out before him!!! =) If it wasn't for me stopping where I was and just start looking at what others may seem "stupid" little things, we would've found the box. We like all types, easy to find, some straining your brain power, or to even think outside the box, they are all great to find.
