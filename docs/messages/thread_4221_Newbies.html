Boxrunner & Froggie,

You might try looking for boxes that are more of the 'drive-by' type or
very short hikes. This information can sometimes be figured out by the
clues. A quick note to a placer might also garner you some terrain
information. Many placers will not or cannot accurately respond to 'is
your box still there' but will respond and let you know of terrain or
length of hike if the information is not posted, especially if you
explain the situation.

I'm sure the "old school" letterboxers would probably frown on this,
but...oh well.
Our family went on vacation recently and wanted to do some
letterboxing along the way.
One particular one that we wanted to get was down a very hilly trail.
It was hot and muggy, and mosquito infested. The kids were cranky and
wanted to stay in the car to watch DVDs. So my husband grabbed one
walkie-talkie and the back pack and hit the trail. I stayed in the
car with the clues, the kids and the other walkie-talkie. I read him
the clues while he looked for the box. I didn't get the full effect
of the journey, but I still felt like I was contributing to the hunt.
When he found the box he stamped in for the both of us.
We continued doing this for many of the letterboxes on our trip,
alternating who had to stay in the car.
You might want to try doing that for the boxes that are too much for
your wife. (It also is nice for those really long hikes from one end
of a park to the other. You don't have to walk back to the car. Just
have the other person drive around to pick you up!)

From: (Mathw613@aol.com) | Date: 2007-07-09 22:30:42 UTC-04:00
We have used walkie talkies now for several months and they are GREAT. A few
times either my wife or I would be down a trail that ends up at a road and
we can communicate this via Walkie talkies and have the other drive around for
pick up.

Also, when the kids are really cranky, I especially feel guilty, so the
quicker I can say "FOUND IT" the better, then my loving wife knows I will be back
soon.

One funny story we just got back from a near 6,000 mile trip out to the west
coast (including all side trips). I wanted to find a box on Expedition
Island in Green River, Wyoming.

The sprinklers were going, and I was dodging the water shooting into the air
trying to find my way with a spot light. It was about 1 am.

My wife got me on walkie talkie saying that a car was pulling into the lot.
I started making my way back to the car so she would feel safe when she said,
"it's a cop".

When I got back to the car the cop was standing at her window talking.
Believe it or not he never asked what we were doing there or why I was dodging
sprinklers with a spot light.

He just asked if we had plans of camping out or sleeping in our car. We said
no and he said that if we had he would have an extra patrol car swing by
during the night to make sure we were safe. Didn't even ask to see our
identification!

It was a great trip, even though we came up empty in our search for this
particular box.

Hart x6---what a delightful narrative of your game of dodging the water
drops and cops! It sounds like you all had an outstanding summer
vacation with your family! Very glad you didn't get "busted"!

To you and M&8X both---I'm so glad you have mentioned using
walkie-talkies to share boxing with someone who isn't able to hit the
trail for one reason or another. On several occassions---I have had
someone on my cell phone box with me---either at my request or theirs
where they are the only ones who had the clues. The first time it
happened, I was in another state and suddenly realized that I was in the
VERY location where a box was and did not have the clues. So I asked my
friend to get online and look up the clues. THAT in itself was a hoot
for someone who had never been letterboxing. I felt like it was a mild
version of me telling them how to land a plane over the phone, but then
it reversed as the clues were being read to me. So when WE finally
found the box, I wrote both our names in the log. I've also had my
middle son use his high-powered satallite at Ohio State University to
help me with one of your extremely tricky and well-done boxes too,
M&8X!! Doing those together definitely takes your communication skills
up a notch and adds a wonderful dimension to this game!

It's great that it can be done!!
~Trailtracker
