As far as whether it's Jeff or not, here's my story. I live on a lake with wetlands outside my back door. We get one pair of nesting swans every year. Unfortunately, since then we haven't been able to tell the rest of the pairs apart, so now they all have the same name!

We have no idea why a turkey has returned or why the first turkey came to our village last year. We have never had turkeys in this area. But there are big smiles on the faces of many Lake Bluff residents today. Despite the police department's 'evil' efforts last year, a wild turkey has returned to that very same corner. We watched as Jeff was chased with a net, forced into the street, and hit by a car. He lost some feathers, but he survived. Strong bird. He was caged and taken away. And now, a turkey has returned. A local resident and a local artist created a book called The Town Turkey. Unlike what really happened, the storybook had a happy ending; the town turkey returned with his friends, "thousands of gleeful gooblers." So one might say....dreams come true. Gobble Gobble Gobble.
