CatHerders:

My computer was broken, but it's fixed now and I'm back on line tonight.

Last Thursday, I was up rather late into the wee hours of the morning making white birch furniture for my fairy house. Over the weekend, I attended a GS workshop all about fairy houses, although I already knew more than most because I have the book. But I did get to see the video!
Today (Thursday) I found an 'inviting' piece of bark and a gnarly tree branch which inspired me to create an elaborate and weatherproof fairy house in my front yard. Entrance way (tall grass, rocks, grapevine,[ine cones, pods). Under a large piece of bark is the living room with a paper birch floor covering, dining room with crab apples on the table, and a bedroom with a moss floor and a feather bed. Also, there is a pool (sea shell), beach house (small gourd), and a seating area with a teepee-shaped screened patio. Lots of turkey feathers for shade. And the tiniest piece of wood shaped like a canoe.

For those who don't know what a fairy house looks like.... www.fairyhouses.com It's amazing the projects we get involved in when we spent time outside enjoying nature.

How special it would be to be walking through the woods or along a shoreline and stumble across a fairy house located near a letterbox. Believe. There is magic in nature.

Maybe a fairy house will magically appear at the 10th WI Gathering in Door County.

Beetle
