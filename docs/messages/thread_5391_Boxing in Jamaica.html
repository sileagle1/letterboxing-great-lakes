Boxing in Jamaica

From: beckers410 (Mathw613@aol.com) | Date: 2008-11-04 13:40:44 UTC
While there is much to be said of my trip to Jamaica, most of it is
OT and I will leave it for our blog (I will be getting a disk of
pictures this weekend -- then I will update our blog).

Jamaica was my first boxing abroad, and boy what adventures I had :-)

We had a couple free days at the end of the missions trip in Ocho
Rios where two boxes reside. After climbing Dunns River Falls, and
buying junk . . . er . . . gifts for the kids ;-) I took a buddy of
mine (Oster Toaster) to find his first two boxes (and to hide one of
my own!).

So, off to find "Smile Mon". Hidden in a bunch of ivy behind a water
spout on a wall.

We couldn't find the water spout with all the ivy there. We decided
to go look somewhere else and gave up and went back to where the
spout is, however there was a Jamaican man standing there going to
the bathroom on the wall!!!

We apologized for "interrupting" him and kept walking. After he was
done, I still couldn't find the spout!

Not wanting to give up on my first overseas box, I went to that same
Jamaican (who ran the little stand at the entrance) and asked if he
knew where I could find some water to splash on my face to cool off.

He pointed me back to the same corner, I looked another minute and
asked if he would be willing to show me -- as I was "very hot and
needed water to splash on my face" he wasn't to happy, but walked
right up and touched it for me.

How could I have missed it!! DUH!

I dutifully splashed water on my face as he returned to his stand and
when he was out of sight, I grabbed the box!! LOL

As we were stamping in this couple were walking around the parking
lot, just looking at the vegetation and taking in the scenery.

I knew they had spotted us earlier looking around like a couple of
buffoon's so I invited them over and explained letterboxing to them.

Turns out they were from Belgium and had been to many of the sites in
Jamaica already. They seemed very interested in the hobby and
wondered if there were any in Belgium.

I assured them that there probably were boxes in Belgium.

I gave them a copy of the stamp image and my signature stamp, with
both LBNA & AQ web addresses, with of course my dissertation on
letterboxing. :-)

All this and we still had one more box to find in town . . . a mile
outside of town. We decided not to spend $8 on a cab ride, we could
just hike it.

You would be amazed though at how many cabs slowed down for the poor
white guys trudging up the hill :-)

It was quite the climb, but we made it. Shaw Park Gardens was the
location. We were taken around the gardens where James Bonds "Live
and Let Die" was filmed.

We were taken on a tour of the magnificent gardens, we were the only
two on the tour. So, it was easy for me to slip away and hide my box
under the massive trunk of a banion tree.

After the tour was over we headed to the bathrooms, the sidewalk to
which led right by the other letterbox in town.

I went to grab it and Oster Toaster alerted me someone was coming. I
jumped back to the sidewalk and we casually walked to the bathrooms.

After some relief :-) I went back and grabbed the box.

It was a very successful trip finding 2 of 2 and hiding one of my
own :-)

hx6

P.S. Oh, forgot to mention. The first box we found was also found by
none other than Great Lakes own Travel'n Turtle!!

You're right WiHi, the Great Lakes Group's reach is far and wide :-)

And TT, it was awesome seeing a familiar sig. stamp, even with the
disguise of dreds :-)
