From: azobox (jparkerg@yahoo.com) | Date: 2009-10-23 15:14:11 UTC

I voted to keep the date close to the original (Memorial Day) weekend.
Now, it just so happens that I've been able to attend each of the events over the past years, so those June dates have worked for me.
As Atom118 notes, there are many variables.
So let's control for *one* of them -- the date -- and leave it where it's been.

The prospective hosts will at least know the parameters within which they're working and can either move their proposals forward if the date works, or if they can't manage the task on that date, offer to help someone else who CAN do it.
There are active letterboxers in the region who can not even attend an event in the late spring. Likewise, there are boxers who can't attend summer events.

It's never going to be a perfect fit for everyone.

That said, I am admittedly a traditionalist when it comes to letterboxing. And the proximity to the end of May/first of June has been the tradition for the Annual Great Lakes Gathering.
