From: LindaFay (coachlindafay@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-07-15 11:23:47 UTC
Three Things:
1. Treasure Trove: I was just wondering if anyone was trying to solve
the Treasure Trove book puzzle. Although 8 of the 15 prizes have been
found (you find a gold token - sorta like CATCF) and can redeem it for
a jeweled bug. Three of the ones found were in knot holes of trees.
Any way, I bought the book for my great nieces at Christmas and I
finally started looking at mine copy because they were pestering me and
it is fun. Why I'm asking - I'm in NW Ohio and if someone near me is
working on it, I'd be glad to help and mostly I'd like to learn more
about doing these type of mysteries. My goal - sharpen my ciphering
skills.

2. Rainy Day Boxers: Yesterday as I was all dressed for work (that
means stockings, suit, polished shoes) jumped in my car and roared out
the driveway only to be stopped by a truck at the end of the driveway!
It was Letterboxers that were checking out a nearby box and it was
raining, so they parked in my driveway to stamp out of the rain. I
think I really startled them! Such fun to finally meet someone while
boxing.

3. Billy Bob: While looking for one of Spring Chicks Lighthouse boxes
in MI, we arrived at the park with
1)gas light of car blinking
2)9:30 PM and yes, it was getting dark even in MI in the summer
3)no sticker for park on car. That is where Billy Bob Thorton comes
in. You see, I'm sure that the DNR Ranger was really the famous actor
working on his next movie getting first had knowledge about parks. He
flagged me down at the gate (I was going to stop, really) and asked
what I wanted to do this late in the evening - due to my honest nature
and dumb struckness at the thought that this was Billy Bob - I blurted
out:
"I'm here to look at the 'letter-light house'". The passengers in my
car went silent, you could cut the air with a lumberman's axe and he
responded:
"The Letter-Light House? Well, just get out of my hair and go do what
you need to do". With that Billy Bob waved us through without a
sticker and we directly found what we came for and even made it to the
gas station. I did purchase an out of state sticker for MI at the next
park want to support those great parks.

So stealth is something I have to work on - but FUN I am having.
You'll know me if you see me on the trail - I'm probably still looking
for Billy Bob and am often confounded by the three dog leases tangled
about my legs with a compass in my hand.

Happy Boxing.
LindaFay
