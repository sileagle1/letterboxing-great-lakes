From: Tom and Jill (jillonline@ameritech.net) | Date: 2004-10-25 20:46:09 UTC

This past weekend, Tom and I traveled to the UP for a combination
family visit and letterboxing trip. On the way home, we drove past
a Supper Club -- a common sight in the UP. It started a discussion
about how far-reaching the term "Supper Club" really was.

We know they exist in the UP and in NE Wisconsin. We also know that
the term isn't common in the SE Michigan area -- a discovery made a
few years ago when we got odd looks and comments from friends down
here after seeing our wedding reception was held at a Supper Club up
north in our hometown. ("Do you have to be a member?" "What about
lunches?")

So is the concept of a "supper club" something limited to the UP/NE
Wisconsin? We figured this list would be a good geographic sample
for our unofficial, completely off-topic poll :).

Thanks,

Jill of TJ-Mich, who chooses odd topics of conversation after being
in a car with her husband for over 25 hours in a 4 day period...

From: aine_42 (aine_42@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-10-25 22:28:17 UTC

Well, we've got a few here in Milwaukee, and I've heard the
term used since I was a little kid, so it extends at least as
far as SE WI.

Didn't know it was regional. Also didn't know until recently
that the term "Polish Flat" was a local Milwaukee term for
a specific style of house, so I may not be the best one to
ask about this. :-)

(And before anyone asks, here's a link explaining Polish flats:
http://www.uwm.edu/News/Features/04.09/polishflat.html)

Oh, and for those of you unfamiliar with them, a "supper
club" is a kind of combination restaurant & nightclub. You
know -- have yourself a nice big steak and watch a lounge
singer. Maybe do some dancing. That kind of thing. Think "1950s."

From: (papeseed@tds.net) | Date: 2004-10-25 19:03:54 UTC-05:00
Czeltic Girl beat me to it...I was going to say I'm originally from Sheboygan (about 60 miles north of Milwaukee) and we had supper clubs there. I learned the hard way that Sheboygan has its own language! Terms I grew up with, and EVERYBODY used, got me VERY strange looks when moving just 65 miles north to Green Bay. Let me share a few:

Bubbler = water fountain (people do say this even in GB, though)
Hot tamale = sloppy joe
Fry out = grill out or BBQ
Cooler = popsicle
Bakery = sweet rolls
Sliding Board = slide

Funny, huh? --Papeseed
