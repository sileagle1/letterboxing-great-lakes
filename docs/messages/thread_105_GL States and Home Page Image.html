In following the recent thread about GL states, and a few private e-
mails I have received, I wanted to comment on the scope of this
group and the stamp image on the home page.

This group is for LB'ers in the upper Midwest area of the US and for
anyone else who may have an interest in LB'es and LB'ing in that
region. There are folks who have joined in although they live in
other regions because they have boxes here, because they travel here
or because they are just generally interested in what is happening
in LB'ing in other areas of the county. There are not meant to be
any boundaries and everyone is welcome.

I realize the stamp image on the home page depicts only 5 states,
and does not include MN, which is indeed a GL state, as also it does
not include NY, which could easily be considered a GL state also.
There certainly was no intent to exclude any particular state or
states. The stamp is from the Great Lakes Gathering last year and
was not made especially for this group. At the time the gathering
was being organized, the original scope was to have a Michigan LB
gathering. Once word got around, several people from OH and IL
expressed interest in being a part of the party. Rather than
calling it a MI gathering -- which I suppose technically could have
been called the GL gathering all on its own, since MI is known as
the GL state, the name was changed to encompass surrounding states --
and the Great Lakes gathering was born. The stamp was carved based
on the locations of people who were planning to attend the
gathering. Originally WI was not even included, not because it is
not a GL state, rather because there were no LB'ers from WI at the
time, or at least none expressing an interest in coming to the
gathering. At the 11th hour a gal from VA who was originally from
WI decided to make the trip and come to the gathering and I carved a
new stamp which included WI.

The stamp image is meant to be symbolic of the GL area not
necessarily as an accurate map of everyone included in this group
and certainly not meant to exclude or limit the scope. That being
said, I think it is an appropriate image for the home page, however
if there is strong feeling that it is exclusionary, I will remove it.

Thanks!

SpringChick
