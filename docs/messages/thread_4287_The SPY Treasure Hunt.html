> From everything I can see, this is a group of primarily adult
> letterboxers out having a great time letterboxing. While we may or
> may not agree with the way they are going about enjoying the hobby,
> let's take a step back, and perhaps get the facts, before roasting
> them.
>
> We all were new letterboxers at one time and we all unknowingly did
> things against the ethics or unwritten rules of letterboxing. Most
> who have been around the hobby for some time would agree that stealth
> is a large and rewarding part of the game, but most of us did not
> *get it* from day one, and some still don't, or choose not to
> consider that a crucial element of the game. By nature stealth is
> illusive and does not come with a written set of instructions.
>
> As for respect -- for the environment, by practicing leave-no-trace
> ethics, and for other people's boxes, by not sharing clues and being
> certain to rehide boxes well -- these are things that seem obvious to
> us, but are often a matter of education and experience with new
> letterboxers.
>
> I am certainly not saying we should ignore the situation, but I do
> think it has become a bit dramatic and we haven't really given this
> group a chance. They may be very nice folks and once they understand
> the hobby, could prove to be very enthusiastic letterboxers who plant
> great boxes to contribute to *our* fun.
>
> I have written to the contact e-mail at the web link, expressing some
> of our concerns and offering suggestions for learning more about the
> hobby. I would also like you to be aware that there may be members
> of the group who have joined our list and we should welcome them to
> the hobby and our group and help them learn and understand the things
> we are saying here, rather than come out swinging.
>
> SpringChick
