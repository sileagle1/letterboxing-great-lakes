Ok, as long as we are talking stats
What is the decent FTP (Found To Planted = boxes found/boxes planted)
ratio?

found)?
Or worthy MPB (Miles Per Box = miles hiked/boxes found)?

Atom118 of Atomic Beans
==================

FTP - When we first started boxing in 2003 there weren't very many boxes
around. We therefore set a goal of planting 1 box for every 10 found.
However more boxes started appearing and we revised that to 1 for every 20.
Now we don't have a specific goal - we try to plant to keep things active in
our area as well as planting in areas we visit while traveling. Our current
FTP is 1 box planted for every 26 found.

BPM - We like longer hikes and like finding several boxes along the way.
We've done 10-mile hikes to find 1 box and planted only 1 box on a 7-mile
hike with over 2,000 ft elevation gain. However we generally like to find a
box every 2 miles or so. We've planted a 5-box series on a 9-mile hike, 4
boxes on a 6-mile hike, 3 boxes on a 6-mile hike, etc. Of course we realize
that not everyone likes to hike as much as we do!

HHR - We've only released 1 hitchhiker each year we have been boxing. Still
need to launch one for this year.... A different hitchhiker statistic that
is on our spreadsheet is how many HHs we find vs. total boxes found. We
don't go to HH hostels, so these only represent HHs that we have found "in
the wild". I guess you could call this HHF. Our current percentage is 4.8%
(1 HH found for every 21 boxes found).

MPB - see BPM above.

ABT - we quit keeping track of this statistic a LONG time ago!

Other statistics that we track on our spreadsheet are adopted boxes, and
what we count as "miscellaneous" stamp images (personal travelers, cooties,
stamps at events that were simply sitting out on a table, etc.) Our current
"A" count =1 and our "M" count = 246. Of course we also track how many
events we have attended E=36. Our spreadsheet also tracks how many times we
were the first to find a box.

Our spreadsheet is pretty straightforward. We created it many years ago,
way before on-line tracking started. For each stamp image, we record the
name, carver, city, state, date, and type of stamp (P, F, X, E, HH, HP, A,
M). We can sort and graph any which way we like....

FUN with statistics!!! (Sorry Tiptoe, it really IS fun for those of us who
like numbers!)

Wisconsin Hiker

Ooh, you make me so sad! I'm in Illinois without my spreadsheet so I can't
do the math, too. I'll have to wait until I get home.

As for the numbers Atom118 was asking for:
When I started boxing, there were 6 boxes within 2 hours of where I lived,
so my FTP ratio was very low, probably around 5 found for every 1 planted.
I don't know that I would want every one to have that ratio though (heck, I
didn't *want* that ratio). Lately, I just plant when I get an idea, no
thought of ratios. I do, however, think that every one should plant at
least one box. Ya know, giving back and all that.

Ideal BPM: I agree with WiHi, I like hiking. About 1 box every 1-2 miles
works well for me. I don't think I've ever hiked more than that to get a
box but I have contemplated putting on one the 7 mile trail at Kennekuk, and
it would not at all deter me to hike that far for a box. Since I like
hiking more than stopping (and the dogs tolerate stamping in breaks only
because they get treats), I prefer not to have more than 2 boxes per mile.
Frankly, I never think to look at the clues for the next box in a series
until I've hiked for a little while (generally about 1/4 mile), so if the
boxes are closer together than that, I end up backtracking and make the BPM
closer to my personal ideal. And sometimes I think the dogs prefer the
backtracking, since they get to sniff both sides of the trail much more
extensively that way.

As for the rest, I hardly ever seem to find hitchhikers, which makes them a
pleasant surprise when I do, so whatever the current rate is, I think it's
ideal. If'm I'm recalling my numbers correctly, I've found roughly 1 HH for
every 30 boxes, so a little lower than WiHi. I don't record attempts, so I
can't add anything there. I suppose I could add a sheet to my Oo file for
that.... I keep a separate sheet for plants, finds, exchanges, hitchikers,
and personal travelers.

Katy

First. I'm glad that there were at least two replies to my crazy email
with all the stats. I was told I might have gone a bit over the edge.

As for F1000,......YES, we got there this weekend! Yea!!!!!!! And for
those that find it after us, CB added some very nice fireworks to the
box logbook.

Also, WI Hiker, very cool pie charts. I like how you have them grouped
by region. Have any idea when you'll get all the states? ;-)

For the stats I posted, in actuality, only a few were ones we try to
consider. We have been aiming for a FTP of about 10 (1 box planted for
every 10 we find). It might be a bit high, but we tend to remove alot
of our boxes for one reason or another. The current number of our
'active' FTP is more like 20 (1 box for every 20 we find). This way we
can give back a relative amount to those we find, but also do it at a
reasonable rate to give us time to go boxing. As for BPM, we shoot for
about 1 box per mile except for our few drive-bys or other special
circumstances. We find this to be sufficient to keep the flow of a
hike, but high enough to keep people motivated. For our HHR, I like
HHs. I've been shooting for a HHR=FTP, but we've lagged a bit. Our HHR
is more like 5 which is oddly similar to our HHF of about 1 HH found (or
planted) for every 20 boxes we find. The ABT and MPB may have been a
bit ill-conceived so I won't consider them....one is higher than we'd
like and one is probably lower than we expect!

Ok, enough fun with numbers.

Atom118 of Atomic Beans
