We just returned home from the Boar's Head Festival in which our own
GLK letterboxers, the Waltzing Pigs, along with over a hundred
others treated us to an extraordinary performance that instilled the
wonder and awe of the true spirit of the Christmas season!

This has to go down as one of the best "places" that a letterbox
series has brought us! The church was stunning!

Anyone in the Chicago area that has tomorrow (12/10/06) afternoon
available (4PM) and are able to make your way to St. John's
Evangelical Lutheran Churh in Forest Park, IL, can still experience
the beautiful tradional music and pagentry that this event has to
offer....let alone the fifth stamp in the Boar's Head Festival
series!

As mentioned in an earlier post, we took our non-letterboxing
friends that make fun of our little hobby of letterboxing
(matchbooking). At the end of the performance they both had tears
in their eyes and repeatedly thanked us for bringing them...and
letting them share in the stealth of "stamping in"....they have a
whole new view of letterboxing!

Thanks so much, Waltzing Pigs! It truly ushered us into the
Christmas Spirit!

Boar's Head Festival

From: cdem_ltrbox (wp4@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2007-11-13 05:19:43 UTC
Our church in Forest Park, IL will once again be presenting the
Boar's Head Festival this December. Last year we did a 5-box series
on the festival4 boxes based on the parts each of us 4 Waltzing Pigs
played and the last box about the festival in general. We won't be
doing the first four boxes, but the last box will be availableonly
at the performances. I will post the clues for it once I figure out
where to hide it this year. Not that I am biased or anything, but I
think the Boar's Head Festival is wonderfuland it is one of the
highlights of the Christmas season for us. I hope many of you will
be able to come!

The performances will be held on Saturday, December 8th at 4pm and
7pm. It will also be held on Sunday, December 9th at 4pm. Advance
tickets are $12 general audience and $10 for students and seniors.
There is also family pricing for larger families. To order tickets,
you can call 708-453-2654. Tickets are also available at the door
for $15. The church is St. John Lutheran Church at 305 Circle Ave in
Forest Park, IL.

In case you are wondering what a Boar's Head Festival is, here is the
description from the poster:

"What began as a pagan festival was endowed by the early Christians
with symbolic religious meaning. The boar, being the most ferocious
beast in the forest, was considered the ultimate symbol of evil. Its
death came to symbolize Christ's victory over sin.

Into the midst of medieval gallantry, elaborate costumes, Yule log
and King Wenceslas, appears the Christ Child, the angels, the
shepherds and the three kings.

How can this happen together? St. John Ev. Lutheran Church in Forest
Park, IL will show you. Come and experience the pomp and pageantry,
the entertainers and musicians of the royal manor house. Come and be
a part of the birth of Christ and let His light penetrate and
rejuvenate your spirit during this festive time of year."

We now return to our regular programming :~)

BirdWoman/Waltzing Pigs

Boar's Head Festival

From: cdem_ltrbox (wp4@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2009-12-06 01:33:27 UTC
Once again, our church is presenting the Boar's Head Festival--and I will be putting out the Boar's Head Stamp somewhere in the building (clues to follow later this week). The Boar's Head Festival will be presented at 4pm on Saturday, 12/12 and Sunday 12/13. So, head out to see the Bunnies on the 12th and the Boar's Head on the 13th.

Here is a short description of the event:

One of the oldest Christmas festivals is the Boar's Head Festival, which was first observed around 1340 A.D. at the Queen's College in Oxford. It started as a pagan festival (roasted boar was a staple at medieval feasts) and was then given symbolic religious meaning. The boar (the most ferocious beast in the forest) became the ultimate symbol of evil and death, defeated by the birth of Christ. The festival is set in a medieval English noble's court and includes beefeaters, lords and ladies, a court jester, dancers, serving wenches and wassailers, a beggar, and good King Wenceslas and his page. The second part of the performance takes a more serious turn. The Christmas nativity is enacted, with angels and shepherds and magi coming to see Mary, Joseph and the Christ Child. In a grand finale, the medieval court joins the scene to pay homage to the newborn King. This last scene is very moving (I cry every time).

Our church, which has presented the Boar's Head Festival in the past, is presenting it again this year. We are blessed with several theater professionals and a number of talented musical people. Even so, this is a major undertaking, as it includes about 100 adults and children in the cast, a 40-voice choir, an orchestra, and gorgeous costuming. Performances will take place on Saturday, Dec. 12(at 4 pm) and on Sunday, Dec. 13 (at 4 pm). It will be held at St. John Lutheran Church, 305 Circle Avenue in Forest Park, IL (a suburb of Chicago). Advance sale tickets are $12 for adults and $10 for seniors/students and can be ordered by calling 708-366-2593 Tickets will also be available at the door for $15.

http://www.stjohnforestpark.org/boarshead.htm

Hope to see some of you there!

BirdWoman/WP
