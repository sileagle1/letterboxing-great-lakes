If you are traveling US-2 a "must see" is the Cut River Bridge about 25 miles NW of St Ignace, near the town of Epoufette, MI. It doesn't look like much from the road but underneath WOW. There is a rest area, steps down to the lake, some trails and even a great letterbox of the same name! (there are also quite a few other nice boxes along this route)

From: (norsegnome@yahoo.com) | Date: 2016-05-04 16:50:01 UTC-07:00

More recommendations for your Lk. Michigan Circle Tour:

*Seven Wonders of Michigan Series by Angel Winks, Sleeping Bear Dunes National Lakeshore
*Mother Bear by ledgeworth von haus, Glen Arbor, MI (historic cemetery with a beautiful view)
*A Nuclear Plant Was Here by Ezmerelda, Charlevoix, MI
*Glacial Series by Angel Winks, Bellaire, MI (great stamps, great trails)
*Father Marquette National Memorial, St. Ignace, MI (lovely park with views of Mackinac Bridge and letterboxes by Windjammer)
Antique Snowmobile Museum in Naubinway, MI (if you're into snowmobiles)
*Oswald's Bear Ranch (http://www.oswaldsbearranch.com/) largest bear ranch in US
*The Christmas Tree Ship - historic marker and letterbox by 4EyesMcGee, Thompson, MI
*Fayette State Park, Garden Peninsula, MI.(https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fayette_Historic_State_Park) 
*Palms Book State Park near Manistique, MI - a deep natural spring which can be viewed from a raft
