From: klk-fw (krank@ameritech.net) | Date: 2012-08-07 03:19:46 UTC
We were limited to searching in the Savannah Historical District and Tybee Island. Cheekee Monkey's "Savannah" is a beautiful carve; Eidolon's "Tour of Savannah" was a lot of fun to research and find. On Tybee we did "Tybee Island Lighthouse" and "Ghost Crab"...time and weather prevented us from searching for more there. In a certain nameless cemetery (spoiler) we found three boxes, including one from a member of this group.

"MN flower for the garden..." and "100 years and still strong" are definitely missing.

And of course there's a newly hidden box in town thanks to Yours Truly..........LbNA #62204, AQ #199943.

From: rlovely1@sbcglobal.net (rlovely1@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2012-08-07 07:27:34 UTC

We were in Hilton Head in March of 2011. There weren't nearly the number of boxes to look for as there are now.

We enjoyed finding the following boxes....
The Gullah Storyteller - a quick driveby, loved the colorful stamp and history
Doodle Box: Ellie - enjoyed the walk through the Audobon Newhall Preserve
The Ghost of William Baynard - great historical spot....Baynard Ruins
Beach Soire series - the wildlife stamp provided a nice hike through Sea Pines Forest Preserve and the sailing stamp at Baynard Ruins

As far as the newer boxes planted that you might want to try (based on our visit to the island)...........Salty Dog is a fun place to eat, we loved Harbor Town and the lighthouse w/museum, and we rode our bikes through and past tCompass Rose Park quite a few times and took pictures of the Charles Fraiser sculpture. The compass rose in the park is pretty cool.

Hope this helps. Enjoy your trip!!!
Hppy2bmom
