letterboxing vs. dinner?

From: klustofin (katy@dsa132.org) | Date: 2007-06-19 04:41:01 UTC
I'm currently in Georgia for business. There are about a dozen of us
from my company staying at the hotel and three of us are working
second shift, from 1 pm to 11 pm. The day shift people have been
pitying us because we can never go out to dinner with them, but this
morning I was pitying them.

This morning I enjoyed a breakfast of ripe wild blackberries while
hiking at a gorgeous park just a few miles from our hotel. I found a
very interesting letterbox, learned a bit about local history and
cotton farming, played in a creek with some dogs (I'm missing
Claire-dog and Yuki quite a bit), and saw some really cool lizards.
Somehow, dinner at the local Italian place with a bunch of people I
see half the day at work anyway pales in comparison to all that.

And tomorrow there's another park and another box and, I'd bet money,
more berries.

Katy

I SO wish I had known about letterboxing when I had to travel with my job.
I traveled at least one week a month, and one month a quarter I was gone for
all but one week. And then during Texas' legislative year I was gone even
more!

I spent way too much time doing happy hour, eating out in heavy expensive
restaurants, going to bars with colleagues I didn't particularly like but
had to politic with, or slaving over my laptop in my room. I was such a
workaholic!

way to escape all that and get out and go boxing. So, Katy - Way to go,
Girl!
