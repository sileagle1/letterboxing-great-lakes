I think you should absolutely contact them. Explain that this isn't
the spirit of letterboxing and that it takes out most of the fun, as
well as endangers the boxes. Contact the main number of the
organizers, but also, I'd email all of the finders that you know of
within this group to explain it, too. Maybe if you reach enough of
them, it'll sink in and the word will spread among them.

Also, please let us know which town this is originating from and post
links to the newspaper article you mentioned and to whatever else
they've posted. Maybe others here are interested in contacting them as well.

I have one box in Mid-Michigan that concerns me. It's technically a
mystery box, but if someone figures out where it is and just posts
the exact location to the box, it'll be in jeopardy, too. Plus, I'm
only a couple hours from you, so when they run out of boxes in your
area, it's conceivable that they'll head this way.

I found one of your boxes recently and the stamp was awesome. I plan
on coming back over there to find the rest sometime, so I'd hate to
see them be destroyed. I was pretty surprised at the number of
log-ins in the log book when I was there, but now that you mention
this, that explains a lot!
