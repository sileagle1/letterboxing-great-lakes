Hello everyone, and Happy New Year!!!! I'd like to share some
information I've received with the rest of you. I had seen in a
recent post that the Geauga Park District is embracing the hobbies
of Geo Caching and now Letterboxing. Here is a portion of
information provided in the description of the box Lions, Tigers and
Bears. "Geocaching, the high-tech sport of hide & seek, continues
in Geauga Park District with the introduction of letterboxing. These
hybrid caches will allow both letterboxers and geocachers to enjoy
the sport of searching for these hidden containers.

As Geauga County celebrates its bicentennial in 2006, the
letterbox/caches will have a historic theme and introduce you to the
history of some of our parks. The first of four letterbox/caches for
2006 is located in Beartown Lakes Reservation."

They are asking anyone who places a box to apply for a permit.
Since I had a box placed at Swine Creek, I called today to find out
how this is done. I was told a letter needs to be written to Geauga
Park District Atten: Thomas Curtain, Director 9160 Robinson Road
Chardon, Oh 44024. They said the letter should explain what the box
is, where it is to be located (both park and best description
possible of hiding spot). When this information is received they
will send a ranger out to determine if the traffice from finding the
box will have an adverse affect on the location. If the feel all is
well, they will isssure the permit. Their main objective for this
is to protect the integrity of the vegitation etc. They were very
nice when I called. If you would like to contact them for any more
information their number is 440-286-9516.

I'd like to give the Geauga Park District a thumbs up for embracing
both hobbies and encouraging it in their parks! They also told me
that on January 8th 2006 from 2:00 to 4:00 pm at Best Lake Lodge
they are hosting a Geo Caching event. Gander Mountain will be
providing GPS systems and it is open to anyone who would like to try
out the hobby.

So, thanks all for giving me a few minutes of your time. I figured
I'd pass this information along. I know I want to comply with their
wishes, so they will continue to welcome letterboxers to their
parks!

As always, happy hunting
