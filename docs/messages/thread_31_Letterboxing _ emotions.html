Letterboxing & emotions

From: bindlestiff_boxer (bindlestiff_boxer@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-03-26 02:27:46 UTC
Today I lost my first letterbox. First, in more ways than one ...
not only the first time a box has gone MIA for me, but this was also
the very first stamp I ever carved and the first box I ever planted.

The stamp wasn't anything special to look at, in fact it was really
quite crude. I had found the letterboxing site and gotten excited
enough to want to participate, but had not explored the carving
tutorials yet. I took an eraser and hacked it with a kitchen knife
into a rough aproximation of a pine tree and called it good. LOL

I have since gotten better at carving (IMHO) and have placed other
boxes. If anyone had asked me about my philosophy on it, my off-the-
cuff reply would've been something along the lines of those
sentimental things I've seen for sale in the tourist traps. I don't
remember exactly how it goes, but it's something like "If you have
something, set it free. If it comes back to you, keep it forever. If
it doesn't, it's no big deal." (Obviously I don't have one of
those cross-stitch throw pillows around to refer to ... LOL, but I
hope you get the drift of it.)

Ok .... so it's like my kids. I always had this belief that my "job"
as a parent was to work myself out of that job ... ie: to have them
become self-sufficent, productive members of society.

So how come I miss that ulgy little stamp so much?

I expect I'll loose some more before all is said and done .. that's
the nature of this activity and a risk I'm willing to take.

Now, on the "good" emotional side ... today while we were out
placing boxes an elderly gent who was walking his dog yelled out to
us, "Hey! What do you kids think you're doing?" I looked around to
see who he was yelling at and my daughter whispers, "He's talking to
us, Mom." LOL ... I'm in my mid 40's!!! I could've run over and
kiss the old guy for calling me a kid. (grin) Fortunately, we
practice "trash out" so my daughter held up our trash bag and
said, "Cleaning up the mess other people left." Smart girl, hey?

Anyone else have a story they're willing to share?

Enjoy!
---PJ

From: Deb (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-03-26 11:50:35 UTC
Yes, they become like your babies. Fortunately my first carved box
stamp is still in place. I keep close tabs on that box because
although it is not my first box (did 3 with purchased stamps first),
it was my first carving -- well, okay my second... the first one
was identical with backward letters.

Sorry to hear your box went missing... that is the frustrating part
of this hobby. But I find it inspires me to look for better hiding
places -- both in terms of where the box rests, and also the area
around it -- high traffic areas make it difficult for people to
search, stamp and replace without being seen. Also prompts me to
make my clues not quite so straightforward as I did in the
beginning, which I think deters any intentional vandalism, although
I don't even want to admit that happens. Still not foolproof, but
it does seem to help some.

SpringChick
