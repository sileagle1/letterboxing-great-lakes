Doodle & Deedle Bug wrote:
Concerning the 2006 Great Lakes Gathering, my personal
selfish request is to please have it as far away from
Cleveland as possible, because then I won't be upset
that it was reasonable for us to get to but d'oh,
we're getting married and can't make it :) (I know,
we should have taken the Great Lakes Gathering into
consideration when we set our wedding date.... ;)

As for disbanding the Great Lakes Gathering all
together - no! Please, please no! Even though, based
on the state that's hosting it, it won't be feasible
for everyone to always attend, it's part of life. We
were able to attend this past year's gathering and it
was amazing. We loved meeting all these people from
all over that we otherwise might not have, and all the
fun, hidden boxes, word of mouth boxes, all the neat
things people came up with to make the gathering
great. Now, we attended the Lake Eerie Gathering this
past October, too, and it was also amazing - all the
work that bibliophile & crew put into it had us in
awe. Comparing these two "types" of gatherhings, I
think we need both types. We need the smaller
state-based gatherings - it helps build ties between
local area boxers, and is also always a treat when
someone from out of state takes the time to attend the
gathering, too. Taking the Lake Eerie gathering as my
example, it was much smaller than the Great Lakes
Gathering and allowed for a more intimate setting.

Now, considering the big Great Lakes Gathering,
someone made the comment about letterboxing
conventions, and I guess in a way the Great Lakes
Gathering is already our group's unofficial
convention, where those who are able to travel to meet
on a larger scale to have a grand event. One of the
things I loved most about the 2005 Great Lakes
Gathering is that it was so big, and we saw first hand
that hey, letterboxing IS a big thing that we're
involved in and there are lots and lots of others as
crazy as we are. Akin to the Blind Melon music video
for "No Rain," where the BumbleBee Girl is searching
for others like her and finally finds them at the end
of the video.

OK, that's all for now.

Doodle (& Deedle) Bug.

Journey,

No, no, no, we're right here, right here! *waving
arms wildly from the back of the room* Our contact
information should have been listed in The Wandering
Rocker's logbook.... I only check AtlasQuest for
messages about once a month, since we get mail so
rarely through there.

We do NOT want this hitchhiker listed on AtlasQuest or
at letterboxing.org. We're tracking his travels
through our personal webpage at
http://www.geocities.com/doodle_n_deedle/hitchhikers.html
He's also listed on Pete & Wanda's Hitchhikers page
http://alum.wpi.edu/~p_miner/travelers.html

The letterbox that we released this hitchhiker in was
our contribution to the 2005 Great Lakes Gathering,
the Cleveland Rocks! box. This box is not listed
anywhere because as we explain on our website, we were
going to replant the box locally here in Cleveland
after the gathering, but it got lost in the mail (or
more likely, the punk who used to live upstairs swiped
the package when the mailperson left it in front of my
apartment door, which the mailpeople were told not to
do, grrrrr). (So if you found the box at the
gathering, that image is now a collector's item ;)

I'm not sure what you mean by the "Please let me know
if his owner abandoned him or if he would like him
returned to Green Bay" since we're from Cleveland,
Ohio, but The Wandering Rocker is a hitchhiker and
meant to be placed in another traditional letterbox
for another letterboxer to find. Reading your
AtlasQuest message just now, sounds like you've got
plans to send him on his way soon, so please do. With
Ohio blood in his veins and his guitar in tow I'm sure
he can withstand hanging out in Minnesota for a bit
until someone else picks him up. We're amazed that he
made it so far so quickly! If you could help us track
his travels, please send us a private email at
doodle_n_deedle @ yahoo.com and let us know where he's
been since The Christmas Elves found him at the
gathering.

Thanks!

Doodle (& Deedle) Bug.
