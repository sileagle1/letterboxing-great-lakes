From: (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2007-05-02 14:56:29 UTC-07:00
Well, I don't have my stamp carved yet, but Martini Man has his done. We will be placing them ourselves when we arrive.
As far as logbooks go, we have always logged everything in one book, chronologically. The books provide a history of where we've been and who we have met. For an event, we usually make sure to stamp the official Event stamp first (or save a page for it), then as we meet people and find boxes at the event, they are all stamped in on the following pages. We use whatever logbook we are currently using, not a separate one.
However speaking of logbooks at an event - be sure to bring a spare if you are nearing the end of one!
Wisconsin Hiker

I stamp on index cards. This lets me move them around to suit
different needs later. All the important information gets written on
the front, right next to the stamp.

Larger stamps? Well, depending on how large..I can either stamp the
larger portion, or I can lay index cards side by side until I have
enough space!

It also means I can stamp in at work, or at home, or in the car, or
wherever and not HAVE to find my logbook first. Since I live in the
Kingdom of Clutter, this helps significantly. :)

From: Pats (votremerci@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-05-03 12:20:58 UTC-07:00
I can't do index cards. It's laudable and I admire people that can, but really, I know myself and messy marvin tendencies. If I had PTs and event stamps all on index cards and I'm trying to carefully put them away, 20% will somehow get smudged and/or bent; 10% will somehow miraculously pick up water from an unknown source and run onto another 20%; 40% will blowoutofmyhands/bedropped and of that 40%, roughly 85% will be lost to enterprising squirrels; my giant feet as I'm trying to leap to the area to get the index cards before they get smudged, thus smudging them AND bending them in the process; mother nature and her wind *ahem*; 3 will find their way into unsuspecting lber's carry-alls and at least 2 will vanish into the unknown, quite possibly the land where one sock goes after making DryerGod offerings. Better to keep it all in a book for me.

I have my smaller logbook for traditional finds.

For event stamps, exchanges, anything event oriented - except traditional permanent plants, and other, I have a 9 x 12 spiral bound art sketchbook. That way, I can shelve the book with lb supplies when not needed and don't risk wetting/dropping/smudging the stamps of people I've met and parties attended when I drop, drop kick, spill water on while trying to clean a stamp before rehiding and other such calamities while boxing in the wild.

Postals get their own logbook too.

MoWizLiz

Re: Great Lakes Gathering - 1 Month Away!!!

From: vanman021657 (fholbein@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-05-03 19:51:11 UTC
I use the 3x5 index cards for all traditional plants and finds. I
also have 5x7 cards for those larger than life carvings. I have two
5x7 log books. One is hard cover for PT's and Specials only, and the
other is a soft cover for Events and misc. anything else. My 3x5 and
5x7 cards all go into a photo album. I always take a digital photo
and this is in the album next to the stamp. When I am on the trail I
also cary a GPS and record that too. I'm very anal about the entire
process.

I try to limit just two stamps per page in my log books and it must
always be on the facing page. All info goes on the face of the cards
and the pages of the log books right next to the stamp. The info
also includes the date, time, and location so that I know what order
they were received. I said I'm anal about it.

VanMan
