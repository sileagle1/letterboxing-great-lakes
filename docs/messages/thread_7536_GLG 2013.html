It's great to have a potential volunteer to host in Ohio! As far as I know,
SpringChick still does the official selection of the host, or organizes a
poll if there are multiple hosts/locations that volunteer. You may also
want to contact her directly.



My husband & I hosted the GLG in 2006 (and another event prior to that). I
generated some notes afterwards that I have passed along to several others
that have hosted in subsequent years. However it sounds like you already
have experience hosting an event, so are probably a "pro" already!



We've never been to Hocking Hills but have heard a lot about it over the
past several years. It sounds like a VERY nice park and we'd love to have
an excuse to get there. Perhaps you can get some assistants that live
nearer to plant boxes. However hosts can select any location that is
convenient for them. Things to keep in mind:

- Expect 100-150 attendees

- It is good to have cover/shelter for a large group in case it rains

- Good to have enough acreage & trails to spread boxes out a bit.

- Many people like to come for the whole weekend, so a campground at the
park or nearby is a plus (same for hotels)



A host can choose a theme themselves, or open it up to discussion and/or a
poll. When we hosted in 2006 I created a poll on this board (there is a
specific POLLS feature on this message board) and listed several possible
theme ideas. People had a few weeks to vote on their favorite theme and the
one that got the most votes is what we went with.



My husband & I prefer to be out on the trails to find boxes and then spend
our time at the shelter simply eating and socializing, so the only stamping
we do there is for exchanges. Tabletop boxes therefore aren't of much
interest to us.



# of boxes? Hmmmm.... enough to keep us busy on Saturday and possibly even
Sunday. Since we are hikers, we like it when an event uses all the trails
within a park and spreads out the boxes. We really appreciate events where
the boxes are kept out for at least the entire weekend or a week if they
can't be permanent boxes. With a large event it is also a very good idea to
group boxes into loops with different starting points. That way you reduce
the likelihood of having mobs of people converging at the same box.



Great Lakes Gatherings have always been potluck in terms of food and that
has worked out well. If grilling facilities are available you can suggest
that some people may want to bring charcoal as their contribution and others
hot dogs/sausages or other grill items. Meal and group photo could be
either mid-day or at the end of the day.



Glad to see someone in Ohio getting the ball rolling for next year!!

I mentioned it in the post I made on OCT 17 and here is an excerpt from the
FAQ file about the GLG event:



Q. Who hosts the event?

A. Each fall, the moderators of the LB Great Lakes Yahoo! Group will
solicit the list for volunteers from the eligible state(s) to host the event
for the following year. During the first six years of the event, an
eligible state is any state that has not previously hosted the event. After
all states have had a chance to host and a schedule is set, the next state
in the rotation is the eligible state. In the event there are no willing
hosts from an eligible state, it will move to the next state in the
rotation. If more than one person/state volunteers, a poll will be used to
allow group members to choose their preference of location.

SpringChick is the moderator/owner of this board and she has always made the
official announcement/confirmation as to who hosts the event. You may want
to look at the Gatherings folder in the Files section of this board to
review the file with more info about the annual GLG. This link might get
you there directly:
