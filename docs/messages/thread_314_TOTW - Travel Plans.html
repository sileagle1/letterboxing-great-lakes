Re: TOTW - Travel Plans

From: lesann847 (l-fischer@northwestern.edu) | Date: 2004-06-21 12:30:05 UTC
Indiana was sadly under-represented at the June gathering, but
there are some great boxes there and there were NO
MOSQUITOES. We just got back from Indiana where we were
not able to visit every box we wanted to, but we did go to
Lafayette and Turkey Run.

Cairo Skywatch is a box that evokes history; we loved it. Morel
Mushroom combines a little internet research before you leave
and a pleasant little walk in the woods. (We had to do some
maintenance on Morel Mushroom which was soaked).

If you've loved the canyons of Starved Rock in Illinois, you'll love
Turkey Run State Park in Marshall IN which plays host to four
letterboxes, all hidden in interesting places. Turkey Run is an
excellent place for a weekend trip. If you camp, we like the
camping in Shades SP nearby. Reservations in the Turkey Run
Lodge may be difficult to come by, book early.

Acorn

From: grayfamilyof5 (grayfamily@foxvalley.net) | Date: 2004-06-21 13:38:14 UTC
My family and I just returned from a trip to Colorado and the
beautiful Rocky Mountain National Park. We found 3 boxes; one in
Estes Park, one near Grand Lake, and the last one near Granby. All
were fun to find. The last required a 3-mile hike around Monarch
Lake to find both boxes. The first two were very near the parking
areas. On the way out there we tried to find a box hidden in an
Iowa rest area. It was dark and we didn't really want to go
reaching under a small bridge when we couldn't see, so we didn't
find that one. We were also going to stop in Lincoln, NE on the way
back as there is a park there with numerous boxes based on the
book, "Sarah, Plain and Tall". Everyone was asleep when we passed
through, so we will save that one for another time. My children
think letterboxing is fantastic and I use it as a great reward for
hiking. Incidently, my wife is a 1st grade teacher and I used to
teach 3rd grade.

From: Doodle & Deedle Bug (doodle_n_deedle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-06-21 06:44:54 UTC-07:00
He he he, funny you should ask that! Jeff & I will be heading to, of all places, Michigan in August! We did NOT plan this trip around letterboxing but rather my friend Shawn's wedding in Adrian, where there happens to be a letterbox hidden. And then Jeff said while we're in Michigan, if it's not too much farther he'd like to go to the Henry Ford Museum & Greenfield Village in Dearborn...and I found clues for another box hidden in Dearborn. Plus a few other boxes in Wayne county.... and I figured that while heading out to Michigan we'll pass a few boxes on the way in Ohio, and hey, if we're already in the Adrian area I'm sure the surrounding counties aren't too far away and, oh look, I've found some clues to letterboxes in those counties, too... and next thing you know I've got a list of about 42 letterboxes to hunt while in & on our way to Michigan ;)

Needless to say, we're probably not going to have a chance to hunt all 42 boxes since we're only going to be there from August 13th to the 17th, and we kind of have that wedding to attend...but for those in the Southern-Eastern Michigan area who knows, maybe we'll run into some of you on the trails. And Jeff, being such a good sport, said to go ahead and bring all 42 sets of clues along and we can see which ones might be in our reach once we're actually in the state.

Oh, and of course I told Shawn about our obsession--I mean, hobby, and he kindly reported that there will be a two hour gap between the wedding & the reception that we could use to find the Adrian letterbox ;)

Not that we need to add to our list any more, but if you have info on boxes in the area (esp. if you know of any currently out of commission), our email is doodle_n_deedle @ yahoo.com :)

Doodle aka Diane.

We were at Mackinac Island a few weeks ago... Sadly, we weren't able
to find DogIsMyCoPirate's "Let Our Oceans Live" box at the Shepler
Dock. And Radio's "Robinson's Folly" box was pretty clearly missing,
as we found the exact spot he described, but no box. The other 2
boxes are doing fine. "Anne's Tablet" is a fantastic spot, and "Arch
Rock" is great too. The views from East Bluff Road are beautiful,
overlooking the harbor, Main Street, and the bridge.

We hope to place a new box along northbound I-75 in Cheboygan County -
- probably in a couple of weeks -- so that might be another one for
you to look forward to!

What is the 2nd St. Ignace box? (Or is it a secret?) The only one
we've found is Mighty Mac at Straits State Park. And there's also
Mackinac Mush, from the 1st GLG's "Visit Michigan" series, which has
its permanent home in Mackinac City.
