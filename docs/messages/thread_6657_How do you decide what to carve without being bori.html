How do you decide what to carve without being boring?

From: cpjenks2001 (cpjenks2001@yahoo.com) | Date: 2010-06-18 14:22:03 UTC
Most of my carved stamps have been running/race related because I plant them while we are out waiting for dh to finish a race. My most recent series was birthday themed because Louie wanted to teach her friends how to LB so I planted them for her birthday and that was our "activity" for the party. I'm working on a series that I will plant in the park near where I grew up. Those stamps are all of activities that we used to do in the park.

Most of the finds that we've found have something to do with the place it's located (like if it's a historical or tourist spot), or maybe it's a series with several boxes to find.

But what about the general single box that I want to plant here or there simply because the park/woods are beautiful and have lots of good spots available? Nothing historical or touristy about that park. Just a nice hike and beautiful woods. Do woodland animal stamps get boring? How else to come up with ideas for carvings?

~pjynx

From: Sara Byron (sarabyron@att.net) | Date: 2010-06-18 11:46:15 UTC-05:00
I found that I had difficulty thinking of ideas for carves. Some boxes seem
so uniquely suited to their environment, and some hikes are very creative.
When I stumbled upon postal letterboxes, I found a huge wealth of carving
ideas. Just browsing through the postal trackers set up in Atlas Quest
should keep you in ideas for years. Here is a post that someone re-posted
on AtlasQuest. It is a list of ideas for postal trackers - a series of
postal letterboxes - but each of these can be done as planted singles. Hope
it generates an idea or two.

And don't worry about being boring. We are an appreciative group!

JeffersClan
