We're planning a trip with our RV in October (weather permitting)
to do the circle tour around the lake. We're hoping to have about 12
days and end up at the gathering in MI on the 22nd.

We have 2 small children (boy 5, girl 2). Does anyone have any
HAVE TO boxes that fit into the area?? Any suggestions of places to
visit or things to see?

I'm new to the board but have to say I'm loving all the
interesting things you guys are talking about and can't wait to get
back into Wisconsin and box some more there.

We started boxing in May of 2004 after I read an article in Family
Fun magazine a few months before that. Our first attempt was the
Eway Trail box in Madison, WI. We found an empty container and
contents scattered no papers or stamps. Didn't really know what we
were looking for picked up what we could put it all back where we
thought it would go but we were hooked.

Went to Red Arrow Park in Marinette, WI. Found that one. We went
immediately to Walmart bought a stamp and cardstock and went back.
We've been hooked ever since. Our vacations now revolve around
boxing. When we can take them LOL.

On the day of our first year anniversary we found our 50th box.
LOL There aren't too many boxes in NW Indiana but we have some plans
to change that in the next few months. LOL

Cindi - DUCA family

Here are a few quick thoughts:

1. "Sweet Praireland" box in Pleasant Prairie is near your route, an easy
box for kids and you'll go right by a candy distribution center that offers
free tours almost every day of the year. They don't make the candy there,
but you ride around the warehouse watching videos about how the candy is
made. There are also seasonal displays and free samples at the end. A
fairly quick tour, no walking involved. Check out the great deal on "Belly
Flops" in the retail store before or after the tour.

2. "Sailing the Great Lakes" box in Kenosha is also easy for kids and right
on the lakefront, near a small interesting museum with free admission.

3. If you stop in downtown Milwaukee, the Betty Brinn Children's Museum
(admission fee required) is great for kids that age. Easier boxes nearby
are "Happy Days" and "Harbor Island".

4. In Sheboygan, "Danger on the Great Lakes" would be good with kids.

First of all, welcome to the group!

Your planned trip sounds like a lot of fun and should beautiful at that time of year. Funny you should mention the Circle Tour... Not sure if you are aware or not of the list being compiled of letterboxes that are along the Lake Michigan Circle Tour.

Check out this project, including a list of boxes that are currently listed, here:

http://www.stampinthebox.com/projects/lmct.asp

It is a relatively new project, just getting off the ground and response has been quite limited -- not sure if there just aren't as many boxes along the route as I thought there were or if people just aren't interested in seeing a composite listing like this, but it is a start.

In Lower MI there are quite a few boxes along your route, primarily concentrated between South Haven and Montague, but scattered boxes at other points south and north of here. They vary in difficulty and length of time required and the best thing is just to read through the clues to get an idea of what meets your needs.

Sounds like a great trip! Have fun!

SpringChick
