Well, i've already posted my "get-up"...a tie-dyed
shirt in yellow, purple, and green (the Mardi-Gras
shirt, as I call it), olive green cargo pants that
roll up, a tan visor, and hiking shoes, sandals.
Sometimes I vary the shirt and will wear a red ringer
shirt with UTAH on it.

I am surprised with the number of backpacks...I tried
that, but decided that the bigger the area, the more
crap i'll try to stuff into it. I carry an old
messenger purse in olive green I got off the clearance
rack at Old Navy. It's already got the obligatory ink
stains from my markers and is ready for some patches.
It's big enough to carry the essentials (log, markers,
stamp, ink pads, compass, kleenex, bug spray, extra
ziplocs, pen, cell phone, beach glass from Lake
Michigan) and small enough to prevent me from carrying
everything but the kitchen sink, which I would do if I
had the space.

Also in the news, the International City letterbox I
just planted is out of commission for the time being.
The Lorain County Metro Parks is requiring permits for
letterboxes now, so they pulled all letterboxes
without permits. French Creek has definately been
pulled and probably One Hundred Acres Wood series as
well. I'm in contact with Polly, placer of French
Creek, and hopefully both boxes will be back shortly.
