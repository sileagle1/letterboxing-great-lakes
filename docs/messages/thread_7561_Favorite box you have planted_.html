Yeah, I also can't pick just ONE favorite! Here are a few:

"Who Is It?" because it is a mystery box that ties together the clue, location & image and I like how the carving turned out.

"All Fool's Day" because I used the image to play a joke on some co-workers (stamped the image on a post-it note and covered the bottom of a computer mouse, so the mouse couldn't "see". When they finaly picked up their mouse to figure out what was wrong, they saw a little fool. They all cracked up!) and because I found an interesting container to use.

"Wisconsin Zombie Alert!" because it reminds of us of the great gathering we attended out east, the images are funny and I like the amusing clues Martini Man wrote for our boxes.

"Horse Sense" and "Eggopolis - John & Yolko" because I was able to do a good job on some tiny lettering and I liked the humor of the images.

"Sawtooth Lake" because it was a GORGEOUS mountain hike and I created an image that fit the location.

My favorite was my Nomadic Gnome series. It was a gnome similar to the Travelocity guy. He had 4 different homes around the area featuring different aspects for different seasons. (A water park, a cider mill, a great place for spring flower viewing, a place with a toboggan run and skiing trails). I carved a smaller version of it and made it a hitchhiker. So there is still a nomadic gnome around somewhere presumably.

From: Atom118 (Aatom118@gmail.com) | Date: 2012-11-19 11:20:40 UTC-06:00
I think my favorite boxes stem more from the hike than any particular
clue or artwork (which is sad considering all the great clues and carves
we've found). And, sadly, many of the favorite hikes come from some
'misunderstanding' or horrendous miscalculation or reading of the
clues...but then finally managing to find the box anyways.

For example, a box we found last year by 'Guess Who' that involved
fording a stream with our stroller, getting lost as our compass started
to point S vs N, and then realizing the disconnect between how the trail
looked on that particular day vs when the clues were written. We only
walked the trail probably 4 times...and while it ended up being a crazy
hike, we found the box (FF to boot!) and managed to find our way home.
A great day on the trail!

Atom118

Oh, and to actually respond to the question of this tread (misread the
subject! oops).

I think our favorite boxes that we've planted (if I had to pick one)
would be our 'Hunt for the Hidden Hollow'. Here we planted three
individual boxes that where double sided. On the back where pieces of a
map. When pieced together, it was a treasure map to find a Bonus Box
for the series. Sadly, the final box ended up being in an area that
flooded more than we expected, and we had to remove the boxes as the
clues became almost impossible to follow.

I also like our 'City Views' and 'Powers of Observation' as they take
you around the city, and I'm rather proud of those clues.

From: klk-fw (krank@ameritech.net) | Date: 2012-11-21 03:28:57 UTC
It was my Dad's first find too...which leads me to my favorite plant.

(I actually had to look through my AQ logbook...I'd forgotten many of my plants over the years.)

"Little Falls Native Son" I think wins the title for me. It has a lot of personal history for me. My first carve of a "real live dead person" (and his signature, both of which came out quite well, if I do say so myself). It was sent to the GLG my Dad and I wanted to go to in Minnesota (but alas he did not live to see). The title comes from the inscription on a refrigerator magnet I found amongst his things, which he bought himself on a trip to Minnesota. The fact that Lindbergh landed on an airstrip which is now a road I drive every day leaving work was always really cool to me. Last, I think it's just a really cool hiding place (which I think more of you need to experience <*wink*>).

Oh, wait...there's one other thing about this box that makes it special...some of you may remember when I posted a few years ago about little 7 year old Devon who sent me a message via LbNA saying he found my letterbox and took it home, asking me what he should do with it. I emailed and then called his mom, assured her it was nothing dangerous, thanked her for the very mature thing her son did by contacting me, and asked her to return it to the hiding place, which she did.

Whew...that's a lot of story for one box!

--klk/fw
