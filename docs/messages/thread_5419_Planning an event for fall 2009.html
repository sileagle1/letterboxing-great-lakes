Planning an event for fall 2009

From: mkeaims (aimlesst@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2008-11-22 20:20:02 UTC
Hi all,

I am in the advance planning stages for an event next fall. I am
planning on Saturday October 3rd at Minooka Park in Waukesha. I'll
be able to reserve the shelter on January 2nd, and will post firm
information for the date as soon as I have the reservation in hand.

I figured I would let people know in advance if they are planning on
doing carving over the winter and are looking for inspiration. The
theme is science fiction. Science fiction fandom holds conventions
just about every weekend of the year. The term is shortened to be a
con and that usually gets added into the name of the convention.
Windycon is in Chicago, Oddcon in Madison, Minnicon in Minneapolis.
This will be Boxacon.

I would like to stick to straight science fiction and save the
fantasy for another con. So if you are thinking of ideas, I'll throw
some out. Just off the top of my head from the media side of life
there is Firefly, Star Trek, Star Wars, Babylon 5, Battlestar
Galactica, Red Dwarf, Firefly (I really like Firefly!). Book lists
are longer, some of the big names again off the top of my head, the
big 3 of Heinlein, Asimov, Clarke (2001 and Hal anyone?). Cherryh has
some interesting aliens. Dave Weber has a lot of great insignia and
items from various ships and worlds that would make great stamps. I
already have a stamp from Bujold in the parade of books series, but
there is alot more there. Larry Niven, Anne McCaffrey, Clifford
Simak (a Wisconsin native!), I could keep the list going on and on.
My general differation is that if the book has magic, elves,
vampires, you are looking at fantasy. If it is, or was at the time
of its writing, possible in the world as we know it, it is science
fiction.

I'm going to try playing with the free website places that were
mentioned here a while back and will let everyone know when I have
something up and running.
