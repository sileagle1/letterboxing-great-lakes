Holy Grail Series update

From: duncan53211 (duncan53211@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-03-18 21:32:59 UTC
Many of you will be happy to know that I'm spending my winter break
working on the webpage for this series (since the snow hindered my
plans to work on boxes outdoors). I'll be uploading and testing the
new webpage soon. If you are going to check it out, be advised that
it will take a while to load especially if you have dial-up, but I
hope you feel the wait is worth it! It probably also works best on IE.

In the meantime, I have not recieved the clues to many of the boxes,
so I'm requesting that if you have clues to send me, please send them
soon. I know that a number of the missing clues are my fault - either
boxes I need to hide, or boxes I've just sent out to their placers.
(BTW, Papeseed - PLEASE contact me!!!) Although I would like the clues
ASAP, I would rather the boxes be well hidden and clues be
well-written, rather than be rushed. In general, it would be nice to
have this series all set by May 1 for the summer letterboxing season.

As far as I know, all boxes have been sent to their placers (except
for the one I need to send to Papeseed). In case you're wondering,
here's a list of the clues I have and don't have:

Ok, the site is up and (hopefully) ready for your amusement. If you
find any bugs, let me know. (Any web designers out there who want
to give me, a fledgling, advice, feel free.) I've only tested it on
IE, so I don't know how it'll work on other browsers. Any links you
click on will open a new window, and I've noticed that IE
occasionally prevents those "pop ups". Just try the link again
until it pops up. It worked for me.

If you want all the "bells and whistles" and to have lots of HG
amusement, go to the regular page at:

http://geocities.com/the_lb_dragon/Search_for_the_Holy_Grail.htm

If you have dial-up and don't want to wait a while, here is the
alternate site w/o the neat features:

http://geocities.com/the_lb_dragon/Search_for_the_Holy_Grail2.htm

For now, any links in green are boxes that have clues and are ready
to be found. Any in red are not ready. When all the clues are
ready, all of the links will turn yellow like the rest of the page
text. At that time, I'll also set up the links on the LBNA site and
let the box creators know how to do the same for their boxes. Until
all the boxes are in place, I'd prefer to keep site visitors within
our own GL group. (This will help me monitor my bandwidth allotment
anyway.)

Enjoy, and keep getting those clues in to me!

Dragon

Re: Holy Grail Series update

From: lesann847 (Acorn@fools-errand.org) | Date: 2006-03-22 23:03:08 UTC
At the behest of the Fischer Queen, this humble Fool journeyed through Lincoln's
realm on a quest for the Holy Grail.

A great spotted hound threatened me on the path to the "Grail Beacon," yet all the
time he was baying at me with slavering jaws, he wagged his tail. I proceeded on to
victory.

Continuing my journey, I was nearly undone by sucking mud that threatened to
swallow me and devious clues that nearly confounded me. However, I found a knight
lying prone and grievously wounded; I offered assistance but he assured me, "It's just
a flesh wound."

I met "Sir Bedevere"who had already received the visits of other worthy knightsin
the woods where he appeared to be hiding from an infestation of muggles.

I wandered on and heard troubadours singing of Sir Robin who "When danger reared
its ugly head, he bravely turned and fled." Unlike Robin, I persevered, crossing a
treacherous path requiring a finely honed sense of balance, and found another
treasure.

I sought the "Catapulted Cow" and traveled on through the woods, but my knees
shook as I found my way toward an awful roaring. At the top of a hilljust where I
believed my path should bea huge yellow beast, spouting reeking black smoke and
tearing up the earth, had eaten my road. This monster was so unnerving that I have
retired to a hermitage where I will pray for guidance before I battle the beast.

Acorn
