From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-07-26 20:31:20 UTC-04:00
Not sure how "mini" you are looking for, but here are a few logbook ideas...

- spiral bound index cards -- flip it over and use the plain side
- Wal-Mart has been carrying little mini-scrapbook kits -- a plain covered
3.75" x 3.75" book that comes with stickers -- for $1. I throw out the
stickers and use decorative paper on the cover instead. These are in the
scrapbooking area.
- I found some really nice little books recently at Meijer (in MI and
northern IN, not sure where else) -- they are 4" x 3.5" spiral bound
notebooks with plain pages and the cover is a nice heavy printed acetate
material -- some striped, some polka dots, etc. They even have a neat
little elastic band attached to the back cover with eyelets to snap around
the front to keep closed -- only $1! I bought all they had at my Meijer
here in Muskegon and they have not restocked. These were on an endcap in
the greeting card area.
- Use adding machine tape -- cut sheets and staple down centerfold or on
one end or just roll it up.
- Make one -- just use a simple pamplet stitch to bind together the desired
number of pages. People love finding handmade books and the nice thing is
that you can make them any size you want... not as hard as you might
think... here are some links:

Just the basic pamphlet stitch book >>
http://gort.ucsd.edu/preseduc/bookmkg.htm
One with a nice button closure >> http://tinyurl.com/g7x5p
One with a neat slotted slipcover >>
http://www.enchantedink.com/projects/class18.htm

Even if you prefer not to make your own books... keep your eyes open and
stock up when you see things that will work even if you aren't in need of
something at that particular moment. Before you know it, you will have a
nice little stash to choose from when you plant a box. Now is a good time
to be on the lookout because stores are gearing up for back-to-school and
have all kinds of neat little books (the trick is finding the ones without
lines).

SpringChick
(who just keeps on collecting cute little logbooks even though I make almost
all my books and never use any of the ones I buy)

One easy & relatively simple logbook is spiral bound index cards. The paper
is heavy so ink doesn't bleed through. They also have the advantage of
being relatively inexpensive. You can make some type of creative cover
design and glue or otherwise apply it to the BACK of the index card pack.
Generally you would want to use the back so that people will stamp on the
unlined side of the cards.

Another approach is to get some heavy weight paper and cut it to the size
you want. You can then punch holes in one or two corners and use a simple
key chain loop to keep them together (or use some other type of fastener
through the pages). If you want to get REALLY creative, you can make a
homemade logbook using several methods. There are some other boxers on this
board who have created some amazing logbooks and could give you more info on
this approach.

One reminder: Don't forget to stamp the first page of the logbook with the
stamp you carve and also stamp your own signature stamp in the book. I've
seen several newer boxers forget to do this in their excitement over placing
their first boxes. It's not "required" to do this, but is more of a
convention. We like seeing an image of the stamp before inking up so we can
decide what color(s) we might like to use. Sometimes it is easier to do
this looking at an image rather than the stamp itself. We also like to see
the stamp of the people who planted the box, especially if it is someone
new.

Good luck!

From: Gooble Gobble (gooble_gobble_3@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-07-27 00:02:42 UTC-07:00
I like to use lined note cards. You can buy 250 of
them at the dollar store for... guess how much? I then
find cool artsy paper to make a front and back cover
for and use spray glue to attach them. True it's a
little more work than just buying a logbook, but in
the long run you're left with a very personal and cool
logbook. I like finding homeade logbooks myself,
they're kind of rare in the wild. Also target sells
packs of cardstock for $5. and you can cut any size
you want (hint, invest in a paper cutter and save
yourself some frustrations). For holding the books
together I like to use metal rings. The kind with a
hinge and a simple snap on them. Buy a hole punch, and
go to town. The rings you can find at an office supply
store for relatively cheap (about $1.50 for 5 1"
rings). Total cost per logbook= right around a dollar
for a logbook you'll never see in another box. Your
clues for your boxes you hide generally tend to be a
little harder to figure out when you make your own
logbooks. With the extra time you put into making the
logs you don't want them accidentally found. Everyone
(at least I think everyone likes a little challenge to
finding a box) will enjoy looking for your boxes
wondering what the next logbook will look like or what
crazy adventure you'll send them on next.


Gooble Gobble


P.S. Also, it would be nice to include some larger
pieces of paper in your boxes with mini logbooks. That
way those of us with large signature stamps can leave
our entire image for you. I just decided to do this
myself. I can't tell you how many times I can leave
only a small part of my stamp in a log due to the fact
that my stamp is on the large side ( 3.25" x 2" ).

My hole punch isn't strong enough (or maybe *I'm* not strong enough) to go
thru more than a couple of pieces of cardstock at a time, so I just use a
drill.

I cut the cardstock with a papercutter to the size I want, I put a stack of
cardstock between two small pieces of plywood, send the drillbit thru it,
and away we go.
