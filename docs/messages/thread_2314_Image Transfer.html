Image Transfer

From: Dick and Anne (rhesse34@hotmail.com) | Date: 2006-03-22 13:53:10 UTC
I finally got around to re-carving a missing stamp yesterday (soon,
Dragon... soon) and tried out a "new" (combination) image transfer
technique. I've never had very good results with either the acetone
(nail polish remover) or the iron-on method. The image was either
smudged or indistinct and the details were lost. So I tried combining
the two. No, there's no danger of an explosion.
First, I set the iron to the low end of the steam setting and plugged
it in to warm up. Then the acetone. I use PVC pipe cleaner available
from the hardware store in 4 oz. cans. There's no perfume or oils
added to it.
I used a Q-Tip to wet the surface of "The Pink Stuff" with acetone
over the area where I wanted the image. It evaporates quickly and as
soon as the surface looked dry I placed the laser printer image on the
wetted surface, placed a sheet of paper over that and then the hot iron
on top for maybe 30 seconds. When I pealed off the printer image there
was a perfect copy on "The Pink Stuff".
Merlin
