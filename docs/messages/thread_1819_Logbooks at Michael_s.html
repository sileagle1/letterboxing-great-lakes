From: azobox (jparkerg@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-09-14 13:51:24 UTC
We have found the Michaels' selection to be quite store-specific.
There is a well known brand of artists' supplies that our local
Michaels does not ever stock, yet the Michaels in Battle Creek --
barely 15 miles away -- carries the whole line.
When I inquired of corporate Michaels about these local differences I
was greeted with huge silence.

So I'd say grab those bargains when you see them.

From: Doglvrs RASW (doglvrs_4@msn.com) | Date: 2005-09-14 14:46:46 UTC
I guess I'd better speak up here...I ran out to Michael's right away, and
after thoughraly perusing all $1 book type things, I found the "logbooks" of
which J & B were speaking. They are not exactly logbooks, as you might
think....They are actually like notepads....with a nice cover, and a pen.
They are not spiral bound, or really "bound" at all in the way we think of
them....
However, a small drill bit and a little ribbon did the trick.

Had to chime in here. They worked perfectly for the billion Holy Grail
boxes I needed to mail out!

A tip for Lansing-area letterboxers: Meijer on Lake Lansing Rd. (and probably other Meijers,
but that's the only one I can confirm) has square blank journals, approximately 4" square
(that's a rough estimate as I don't have one nearby). They are in the "stationery" section of
the store with other small notebooks and journals. Interestingly enough, the shelf tag for
them says "box notes." :)
