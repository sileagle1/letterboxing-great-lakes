So far I've usually found/created an image to match a location I'd like to
hide a box in. However I have to admit that I constantly see potential
stamp designs jumping out at me from passing trucks, buildings, packaging
materials, etc. and am often exclaiming - THAT would be a great stamp
design! I maintain a list of locations I'd like to place boxes and ideas for
stamp images to match. This list seems to grow faster than my actual stamp
production...

I've loved the stamps that we have found that seem to be based on photos,
but I don't think my carving abilities are that good yet to try something
similar myself.

From: Doodle & Deedle Bug (doodle_n_deedle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-09-28 06:26:07 UTC-07:00
I carved the stamp for our first-placed box, Kumbaya. The inspiration was originally from a spat on the Big List, but I also have a personal story about the song, and decided to place the stamp in the park related to that story (and if you want to know the story, just look for the Kumbaya clue page at letterboxing.org or our personal website).

We have one other stamp carved, and it's kind of along the same lines...it's being placed in the park that inspired it, also. As soon as we have a chance to get out that way again, since the park's about 45 minutes from where we live. That seems to be the theme...we've been coming up with ideas for stamps, but the ideas are also directly linked to the places we want to hide them.

Deedle's come up with some hitchhiker ideas just from his general likes (Elvis, monsters, etc.). He just has to get carving!

Doodle.
