From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-09-06 05:35:08 UTC-04:00
I've noticed that also when reviewing clues for pending trips (we are headed to RI/CT/MA this fall). Although you can find some really great boxes anywhere you go across the country -- this is particularly true in the Pacific NW, there are also a lot of what I call "gimme" boxes -- where, as you say, they all but hand you the box. When I first started boxing I was amazed at reports from other areas where people were finding 15-20 boxes in one day. I chalked it up to greater box density in places like CT. But I think it is more than that -- it is also about the quality of the clues.

It's hard to generalize, but I do think that in the Great Lakes area there are a lot of real quality boxes with clues that make you think and hike, perhaps more so than other areas of the country. I'm really proud of that about our area.

SpringChick
