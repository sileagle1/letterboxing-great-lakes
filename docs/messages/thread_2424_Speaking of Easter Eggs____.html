Kimono Girl,

We were hoping someone else would answer this because we didn't time
the walk. We are merely guessing and may be off because we know
exactly where the eggs are, we placed them. We don't have to take
time reading clues, hunting around, stamping, replacing, etc. etc.

We are guesstimating that it might take about 45 minutes to do our
eggs. The first 7 are concentrated in one area and its easy to
collect all 7 and then stamp in and replace them when you're done.
There's no logbook in the first 7 boxes, the logbook is only in the
last box. The second 7 eggs are a little more spread out than the
first ones, but they still be able to be collected and then stamped in
and then replaced.

Our 45 minute time is a long estimate, but again we're merely
guessing. We hope that you have time to do them.

Is there anyone who's done the eggs that did keep track of time?

From: tiptoetonto (tiptoetonto@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-04-21 22:37:57 UTC
These can easily be done in less than 30 min... if there aren't a lot
of people milling about. We did the series under 30 minutes, but no
one was around. It wasn't exactly raining, but close to it with
drizzle, so I sent Tonto off to find them, and he dutifully would
bring them to me sitting in the car to stamp in. This only worked for
the first general location! I actually had to get out with him to do
the remaining! Can you imagine? (It had been a long, hard day of
boxin' by that point!) He did a lot more scurrying than I did!
