From: Kathi Krankoski (krank@ameritech.net) | Date: 2007-04-22 18:42:27 UTC-07:00
Well, I only had time to nab two boxes on my way to GS camp this morning ("Joliet Junior College" and "Got Dog?"), but I was able to do spring maintenance on my US 52 - IL 71 boxes. "Whispering Oaks", "MPHG: I'm not dead yet", "Duane's Motel", "FOOW2" and "FOOW6" (yes, contrary to reports, #2 is alive and kicking...and I even carved a replacement and had it with me ready to go in case I needed it), and "Lutefisk out of Water" are all ready for visitors. CMoon's "Rev. Elling Eilsen" is also in good shape.

And maybe a couple HH's were released...or maybe not. I guess you'll just have to go find out for yourself.

From: Carol Osada (cjosada@hotmail.com) | Date: 2007-04-22 22:10:23 UTC-07:00
Fungus Woman was at GS camp, and so was Beetle...but different camps. Great weather! I taught a letterboxing course and set up several temporary traditional boxes. HH, Bonus Boxes, and Cooties were also present. There are now several new enthusiastic letterboxers among us. I taught compass usage because the clues contained compass bearings, and some cryptic information. They did a great job replacing the boxes. I guess they listened well to 'the rules.' I discourage this activity with younger GS; older girls only. Also, I stress being sneaky and breaking up a large group which can draw too much attention. Hopefully this advice will help protect boxes. If the members of LBGLK see a new series of boxes called LEO 2007; ignore them. These were 4 temporary boxes placed for the past three days as part of the workshop. Participants will be signing up on AQ and listing their finds. I will retire these boxes in a couple weeks. Thanks for understanding.

Beetle
