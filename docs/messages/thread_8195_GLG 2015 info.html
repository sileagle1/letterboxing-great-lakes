Wow, it’s June… that means the gathering is just around the corner.

 

Getting there:

Castle Rock State Park is about 4 miles south of Oregon, IL off Hwy 2.  If traveling west from the Chicago area, take I-90 to Rockford. At Rockford, take I-39 south 20 miles to IL Hwy 64. At Exit 104, turn right at Oregon exit, take Hwy 64 west to Oregon. Turn south on IL Hwy 2 for 4 miles to park.  If traveling west on I-88, take Exit 97 at I-39 at Rochelle. Take I-39 north 7 miles to Hwy 64. Go west on Hwy 64 for 15 miles to Oregon. Take IL Hwy 2 south to the park. If traveling north on IL Hwy 26 south of Dixon, take Hwy 26 to Dixon. In Dixon, take IL Hwy 2 north 12 miles to the park. If traveling east on I-88 west of Dixon, take the Dixon exit for IL Hwy 26 north. In Dixon, take IL Hwy 2 north 12 miles to the park.

 

Your GPS might try to steer you down a side-road just as you are nearing the park.  Don’t pay it any attention.  There is a large “CASTLE ROCK STATE PARK” sign across from the main entrance.  The gathering will be near the north trails, following the signs to the picnic areas.  The main stuff will happen in the ‘OLD ORCHARD’ shelter.  Just follow the signs to the right.  There is a paved parking area and grass overflow parking area.   We have also reserved the ‘VALLEY VIEW’ shelter that is at the top of the hill near the trail heads.  The valley view shelter will house some snacks and beverages for you to eat after coming off the nearby trails until about 430pm.  The kids’ series of boxes will also be planted around that shelter.  Note: There is a shelter between our two that is ‘walkup only’ and not ‘ours.’

 

The pre-event (and a handful of boxes) will be near Lowden State Park.  Lowden State Park is ~1.5miles north of Castle Rock.  From Castle Rock, take Hwy 2, north (left) 5 miles.  Turn right on Washington/Hwy 64 (downtown Oregon).  Cross the river, and then turn left on River Rd (by the BP gas station).  Go 1.5 miles to the entrance to the park on the left, where the electric camping sites are located.  The White Pines, non-electric, campground (where there is a series of boxes) will be on the right.  The pre-event on Friday evening is at Oregon Park East at the Inland shelter.  Instead of turning at the BP, go another block on Washington and turn left on Jones Terrace, just past the DQ.  Go about 1 block to 200N.

 

Amenities:

Illinois state parks do not charge any admission fees.  As such, you get what you pay for.  Adjacent to each of the shelters near the north trails area of the park are rustic bathrooms that are a slight step up from porta-potties.  No running water.  Also note, there are NO bathrooms located at the south trails (1.5 miles south on Hwy 2).  There is a water pump near each shelter, and the Old Orchard shelter has a water fountain.

 

Castle Rock has no concessions.  However, Oregon is 4 miles up the road, and it does have a few convenience stores, a grocery store, McDonalds, and a Dairy Queen among other things.  There is a newer concession hut at Lowden that seemed to have firewood and snacks.

 

What to see:

Make sure you go 0.5 miles past the Castle Rock entrance, picnic areas, and north trails to see the scenic overlook on the east side of the road.   Take the stairs all the way up the outcropping for a nice view of the Rock River.  At Lowden, there is a large statue of Chief Black Hawk.  Unfortunately, it is currently undergoing renovations, and is draped with scaffolding. However, the view there is also very nice.   Fishing along the Rock River also seems to be very popular based on all the people we’ve seen with rods in the water.


Cell phones:  They may not work in the park or the area.  Signal seemed to be very spotty depending on carrier and specific location.

 

Tentative Schedule (Check Event Page for Updates):
*9am-6pm. Check-in. See friends. Go Hiking. Have Fun.
Old Orchard Shelter (bottom of hill): Event stamp, tabletop stamps, food, and drinks. Grill will be going between ~Noon-2pm for lunch.

Prairie View Shelter (top of hill): More tabletop stamps, snacks, and water. This shelter is next to several trail heads. Until 430pm.
*5:30pm. Event group picture at the Old Orchard Shelter.
*5:30-6pm. Clean-up. Say goodbyes.

Boxing:

We will be setup at the Old Orchard shelter and have clue packets available starting around 9am on Saturday.  The boxes are all temporary, and pick-up times will be posted in the clue packet.  There are about 8 miles of trails in the park (depending on which signage is correct), and we’ve planted on a 1 mile loop at Lowden near the non-electric campsites.  The trails are mainly wide, hilly paths of hard dirt or grass – doable with a hiking stroller.  Most of the trails are shaded by woods that open onto well-spaced clearings.  We’ve found that even after rain, they are still walkable with only a few muddy sections.  Beware, the park hosts all the dangers commonly found in the woods such as poison ivy and ticks.  We’ve tried to avoid them, but no promises.  On the upside, from our visits so far, the flies are not bad.

 

We may resend this notice with any modifications, next week, in advance of the gathering.  Otherwise, see you on the 13th!
