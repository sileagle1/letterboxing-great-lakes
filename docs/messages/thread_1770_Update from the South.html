Update from the South

From: Delirium (donaldson_home@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-09-01 14:11:02 UTC
Hi y'all (No, I'm just kidding)

Seems we had ourselves a little tropical storm action the other day.
Which is to say, the wind gusts only got up to 50 mph, instead of the
140 mph that Katrina worked herself upto as a hurricane.
We're all fine. An uprooted birdfeeder, some patio furniture flung
about, nothing compared to what New Orleans and Biloxi have. Heck,
we only got 5 1/2 inches of rain that night, which was nice, since it
was the first rain we'd gotten since mid-July (not kidding).

The change in barometric pressure or the psychic abilities of animals
must have make the local fauna crazy, because we saw our first
copperhead in the yard a day before Katrina reached us. But
considering that it was dead, I'm guessing that it wasn't too
psychic, because wouldn't a fact like your imminent demise be
something you might forsee? It was pretty, anyway, which is more
than I can say for the bugs down here. One word. Locusts. If you
ever want a practical lesson in self-discipline and patience, come
down here during locust season and sit outside for 15 minutes. If
that racket doesn't drive you absolutely bonkers, your a stronger
person than I. The noise they make, which is like what I would
imagine a rattlesnake's rattle would sound like if pumped through a
speaker, is enough to make even the most placid person a little bit
peevish.

The letterboxing still stinks down here. And since I'm the only
person who's planting down here, and I've effectively stopped since
May (too dang hot), that means it's REALLY been dead.

And oh yeah, I had the baby in June. Did I mention that? Joaquin
Orion (that's Wah-Keen, not Jwa-kwin for those of you who didn't take
Spanish). He's a big tubby baby who will probably let me go
letterboxing when the weather breaks, as long as I keep him in the
baby sling and don't make him sit in the stroller. But since there's
nothing to letterbox, it's mostly a non-issue.

That's all. I miss you, Michigan, letterboxing, and a regional
cuisine that doesn't feel it necessary to fry and barbeque every item
of food.

Cheers,
Delirium
