I did accidentally find one box at the GLk gathering when I was
trying to plant a box near the shelter. That's not all that
surprising, though! The funny thing is, when I fake-found the box, I
didn't sign into it. But, then when we went to find it for "real" the
next day, it was gone! (It turned back up though, so I did get to find it)

I seem to have a knack of planting boxes near other boxes and not
knowing about it until later.

I planted a box, literally, right on top of a micro geocache and had
no clue until a geocacher emailed me several weeks later. Lots of
cachers had found my box in the meantime. I'm not sure if they
mistook if for the cache or just signed into both. I moved this one
to another place in the same cemetery a few weeks after it was planted.

I planted a box 20 feet away from a box that TrailTroll had planted 4
hours earlier and didn't know if until we both posted our clues and
it was obviously in the "same spot". Mine was supposed to be a
mystery box, but anyone who looked at both sets of clues would know
they were in the same park. I moved this one to another place in the
same county the next week.

I planted another box within X distance from another geocache which I
still don't know exactly where is hidden, but several people signed
into my box that there was a cache nearby. I couldn't find it, but I
removed my box anyway.

I always look on AQ and LbNA to make sure I'm not planting near know
letterboxes, but apparently I have to look on geocaching.com, too!

Oh yes! We have found geocaches several times when looking for a spot
to plant a box and have sometimes found them when looking for a box.
We've also found several geocaches while out on hikes just because we
noticed a "suspicious pile of something".

We just let the geocaches alone since we don't have any interest in
them. If planting our own box, we move on to a different area if we
find a geocache (except on one notable occasion when we somehow managed
to plant a gathering box almost on top of a geocache. We found out
about it later, but both managed to peacefully exist for the month that
the temporary circus box was in WI!)

We've also found a WOM letterbox that we knew nothing about because we
were so far off track in following the clues to find the box we DID
know about (which we did eventually find, but it was quite a far ways
from where we found the "surprise" box).
