Team Taco boxes bye-bye

From: moonsunmagik (moonsunmagik@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-06-25 19:48:37 UTC
Hi, guys--

In light of a recent rash of M.I.A.'s among our letterboxes, we've
come to the conclusion that someone, most likely a non-letterboxer,
is deliberately taking our boxes. Why? I'm not sure, who knows why
people do the things they do?

Kate and I have taken all our clues off the LbNA site, and they will
stay off indefinitely. We're in the process of making our boxes
available through some alternative means, so keep an eye out for
more information.

Illegitimus non carborundum! Viva el TACO!
Melinda and Kate

Re: Team Taco boxes bye-bye

From: moonsunmagik (moonsunmagik@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-06-26 00:04:36 UTC
Thanks for your encouragement. We also thought it might have been a
coincidence there for a while... Until the total of missing boxes
for the year went up to six earlier this month. Now we're already
at nine missing, and we've come to a point where it seems like
having the clues widely available seems incredibly foolhardy.

I think our mistake was telling friends and coworkers about
letterboxing in a naive attempt to share the fun. The word must
have gotten around to someone who thought the fun in letterboxing
lies in messing with letterboxers. And you may be right, WI Hiker,
this new wave of publicity may spark pranksterism on even a national
level. Who knows? Tough clues may HAVE to be the way to go, just
for the sake of box survival.

As for Team Taco, we'll figure something out, a new way to get clues
in circulation. The words "fun" and "challenging" come to mind, and
we hope you think so, too.

Melinda
