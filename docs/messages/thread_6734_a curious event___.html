a curious event...

From: azobox (jparkerg@yahoo.com) | Date: 2010-09-13 01:54:08 UTC
Artistamp and I were walking along the Lake Michigan shoreline, returning to the car after an enjoyable late afternoon/evening on the beach At Van Buren State Park near South Haven, Michigan. She was picking up trash as she often does for disposal away from the beach.
Imagine my surprise when I saw her pick up an 8 x 11 sheet of paper -- I said that it looked like a letterbox clue and sure enough, we saw it had the LbNA header.
To my greater amazement, it was for one of my nearby boxes: "I Spy".

Since the paper had not been on the beach a few hours earlier when we'd walked that way, I was left wondering if some searcher had walked past me on his/her way to find the box. Since I wasn't ready for a trip up the dune to see if there had seen a recent find, it will remain a mystery.
Did someone on this list recently search for "I Spy"?
Hmmmm.

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2010-09-12 21:12:15 UTC-05:00
Ooooh! We always worry about leaving some clues behind, but I think we have
actually only done so twice. One time it turned out some boxers were close
behind us, so they tucked them into the next box at the park and when they
met us on the trail going back to pick them up, they told us where we could
retrieve them. The other time was at a pretty remote spot and we didn't
notice we lost them for quite a while. All we could hope was that wind &
rain would carry/wash them away before prying eyes found them. Oh - and
one other time a strong wind WHISKED a whole set of clues from a picnic
table and we immediately went chasing after them, but they quickly landed in
a creek and the current swept them away.



But no, it wasn't us in Michigan this weekend...
