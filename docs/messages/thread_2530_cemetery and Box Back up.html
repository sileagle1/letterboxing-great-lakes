The cemeteries near us date back to the 1800's. In earlier times,
families would take the train out to the cemeteries and make a day of
it--packing their picnic lunches, etc (almost like a trip to the park
for us). I figure that if they could be used for recreation at the
start of their existence, then why not continue that.

I do believe in treating the cemeteries and their residents with
consideration, though. For instance, I wouldn't plant the box under a
marker or in a mausoleum (unless it was a family member, maybe). So
far, we've done historical topics for our boxes (like the circus train
wreck in our Showmen's Rest box)--and I try to be respectful of the
people involved. There is one box I would love to do, but I fear it
wouldn't be very respectful (although the story is fascinating): one
of Liz Taylor's husbands is buried in our neck of the woods. A number
of years after his death, graverobbers dug up his body, presumably
looking for jewelry. They never did find the culprit--although they
did find the body to rebury.
