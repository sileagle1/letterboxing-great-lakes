> BirdWoman/WP

>
> This day is call'd GLk 2010.
> They that outlive this day, and come safe home,
> Will stand a tip-toe when this day is nam'd,
> And rouse them at the name of Jubilee.
> They that shall live this day, and see old age,
> Will yearly on the vigil feast their neighbours,
> And say 'To-morrow is the gathering
> Then will they open their logbooks and show the images,
> And say 'These stamps I had on GLk 2010.
> Old men forget; yet all shall be forgot,
> But we'll remember, with advantages,
> What feats we did that day. Then shall our names,
> Familiar in the mouth as household words-
> Be in their flowing cups freshly rememb'red.
> This story shall the good man teach his son;
> And a Great Lakes gathering shall ne'er go by,
>>From this day to the ending of the world,
> But we in it shall be remembered-
> We few, we happy few, we band of boxers;
> For those to-day that slip in mud with me
> Shall be my family; be they ne'er so vile,
> This day shall gentle their condition;
> And boxers in the Great Lakes now-a-bed
> Shall think themselves accurs'd they were not here,
> And hold their manhoods cheap whiles any speaks
> That boxed with us upon GLk 2010.
