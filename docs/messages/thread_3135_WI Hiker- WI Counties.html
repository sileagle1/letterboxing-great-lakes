Oh ho! You MUST know what the true LB capital of the USA is, right?
It is definitely Connecticut which has almost 3,500 boxes. Based on
square miles, WI is almost 12 times larger than CT. If we had the
same proportion of letterboxes to square miles, we would have over
41,000 boxes in our state!!! However we actually only have 632 boxes
based on checking the letterboxing.org site tonight.

DISCLAIMER: I know not all boxes are listed on the LBNA site and I
also know that not all boxes listed on LBNA are publically accesible or
active boxes. I'm just using it as the most popular box listing site
for a point of comparison.

Given the disclaimer above, I was interested in how the 6 states in the
Great Lakes region compared to each other in terms of how many boxes
were listed. Here are the results based on a look tonight (based on
number of boxes, not just number of listings, since some listings are
for a series with multiple boxes):

1,318 Ohio
691 Illinois
632 Wisconsin
521 Michigan
423 Indiana
158 Minnesota

So you can see that not only is WI not the letterboxing capital of the
US, it isn't even that high in our region. I have to admit I was quite
surprised to see the large count for Ohio because it doesn't seem like
this list has many Ohio representatives. I guess we'll have a chance
to find lots of boxes when we head to the Great Lakes Gathering in Ohio
next year!

Wisconsin Hiker
