Pageflakes? Netvibes?

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2007-08-08 11:13:47 UTC
Is there anyone using Pageflakes (http://www.pageflakes.com/) or
Netvibes (http://www.netvibes.com/)? In case you aren't familiar with
either of these, they are web 2.0 personal portal applications. The
idea is a lot like "My Yahoo" where you build your own page or set of
pages using modules that contain small applications, RSS feeds or
podcasts. I've been using My Yahoo for several years, but recently
switched because the array of stuff you can put on your page with
Pageflakes and/or Netvibes is leaps and bounds ahead of My Yahoo.
Each of these two has its merits and drawbacks. I'm using Netvibes,
but lately have been flirting with Pageflakes and like what I see
there too.

Anyway, I won't bore you with my geekiness, if you are interested go
and check them out for yourself. They are both free. Feel free to
e-mail me if you have any questions.

So you may be wondering, how does this pertain to letterboxing? Well
Pageflakes and Netvibes allow you to make a page and publish it for
other users to add to their set of pages. I have created a
"Letterboxing Resources" page that contains links to clue sites, yahoo
groups and informational sites about the hobby, as well as RSS feeds
from Mark & Sue Pepe's blog and the AQ Event calendar. All episodes
of Choi's Letterpod podcast are also there. It is basically
everything letterboxing all in one place. I plan to add some
additional blogs or links to blogs yet, but want to get permission
from the authors first. I have the Pageflakes edition published and
publicly available -- just search the Pagecasts section for
"letterboxing" and you should find it. I'm finishing up on the
Netvibes edition and should have that publicly available by the weekend.

The really cool thing I foresee is individual letterboxers using this
as a kind of homepage, where they can place box information, reports
and/or clues and then publish their page for other letterboxers to
access... I envision having a whole collection of pages from
different users, each being dynamically updated by that user with new
box clues and letterboxing information they wish to share with the
community. I am working on a SpringChick page to make publicly
available -- some exclusive clues, other letterboxing things of
interest, but can't decide if I want to commit to Pageflakes or
Netvibes or offer both editions.

SpringChick
