new discoveries in Rubbermaid!

From: Armadillo Jo (jes7o@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-10-09 17:20:24 UTC-07:00
So today I went to Target to look for potential
containers for a new series I'm working on....and I
stumbled across these new containers called Lock &
Lock containers. They have a lid with tabs on each
side that clamps the lid down over the box with a
silicone seal. The claim is that it is air, water,
tight, can be frozen and microwaved...and seems pretty
dang durable. Does any one have any experience with
these containers? I bought the smallest one they had
(which is still pretty big, about a quart) as a test
subject to see how they hold up. Apparently there are
a variety of sizes that can be ordered off of their
website. They're slightly more pricey than Rubbermaid
(about 3 dollars a box) but if they'll withstand a
nuclear holocaust (or an Indiana winter), i'll fork
out the extra change....

--Armadillo Jo

From: Timbertoes (toomey@genevaonline.com) | Date: 2004-10-10 02:28:13 UTC

Haven't seen those yet - I've been using some quite nice containers
that I found at Kmart. Yep, Martha Stewart brand. We'll see how
they hold up through a Wisconsin winter. I just bought some olive
drab spray paint that I'm going to use on them so the white rim of
the lid doesn't show so much. Otherwise they're clear but shiny.

Remember in a previous post I said I was on my fifth pack/bag to hold
all my boxing stuff? Make that SIX. After all the talk recently
about organizing, I thought I'd try the binder idea where you put all
your "placed" box clues in one binder and your "found" box clues in
another. I found some forest green binders (how appropriate!) at
Walmart along with some sheet protectors. So while I was there I saw
these clipboards that actually open up to hold stuff under them. I
think it was Lipstick Chick that said those worked for her so... Now
I just have to get out and 'box so I can try it out!

Timbertoes
