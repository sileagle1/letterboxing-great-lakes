Hey, how great! Looks like a Muskegon area get-together could
really happen for 10/16 .... yippie!!

I just did a quick scan of the boxes listed on the letterboxing.org
site and found out there are 41 boxes in the Muskegon / White Lake
area!!

I'm getting familiar enough with the area, I could probably lay out
a rough South to North route but don't get the idea that all 41
could be found in a single afternoon! Some clues indicate that a
significant hike would be involved. Not to mention the usual trying-
to-figure-it-out hokey pokey that takes up its own amount of time.
(grin -- with me along, that could be a significant amount of time!)

So what kind of boxing trip is everyone looking for? Let's plan the
get-together around that. I'll chime in after I hear what some of
the rest of you are hoping to get out of meeting up.

Besides being able to meet other boxers, learn a little about each
other and have some fun what would you like the main goal of our
get-together to be?

Increasing the "F" counts significantly? (That would mean we should
search the clues and rule out those that involve a significant time
investment, and try to `route plan' an efficient way to collect as
many boxes as possible in the time we have available.)

Solving some really toughie type clues to get a box that's been
elusive? (There are some very tricky clues among the boxes listed
for this area!)

Maybe just take one or two nice long hikes with plenty of time to
talk along the way and find just a couple, or a few boxes?

Should we compare which boxes we already have (if any) and rule
those out first? (Let's be careful not to be spoilers. Maybe we
should do that sort of thing off the list, via email)

Enjoy!

Re: Muskegon get together / goal??

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-10-04 15:10:33 UTC

Although I won't be able to join in the boxing part of the day, as
the owner of many of those 41 boxes, I would like to give some input.

First of all, I appreciate the comments about being careful not to
give spoilers. There are some mystery boxes in the bunch, and there
are others where bits and pieces of information are missing or need
to be fit together to arrive at the location. For somebody familiar
with the area or somebody who has previously been to the park or box,
this may seem obvious, however to another person, these would be
things that need to be figured out. Although I certainly cannot stop
people from collaborating to solve clues, mystery boxes are not
really meant to be group projects.

Secondly, I am concerned about a group boxing activity. Several of
the boxes in this area are in Muskegon State Park. As many of you
know, the Michigan DNR recently implemented a "pay to place" policy
for geocaches and letterboxes. Fortunately the staff at Muskegon
State Park have taken a lenient stance on this policy and rather than
pull the boxes or charge for their placement, they are monitoring the
boxes, watching for social trails and other environmental impact. I
have worked very hard to establish a relationship with them and to
show them the positve side of letterboxing. A handful of boxers
coming through every couple of weeks or so isn't a problem; 25 people
descending on a box at one time is a whole other game. In addition
to the environmental aspect of many people boxing together, I have
concerns about the discretionary aspect. How inconspicuous can a
group of letterboxers really be? It is hard enough to keep a low
profile when there are 2or 3 of you and a dog, let alone an entire
group. I would hate to see any of the area boxes -- mine or
otherwise -- be compromised because a flurry of activity raises the
curiosity of other hikers or nearby observers.

So in short, I am wondering if it is not better to provide a
specified period of time for people to do some boxing in the area --
individually or as small teams, rather than as a large group, and
then meet up with each other after that? That way those who are
interested in jacking up the "F" count can head off to the drive-bys,
those looking for a hike can head another direction and those looking
for some awesome scenery can head yet another way. This will also
allow those who want to take the time to work through some of the
mystery clues the opportunity to head off to those boxes without fear
of giving anything away to others in the group. Everyone has the
same access to the clues at letterboxing.org and should be able to
decide what works best for them without having to create a group
itinerary.

If anyone has specific questions about any of my Muskegon/White
Lake/Montague area boxes, or may be wondering which would provide the
easiest/toughest/most scenic hike, please feel free to write me off
list.

Also, I am interested in what may transpire for later in the day --
would love to meet up with the group for an evening meal or bonfire
if there are folks who will be staying around that long.

Thanks!

SpringChick
