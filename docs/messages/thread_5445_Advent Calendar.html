From: Coffee Beans (coffeebeans06@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-12-10 09:44:53 UTC-06:00
My family had a different advent calendar tradition, and I'm not sure
how/when it actually started. We had a cloth wall hanging with an
outline of a house with Santa up on the roof and inside there were small
numbered pouches counting down the days to Christmas. There was a
little plush mouse that hopped from pouch to pouch each day. My sister
and I always competed over who would be the one to move the mouse,
rushing to get downstairs first in the morning to do it. At some point,
my parents decided that we just had to share so that I moved on even
days and she moved on odd days (or vice versa). My mom still hangs it
up at home, but I don't think her and dad fight over who gets to move
the mouse like we did. ;)

Coffee Beans

From: Doodle & Deedle Bug (doodle_n_deedle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-12-12 06:50:52 UTC-08:00
We had something kind of along those lines, too, only we never called it an "advent calendar," it was always our "countdown to Christmas" calendar. Mom had made a felt Santa face, and put a bunch of small velcro pieces in the beard area, with numbers by each velcro tab. Each day, we'd put a white pom-pom onto Santa's face on the appropriately-numbered tab so that by Christmas day his beard would be complete. And yes, arguments about who got to put the pom-pom on Santa's face each day ensued, so we did the "every-other-day" taking turns thing, too. And Mom also still hangs it, too, only Santa now starts off the month with his full pom-pom beard in place rather than her and my dad taking turns putting the pom-poms on as the month progresses :)

Doodle Bug.
