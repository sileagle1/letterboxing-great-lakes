From: Kathi LK Krankoski (krank@ameritech.net) | Date: 2005-07-28 23:04:48 UTC-05:00
Postal letterboxing is kind of a hybrid between letterboxing and mail art.
The concept of logbooks and stamping into them remains the same, but that's
about it. I think postals tend to be more artistic in that people often
include little extra stuff you wouldn't typically include in an outside box,
mostly because you don't have to worry about it weathering the elements.
The logbooks are often more elaborate, and stamps often lend themselves to
use of more multicolor images (just because you have more opportunity to do
so, since you aren't precariously balancing everything on your knee in a
mosquito-infested forest, watching out for naked peeing men! I suppose you
could find someone at home to do that for you if you want that kind of
ambiance, but I won't go there!!!!). Anyway, back to postals. Most do not
require solving clues to find them (exceptions: my "Fish Out of Water 3" and
"A question for you", Cherokee's "Pirate's Plunder", etc.), but instead
involve signups, sometimes on AtlasQuest or through WOL, most on the postal
yahoo group. A currently popular offshoot is the postal ring, where a group
of people create boxes on a particular subject (Disney characters, medical
professions, book titles, etc.), and the boxes are rotated amongst the
members (A to B to C to A, etc). Some are open to everyone, others require
some sort of condition to qualify for receiving it. There are special boxes
for newbies (our very own JACKS maintains the "Newbie Pool"), and some
considered Randoms (get sent from person-to-person as a surprise, without
the recipients knowing they are on the list to receive them). Some people
count postals in their PFX (usually as separate categories), others don't (I
don't, but then again, I don't count any of my boxes). They are a good way
to get a boxing "fix" if you don't have the opportunity to find many outside
boxes, especially during the winter.



For more info, check out the postal letterboxing yahoo group, and read the
FAQ's, or drop me an offlist message, and I'll be happy to tell you more.
