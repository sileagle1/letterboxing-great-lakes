Trout Lake & the Mankoffs

From: villaflotilla (villaflotilla@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-11-07 17:54:50 UTC
People please stop and remember why you letterbox. This activity is
supposed to be FUN! My mom kept the copy of the Argus to show me
since she knows that I'm crazy about letterboxing. Little did she
know that the stamp pictured in the article and the directions to
the Trout Lake LB were mine! It was the first stamp I attempted to
carve after getting hooked. Yes, I'd be disappointed if that stamp
went "missing" yes, I would hope that any newbies would read up on
LBing before going out to hunt. Yes, it is important not to
announce our presence in the woods when letterboxing, but PLEASE
don't let these articles raise your blood pressure. I found out
about letterboxing from an article in TIME magazine. It talked
about a park in Connecticut that I had just visited a week previous,
I was bummed because I live in Michigan and may never get back to
that park. But my point is, without that article I may never have
found out about letterboxing at all. I personally think that if a
stamp I carved and a hike that I love brings enjoyment to any
weekend thrill seekers then I'm cool with that. I think the photo
of the kids with the stamp is worth the effort it took to carve.
Don't make the Mankoff family out to be villians, I think they are
just having fun with their kids and I'm thrilled that it was my
stamp and box that they enjoyed finding! I wish that the clues
hadn't been printed like they were, but hopefully that won't be a
problem. The article mentions 9 letterboxes in Livingston County.
I've planted 7 of them and hope they will be safe, but I accept that
as part of the hobby. TO ANYONE THINKING OF PULLING MY BOXES FOR
SAFE KEEPING - STOP! I accept the risk involved and will recarve
them if need be, but I took the time to plant them for ALL to enjoy,
not just seasoned letterboxers!

Sincerely

FLOTILLA THE HUN
