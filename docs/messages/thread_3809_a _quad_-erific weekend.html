a "quad"-erific weekend

From: Coffee Beans (coffeebeans06@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-03-05 18:40:34 UTC-06:00
To celebrate my birthday (yesterday), Atom planned a weekend of
letterboxing for us (without my knowledge of the location), and I just
had to share. :) To start, he'd made plans to meet a boxer from Denver
(murmur) who was in town for a conference on Saturday morning. The
three of us drank coffee and swapped letterboxing stories and stamps,
which was great fun. Afterwards, we headed out of town towards our
"mystery" destination: the Quad cities! :) He'd gotten some detailed
advice from Hart x6, so we had great directions from box-to-box along
the way west on I-80 as well as in the quad city area, plus some great
restaurant recommendations. Following his advice for the order to find
the boxes was great and efficient, resulting in very little/no arguments
over driving and navigating which can sometimes be a problem for us. :P ;)

Anyway, for the most part, the boxes we found had only been visited by
the local boxers (although Tiptoe & Tonto and KissMeKate & Barefoot Ankh
had been out there in a few of the boxes), but let me tell you, there
are some fun finds out there! A lot of the boxes were in historically
and/or geographically significant places, which were fun. We got some
great views of the Mississippi and the various cool bridges across it.
Mostly all were hand carved stamps as well, which were much appreciated
and admired. Though the boxes were mostly drive-bys, the clues were
well done and many included funny stories/poems to liven things up, in
addition to some interesting facts on the area. :) My favorite boxes
from the weekend include Campbell's Island, Captain's Wheel, and Monkey
Island, plus the 2 Broadway series boxes, since we'd been wanting to see
what the other nearby musical series was like. ;)

Overall, it was a great weekend. :) We managed to get 24 boxes over the
two days -- 2 of which we were first finders, and 2 of which were
hitchhikers. And we left 3 hitchhikers at various points along the way,
so get out there and find them! :)

So thanks to the quad-cities planters and especially Hart x6, who also
helped Atom organize our weekend. And, of course, a special thanks to
Atom for planning the whole thing for my birthday -- he's such a
sweetheart. ;) :)

Coffee Beans
