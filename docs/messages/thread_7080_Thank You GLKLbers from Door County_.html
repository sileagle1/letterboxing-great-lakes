Thank You GLKLbers from Door County!

From: sunnysideupm (sunnysideupm@yahoo.com) | Date: 2011-04-19 03:01:13 UTC
It has been great to see so many Great Lakes LBers signed in to the LBs here in Door County. And many of the planters are familiar faces/stamps as well!

Today's report: I started the day by locking myself out of my brother's place in Algoma. I ended the day by trying to return to Algoma via Brown County (oops - missed a turn to stay on 42). But I count the day successful because I found 10 of the 14 boxes I tried to find!

I found ALL of Tiptoe and Tonto's Tortoise and the Silly Hare LBs!!!!! ;) And yes, T&T, I get that the moral of story is slow and steady wins the race. And Merlin and Ardea's boxes were great overall experiences - if you all ever get a chance to go for boxes by these 2(4?) placers - DO IT!

After I went for the Deciduous Monkey III box, I kept thinking that DM had followed me to Peninsula SP. Maybe I was monkey-led there, rather than pixie-led???? Not too surprising is that the boxes I find challenging up here are placed by people whose other boxes have given me fits. But I told my DB and DSIL that the landmarks changed. Yeah - that's it. ;)

A friend of my niece was asking me if I'd found the LB's in the Algoma area, including the one at Cave Point! I tried not to laugh, then explained about the 10th Anniversary gathering in '08, and how that was the first time I'd ever been to Door County. When I found the Algoma LB back then I didn't know my DB (actually half-bro) lived in Algoma not too far from the LB. I'm sure glad we re-connected!

There are so many boxes here that even a two day trip is not enough! I guess I'll have to come back! And by then maybe it'll be frozen custard season at my DB's coffeeshop! :P

- Sunny, who has seen a lot of cherry today!
