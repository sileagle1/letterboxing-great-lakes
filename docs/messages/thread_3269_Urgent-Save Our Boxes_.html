Dragon, we feel your pain and frustration. As I told
this 'reporter', and I use that designation term loosely, he
obviously doesn't have a clue how much time and effort goes into
researching and writing a clue, before an individual creates a
letterbox. SHAME ON THIS REPORTER!!!

So what are serious letterboxers learning from these recent media
articles?

1. Nothing on the Internet is private.
2. Clues need to be challenging. I recall spending 6 months
reseraching one clue just to reveal the starting point. Most
newbies won't bother with that clue. I found that box!
3. Never trust the media!
4. Once, a wise letterboxer advised me: Always include compass
bearings in your clue. This will prevent most weekend family thrill
seekers from looking for that box. This is true.
5. Another wise letterboxer shared this thought with me: It is OK
to post a clue on LbNA or AQ, but when it comes to specifics, force
the person to contact the placer of the box. So, I tried this
approach. WOW!!! You cannot believe the type of emails you will
receive. The inquires you will receive will make your toe nails
curl!

Sometimes, I will create s simple drive by box for beginners. If it
goes missing, so what. I don't spend a lot of time on drive by box
stamps or logbooks.

When boxes go missing, we as clue writers must realize that we are
partically to blame. Yes, I will accept part of the blame when my
boxes go missing. I vow to spend more time writing the clue in a
way that also protects the box. Maybe then, boxes will remain safer
even when the media crosses the line and reveals clues in their
publications.
