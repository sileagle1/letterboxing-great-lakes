Letterboxing Wedding

From: Atom 118 (hersh118@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2007-11-11 14:06:47 UTC-06:00
Thank you to everyone for making yesterday such a wonderful afternoon.
Although we only met many of you last summer at the GLK 2006 gathering,
you are quickly becoming another extended family. We were a bit
overwhelmed by all the warmth and genuine well-wishes we received from
the local letterboxing community. Our expectations for yesterday were
much the same as any other gathering we have attended. However, we got
something a bit different. Rather than just a group of people stamping
in our logbooks and having fun exchanging our little artworks, it seemed
much like our actual wedding engagement party (fun, friendly, and filled
with love). I've posted a few photos from yesterday, and don't worry;
I'll share some photos from the actual big day next spring. Ok, on to
the event.

People started showing up promptly at noon, and before we knew it, there
were letterboxers EVERYWHERE!!!! There was a wide geographic variety to
our guests. Beachmom and her kids lived only blocks from the park while
Doglvrs, Cherry on Top, and Inky Oiker from MI, Hartx6 from the west
edge of IL, Mrs. Drs. B from IA, the Olsentrio and MM and WI Hiker from
WI, and Tricycle and Grumpy Bear from WA had to travel a bit further. We
were also excited to finally meet some of the newer boxers in our area
such as Questar, Ringmaster (a transplant from upstate NY), RBQ, and
Lock Stock and Barrel. Please check out our group photo on the AQ event
page (http://www.atlasquest.com/events/event.html?gEventId=381) to see
all of our guests [thank you to everyone for giving me three shots to
take the photo since I had some difficulty working the timer on my
camera] If any of you have pictures, please feel free to upload them to
the album to share with the rest of us.

The stamps, as usual, were gorgeous. I think everyone had a great time
tracking down all the Registry stamps by Beetle and Fermonts
off-registry Registry Shmegistry stamps (uh, CB what are we going to
do with all these toasters?!!?). Overall, we are counting the day as a
huge success! Thank you to everyone who helped with stamps, food,
supplies, labor and advice. I will also take under advisement all the
keys to marriage that received in particular, the many yes dear and
go letterboxing suggestions. We hope to catch up on all our personal
communications by the end of the week.

Cool, one event under our belts...hey CB, when are we hosting the next?!

Atom of Atomic Beans

We've been gone for the past two days and were surprised to come back to our computers
and hear about Tonto's day at the wonderful Letterbox Wedding hosted by love birds,
Atom118 and Coffee Beans...So cute!...He certainly did not hate it...Actually, I think his
words were that he really enjoys meeting and greeting other letterboxers at these
doings....the "coloring" part is what he dislikes...OK, he hates it!...But, after a bit more
thought he was overheard saying,...."It sure beats getting poked in the eye with a sharp
stick!"...Whatever!

At one point during the "wedding" he did whisper to Tiptoe..."Do you think ANYONE here
knows the Illinois/Ohio State score? Or cares? A quick scan around the room proved he
might be right..We saw huddling around tables to discuss mystery gatherings, but no talk
of quarterback sneaks. We saw interceptions, not of a ball, but incognito stamps. There
were shouts and cheers regarding well-carved stamps, but not over pass completions.
Photo ops and inteviews with a guy and gal in funny wedding garb, but not with coaches
and star running backs.

But, he knows the game plan...make Tiptoe happy and all is well with his world!

He fought his way through the first quarter of the wedding by eating...a lot! Second
quarter the game became more interesting as he began to meet new
letterboxers...especially a fellow bench warmer, like himself, the patriarch of the Brat Pack
who shared a same hometown status. Third quarter, brought him into the game even if it
was only to go for the extra point by getting up his game face and asking Pastor Wife for
her PT...He recited his favorite Bible verse and was able to procure her stamp! A first score
for him! But, he made Tiptoe stamp in. Finishing the game in the fourth quarter led to the
upset of letterboxing history! So exciting in fact, that we will defer to the play-by-play
transcript by sportscastors Brent Muskberger and Kirk Herbstreit.

"Kirk, here we are heading into the final few minutes of the game... and what is that we
see down on the field? Why it can't be! Yes, I believe we see Tonto coming into the game!
Is the coach crazy sending in this rookie to stamp into FungusWoman's letterbox
appropriately named..."HIS RED UNDIES"? He has never inked up before! Can he do it?

Next to him at the table Tiptoe coaches him on, "Just do it!," she shouts from the sidelines.
He appears to be a little apprehensive as if he is trying to recall exactly what he has
witnessed her doing all these years."

"Let me interject here Brent. The stats on this letterboxing couple show that they in fact
have been letterboxing 1,710 days since there debut to this sport...an extra day added for
Leap Year 2004..."

" Sorry to interupt, but you mean to tell me, Tonto has NEVER inked up ANY stamp in all
that time?"

"Nope! Amazing isn't it?... Oh, wait, I think I see Mr. Cottontail with his camera capturing
this historic moment. Let's go down to the sidelines and see if we can pick up more on
the action. Back to you Brent."

"Kirk, I'm right behind you fighting my way through the stunned fans to see that Fungus
Woman is the first to realize that Tonto has not only stamped into her box, but has added
his signature as well...an alert to Antiques Roadshow....Tiptoe's signature is going to be
worth a dime a dozen...But, Tonto's! Wow, that could be worth it's weight in PZ-Cut. It
may be the only one known to exist!"

"I think we need to call in the paramedics, Tiptoe looks to be a little shook up from that
last play! Let's see if we can get a few words from her before she heads for the locker
room...."

Tiptoe, are you all right?"

"Ohhhh, I'm fine...just a little stunned! I can't believe it! I've never seen him ink up a
stamp, let alone sign in! I've waited how many years for this level of participation from
him?...I knew Tonto had it in him!...I'm glad I didn't give up out on all those trails and
events hoping that someday he'd gain the confidence needed in this sport to grab those
pens and ink pads and kick some..aaa..actual rubber!"

Kirk, can you get a few words from Tonto...he looks confused as to what to do with that
felt and plastic baggie...I think he's in shock as well...Not sure he even comprehends what
has just transpired...a dream maybe!"

"Tonto, this has to be the biggest moment of your letterboxing career...Do you think you'll
ever be able to repeat the victory? Anything you'd like to say to the crowd?"

"Ahhhh, Does any one know how Illinois is doing?"

"Brent, he doesn't seem to have a clue to what he has just accoplished. Tiptoe is now
leading him out the door and it seems that we have just experienced a great moment in
letterboxing history. The question remains though, if Tonto can continue this level of play
in the future. For the time being, we'll let them savor today's victory and worry about
future strategies next season."

There you have it folks! We'll keep you posted...

A side note..We have heard that good things happen to those that wait, give of themselves
unselfishly, take risks...or something of that manner...and Tonto was richly rewarded by
an outstanding victory of Illinois over Ohio State!...and he got to see the last quarter of the
game for himself making for a very Happy Tonto...and Happy Tiptoe!

A very good wedding reception indeed!
Tiptoe & Tonto
