Letterbox workshop for homeschoolers

From: dawninger (dawninger@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-03-28 20:20:04 UTC
I have again been asked to host a workshop on Letterboxing for the
WPA's homeschooling conference May 3rd. Last year was the first year I
hosted this workshop and to my surprise over 70 people attended to
learn more about Letterboxing. I wasn't expecting this many people,
and I had wished I would have planted more boxes that day. We had only
planted 12 temporary boxes for the duration of the conference.

This year I'd like to go bigger and better by planting permanent boxes
in the Oshkosh area, but I don't know if I should plant boxes I won't
be able to tend to more than once a year.

What is the ethical thing to do here? How often do you check up on
your boxes? Maybe I'm just a Letterbox mom who has trouble letting my
babies go! :)

Most Letterboxers I know will give TLC to boxes they find in need of
repair so I'm leaning towards going ahead with the permanent plant.

(Also if anyone wants to donate boxes, I will plant them for you in
the Oshkosh area this April.)

Thanks for any feedback you can offer me!
