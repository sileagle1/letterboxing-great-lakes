Holiday Magic 2005

From: Kathi LK Krankoski (krank@ameritech.net) | Date: 2005-12-01 13:13:38 UTC-06:00
No December weekend would be complete without a visit to Brookfield Zoo to
experience the Holiday Magic festival. Even better, you can now get a
letterbox while you are there!



Go to the far northwest corner of the North Parking Lot (off Golfview Road).
Stand at the corner curb and look NW towards the corner of the fence. See
the little Charlie Brown Tree? A few feet behind it, just about in line
with where you are standing and the corner of the fence, there is a tree
with a stump near it and a lot of scrubby undergrowth. Three guesses (and
the first two don't count) what is hiding there.



If you're feeling creative and the weather isn't too cold, bring a red,
yellow, orange, green, blue, and black marker. Otherwise any old stamp pad
works just fine. It is a dark corner, so don't wait until it's time to go
home to stamp in!



Just be alert for zoo security - have an excuse ready for why you are at the
furthest corner from the entrance to the zoo (uhhh, watching the sunset???).
If it's extremely heavy snow, it might be hard to find and cover adequately,
and watch for leaving telltale footprints (you knew that!).



Might be a temporary placement, might be permanent, stamp might be
seasonally changed.haven't decided yet!



See www.BrookfieldZoo.org (special events)
for hours and info on Holiday Magic.



Chicago Tribune subscribers can get free parking and free admission passes
(one adult/one child per family) at www.chicagotribune.com/sa.
