The Metroparks plant and advertise their own geocaches... they even have competitions and prizes (find a geocache in all 13 parks and get entered in a drawing for a free admission pass, etc.) so it would be surprising if they were planning to remove letterboxes.

They have geocaching listed as a sanctioned activity on their website: http://metroparks.com/activities/geocaching.php They do have a permit process for planters of geocaches, which requires no fee, but does mandate that cache locations change as of March 1st each year. http://metroparks.com/images/activities/geo_rules_app.pdf

--TJ_Mich

From: Barb Funk (mmfunk99@greenapple.com) | Date: 2007-05-03 21:24:55 UTC-04:00
Hey DD,

I don't know about all Metro Parks here in Ohio, but at least in the
Cincy area and southern Ohio in general, placing letterboxes is either
prohibited or the rules are so suffocating that most put their boxes
elsewhere.

If anyone does place a box in a metro park anyway (ignoring the
warnings) the reactions can range from rangers giving a mild scolding
and warning making you feel like an idiot, to using words like
"confiscating" and implying that they have the authority to press charges.

Most of us have since stayed the heck away from these parks since there
are plenty of other places available. One park very close to me offered
to let someone place boxes, but the park ranger would pick the location
and the box had to be checked once every two weeks minimum---and that
they would check to make sure the box was checked. Much of this is I
have been told, is because of drug-running that has been done in some
similar manner. :( Once I heard that, I was more understanding of why
there are such strict rules in some places. It's just one more example
of how some people can ruin something great for others---like Trick or
Treat having to be so much more limited than before. Make it stop!!

Trailtracker
