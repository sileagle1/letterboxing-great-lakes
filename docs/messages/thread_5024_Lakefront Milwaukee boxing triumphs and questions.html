Lakefront Milwaukee boxing triumphs and questions

From: Martha Hyland (martha.phillips@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-05-05 12:40:01 UTC-05:00
The very first letterbox I ever found was Agent Ink's "Lake Park
Series" box #1 - Lake Park Lighthouse. It was mid-July of 2004, and I
had no clue what I was doing, so imagine my excitement when I opened
the box (after brushing the mushrooms off the top) and then my
surprise and chagrin when I realized that most letterboxes do not come
with their own ink pad. :-) I returned a week later and logged in,
inkpad and handcarved stamp in hand.

(I hope that box is still there; some of the logs I saw today
indicated that it's gone).

I was never able to find the second box of that series, and about a
month ago, still staring at the space left for the Water Tower stamp,
on a page in my logbook that last saw ink nearly 4 years ago, I logged
on to Google Earth and tried to re-work the directions using my
computer. Turns out the directions were fine -- it was my math that
was off. I had been looking for this box for FOUR YEARS in the wrong
park, half a mile down the street from the right park (I walked 1000+
paces instead of 26. Duh).

My husband and I found the second box today, 46 months after I started
letterboxing (and before I had the husband), and he amusedly scraped
cobwebs and spiders off the box while I yammered on excitedly and our
dog napped in a shady spot under the tree.

In a funny way, I feel like I can move on with letterboxing now --
that blank space in my logbook no longer mocks me as a quasi-competent
boxer, mostly unable to add.

Anyhow. That's the triumph. Now the questions.

1) Milwaukee-area boxers -- has anyone tried for the Cambridge Parkway
series recently? I have found 8 of the 10 boxes in the series -- I
cannot find "Out of Water" or "The Bridges of Cambridge Parkway."
Does anyone know if these two are officially missing?

2) My husband and I are heading to Hawaii for a belated honeymoon --
we leave in about two weeks. I am taking a HH that I pulled from the
Dragon's HH Hostel, as well as one of my own boxes for placement.
Would anyone else like to send a HH or a box to paradise? We will be
spending a week on O'ahu. I would be happy to take Wisconsin
letterboxes with me. Just let me know.

--Kermit


[Addendum -- My husband, patient and kind soul that he is, knew
nothing about letterboxing until he saddled himself with me and I
began dragging him along on the hunts. When we visited Moab, Utah, he
read a book in the hotel room on the first night while I sat with
printouts of clues surrounding me and mapped them out for finding the
following day. I've already done the same with the O'ahu map. When
we thought about buying the tickets, I said, "I don't know if we can
really afford this," and he, wanting to also visit his old Navy
buddies in Pearl Harbor, countered with "If we go, I'll let you have
two entire days to go letterboxing."

I responded to this by immediately handing over my Visa card. :-) ]
