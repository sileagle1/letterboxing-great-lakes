Boxes in Bags

From: timbertoes33 (toomey@genevaonline.com) | Date: 2004-07-15 13:40:35 UTC
Reading WI Hiker's comment that she heard putting boxes into bags
wasn't a good idea made me curious. Most of the boxes I've found
have been in plastic bags, but it seems like overkill if the box
itself is waterproof. I know it's better to be safe than sorry, but
when you're trying to stamp in it can get confusing with all those
bags floating around (I keep my own things in bags, too). Also on
the down side is that the bag makes the box that much harder to
conceal since it's edges stick out all over. So far I've used bags
when placing my own boxes.

What do some of you think of the practice?


Re: [LbGLK] Boxes in Bags

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-07-15 12:23:12 UTC-04:00
I have found that the outer bag makes it much easier for a critter to get
hold of and drag away. Also, if the box is in a tight place, or even just a
stump with rough edges, the bag tears easily and is then worthless. I find
it much more effective to put the individual baggies (i.e. one for stamp,
one for log book) into a zip lock box INSIDE the box. A disadvantage of not
using the outer bag is if the box gets open, things scatter easier.
Sometimes I will put a rubberband around the outside of the box if it feels
like to lid is not tight enough.

SpringChick
