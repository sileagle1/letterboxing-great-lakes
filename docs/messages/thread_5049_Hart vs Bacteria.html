Well Guys, Its me, Matt!!

I'm back at home now and want to first publicly thank my wonderful
wife for sending you updates on me -- something I hadn't asked her to
do.

I also want to thank each and everyone of you for your thoughts,
prayers and Emails.

When I woke up Sunday morning and an elderly man walked in with a
STACK of Emails . . . all from the Great Lakes board (I mean really,
not even a single one from a family member . . . geesh). This
morning, another stack of Emails came to my room (this time some
family members did write).

You guys all brought the biggest sense of community and belonging
I've felt in my entire life, and I thank you.

Apparently all this ruckus was caused by pneumonia. The pneumonia
settled into my lower right lung and dragged it (my lung) down past
my diaphragm and onto my liver.

Once my lung hit my liver it started killing off liver cells (I guess
that's a bad thing) and my liver went into hyper drive and sent out
all these enzymes (so I'm told) apparently my enzyme count was over
400 I just googled what's normal and for an adult should be between 0-
42 with an optimal enzyme count of 21.

Needless to say I went through a lot of tests and one showed my right
lung sitting on top of my liver. As soon as I got some medication to
lower the inflammation in my lung my pain started going down.

I'm going to be on a couple of medications for the next 8 days -- one
to fight the pneumonia and one to fight the inflammation. That puts
us right up to the day that we leave for MN!!

To paraphrase one of ya, "If you can box in the pouring rain, this
wont keep you back."

God willing, we will be in MN boxing our hearts (and livers) out :-)

Thank you all again for being such wonderful people and caring so
much for me, Becki & the Kids. I'm truly touched!!
