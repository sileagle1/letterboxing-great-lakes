Weekend boxing

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2005-07-26 00:56:37 UTC-05:00
Hi!



Just wanted to say we had a great weekend boxing with our niece (Superfrog)
and nephew (Jake the Snake), even though the weather could have been better.
We managed to find 10 fantastic Puddlejumper boxes (hope that poison ivy
doesn't really cause them to quit - they have some great stamps and fun
clues!). We also found a nice box by the M-Clan (another good carver).



We found a few boxes Friday on our drive up, but on Saturday we were plagued
by on again, off again rain. As Jake noticed, as soon as we found a box, it
promptly started to rain again. We ran into some rain at Rock Springs and
didn't have our full packs with us, so no umbrellas. However it was a light
rain that didn't start until after we stamped in and visited the spring, so
it wasn't too bad - just a short walk back under tree cover. We managed the
400 Trail with no rain, but as soon as we found Herbie it started. Martini
Man & I each retrieved an umbrella from our packs, and they protected the
kids & us while stamping in and returning to the car. We decided we really
should take the other 2 umbrellas from the car for the next hunt. However
we tossed ours loose into the car to dry off while we ate lunch at a
restaurant and waited for the rain to stop. It did, so we headed out
searching for "Run, Run, As Fast As You Can" series. Little did we realize
that the title would become our mantra on that hike! Not only did we forget
to take the 2 car umbrellas, we also forgot to put our pack umbrellas back
in our packs, so we had NO umbrellas when a downpour started JUST as we
found the first box. It was a long walk/run back to the parking lot with
the box in hand! We took cover under a shelter and Martini Man got our
beach towels (& umbrellas!) from the car. We dried off, stamped in, and
then MM headed back on the trail to replace the box. Of course by now the
rain had stopped again, so we decided to go for the 2nd box. We enjoyed
raspberries along the trail and quickly found the box. However once again,
the weather changed - it got very dark and very windy. We thought it might
be a tornado on the way... We did have our umbrellas along, but since it
wasn't raining yet, we made a mad dash to the car and made it back just
before a huge downpour! We stamped in inside the car and after 10 minutes or
so the rain subsided. Jake & MM went out to replace the box but we then all
agreed we were jinxed and gave up boxing for the rest of the day.



Sunday we resumed but even though we got an early start, it was HOT and
HUMID. We found the 2 boxes at Mirror Lake and attempted one at Rocky
Arbor. After this, a 5-hour visit to Noah's Ark (huge waterpark) was very
much appreciated by all of us. However we had a mission - so we headed next
to Devil's Lake. We hiked all the way around the lake, doing both bluff
trails, to place 3 new boxes. It was HOT! HOT! HOT! A quick dip in the
lake cooled us off for the drive home (and to hunt for one more box at the
Merrimac ferry!)



Hope you all also had a fun weekend (it sounds like the MI crew did, even
though they had some rain).



Wisconsin Hiker

Weekend boxing

From: Pale Puppy Posse (advantagek9@voyager.net) | Date: 2005-07-26 16:52:19 UTC-04:00
Well we headed off on Friday to go up North to the Pine River for camping
and canoeing. Last year's outing was our first letterboxing ever.. so guess
our one year anniversary of boxing was this month too!
We did the Newaygo Dry Sand Prairies and Jolly Roger. All boxes doing fine!
Odd that we sometimes look at a tree or log and say, Man that would be a
great place to hide a box.. then after following the clues we end up at
that same tree or log... We managed to make it to the campground after dark
of course and pitched the tent... Next day was great to kayak down the
pine, thank goodness there was a breeze. On Sunday (after the terrible
storms all night, leaking tent and a dead tree branch hanging over the tent
that we did not see on Friday night) we tore down camp and headed off for
more boxing. The Serendipity box which is a mystery box so I can't say
much, but it eluded us last year due to a crucial mistake on my part but I
swore we would be back this year to capture it. And we did! I wish you
could have seen us enroute. For those of you who have found this box you
know what I mean as we did it without the normal transportation. Anyway we
decided to hit Grand Rapids on the way home. Grandvilla boxes seemed to
surround our route home so we decided to hit a few of those. First was the
Make a wish box. Nice park and fun hike. We missed one part of the clue but
manage to locate the box anyway. Our thermometer said 99 degrees.. are we
nuts! Off to the Sphinx and Serpentine boxes... Sphinx we found no problem
well besides the stinging nettles. Sweaty and getting a little agitated we
headed for Serpentine... I really enjoyed this area but did not enjoy the
last of these clues... As naughty dog is a math major I let him handle
these clues but I am sure he didn't do it right :-).. Grandvilla I will be
contacting you off list to confirm... but walking along a man came jogging
by with his dog! Now remember it is 99 degrees out and he was wearing a
sweatshirt! At this point I am ready to strangle Naughty dog and we never
did find the box... dang! Oh well I was glad to be back in the car with the
A/C on.
