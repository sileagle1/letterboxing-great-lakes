From: jwalkersmich (jefflaura88@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2007-01-19 01:56:34 UTC
I've been reading with interest the Bingo thread on choosing the free
space. When our family was deciding what to choose, I initially
suggested one of the corner spots. My children wanted other spaces on
the board because they liked the words. After some discussion, we
decided that whatever Spring Chick chooses next is random anyway, and
if what we choose is picked next, then we get no advantage over others
(like those who picked "Exchange.") So in the end, we picked "The
Raven." It is the only letterbox on the list that we found. We
decided that we would be rather disappointed if, at the end of the
game, that particular item hadn't been chosen. It's not in a
particulary strategic spot on our board, yet when "Exchange" was
chosen, it gave us 3 in a row. (We haven't had any since, however.)
Our choice wasn't entirely random, yet it wasn't strategic, either.

By the way, the couple of times I have been in a bingo parlor, I've
noticed that those who have some system, or some logical way of
playing, don't seem to win any more often than those who rely purely
on chance.
