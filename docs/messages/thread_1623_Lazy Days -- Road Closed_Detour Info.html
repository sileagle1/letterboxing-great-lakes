Lazy Days -- Road Closed/Detour Info

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-07-21 09:39:09 UTC
I was out at Muskegon State Park yesterday doing a little last-
minute letterbox stuff before the Lazy Days Gathering on Saturday.
The good thing is that the trails there are still as gorgeous as
ever and the lake is crystal clean -- it even had that wonderful
blue-green tropical look to it yesterday. I hiked for a little over
3 hours and never ran into another person on the trail, although I
did see lots of wildlife, lots of swans in the Muskegon Lake area --
one lonely swan in particular interested me, and I thought I even
saw traces of a bear up in the sand dunes! I didn't see any poison
ivy at all and there were virtually no bugs (could change by
Saturday though, so bring bug spray just in case).

The bad thing is that the road to get to the park is closed due to
bridge work. There is a posted detour, but only 1-2 of the detour
signs mention this is the way to the State Park. You will need to
follow the signs for the Ruddiman Ave. detour. (Ruddiman turns into
Memorial Drive which may be the road named in directions you have
for the park).

So, in short... coming off U.S. 31 north or south, take the M-120
(North Muskegon) exit -- this is signed for the State Park. Head
west to Whitehall Road. At Whitehall Road head north. Normally you
would then turn onto Ruddiman, but instead you are going to continue
north to Giles Rd. At Giles, follow the detour signs and head
west. Take this to Buys Rd., where you will follow the detour signs
and go south. Buys Rd. ends at Ruddiman/Memorial Drive (actually
this is the point where the name changes). You are now back on
course and should head west to the park. It is a couple of miles
from this point to the Snug Harbor entrance -- if the road ends and
you are staring at Lake Michigan, you've passed Snug Harbor -- turns
around and come back about 1/4 mile.

This detour also turns the "4 miles back to restaurants and grocery
store" to about 10 miles back, so if you were planning to grab a
bite at one of the local places, you may want to rethink and pack a
picnic.

The forecast is looking good for Saturday -- sunny to partly
cloudy. You should be able to catch a splendid sunset while there --
who knows you may even see a mink while checking out the sunset!
And there is an awesome view of the lighthouse from here, so bring
your camera!

If you are planning to attend and have not already, please drop me
an e-mail to let me know you will be coming so I have an idea how
many folks to expect and we will know who to wait for before hitting
the trail as this is primary a "go look for boxes" kind of day and
we will only be at the park for a short time before we get going in
the morning. Alternately, I will have my cell phone on me while we
are hiking and I get pretty good reception there. If you show up
later in the day and are looking to meet up with us, you can give me
a ring (e-mail me privately for the number if you think this may be
the case for you).
