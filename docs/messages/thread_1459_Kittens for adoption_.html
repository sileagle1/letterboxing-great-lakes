Last week we were on vacation and went camping at High Cliff State Park
in WI. We made it a letterboxing trip as well. Some of the boxes we
found were:
EAA in Oshkosh
Badger Park in Peshtigo (also visited the Peshtigo Fire Museum)
Red Arrow Park in Marinette
Henes Park Series in Menominee
Mountain Bay Trail in Howard
Three Little Pigs in Green Bay
High Cliff Letterbox Series
Home is Where the Heart is WI in Greenleaf
UWGB Series
Kewaunee Harbor Lighthouse Letterbox
Algoma Pierhead Lighthouse Letterbox
LaSalle Letterbox
Sherwood Point Lighthouse Letterbox #2
Door County Tradition
Sherwood Point Lighthouse-Cabot Point
Sturgeon Bay Shipping Canal Series

We had a great week and lots of fun on these hunts. The kittens are
"guarding" the Kewaunee Harbor Lighthouse Letterbox. At first sighting,
we thought it was one little gray kitten with white paws, but while
replacing the box after stamping in, there were at least 6 kittens who
all scampered back into the rocks. Very cute and not very old!

Thanks to all who have planted, replaced and maintained all these great
boxes. Our favorite by far was the Red Arrow Park boxes. We kicked off
the shoes and letterboxed barefoot! As a family with "young'ns", we've
decided the best way to get some of these boxes further from home is to
plan a camping trip. We had a great week, though the temps were a bit
warm and the mosquitos were out in full force. We didn't even see one
raccoon in the campground! Deer were plentiful and so were other flying
buggy things. The tornado sighted over Lake Winnebago heading straight
for High Cliff never phased us because we were up in Sturgeon Bay
completing the hunt for the Sturgeon Bay Shipping Canal Series when we
heard the weather warning. We drove back to camp in the rain, but
thankfully the tent doesn't leak and we had a good night sleep. The
most unusual thing we saw while boxing was the live bear trap in Henes
Park! This is not a rural park, so we were a bit surprised by that! At
least the kids can now say they have been to Michigan! And better yet,
letterboxed in Michigan!
