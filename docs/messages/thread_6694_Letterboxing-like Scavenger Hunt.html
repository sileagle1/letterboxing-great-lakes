Letterboxing-like Scavenger Hunt

From: Atom118 (Aatom118@gmail.com) | Date: 2010-07-14 11:09:18 UTC-05:00
This past weekend, my sister Fermont came to town to take me and Coffee
Beans on a fun adventure in the city. She had registered us for a
'Watson Adventure' scavenger hunt in the Field Museum, and it was AWESOME!

You can go to their website for more details
(http://watsonadventures.com/schedule_chicago.html), but basically, we
were given a set of clues to solve that required us to run around the
museum for a few hours while collecting clues. This hunt was one of
their 'adult' hunts which were meant to be more challenging, but we
found that our letterboxing skills were VERY helpful. While some of the
clues required simply reading a informational board...a few others
required seeing things that, while in plain sight, were not obvious
(sounds familiar, eh). Anyway, needless to say, we WON beating out the
nine other teams on that day, and we were actually the only ones who
were able to solve the murder.

We'd recommend it to any letterboxers, maybe even as a way to pull in
those extra family members/friends who aren't so keen on finding
tupperware in the woods and use this as a compromise position. In
addition to Chicago, they are offered in several other cities, and they
do have some kid-friendly ones (Like the Harry Potter one at the Art
Institute).

Atom118 of the Atomic Beans
