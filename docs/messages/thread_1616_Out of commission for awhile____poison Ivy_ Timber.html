Out of commission for awhile....poison Ivy, Timber Rattlers & West Nile!

From: (col.one923@spwl.net) | Date: 2005-07-19 23:22:42 UTC-05:00
Mr. Puddlejumper and I both encountered poison ivy somewhere along the
way recently. Thankfully the kids did not. I must have unknowingly
done enough of the right things to keep it from spreading, but I only
had one little spot that is healing. Mr. Puddlejumper was at urgent
care last night and again tonight. It wasn't until tonight that they
diagnosed it as poison Ivy rash. He has a nasty rash on his lower leg,
but since yesterday has been spreading to other areas on his legs, arms
and torso. He thinks even his ears are affected. We are hoping the
prednisone will stop it in it's tracks, otherwise he is in for a full
body rash. There is no way he got poison Ivy oil all over his body, so
we are thinking it is a systemic reaction because it all started with a
scratch, that he scratched, it opened, became infected and it just
seemed to spread like wildfire after that. I really hope the meds he is
on is adequate. If anyone knows differently please let us know. He is
on an antibiotic and then also the Prednisone.

We are going to be out of commission for awhile, partially because Mr.
Puddlejumper needs time to heal, but also because we are now very wary
of getting back out there. We had no idea that poison Ivy rash was
soooooo bad! Ironically, we had just been trying to educate ourselves
better about poison Ivy and had been reading this website,
http://www.poison-ivy.org/index.htm There is some scary looking photos
and stories on that site and Mr. Puddlejumper could rival some of those
photos.

Obviously, we don't know how to identify it even after looking at all of
those photos. (that might be a good idea for a workshop at the WI
gathering this fall if anyone has any knowledge on this to help us who
are ignorant - the dangers of the "wild") I guess we are just really
concerned because we have 3 very active kids and I'm so glad they were
not the ones to come home with the poison ivy.......this time!

Mr. Puddlejumper's mom is obviously very concerned about our hiking
activities now and she began to ask more questions about this
letterboxing hobby of ours. Mr. Puddlejumper"s dad decided to pipe in
and tell us that we also need to be careful of the "timber rattlers"
people have seen in the Devil's Lake area. He said they are found in
the rocks. Is this true?!!! He said "you'll be fine if you stay on the
marked trail", to which we both told him that letterboxing often takes
us off the trail. I personally am officially scared to go letterboxing
now! It is like all of my letterboxing ambitions have come to a
screeching halt. (found info on timber
rattlesnakes...http://www.dnr.state.wi.us/org/land/er/factsheets/herps/timber.htm)
Is that why there are so few letterboxes in the southwest corner of WI?
Just kidding. I read through that DNR info and it does help to easy the
fears a abit.

Then to top it all off, while we were at urgent care with Mr.
Puddlejumper tonight, the kids stayed with Grandma and Grandpa. At some
point in the evening the mosquitos started biting and Grandpa hustled
the kids inside right away telling them all about West Nile virus!
Needless to say, the kids are afraid now too and told us on the way home
that they don't want to ever go letterboxing again!

Now Grandpa isn't normally an alarmist, in fact he is an old northern
Minn. farm boy himself as well as a jet fighter pilot retiree. Nothing
seems to phase him much. Yet we know he really dislikes snakes.
Mosquitos? I don't know, I thought that was the Minn. state bird?

Does anyone have any words of wisdom? Encouragement? Sympathy? How do
we get the kids back out on the trail?

Mrs. Puddlejumper

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2005-07-19 23:42:46 UTC-05:00
Oh my! Sounds just like the type of reaction Martini Man has had. We got
our first dose of P.I. last April after boxing in Maryland. I was the one
who got it, but it wasn't TOO bad. We both got poison oak this fall when
boxing in California. Again, mine wasn't too bad, but MM's arm looked
horrible. He experienced another outbreak after the Great Lakes Gathering
last month. Luckily none has been bad enough to warrant a doctor visit, but
it is horrible looking and decidedly itchy. He tried something BindleBabe
recommended during this last outbreak and it seemed to relieve some of his
suffering.

As far as some of your other concerns..... Stay tuned for a box we are
hiding in your area this Sunday!!

I sincerely hope Mr. P. recovers and that the whole family is able to take
this in stride and will get out there boxing again soon. Heat, poison ivy,
poison oak, snakes, ticks - none of this is as bad to us as NO BOXING!
We've decided to continue to take our chances as we pursue hunting the
elusive boxes....

Wisconsin Hiker

P.S. The idea of a workshop on PI is interesting. If you, or someone else,
wants to give a little talk, show some photos of PI, recommend medication,
etc. that would be great. At a minimum, perhaps we could have a photo
contest to determine who has had the worst outbreak!

I don't know what we'd do if all those great Puddlejumpers boxes

From: Angi (ShutterflyIL@aol.com) | Date: 2005-07-20 19:56:37 UTC
First off, good luck with the poison ivy. I hope he gets well soon.

As for the timber rattlesnakes...yes, they can be found in Devil's
Lake. I was hesitant to place a letterbox there because of all the
children who letterbox in our area. I didn't want them to reach a
little hand into a dark hole and get bitten. If I do get up there again
sometime soon and place one, you better believe there will be huge
warnings in the clues.
If you want more information about the snakes in Devil's Lake, go to
this website:
http://www.devilslakewisconsin.com/snakes.html
The place really is beautiful and I encourage everyone to visit at one
time or another, but just as with everything, you have to be careful.
My father used to camp there when he was younger and I asked him about
the snakes. He said he mainly saw them in the early morning and late at
night; usually when they are sunning themselves. I suppose it is good
that you are able to hear these snakes before you get too close. :)
Oh yeah, and if you go in the water...you'll love me for this
one...remember about swimmer's itch! My dad got it a few years ago when
we were canoeing there and his lower leg/ankle area had bumps for
months. You can read about that at their website also.

There are dangers all over the place when you step out your door. Don't
let that keep you inside though. My dad and I were just joking that if
you try to keep yourself cooped up indoors to avoid everything else,
you'll probably die of carbon monoxide posioning. Ah, the irony of it
all.

Shutterfly - who really isn't as dark as she sounds...lol
