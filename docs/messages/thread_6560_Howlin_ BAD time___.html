Howlin' BAD time...

From: tiptoetonto (tiptoetonto@yahoo.com) | Date: 2010-04-26 23:16:09 UTC
I have been stuck inside for the last three days...(heaven forbid!) Tonto had to work in the city today. Our son (a non-letterboxer) had the day off and it was a beautiful day....hmmm wonder if I could talk him into taking Kemosabe (our letterboxing golden retriever) out for a run and nab a quick LB with me. He was so bored that he agreed.

The box was off a bike trail in a Cook County Forest Preserve that is close to my work. Couldn't be that hard. I figured the bike trail would keep Kemosabe somewhat dry...The clues soon led us off trail along a creek. We had no problem finding the box - quick and simple...and a first finders prize to boot. Only my second box without Tonto...I missed him.

We decided to keep following the creek as the dog was already a muddy mess, and take a gander to see if any morels had popped up. Heck, it was an absolutely gorgeous day..why not? (In hind sight...maybe getting back on the bike path would've been a better option????)

We hiked a good mile following the creek following a rough old dirt trail- no morels - but I did score a good top of a skull and partial jaw bone...we decided to cut back through a prairie that the creek encircled that eventually would lead back to the car. We were in the middle of the prairie when all of a sudden a blood-curdling shriek came about 30 yards to our left. We stopped dead in our tracks to see a coyote starring at us. We decided to just keep going, although at a little quicker pace. Thankfully, we were obeying the "dog on leash" rule ...not allowing Kemosabe to run over to see if he wanted to play! It was obvious this coyote was NOT happy that a muddy dog and his people were cutting through his prairie.
Within minutes, we heard a sound I NEVER want to hear at that close range again! Our coyote was not alone! We hardly had time to go ten steps when we could see about eight coyote all howling, screeching, pacing, agitated and advancing closer toward us. "Mom," lets get the "____" out of here! My son shouted, as he went into a full sprint in the direction of the car.
"RUN, Mom, RUN!!!" he yelled over his shoulder. The fear in his voice instantly sprung open the adrenaline valve in my own body.
My little feet have never moved through soaking wet prairie ruts so fast...all the while with a crazy dog pulling my arm sockets out and my letterboxing bag flying all about! It was a good two blocks to our car. As soon as we were in sight of the car the howling stopped and the coyotes backed off from their frenzy, but my heart is still pounding! ...But, the adventure did cure the boredom our son was experiencing...

In all my years of hiking, I've heard coyote and certainly have seen several off in the distance, but never this close, or this many in the middle of the day making this kind of racket. All I can figure is that they must have a den of new pups...it is that time of year. So be aware of the critters out there parenting their young'ins this spring - especially those of you that letterbox with dogs!
Tonto came home and asked how our day went...and why the dog was panting so hard. I told him that I don't believe our son will go out boxing with us for a while...it was the scariest letterboxing moment I've had...and Tonto missed it!
Tiptoe...who is still panting...but grateful not to have ended up in a dog fight!
