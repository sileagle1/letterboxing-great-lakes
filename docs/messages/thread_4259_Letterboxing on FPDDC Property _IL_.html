From: duncan53211 (duncan53211@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-07-19 23:31:10 UTC
Here's the post...


The FPDDC is beginning a letterbox program and I have placed two boxes
to get things started. I recently placed the clues on the
letterboxing.org website, but would appreciate some assistance. We are
trying to catalog all of the current letterboxes on our property and
have their owners apply for approval. We are doing this to help ensure
they are following all of our rules and regulations, while not
disturbing any sensitive natural areas. Towards that I was hoping to
get our official Letterboxing Rules and Regulations information posted
on letterboxing.org and contact all owners who have placed boxes in
DuPage County, IL to ensure compliance. Please let me know if you can
help me with either of these matters. I hope that we can continue to
help letterboxing grow in DuPage County and give people another way to
enjoy visiting our beautiful forest preserves. Thanks for your time and
consideration, it is appreciated.



Dan Hebreard

Forest Preserve District of DuPage County

Senior Ranger

East Sector

580-7013

RE: [LbGLK] Re: Fwd: [LbNA] Letterboxing on FPDDC Property (IL)

From: xxxxxxxx (BrighidFarm@comcast.net) | Date: 2007-07-20 20:07:10 UTC-05:00
Possibly because of the heavyness of some of the rules, they haven't gotten
anywhere near the permit applications that they thought they'd be getting,
so they decided to do something on their own to stir up both interest and
adherence to the rules? I can certainly understand where they're coming
from with the rules as far as wanting to know where the letterbox is going
to be placed so that no fragile environments are harmed, like they sometimes
are out in Kane County, but some of the other rules are a bit much.
Especially all the wording that has to be on or in the letterbox besides
just putting the permit on the box. It just kinda takes the "art" out of
boxing, to me. And there are too many other places available to put boxes
other than DuPage County Forest Preserves. There's nothing "special" really
about DuPage forest preserves that the same stuff can't be found anyplace
else around. I think the rules came about mainly as a geocaching problem.
There were probably 100 caches in each preserve vs 1 letterbox, and a lot
more "tearing up" of the environment is risked with geocaching because they
don't have the clues like letterboxing does.

Okay, I know With a Cherry on Top found the York Woods box...has
anyone else tried and would like to comment?

I went there today to find it. Found the landmark/hiding place he
quotes in his clues...a good 50-100 feet off the trail...with no
apparent way to get to it except to trample all the foliage,
including poison ivy! Am I missing something here? That seems
awfully contradictory of their mission, to protect the sensitive
natural areas, not to mention the risk of the PI. Needless to say, I
took a pass on finding his box. I sent a Contact the Placer to
him...I'll be curious to see his response.

Another rather ironic point...I didn't realize it until after I got
home...I missed the fact that Nature Nerd has a box in the same
preserve...and she has noted at the bottom of her clues that she has
a DuPage permit. Her clues even follow the same route and use his
hiding place as one of her landmarks.

From: xxxxxxxx (BrighidFarm@comcast.net) | Date: 2007-07-25 22:12:11 UTC-05:00
I found that box yesterday morning. Yes, I made my way thru to it. It was
quite badly hidden and no camouflage on the box whatsoever. Hobby Lobby
stamp -- the exact same stamp the placer of the Behrens Park one in Elmhurst
used, so if you have that one, then you have the York Woods one. :-) I also
went looking for the other box yesterday morning -- the one at Oldfield
Oaks -- and all I found was an empty container -- no stamp or logbook.

It's kind of a shame because they had the box labelled as an "Official
educational letterbox." Well, if educational means a store bought stamp
with no effort put into hiding or camouflage and making a "social trail"
thru poison ivy...........then I guess the box is "educational." I hope new
boxers don't come away thinking this is all there can be to letterboxing
because it CAN be so much more creative and imaginative.

I found Nature Nerd's box to be a much better idea as far as "educating" new
boxers about letterboxing.
