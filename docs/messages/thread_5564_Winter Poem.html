Winter Poem

From: Carol (cjosada@hotmail.com) | Date: 2009-01-19 19:18:03 UTC

It's winter in Chicago
And the gentle breezes blow,
70 miles per hour at 52 below!
Oh, how I love Chicago
When the snow's up to your butt;
You take a breath of winter air
And your nose is frozen shut.
Yes, the weather here is wonderful,
I guess I'll hang around.
I could never leave Chicago,
'Cause I'm frozen to the ground.

....and I'm just hoping that my letterboxes aren't frozen to the ground
and damaged. This has certainly been a harsh winter in the Chicagoland
area. ---Beetle
