Since we are doing intros again, we just decided to
repost our original intro. Some things have changed
our F count is no longer 200 and we are married now!
Things are finally starting to slow down again and
we're trying to get all of our letterboxing life back
in order! Anyway, here's the "throw back"
introduction.


Hi! We're John and Sara, otherwise known as Leapin'
Lizards.
It was great to meet those who made it to the
Wisconsin gathering and we hope to meet more fellow
letterboxers at the Great Lakes gathering this summer.
We thought we would take a minute to introduce
ourselves and share how we got into this crazy thing
called letterboxing. . .

Sara: I have created a monster! About a year and a
half ago, I came across a blurb about letterboxing in
an outdoor travel magazine. So I asked John what he
thought and he said...

John: Cool! Let's try it!

Sara: We started letterboxing in January 2003 and
since then we've seen lots and lots of great places.
Places which I'd personally love to stay and visit the
non-letterboxing points of interest, as well, to which
John always assures me, "We'll come back." But as
time goes by, and we have yet to return, I know it's
just a line . . . so typical of a guy.

John: Well, if Sara's legs weren't so short, we'd get
to the boxes faster and have time to visit the other
places.

Sara: Like in Michigan! So many cute towns and shops!

John: So many letterboxes! The makers of Advil
really need to thank Spring Chick for the amount of
Advil we purchased during our trip.

Sara: But it was worth every sand dune we had to
climb! We must have walked 70 miles during those five
days.

John: But really, letterboxing has been a great way
for us to spend time together, take walks together....

Sara: I'm still convinced he only holds my hand to
drag me along...

John: ...and teach us patience....like waiting for
Sara.

Sara: . . . and gain endurance, like when John makes
me walk in sub-zero temperatures into straight-line
winds off of Lake Michigan.

John: And criminal law, like when were stopped by
Missouri police for a random drug check of our vehicle
while driving home from a letterboxing trip. "Really,
officer we're just looking for boxes with stamps in
them. " Thank goodness we had the pictures to prove
it!

Sara: We recently marked our 200th find (and retired
our first signature stamp). Currently, we've planted
16 letterboxes in 6 states and released 5
hitchhikers.

John: We have several series ready to be planted,
too...

Sara: Yes, he has close to 100 boxes ready to be
planted. He tells me that he has carved all of the
stamps, but I'm convinced he has his art students
carving them...Sometimes when he's sleeping, he yell's
out, "You call that a stamp! I've seen better stamps
carved out of potatoes by kindergartners!"

John: I would never let that happen in my sweatshop,
er, classroom.

Sara: Like I said, I've created a monster. I've now
got him on a 12-step program.

John: Yes, I'm on step 10 at 340 degrees.


Happy boxing--

Leapin' Lizards :-)
