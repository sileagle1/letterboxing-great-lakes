I am so excited you asked the 2007 goals question SpringChick!

(standing up and reading....)

My name is Trailtracker and I am a letterboxing addict. (Is that how we
are supposed to start??) :) I cannot stop and I have no intention of
stopping. I only intend on buying more ink and more PZ Kut!

Over the past 3 days I have accomplished some of my goals, but have more
to go. One of my goals is to get to P100 and F1000. I'm not sure I will
get there, but I'm gonna give it a try! I have another 30 planted
boxes that I have not posted on AQ that are only on letterboxing.org. I
am hoping to work on that this weekend from my igloo here in Ohio?!

I also am signing up for Scout Dog Camp so that my new German
Shorthaired Pointer learns first of all, how to put his hunting
instincts on quiet mode while we hike and hunt for letterboxes. Thanks
Sirius Star Gazer and Scoutdogs for sharing so much about this camp in
MI!!! (I'm coming back to my homeland!!) :)

But the number one thing I wanted to do was a letterbox asking for
support for our troops! I had a really hard time trying to figure out
how to do this but finally got it posted as a PT this morning!!! I have
had 6 hits already and I haven't even turned the picture upright on it
yet?! lol

My son serves in the 82nd Airborne and he just deployed to Afghanistan
for the next year. I want to make sure these soldiers know that they
have support from Americans at home.

This PT has an option where you can get the stamp via snail mail! The
name of the PT is POST CARDS FOR THE 82ND AIRBORNE. Here is the link
http://www.atlasquest.com/lboxes/clue/index.html?gBoxId=31650

(Stay tuned as Granny Butterfly is doing a similar postcard PT box as
well!! Both her grandson and his wife are serving in Afghanistan as well!!)

Last but not least, I am trying to reach out to newer letterboxers to
help them get signed up for events, message boards or whatever else they
need. When I first started, there was almost NO ONE in many counties
around that letterboxed. Some boxes were abandoned and/or they never
returned my emails. I even skipped picking up some HH because I just
had no clue what they were when I first started?! LOL I just didn't
know?! I want to make it better for others. There is a lot of brand new
talent as this hobby is really expanding. In Ohio, the number of
letterboxes has increased x 6 times since I first started! Happy,
happy! :)

Back to carving and posting!
Trailtracker

From: Tammy (rockhoppinmama@suddenlink.net) | Date: 2007-02-11 12:09:08 UTC-05:00
I do not remember much about when my brother was in Vietnam. I was very
young. He went to boot camp and they were sending him straight to Nam. He
wasn't going to get to see us before he went. He was upset because he knew
the chances of him coming home alive were slim. He was a Marine and headed
for the front lines. He went AWOL to see us for the weekend. It must have
been common because they didn't get too upset about it. I was afraid of him
when he came in the door because he was in uniform and I didn't recognize
him. I have a few of his letters and a poem he wrote about having a bullet
in his chest and seeing a child without a leg. Heartbreaking. His last
letter to us said goodbye to us. He knew he was not coming home. Before we
got the letter someone in his platoon set up a chain reaction bomb and the
whole group was killed. My mother told me once that when he was in Nam I
went around telling everyone "Jim Nam." I also remember that the tv was on
all the time with war news. I don't know if it was a local channel or a
cable channel but I remember seeing it on. We didn't have many channels
then so it was probably local.

My daddy didn't have to see combat in WWII but his job was very dangerous.
He was in Hawaii. His job was to put the bombers on the planes or whatever
they are called. They had to be timed so that they would go off without
hitting the propellers or something. He said they made him test fly each
one because if it wasn't right the plane would blow up and they didn't want
to die if he made a mistake. He didn't talk too much about the service. He
was able to go to college and get a two year degree. That was a huge thing
for a farmer's son in the 40's. He was also part of the Civilian
Conservation Corps. I would love to do a series of boxes about the CCC. He
was involved in creating Watoga State Park in WV. He talked a lot about
making a hand made dam and bridges etc. He graduated high school at 16 so
he was too young to go straight into the service. CCC gave him a good job
until he was old enough to go to the Navy.

My grandfathers were all in wars. I've gone back to great great great great
grandfather so far. I find it quite interesting. I am the youngest of nine
children so I depend on my siblings' memories more than mine since I was too
young to remember the stories my grandmothers told. Both my grandfathers
were gone before I was born.

Anyway, too much information I'm sure.

Tammy
Rock Hoppin' Mama
