I used this information yesterday that I found on the LBNA list. It
worked - the key is clicking the word Cached - I use google search
engine. I was looking for a box in Southeast MI and when I clicked
the cached link, I got the name of the box I was looking for. Then I

Thank you to Larry for the info:

"Boxers,

Here is what I do for just such an unfortunate event. In Internet
Explorer you can add each of your favorite regions (I wouldn't
suggest too many) as a favorite place. When the pop up window comes
up, you can check the box that says "Make available offline". Then
you'll be able to check the "Customize" button". On the next pop up
window, you can tell it how many pages deep you want the favorite
place to save. I suggest keeping it at one, although that won't
get you every clue.

Now, you have the clues available to you (as of the point in time
when you did this) for viewing when the site isn't available. You
can synchronize the offline pages periodically under the Tools menu.
This doesn't work for search results, but it will work when you view
a specific region. It also will not work with Atlasquest clues.

If you really want to be prepared, you can do this on your laptop and
bring your laptop with you just in case you find yourself in an area
and want to do a box for which you didn't prepare by printing out
the clues in advance.

Larry"
