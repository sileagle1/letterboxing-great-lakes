From: Merlin and Ardea (merlinandardea@sbcglobal.net) | Date: 2007-10-17 14:31:51 UTC
We've also found camo tape (Duck Brand) at Menard's (building
supplies) and some hardware stores in the paint department with the
other colors of duct tape. Sporting goods stores often carry a cloth
type of camo tape (in the bow hunting section) which would be
preferable but for the fact it's very expensive.
It's also good to remember the three things camouflage should
accomplish: match the COLOR, cover any SHINE, and breakup the SHAPE.

Re: Camo tape?

From: azobox (jparkerg@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-10-17 17:52:57 UTC
I've had a couple of types.
The best we we've used we got at Gander Mtn, but they don't seem to
carry it any more.
It was cloth/fabric and was *not* shiny. It was available in several
'designs' -- i.e. hardwood forest, evergreens, and some kind of mixed
woods. Each was a bit different in its color. The main benefits were
that it was non-relective and was flexible around box contours. The
fabric allowed it to conform to box curves.

I've searched for something similar but without success. The best
substitute I've found was also at Gander Mtn and it's a duct-type
tape: cloth backed and 'tearable' but still reflective.

Good luck,

azobox

From: xxxxxxxx (BrighidFarm@comcast.net) | Date: 2007-10-18 12:12:59 UTC-05:00
I love the tape from Walmart too. It sticks really well. At my Walmart,
it's in the paint section with the other duck tapes. It's available all
year round but every so often someone (like me) will go in and buy them out,
so there won't be anymore there until they get in a new shipment.

After taping the box and before releasing it into the wild, I grind some dry
dirt into the tape on the sides & top of the box. That's because the stuff
at my Walmart does have a little bit of a shine to it, not much tho, and
rubbing some dry dirt or sand into it takes away that bit of shine.
