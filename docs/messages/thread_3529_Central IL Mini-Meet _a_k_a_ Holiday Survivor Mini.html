Central IL Mini-Meet (a.k.a. Holiday Survivor Mini-Meet)

From: lcoil79 (lcoil79@insightbb.com) | Date: 2007-01-06 03:59:52 UTC
OK, possible change of venue for the Holiday Survivor Mini-Meet. We
found out today that Lincoln Square Mall has a $25 application fee and
requires groups to carry their own insurance for the event. And
judging by the response we've gotten, we just might have enough people
that we won't be able to just blend in as a random group of people.

So we are looking at the auditorium of the Public Library right next
door to Lincoln Square. Which will be free and also allow us to bring
in food (something we probably can't do at LS and still remain
anonymous). However, this location isn't without it's own faults. We
are planning a raffle of prizes (a few people have already expressed
an interest in donating prizes), the proceeds of which we plan to
donate to the letterboxing.org website since they have had such an
increase in their costs and don't charge us for the service they
provide. The problem comes in that as part of being a free resource,
the library won't allow us to do any fundraising or money collecting
on-site, but they will allow us to raffle off the prizes at the
library, just as long as no money changes hands while there. So we
would have to figure a process for selling tickets to the raffle, but
not break this rule. The other possible issue is that the event would
have to be open to the public, so anyone could literally come in off
the street and come into the auditorium. This means non-letterboxers
could come in and discover what we are doing, and not everyone in our
area would respect the boxes hidden in town if they knew they were
there. In fact, quite a few would go out with the express purpose of
destroying the boxes.

Unfortunately this is kinda last minute here (this room just opened
back up a few days ago for 1/21/07 due to a cancellation) so I plan on
reserving it tomorrow first thing, but I would also like to get any
input I can on this, feelings or thoughts. One of the other things we
need is a rough headcount, the room can hold up to 80, but if there
are too few people at the event, they reserve the right to move us to
a much smaller room (capacity of 14) or cancel our meeting. I'm going
to post this event over at AtlasQuest (www.atlasquest.com) and would
like everyon planning on coming to sign up and let us know how many
are coming in your party. Also, anyone wanting any more info about
the raffle is free to email me at lcoil79@insightbb.com since Kimono
Girl has given me the task of arranging it. Thanks for your help and
insights.

Flying Dragon
