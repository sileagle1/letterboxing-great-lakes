Re: GLk 2010 Preliminary Report - Where were you at 8 pm Saturday...

From: be_a_badger (schuppner@uwalumni.com) | Date: 2010-06-09 02:39:59 UTC
We were back at the hotel, in the middle of supper, when we heard the tornado warning announced on the radio and then heard the sirens. We gathered a few things, including the rest of our little elf's supper and made our way to an appropriate ground floor location. As other boxers arrived, Beetle introduced us to her elusive Mr. Beetle and we heard about the different paths taken by Beetle and her family during the day.

One of The Christmas Elves managed to box without getting muddy. Her off-roading stroller made boxing a breeze. She popped just a handful of wheelies for the biggest and squishiest puddles and we engaged the manual vertical feature only a few times to hop her over logs. Our huge thanks again to TJ_Mich for their stroller recommendation.

While we hoped to spend Sunday polishing off a few more coins in the park, the extra rain didn't bode well for a second day of stroller friendly trail conditions, so we headed for home a day early.

We echo TJ_Mich's thanks for the hotel block. We really appreciated that convenience. We liked the booklet style for the clues and including the maps. Thanks to all for planning, carving, planting, arranging, supporting, attending, etc.

The Christmas Elves

From: Leapin (emptyenergy1@yahoo.com) | Date: 2010-06-09 19:19:36 UTC-07:00
At roughly 8:00, Leapin' Lizards and The Dragon were trying to finish the last two boxes of the coin operated series. Sara and Dragon headed back to the car as the rain poured down and John replaced the last box. Sara handed John the lightning rod...er, umbrella to hold. Thanks.:) We started running to the car, then made it back to the campsite to find Dragon's tent tipped on its side. He had "heavy stuff in it, but didn't stake it down." Window was down, so water was inside. LL frantically tried to find shower gear and clothing in their tent as the sirens wailed from what seemed like all directions. Made it to the showers and then tried to figure out where the tornadoes where going through by listening to the radio and checking the gps.
