the secret is getting out - Girl Scouts and letterboxing

From: Kathi LK Krankoski (krank@ameritech.net) | Date: 2005-11-09 10:58:04 UTC-06:00
The entire LB community is going to have to be on its toes with the release
of the new Letterboxing and Geocaching Interest Project Patch by GSUSA. I
know the council I am affiliated with (DuPage) is planning a workshop for
girls in April. I and several of the other local boxing GS volunteers have
offered to be involved just because we feel it is so important that the
girls completely understand the "rules"/conventions/etiquette/whatever you
want to call it, to keep the activity as under the radar as much as
possible. It scared me a bit when I first heard about the workshop (long
story, because of council mergers, we are working with a bunch of new people
with new programs, but I digress) because they just "heard about it" and
decided to offer a workshop. No one affiliated with planning or leading the
workshop has ever boxed before!!! I immediately volunteered - and blew them
away when I started explaining stuff they had no idea of. (Their idea was
to have them make and hide a box right away, without even finding or even
seeing what one looks like.)



I understand the Illinois Crossroads Council has had an active Lb training
for leaders for a while now, lead by Beetle. I know she is doing a good
job, because I have been in contact with several of her trainees who are now
active responsible boxers (I know you guys are out there reading this - you
know who you are!).



And before anyone panics about Girl Scouts hitting the letterboxing trails
(I know there was a lot of GS bashing on the big list when I first started,
which is why I am so adamant about teaching the "proper" way to box), I want
to point out that there are a lot of very responsible Scout boxers out there
- Funkey Munkey, Kookaburra, Splash, Squeek just to name a few on my far
from all-inclusive list!



--klk/FungusWoman
