Funny you did not find any HH's - we went out this week specifically
to send one on its way and ended up with another in our possession,
LOL! The little Cat Herders thought this was VERY funny!

Speaking of checking on boxes (Dragon's email) - Wisconsin Hiker - I
was going to do a quick check on the Winter Fun #1 since we were out
"hiking" there in the snow, slush and mud at another time this week
and were going right by it - but the location was so nicely covered
with snow, any checking would have been too obvious, LOL! I did show
my husband the spot where we did finally find it (he was not with on
that triumphant day, LOL) when we were out there again on
Saturday. I would have checked again then, but there were too many
muggles - everyone just had to be out when it was so beautiful
yesterday, LOL! I would assume it was safe and sound since there was
absolutely no disturbance in the snow on it or around it.

Hiking in a familiar place before things green up certainly gives a
different perspective on how clues might be seen! For the Winter Fun
#1, it was so obvious where the clues were sending you vs when we
started looking for it last summer and all the foliage and plants
were in place!

We went looking for a box that has eluded us (3 times and yet we know
of one other letterboxer that could not find it and someone else
found it afterwards, LOL). The terrain was totally different in the
winter (the box was planted in the early spring) than when we had
been looking in the summer! However, there were numerous trees that
were down (storms and looked like some park planned cuttings) and we
STILL could not find that box! My husband says we are not looking
for it anymore, LOL - it is driving him buggy that we cannot find it!

Anyway, happy Letterboxing and enjoy slogging through the mud - I
know my son did, heehee!

The Cat Herders
