From: Doodle & Deedle Bug (doodle_n_deedle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-04-07 07:38:32 UTC-07:00
We have a sponge that we keep in a dollar-store soap
container (had to cut the sponge a little so it would
fit). Before we head out, we wet the sponge (just
plain water), and then we can clean stamps on the
trail, and since it's a hinged soap container, it's
easier for us to use than a sponge in a plastic
baggie. We also bring paper towels to then blot the
stamp dry after we've pressed in into the sponge a few
times.

Doodle Bug.

Re: [LbGLK] New to stamping too :) cleaning stamps?

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2005-04-08 05:59:14 UTC-04:00
Yes this would be true if using alcohol or lanolin based baby wipes, but most of the wipes I have seen are alcohol-free. Wouldn't want to be wiping a little one's behind with alcohol. I guess I am learning something here because I have been a rubber stamper for several years now and have always been taught that baby wipes are ideal for quick stamp clean-ups.

One thing to keep in mind about cleaning hand-carved stamps versus store-bought stamps is that they tend to be much more delicate. I often use an old toothbrush for cleaning my art stamps, especially after some of the pigment inks that can almost be kind of gooey. But when cleaning hand-carved stamps, never use anything abrasive or a rough side-to-side cleaning motion as this can easily damage the carving.

I really like the sponge in the soap dish idea from Deedle -- Seems quick and not messy. I am stopping this afternoon at the local $$ store for a dish and a sponge and adding this to my pack. Thanks!

SpringChick
