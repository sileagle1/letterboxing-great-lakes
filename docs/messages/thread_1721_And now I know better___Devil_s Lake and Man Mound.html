And now I know better...Devil's Lake and Man Mound

From: Angi (ShutterflyIL@aol.com) | Date: 2005-08-17 00:29:54 UTC
I've been going back and forth for the past week or so with a stuffy
nose and sore throat. Yesterday I felt like my sinuses were all
stuffed up as well and I had a really bad headache. So what did I
propose my family and I do today? Go letterboxing in Devil's Lake!
Yeah, that sounds like the perfect plan!

If you have found the boxes placed there by Wisconsin Hiker, you know
what the trail is like to get to the boxes. Well, combine my cold and
already-feeling-nastiness with the steep climb and you get trouble.

I hadn't even made it to the first landmark in one of the clues,
about 50 to 100 feet up a bluff, when I started feeling sick. I
stopped to stretch my legs and started seeing nothing but white. I
quickly sat down on one of the rocks so that if I passed out, I
wouldn't go tumbling down the steps. While I sat there I felt like I
had to throw up, but tried my hardest not to because there was this
beautiful yellow caterpillar crawling on the rocks and I didn't want
to kill it with my breakfast. (You know I'm sick when I can't even
take a picture of the thing.)

I had to of sat there for 10 to 15 minutes, talking with my dad,
before I could get back up and go on. I was really upset that I was
now going back down the bluff trail and missing out on not one, but
three letterboxes, but that was better than going on and becoming
worse. I am stubborn, though, and still wish I would have pushed
myself to keep going. ;) By that time, I probably looked like I made
the trek twice!

Still feeling sick, but determined, we went to find the Man Mound
letterbox location. I figured that it would be a lot easier on me,
with the paces being in the double-digits and all. I was right. I
found that one in good shape. It made me feel a lot better to have
found one letterbox.

I suppose I've learned my lesson. If I'm sick to begin with and feel
like I'm going to yak just being in the car, I probably shouldn't be
climbing the bluffs (or even small hills). I will be back,
though...those letterboxes will not go unfound by me again. After
all, I bought some Devil's Lake patches to add to one of my
letterboxing bags. If I don't find the boxes, the patches will taunt
me forever.

Shutterfly - who has climbed the bluffs many times before (even while
being ill once) and had no troubles...lol

Oh, I'm sorry you couldn't do the hike! We were pretty tired ourselves when
we planted the boxes since we had been boxing in Mirror Lake in the morning
and then spent 4-5 hours at Noah's Ark waterpark. It was a scorcher of a day
and it was still amazingly hot & humid 5pm - 8 pm when we planted the boxes.
Our niece (12) and nephew (9) were real troopers to keep going all the way
around the lake on those bluff trails after such a busy & tiring day. They
were daunted by the distance at first, but by the time we got halfway around
(and soaked our heads and refilled water bottles) they got their second wind
and clambered up the Balanced Rock trail like mountain goats. When we
finally finished it was getting darker, but we enjoyed a quick swim in the
lake before changing and heading across the ferry and finding the "I Believe
in Ferries" box on the way home.

I only know of one finder so far for the Devil's Lake boxes. They did one
box one day and a 2nd box the next day, but didn't have time to find the
3rd. Hot summer days are probably not the best for this hike!

Hope you're feeling better soon...

Wisconsin Hiker
