The feeling of being watched is an awarenesses most letterboxers
experience at one time or another.
There have been many discussions on this list over the years about how
to create diversions.
After all, if we *appear* to be doing something very expected or
normal for a particular place, we hardly stand out at all.

Artistamp and I almost always have a camera with us and very visibly
present. We can appear to be taking pictures of trees, flowers, bugs,
even eachother .
Other boxers assume a birdwatching persona, staring very intently at
who-knows-what? Pointing at a tree with obvious curiosity is part of
the role.

I planted a box in a cemetary once and had to return several times for
maintenance. I always had the camera. And even though I know that some
of the neighbors were watching me, I made a point of visiting several
of the sites and stones on each trip. I did not have any problems there.

Blend in.
