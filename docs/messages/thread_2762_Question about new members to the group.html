Re: Question about new members to the group

From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-07-11 13:39:11 UTC
All membership requests that mention anything even remotely
connected with letterboxing as being the reason they would like to
join are approved. Membership requests that are received without
any comments or indication of why they are interested in joining the
group are denied. This is a precaution against spammers and other
bots systematically joining the group. I suspect the reason their
membership was denied was lack of comments on the membership
request. Please have them submit their request again with a breif
comment related to letterboxing. Thanks!

SpringChick

Tiptoe & Tonto are SOOOO glad that we didn't have to be nominated to
join this group...We'd surely still be hitting the send button on
our continually denied application!

We've been out in the Falls Church, VA area for a quick visit with
family...Although we were only able to box two afternoons on the
Potomac, we (as usual) had some very wacky things happen!

We went alone on the first day...in the rain! Tonto packed the rain
ponchos - when Tiptoe put hers on she realized that it was NOT hers,
but one belonging to our 6' 2" son! Thank heavens for the bungie
cord that was comandeered to serve as a belt!

We hiked for miles enjoying the nice views of the
river...thankfully no one else was on the trails! We looked very
suspicious stamping in! Tiptoe squatting down while Tonto covered
her with his poncho - we didn't realize how it might have looked if
someone had come upon us out in the woods! They might not have
bought into the idea of... in this case... "function over form"!

Our guess is that we were the only ones foolish enough to splat
around in the mud and rain. We found all but the last box we were
looking for..that one (similar to the fate of the old "catapulted
cow") had the trail come to an abrupt end. With all the rain
they've had on the east coast the "small creek" had turned into a
mighty rushing river and washed out any possible way to get
across....Tiptoe was determined to go up creek and find a good place
to ford...Unfortunately, the "No Trespassing" and "Private Keep Out"
signs made Tonto yell, "Enough is enough!"...and added that she had
to face the defeat with a little more dignity than loudly cursing in
the woods. Besides, he reasoned that if she was caught...or
worse...drowned in the fast current, he wouldn't be able to explain
the bungie cord tied around her waist and a weighted "belly-pack"
filled with markers hidden underneath!
Being the good little wife that she is..she momentarily hesitated
before submitting to her good husband's wishes (Take note Doodle &
Deedle/Leapin' Lizards - secrets to 25 plus years of marriage!)...
Tonto says this unusual show of respect is rare!

She is still pouting, but safe and dry back in Illinois!

On Day two we took an assortment of nieces, nephews and friends out
for another afternoon of letterboxing along the Potomac. Beautiful
day and in search of some great boxes by well known carver, Scarab.
We couldn't wait to get out on the trail. After much lecturing on
the importance of keeping natural places unscathed, our 13 y/o
nephew had scurried down into a stream bed and was poking around
some rocks. His 9 y/o sister in quick formation behind him. He
screeched out, "Hey, I found a baby snake!" Before Tiptoe or his
mother could get him to retreat, he had grabbed a stick and the poor
little snake had quickly wrapped itself around the stick and was
hissing. Tonto had gone on ahead with another neice and was nowhere
to be found to help with the disipline!
As Tiptoe was exhorting him to put the snake back, he turned
swinging the dangling thing less than three inches from his sister's
face. "Stop!" Tiptoe yelled.
He raced up the bank begging to have his picture taken with his
prize...Tiptoe took one look at the cute little baby, gasped,
snapped a quick picture and calmly told him to put the snake down...
IMMEDIATELY!!
She was certain (and later the photo verified) that the cute little
baby snake was indeed a baby copperhead!

Tiptoe has even more gray hair than before!

One of the boxes had a clue to follow an unofficial trail. A park
volunteer had just finished closing off the unofficial trail with a
huge pile of debris. We returned later to try and skirt around the
pile. Tonto decided to take the male children over the pile while
Tiptoe stayed back with the females (sounds a little sexist?????)The
clue states to "pass through/under the fence"..Tonto, wanting to be
different (or not reading the clues as usual) thought the clue
said "have the fence go through you!" ..that's exactly what he did!
He was successful in finding the box and brought it back to the
female group to stamp in, but also came back with a nasty, bloody
gouge in his ankle from the fence. Yes, we hear ya...the girls
should've gone to fetch the box!

We cleaned it as best we could, put Neosporin on the "Flesh wound",
and covered it with the only bandaids we had ..."BARBIE" bandaids!
(These things have happened to him before and fortunately he was up
to date on his tetnus shot! By the next day the wound was so swollen
and infected that a doctor friend had to put him on antibiotics!)

Nonetheless, we did find all three of Scarab's terrific stamps
at "NMAI: Anishinaabe Art" letterbox!
Now, if only we can get up to Michigan to finish that darn 80 Days
Series!
Tiptoe & Tonto
