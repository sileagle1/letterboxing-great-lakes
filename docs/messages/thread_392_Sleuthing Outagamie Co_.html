Sleuthing Outagamie Co.

From: merlinandardea (rhesse34@hotmail.com) | Date: 2004-07-09 14:20:42 UTC
Some you may have noticed the new posting (NE,WI) on the LB site
for this series of LBs. We saw the question about it on this list
awhile back, did a bit of sleuthing ourselves to find out about it
and got to several of the boxes over the weekend.
I sent an email to the museum on Monday with our thanks and results
and got a reply saying they were pleased that "someone" was enjoying
the series. So, I volunteered "to place an ad" on the LB site.
The museum has put a lot of work into the series. The woman I
exchanged emails with said she got the idea when she was in England
and was taken along on an LB trip to Dartmoor (sigh!). The
little "passbook" is commercially printed, the clues are interesting,
the stamps depict something of the site and are made commercially
(they remind me of pictures of stamps I've seen from the UK), the
sites all have some historical and/or natural theme, the logbooks are
commercially made with embossed covers, and the boxes we found were
all ammo cans.
There were 12 LBs but one has been abandoned due to repeated
vandalism. Too bad, as the site, a stone arch bridge, is really
neat. If you try the series you should have a look at it anyway.
It's not far out of the way.
We really enjoyed it and hope to get back before too long to finish
the series.
Merlin
