Did someone say Polish food? OT

From: pell_lake_girl (mishiekins@prodigy.net) | Date: 2006-12-19 16:43:05 UTC
Last week I had to do a photo essay as an assignment, and I looked on Google as
instructed, and there were no decent pictures of a Polish buffet. Oh there were a few tiny
pictures, but mostly of banquet halls. Nothing that really conveys the splendor of it all. So
on Sunday my dear friend Wendy and I met up at Czerwone Jablusko, aka The Red Apple
on Milwaukee Ave. in Chicago.

http://redapplebuffet.com/

It was her birthday, and also Koliada -- the Slavic heathen celebration of mid-winter
[technically, koliada is Ukranian, and it's a whole cycle of winter rituals happening between
winter solstice and mid-January]. As usual, I ate so much it was all the calories I would
need for a month. I've gone there for Koliada before, but I never took pictures! So enjoy
and I hope no one is hungry.

It's a restaurant ...AND a laundromat
http://img.photobucket.com/albums/v80/mountaineer/Slavic/01RedApplesign.jpg

This is not just the Polish eagle...it is done in latch hook of all things
http://img.photobucket.com/albums/v80/mountaineer/Slavic/02Polishflag.jpg

We exchanged Christmas gifts and Wendy gave me a lovely handmade whisk broom she
bought on a trip to Tennessee this fall. Just as a side note, as I was waiting to pay before
we left, the funniest thing... the pretty blonde lady running the register admired the box
my gift came in. I opened it up and showed her the whisk broom Wendy gave me. She
picked up the broom to look at it and got a very wicked look on her face and started
flicking it like a priest with holy water. "oh, you are exorcist?" she inquired in her heavy
Polish accent. I started laughing and she just kept flicking the broom like she was blessing
me. "this is for boyfriend" *flick flick* "if it start to burn, LEAVE" *flick flick* I laughed so
hard I almost threw up all my lovely fried carbs.

Right next to the cashier, perfect for those impulse pierogi purchases
http://img.photobucket.com/albums/v80/mountaineer/Slavic/03pierogiestogo.jpg

Anyway.... after we opened our presents we still hadn't seen our waitress so we gave up on
our first course, zupy* (soup), which is usually rosl (chicken noodle soup made with
parsley) or grzybowa (creamy mushroom and noodle soup). That was ok though, there was
plenty to eat. As we were walking to the buffet, I said our motto: "Time to eat The Starchy
And Greasy Foods Of Our People." This Polish guy loading his plate at the buffet started
laughing.

This is just one of the three buffets loaded with food -- it goes around the corner and into
the party room at the back
http://img.photobucket.com/albums/v80/mountaineer/Slavic/04buffet.jpg

First, in Polish eating, there is the gelatinous course. Always need to have a gelatinous
course. No one knows why.

I have no idea, but I bet it is a part of some pig, because everything is pork-related here
http://img.photobucket.com/albums/v80/mountaineer/Slavic/07gelatinouscourse.jpg

Thus we evolved The Traditional Polish Winkie, which is far more appetizing. They do not
have winkie at The Red Apple, because if you haven't guessed, that's a sarcastic name for
a jello salad our grandmothers and aunts always served, not an actual dish from Poland.
However, the Red Apple does have other gelatinous, jello-related items.

We must have The Red Gelatinous Mass Of Our People
http://img.photobucket.com/albums/v80/mountaineer/Slavic/06buffetfromsalads.jpg

Except Polish jello is not slimy...it's CHEWY. You can cut it with a knife
http://img.photobucket.com/albums/v80/mountaineer/Slavic/07Wendyjellospoon.jpg

If you would like to see how we try to eat The Red Gelatinous Mass
http://www.veoh.com/videoDetails.html?v=e182774e2MMyzrn

Then usually we move on to salatki na zimno -- the salad course.
http://img.photobucket.com/albums/v80/mountaineer/Slavic/08saladcourse.jpg

This time I was not a good Polish girl. The only vegetables I ate were a few plain
cucumbers, and I forced those. No, this time I went directly to the GREASY FRIED CARBS!

Time for the pierogi course
http://img.photobucket.com/albums/v80/mountaineer/Slavic/09pierogiecourse.jpg

I love how the buffet has RED LIGHTS to make you feel like this is all so appetizing and
warm... hot fried carbs! yum yum! I had placki ziemniaczane (potato pancakes), and
racuszki (apple pancakes), nalesniki (cheese blintzes), all with apple sauce....

And then dania gorace -- the hot dishes, and the meat course. If you want some kind of
pig, a Polish buffet is the place to find it. They've got pig everything.... kapusta z kielbasa
(sauerkraut and Polish sausage), papryka faszerowana pomaranczowym (baked ham,
carved off the bone), cold ham slices, pork cutlets, pork this and pork that.

pork pork pork pork....
http://img.photobucket.com/albums/v80/mountaineer/Slavic/10porkcourse.jpg

Thank God the lady brought out MORE PORK because I was scared we would RUN OUT
http://img.photobucket.com/albums/v80/mountaineer/Slavic/10worker.jpg

That big ham on the end was so delectable I swear I could cut it with my FORK. I had a
ginourmous piece.
http://img.photobucket.com/albums/v80/mountaineer/Slavic/05buffetfromham.jpg

Well sometimes they do have more than pig products. The other big piece of meat in the
photo above is a giant roast beef. Since we arrived when they opened at 11:00 am, the
meat was fresh out of the ovens. They also had ryba pieczona w sosie cytrynowo
maslanym (baked fish in lemon butter sauce), and kaczka pieczona z jablkami (baked duck
with apples). And to encourage some kind of vitamin and nutrient ingestion they also have
jarzyny na goraco (hot veggies) -- ziemniaki (potatoes) of course, both gotowane (boiled,
with dill) and pur, or mashed, zielona fasolka (green beans) and kukurydza (corn -- on
the cob). Okay, wait, I did have some mashed potatoes. More starch! But at least it's not
fried.

And then they have the desserts...some fresh fruit to cut the grease...
http://img.photobucket.com/albums/v80/mountaineer/Slavic/11fruitsalad.jpg

Like most of the dishes, the bowl of chopped up fruit had a label on it. The label said:

Fruit Salad
VEGETARIAN

[just in case you were worried there was pork of some kind in it]

So that was helpful.

And then....kolacky time!
http://img.photobucket.com/albums/v80/mountaineer/Slavic/12deserts.jpg

Notice under the kolacky, on the 2nd tier...what is that? More RED GELATINOUS MASS.
That's right, here it is attached to cake. It's a salad AND a dessert.

We finally got that gelatinous course down...
http://img.photobucket.com/albums/v80/mountaineer/Slavic/07Wendyjellosmile.jpg

*(unfortunately the specialty characters are a mystery to me in this software, so some
words will be missing their add on's)

This is sad -- I am looking at these pics and now I want pierogis. AGAIN!!! Hope the links
to pictures work for you. Wish my camera was better but at least I got some shots. Next
up, Polish deli. Probably will take some shots on Paczki Day in February.

--Freelance Mystic
