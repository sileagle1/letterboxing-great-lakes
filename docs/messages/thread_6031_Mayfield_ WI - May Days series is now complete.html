Mayfield, WI - May Days series is now complete

From: TJ_Mich (tj_mich@ameritech.net) | Date: 2009-08-29 18:35:31 UTC-07:00
For anyone who may have been waiting, the final 2 boxes in the "May Days Near Mayfield" series have finally been planted. Sorry they couldn't be available back in May with the other 6 boxes... but this location, with newly-completed trails, is well worth a visit any time of year.

Note that it's a significantly longer trek than the other two sites: a 3 mile hike minimum, with an optional loop making it 4.2 miles. The Nickerboxer and I had fun placing them today... even though it seemed more like a cool fall day than the last Saturday in August!

--TJ_Mich

What are the chances! Due to all sorts of situations we haven't been able to go letterboxing since July 11th when we left WiHi and MM in Wisconsin! We finally had a free day today and decided to try for a few boxes. By noon we hadn't decided what direction we were heading. But eventually nixed Indiana, Michigan and Central Illinois (all in the running) We finally decided on a few clues up by the IL/WI border. What, are there almost 700 listed boxes in Northern IL?

We had looked for quite some time for one of the 9 boxes planned. It seemed to not be there, but we figured we'd return to the car, fire up the laptop and check when it was last found. All of a sudden a van backed in RIGHT next to us where we were unable to see the occupants. We were annoyed that we now couldn't go back out and look without being obvious. Tiptoe asked Tonto "What in the world is that idiot doing parking RIGHT next to us and not getting out of his car?" Soon a man got out and Tonto was looking through his rearview mirror to see what he was up to.

"He looks like Martini Man!" he exclaimed.

Sure enough it was none other! Ohhh, repentance for calling them idiots will be in order, but maybe some of you have been in that situation while letterboxing! Tiptoe turned to see MM's other half happily sitting in the passenger seat! What fun to run into them...just what we needed to lift our spirits! Tonto kept saying "Of all the gin joints in the world, and they had to pick this letterbox!"

On top of that, they had also picked the same set of boxes to visit this afternoon...and of course beat us to 3 first finders! But all is forgiven because they WERE the perfect find!

The day turned out 8 of 9 boxes found...nice walks in the great outdoors and a perfect French meal with a glass of great wine...AHHH the perfect letterboxing day!
Tiptoe & Tonto
