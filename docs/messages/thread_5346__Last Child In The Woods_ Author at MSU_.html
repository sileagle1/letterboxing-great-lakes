Hey All,

Some of you might be interested in this little tidbit:

Richard Louv, Author of Last Child in the Woods: Saving our Children
from Nature Deficit Disorder, will be speaking at MSU on October 22nd
at 7:30 in the MSU Concert Auditorium on Farm Lane. Admission is
free with ticket (available at the Wharton Center box office, -
lpanich@naturecenter.org or 269-381-1574 ext 38).

Richard Louv has launched a growing movement to reconnect children
with nature. Unstructured play outdoors has a positive impact on
healthy child development, creativity and cognitive functioning while
improving health risks such as ADHD, child obesity, and stress. He
will discuss new scientific evidence and offer practical suggestions
for parents, teachers, and anyone concerned with the future of our
children and the environment.

This ties in with Michigan's "No Child Left Inside" movement and is
sponsored by the Kalamazoo Nature Center and the W.K. Kellogg Foundation.
