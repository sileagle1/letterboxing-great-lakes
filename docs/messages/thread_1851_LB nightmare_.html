LB nightmare!

From: (katy@dsa132.org) | Date: 2005-09-27 14:40:23 UTC
I guess it's a sign that I am well and truly addicted. Last night I had
my first letterboxing nightmare.

It started off well enough: I was hiking down this gorgeous woodland
trail with Team Armadillo and a few other people. We came out onto a
sandy beach and rounded a large rocky cliff to find the letterbox we were
seeking sitting out in plain sight on top of this enormous flat rock. I
opened the box and it was crammed full of stuff. Initially I was worried
it had been taken over by some geocachers but I soon found it was
something far more sinister. Some one had collected the contents of
every letterbox from that park into the one container! There were 2
logbooks, one stamp, a first finder prize and a part of another stamp.
Of course, in my dream it made perfect sense that we now had to scour the
beach for the rest of the pieces of that one stamp, somehow without
attracting the notice of the gaggle of sunbathers just 20 yards away. As
the others looked for the pieces, I tried in vain to cram one of the
logbooks or stamps into the only container I had, a centrifuge tube, but
they were all too big. No one else had any containers and we had 4 boxes
to re-hide...

It was almost a relief to wake up and come to lab this morning. Luckily,
the only boxes I plan to go after this week are in centrifuge tubes, so
if my nightmare does come true, I will be prepared!

Katy

LB nightmare!

From: klustofin (drkaty@gmail.com) | Date: 2009-01-20 23:31:45 UTC
Does any one else have nightmares about letterboxing? Last night I
had the most frustrating nightmare! I dreamed I was at the GLKG and
had just found the first box ever planted in Ohio (yes, I know the
gathering isn't in Ohio this year, but dreams are like that). Oddly
enough, the box was hidden in a SPOR on the counter of the women's
bathroom. Even in the dream, I wondered how that pile of rocks had
been overlooked by so many muggles for so long, but I was so excited
to have finally found the box that I didn't ponder that for long. I
just took the box over to a picnic table to stamp in.

First lots of muggles prevented me from stamping in. Then lots of
other boxers showed up and started passing the stamp around. For some
reason, I was in a rush, too, so the delays were really getting to me.

Then, when the stamp was finally returned to me, I pushed the stamp
into the ink pad and the stamp dissolved! Just melted right into the
ink...

I woke up really upset. And resolved not to take that particular
brand of cold medicine again.

Katy
