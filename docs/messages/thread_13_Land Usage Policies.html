Hi everyone,



Unfortunately, I've received some not-so-good news on the topic of
proper placement and land usage in State Parks. I was contacted last
week by a (very friendly) employee at a Michigan State Park where we
have multiple letterboxes planted. She let me know that there is a new
policy on geo-caching at Michigan State Parks. Essentially, they've
decided that a Use Permit must be filed for each letterbox or other
physical object placed in a park, and a $35 fee must be paid. The
letterbox can remain in place for a maximum of 3 months.



This is quite a bit different than the existing State Park practices
which are shown on the Michigan Geo-Caching website. Perhaps this
policy has not been communicated to many people yet. Even though I was
disappointed to hear about it, I was glad that this particular State
Park employee brought it to my attention in such a friendly, positive
way. That will give us a chance to remove our boxes the next time we're
in the area; perhaps we'll be able to find a non-State-Park location for
them in the future.



Below, I have pasted the message from State Park Headquarters, which the
employee forwarded to me. She also forwarded the 2 documents mentioned:
the new policy, and a sample Use Permit. I'll place those 2 documents
in the GLK group's Yahoo! database for anyone who is interested.



My reason for bringing this up, is not to start a flame war on why this
is or isn't a good policy. in fact, I was very excited to see this new
Great Lakes Group was started, since I know this group will be able to
have a much calmer discussion about such topics! But I wanted to alert
you all to it, in case other State Parks don't take the time to e-mail
or call you, like they did for me. As you'll see in the policy, they
have the authority to remove "unpermitted" boxes as of 6/1/2004, and I
would hate to see any of you lose the stamps and log books that you have
out there.

Attached is a new policy addressing geocaching on PRB lands. This is a
new treasure-hunting type "hobby" made possible through the use of GPS
units and the internet. In a nutshell, a geocacher places a container of
interesting items (?) in the park, then posts hints and GPS coordinates
on the geocaching website. Geocaching "hunters" look up these items on
the internet then go look for the cache. Typically, the finder will sign
the required log book and may exchange some items. We already have a
number of these geocaches on our lands.



Another form of this activity is known as "letter-boxing". While this
policy refers to geocaching, any activity which involves the placement
of items on PRB lands for others to find would be covered by this
policy.



Virtual caching involves the posting of hints and GPS coordinates to
direct "hunters" to an existing feature. It may be a sign, a waterfall
or other natural feature. There is no actual placement of materials on
site and there is no Use Permit fee charged for such virtual caches,
although a Use Permit is still required. Virtual caches are preferred
over physical caches (and there is a financial incentive, too!).



This policy is to take immediate effect. A sample Use Permit is also
included to provide guidance. Please distribute to your respective
units.
