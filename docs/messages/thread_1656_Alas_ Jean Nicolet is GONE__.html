Alas, Jean Nicolet is GONE!!

From: Doodle & Deedle Bug (doodle_n_deedle@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-07-30 07:05:17 UTC-07:00
Yes, we know that it's inevitable, that sooner or
later one of your planted letterbox babies will go
missing, but why THIS one, which was the box we
received for the Great Lakes Hometown Landmarks
exchange. *sigh* Last week someone emailed that they
found our Kumbaya box, but couldn't locate Jean
Nicolet (which was planted nearby), even though they
were pretty sure they had the right log. So last
night Deedle & I headed out to check on things, and
sure enough - no more, not even a trace! The log
where we had placed him no longer had a nice hole in
the top as we describe in the clues. Instead, that
whole entire end of the log is now missing. We're
wondering if a scent was left on the box that an
animal picked up on (lots of deer in those woods) and
that in their effort to retrieve the "food" they
crumbled the end of the decaying log and made off with
the box, which of course was in a plastic baggie.

We're especially dismayed because it wasn't one of our
carvings but another letterboxer's. How should we
proceed? Papeseed, as the original creator of this
box, if you want to carve a new stamp and send it to
us, we'll take care of creating the logbook & a new
container & a new hiding spot.

*sigh*

Doodle (& Deedle) Bug.
