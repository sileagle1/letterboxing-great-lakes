From: SpringChick (letterbox@comcast.net) | Date: 2008-10-01 13:10:11 UTC
So I received the following message a couple of days ago in regards to
one of my letterboxes located at Saugatuck Dunes State Park, a box
which has been quietly in place for nearly 5 years:

"I found this container in the woods one day, While out checking on my
Geocache, Didn't think it belonged there. I removed it. Sorry. Not
sure if i still have the coords' where i found it, But you can have it
back. Just let me know what to do with it."

First let me say that I really do appreciate the fact that this person
took the time to write and apologize and offer to let me have my box
back. Many people don't even give that courtesy.

But, here's the part that gets me...

I can understand the average muggle-without-a-clue coming upon a
letterbox and perhaps picking it up thinking it is trash or "didn't
belong" in the woods... but wouldn't you think that a geocacher would
be clued into the fact that people play games hiding little rubber
containers in the woods? Even if this person has been living under a
rock and has no clue what letterboxing is, given that they themselves
hide caches in the same sort of places, wouldn't you think they would
have at least opened it up to see what it was while they were there
rather than just removing it thinking it didn't belong there? Duh!

And then people wonder why letterboxers and geocachers don't always
see eye-to-eye...

Okay, rant over, please return to your regularly scheduled Wednesday.

SpringChick

From: Norse Gnome (norsegnome@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-10-01 17:02:06 UTC-07:00
Imagine our dismay when we received this message this summer...

"we found your box down at the white fish bay state boat harbor next to a rock. we had never heard of boxing so we took it thinking that we were suppose to move it into a different hiding place and leave clues on this web site. your box is now at river mouth state forest campsite on the rustic side. its by the river next to site 117 under neath the huge pine tree leaning over the river. its under the roots. :-) good luck if you want it back"

We honestly didn't even check for a couple of weeks because we thought it was a bad joke from one of our muggle friends who doesn't get our hobby, especially since the box had been reported found just a day prior. No one was more surprised than us when we actually did find our LB under the tree at campsite 117. It was the original Andrus Lake LB which had gone missing and and been replaced for over a year. According to the logbook, it got moved afewtimes, with lots of muggles logging in. The email message wasn't even from the first party to move it, but the last!We were pleased to get our original LB back and properly thanked the party who hid it well and notified us.

We continue to be puzzled when our boxes go missing whyanyone would ever want to take/move/destroy something that's not theirs????!
