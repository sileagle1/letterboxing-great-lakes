For this week's TOTW, I would like to go one step further with the
question asked by Armadillo Jo last week... do you have any special
clothes you wear letterboxing? and ask "If I didn't know you and met
you on the trail, how would I recognize you?"

Whether it be a quirky hat, your purple socks or that funny little
dance you do when you find a letterbox, what are some constants about
your letterboxing attire, gear and/or habits that would enable a
fellow boxer to identify you?

SpringChick

From: Debbie Kotlarek (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2004-08-30 18:53:14 UTC-05:00
We'll be the ones carrying the backpacks that open up to be a handy
campstool. We started using them in the winter so we would have a dry place
to sit, but liked them so much we use them all the time now. It is so much
easier to stamp in when comfortably seated. The "Sit-About" packs also give
us more room to cart things such as bug spray, extra supplies for box
repairs, etc. (Our actual LB pack has the colorful LBNA patch sewn on the
back, but it is so full of colored markers we started running out of room
for other things!)

Of course we'll also have our clue sheet in hand and, if it's summer, my
legs will be full of scratches & scrapes. Why does it seem that boxes are
usually hidden in the densest thickets of thorny brambles?!? (or maybe it's
just due to the number of places we need to look sometimes before we
actually FIND the box...)

Wisconsin Hiker (& Martini Man)

Hiker and Martini Man. They're the ones that convert into a handy sit-
and-stamp campstool. (I think WIHI&MM have a photo on the GL site

accessible to the user while he/she is sitting. I'm looking forward
to using these.

The other identifier is my omnipresent camera in its harness.
Originally carried as a prop, the camera has been recently replaced
by one that I love to use. The camera chest-harness is the best
accessory I've acquired for quite awhile. It holds the camera close
to me so it doesn't dangle around my neck as I walk, or swing down
when I lean over to search for a box. I love it.
Oh yeah, and probably the hat. When the natural head covering thins
to the point of no longer affording any protection from the elements,
it's time for the hat, fulltime.

We don't have a special dance. I'm so afraid of attracting attention
that we act more like a calm discovery, "yup, here it is".

In the fall, during hunting season, we're apt to wear blaze orange
vests if we're anywhere near open hunting areas.

Overall, I'd hope that you *couldn't* know me as a letterboxer. I try
to blend in as a birdwatcher, nature photographer, or wandering hiker.
But the compass and clues are the dead giveaway.
