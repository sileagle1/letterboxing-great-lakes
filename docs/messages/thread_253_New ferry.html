From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-06-03 06:07:54 UTC-04:00
Me! Me! I live in Muskegon, actually only about a mile from the ferry dock.
We were down there Tuesday evening and met some of the VIP folks from
Milwaukee that made the inaugural voyage across to the east shore.
Interesting bunch... OldShoe and I are hoping to make a trip to Milwaukee
on the ferry during the summer also.

Anyway, in regards to your questions... As I understand is the case in
Milwaukee, the ferry dock is not conveniently close to the downtown area.
It is on the south shore of Muskegon Lake, actually in the midst of a
Muskegon neighborhood known as "Lakeside". Furthermore Muskegon, being only
a fraction of the size of Milwaukee, does not have much in the way of
cabs -- and the two we do have concentrate their efforts at the airport,
which is on the completely opposite side of the county from the ferry.
There are rental cars in the city -- the standard companies, but not near
the ferry. Now it is possible that if you arrange a rental ahead of time
that perhaps they deliver to the ferry docks -- we found this to be the case
when we took the Ludington/Manitowoc ferry to Wisconsin a couple of years
ago. It certainly is worth checking out. Other than that, your only other
transportation option disembarking the ferry is our around-town trolley
shuttle, which does stop right near the ferry dock, and makes the rounds to
all the major attractions and areas of the city. If you are planning to
stay for a few days and get around to do letterboxing, it may be more
practical to take your car on the ferry.

As for hotels -- there are a couple of nice places in the downtown area.
These are the closest to the ferry docks (about 3 miles), but they tend to
be the priciest also. My recommendation downtown would be the Shoreline
Inn, a very nice hotel on Muskegon Lake. There is also a very cute little
motel on the north side of Muskegon Lake, very near Muskegon State Park,
called the Snug Harbor Motel. It is just a small strip motel, but very
quaint and pleasant. If you are looking to position yourself convenient to
some boxes and not necessarily intent on being in "the city," this would be
perfect. While on that side of the lake, you must stop at the Bear Lake
Tavern for a perch dinner -- it is a local legend. There are also hotels in
various areas around the city and on the south end of town near the
airport -- when making a reservation, try to avoid anything in the Muskegon
Heights area.

Whatever the case, wherever you stay, would love to try and arrange an
impromptu meeting with anyone who does come across to Muskegon on the ferry.

Can talk more at the gathering...

SpringChick
