Hello everyone, Gooble Gobble here to tell my story. I
found out aboot letterboxing from the article in
Smithsonian magazine, or was it Time? Anyway, here
goes. My wife and I were sitting in the lobby of a
local government center waiting for our number to be
called and I started looking through all the magazines
they had. I happened upon the now fateful article and
knew I was hooked right away. After we got home I
immediately looked at the LBNA site and started
drooling. The next day I carved a very rudimentary
stamp on two pink erasers. One said Gooble, the other
said Gobble. For the first 10 or 12 finds that is what
I used to stamp in with. Soon after I purchased a
Speedball cutter and some Speedball speedy-cut and
went to town. I found carving to be very easy and was
amazed at how cool my first stamp turned out. After
carving 5 stamps, which still wait to be placed, I
carved my signature stamp which I've used now for
about a year and a half.

I have 117 finds, placed no "real" boxes (I don't
count the 2 boxes I did for the 2006 gathering as
"real" boxes because they're not permanent) and have
released 7 HH's. I have a series of 27 HH's in the
works (they're a safety themed HH series. You know the
safety signs you see in industrial places like
"caustic", "emergency eye bath" "crush hazard", etc.)
be on the lookout for those and collect the whole set
: ) I have 3 personal travelers that are kind of hard
for most to get (I'm a juggler and they are only given
out to those who can demonstrate to me that they can
juggle 3, 4, or 5 balls. Start practicing for Ohio) I
do postals during the winter to feed my addiction and
get out to box whenever I can.

My free time to go boxing has been limited by the
fact that my wife doesn't like to go boxing and we
have a 1 year old daughter. Although I will definetely
be getting my daughter hooked A.S.A.P. and will be
using her for leverage to get my wife into it. He he
he he, aren't kids great? ; ) The two times I've
talked my wife into going with me the places we went
were infested with mosquitos, she was pregnant, and it
was 85+ degrees out. Hmm, don't know why she doesn't
like it.

I have been to so many cool places and have seen
things I never would have had I not been boxing. One
of the best parts about boxing is the people involved
in it. Everyone I met this summer in Delafield was so
nice and it is a great feeling to be welcomed into a
community of real, genuinely nice people. You don't
find that very often these days. I look forward to a
lifetime of boxing, getting to know those I've met
better and meeting new boxers.

As far as where my trail name comes from, it comes
from one of my all time favorite movies. The movie is
called "Freaks". It's a 1930's movie about real circus
freaks. I urge you all to see it. There is a part in
the movie where the freaks are having a party and
welcoming a new member into they're circle. One of the
freaks is standing on the table with a large goblet
and doing a funny dance while the rest of the freaks
are chanting " Gooble Gobble Gooble Gobble we accept
you as one of us Gooble Gobble Gooble Gobble. That's
the way I felt from the beginning of being a part of
this group of people. Part of this "secret club" that
runs around the woods getting eaten alive by
mosquitos, hiking for miles, getting scratched up by
thorns, getting dirty, while looking for treasure in
the form of stamps hidden in tupperware.

Thank you to all who make this obsession of ours
possible, you're a great bunch.


Gooble Gobble
