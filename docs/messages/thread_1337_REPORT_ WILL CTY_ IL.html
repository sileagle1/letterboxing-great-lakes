REPORT: WILL CTY, IL

From: Mosey (PonyExpressMail@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-05-21 15:17:41 UTC-05:00
Headed out bright and early this morning to snag the four boxes in Will
County. But it just wasn't meant to be.

1. A Puppus Among Us -- planted by Wormy Wanderers near Romeoville. This
was the first one, got there early in the morning, beautiful morning there.
The woods are rather dense and close-in but the trees aren't totally in full
leaf yet so the early morning light was quite pretty streaming in. Saw a ton
of birds, several of the little mammals (squirrels, chipmunks, etc.) and
some of the slightly larger mammals (couldn't tell if we saw three different
coyotes or saw one coyote three times). Saw absolutely no other humans.

Found the box in good shape. Quite a nifty hiding spot.

2. Route 66 Series -- Gemini Giant -- planted by Leapin' Lizards near
Wilmington. Absolutely could not find this box. Found the Giant easily,
very interesting giant, seems like a remnant from the '50s maybe? Found the
preserve easily, weren't sure which trail to take because the clues say to
"head downhill to the trail" and both trails kind of seemed to head
downhill. So we took the trail that went downhill the *most*. :-) Found the
"short loop", found the "red hand", and found what could easily have been
the "log on your left" -- actually found a couple of possibilities -- but no
box. Spent two hours trying out different possibilities and reading the
clues different ways -- but no box.

3. Then we headed on down Route 6 to look for Illinois River -- planted by
Matt the Rat near Channahon. We found the box, but not exactly in the way
we had expected.

There was a large group of Boy Scouts camping out near the horseshoe pits so
we worked our way around them to the trail and headed on down it. By this
time, the two humans and the two dawgz making this hike were kind of pooped
out from looking for the Gemini Giant box, and were pretty much just
strolling along.

Suddenly the dawgz got excited and bounced on up to some litter sitting in
the middle of the trail. It was a grungy torn baggie and it looked like
someone might have accidentally dropped their lunch. "Woof! Woof!
Snacktime!" I told the dogs "leave it!" and pushed it to the side of the
trail with my foot. I made a mental note to stop on the way back out and
pick it up to toss it in a trash container.

The clues were great, followed them with no problem, very nice glacial type
(up & down & up & down) hike until the very end. No box. Not again! we
thought. Not finding 2 out of 3? Not good. Poopersville! We've looked
for 54 boxes so far and have found all but Johnson's Mound. We searched all
over. We split up and quietly checked out what seemed like every single
"bare half-fallen tree" in that entire woods. No luck. So after another
hour or hour and a half, we decided to call it quits.

On the hike back, the dawgz again got excited about the sandwich bag that I
had pushed to the side of the trail. I stopped to pick it up to carry it
with us out to the trash. Lo and behold, it was Matt the Rat's letterbox!
Aarrrgggghhhhh!!!!!!

The poor thing was not in good shape. Surprisingly, I'm figuring that
something probably happened to it fairly recently because the logbook was
slightly damp but otherwise in good condition. All the baggies were in bad
shape, so we double-bagged the logbook with new bags, single-bagged the
stamp and pen with new bags. The rubbermaid sandwich tub had a chunk broken
out of it, however, so there was no way it was going to be waterproof. I
don't carry extra rubbermaid containers (guess I'd better start), so I dried
out the rubbermaid container, and then took a gallon size ziplock bag and
put the rubbermaid container inside of it.

Then we hid the box in the place where we *think*, according to the clues,
it was supposed to be.

So if anyone is going out to search for it in the near future, maybe
consider bringing along an extra container to replace the broken sandwich
tub?

Lesson to be Learned from Today: The Dawgz Always Know Best. Listen To Our
Dawgz.

There was a fourth box we wanted to get to in the Lockport area along the I
& M Canal. It'll have to wait until tomorrow morning. We were just too
pooped. And the dawgz were just too disappointed there didn't end up being
a sandwich in the works for them. So we stopped at McD's and got them each a
burger. We told them that they're now officially search & rescue dawgz. As
to *what* they rescue -- we'll just keep that to ourselves.

~~ Mosey ~~
