Update on "Postcards for the 82nd Airborne PT"!!

From: trailtracker99 (mmfunk99@greenapple.com) | Date: 2007-04-24 11:31:10 UTC
I will try to make this as concise as I possibly can. I wanted to
be sure to let everyone from the S.I.L.O., the Hoosier
and the Schoolhouse events and others who contributed via snailmail
know what a tremendous impact those 150 postcards and the Girl Scout
cookies had on my son and his platoon TODAY in Afghanistan!!!

Today I got the very first detailed email from my son since January.
The absolute cliff note version is that is has been pure hell for
them. The living conditions have tested even the toughest soldier.
Many days since getting to their two different mission locations, they
have been without both food and water. They had to dig foxholes into
rocky soil in the side of the mountains and from what I can tell lived
in those fox holes for weeks trying to keep scorpions out. They have
been living without electricity or running water and have had to have
everything airlifted in to them. This is all in either frigid temps
or as high as 120 degrees.

They have been working with NATO forces, helping Afghan citizens,
farmers and political leaders stop the Taliban from acts of terrorism
and major, MAJOR drug trafficking that eventually makes it way to the
US--if not stopped. The joke is that there are so many drugs, that
their sandbags could be filled with the drugs rather than sand?! In
Greg's email, it comes sailing through that they are making a decisive
difference over there!

The bads news is that 2 soldiers have been killed in action. The
American soldier is the first KIA for them in their Company in 3
deployments. He was a Sergeant in the 82nd ABN and the other one was
a Dutch recon soldier. Today they got back to their base camp for the
first real R&R since January. They also had memorial and ramp
ceremonies for both soldiers to send their remains home to their
families. Please, please say a prayer for all these soldiers and for
the families of those who lost their loved one.

Timing is everything. TODAY, all those packages of Girl Scout cookies
and all those wonderful notes, postcards, handmade and artistic cards
of Tiptoe's students were there waiting for them---and it had FAR MORE
of an impact to uplift these men than you will EVER know. The email I
got from my son was priceless. He went out of his way by sharing the
VERY postive things they have accomplished over in Afghanistan and
just how INCREDIBLE and HEART WARMING it was for them to each have a
special package waiting for them! Greg could not say it enough!

To the Schoolhouse event attendees, all of those wonderful Easter
basket donations, beef jerky for every soldier(wow Van Man!!!),
JBBK98's students handmade coptic stitch message books, socks and cash
donations are all being assembled into the next major round of care
packages so that these are ready and waiting, following their next
mission! FungusWoman, you really helped get me started on the right
foot! There have been cash donations for shipping and the St. Barbara
medals which have helped make ends meet. Dr. Watson, your generosity
and support cannot be matched. You are an amazing human being!!

So many people have gone way above and beyond in so many different,
thoughtful and creative ways. Hawkeye from GA, Safari Man, Granny
Butterfly & Coffee Mug, Rosmarinus, R-gang, Scoutdogs, scrapper,
Chicago Dogs from...well, you know! :) To the S.I.L.O. and Hoosier
event folks, those soldiers are paging through your postcards and
notes right now!!! FW, would you please let your church folks know
this for me as well?? You all have made a difference in the lives of
soldiers who don't always feel or hear the support! I don't know what
else to do but to say thank you and to give you the feedback you very
much deserve! Thank you for supporting these front line troops!

A very grateful Trailtracker
