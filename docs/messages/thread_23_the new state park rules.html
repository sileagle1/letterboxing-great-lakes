From: Deb (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-03-25 17:38:10 UTC
I understand some of the concerns of the DNR -- in particular spur
trails and damage to sensitive areas, and also their current funding
situation. However, I question that this latest policy is the right
approach to solving either of those problems.

I have to agree with Richard's comment that spur trails develop
whenever trails are used with any degree of frequency with or without
letterboxing -- just an interesting looking tree can cause a path to
be worn off the main trail. We have the good fortune in the Upper
Midwest of letting the land recooperate during the off season.
Whatever spur trails formed during the summer generally are no longer
apparent once the snow melts the following spring. Further in West
Michigan, many of the State Park hiking trails are sand, in which
spur trails are all but washed away during the next good rainstorm.

As for the funding situation... it is politics. Perhaps the State
Parks do need to look for ways to increase their revenue, but this is
not an effective way to do that. And that is where I think ignorance
comes in -- they really have no idea what this hobby is about and
think just slapping down a fee is going to generate dollars. Quite
to the contrary, I think it will just send people elsewhere and
actually decrease their revenue -- sure not enough to close down the
parks or anything, but with the situation they are in, I would think
that every little bit counts.

We will have to wait and see what happens. But I would echo the
feeling at the geocaching board -- I'm not moving my boxes until
somebody contacts me and tells me I have to.

SpringChick
