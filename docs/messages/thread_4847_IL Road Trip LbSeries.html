From: Wisconsin Hiker (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2008-03-15 21:02:17 UTC
Yeah, this year has been tough. 3 weeks ago we found a box in
northern IL, but all but the very top of the box were solidly encased
in ice. We couldn't even get to the flaps on the Lock'n'Lock.
Martini Man wanted to make another try today but we decided to wait
until next weekend instead.

We also were thwarted on a few boxes last weekend in Colorado.
They've had a LOT of snow this year, which made it great for skiing,
but not so good for letterboxing. Even what we thought would be an
easy "drive-by" at the restaurant where we met some local boxers was
unattainable. It was outside next to the building, but there was so
much snow plowed up onto it that we would have needed a bulldozer! In
the end we managed to find 5 boxes (one was indoors) but couldn't get
to 4 because of snow. However our big excitement was meeting several
CO letterboxers at the mini-meet that Front Range Hiker arranged and
logging our 500th Exchange! We had some nice food & conversation and
of course ended up bringing home a few cooties. Luckily we're
planning to attend a few gatherings this spring, so watch out! We
were also able to plant 2 boxes closer to Denver where the snow was
mostly melted.

If you want to see a photo from the MAR 8 mini-meet, you can check out
AQ or our blog at http://wihimm.blogspot.com/ (Just scroll down to
MAR 8). I really like the trail names of one couple we met there - Tai
Chi and Chai Tea. They also love to travel and recently returned from
a trip to Greece, which included planting some boxes!

We're looking forward to some better weather for boxing and hope it
will be fine for the MAY 3 event in Iowa (7 weeks away...)

Wisconsin Hiker

Re: [LbGLK] Re: IL Road Trip LbSeries

From: Carol Osada (cjosada@hotmail.com) | Date: 2008-03-16 02:06:59 UTC-06:00
We headed to Peru and Princeton, IL areas to wander the large antique mall buildings and ... look for letterboxes, of course. The snow was gone. I found 7 boxes. A great way to celebrate the start of Spring. And, I 'officially' logged my 100th Find today and then went beyond that number. I guess I'm hooked on this hobby. I've actually found more than 100 boxes, but some could not be logged. Also today, I filled up my second logbook and I'm starting my third logbook now. I found some soft ground and some muddy areas, but the snow in that area seems to have melted.

I have found two of the IL Road Trip boxes. With the price of gasoline taking a huge price jump this last week, it's going to be important to map out and plan our trips. What? You think for a moment I was going to recommend staying home....NOT! Today, we made a point of supporting those local, small IL town, family owned restaurants too.

I'm hoping that ALL the letterboxes made it through this winter. Good idea for all of us is to travel with extra supplies in case we find a box in need of some tender loving care. Good to notify the placers too.

I plan to go out on the trails again tomorrow. I guess it's spring! The letterboxers are back on the trails.

Happy Hiking to all.

Beetle

They have about a million (or 20) boxes planted in the
Littleton/Centennial area. Which just so happens to be where I go,
so that worked out for me! I had some snow issues this year, too,
but did manage to find a few. I got within a foot of one of their
boxes and couldn't get it without putting up a big red flag that
there was something hidden there, so I had to pass on it. That was a
bummer. Most of theirs tend to be quickie boxes, but they're pretty
clever clues, fun boxes. The one I did find, when you find the tree
and look in the hole, you see the hole is about 4 feet down! Way
deeper than my arm is long! The way you end up having to retrieve
the box is pretty cool. While I was there, when was the day before
Christmas, two ladies came by on horses with actual jingle bells
on. It was great! I don't think I ever actually saw a horse with
sleigh bells on before.

I've been going to that area for about 10 years, but I was only a
'boxer for the last 2 years. I haven't had a lot of luck because all
of a sudden that area is getting slammed with snow over the
winter. Prior to me being a letterboxer, when I went there for
Christmas, there was hardly any snow, 40-50 degree
weather. Perfect. If it did snow, it would only be a few inches and
melt by the next day. The past 2 years have had record snowfalls,
blizzard after blizzard. preboxed is giving me one more year to come
back, but says if it snows again that much next year, I'm banned! LOL

Bali
