LaSalle area report

From: Kathi LK Krankoski (krank@ameritech.net) | Date: 2004-10-09 22:30:32 UTC-05:00
I went down to the Starved Rock/Matthiessen/Buffalo Rock area today with a
couple of friends and their GS troop.

A hitchhiker was released...I won't say where!

Dog in the Water literally was in water...the lid was not on properly, and
the box was full of rain water. Fortunately the inner ziplocks held. Phew!

When was the last sighting of SRL#5: the Point? We were short on time, and
since I knew where it was from this summer, I thought it would be an easy
find for the girls. Wouldn't you know, I was wrong, since it was nowhere to
be found. I'll contact the placer to let her know. (Is she still active?)

If someone who has found St. Louis Canyon can contact me off-list, I'd
appreciate it. Obviously we had trouble with that one too, but I'd like
some insight as to whether or not we were on the right track.

Alas, no new finds for me, but some needed box maintenance was done (going
to have to change out some logbooks soon...I hope there isn't too much
action over the winter!). I also made arrangements for a definite placement
site for a new box and general location for another. Time to start carving!

--klk/FungusWoman
"If you place them, they will come...."

PS: Speaking of hitchhikers, who should have made an appearance in Come
Grumpy, Go Happy but Mr. Dragon of Wisconsin! I missed that one at the
Gathering. Neat design, Team Taco and Acorn!! Where did you get the dragon
hide? (He's still living at my house...I was unable to unleash him on the
world...couldn't find a container to fit him.)

Re: Dragon HH (was LaSalle area report)

From: lesann847 (l-fischer@northwestern.edu) | Date: 2004-10-10 16:12:44 UTC

Hi Grapevine Girl,

It doesn't need to go anywhere in particular, so there's nothing to feel bad
about. Glad you found it.

Team Taco and I released the HH in Dragon's honor and in appreciation for
his services to letterboxing. While it's good sometimes to have a HH travel far
and wide, for this particular HH it's sort of nice to have it make the rounds
among those who appreciate The Dragon here in the Midwest.

As a point of history, Team Taco was very clever about disseminating the
clues for this hitchhiker initially. The hitchhiker was placed at the Great Lakes
Gathering, which the Dragon organized, this summer. The crafty Team Taco
wrote mysterious "Mathman" clues (the Dragon has a series of the same
name in Wisconsin). At the campground before the gathering, never having
met the Dragon, they recognized him. Don't ask me how, it must have been a
special letterboxers' sixth sense. Anyway, they placed the clues under his
windshield wiper while he was in the camp store.

The Dragon is pretty wily. Did he tear off in hot pursuit of this mysterious box,
a box that used his series name? No. Team Taco and I are all rather
impatient, so they put another set of clues on his windshield the next day
during the gathering. Maybe the first one blew away? Nothing. It wasn't until
after the gathering that the Dragon found the time to search for this box. I
suppose it serves us right for thinking we were being so sneaky.

If anyone finds the Dragon HH, be sure to write your appreciation. He is one
of the pioneers of southern Wisconsin letterboxing and one of the inspirations
that letterboxing has bloomed here in northern Illinois.

Acorn
