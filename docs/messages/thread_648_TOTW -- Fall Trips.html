TOTW -- Fall Trips

From: SpringChick (springchick@letterbox-mi.com) | Date: 2004-09-07 11:55:31 UTC
Fall in many parts of the country, the Great Lakes area included
means color touring. I remember as a kid my parents loading us in
the car and heading north for the weekend, following two-tracks and
what my dad used to call "fire trails" through the woods of northern
Michigan collecting leaves and enjoying those last few days of
sunshine before winter set in.

I continued the tradition as I went each fall camping in Michigan's
Upper Peninsula on what I called my "hike and write" trip. For many
years I went for 4-5 days alone and spent my days hiking to
waterfalls, lighthouses, lakes and babbling brooks, and the evenings
writing by the light of the campfire. Over the years I have met
some really interesting people and learned a lot about myself and my
world on these trips. The past few years OldShoe has come with me
and letterboxing has been a big part of the trip. Regardless if I
am alone or with a friend, the U.P. is my autumn place -- my place
to enjoy the halcyon days.

So where is your autumn place?

SpringChick

From: Timbertoes (toomey@genevaonline.com) | Date: 2004-09-07 12:37:17 UTC
My autumn place is anyplace north of where I'm at (SE Wisconsin). In
past years it's been Michigan's upper peninsula, our family cabin
near Hayward, Minoqua, Devil's Lake (near Madison) and other closer
locations for quick weekend trips. This year it looks like a trip to
Door County with my husband (the non-boxer) and kids (the on/off
boxers). I'm planning the trip out now and will attempt to include
as many letterboxes as my companions will allow.

Fall is an awesome time of year - I've always loved it (I'm a
sweatshirt person at heart), but since becoming an adult I've had
some pretty awful experiences in the fall of the year. You know,
despite them, the beauty of the season still comes shining through.
Isn't that amazing?

Timbertoes
