Here are a few more recipes I found:
===================================
Tribble Gumbo

Skin fillet and debone your tribble

toss the bones into a big pot of water with a few stalks of celery, a couple of onions, garlic cloves, and a carrot -- season with salt and pepper. Simmer for several hours to extract all the flavor. Strain and discard the solids.

Make a roux with bacon grease and flour Add the tribble stock, a few cans of diced tomatoes. In another skillet saute trinity --chopped onions celery and bell peppers -- until onions are transparent. Add to gumbo. Add boneless tribble cut into bite size pieces and add a big bowl full of sliced okra. Season well with cajun seasoning and allow to simmer for at least 2 hours so that the flavors meld.

Serve over rice.
========================================

Flaming Tribble.... one a stick!
Ingredients: Tribble, Butter, Seasoning, and a Stick.

1. Build a nice campfire, preferably with de-barked hard wood for better flavor.
2. Swiftly stab the tribble with a standard sish kabob stick (the thick variety) insuring to penetrate completely.
3. Dip the trible, now on a stick, in melted butter
4. Roll the butter dipped tribble in your favorite blackening seasonings
5. Place butter coated and seasoned tribble directly over the campfire flames insuring that all hair is completely singed off.
6. Re dip and season the tribble
7. place directly on the flames for final cook. Note that the flames should be touching the tissue to properly sear the flesh and seal in the juices.
8. Cook about five minutes per side directly on the flames.
9. Disembowl the tribble (simply cut the gut from stem to stern)
10. Re-butter and season the tribble and place back over the fire for an additional three minutes per side.

Serve with fresh veggies that were stir-fried in the tribbles shield, using the remaining butter for flavoring.

Note: if the stick begins to burn, remove immediatly and cover the exposed portion on the stick with spare vamp-braces, or one of those worthless helms.
